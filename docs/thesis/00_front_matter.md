## Abstract

Large language models can accelerate engineering work, but their outputs carry no intrinsic epistemic warrant — correct and incorrect claims are generated by the same mechanism and have the same surface properties. This thesis presents Chirality, a formally specified agent operating system that governs LLM-based agents for use in regulated professional engineering practice. Rather than attempting to improve model reliability, the architecture makes the epistemic status of every claim transparent and auditable through four mechanisms: mandatory provenance, no-invention rules, conflict surfacing, and epistemic labeling.

The architecture rests on four philosophical pillars — ontology, epistemology, praxiology, and axiology — with the epistemology identified as the load-bearing pillar whose removal collapses the system's ability to support professional reliance. The four-document production kit (Datasheet, Specification, Guidance, Procedure) mirrors the philosophical structure at the deliverable level, a fractal property indicating architectural coherence.

The thesis demonstrates that the APEGA practice standard *Relying on the Work of Others and Outsourcing* applies directly to AI agent governance — not by analogy — because the professional's obligations are defined by what the professional must do, not by what the worker is. The Chirality architecture satisfies every requirement of direct supervision and control (APEGA §3.1.1) and thorough review (APEGA §3.1.2) through specific, traceable architectural mechanisms.

The system has been implemented with 35 agent instruction files, 28 deterministic tools, a three-layer invariant system (39 formally stated invariants with four-layer enforcement), and a desktop application. Classical systems engineering disciplines — architecture, configuration management, verification and validation, safety engineering, requirements engineering, control theory, formal methods, and human factors — are not compliance artifacts applied to the agent system but the mechanisms by which agents coordinate, failures are contained, and humans maintain authority.

**Keywords:** systems engineering, LLM agents, professional engineering, epistemic architecture, agent governance, formal invariants, APEGA, professional work products, provenance, write quarantine
