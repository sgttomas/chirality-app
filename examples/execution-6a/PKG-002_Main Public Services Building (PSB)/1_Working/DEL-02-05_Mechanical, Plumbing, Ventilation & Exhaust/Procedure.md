# Procedure: DEL-02-05 — Mechanical, Plumbing, Ventilation & Exhaust

## Purpose

This procedure describes:

1. **Production Procedure** — the steps a Design-Builder's mechanical team should follow to produce the deliverable artifacts (HVAC basis, exhaust design, plumbing basis, bay sumps, water fill stations, and Mechanical Design Brief) for the proposal submission and subsequent design milestones.
2. **Implementation Procedure** — the phases for implementing and verifying the mechanical systems through design, construction, commissioning, and closeout.

**Source:** RFP §7.1.2; OSR §11.1, §11.2; Addendum 03 §1.8, §1.11, §1.12, §1.14, §1.16

## Steps

### Phase A — Proposal Stage: Produce Mechanical Design Brief

**A-1. Establish HVAC Design Criteria**
- Review Owner equipment list (Functional Program, Appendix B) to identify heat-generating equipment, occupancy loads, and lighting loads.
- Establish design criteria: indoor design temperatures by zone, outdoor design conditions (Penhold, Alberta), ventilation rates per code, occupancy loads.
- Document in the HVAC Basis of Design narrative.
- **Source:** OSR §11.1

**A-2. Select HVAC System Type(s) by Zone**
- Confirm heating fuel source (ASSUMPTION: natural gas — available per Addendum 03 §1.6 "gas are within a few feet of the site").
- Propose HVAC system type for each zone: apparatus bays (unit heaters or overhead radiant), PW bays, shared spaces, offices, decontamination area.
- Coordinate with architectural concept for ductwork routing and equipment room location.
- **Source:** OSR §11.1.1; ASSUMPTION for fuel type and system selection

**A-3. Design Direct Exhaust System — Fire Apparatus Bays**
- Select exhaust capture system type (hose reel, rail-mounted, or fixed drop) — see Guidance Trade-off T-01.
- Confirm two (2) exhaust capture points per bay, applied to all four (4) fire apparatus bays.
- Size exhaust fan(s) and duct for diesel exhaust removal capacity.
- Coordinate with apparatus bay layout (DEL-02-02) for hook-up locations and apparatus parking positions.
- Describe system in the Apparatus Bay Direct Exhaust Design narrative.
- **Source:** OSR §11.1.1; Addendum 03 §1.12; Functional Program (OSR Appendix B, p.46)

**A-4. Design PW Bay Ventilation**
- Determine ventilation rate for PW bays to accommodate occasional short-term idling and very occasional welding (confirm with AHJ whether code-specific welding ventilation rates apply).
- Propose supply/exhaust configuration (wall louvers, ceiling exhaust fans, or mechanical supply/exhaust system).
- Describe system in the PW Bay Ventilation Design narrative.
- **Source:** Addendum 03 §1.11

**A-5. Address Bunker Gear Locker Room Ventilation**
- Include room-level exhaust ventilation for the bunker gear locker room in the HVAC basis.
- Consider pressure relationship to adjacent decontamination spaces — see Guidance Consideration C-04.
- **Source:** Addendum 03 §1.14

**A-6. Develop Plumbing Basis of Design**
- Identify all plumbing fixtures by room (washrooms, showers, kitchen, decontamination shower, compressor room floor drain, etc.) using the Functional Program.
- Develop preliminary fixture count and confirm code minimums per Alberta Building Code.
- Establish domestic water service entry point (coordinate with DEL-03-04 for service size and location).
- Plan drainage: positive condensation drainage within walls; roof drainage to natural drainage (no storm sewer) — coordinate with DEL-03-02 for discharge points.
- **Source:** OSR §11.2; Functional Program

**A-7. Design Bay Sumps**
- Confirm sump required in each of the four (4) fire apparatus bays and each of the four (4) PW shop bays — eight (8) sumps total. (ASSUMPTION: four PW bays based on SOW-0211; confirm exact count with DEL-02-03.)
- Determine sump sizing basis (snow melt + minor washing as governing case — see Guidance Trade-off T-02).
- Confirm discharge connection and interceptor requirements with AHJ / municipal utility authority.
- Coordinate sump locations with architectural slab design (DEL-02-04).
- **Source:** Addendum 03 §1.8

**A-8. Design Water Fill Stations**
- Locate one (1) fill station at ground level in the fire apparatus bay area; locate one (1) fill station at ground level in the PW bay area.
- Confirm minimum 2-inch (2") connection for both stations.
- Conduct preliminary hydraulic check to confirm supply pressure is adequate with other simultaneous demands — see Guidance Trade-off T-03.
- **Source:** Addendum 03 §1.16; SOW-0223

**A-9. Confirm Compressed Air Scope Boundary**
- Resolve whether compressed air system (compressor, distribution piping, drops to apparatus bays) is within DEL-02-05 or DEL-02-02 scope — see Guidance Conflict Table CON-M-01.
- Document scope boundary decision and carry forward in the Mechanical Design Brief.
- **Source:** Functional Program (OSR Appendix B); SOW-0203; ASSUMPTION

**A-10. Prepare for AHJ Coordination — Code Review and Pre-Submission Planning**
- Conduct internal code compliance review: identify all applicable standards (ABC, ASHRAE 62.1, NFPA 1500/1581, Alberta Plumbing Code, ASHRAE 90.1 if applicable).
- Document code compliance approach in HVAC Basis of Design and Plumbing Basis of Design narratives.
- Plan for pre-submission compliance self-audit step before formal permit application (Procedure Step C-2) — see Lensing A-004 (regulatory audit preparedness).
- **Source:** OSR §11.1, §11.2; RFP §7.1.2; Lensing A-004

**A-11. Draft Mechanical Design Brief**
- Compile HVAC basis, exhaust design, ventilation approaches, plumbing basis, sump design, and fill station design into the proposal Mechanical Design Brief narrative.
- Ensure the brief is written to be clear to non-technical Evaluation Committee members (per RFP §4.1).
- Brief shall address minimum content items per REQ-M-14 and demonstrate technical merit per RFP §4.1 evaluation criteria (Lensing D-003).
- Submit as part of the Proposal package.
- **Source:** RFP §7.1.2 (.4); RFP §4.1; Lensing D-003

### Phase B — Design Development: Detailed Mechanical Design

**B-1. Refine HVAC Layouts and Equipment Schedules**
- Produce HVAC duct routing plans, equipment schedules, and load calculations.
- Coordinate with architectural drawings (DEL-02-01) for ceiling heights, room layouts, and mechanical room access.
- Submit at Building Design Development milestone and 60% Detailed Design review milestone.
- **Source:** RFP §7.1.8

**B-2. Finalize Direct Exhaust System Design**
- Produce detailed design of the direct exhaust system: hose reel layout, fan specifications, duct sizing, wall/roof penetrations.
- Coordinate with DEL-02-04 (envelope/roof) for penetration locations and weatherproofing.
- **Source:** OSR §11.1.1; Addendum 03 §1.12

**B-2a. Design Bay Door and Exhaust System Interlock (Lensing D-002)**
- Coordinate with DEL-02-04 (bay door operation) and DEL-02-07 (emergency power controls) to design the interlock between bay door opening and exhaust fan activation.
- Per Guidance P-01: "System integration with bay door operation (doors open before apparatus moves)" — ensure exhaust system is capable of active before apparatus departs and is configured to run during apparatus entry/exit.
- Document interlock control logic and coordination requirements in the design drawing set.
- **Source:** Guidance P-01; Lensing D-002 (confirmed operational action of designing the interlock was missing; now added to Phase B)

**B-3. Finalize Plumbing Plans**
- Produce plumbing floor plans showing fixture locations, supply/drain/vent risers, roof drainage leaders, and bay sump drain connections.
- Obtain plumbing permit application inputs.

**B-4. Finalize Bay Sump Details**
- Produce sump detail drawings: sump pit dimensions, grate type, drain pipe sizing, interceptor (if required), and tie-in to building drainage.

**B-5. Finalize Water Fill Station Details**
- Produce fill station detail drawings: connection size, valve type, wall/floor penetration, and freeze protection consideration (within heated apparatus bay — ASSUMPTION: freeze protection not required given heated environment).

**B-6. Coordinate Generator Essential Loads**
- Finalize list of mechanical systems on generator: confirm with DEL-02-07 essential load list.
- Ensure HVAC controls can shed non-essential zones automatically during generator operation.

---

### Phase C — 100% IFC: Sealed Construction Documents

**C-1. Seal Mechanical Drawings**
- Alberta-registered Mechanical Engineer seals all mechanical/plumbing drawings and specifications for permit submission.
- **Source:** RFP §7.1.2 (seal requirement)

**C-1a. Pre-Submission Code Compliance Review (Lensing A-004)**
- Conduct internal pre-submission compliance self-audit before permit application.
- Verify that all sealed documents align with applicable standards (ABC, ASHRAE 62.1, NFPA 1500/1581, Alberta Plumbing Code, ASHRAE 90.1 if applicable).
- Confirm that code edition assumptions documented during Phase A (Step A-10) remain valid or have been updated.
- Prepare RFI response templates for anticipated AHJ questions.
- **Source:** Lensing A-004 (AHJ pre-submission review / code compliance pre-check); RFP §7.1.2

**C-2. Submit for Permit**
- Submit mechanical/plumbing permit application to AHJ, including code compliance narrative and standard edition confirmations.
- Address any AHJ corrections or requests for information (RFIs).

---

### Phase D — Construction: Installation and Inspection

**D-1. Install HVAC Systems**
- Install equipment, ductwork, controls, and exhaust systems per IFC drawings.
- Coordinate rough-in inspections with AHJ.
- **Mandatory hold points (Lensing X-002):** Define hold/witness points for critical inspections:
  - Exhaust duct pressure test (before sealing)
  - Compressed air test of apparatus bay exhaust hose reel system (if applicable)
  - Visual inspection of mechanical room equipment installation and accessibility

**D-2. Install Plumbing, Sumps, and Fill Stations**
- Install plumbing rough-in, bay sumps, and water fill stations.
- Coordinate plumbing rough-in inspection with AHJ.
- **Mandatory hold points (Lensing X-002):**
  - Sump waterproofing inspection (before concrete pour or final slab work)
  - Plumbing drain test (pressure test or dye test per code)
  - Water fill station hydrostatic pressure test at 2" connection

**D-3. Systems Integration**
- Connect mechanical systems to generator essential loads panel (coordinate with DEL-02-07 construction).
- Verify bay exhaust system integration with bay door operation (coordinate with DEL-02-04 construction).

---

### Phase E — Commissioning, Closeout, and Warranty

**E-1. Commission HVAC and Ventilation Systems**
- Perform balancing and commissioning of all HVAC and ventilation systems.
- Test apparatus bay direct exhaust system: confirm capture and fan performance at two-apparatus load.
- Test PW bay ventilation: confirm adequate air changes.
- Test bunker gear locker room ventilation.
- **Source:** RFP §7.3.6; RFP §7.5

**E-2. Commission Plumbing Systems**
- Test all plumbing fixtures, bay sumps (flow test), and water fill stations (flow and pressure at 2" connection).
- Confirm roof drainage discharge per design.

**E-3. Produce O&M Manual — Mechanical Systems**
- Compile O&M manual sections for all mechanical and plumbing systems, including equipment data sheets, maintenance schedules, warranty cards.
- Deliver as part of closeout package (coordinate with DEL-01-07).
- **Source:** RFP §7.3.6; RFP §7.5

**E-3a. As-Built Drawing Verification (Lensing X-004, REQ-M-17)**
- Produce as-built mechanical and plumbing drawings reflecting as-installed conditions (mark up IFC drawings or redraw as appropriate).
- Conduct formal comparison of as-built drawings against IFC design documents to identify and document all deviations.
- For each deviation, document: (a) location/system affected, (b) nature of deviation (dimension change, equipment substitution, routing change, etc.), (c) reason for deviation, (d) approval status (approved change order, field decision, etc.).
- Issue formal As-Built vs. IFC Comparison Report at project closeout, listing all deviations with supporting change order or approval documentation.
- **Source:** RFP closeout requirements; Lensing X-004 (mandatory examination basis — as-built accuracy verification); REQ-M-17

**E-4. Warranty Support**
- Support mechanical and plumbing warranty items through the 12-month warranty period.
- **Source:** RFP §7.6
