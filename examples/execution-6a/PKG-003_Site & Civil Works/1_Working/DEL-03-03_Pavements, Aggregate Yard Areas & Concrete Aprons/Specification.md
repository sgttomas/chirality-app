# Specification: DEL-03-03 Pavements, Aggregate Yard Areas & Concrete Aprons

## Scope

### Included

This specification governs the design and documentation of all surfacing works for the Penhold Public Services Building site, comprising:

1. **Heavy duty asphalt** — direct routing of fire apparatus from the facility to Waskasoo Avenue.
2. **Light duty asphalt** — parking areas near the main structure.
3. **Aggregate surfacing** — heavy equipment yard areas on the remainder of the site likely to be used for equipment travel.
4. **Concrete aprons** — at all overhead door access points, designed for service vehicle loads.

Source: OSR §10.3.7, OSR §10.3.10.

### Excluded

- Internal building floor slabs (governed by architectural/structural deliverables).
- Cold storage building flooring options (asphalt millings or concrete governed by OSR §10.3.8 and separate deliverable).
- Waskasoo Avenue roadway design (municipal jurisdiction; not in project scope).
- Underground utility crossings through paved areas (governed by site servicing deliverables).

## Requirements

### REQ-03-03-01: Surfacing Zone Delineation

The Design-Builder shall delineate three distinct surfacing zones on a site plan:
- Zone A: Heavy duty asphalt (fire apparatus routing to Waskasoo Avenue)
- Zone B: Light duty asphalt (parking near main structure)
- Zone C: Aggregate (heavy equipment yard remainder)

All overhead door access points shall receive concrete aprons (Zone D) regardless of the surrounding surfacing zone.

Source: OSR §10.3.7, OSR §10.3.10.

### REQ-03-03-02: Pavement Design Method

Pavement design shall use the AASHTO 1993 Flexible Pavement Design Method with the following governing parameters:

- Design Resilient Modulus (Mr): 45,000 kPa (sand subgrade, seasonal adjustment factor 0.36)
- Frost penetration depth: 2.0 m
- All pavement cross-sections shall be consistent with or exceed the minimum thicknesses established in Tables 8.1 and 8.2 of the Geotechnical Investigation Report.

Source: Geotechnical Investigation Report, Section 8.

### REQ-03-03-03: Light Duty Asphalt Cross-Section

Light duty asphalt (parking, Zone B, 100,000 ESALs minimum) shall meet the following minimum cross-section:

| Layer | Material | Minimum Thickness |
|-------|----------|--------------------|
| Surface | HMAC | 75 mm |
| Base course | Crushed granular base | 150 mm |
| SGSB | Granular sub-base | 200 mm |
| Geosynthetic | Woven geofabric (min 900 N grab tensile strength) | — |
| **Total** | | **425 mm** |

Source: Geotechnical Investigation Report, Table 8.1.

### REQ-03-03-04: Heavy Duty Asphalt Cross-Section

Heavy duty asphalt (fire apparatus routing, Zone A, 800,000 ESALs minimum) shall meet the following minimum cross-section:

| Layer | Material | Minimum Thickness |
|-------|----------|--------------------|
| Surface | HMAC | 100 mm |
| Base course | Crushed granular base | 150 mm |
| SGSB | Granular sub-base | 200 mm |
| Geosynthetics | Woven geofabric + biaxial geogrid | — |
| **Total** | | **450 mm** |

Source: Geotechnical Investigation Report, Table 8.1.

Note: If design ESAL calculations for the fire apparatus routing exceed 800,000 ESALs and approach 2,000,000 ESALs (industrial collector threshold), the Design-Builder shall use the 550 mm cross-section from Table 8.2 of the Geotechnical Investigation Report. ESAL calculation is the Design-Builder's responsibility.

### REQ-03-03-05: Aggregate Yard Surfacing

Aggregate surfacing (heavy equipment yard, Zone C) shall:

- Use imported pit run gravel, 80 mm minus, conforming to the City of Red Deer gradation specification (Geotechnical Investigation Report, Table 7.1).
- Be compacted to a minimum of 98% Standard Proctor Dry Density (SPDD) per ASTM D698.
- Be of sufficient depth to support the heaviest equipment anticipated on site (graders, tandem dump trucks, compact track loaders) without rutting. Minimum compacted depth shall be established in the pavement design report (ASSUMPTION: minimum 300 mm compacted depth anticipated; subject to design confirmation).
- Drainage cross slope of minimum 2% shall be maintained.

Source: Geotechnical Investigation Report, Section 7 and Table 7.1; OSR §10.3.7.

### REQ-03-03-06: Concrete Apron Design — Location and Extent

Concrete aprons shall be provided at all overhead door access points to the facility. Apron extent shall accommodate vehicle maneuvering at the door threshold and be dimensioned in accordance with the door widths and anticipated vehicle swept paths.

Source: OSR §10.3.10.

### REQ-03-03-07: Concrete Apron Design — Structural Loading

Concrete aprons shall be designed to support the weight and operation of service vehicles expected to use the facility, including but not limited to:
- Fire apparatus (Type 1 engines and aerial apparatus)
- Graders (John Deere GP770 class or equivalent: approx. 10'5" tall × 30' long × 8'2" wide)
- Tandem dump trucks (Freightliner M2 106 class or equivalent: up to 9'9" tall × 8'9" wide × 26' long)
- Compact track loaders, backhoes, skidsteers, sweepers

Source: OSR §10.3.10; OSR Appendix B equipment list.

### REQ-03-03-08: Concrete Apron Mix Design — Sulphate Resistance

Concrete apron mix design shall comply with sulphate exposure Class S-2 per CAN/CSA A23.1-2014 (Table 3):

- Cement type: Type HS Portland cement (sulphate-resistant) or equivalent approved by the geotechnical engineer
- Minimum compressive strength: 32 MPa at 56 days
- Maximum water-cement ratio: 0.45
- Air-entrainment: per CAN/CSA A23.1-2014 requirements for freeze-thaw exposure. **ASSUMPTION: freeze-thaw exposure class applies given Penhold climate (frost depth 2.0 m per Geotechnical Investigation Report, Section 5.5). The specific freeze-thaw exposure class (e.g., F-1 or F-2 per CAN/CSA A23.1-2014, Table 1) must be confirmed by the concrete design engineer. The applicable class will determine required air content range and may affect mix design proportioning.** [Enriched per A-001: strengthened from weak assumption to explicit confirmation requirement]

Source: Geotechnical Investigation Report, Section 8; CAN/CSA A23.1-2014, Table 3 (location TBD — referenced in Geotech Report but full standard not directly accessed). See also Guidance section "CAN/CSA A23.1-2014 Reference Provenance" for rationale on second-hand standard referencing.

### REQ-03-03-09: Subgrade Preparation

All pavement subgrades shall be prepared as follows:
- Strip and remove organic topsoil layer prior to subgrade grading.
- Compact subgrade to minimum 98% SPDD (ASTM D698) within ±2% of optimum moisture content.
- Proof roll with a minimum two passes of a single-axle 80 kN dual-wheel truck in the presence of the geotechnical engineer. Any soft/yielding areas shall be remediated (over-excavation and replacement with structural fill) prior to proceeding.

Source: Geotechnical Investigation Report, Section 8.

### REQ-03-03-10: Layer Compaction Standards

| Layer | Minimum Compaction | Test Standard |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| Subgrade | 98% SPDD, within ±2% optimum | ASTM D698 |
| SGSB | 100% SPDD | ASTM D698 |
| Granular Base Course | 100% SPDD | ASTM D698 |
| HMA Surface Course | Min 97% of 75-blow Marshall Density | Marshall Method |

Source: Geotechnical Investigation Report, Section 8.

### REQ-03-03-11: Drainage Cross Slopes

All driving surfaces, parking surfaces, and shoulders shall maintain a minimum cross slope of 2% for positive surface drainage. Runoff shall be directed to site drainage infrastructure (swales, catch basins, or storm sewer connections) as designed in DEL-03-02 Grading, Earthworks, Drainage & Stormwater (PKG-003). [Enriched per E-002: standardized cross-reference to specific deliverable ID]

Source: Geotechnical Investigation Report, Section 8.

### REQ-03-03-14: Transition Zone Design

Where surfacing types change (e.g., Zone A heavy duty asphalt to Zone C aggregate, Zone B light duty asphalt to Zone D concrete apron, Zone A to Zone D), the Design-Builder shall address:

- Edge support details (curbs, thickened edges, or transition tapers) to prevent edge failure.
- Differential settlement mitigation at the interface between rigid (concrete) and flexible (asphalt/aggregate) surfacing.
- Drainage continuity across the transition (no ponding at surfacing type boundaries).
- Joint or seal details at concrete-to-asphalt interfaces.

Transition zone details shall be shown on the pavement cross-section drawings.

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard civil design practice; Guidance "Transition Zones" consideration. [Enriched per F-002]

HMA pavement surfaces on Zone A (fire apparatus routing) shall meet a surface smoothness tolerance of TBD (e.g., 3 m straightedge tolerance or International Roughness Index threshold). **ASSUMPTION: surface smoothness is relevant for emergency vehicle ride quality on fire apparatus routing; specific acceptance criterion to be confirmed by the pavement designer or per applicable municipal or Alberta Transportation specification.**

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard pavement acceptance practice; no specific smoothness requirement found in accessible sources. [Enriched per F-004]

### REQ-03-03-13: Pavement Design Life and Maintenance Acknowledgment

The pavement design report shall state the assumed design life for each surfacing type and acknowledge maintenance cycle expectations:

- Heavy duty asphalt (Zone A): TBD design life; ASSUMPTION: 15-20 year resurfacing cycle anticipated for asphalt surfaces.
- Light duty asphalt (Zone B): TBD design life; ASSUMPTION: 15-20 year resurfacing cycle anticipated.
- Aggregate yard (Zone C): TBD design life; maintenance and regrading frequency to be stated.
- Concrete aprons (Zone D): TBD design life; should be coordinated with the facility's 50-year design life.

The design shall confirm whether pavement elevation and drainage design accommodate future resurfacing lifts (i.e., grade buildup from overlay courses), and whether aggregate yard sub-base is designed to accommodate potential future upgrade to asphalt if required.

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard practice for long-life facilities (50-year facility design life noted in project context); Guidance "Design Life Alignment" section. [Enriched per A-005]

### REQ-03-03-12: Geosynthetic Requirements

Where geosynthetics are specified in pavement cross-sections:
- Woven geofabric: minimum 900 N grab tensile strength (TenCate Mirafi RS380i or approved equivalent).
- Biaxial geogrid: strength and aperture dimensions as specified by the pavement designer (TBD in pavement design report).
- Geosynthetics shall be installed per manufacturer's recommendations and geotechnical engineer's direction.

Source: Geotechnical Investigation Report, Section 8.
