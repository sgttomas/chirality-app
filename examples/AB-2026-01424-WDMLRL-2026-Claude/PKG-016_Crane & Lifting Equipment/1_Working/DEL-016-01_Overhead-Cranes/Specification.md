## 1. Scope

### 1.1 What This Specification Covers

This specification governs the procurement and installation of two (2) 5-tonne capacity overhead cranes on trolley within the New North Shop of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition (SOW-0067). It defines requirements for equipment selection, installation, commissioning, and documentation that the Crane Contractor must satisfy.

### 1.2 Inclusions

- Supply (procurement) of two 5-tonne rated overhead crane units, each on a trolley.
- Installation of crane units on the structural runway provided under PKG-011 / DEL-002-07.
- Connection to electrical supply circuit provided under PKG-015 / DEL-015-04 (SOW-0059).
- Commissioning and functional testing of each crane.
- Submission of all required documentation (shop drawings, load data, O&M manuals, certificates).

### 1.3 Exclusions

| Item | Assigned Package |
|------|-----------------|
| Crane runway beams and structural supports | PKG-011 (Building Structure & Envelope) / DEL-002-07 |
| Electrical feeder and crane power circuit | PKG-015 (Electrical & Low-Voltage Installation) / DEL-015-04, SOW-0059 |
| Operator training and certification | Downstream — OPS-001 |
| Maintenance scheduling system | Downstream — MAINT-001 |

## 2. Requirements

### 2.1 Equipment Requirements

**REQ-016-01-01 — Quantity:** The Crane Contractor shall supply and install two (2) overhead cranes. No fewer than two cranes shall be supplied.
*Source: RFP §3.4; Decomposition SOW-0067.*

**REQ-016-01-02 — Rated Capacity:** Each crane shall have a minimum rated capacity of 5 tonne (5,000 kg). Rated capacity shall be displayed on the crane in accordance with applicable standards.
*Source: RFP §3.4.*

**REQ-016-01-03 — Trolley Mechanism:** Each crane shall be equipped with a trolley (cross-travel mechanism) to allow the hoist to traverse the full width of the bridge.
*Source: RFP §3.4 ("2 – 5-tonne overhead cranes on a trolley"); Decomposition SOW-0067.*

**REQ-016-01-04 — Ceiling Height Constraint:** All crane components (bridge, hoist, rigging, and lifted load at maximum lift) shall fit within the 35-foot (35') interior ceiling height of the New North Shop.
*Source: RFP §3.4 ("Concrete structure 35' ceiling"); App B (Shop) notes.*

**REQ-016-01-05 — Three-Phase Power:** Cranes shall be designed for connection to a three-phase electrical supply, consistent with the building's three-phase power distribution.
*Source: RFP §3.4 ("The Proposed building shall run on three-phase power"); Decomposition SOW-0059.*

**REQ-016-01-06 — Crane Type:** Cranes shall be of the overhead bridge type, travelling on runway rails mounted on structural supports provided by PKG-011. (ASSUMPTION: end-truck bridge crane configuration based on standard industrial practice and floor plan layout. Confirm configuration with structural engineer responsible for DEL-002-07.)
*Source: ASSUMPTION — consistent with RFP §3.4 requirements and App B layout.*

### 2.3 Installation Requirements

**REQ-016-01-11 — Runway Compatibility:** Cranes shall be procured to match the structural runway provided by PKG-011. Crane Contractor shall coordinate bridge span, wheel load, and rail size with the Structural Engineer (DEL-002-07) prior to procurement.
*Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (STRUCT-001 upstream dependency); Decomposition PKG-016 package description.*

**REQ-016-01-12 — Electrical Connection:** Cranes shall be connected to the dedicated electrical supply circuit provided by PKG-015 (SOW-0059). Crane Contractor shall provide crane electrical data (voltage, amperage, phase requirements) to the Electrical Engineer/Contractor for circuit sizing.
*Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (ELEC-001); Decomposition SOW-0059, PKG-015.*

**REQ-016-01-13 — Building Envelope Prerequisite:** Crane installation shall not commence until the building envelope is closed (walls and roof complete) to protect equipment and allow safe installation and testing.
*Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (SITE-001).*

**REQ-016-01-14 — Safe Installation Practices:** All installation work shall comply with Alberta Occupational Health and Safety (OH&S) regulations. The Prime Contractor's OH&S program shall govern site safety during crane installation activities.
*Source: RFP §2.15, RFP §3.3.2.*

**REQ-016-01-15 — Anti-Collision Protection (where applicable):** If two cranes share any runway rails or operate in proximity within the same bay, an anti-collision system or documented operational protocol shall be provided to prevent crane-to-crane contact. TBD: The specific requirement (anti-collision device vs. operational protocol) depends on the runway configuration (shared vs. independent runways), which must be confirmed with the Structural Engineer and crane supplier.
*Source: ASSUMPTION — standard safety practice for multiple cranes in proximity. Raised in Guidance Section 3.2. (Added per C-003: requirement created to address two-crane proximity consideration.)*

### 2.4 Commissioning and Testing Requirements

**REQ-016-01-16 — Functional Testing:** Each crane shall be functionally tested after installation including: full-span bridge travel, full-width trolley travel, hoist operation at rated load (or proof load test as required by applicable standards), limit switch function, and emergency stop function.
*Source: ASSUMPTION — standard overhead crane commissioning practice; consistent with RFP §3.1 (operational readiness) and OBJ-001, OBJ-004.*

**REQ-016-01-17 — Load Test:** A load test shall be performed and documented for each crane. TBD: The specific load test protocol (rated load test vs. proof load test), the test load percentage of rated capacity, and the pass/fail criteria shall be confirmed against the applicable Alberta Safety Code requirements and the applicable crane standard (CSA Z256 or ASME B30.2 as confirmed). The pass/fail criteria shall include, at minimum: (a) no structural deformation of bridge, runway, or supporting structure; (b) deflection within limits specified by the applicable standard; (c) all safety devices (limit switches, emergency stop) functional under load; and (d) hoist, trolley, and bridge travel operating correctly under load.
*Source: ASSUMPTION — load test is standard practice; specific protocol TBD pending standard confirmation. (Enriched per A-002 and X-004: clarified from hedging language to explicit TBD with minimum pass/fail criteria framework.)*

**REQ-016-01-18 — Completion Deadline:** Crane procurement, installation, and commissioning shall be complete on or before December 31, 2026.
*Source: RFP §3.1.*
