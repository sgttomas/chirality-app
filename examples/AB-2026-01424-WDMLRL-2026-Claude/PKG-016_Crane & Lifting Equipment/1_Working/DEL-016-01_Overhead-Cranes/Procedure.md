**Document Type:** Procedure (Operational)

**Project:** 2026 Design Build — West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition
**Owner:** Camrose County

## 1. Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce, execute, and close out the DEL-016-01 deliverable: procurement and installation of two 5-tonne overhead cranes on trolley within the New North Shop of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. It covers activities from specification development through commissioning and documentation closeout.

This procedure is intended for use by the Crane Contractor, the Design-Builder's project manager, and the project team members responsible for coordinating crane procurement with structural and electrical design packages.

## 3. Steps

### Phase A — Specification and Procurement

**Step A1 — Issue Request for Quotation (RFQ) for Cranes**

1. Develop and issue RFQ to qualified overhead crane suppliers covering:
   - Quantity: 2 units
   - Rated capacity: 5 tonne each
   - Trolley configuration
   - Building span (confirm with structural engineer — runway centre-to-centre dimension TBD)
   - Maximum hook height available given 35-foot ceiling
   - Three-phase power supply requirement
   - Duty class (confirm with County — see Guidance.md Section 3.1)
   - Required deliverables: shop drawings, load data, electrical data, O&M manuals, load test certificate
   - Delivery timeline aligned with project schedule
   - Installer qualification requirements per REQ-016-01-10
2. Record RFQ issuance date and supplier list.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-01, REQ-016-01-02, REQ-016-01-03, REQ-016-01-04, REQ-016-01-05, REQ-016-01-06.*

**Step A2 — Evaluate Crane Supplier Quotations**

1. Receive quotations from candidate suppliers.
2. Evaluate against specification requirements and quality criteria including:
   - Compliance with capacity, trolley, ceiling height, duty class, and delivery schedule requirements
   - Manufacturer's track record and experience with similar crane installations
   - Warranty terms and alignment with the project's 2-year guarantee period (RFP §2.10; see Guidance Section 3.4)
   - Applicable manufacturer quality certifications (e.g., ISO 9001 or equivalent) — ASSUMPTION: quality certification is a reasonable evaluation factor but is not a mandatory requirement unless specified by Owner
   - Cost (evaluated as best value, not solely lowest price)
   - TBD: Project Manager / Owner to confirm evaluation weighting and any additional criteria
3. Obtain preliminary wheel load / rail data from shortlisted supplier(s) for use by the Structural Engineer (DEL-002-07).
4. Document evaluation and selection rationale.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-01 through REQ-016-01-06. (Enriched per F-004: quality evaluation criteria added.)*

**Step A3 — Coordinate Crane Load Data with Structural Engineer**

1. Forward selected/shortlisted crane supplier's load data sheet (wheel loads, rail size, runway span) to Structural Engineer.
2. Confirm structural engineer has incorporated crane loads into DEL-002-07 (Crane Support Structure Details).
3. Confirm any dimensional adjustments required (e.g., bridge span adjustment to match structural runway as-built).
4. Record coordination meeting/correspondence.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-11.*

**Step A4 — Coordinate Crane Electrical Data with Electrical Engineer/Contractor**

1. Forward crane supplier's electrical data (kW, voltage, current, phase) to Electrical Engineer.
2. Confirm crane circuits (SOW-0059) are correctly sized in electrical design (DEL-004 series) and panel schedule.
3. Record coordination.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-12.*

**Step A5 — Issue Purchase Order**

1. Issue purchase order to selected crane supplier after structural and electrical data confirmed.
2. Record purchase order number, supplier, delivery date commitment, and specified lead time.
3. Monitor delivery schedule against project milestone.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-18 (deadline).*

### Phase B — Pre-Installation

**Step B1 — Review Shop Drawings**

1. Receive crane shop drawings from supplier.
2. Route shop drawings to Structural Engineer for review of runway interface (wheel loads, rail, end stops). Structural Engineer is the approval authority for structural interface aspects.
3. Route shop drawings to Electrical Engineer for review of electrical interface. Electrical Engineer is the approval authority for electrical interface aspects.
4. Project Manager is the overall approval authority for shop drawing disposition.
5. Record shop drawing disposition using standard categories: "Approved," "Approved as Noted" (minor revisions required, work may proceed), or "Revise and Resubmit" (significant issues, work shall not proceed until resubmission is approved). TBD: Confirm whether the project uses additional disposition categories.
6. Record shop drawing approval and any required revisions. All approved shop drawings shall bear the reviewing engineer's mark.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-06, REQ-016-01-09, REQ-016-01-11. (Enriched per X-005: approval authority and disposition categories defined.)*

**Step B2 — Confirm Site Readiness**

Before mobilizing crane installation crew:
1. Verify structural runway installed and accepted (PRE-016-01-05).
2. Verify building envelope closed (PRE-016-01-06).
3. Verify electrical power circuit available at crane disconnect (PRE-016-01-07).
4. Verify installer qualifications reviewed and accepted (PRE-016-01-10).
5. Obtain written site-readiness confirmation from Project Manager.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-11, REQ-016-01-13, REQ-016-01-14.*

**Step B3 — Receive and Inspect Equipment**

1. Receive crane equipment on site.
2. Inspect all components against the following acceptance criteria:
   - All components present per supplier packing list / bill of materials — verify item count and descriptions
   - No visible damage to structural members (bridge girders, end trucks), hoist unit, trolley mechanism, or electrical components
   - Nameplate data (capacity rating, model, serial number) matches purchase order specifications
   - All fasteners, hardware, and ancillary items (limit switches, safety devices, manuals) included
   - Any discrepancy or damage shall be photographed and documented on a receiving inspection form
3. Sign delivery receipt only after inspection is complete and any exceptions are noted.
4. Store components safely in enclosed or protected area pending installation.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-02. (Enriched per E-002: specific inspection criteria and acceptance checklist added.)*

### Phase C — Installation

**Step C1 — Safety Briefing and Hazard Assessment**

1. Conduct pre-installation safety meeting with all installation crew per Alberta OH&S requirements and Prime Contractor's OH&S program.
2. Review Lift Plan for rigging crane components into position (ASSUMPTION: a Lift Plan is required for hoisting and positioning bridge girder sections — confirm with Prime Contractor / OH&S officer).
3. Establish exclusion zones below work area.
4. Record safety meeting attendance.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-14.*

**Step C2 — Assemble and Install First Crane**

1. Assemble crane bridge (girder(s), end trucks) per manufacturer's installation manual. *[REQ-016-01-06]*
2. Place bridge onto runway rails. *[REQ-016-01-11]*
3. Install trolley and hoist unit on bridge. *[REQ-016-01-03]*
4. Install festoon system / power rail or conductor bar system for bridge and trolley power supply per electrical drawings and manufacturer's instructions. *[REQ-016-01-12]*
5. Connect electrical supply at crane disconnect. (Work to be performed by qualified electrician under Electrical Contractor scope — coordinate with PKG-015.) *[REQ-016-01-05, REQ-016-01-12]*
6. Install end stops and buffer assemblies on runway. *[REQ-016-01-11]*
7. Install required safety devices: upper and lower limit switches, side-travel limit switches, warning devices. *[REQ-016-01-16]*
8. If anti-collision system is required per REQ-016-01-15, install anti-collision devices per manufacturer's specifications. *[REQ-016-01-15]*
9. Photograph key installation milestones (bridge placement, trolley installation, electrical connection) for audit record. TBD: Confirm whether photographic documentation at installation milestones is a project requirement. *[E-003]*

**Step C3 — Assemble and Install Second Crane**

Repeat Step C2 for the second crane unit.

**Step C4 — Electrical Final Connection and Verification**

1. Confirm electrical connections complete for both cranes.
2. Electrical Contractor to energize crane circuits and confirm correct voltage and phase rotation.
3. Confirm emergency stop function on each crane.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-05, REQ-016-01-12.*

### Phase D — Commissioning and Testing

**Step D1 — No-Load Functional Test (each crane)**

1. Operate bridge travel: full runway travel in both directions.
2. Operate trolley travel: full cross-travel in both directions.
3. Operate hoist: up and down through full range of travel.
4. Verify limit switch operation (upper hoist limit, lower limit, bridge end limits, trolley limits).
5. Test emergency stop function.
6. If anti-collision system installed (REQ-016-01-15), test anti-collision function.
7. Document results. Record any deficiencies.
8. Photograph test activities for audit record. TBD: Confirm whether photographic documentation at commissioning is a project requirement.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-16.*

**Step D2 — Load Test (each crane)**

1. Perform load test per applicable Alberta Safety Code requirements and applicable crane standard (TBD: test standard and protocol to be confirmed per REQ-016-01-17).
2. Verify pass/fail against defined criteria per REQ-016-01-17: (a) no structural deformation, (b) deflection within standard limits, (c) all safety devices functional under load, (d) hoist, trolley, and bridge travel operating correctly under load.
3. Load test to be witnessed by Authority Having Jurisdiction (safety code officer) if required.
4. Record load test results; obtain load test certificate documenting criteria and measured values.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-17.*

**Step D3 — Safety Code Inspection**

1. **Advance scheduling:** Initiate contact with the Authority Having Jurisdiction to schedule the Alberta Safety Code inspection as early as practicable — ASSUMPTION: at minimum 4–6 weeks before the anticipated commissioning date to allow for scheduling lead time and potential re-inspection. The scheduling request should be initiated during Phase C (installation) or earlier, not after installation is complete. TBD: Confirm AHJ scheduling lead-time requirements.
2. Arrange and attend Alberta Safety Code inspection for each crane as required.
3. Obtain inspection approval / certificate.
4. Resolve any deficiencies identified by inspector before declaring commissioning complete.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-07. (Enriched per A-004: advance scheduling guidance and lead-time allowance added.)*

**Step D4 — Operational Demonstration**

1. Demonstrate operational cranes to County project representative.
2. Walk through basic operating controls and safety features.
3. Provide O&M manuals to Owner.
*Traces to: REQ-016-01-01, REQ-016-01-16.*

### Phase E — Closeout and Documentation

**Step E1 — Compile Documentation Package**

Assemble the following for handover:

| Document | Provided By |
|----------|------------|
| Crane manufacturer's O&M manual (2 copies or electronic) | Crane supplier |
| Shop drawings (as-approved set) | Crane contractor |
| Load test certificate (both cranes) — must include pass/fail criteria and measured values per REQ-016-01-17 | Crane contractor / testing agency |
| Safety code inspection certificate / approval | Authority Having Jurisdiction |
| Warranty documentation (manufacturer warranty) | Crane supplier |
| As-installed dimensional record — including actual crane span, hook height achieved, runway rail alignment measurements per Specification Section 5.1 | Crane contractor |
| Electrical connection record | Electrical Contractor (PKG-015) |
| Installer qualification records | Crane contractor |
| Photographic records (if required — see E-003 TBD) | Crane contractor |

**Step E2 — Verify Warranty Alignment**

1. Review crane supplier warranty terms against the project's 2-year guarantee period per RFP §2.10.
2. Confirm that the manufacturer warranty covers at minimum the 2-year guarantee period from the date of the Construction Completion Certificate (CCC).
3. If the manufacturer warranty is shorter than 2 years, document the gap and notify the Project Manager for resolution (e.g., extended warranty procurement or risk acceptance).
4. Record warranty verification in the documentation package.
*(Added per C-004: warranty alignment verification step created from Guidance Section 3.4 consideration.)*

**Step E3 — Submit Documentation to Project Manager**

1. Submit complete documentation package to Design-Builder's Project Manager.
2. Confirm documentation is incorporated into project closeout package (PKG-020 / DEL-020-03).

**Step E4 — Confirm Commissioning Complete**

1. Project Manager records crane commissioning as complete in project records.
2. Notify downstream parties: operator training can now be scheduled (OPS-001).
3. Notify MAINT-001 responsible party: crane equipment specs available for maintenance planning.

**Step E5 — Post-Commissioning Warranty Review (during guarantee period)**

1. TBD: Schedule a warranty review at an appropriate interval (ASSUMPTION: 12 months after CCC, or as determined by the Owner) to confirm crane operational status and identify any warranty claims.
2. Document crane operating condition, any reported issues, and warranty claim status.
3. Ensure any warranty claims are submitted to the manufacturer before the warranty/guarantee period expires.
