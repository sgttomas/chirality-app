## Scope

### In Scope

This specification governs the interior construction and fit-out of the Utility Room within the New North Shop building, as identified in SOW-0030 (Decomposition S3-D). The utility room is a dedicated infrastructure host room located in the south zone of the New North Shop, situated between the Parts Storage Room (west) and the Wash Bay (east), per the conceptual floor plan (App B -- Shop).

The following work is within scope of this deliverable:

- Structural completion of utility room (interior partition walls, openings, blocking)
- Wall and floor finishes rated for industrial/mechanical equipment environments
- Fire-rated wall assemblies if required by Alberta Building Code or National Fire Code (TBD -- see REQ-012-02-014; enriched per A-004)
- Equipment platforms and structural mounting infrastructure for PKG-013 mechanical equipment
- Access doors and service access provisions (including fire-rated doors if applicable)
- Access panel provisions where required
- Coordination of floor drain rough-in location with PKG-014 (Plumbing)
- Coordination of 50,000 L cistern installation location and plinth/mounting with PKG-014
- Mezzanine stair landing interface and access provisions
- Lighting rough-in coordination with PKG-015 (Electrical) for 2x 8' LED fixtures
- Ventilation interface coordination with PKG-013 (Mechanical & HVAC)
- Penetration sealing and fire-stopping of wall/ceiling penetrations after MEP installations (coordination with PKG-013, PKG-014, PKG-015; enriched per E-003)

### Out of Scope

The following are explicitly outside this deliverable:

- Building structural system and concrete envelope (PKG-011)
- Mechanical and HVAC equipment installation (PKG-013)
- Plumbing and cistern equipment installation (PKG-014)
- Electrical and lighting fixture installation (PKG-015)
- Mezzanine structural construction (PKG-011)
- Foundation construction (PKG-010)
- Renovation of existing building (PKG-017)
- Parts Storage Room (DEL-012-01)
- Office Space (DEL-012-03)

## Requirements

### REQ-012-02-001 — Room Construction
**Statement:** The utility room shall be constructed as a fully enclosed interior room within the New North Shop, with walls, ceiling interface, and access door(s) as shown on the Issued for Construction (IFC) drawings. Access door specifications (count, size, type, fire rating, swing direction) shall be confirmed at design stage based on equipment access requirements and code compliance. (Enriched per B-002)
**Source:** RFP S3.1, App B (Shop), SOW-0030
**Verification:** Visual inspection of completed room; IFC drawing conformance check; door specification conformance

### REQ-012-02-002 — Code Compliance
**Statement:** All construction shall comply with applicable Alberta Safety Codes Act (RSA 2000), Alberta Building Code (current edition), National Fire Code (Alberta adoption -- ASSUMPTION: likely applicable), and all applicable local regulations. Issued for Construction drawings shall be signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer licensed in the Province of Alberta. P.Eng. stamp shall be verified by County PM before construction commences. (Enriched per X-004)
**Source:** RFP S3.3.2, S3.4
**Verification:** Building permit issuance; P.Eng. stamp verification (hold-point -- see Procedure Step 2A); inspection sign-offs

### REQ-012-02-003 — Equipment Environment Finishes
**Statement:** Wall and floor finishes shall meet measurable performance criteria appropriate for a mechanical equipment environment. The General Contractor shall submit a finish schedule for County PM review and approval prior to material procurement, specifying: (a) moisture resistance rating, (b) abrasion/durability class, (c) cleanability standard, and (d) chemical resistance where applicable. Finishes shall be durable, cleanable, and moisture-resistant where drainage is present. (Enriched per A-002, F-002)
**Source:** _CONTEXT.md -- Key Requirements; RFP S3.1
**Verification:** Finish schedule submittal review and approval (hold-point before procurement -- enriched per F-002); site inspection of installed finishes

### REQ-012-02-004 — Floor Drain Interface
**Statement:** The utility room floor shall include provision for a floor drain as indicated on the conceptual plumbing drawing. The General Contractor shall coordinate rough-in location and slope with the PKG-014 plumbing contractor prior to concrete placement.
**Source:** App B (Plumbing) -- floor plan showing drain in utility room zone
**Verification:** As-built plumbing confirmation; inspection prior to concrete pour

### REQ-012-02-005 — Service Accessibility
**Statement:** The utility room shall be designed and constructed to provide adequate clearance for maintenance and service access to all mechanical, electrical, and plumbing equipment housed within it. Clearances shall conform to equipment manufacturer requirements (TBD at design stage) and applicable codes. The General Contractor shall submit clearance verification documentation demonstrating compliance with PKG-013 equipment submittals at design stage, prior to construction commencement (enriched per D-001). TBD: confirm whether Alberta Building Code barrier-free access requirements apply to this mechanical equipment room in a public-authority-owned building (enriched per F-001).
**Source:** _CONTEXT.md -- Key Requirements; _DEPENDENCIES.md -- Constraint Notes
**Verification:** Clearance verification submittal at design stage (interim hold-point -- enriched per D-001); design review against equipment footprints; site inspection at completion

### REQ-012-02-006 — Equipment Platforms and Mounting
**Statement:** The utility room shall include structural equipment platforms and/or mounting provisions for PKG-013 mechanical equipment (heating unit, air exchanger, makeup air system). Platform design shall be coordinated with PKG-013 equipment specifications prior to construction.
**Source:** _CONTEXT.md -- Scope ("Equipment platforms and mounting infrastructure"); _DEPENDENCIES.md (PKG-013 dependency list)
**Verification:** Design coordination confirmation from PKG-013; inspection of installed platforms

### REQ-012-02-007 — Mezzanine Load-Bearing Interface
**Statement:** The utility room construction shall be compatible with the overhead load-bearing mezzanine above, which must be capable of supporting heavy items including oil totes and pumping equipment. Structural interface coordination with PKG-011 is required. The actual clear height of the utility room (floor to underside of mezzanine deck) shall be confirmed by the Structural Engineer and recorded in IFC drawings (enriched per B-003).
**Source:** RFP S3.4 ("Mezzanine storage above utility and parts room capable of handling heavy items such as several oil totes and oil pumping equipment"); App B (Shop) notes
**Verification:** Structural engineer coordination; IFC drawing conformance; clear height confirmation documented

### REQ-012-02-008 — Cistern Housing
**Statement:** The utility room shall provide housing for the 50,000 L water storage cistern, including any required structural pad, access clearance, and plumbing interface points, coordinated with PKG-014.
**Source:** RFP S3.4 ("Minimum 50,000 L Cistern complete with the necessary plumbing"); App B (Shop) -- cistern shown in utility room zone; App B (Plumbing)
**Verification:** Coordinate with PKG-014 scope; inspect cistern mounting provisions

### REQ-012-02-009 — Mezzanine Stair Access
**Statement:** The utility room shall incorporate or immediately adjoin the stair access to the mezzanine level, as shown on the conceptual floor plan.
**Source:** App B (Shop) -- "Stairs to Mezzanine" label in utility room zone; App B (Plumbing)
**Verification:** IFC drawing conformance; site inspection

### REQ-012-02-010 — Ventilation Provision
**Statement:** The utility room shall be ventilated in accordance with the mechanical engineer's design calculation (PKG-013), which shall define the specific ventilation rate (e.g., minimum air changes per hour or cfm) based on the equipment to be housed and applicable codes. Ventilation interfaces (openings, ductwork penetrations, exhaust provisions) shall be coordinated with PKG-013 prior to wall/ceiling completion. TBD: mechanical engineer (PKG-013) to define specific ventilation requirements; "adequate ventilation" is not a testable criterion without quantified parameters. (Enriched per C-001)
**Source:** _CONTEXT.md -- Key Requirements ("Adequate ventilation and drainage"); RFP S3.4 -- HVAC requirements
**Verification:** Receipt of mechanical engineer's ventilation design calculation; design coordination with PKG-013; inspection of penetrations and provisions

### REQ-012-02-011 — Lighting Provision (Coordination)
**Statement:** The utility room construction shall accommodate 2x 8' LED light fixtures per the conceptual electrical drawing. Blocking, conduit rough-in, and fixture mounting provisions shall be coordinated with PKG-015.
**Source:** App B (Electrical) -- "2x 8' LED in Utility Room"; SOW-0055
**Verification:** Electrical rough-in inspection; coordination confirmation with PKG-015

### REQ-012-02-012 — Completion Deadline
**Statement:** All utility room construction work shall be complete and available for PKG-013 mechanical equipment installation prior to the project completion deadline of December 31, 2026.
**Source:** RFP S3.1 -- "All Work... must be completed on or before December 31, 2026"; _DEPENDENCIES.md -- "Utility room must be complete before any PKG-013 equipment installation"
**Verification:** Construction schedule milestone; site inspection

### REQ-012-02-013 — Upstream Dependency Sequencing (CONSTRAINT)
**Statement:** Utility room construction shall not commence until the building shell (PKG-011) is structurally complete and the equipment footprint for PKG-013 mechanical systems has been finalized.
**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md -- Constraint Notes; _DEPENDENCIES.md -- External Dependencies
**Verification:** Hold-point sign-off before construction start

### REQ-012-02-014 — Fire Rating / Fire Separation (NEW -- enriched per A-004)
**Statement:** The General Contractor shall confirm with the Design Team and Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) whether the utility room, as a mechanical equipment host room, requires fire-rated wall separation from adjacent spaces under the Alberta Building Code or National Fire Code. If fire-rated separation is required, wall assemblies and access doors shall meet the specified fire rating. ASSUMPTION: fire-rated separation is likely required for a room housing mechanical/heating equipment; specific rating TBD at design stage.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION based on standard code practice for mechanical equipment rooms; RFP S3.3.2, S3.4
**Verification:** AHJ determination of fire rating requirement; design review; fire-rated assembly documentation; inspection

### REQ-012-02-015 — Gas Code Provisions (NEW -- enriched per C-002)
**Statement:** If any PKG-013 equipment to be housed in the utility room is gas-fired, the utility room construction shall accommodate Alberta Gas Safety Code requirements including combustion air provisions, flue penetrations, and gas line routing provisions. TBD: confirm fuel type for PKG-013 heating system (DEL-013-01) to determine gas code applicability.
**Source:** RFP S3.3.2 (Alberta Safety Codes compliance); Procedure Step 2.2 (Safety Code permits)
**Verification:** PKG-013 equipment fuel type confirmation; gas code compliance review if applicable; AHJ inspection

### REQ-012-02-016 — Penetration Sealing / Fire-Stopping (NEW -- enriched per E-003)
**Statement:** All wall and ceiling penetrations through utility room partitions shall be properly sealed and fire-stopped (where fire rating applies) after MEP installations by PKG-013, PKG-014, and PKG-015 have passed through them. The General Contractor shall verify that penetration sealing is complete and code-compliant as part of final inspection.
**Source:** Alberta Building Code (fire-stopping requirements -- ASSUMPTION: likely applicable); Procedure Step 5.5 ("fire-stopped if required by code")
**Verification:** Post-MEP-installation penetration sealing inspection; AHJ sign-off if fire-rated
