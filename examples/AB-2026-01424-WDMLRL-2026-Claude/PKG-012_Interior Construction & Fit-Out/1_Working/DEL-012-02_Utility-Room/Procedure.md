# Procedure — DEL-012-02: Utility Room

**Document Type:** Procedure (Operational)
**Deliverable ID:** DEL-012-02
**Package:** PKG-012 — Interior Construction & Fit-Out
**Project:** 2026 Design Build — West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition
**Owner:** Camrose County
**Revision:** R1 — 2026-02-26 (Pass 3 — Semantic Lensing Enrichment)

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce (construct) the Utility Room deliverable within the New North Shop. It covers the sequence from design coordination through construction completion and handoff, enabling the utility room to be ready for PKG-013 mechanical equipment installation, PKG-014 plumbing equipment installation, and PKG-015 electrical fixture installation.

---

## Steps

### Step 1 — Design Coordination (Pre-Construction)

**Responsible:** General Contractor (Design-Builder), with Design Team, PKG-013/014/015 subcontractors

1.1 Confirm utility room dimensions and layout with the Design Team and structural engineer, referencing the conceptual floor plan (App B -- Shop). Resolve Conflict CON-012-02-01 (dimensions TBD) and CON-012-02-04 (clear height below mezzanine TBD) at this stage. (Enriched per B-003)

1.2 Obtain PKG-013 equipment submittals (heating, air exchanger, makeup air, exhausts). Confirm equipment footprints, weights, mounting configurations, and service clearances for all equipment to be housed in the utility room. Confirm fuel type for heating system to determine gas code applicability. (Enriched per C-002)

1.3 Submit clearance verification documentation demonstrating that utility room layout accommodates all PKG-013 equipment clearance requirements. Obtain sign-off before proceeding to construction. (Enriched per D-001)

1.4 Coordinate with PKG-014 (Plumbing) to confirm: cistern dimensions, cistern pad/pit requirements, floor drain location(s), water tap location(s). Resolve Conflict CON-012-02-02 (cistern pad scope boundary) at this stage.

1.5 Coordinate with PKG-015 (Electrical) to confirm: 15A/120V outlet locations, conduit rough-in paths, lighting mounting points (2x 8' LED fixtures per App B -- Electrical).

1.6 Coordinate with PKG-013 (Mechanical/HVAC) to confirm: ductwork penetration locations, exhaust penetrations, makeup air supply connection points, and required ventilation rate (air changes per hour or cfm) for the utility room. Resolve Conflict CON-012-02-03 (penetration sleeve responsibility) at this stage. (Enriched per C-001)

1.7 Conduct code branch assessment with Design Team and AHJ: confirm fire separation requirements (CON-012-02-05), gas code applicability, and barrier-free access requirements for the utility room. (Enriched per A-004, C-002, F-001)

1.8 Confirm mezzanine stair position within utility room zone (App B -- Shop) does not conflict with equipment layout; adjust if required in IFC drawings.

1.9 Produce and submit finalized utility room layout within IFC drawings for P.Eng. stamp and design approval. IFC drawings shall include: room dimensions, clear height, door specifications (count, size, type, fire rating, swing direction), wall fire rating (if required), equipment platform locations, penetration locations, and finish specifications.

**Output:** Approved IFC drawings; equipment coordination records; scope demarcation agreements with PKG-013, PKG-014, PKG-015; clearance verification documentation; code branch assessment results.

---

### Step 2 — Permit and Regulatory Approvals

**Responsible:** General Contractor (Design-Builder)

2.1 Obtain development permit and building permit from Camrose County.
> Note: County forces pay permit fees; Proponent excludes permit fees from proposal (RFP S3.3.1).

2.2 Obtain all applicable Alberta Safety Code permits (Electrical, Gas, Plumbing, Fire -- as applicable per code branch assessment from Step 1.7).

2.3 Confirm inspection schedule with the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ). Note: County project representative must be present for all inspections (RFP S3.3.2).

**Output:** Active building permit; Safety Code permits; inspection schedule.

> **HOLD POINT (enriched per X-001):** Building permit must be in hand before proceeding to Step 3. Construction shall not commence without an active building permit. This is a formal hold-point requiring documented confirmation.

### Step 2A — IFC Drawing and P.Eng. Stamp Verification (NEW -- enriched per X-002, X-004)

**Responsible:** General Contractor + County PM

2A.1 Verify that IFC drawings bear a valid P.Eng. stamp from a Professional Engineer licensed in the Province of Alberta. County PM to confirm receipt of stamped drawings.

2A.2 Confirm that IFC drawings have been formally issued for construction.

> **HOLD POINT:** IFC drawings must be P.Eng.-stamped, approved, and formally issued for construction before any utility room construction work begins. Documented sign-off required from GC and County PM.

**Output:** P.Eng. stamp verification record; IFC issuance confirmation.

---

### Step 2B — Material Procurement and Submittal Approval (NEW -- enriched per C-003)

**Responsible:** General Contractor

2B.1 Establish and maintain a consolidated submittal register tracking all required submittals (finish schedule, equipment coordination records, clearance verification, platform design, door specifications) with status, due dates, and approval responsibilities. (Enriched per E-002)

2B.2 Submit finish schedule to County PM for review and approval per REQ-012-02-003. Finish schedule shall specify moisture resistance rating, abrasion/durability class, cleanability standard, and chemical resistance where applicable. (Enriched per F-002)

> **HOLD POINT:** Finish schedule must be approved by County PM before material procurement commences.

2B.3 Procure interior finish materials, access door(s) (including fire-rated door if required per Step 1.7 assessment), equipment platform materials, and access panel components per approved submittals.

2B.4 Procure penetration sealing and fire-stopping materials (if fire rating is applicable per Step 1.7 assessment).

**Output:** Approved finish schedule; procurement records; submittal register established and current.

---

### Step 3 — Interior Partition Construction

**Responsible:** General Contractor

3.1 Confirm building shell (PKG-011) is structurally complete and accepted at hold-point sign-off (see Prerequisites).

3.2 Lay out utility room partition wall lines per approved IFC drawings.

3.3 Construct interior partition walls to the specified height, using materials and assemblies shown on IFC drawings. If fire-rated separation is required (per Step 1.7 code branch assessment), use fire-rated wall assemblies per IFC drawings. Ensure wall framing includes blocking for equipment hangers, access panels, and door frames as specified. (Enriched per A-004)

3.4 Install access door(s) per IFC drawings. Door count, size, type, fire rating (if applicable), and swing direction per approved specifications. Door width and configuration must accommodate maintenance access for the largest equipment item to be installed or replaced (confirm against PKG-013 equipment submittals). (Enriched per B-002)

3.5 Install access panel rough-ins at locations specified on IFC drawings.

3.6 Apply wall finishes per approved finish schedule. Finishes must meet the measurable performance criteria approved in Step 2B. (Enriched per A-002)

**Output:** Utility room walls complete to rough-in stage; finishes applied; fire-rated assemblies installed (if applicable).

> **PHOTOGRAPHIC DOCUMENTATION (enriched per X-006):** Photograph wall framing, blocking, and fire-rated assemblies (if applicable) before finishes are applied. Retain in project file.

---

### Step 4 — Slab and Floor Work (Rough-In Coordination)

**Responsible:** General Contractor, coordinated with PKG-014 (Plumbing)

> CRITICAL HOLD POINT: All below-slab rough-ins must be inspected and approved before concrete placement.

4.1 Confirm floor drain location(s) with PKG-014 contractor per App B (Plumbing). Verify floor slope to drain is achievable within slab design.

4.2 Confirm cistern pad, pit, or foundation requirements with PKG-014 and structural engineer. Construct any required pad or structural provision per IFC drawings.

4.3 Confirm water tap rough-in location(s) with PKG-014.

4.4 Mark and protect all rough-in locations during concrete pour.

4.5 Complete concrete floor/slab work. Apply floor finish per approved finish schedule (sealed or treated concrete, per IFC drawings).

**Output:** Slab complete with all rough-ins in place; floor drain operational at rough-in stage; inspection record.

> **PHOTOGRAPHIC DOCUMENTATION (enriched per X-006):** Photograph all rough-in locations and below-slab provisions before concrete pour. Retain in project file.

---

### Step 5 — Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing Penetration Preparation

**Responsible:** General Contractor, coordinated with PKG-013, PKG-014, PKG-015

5.1 Install rough openings or blocking in walls and ceiling for HVAC penetrations (ductwork, exhaust, makeup air supply) per coordination with PKG-013. Size penetrations per mechanical engineer's ventilation design calculation. (Enriched per C-001)

5.2 Install conduit rough-in and junction boxes for 2x 8' LED lighting fixtures per coordination with PKG-015.

5.3 Install electrical outlet rough-ins (15A/120V) per App B (Electrical) and PKG-015 coordination.

5.4 Install mezzanine stair structure or stair rough-in provisions per IFC drawings and PKG-011 interface.

5.5 Confirm all penetrations are sized, located, and fire-stopped (if required by code per Step 1.7 assessment) per IFC drawings and Alberta Safety Codes. If gas-fired equipment is present (per Step 1.7 determination), confirm gas line routing and flue penetration provisions per Alberta Gas Safety Code. (Enriched per C-002)

**Output:** All rough-ins complete; penetrations prepared; mezzanine stair provisions in place.

> **PHOTOGRAPHIC DOCUMENTATION (enriched per X-006):** Photograph all penetrations before close-up/finishing. Retain in project file.

---

### Step 6 — Inspections (Rough-In Stage)

**Responsible:** General Contractor; County project representative must be present (RFP S3.3.2)

6.1 Request rough-in inspection from AHJ for applicable trades (framing, plumbing rough-in, electrical rough-in, mechanical rough-in, gas rough-in if applicable).

6.2 Provide copies of all completed inspection reports to County project manager within 5 business days of inspection. (Enriched per X-005)

6.3 Resolve any inspection deficiencies before proceeding. Deficiency resolution process: (a) GC documents deficiency and proposed remediation; (b) GC implements remediation; (c) GC requests re-inspection from AHJ for the deficient item(s); (d) GC provides deficiency resolution report (including original deficiency, remediation action, and re-inspection result) to County PM. Timeline: deficiencies shall be remediated within 10 business days of inspection report or as agreed with AHJ. (Enriched per F-004)

**Output:** Signed rough-in inspection reports; copies to County PM; deficiency resolution reports (if any).

> **RECORD RETENTION (enriched per X-005):** Original inspection reports retained by GC in project file. Copies provided to County PM. Records shall be retained for the duration of the warranty period (2 years post-CCC) at minimum, or as required by Alberta Safety Codes.

---

### Step 7 — Equipment Platform and Mounting Infrastructure Installation

**Responsible:** General Contractor, coordinated with PKG-013

7.1 Install structural equipment platforms and/or mounting infrastructure per approved IFC drawings and PKG-013 equipment submittals.

7.2 Confirm platform dimensions, heights, and load ratings against PKG-013 equipment submittals.

7.3 Coordinate with PKG-013 contractor to verify that platforms are correct before PKG-013 equipment delivery.

**Output:** Equipment platforms installed and accepted by PKG-013 contractor.

> **PHOTOGRAPHIC DOCUMENTATION (enriched per X-006):** Photograph equipment platforms before PKG-013 equipment installation. Retain in project file.

---

### Step 8 — Final Finishes and Completion

**Responsible:** General Contractor

8.1 Complete all wall and ceiling finishes per approved finish schedule.

8.2 Install any remaining trim, covers, access panel doors, and fixtures within PKG-012 scope.

8.3 Clean utility room of all construction debris; ensure the room is clean and tidy and ready for equipment installation.

8.4 Complete the Readiness Acceptance Checklist (enriched per D-003). The checklist shall confirm the following as a minimum:
- All partition walls complete per IFC drawings (including fire rating if applicable)
- All finishes complete per approved finish schedule
- All access doors installed and operable (including fire rating if applicable)
- All equipment platforms installed per PKG-013 submittals
- All penetrations prepared and sized per coordination records
- All rough-ins in correct locations per IFC drawings
- Floor drain operational
- Cistern structural provisions complete (per CON-012-02-02 resolution)
- Mezzanine stair provisions complete
- Room clean and free of construction debris
- All inspection deficiencies resolved
- Photographic documentation complete

8.5 Obtain sign-off on the Readiness Acceptance Checklist from PKG-013, PKG-014, and PKG-015 contractors confirming the utility room is ready for their equipment installation. County PM to witness. (Enriched per D-003)

**Output:** Utility room construction complete; room clean and available for downstream package installation; signed Readiness Acceptance Checklist.

---

### Step 8A — Post-MEP Penetration Sealing Verification (NEW -- enriched per E-003)

**Responsible:** General Contractor, coordinated with PKG-013, PKG-014, PKG-015

> Note: This step occurs after PKG-013/014/015 have completed their equipment installations through utility room penetrations.

8A.1 Verify that all wall and ceiling penetrations are properly sealed and fire-stopped (where fire rating applies) after MEP installations have passed through them.

8A.2 If penetration sealing is a shared responsibility (see CON-012-02-03 resolution), confirm that each responsible party has completed their sealing work.

8A.3 Request AHJ inspection of fire-stopping if required by code.

**Output:** Penetration sealing verification record; AHJ sign-off (if applicable).

---

### Step 9 — Request Final Inspection and Construction Completion Certificate (CCC)

**Responsible:** General Contractor (as part of overall project closeout per RFP S2.14)

> Note: The CCC is issued for the overall project, not the utility room alone. This step applies at project completion.

9.1 Confirm all utility room work is complete and all deficiencies resolved. Verify Readiness Acceptance Checklist (Step 8.4) is signed.

9.2 Submit written request to County for final inspection (RFP S2.14.2).

9.3 Facilitate final inspection by the County and AHJ; County project representative to be present.

9.4 Resolve any deficiencies identified during final inspection within 10 days of written instruction (RFP S2.10.6). Deficiency resolution process per Step 6.3 applies. (Enriched per F-004)

**Output:** Utility room accepted as part of overall project; CCC issued by Owner (RFP S2.14.3).
