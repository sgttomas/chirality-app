## 1. Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce (construct and fit out) the Parts Storage Room deliverable (DEL-012-01), from pre-construction prerequisites through final inspection and handover. It is directed at the General Contractor responsible for PKG-012 Interior Construction & Fit-Out.

The procedure covers: confirming prerequisites, executing construction, coordinating with adjacent scopes, completing fit-out, obtaining required inspections, and producing the required records.

## 3. Steps

### Step 1 — Pre-Construction Coordination Meeting

**Responsibility:** General Contractor (GC) Project Manager
**Timing:** Before any work in PKG-012 commences

1.1 Convene a coordination meeting with:
   - Mechanical contractor (PKG-013)
   - Electrical contractor (PKG-015)
   - Plumbing contractor (PKG-014) if applicable
   - Architect and/or Structural Engineer as required

1.2 Confirm the following for the parts room specifically:
   - HVAC stub-out locations and register positions relative to proposed shelving layout
   - Electrical rough-in locations for lighting fixtures (6× positions per SOW-0056) and roll-up door circuit
   - Roll-up door rough opening dimensions and header requirements
   - Partition wall layout and heights against IFC drawings
   - Mezzanine structural interface — confirm no fit-out elements conflict with mezzanine framing

1.3 Document coordination meeting minutes and distribute to all parties.

Source: R-01 §3.3.2; _DEPENDENCIES.md; Decomposition SOW-0056, SOW-0060.

---

### Step 2 — Layout and Partition Construction

**Responsibility:** General Contractor
**Timing:** After building shell and rough-in prerequisites are complete

2.1 Lay out the parts room partition walls per the approved IFC floor plan.
   - Confirm room dimensions achieve approximately 400 sq ft of useable area (SOW-0029).
   - Confirm roll-up door rough opening is centered/located per IFC drawings.

2.2 **Mezzanine-partition structural interface verification (HOLD POINT):** Before partition framing begins, verify the mezzanine-partition structural interface details with the structural engineer per IFC structural drawings. Confirm connection type, blocking requirements, and load path at the partition-to-mezzanine interface. Document verification with sign-off. (Lensing item A-004)

2.3 Construct interior partition walls:
   - Use approved materials per the IFC architectural drawings and finish schedule.
   - Coordinate wall height with mezzanine underside elevation per verified structural interface details (Step 2.2).
   - Ensure walls are plumb and square; prepare for finishes.

2.4 Frame and complete the roll-up door rough opening:
   - Confirm 6-foot clear width (SOW-0029).
   - Install lintel/header as required by structural drawings.
   - Prepare opening for roll-up door door frame/guides.

Source: R-01 §3.4; Decomposition SOW-0029; IFC drawings (TBD).

---

### Step 3 — Roll-Up Door Installation

**Responsibility:** General Contractor (may subcontract to door specialist)
**Timing:** After rough opening is complete and framed

3.1 Procure the 6-foot wide roll-up door per approved submittals/shop drawings.
   - Confirm door type (motorized vs. manual — TBD per Guidance CF-001).
   - Confirm insulation rating per design requirements.

3.2 Install roll-up door guides, barrel, curtain, and drive mechanism per manufacturer's instructions.

3.3 If motorized: coordinate with electrical contractor (PKG-015) for connection to the power circuit (SOW-0060).

3.4 Test door operation — confirm smooth operation, full open/close travel, and locking mechanism function.

Source: R-01 §3.4; Decomposition SOW-0029, SOW-0060.

### Step 5 — Shelving and Storage Infrastructure Installation

**Responsibility:** General Contractor
**Timing:** After finishes are complete and dried/cured; after electrical rough-in is complete

5.0 **HVAC access confirmation (HOLD POINT):** Before shelving installation begins, confirm with the mechanical contractor (PKG-013) that HVAC commissioning activities in the parts room area are either complete or that access to HVAC registers and equipment will not be blocked by installed shelving. If commissioning is not yet complete, coordinate shelving installation sequence to maintain required access. (Lensing item C-003)

5.1 Receive approved shelving system per shop drawings / submittals.

5.2 Install shelving units along the walls as shown on the approved IFC floor plan:
   - Follow manufacturer's installation instructions.
   - Anchor shelving to floor and/or wall per structural requirements and manufacturer's guidance.
   - Confirm shelving is level and plumb.
   - Confirm shelving layout does not obstruct HVAC registers, electrical panel access, fire protection devices (if any), or emergency egress.

5.3 Confirm shelving is load-rated per approved submittals.

5.4 Photograph installed shelving for records.

Source: App B (Shop) — floor plan; Specification REQ-012-01-009 through REQ-012-01-011.

### Step 6 — Final Coordination with PKG-013 and PKG-015

**Responsibility:** General Contractor (coordinating)
**Timing:** Before final inspection

6.1 Confirm with electrical contractor (PKG-015) that all 6× 8' LED lighting fixtures are installed and operational (SOW-0056).

6.2 Confirm with electrical contractor (PKG-015) that roll-up door motor is wired and tested (SOW-0060).

6.3 Confirm with mechanical contractor (PKG-013) that HVAC registers are installed in the parts room and system is operational (SOW-0035–SOW-0037).

6.4 Confirm that no PKG-012 elements obstruct or conflict with PKG-013 or PKG-015 installations.

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md; Decomposition SOW-0035–SOW-0037, SOW-0056, SOW-0060.

### Step 7 — Pre-Inspection Readiness Review

**Responsibility:** General Contractor
**Timing:** Before requesting Owner inspection

7.1 GC performs a self-inspection walk-through of the completed parts room:
   - Room area approximately 400 sq ft confirmed.
   - Roll-up door: operates correctly, locks, 6-foot wide opening confirmed.
   - Partitions: plumb, complete, full height.
   - Finishes: complete, no deficiencies visible.
   - Shelving: installed, level, anchored, clearances maintained.
   - Lighting: 6× 8' LED fixtures operational.
   - HVAC: supply register(s) operational, no obstructions.

7.2 **Permit verification check:** Confirm that all required permits (development permit, building permit, Safety Codes permits) are on file and current before requesting Owner inspection. (Lensing item F-002)

7.3 Compile deficiency list and resolve all items prior to Owner inspection.

7.4 Prepare record package (see Section 5 — Records).

Source: R-01 §2.14; Specification — Verification Table.

   - Alberta Safety Codes inspector(s) conduct applicable code inspections.
   - Inspection reports are provided to Owner.
