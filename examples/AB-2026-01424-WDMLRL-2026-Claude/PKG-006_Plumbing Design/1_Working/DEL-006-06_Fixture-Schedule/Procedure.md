# Procedure: DEL-006-06 — Plumbing Fixture Schedule

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to **produce** the Plumbing Fixture Schedule (DEL-006-06) for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. It is directed at the Plumbing Engineer of Record responsible for the PKG-006 Plumbing Design package.

The procedure also includes acceptance checks confirming the schedule is complete and coordinated before it is issued for construction.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Establish Fixture Inventory from Conceptual Drawing

**Action:**
1. Review the Appendix B (Plumbing) conceptual drawing (R-06) in full.
2. Identify and list every fixture symbol and label on the drawing, including:
   - Floor drains (labeled "Floor Drain" — 4 to 5 locations shown in New North Shop bays and washroom area)
   - Industrial Wash Sink (interior, near roll door)
   - Water Tap (utility room area)
   - Water Tap & Pressure Washer Reel (bay area)
   - Emergency Shower (near exterior wall, New North Shop)
   - Drain labels in washroom and near industrial wash sink
3. Record the fixture count and location description for each item.
4. Note any ambiguous locations or fixture types requiring engineering judgment.

**Output:** Draft fixture inventory list (pre-engineering)

**Source:** App B-Plumbing (R-06)

---

### Step 2 — Establish Fixture Requirements from RFP and SOW

**Action:**
1. Review RFP §3.4 Design Requirements for all plumbing-related items:
   - 50,000 L cistern and plumbing (SOW-0041)
   - 100 LPM pump with 2.5" cistern fill connection (SOW-0042)
   - 2,000 US gallon septic holding tank (SOW-0043)
   - Sump drains in repair bays (SOW-0045)
   - Floor drains as shown on plumbing drawing (SOW-0046)
   - Emergency shower (SOW-0048)
   - Water taps and pressure washer reels (SOW-0049)
   - Industrial wash sink (SOW-0050)
   - Bulk water fill (SOW-0044)
   - Urinal addition to existing washroom (SOW-0074)
   - Laundry services rough-in — new locker/change room (SOW-0073)
   - Washroom renovation fixtures — existing washroom (SOW-0072)
2. Cross-check the SOW-derived list against the conceptual drawing inventory from Step 1.
3. Identify any SOW items not visible on the conceptual drawing and flag for engineering decision.

**Output:** Reconciled fixture requirement list, with SOW reference for each item

**Source:** RFP §3.4; WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md (SOW table); App B-Plumbing

---

### Step 3 — Perform Plumbing Engineering Design

**Action:**
1. Design the complete plumbing system in coordination with architectural floor plans (DEL-001-02, DEL-001-10) and structural plans (PKG-002).
2. Determine final fixture locations, drain sizing, and vent routing (DEL-006-03).
3. Confirm water distribution routing and connection points for all fixtures (DEL-006-02).
4. Resolve Conflict C-003 (Guidance.md): confirm fixture scope for the New North Shop washroom area.
5. Resolve Conflict C-001 (Guidance.md): confirm whether cistern and pump equipment items appear in this schedule or cross-reference to DEL-006-04.
6. Calculate water demand (fixture unit method per NPC — ASSUMPTION) and confirm sizing of supply piping and cistern distribution pump (DEL-006-07).
7. Confirm septic drainage loads from fixture drainage connections.

**Output:** Coordinated plumbing design; fixture locations confirmed on drawing set

---

### Step 4 — Select Fixtures and Prepare Schedule Table

**Action:**
1. For each fixture identified in Steps 1–3, select a specific manufacturer and model (or mark TBD if selection is deferred).
2. Populate the schedule table with the following columns at minimum:
   - Fixture Mark / Tag
   - Fixture Type / Description
   - Quantity
   - Manufacturer
   - Model Number
   - Rough-in / Connection Size(s)
   - Location Description
   - Plan Sheet / Drawing Reference
   - Accessibility Note (where applicable)
   - Code Reference (where applicable)
   - Remarks
3. Include cut sheet references for specified manufacturer/model items.
4. Apply "or approved equal" language for non-safety-critical fixtures where appropriate (ASSUMPTION).
5. For the emergency shower: specify to applicable standard (ASSUMPTION: ANSI/ASSE or equivalent — confirm with engineer of record).

**Output:** Draft Fixture Schedule table

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (anticipated artifacts); Specification REQ-002, REQ-011, REQ-012, REQ-013, REQ-014

---

### Step 5 — Apply Code and Accessibility Review

**Action:**
1. Review the fixture schedule against:
   - Alberta Building Code (plumbing fixture requirements for occupancy type)
   - Alberta Plumbing Code / NPC (fixture unit values, minimum fixture counts)
   - Accessibility requirements for washroom fixtures (ASSUMPTION: ABC accessibility provisions)
2. Confirm that the minimum number of fixtures required by code is met.
3. Add code compliance notes to the schedule remarks column.
4. Add accessibility notes to applicable fixture rows.
5. Confirm emergency shower installation standard reference is documented.

**Output:** Code-reviewed schedule with compliance notes

---

### Step 6 — Coordinate with Other Disciplines

**Action:**
1. Issue draft schedule to Architect (PKG-001) for confirmation of fixture locations against room layouts and renovation plans.
2. Issue draft schedule to Mechanical Engineer (PKG-003) for washroom ventilation coordination.
3. Issue draft schedule to Electrical Engineer (PKG-004) for:
   - Pump motor electrical loads (cistern pump)
   - Emergency shower heat tracing coordination (ASSUMPTION: may be required in Alberta climate)
4. Resolve any coordination issues and update schedule.
5. Confirm cross-discipline consistency: fixture locations in the schedule match locations on architectural and plumbing drawing sets.
6. **Record coordination signoff (Enrichment X-001):** Complete a discipline coordination signoff record documenting that each coordinating discipline has reviewed and accepted the fixture schedule. The signoff record should include:
   - Discipline name and reviewer
   - Date of review
   - Confirmation of coordination (accepted / accepted with comments / rejected)
   - Comments or outstanding items
   This record shall be retained in the Records table (see below).

**Output:** Coordinated schedule, coordination issues resolved, coordination signoff record completed

---

### Step 7 — Internal Quality Check

**Action:**
1. Verify every fixture listed on DEL-006-02 (Water Distribution Plans) appears in the schedule.
2. Verify every floor drain and sump drain on DEL-006-03 (Drain & Vent Plans) appears in the schedule.
3. Verify that fixture quantities are consistent with the water demand calculation in DEL-006-07.
4. Confirm all SOW items (SOW-0045, SOW-0046, SOW-0048, SOW-0049, SOW-0050, SOW-0073, SOW-0074) are represented in the schedule.
5. Confirm that the schedule table has no blank cells except where "TBD" or "N/A" is explicitly entered.
6. Confirm cross-references (plan sheets, drawing numbers) are populated.

**Output:** Internally consistent, quality-checked schedule

---

### Step 8 — P.Eng. Review and Stamp

**Action:**
1. The Plumbing Engineer of Record reviews the schedule for completeness, accuracy, and code compliance.
2. The schedule is stamped and signed by a Professional Engineer licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.
3. The schedule is incorporated into the PKG-006 IFC drawing/document package.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (IFC drawing stamp requirement); OBJ-003

**Output:** Stamped, IFC-ready Fixture Schedule

---

### Step 9 — Issue and Record

**Action:**
1. Issue the Fixture Schedule as part of the PKG-006 IFC package (SOW-0016).
2. Provide a copy to the County project representative for review per RFP §3.3.2.
3. Retain the schedule in the project record set.
4. Provide the schedule to the plumbing installation contractor (PKG-014) as a procurement and installation reference.
