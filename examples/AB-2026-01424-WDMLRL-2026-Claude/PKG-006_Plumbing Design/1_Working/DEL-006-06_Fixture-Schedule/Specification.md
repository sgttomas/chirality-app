# Specification: DEL-006-06 — Plumbing Fixture Schedule

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-006-06 (Plumbing Fixture Schedule) is the normative tabular schedule produced by the Plumbing Engineer for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project. It covers:

- All plumbing fixtures, fittings, and appurtenances in the New North Shop (new construction)
- Plumbing fixtures in the Old North Shop renovation scope (washroom, locker/change room)
- Fixture types, quantities, models, manufacturers, specifications, and system coordination data
- Cross-references to fixture locations on the plumbing drawing set (DEL-006-02, DEL-006-03)

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (DEL-006-06 Description); WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md (PKG-006, DEL-006-06 entry); RFP §3.1, §3.4, App B-Plumbing

**Building Occupancy Classification:** TBD. The building occupancy classification under the Alberta Building Code (e.g., Group F, Division 2 or 3 — **ASSUMPTION** based on industrial maintenance shop use per RFP §3.1) determines code-required minimum fixture counts and accessibility provisions. The Architect / Plumbing Engineer shall confirm the classification. (Enrichment C-001)

### What This Deliverable Does Not Cover

- Design of the water distribution system (DEL-006-02)
- Design of drain and vent plans (DEL-006-03)
- Cistern and pump engineering details (DEL-006-04)
- Septic system engineering details (DEL-006-05)
- Plumbing calculations package (DEL-006-07)
- Plumbing specification document (DEL-006-08)
- Wash bay drainage and mud sump structural construction (PKG-011/PKG-018)
- Plumbing installation (PKG-014)

## Requirements

### REQ-001 — Complete Fixture Enumeration

**Requirement:** The Fixture Schedule shall include all plumbing fixtures, fittings, and appurtenances associated with the New North Shop and Old North Shop renovation scope, with no omissions.

**Source:** RFP §3.1 (building program), App B-Plumbing (conceptual layout), _CONTEXT.md (description)

**Priority:** Mandatory

---

### REQ-002 — Fixture Schedule Table Format

**Requirement:** The schedule shall be presented in tabular format and shall include, at minimum, the following columns for each fixture:
- Fixture tag / mark number
- Fixture type / description
- Quantity
- Manufacturer (or TBD)
- Model number (or TBD)
- Rough-in dimensions / connection sizes
- Location / drawing reference
- Remarks / notes (including accessibility and code compliance notes)

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Anticipated Artifacts — Fixture schedule table); industry standard practice for plumbing fixture schedules

**ASSUMPTION:** Column schema reflects standard Plumbing Engineer practice; final schema to be confirmed by the engineer of record.

---

### REQ-003 — Floor Drains

**Requirement:** The schedule shall include all floor drains as located on the conceptual plumbing drawing (App B-Plumbing) and as directed by the Plumbing Engineer. Floor drains shall be scheduled for:
- Service/repair bay areas (minimum two locations shown on App B)
- Mid-bay / crane bay area (one location shown)
- Wash Bay (one location shown, connecting to mud sump)
- Washroom drainage

**Source:** SOW-0046 (App B-Plumbing); App B-Plumbing conceptual drawing

---

### REQ-004 — Sump Drains

**Requirement:** The schedule shall include sump drains for the repair bays.

**Source:** SOW-0045; RFP §3.4 ("Sump drains in the repair bays")

---

### REQ-005 — Emergency Shower

**Requirement:** The schedule shall include one (1) emergency shower station at the location shown on the conceptual plumbing drawing (App B-Plumbing).

**Source:** SOW-0048; App B-Plumbing (labeled "Emergency Shower" near exterior wall of New North Shop)

**Open Question (C-002):** Determine whether an eyewash station is required in addition to, or in combination with, the emergency shower. The Guidance Examples table references "ANSI-compliant combination shower/eyewash unit (ASSUMPTION)." If a standalone eyewash is required by the applicable safety standard, it should be captured as a separate requirement or REQ-005 should be amended to specify a combination unit. **ProposedAuthority:** Plumbing Engineer / safety codes officer. **HumanRuling:** TBD.

---

### REQ-006 — Water Taps and Pressure Washer Reel

**Requirement:** The schedule shall include all water taps and pressure washer reel(s) as shown on the plumbing drawing. The conceptual drawing (App B-Plumbing) indicates:
- One (1) combined water tap and pressure washer reel station in the bay area
- One (1) water tap in the utility room / lower service area

**Source:** SOW-0049; App B-Plumbing

---

### REQ-007 — Industrial Wash Sink

**Requirement:** The schedule shall include one (1) separate industrial wash sink in the New North Shop interior.

**Terminology Note (B-003):** The canonical term for this fixture is "industrial wash sink." The conceptual drawing (App B-Plumbing) labels this as "Wash Station" and the RFP §3.1 uses "separate industrial wash sink." This document set adopts "industrial wash sink" as the normalized term. **ProposedAuthority:** Plumbing Engineer (confirm canonical term). **HumanRuling:** TBD.

**Source:** SOW-0050; RFP §3.1 ("separate industrial wash sink"); App B-Plumbing (labeled "Wash Station" on drawing)

---

### REQ-008 — Washroom Fixtures

**Requirement:** The schedule shall include all fixtures for the renovated washroom in the Old North Shop, including the addition of one (1) urinal.

**Source:** SOW-0074 (urinal); SOW-0072 (washroom renovation); RFP §3.4 ("Expand washroom facilities to include urinal and locker change room, including laundry services")

---

### REQ-009 — Laundry Services Fixtures

**Requirement:** The schedule shall include fixture rough-in provisions for laundry services in the new locker/change room (Old North Shop renovation).

**Source:** SOW-0073; RFP §3.4 ("Expand washroom facilities to include urinal and locker change room, including laundry services")

---

### REQ-010 — Water System Support

**Requirement:** The schedule shall note, at minimum, the cistern-related appurtenances that the Plumbing Engineer includes within the fixture/equipment schedule boundary, including:
- 50,000 L minimum cistern (scheduled under DEL-006-04 but cross-referenced)
- 100 LPM distribution pump
- 2.5 inch cistern filling connection
- Bulk water fill system

**Note:** Whether these items appear in this schedule or in DEL-006-04 is a design decision for the Plumbing Engineer. This schedule shall, at minimum, cross-reference those items with their scheduling location.

**Source:** RFP §3.4; SOW-0041, SOW-0042, SOW-0044

---

### REQ-011 — Manufacturer and Model Selections

**Requirement:** The Fixture Schedule shall identify specific manufacturer and model number selections, or explicitly mark items as TBD pending detailed design. Cut sheet references shall be noted for specified items.

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Anticipated Artifacts — "Manufacturer selections and cut sheets")

---

### REQ-012 — Accessibility Compliance

**Requirement:** The Fixture Schedule shall include accessibility compliance notes for applicable fixtures (washroom fixtures at minimum).

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Anticipated Artifacts — "Accessibility compliance notes"); ASSUMPTION: Alberta Building Code accessibility requirements apply (location TBD — code clause)

**Enrichment Note (A-001):** The specific Alberta Building Code accessibility clauses governing washroom fixtures in this occupancy classification are TBD. The Plumbing Engineer shall identify the applicable ABC accessibility provisions (e.g., barrier-free washroom requirements for Group F occupancies) and record the specific code clause references. Without confirmed clause references, compliance determination remains ambiguous. **ProposedAuthority:** Plumbing Engineer / Alberta Building Code. **HumanRuling:** TBD.

---

### REQ-013 — Code Compliance References

**Requirement:** The Fixture Schedule shall include code compliance references for the fixture types specified.

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Anticipated Artifacts — "Code compliance references"); RFP §3.3.2 (adherence to all applicable building codes and regulations)

---

### REQ-014 — Drawing Cross-Reference

**Requirement:** Each fixture row in the schedule shall include a cross-reference to its location on the plumbing drawing set (plan sheet and grid reference).

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Anticipated Artifacts — "Fixture locations cross-referenced to drawings")

---

### REQ-015 — P.Eng. Stamp

**Requirement:** The Fixture Schedule, as part of the IFC plumbing design package (SOW-0016), shall be signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 ("All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta")

---

### REQ-016 — Water Demand Calculation Basis

**Requirement:** The Fixture Schedule shall be consistent with the water demand calculations in the Plumbing Calculation Package (DEL-006-07). The fixture count and types shall be sufficient to support the calculated water demand.

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Anticipated Artifacts — "Water demand and drainage calculations"); SOW-0016
