## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-006-02 is the Water Distribution Plans drawing set, forming part of the Plumbing Design package (PKG-006) for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition (AB-2026-01424).

This deliverable covers:
- Plan drawings showing the potable and service water supply distribution network within the New North Shop Addition
- Isometric schematic diagrams of the supply system
- Detail sheets for connections, fittings, valves, and penetrations
- Pressure zone and hydraulic schematic diagrams
- Material, pipe, and valve schedules
- Construction notes applicable to water supply installation

This deliverable does not cover:
- The cistern storage vessel and pump systems (covered by DEL-006-04)
- Drain, waste, and vent systems (covered by DEL-006-03)
- Septic system design (covered by DEL-006-05)
- Plumbing fixture schedules (covered by DEL-006-06)
- Plumbing hydraulic calculations (covered by DEL-006-07)
- The overall Plumbing Specification document (covered by DEL-006-08)
- Mechanical/HVAC systems (covered by PKG-003)
- Renovation of the Old North Shop plumbing (may share coordination; scope TBD per design development)

### SOW Coverage

This deliverable is the primary drawing vehicle for SOW-0016 (complete final plumbing design — water supply component) and provides the design basis for the installation scope items: SOW-0041, SOW-0042, SOW-0044, SOW-0048, SOW-0049, SOW-0050.

## Requirements

### REQ-001: Cistern-Fed Distribution System

**Source:** RFP §3.4, SOW-0041, SOW-0042
**Statement:** The water distribution system shall be designed for supply from a minimum 50,000 L cistern via a pump capable of a minimum 100 LPM flow rate with a 2.5 inch filling connection.
**Rationale:** Site has no municipal water; cistern is the sole potable/service water source (RFP §3.4).

### REQ-002: Emergency Shower

**Source:** RFP App B-Plumb (drawing), SOW-0048
**Statement:** The drawing set shall show the water supply connection (cold water) to the emergency shower, at the location indicated on Appendix B (Plumbing) conceptual drawing.
**Notes:** ASSUMPTION: emergency shower supply shall comply with ANSI Z358.1 or equivalent Alberta-applicable standard for emergency eyewash and shower equipment; standard identity and clause reference TBD pending design development. **[C-001] The applicable standard must be confirmed by the Plumbing Engineer. Worker safety requirements should not rest on unconfirmed assumptions. Until confirmed, this remains an ASSUMPTION, not a binding requirement.**
**Acceptance Criteria [C-002]:** The following acceptance criteria shall be met for emergency shower supply compliance:
- Temperature range: TBD (degrees C) — tepid water per applicable standard (ASSUMPTION: 16-38 degrees C per ANSI Z358.1 Section 7.4.4, pending confirmation of applicable Alberta standard)
- Minimum flow rate: TBD (LPM) — per applicable standard (ASSUMPTION: 75.7 LPM / 20 GPM per ANSI Z358.1, pending confirmation)
- Minimum duration: TBD (minutes) — per applicable standard (ASSUMPTION: 15 minutes per ANSI Z358.1, pending confirmation)

### REQ-003: Water Taps and Pressure Washer Reel Supply

**Source:** RFP App B-Plumb (drawing), SOW-0049
**Statement:** The drawing set shall show water supply to the tap(s) and pressure washer reel(s) at the location(s) indicated on the Appendix B (Plumbing) conceptual drawing.
**Notes:** Number of taps and exact locations to be confirmed in design development; conceptual drawing shows one "Water Tap & Pressure Washer Reel" position and one additional "Water Tap" position.

### REQ-004: Industrial Wash Station Supply

**Source:** RFP §3.1, App B, SOW-0050

**Notes:** Exact fixture count and types to be confirmed in design development; fixture schedule in DEL-006-06.

### REQ-006: Bulk Water Fill System

**Source:** RFP §3.4, SOW-0044
**Statement:** The drawing set shall show, or reference by cross-drawing, the piping associated with the bulk water fill system (high-volume pump for external filling).
**Notes:** Bulk water fill system piping interface with the cistern and distribution network shall be coordinated with DEL-006-04 (Cistern & Pump Details). **[D-001] The scope boundary for bulk fill piping is unresolved (see Guidance.md CF-002). REQ-006 uses "shall show, or reference by cross-drawing" to accommodate either scope split. The Plumbing Engineer shall resolve the definitive scope boundary at preliminary design stage (DEL-006-01) and update this requirement accordingly.**

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2, Decomp. DEL-006-01

### REQ-012: Freeze Protection [F-001]

**Source:** RFP §3.4 (Alberta location), Guidance.md — Alberta Climate, REQ-008 (implicit code compliance)
**Statement:** Water supply piping in unheated areas, exterior walls, or locations exposed to freezing temperatures shall be provided with appropriate freeze protection measures as determined by the Plumbing Engineer.
**Notes:** ASSUMPTION: freeze protection is required for at least some piping runs given the building type (large shop with overhead doors, exterior connections for bulk fill) and the project location near Ferintosh, Alberta. Freeze protection strategy (insulation, heat tracing, drain-down provisions) and applicable code provisions to be confirmed by the Plumbing Engineer during design development. Parameters to be documented in Datasheet.md — Freeze Protection Parameters.
**Rationale:** Freeze protection is identified as a key concern in Guidance.md (Alberta Climate section) and Datasheet.md (Insulation TBD). An explicit normative requirement ensures this safety-critical design parameter is not overlooked.
