# Specification — DEL-006-01: Preliminary Plumbing Design

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-006-01 is the Preliminary Plumbing Design presentation for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition (the "Addition"). It establishes:

- Conceptual system layout and design parameters for all plumbing systems in the Addition and related Old North Shop renovations.
- Preliminary specification outlines for plumbing materials, equipment, and fixture selections.
- Design intent statements and system overview documentation sufficient for County approval.
- Coordination reference for other disciplines (structural, mechanical, civil) prior to detailed design.

This deliverable covers SOW-0010f and directly supports OBJ-003 (complete, coordinated preliminary design for County approval) and OBJ-004 (mechanical, plumbing, and water storage systems installed and commissioned to operational readiness).

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Final/IFC plumbing drawings (covered by DEL-006-02 through DEL-006-08 under SOW-0016).
- Plumbing installation (covered by PKG-014 construction deliverables).
- Mechanical/HVAC design (PKG-003).
- Structural design for cistern foundation, mud sump, or service pit (PKG-002).
- Civil design for site drainage or utility routing external to building (PKG-005).
- Electrical circuits for pump motors or emergency shower heaters (PKG-004).

## Requirements

### REQ-001: County Approval Gate
The preliminary plumbing design shall be submitted to Camrose County for review and approval before detailed (final) design commences.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.3.2, SOW-0010f
- **Verification:** Documented County approval sign-off or written acceptance.

### REQ-002: Cistern — Minimum Capacity
The design shall include a cistern with a minimum capacity of 50,000 litres, complete with all necessary plumbing connections.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.4, SOW-0041
- **Verification:** Cistern capacity confirmed on drawing or equipment schedule.

### REQ-003: Service Water Pump
The design shall include a pump capable of a minimum 100 LPM flow rate, with a 2.5-inch cistern filling connection, for internal service water distribution.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.4, SOW-0042
- **Verification:** Pump schedule shows minimum 100 LPM and 2.5" connection.

### REQ-004: Bulk Water Fill System
The design shall include a bulk water fill system with a high-volume pump for external vehicle or equipment filling.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.4, SOW-0044
- **Verification:** Bulk water fill shown on conceptual layout with pump indicated.

### REQ-005: Septic Holding Tank — New Installation
The design shall include a new 2,000 US gallon septic holding tank.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.4, SOW-0043, R-06
- **Verification:** Septic tank capacity confirmed on drawing or equipment schedule.

### REQ-006: Removal of Existing Septic Tank
The design shall include removal of the existing septic tank. Relocation to another site location is OUT of scope (County responsibility).
- **Source:** R-01 §3.4, SOW-0043; Decomposition §4 Scope Boundaries SOW-0205, D-002
- **Verification:** Design notes or demolition scope confirm removal only, no relocation.
- **Note (Lensing F-001):** The RFP uses the phrase "potentially removal and relocate existing septic tank" (R-01 §3.4), which is ambiguous. The Decomposition resolves this via D-002 (removal IN, relocation OUT). This conflict is tracked in Guidance > Conflict Table CF-001. Proposed authority: Decomposition D-002 decision preferred; County ruling required to confirm.

### REQ-007: Repair Bay Sump Drains
The design shall include sump drains in the repair bays.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.4, SOW-0045
- **Verification:** Sump drain locations shown in each repair bay on layout drawing.

### REQ-008: Floor Drains Throughout Shop
The design shall include floor drains throughout the shop as shown on the conceptual plumbing drawing (Appendix B Plumbing).
- **Source:** SOW-0046, R-06
- **Verification:** Floor drain locations on preliminary layout consistent with or refining R-06 conceptual drawing.

### REQ-009: Wash Bay Drainage and Mud Sump Connection
The design shall include wash bay floor drains connected to an exterior mud sump. The mud sump shall be located exterior to the building and shall be cleanable by excavator.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.4, SOW-0027b, R-06
- **Verification:** Mud sump shown on drawing with connection from wash bay floor drain; exterior location confirmed.

### REQ-010: Emergency Shower
The design shall include an emergency shower as shown on the conceptual plumbing drawing. The emergency shower location shall be within the code-required travel distance from all hazard zones (repair bays, wash bay, welding station area).
- **Source:** SOW-0048, R-06; Guidance Principle 3 (10-second travel requirement reference)
- **Verification:** (a) Emergency shower shown at location consistent with R-06 (west wall area near repair bay entrance); (b) travel distance from all hazard zones confirmed within code-required maximum (**TBD** -- applicable code edition and travel-distance standard to be confirmed; see Standards table, ANSI Z358.1 entry).
- **Note (Lensing X-001):** Original verification only checked location consistency with R-06, not code-required travel distance. Updated to include travel-distance compliance verification.

### REQ-011: Water Taps and Pressure Washer Reel(s)
The design shall include water taps and pressure washer reel(s) as shown on the plumbing drawing.
- **Source:** SOW-0049, R-06
- **Verification:** Water tap and pressure washer reel locations shown on preliminary layout.

### REQ-012: Industrial Wash Station
The design shall include a separate industrial wash station.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.1, SOW-0050, R-06
- **Verification:** Industrial wash station shown on preliminary layout.
- **Note (Lensing B-003):** Canonical term established as "industrial wash station" per Guidance terminology normalization. Previous variant terms ("industrial wash sink", "wash station", "industrial wash station (wash sink)") are superseded.

### REQ-013: Washroom Plumbing — Existing Building Renovation
The design shall include plumbing for the renovated washroom in the Old North Shop (below mezzanine), expanded to include a urinal, and for the new locker/change room with laundry services.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.4, SOW-0073, SOW-0074, R-06
- **Verification:** Washroom fixtures and laundry connections shown or listed in preliminary fixture schedule.
- **Note (Lensing A-002):** The laundry connection in the new locker/change room is tagged as **(ASSUMPTION)** in the Datasheet. The RFP (R-01 §3.4) states "new locker/change room with laundry services" but the specific plumbing basis for laundry (dedicated water supply, drain, hot water) is inferred, not explicitly stated as a plumbing requirement. If the assumption is incorrect, the scope of REQ-013 changes. Proposed authority: County or RFP addendum to confirm.

### REQ-014: Kitchenette Plumbing — Existing Building Renovation
The design shall include plumbing for the 250 sq ft kitchenette renovation in the existing Old North Shop building.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.4, SOW-0070
- **Verification:** Kitchenette plumbing scope identified in preliminary design (fixture schedule or design intent statement).

### REQ-015: Code Compliance
The plumbing design shall adhere to all applicable Alberta Safety Codes and the Alberta Building Code. All Issued for Construction (IFC) drawings (produced in later deliverables) shall be signed and stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.3.2, SOW-0008, SOW-0009, SOW-0018
- **Verification:** Design intent statement in preliminary package confirms code basis and identifies the governing code edition(s); P.Eng. stamp required at IFC stage (not at preliminary stage).
- **Open item (Lensing A-001, A-003):** The governing editions of the Alberta Building Code and National Plumbing Code of Canada are currently **TBD**. Implementation decisions (pipe sizing, fixture spacing, vent requirements, emergency shower travel distance) depend on the specific edition. The Plumbing Engineer shall confirm the applicable code editions at project kickoff, prior to finalizing the preliminary design. This TBD also affects REQ-010 (emergency shower) and the Standards table below.

### REQ-016: Safety Code Permits
A plumbing Safety Code permit shall be obtained prior to installation. Preliminary design shall be of sufficient quality to support permit application preparation.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.3.2, SOW-0007
- **Verification:** Preliminary design package includes the following minimum information for permit preparation: (a) plumbing layout plan with fixture locations and drain routing; (b) fixture schedule with counts and types; (c) system descriptions for water supply, drainage, and septic; (d) identification of governing code editions. Acceptance criteria: a Safety Codes Officer can identify the scope and systems requiring permitting from the package without additional information requests. **ASSUMPTION:** These acceptance criteria are a reasonable proxy for "sufficient information" pending confirmation with Alberta Safety Codes authority.
- **Note (Lensing X-003):** Original verification ("sufficient information for permit preparation") was subjective. Updated to include measurable acceptance criteria.

### REQ-017: Interdisciplinary Coordination
The preliminary plumbing design shall be coordinated with structural (cistern foundation, mud sump, service pit), civil (external drainage, utility routing), and mechanical (utility room equipment) disciplines.
- **Source:** OBJ-003, R-01 §3.3.2
- **Verification:** Coordination meeting minutes or design review record confirms no unresolved clash between plumbing and structural/civil/mechanical at preliminary stage. Evidence shall include: (a) documented distribution of preliminary plumbing layout to each coordinating discipline; (b) written confirmation or comment log from each discipline; (c) resolution record for any identified clashes.
- **Note (Lensing F-002):** Original verification was tagged "(ASSUMPTION)" with no defined verification method. Updated to require coordination meeting minutes and/or clash-check records as concrete verification evidence.

### REQ-018: Freeze Protection for Exterior and Exposed Plumbing
The design shall include a freeze protection strategy for all exterior and exposed plumbing, including but not limited to: cistern filling connection, bulk water fill connection, and mud sump connection. The strategy shall be appropriate for Alberta winter conditions at the project site (near Ferintosh, Alberta).
- **Source:** Guidance > Considerations > Freeze Protection in Alberta Climate; Procedure Step 2 item 5; R-01 §1.0 (site location)
- **Verification:** Design intent statement or preliminary specification identifies freeze protection approach (insulation, heat tracing, building envelope integration, or other) for each exterior/exposed plumbing run.
- **Note (Lensing C-002, C-003):** Freeze protection was identified as a consideration in Guidance and referenced in Procedure Step 2 item 5 but had no corresponding Specification requirement. Added to ensure verifiable tracking. Specific code requirements per NPC/ABC -- **ASSUMPTION: likely applicable**; code edition TBD.

### REQ-019: Drain Venting
The design shall include drain venting per the applicable plumbing code for all sanitary and floor drain connections.
- **Source:** Procedure Step 3 item 6; NPC (as adopted by Alberta) -- **location TBD** (code edition TBD)
- **Verification:** Drain vent routing shown on preliminary drainage schematic or identified in design intent statement.
- **Note (Lensing F-004):** Drain venting was referenced in Procedure Step 3 item 6 ("Design drain venting per applicable plumbing code (TBD)") but had no corresponding Specification requirement. Added to provide a verification target.

### REQ-020: Cistern Overflow and Vent Provisions
The design shall include overflow and vent provisions for the 50,000 L cistern.
- **Source:** Procedure Step 2 item 2; NPC (as adopted by Alberta) -- **location TBD** (code edition TBD)
- **Verification:** Cistern overflow and vent shown on preliminary cistern detail or identified in design intent statement.
- **Note (Lensing X-002):** Cistern overflow and venting was referenced in Procedure Step 2 item 2 ("Overflow and vent provisions") but had no corresponding Specification requirement or verification. Added to ensure verifiable tracking.
