# Procedure — DEL-006-01: Preliminary Plumbing Design

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to **produce** the Preliminary Plumbing Design deliverable (DEL-006-01) — from inputs through County submission and approval. It is intended to guide the Plumbing Engineer and project team in completing this deliverable within the design-build workflow.

## Steps

### Step 1: Review and Interpret Source Documents

1. Review R-06 (Appendix B Plumbing conceptual drawing) in detail. Record all plumbing fixtures, drain locations, equipment, and labels as shown.
2. Review R-01 §3.4 Design Requirements and extract all plumbing-specific requirements (SOW-0041 through SOW-0050).
3. Cross-reference R-06 against the text requirements in R-01 §3.4. Note any discrepancies or items shown on the drawing but not in the text, and vice versa.
4. Document open questions or ambiguities for resolution with the County or project team (e.g., water tap labels, cistern location confirmation).

**Output:** Annotated review notes; open questions log.

---

### Step 2: Establish System Layout — Water Supply

1. Confirm cistern location relative to the architectural floor plan (R-06 shows cistern in the Utility Room/mezzanine area — confirm with Architect).
2. Lay out the 50,000 L cistern, including:
   - Fill connection (2.5 inch) from bulk water fill point.
   - Service water pump (100 LPM minimum) and distribution header.
   - Overflow and vent provisions.
3. Establish bulk water fill point location at the building exterior — coordinate with civil grading for vehicle access.
4. Route distribution piping to all demand points:
   - Repair bay water tap and pressure washer reel.
   - Emergency shower.
   - Industrial wash station.
   - Washroom (new and renovated fixtures).
   - Kitchenette (renovation).
   - Locker/change room with laundry.
5. Identify freeze protection strategy for all exterior and unheated-space plumbing runs.

**Output:** Preliminary water supply schematic or plan layout.

---

### Step 3: Establish System Layout — Drainage

1. Lay out floor drain locations for repair bays (sump drains) and throughout the shop, consistent with or refining R-06 locations.
2. Design wash bay drainage system:
   - Floor drain(s) in wash bay.
   - Drain connection to exterior mud sump.
   - Mud sump location exterior to building — confirm with civil and structural for excavator access clearance.
3. Separate wash bay effluent drainage from clean drainage to septic. Confirm no cross-connection.
4. Route sanitary drains from all sanitary fixtures (washroom, industrial wash station, kitchenette, laundry) to septic holding tank.
5. Confirm drain invert elevations against existing grade from survey (DEL-008-02). Gravity drainage assumed; confirm feasibility.
6. Design drain venting per applicable plumbing code (TBD).

**Output:** Preliminary drainage and vent schematic or plan layout.

---

### Step 4: Establish System Layout — Septic

1. Confirm new 2,000 US gallon septic holding tank location at north exterior of building (per R-06), coordinating with:
   - Civil design for access road/pump-out truck approach.
   - Structural for any required tank pad.
   - Required setback distances from building and property boundaries (per applicable code — TBD).
2. Document removal of existing septic tank in design notes. Confirm no relocation scope (relocation is County responsibility per D-002).
3. Size the holding tank connection and confirm pump-out access hatch arrangement.

**Output:** Septic system layout with tank location, connection sizing, and removal note.

---

### Step 5: Locate and Detail Fixtures

1. Confirm and finalize locations for all fixtures per R-06 and REQ-010 through REQ-014:
   - Emergency shower (west wall location per R-06 — confirm code travel-distance compliance).
   - Industrial wash station (adjacent to parts room per R-06).
   - Water tap and pressure washer reel (at repair bay boundary per R-06).
   - Water tap in utility room/washroom area (per R-06 label).
2. Develop preliminary fixture schedule listing all plumbing fixtures, equipment, and connections.
3. Confirm washroom fixtures (WC, lavatory, urinal) for renovation in Old North Shop.
4. Confirm laundry connection in new locker/change room.
5. Confirm kitchenette fixtures (sink minimum).

**Output:** Preliminary plumbing fixture schedule (draft).

---

### Step 6: Interdisciplinary Coordination

1. Issue preliminary plumbing layout to:
   - Structural Engineer — for cistern pad/foundation, mud sump structure, floor slab openings for drains.
   - Civil Engineer — for septic tank setback, bulk water fill vehicle access, exterior drainage grading.
   - Mechanical Engineer — for utility room layout confirmation, no conflicts with HVAC equipment.
   - Architect — for washroom layout, industrial wash station location, and kitchenette plumbing rough-in confirmation.
2. Receive and resolve coordination comments. Document any unresolved items for the County submission cover letter.

**Output:** Coordination comment log; resolved preliminary layout.

---

### Step 7: Prepare Preliminary Design Package

1. Compile the following into the County submission package:
   - Preliminary plumbing layout plan (conceptual diagram, developed from R-06).
   - System overview documentation (written descriptions of water supply, drainage, septic, fixture systems).
   - Design intent statements for each major system.
   - Preliminary fixture/equipment schedule.
   - Preliminary specification outlines (materials, pipe types, valve standards — TBD pending code review).
   - Coordination status summary.
   - List of design assumptions and open items.
2. Confirm the package does not include items outside PKG-006 scope (e.g., mechanical, structural, civil content should be referenced, not included).

**Output:** Preliminary Plumbing Design presentation package.

---

### Step 8: Submit to County for Approval

1. Submit the preliminary design package to Camrose County through the County project manager (per R-01 §3.1 — County project manager confirmed at project award).
2. Record submission date and package reference.
3. Track County review and document any comments or required revisions.
4. Upon County approval, record written acceptance and retain in project file.
5. Issue County-approved preliminary design to detailed design team as the baseline for DEL-006-02 through DEL-006-08.

**Output:** County-approved preliminary plumbing design; documented approval record.
