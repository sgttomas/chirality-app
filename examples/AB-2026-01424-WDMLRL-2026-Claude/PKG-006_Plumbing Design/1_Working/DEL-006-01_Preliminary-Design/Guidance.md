# Guidance — DEL-006-01: Preliminary Plumbing Design

---

## Purpose

DEL-006-01 exists to establish County-approved plumbing design intent before detailed engineering begins. Under the design-build delivery model (CCDC 14–2013), the Owner has provided a concept (Appendix B Plumbing drawing) rather than a developed design. The Preliminary Plumbing Design is the proponent's first opportunity to interpret that concept, resolve ambiguities, and obtain County approval — thereby locking design direction before significant engineering expenditure is committed.

**Sources:** R-01 §3.3.2 (requirement to deliver preliminary design for County approval), R-01 §3.4 (design/build framing — "The County only has a concept in mind"), OBJ-003, OBJ-004.

---

## Principles

### 1. Cistern as the Central Water Source
The site has no municipal water supply. The 50,000 L cistern is the sole water source for the Addition. All plumbing systems — service water distribution, bulk water fill, pressure washer, emergency shower, washroom, and industrial wash station — are ultimately fed from or coordinated with this cistern and its associated pump system. The preliminary design should establish the cistern location, filling strategy, distribution routing hierarchy, and pump arrangement at a level sufficient for structural and civil coordination.

**Source:** R-01 §3.4, R-06, SOW-0041, SOW-0042.

### 2. Drainage is Safety-Critical in a Heavy-Equipment Shop
The wash bay effluent (containing equipment wash-off, potential oil contamination, and debris) must be separated from clean floor drainage and must flow to the exterior mud sump — not directly to the septic system. This separation is fundamental to environmental compliance and to maintainability of the septic holding tank. The preliminary design should make this separation explicit.

**Source:** R-01 §3.4 (mud sump from wash bay for excavator clean-out), R-06 (wash bay floor drain shown separately), SOW-0027b, SOW-0043.

### 3. Emergency Shower Location Must Respect Coverage Requirements
The emergency shower shown on R-06 is positioned at the west wall near the repair bay entrance. Plumbing codes typically require an emergency shower to be accessible within 10 seconds from the hazard area. The plumbing engineer should confirm the location is within code-required travel distance from all hazard zones (repair bays, wash bay, welding station area) and that freeze protection or tempering is addressed for the Alberta climate.

**Source:** R-06, SOW-0048. **ASSUMPTION:** Specific travel-distance requirement governed by applicable Alberta Safety Code; edition TBD.

### 4. Preliminary Design as a Coordination Instrument
The preliminary design will be reviewed by the County and must also serve as the coordination baseline for structural (cistern pad/pit, mud sump structure), civil (utility routing, septic tank setback, bulk fill access), and mechanical (utility room equipment placement) disciplines. The Plumbing Engineer should issue the preliminary package to these disciplines for review before County submission.

**Source:** OBJ-003 (fully coordinated across disciplines), R-01 §3.3.2.

### 5. Septic Holding Tank — Design for Access and Maintenance
The site is off-grid; the 2,000 US gallon holding tank must be pumped out periodically. The preliminary design should confirm tank location, access hatch arrangement, and vehicle access for pump-out truck — coordinated with the civil grading and sitework design.

**Source:** R-01 §3.4, SOW-0043, R-06. The holding tank is shown at the north side exterior on R-06.

### Freeze Protection in Alberta Climate
The site is near Ferintosh, Alberta (approx. 30 km south of Camrose). All exterior and exposed plumbing — including the cistern filling connection, bulk water fill connection, and the mud sump connection — must be designed for Alberta winter conditions. Insulation, heat tracing, or building envelope integration strategies should be identified at the preliminary stage.

**Source:** Site location stated in R-01 §1.0 and Appendix C. Specific design requirements per NPC/ABC — **ASSUMPTION: likely applicable**; specific code edition TBD.

### Cistern Sizing Adequacy
The 50,000 L minimum is Owner-specified. The preliminary design should note whether this capacity is adequate for peak demand across all users (wash bay operations at 100 LPM pump, bulk water fill, pressure washer, washroom, industrial wash station, emergency shower) or whether demand analysis at detailed design may require upsizing. Capacity adequacy is not confirmed from available sources.

**Design direction for preliminary stage:** The preliminary design should proceed using the Owner's 50,000 L minimum as the design basis, while explicitly flagging that a demand analysis has not been performed. The preliminary package should include a recommendation that a demand analysis be performed at the detailed design stage (DEL-006-07 Plumbing Calculation Package) to verify adequacy for peak simultaneous demand. If demand analysis reveals that upsizing is required, this should be treated as a design development item, not a scope change, since the RFP states "minimum" capacity.

**Source:** R-01 §3.4 ("minimum 50,000L"), SOW-0041, SOW-0042. **ASSUMPTION:** 50,000 L is Owner-directed minimum; adequacy for peak simultaneous demand requires engineering analysis at detailed design. Proceeding on the Owner's minimum is a reasonable preliminary design position since the RFP explicitly uses "minimum."

> **Note (Lensing A-005):** This section previously raised the adequacy question but did not resolve the design direction for the preliminary stage. Updated to provide a recommended approach: accept the Owner minimum for preliminary design, flag the demand analysis gap, and recommend resolution at detailed design.

### Mezzanine and Plumbing Clearance
The mezzanine (above Parts Room, Utility Room, and Wash Bay) is load-bearing and intended to store oil totes and pumping equipment. The plumbing engineer should confirm that cistern venting, drain vent stacks, and any overhead plumbing routing do not conflict with the mezzanine structure or the 35' ceiling overhead crane envelope.

**Coordination action required:** This item should be tracked as a coordination requirement under REQ-017. Specifically, the following clearance checks are needed at the preliminary stage:
1. Cistern vent stack routing vs. mezzanine floor framing -- confirm the vent can be routed without penetrating or conflicting with the mezzanine structure.
2. Drain vent stack routing vs. mezzanine and crane envelope -- confirm vent stacks do not encroach on the 35 ft crane operating envelope.
3. Overhead plumbing distribution routing (if any runs above the mezzanine) vs. mezzanine access and crane clearance.

These checks should be documented in the interdisciplinary coordination record (REQ-017 verification evidence).

**Source:** R-01 §3.4, R-06, SOW-0032, SOW-0033.

> **Note (Lensing E-001):** This consideration was raised but not actionably tracked. Updated to identify specific clearance checks and link to REQ-017 coordination requirement for verification tracking.
