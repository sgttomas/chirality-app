# Procedure — DEL-006-05 Septic System Details

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce the Septic System Details drawing set (DEL-006-05) for the New North Shop Addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill. The drawing set is a Plumbing Engineer deliverable and must be issued as IFC documents signed and stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta. (Source: RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0018)

---

## Steps

### Step 1 — Confirm Scope and Existing Conditions

1.1 Review RFP §3.4 and Decomposition SOW-0043 to confirm deliverable scope: supply and install 2,000 US gallon septic holding tank; remove existing septic tank. (Source: RFP §3.4; SOW-0043; Decomposition D-002)

1.2 Confirm with project team that the existing septic tank is to be removed (IN scope) and not relocated (OUT of scope per SOW-0205 and D-002). (Source: Decomposition D-002)

1.3 Locate the existing septic tank in the field during site investigation. Record its position, connection points, and condition. ASSUMPTION: existing tank location is not explicitly shown on conceptual drawings; field verification is required.

1.4 Confirm existing tank removal procedure requirements: the existing tank contents must be pumped out and disposed of in accordance with applicable Alberta environmental regulations prior to physical removal. **TBD: Identify the specific Alberta environmental regulation references governing existing tank content disposal. Include disposal procedure and any required documentation (e.g., waste manifest) in the drawing set removal notes.** ASSUMPTION: applicable environmental regulations govern disposal; specific regulation references TBD. (Source: Guidance — Existing Tank; Lensing A-004)

1.5 Review Appendix B (Plumbing) conceptual drawing to confirm the intended new tank location on the exterior north side of the New North Shop. (Source: App B — Plumbing)

1.6 Confirm the proposed tank location with the civil engineer (PKG-005) to ensure no conflict with site grading, drainage, or driving surfaces.

---

### Step 2 — Confirm Regulatory Requirements

2.1 Identify the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) for private sewage systems in Camrose County, Alberta. TBD — confirm with project permit coordinator (DEL-009 series).

2.2 Confirm the applicable Alberta Safety Codes edition and any local amendments. (Source: ASSUMPTION: applicable — specific editions TBD)

2.3 Confirm required setbacks from the holding tank to: the building, water supply systems (cistern — DEL-006-04), property lines, and driving surfaces.

2.4 Confirm whether a Safety Code permit for the private sewage system is required separately or is covered under the building permit (DEL-009-02). (Source: SOW-0007; ASSUMPTION: separate permit likely required)

2.5 Confirm with the AHJ whether a 2,000 US gallon holding tank is adequate for the fixture unit count expected from DEL-006-06. If not, escalate to project management for scope discussion.

---

### Step 3 — Produce Drawing Set

3.1 Prepare site plan sheet showing:
- New septic holding tank location with dimensions and setbacks from the building and other features
- Pump-out vehicle access route
- Existing septic tank location and removal notation
- North arrow, scale bar, and drawing reference block

3.2 Prepare tank plan and section detail sheets showing:
- Tank plan view with inlet and outlet pipe connections
- Tank section(s) showing internal configuration, inlet/outlet invert elevations, access riser(s), and lid(s)
- Pipe sizes, materials, and slopes from building to tank (coordinate with DEL-006-03)
- Bedding and backfill details (per geotechnical recommendations from DEL-008-01; mark TBD if geotech not yet available)

3.3 Prepare installation notes sheet including:
- Applicable Alberta Safety Codes references (TBD — confirm with AHJ)
- Tank material and manufacturer requirements (TBD — specify or performance specify)
- Backfill compaction requirements
- Testing requirements before backfill (e.g., water test)
- **HOLD POINT: Pre-backfill inspection.** Include a mandatory hold point on drawings requiring inspection of the installed tank (alignment, connections, water test) before backfill is permitted. This is standard practice for buried tank installations. The drawing note should specify who must inspect (Plumbing Engineer / Safety Codes Officer / both) and what evidence is required to release the hold. (Lensing X-002)
- Pump-out service access requirements. **Include measurable acceptance criteria:** TBD — specify minimum vehicle clearance width, turning radius, and/or access surface requirements for pump-out vehicle access (Lensing F-003).
- Environmental requirements for existing tank removal and content disposal (ASSUMPTION: applicable — specific requirements TBD; see Step 1.4)

3.4 Include cross-reference notes to:
- DEL-006-03 (Drain & Vent Plans) for building drain connection
- DEL-006-04 (Cistern & Pump Details) for water supply separation confirmation
- PKG-004 (Electrical) if alarm or level indicator is required (ASSUMPTION: TBD)
- PKG-005 (Civil) for grading and access coordination

3.5 Prepare or cross-reference tank entry in DEL-006-06 (Fixture Schedule / Equipment Schedule) showing: capacity, manufacturer (TBD), material (TBD), model (TBD).

---

### Step 4 — Internal Quality Review

4.1 Conduct internal drawing review against the specification requirements (REQ-001 through REQ-010 in Specification.md).

4.2 Verify that:
- Tank capacity is stated as minimum 2,000 US gallons (REQ-001) (Source: RFP §3.4)
- Existing tank removal is annotated (REQ-002) (Source: SOW-0043; D-002)
- Tank location with setbacks is shown (REQ-003)
- Installation notes reference applicable Alberta Safety Codes (REQ-005)
- Pump-out access route is shown (REQ-010)

4.3 Cross-check invert elevations, pipe slopes, and connection points against DEL-006-03 (Drain & Vent Plans).

4.4 Verify no conflicts with site grading plan (PKG-005).

---

### Step 5 — Preliminary Design Review (County Approval)

5.1 Issue preliminary septic design to County project manager for review and approval prior to finalizing IFC documents. (Source: RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0010f)

5.2 Record any County comments and incorporate into the IFC drawing set.

---

### Step 6 — IFC Issue

6.1 The Plumbing Engineer of record reviews and signs/stamps all drawings in the set. (Source: RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0018)

6.2 Issue the signed/stamped drawing set as Issued for Construction (IFC).

6.3 Submit IFC drawings to the AHJ as required for Safety Code permit application (DEL-009-03). (Source: SOW-0007)

6.4 Transmit the IFC drawing set to the project file and make available to the Plumbing Contractor (PKG-014) for construction of DEL-014-02 (Septic System installation).

---

### Step 7 — Construction Verification and Commissioning Handover (Lensing C-003)

> **Note:** This step occurs after construction, not at IFC issue. It is included here to establish the full lifecycle of the deliverable's use, supporting OBJ-004 (commission all plumbing systems to operational readiness). The commissioning process itself is governed by DEL-020-01.

7.1 After the Plumbing Contractor completes septic system installation (DEL-014-02), verify that the installed system matches the IFC drawing set:
- Tank location, orientation, and setbacks match site plan
- Pipe connections, slopes, and invert elevations match detail drawings
- Backfill and bedding installed per specification notes
- Access risers and lids installed and accessible

7.2 Confirm that the pre-backfill hold point inspection (Step 3.3) was completed and documented prior to backfill.

7.3 Confirm that the Safety Code inspection (REQ-005) has been completed or scheduled.

7.4 Hand over as-built verification records to the commissioning deliverable (DEL-020-01) for integration into the overall commissioning package.

ASSUMPTION: This step describes the Plumbing Engineer's role in construction verification; the full commissioning procedure is governed by DEL-020-01. (Source: Guidance — Purpose; OBJ-004)
