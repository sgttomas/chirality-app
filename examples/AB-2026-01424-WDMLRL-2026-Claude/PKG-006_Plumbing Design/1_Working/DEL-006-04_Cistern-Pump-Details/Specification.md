# Specification — DEL-006-04: Cistern & Pump Details

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-006-04 covers the detailed engineering drawing set for the cistern water storage system and associated pump systems for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. Specifically, this deliverable addresses:

- Cistern sizing, structural integration, and all associated plumbing connections
- Internal service water distribution pump (100 LPM minimum, 2.5" filling connection)
- Bulk water fill system and high-volume external fill pump
- Pressure-side distribution piping from cistern to building distribution (as required for coordination with DEL-006-02)
- Control system provisions for cistern level and pump operation
- Electrical interface information for pump power connections (by coordination with electrical discipline)
- Installation and maintenance access provisions

### What This Deliverable Does Not Cover

- Water distribution plans throughout the building (DEL-006-02)
- Drain and vent plans (DEL-006-03)
- Septic system details (DEL-006-05)
- Plumbing fixture schedule (DEL-006-06)
- Plumbing calculation package (DEL-006-07)
- Plumbing specification document (DEL-006-08)
- Electrical design for pump power circuits (PKG-004 / DEL-004)
- Structural design of cistern support or enclosure (PKG-002)
- Civil sitework for bulk fill point access (PKG-018)

## Requirements

### R-001 — Cistern Minimum Capacity
**Requirement:** The cistern shall have a minimum storage capacity of 50,000 litres (50,000 L).
**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Minimum 50,000 L Cistern complete with the necessary plumbing"); SOW-0041.
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-002 — Cistern Plumbing Completeness
**Requirement:** The cistern shall be supplied and installed complete with all necessary plumbing connections, including: inlets, outlets, overflow, drain, venting, access/man-way, and level sensor ports. [F-002: enumeration strengthened from illustrative ("including") to exhaustive list; level sensor ports and man-way access added as operationally necessary connections.]
**Source:** RFP §3.4; SOW-0041.
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-003 — Internal Service Distribution Pump Flow Rate
**Requirement:** The internal service water distribution pump shall be capable of a minimum flow rate of 100 litres per minute (100 LPM).
**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Pump capable of 100 LPM"); SOW-0042.
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-004 — Cistern Filling Connection Diameter
**Requirement:** The cistern filling connection serving the internal service distribution pump shall be 2.5 inches (2.5") in diameter.
**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("complete with a 2.5 inch cistern filling connection for internal service water distribution"); SOW-0042.
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-005 — Bulk Water Fill System
**Requirement:** A bulk water fill system shall be provided, including a high-volume pump for external filling of the cistern.
**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Bulk water fill. With high volume pump for external filling"); SOW-0044.
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-006 — Water Tap and Pressure Washer Reel Connections
**Requirement:** Water tap and pressure washer reel connections shall be provided as shown on the plumbing drawing.
**Source:** RFP App B-Plumb (conceptual drawing); SOW-0049.
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-007 — Code Compliance
**Requirement:** All plumbing design shall comply with applicable Alberta Safety Codes and all applicable Alberta building codes and regulations. IFC drawings shall be signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer licensed in the Province of Alberta.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; RFP §3.4; SOW-0008, SOW-0009, SOW-0018.
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-008 — Pump Selection Documentation
**Requirement:** The drawing set shall include a pump equipment schedule documenting, at minimum: manufacturer, model (or equivalent), flow rate (LPM), total dynamic head (TDH), motor power (kW or HP), voltage/phase, and connection size.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — consistent with standard plumbing engineering practice for pump design documentation; not explicitly itemized in RFP beyond performance parameters.
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION).

### R-009 — Cistern Location Coordination
**Requirement:** Cistern location and spatial coordination shall be consistent with the conceptual plumbing layout shown in Appendix B (Plumbing), which positions the cistern in or adjacent to the utility room area.
**Source:** App B-Plumb (conceptual drawing — "50,000 L Water Storage" noted adjacent to utility room / stairs to mezzanine).
**Priority:** Mandatory — location may be adjusted by Plumbing Engineer subject to structural and spatial constraints.

### R-010 — Electrical Interface Coordination
**Requirement:** The drawing set shall include electrical interface information (voltage, phase, amperage, connection point) for all pump motors, to enable coordination with the electrical discipline.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — required for design coordination; consistent with SOW-0016 (complete final plumbing design).
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION).

### R-011 — Maintenance Access
**Requirement:** The cistern and pump system design shall provide adequate maintenance access for inspection, cleaning, and equipment servicing.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — consistent with standard plumbing design practice and operational requirements of an industrial maintenance facility; not explicitly stated in RFP beyond general operational intent.
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION).

### R-012 — Cistern Overflow Protection [F-001]
**Requirement:** The cistern shall be provided with overflow protection comprising: (a) an overflow outlet sized to handle the maximum bulk fill inflow rate, (b) a defined drainage path from the overflow outlet to an appropriate discharge point coordinated with civil/drainage design, and (c) overfill prevention measures during bulk water delivery operations.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — derived from Guidance.md §Considerations > Overflow and Drain Provisions ("overflow into the building... would be a design deficiency"); R-002 mentions overflow generally but does not establish capacity, discharge location, or coordination requirements. Plumbing code requirements for overflow are assumed applicable (code edition TBD per A-001). [F-001]
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION).

### R-013 — Freeze Protection [C-002]
**Requirement:** The cistern, all associated piping, and the bulk fill connection shall be protected against freezing in Alberta climate conditions. Freeze protection measures (heat tracing, insulation, burial below frost line, or location in heated spaces) shall be documented on the drawing set.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — Guidance.md §Considerations > Freeze Protection identifies this as "a significant design consideration that is not addressed in the RFP source documents." For a rural Alberta site (SW 14-44-21-W4), freeze protection is a necessary obligation. Not explicitly required in RFP. [C-002]
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION).

### R-014 — Pressure Tank Inclusion Decision [E-002]
**Requirement:** The design shall include a documented decision (include or exclude) for a pressure tank / hydropneumatic tank as part of the distribution pump system. If included, the pressure tank shall be shown on the drawing set with capacity, pre-charge pressure, and connection details. If excluded, the alternative pressurization approach (e.g., VFD) shall be documented.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — Datasheet lists pressure tank as TBD; Guidance §Principles > System Pressurization identifies this as a likely design decision; no current requirement governs the inclusion/exclusion decision. [E-002]
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION).
