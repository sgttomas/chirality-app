# Procedure — DEL-007-04 Landscape Specification

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps required to produce, review, coordinate, and finalize the Landscape Specification (DEL-007-04) for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project (AB-2026-01424).

The Landscape Specification is the document through which the Landscape Architect establishes material standards, workmanship requirements, quality criteria, and performance parameters for all landscape installation, hardscape, planting, and site restoration work. This procedure covers the production workflow — not the field construction workflow.
_Source: _CONTEXT.md; R-01 §1.0 (SOW-0017)_

## Steps

### Step 1 — Project Familiarization

1.1 Read the RFP (R-01), Addendum 1 (R-02), and Appendix B conceptual drawings.
1.2 Read the project decomposition (WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md), focusing on PKG-007 scope, SOW-0017, and SOW-0076.
1.3 Read DEL-007-04 context files: `_CONTEXT.md`, `_REFERENCES.md`, `_DEPENDENCIES.md`.
1.4 Confirm the upstream deliverable status (DEL-007-01, DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03) — if not approved, document TBD dependencies in the specification.
_Source: _CONTEXT.md; R-01_

### Step 2 — Site and Discipline Coordination

2.1 Review the approved Landscape Site Plans (DEL-007-02) to extract: site boundary, pad locations and dimensions, driving surface extents, planting zones, and restoration areas.
2.2 Review the approved Planting & Restoration Plans (DEL-007-03) to extract: species list, planting sizes and spacing, soil preparation requirements, mulching and irrigation standards (if applicable), and establishment period.
2.3 Coordinate with PKG-005 Civil Design to obtain: site grading plan, drainage plan, finished grade elevations for landscape areas, swale locations.
2.4 Coordinate with PKG-006 Plumbing Design to obtain: wash bay drainage interface details and mud sump design parameters.
2.5 Review geotechnical report (DEL-008-01) for: subsoil conditions, bearing capacity (for pad design), any contamination or special conditions affecting planting.
_Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md; Decomp PKG-007_

### Step 3 — Establish Specification Framework

3.1 Organize the specification using CSI MasterFormat Division 32 (Exterior Improvements) or equivalent framework. ASSUMPTION: standard industry practice; confirm with project team preferred format.
3.2 Identify all landscape specification sections required:
   - Site preparation (topsoil stripping, subgrade preparation)
   - Gravel driving surface
   - Cement pads
   - Gravel pad
   - Site drainage / grading integration
   - Exterior mud sump (coordinate with plumbing/civil)
   - Planting (TBD pending DEL-007-03)
   - Site restoration (TBD pending DEL-007-03)
   - Warranty and maintenance
3.3 Cross-reference each section to the relevant RFP requirement, SOW item, and upstream deliverable.
_Source: R-01 §3.3.2, §3.4; Decomp PKG-007_

### Step 4 — Draft Specification Sections

4.1 For each section identified in Step 3:
   - State the scope of work covered
   - Define materials (type, grade, standard, source constraints -- noting County/Landfill aggregate (gravel) supply)
   - Define workmanship standards (installation method, tolerances, compaction requirements)
   - Define quality control requirements (testing, inspection hold points -- see hold point list below)
   - Define performance criteria (drainage slopes, surface load capacity for motor scraper class equipment)

**Inspection Hold Points** (TBD -- to be enumerated by Landscape Architect / Construction Manager when specification is finalized):
   - HP-1: Subgrade preparation and compaction testing for gravel driving surface areas (prior to aggregate placement)
   - HP-2: Cement pad subbase and formwork inspection (prior to concrete placement)
   - HP-3: Concrete placement and finishing for cement pads (during pour; strength verification after cure)
   - HP-4: Gravel pad subgrade and compaction testing (prior to gravel placement)
   - HP-5: Planting material delivery inspection (at delivery; TBD pending DEL-007-03)
   - HP-6: Final grading and drainage conformance inspection (prior to landscape acceptance)
   Note: Specific testing methods and acceptance criteria for each hold point are TBD. Resolution source: Landscape Architect / Construction Manager.
_Lensing note [E-003]: Procedure Step 4.1 requires "inspection hold points" but none were enumerated; this provides an initial framework._
4.2 Use TBD wherever information is not available from accessible sources. Do not invent values.
4.3 Label inferred requirements as ASSUMPTION.
4.4 Cite the RFP section, appendix, or other source for each non-trivial requirement.
_Source: R-01 §3.3.2, §3.4; App B (Shop)_

### Step 5 — Address Specific RFP Requirements

5.1 Confirm gravel driving surface specification addresses:
   - Subgrade preparation (topsoil stripped, subgrade compacted to TBD standard)
   - Gravel depth and gradation (TBD — coordinate with County/Landfill supply specs)
   - Load capacity for motor scraper class equipment
   - Drainage slope (coordinate with civil grading)
   _Source: R-01 §3.3.2_

5.2 Confirm cement pad specification addresses:
   - Dimensions (from DEL-007-02 or App B)
   - Concrete mix design and strength (TBD — coordinate with structural/civil)
   - Surface finish (TBD)
   - Contractor to supply cement; prep scope included
   _Source: R-01 App B (Shop)_

5.3 Confirm exterior mud sump specification addresses:
   - Location (east side of wash bay, per App B)
   - Dimensions sufficient for excavator cleanout
   - Material, drainage connection, overflow provisions
   - Coordinate with PKG-006 plumbing for connection design
   _Source: R-01 §3.4; App B_

5.4 Confirm site drainage requirements addressed:
   - Positive drainage from all landscape areas
   - No drainage impact to neighboring properties
   - Swale and conveyance details coordinated with PKG-005
   _Source: R-01 §3.4_

5.5 Confirm warranty provisions addressed:
   - 2-year post-CCC warranty for all landscape materials and workmanship
   - Defect rectification within 10 days of Owner written notice
   _Source: R-01 §2.10_

### Step 6 — Internal Review and Cross-Discipline Coordination

6.1 Circulate draft specification to:
   - Civil Engineer (PKG-005) — confirm grading and drainage interface
   - Plumbing Engineer/Contractor (PKG-006) — confirm mud sump interface
   - Structural Engineer (PKG-002) — confirm cement pad structural requirements
   - Project Manager — confirm scope boundary alignment with County responsibilities
6.2 Resolve comments and update TBD entries where information is now available.
6.3 Document unresolved conflicts in the Conflict Table in Guidance.md.
_Source: _CONTEXT.md; Decomp PKG-007 (interface: civil grading, PKG-018)_

### Step 7 — County Review and Approval Integration

7.1 Confirm County approval of Preliminary Landscape Design (DEL-007-01) is on file before issuing the specification for construction.
7.2 Incorporate any County-directed changes from the preliminary design approval.
7.3 Ensure the final specification is consistent with all approved upstream landscape deliverables (DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03).
_Source: R-01 §3.3.2_

### Step 8 — Issued for Construction (IFC) Package Preparation

8.1 Prepare the specification for inclusion in the IFC package.
8.2 Confirm P.Eng. stamping is applied to all engineering drawings that accompany this specification (grading plan interface, pad design, etc.), as required under SOW-0018.
8.3 Submit for building permit and applicable Safety Code permits.
8.4 Provide copies of all completed inspection reports to the County per RFP §3.3.2.
_Source: R-01 §3.3.2; SOW-0018_
