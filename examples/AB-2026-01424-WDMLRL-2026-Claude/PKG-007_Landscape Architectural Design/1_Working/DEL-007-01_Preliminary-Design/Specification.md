# Specification — DEL-007-01 Preliminary Landscape Design

---

## Scope

### In Scope
This specification governs the production and delivery of DEL-007-01, the Preliminary Landscape Design presentation for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill (WDMLRL) Maintenance Shop Addition project.

The deliverable covers:
- Preliminary landscape architectural design for the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition expansion area (SW 14-44-21-W4, near Ferintosh, Alberta).
- Design concept, principles, and overall site landscape strategy for County approval.
- Coordination of landscape design interface with civil grading (PKG-005) and sitework (PKG-018).
- Landscape approach for the expansion area, including areas adjacent to the new ~13,000 sq ft shop addition and any associated site areas within the expansion area shown in Appendix C.

> **Terminology note:** "Expansion area" is the canonical term for the geographic scope of landscape treatment. See Guidance.md "Canonical Terminology" for definition. Source terms "proposed lot" (R-01, SOW-0076), "expansion footprint," and "site area" are treated as synonyms. (Enrichment ref: F-004, X-001)

Source: R-01 §3.3.2 (SOW-0010g); Decomposition §6 PKG-007, §7 DEL-007-01.

### Out of Scope
- Civil grading design and calculations (PKG-005).
- Final Landscape Site Plans (DEL-007-02).
- Planting and Restoration Plans in detail (DEL-007-03).
- Landscape Specification document (DEL-007-04).
- Construction execution of grading and landscaping (PKG-018, SOW-0076).
- Brushing and clearing (County responsibility, SOW-0200).
- Bulk cut and fill (County responsibility, SOW-0201).
- Aggregate/gravel supply (County/Landfill responsibility, SOW-0203).

### REQ-007-01-001: County Approval Gate
The Preliminary Landscape Design shall be delivered to the Camrose County project team for review and approval before proceeding to final landscape design (DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03, DEL-007-04).

**Source:** R-01, §3.3.2 ("Deliver Preliminary Design for the County project team to approve."); SOW-0010g.

---

### REQ-007-01-002: Positive Site Drainage Integration
The landscape design approach shall account for positive site drainage and accommodate future storm events. The design shall not alter the drainage conditions of neighbouring properties.

**Source:** R-01, §3.4 (SOW-0020, SOW-0021).

---

### REQ-007-01-003: Driving Surface Design Compatibility
The landscape design shall be compatible with a final gravel driving surface designed to accommodate the weight and turning radius of large construction equipment (motor scraper class).

**Source:** R-01, §3.3.2 ("The final driving surface shall be gravel and designed to accommodate the weight and turning radius of large construction equipment, such as a motor scraper."); SOW-0077.

---

### REQ-007-01-004: Topsoil Stripping Acknowledgement
The preliminary design shall acknowledge the requirement to strip topsoil from all driving surfaces (unless already performed by Owner). This constraint shall be reflected in the landscape approach.

**Normative status clarification:** The Datasheet records topsoil stripping as "assumed required per Decomposition D-010." The RFP states "Strip topsoil from all driving surfaces (if not already performed by Owner)" (R-01, §3.3.2). D-010 resolves this as assumed required. This requirement treats topsoil stripping as a confirmed design constraint per D-010, but the conditional ("if not already performed by Owner") means applicability depends on whether the Owner has already completed this activity. The Landscape Architect should confirm the current status of topsoil stripping at the site before or during the preliminary approval meeting.

**Source:** R-01, §3.3.2 ("Strip topsoil from all driving surfaces (if not already performed by Owner)"); SOW-0075; Decomposition D-010. Enrichment ref: A-002.

---

### REQ-007-01-005: Interface with Civil Grading
The preliminary landscape design shall define the interface boundary with PKG-005 Civil Design (site grading, drainage features, driving surface layout). Landscape planting and restoration scope is PKG-007; civil grading and drainage are PKG-005.

**Source:** Decomposition §6 PKG-007 ("Landscape design, planting/restoration plans, integration with civil grading. Exclusions: Civil grading (PKG-005), construction execution (PKG-018).").

---

### REQ-007-01-006: Alignment with Alberta Codes and Regulations
The landscape design shall be consistent with the requirement that the building design adheres to all applicable Alberta building codes and regulations and all Alberta Safety Codes.

**Preliminary-stage professional obligation:** At the preliminary design stage, the Landscape Architect's professional obligation under this requirement is to ensure that no elements of the proposed landscape design concept are known to contravene applicable Alberta codes. This is a due-diligence screening, not a formal code compliance certification (which occurs at the IFC stage through PKG-009). The Landscape Architect should document any code-related considerations or potential conflicts identified during preliminary design development.

**Source:** R-01, §3.3.2 (SOW-0008, SOW-0009). Note: specific Alberta landscape/site codes applicable to this deliverable are not enumerated in the RFP; see Standards section. Enrichment ref: X-002.

---

### REQ-007-01-007: Supports OBJ-001 and OBJ-003
The preliminary design shall contribute to:
- OBJ-001: A code-compliant, fully functional facility that satisfies the County's operational program.
- OBJ-003: Complete, P.Eng.-stamped IFC design documentation across all disciplines before construction begins. (Note: IFC stamping obligation is on final drawings; preliminary design is a precursor gate.)

**Source:** Decomposition §5, §8 (SOW-0010g mapped to OBJ-001, OBJ-003).
