# Procedure — DEL-007-01 Preliminary Landscape Design

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps required to produce, review, coordinate, and obtain County approval for the Preliminary Landscape Design (DEL-007-01) for the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition project. Completion of this procedure and receipt of County approval is the gate condition for commencing final landscape design (DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03, DEL-007-04).

Source: R-01, §3.3.2 (SOW-0010g); Decomposition §7 PKG-007.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Gather and Review Source Information

1.1 Read and extract relevant requirements from R-01 (RFP §1.0, §3.1, §3.3.2, §3.4) pertaining to landscape and site design.

1.2 Review Appendix C (Site Maps, R-07) to understand the expansion area footprint, relationship to the existing Old North Shop, and surrounding landfill site context.

1.3 Review the project decomposition (WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md) for:
- PKG-007 scope and exclusions.
- DEL-007-01 through DEL-007-04 structure.
- Interface with PKG-005 (Civil Design) and PKG-018 (Sitework).
- County-performed scope (brushing/clearing, bulk cut/fill, aggregate supply) that is OUT of Proponent scope.

1.4 Record any information gaps (site-specific soil data, plant community data, utility routing, etc.) as TBD items to be resolved before or during final design. Cross-reference with Datasheet Data Gaps register (DG-001 through DG-006).

---

### Step 2 — Establish Design Interface with PKG-005 Civil Design

2.1 Contact the PKG-005 Civil Engineer to obtain:
- Preliminary site grading intent and slope targets.
- Drainage strategy (positive drainage, storm management approach).
- Driving surface layout and setbacks.
- Topsoil stripping extents and any topsoil stockpile/reuse plan.

2.2 Define and document the interface boundary between landscape architectural scope (PKG-007) and civil grading scope (PKG-005). This boundary must be explicit in the preliminary design presentation to avoid scope gaps or overlaps.

2.3 If PKG-005 civil information is not yet available, document the interface as TBD and note the dependency clearly in the preliminary design presentation.

Source: Decomposition §6 PKG-007 (exclusions: civil grading, construction execution); Specification REQ-007-01-005.

---

### Step 3 — Develop Preliminary Landscape Design Concept

3.1 Develop the overall landscape design concept for the expansion area, including:
- Site context: how the new shop addition relates to existing site elements, access routes, and the active landfill operations area.
- Landscape strategy: approach to treatment of disturbed areas, revegetation or planting zones, and compatibility with gravel driving surfaces.
- Drainage compatibility: demonstration that the landscape approach supports positive site drainage and does not alter drainage to neighbouring properties.
- Topsoil: acknowledgement of topsoil stripping requirement from driving surfaces; indicate whether stripped topsoil is proposed for reuse in restoration areas (TBD pending civil coordination).

3.2 Prepare the preliminary design presentation materials. The following minimum content elements are required (per Specification Acceptance Criteria AC-001):

| Element | Description | Required? |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| (a) Site context plan | Illustration locating the expansion area within the WDMLRL facility | Yes |
| (b) Design concept narrative | Description of landscape design approach, principles, and site strategy | Yes |
| (c) Interface diagram with PKG-005 civil | Explicit documentation of the PKG-007/PKG-005 scope boundary | Yes |
| (d) Indicative restoration/planting zones | High-level indication of planting/restoration intent and extent | Yes |
| (e) Key design constraints and assumptions | List of constraints from RFP, codes, and site conditions; all assumptions flagged | Yes |

Additional elements may be included at the Landscape Architect's discretion (e.g., design principles statement, coordination notes, preliminary plant concept). ASSUMPTION: the above minimum content list represents typical elements for a preliminary landscape presentation for an industrial/utility facility in Alberta.

Source: R-01, §3.3.2 (SOW-0010g, SOW-0076); Specification REQ-007-01-001 through REQ-007-01-006; Specification Acceptance Criteria; Specification Documentation table. Enrichment ref: A-004.

3.3 Label all inferred or assumed content as ASSUMPTION. Do not assert requirements or specifications without source support.

Source: R-01, §3.3.2 (SOW-0010g, SOW-0076); Specification REQ-007-01-001 through REQ-007-01-006.

---

### Step 4 — Internal Review and Coordination

4.1 Conduct internal design team review of the preliminary landscape design for:
- Consistency with RFP requirements (REQ-007-01-001 through REQ-007-01-007).
- Alignment with civil interface (Step 2 outputs).
- No conflicts with County-performed scope (brushing/clearing, bulk cut/fill, aggregate).
- Compatibility with driving surface, drainage, and topsoil requirements.

4.2 Apply the quality evaluation framework from Guidance.md (Preliminary Design Success Criteria) to assess design quality beyond rule compliance:
- Fitness for purpose
- Proportionality of detail
- Coherence of design concept
- Defensibility of design decisions
- Downstream viability

4.3 Confirm that the preliminary design is consistent with other preliminary design deliverables (e.g., DEL-001-01 Preliminary Architectural Design, DEL-005-01 Preliminary Civil Design) at the interface level.

4.4 Resolve any internal coordination issues before submission. If issues cannot be resolved from available information, document as TBD or Conflict (refer to Guidance.md Conflict Table CONF-007-01-001 for the scope-extent conflict). Consult the Escalation Criteria in Guidance.md to determine whether to proceed with a documented assumption or halt and seek input.

Source: Specification cross-reference; Guidance Principles 1-4; Guidance Escalation Criteria; Guidance Preliminary Design Success Criteria.

### Step 5 — Submit to Camrose County for Approval

5.1 Prepare the preliminary design presentation in the format and submission channel required by the County project manager (TBD at project award per R-01, §3.1). **TBD (F-002):** County PM identity and submission channel/format requirements are not yet confirmed.

5.2 Submit DEL-007-01 to the County project manager for review and approval.

5.3 Coordinate weekly project meetings with the County project manager as required (SOW-0086).

5.4 Respond to any County review comments or requests for clarification within a reasonable timeframe consistent with the project schedule and the December 31, 2026 completion deadline.

Source: R-01, §3.1 (SOW-0086); R-01, §3.3.2 (SOW-0010g approval gate); REQ-007-01-001.

---

### Step 6 — Obtain and Record County Approval

6.1 Obtain documented approval of the preliminary landscape design from the County project team. **TBD (E-002):** Confirm required form of approval evidence (e.g., signed letter, email confirmation, meeting minutes, or other format acceptable to County). Record the specific form of approval received.

6.2 Record the approval (date, approving party, form of approval, any conditions) in the project records.

6.3 If County requests revisions, incorporate feedback, re-review internally (Step 4, including Step 4A completeness check), and resubmit (Step 5). Document revision cycles.

6.4 Upon County approval, confirm that final design work (DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03, DEL-007-04) may commence.

Source: R-01, §3.3.2 ("Deliver Preliminary Design for the County project team to approve."); REQ-007-01-001.
