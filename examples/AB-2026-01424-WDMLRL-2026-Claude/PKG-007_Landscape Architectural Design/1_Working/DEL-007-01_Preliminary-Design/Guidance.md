# Guidance — DEL-007-01 Preliminary Landscape Design

---

## Purpose

DEL-007-01 exists to present a preliminary landscape architectural design concept for the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition expansion area to Camrose County for approval. This approval is a gate before the Landscape Architect proceeds to final design documents (DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03, DEL-007-04).

The deliverable initiates the landscape design work stream within PKG-007. It establishes design vision, principles, and the interface framework between landscape architectural scope and civil grading scope (PKG-005), which must be coordinated from the outset. An approved preliminary design ensures the County's expectations are aligned with the design approach before significant design effort is invested in final drawings and specifications.

Source: R-01, §3.3.2 (SOW-0010g); _CONTEXT.md ("Preliminary landscape design phase presenting conceptual approach, design principles, and overall site strategy"); Decomposition §5 OBJ-003.

## Principles

**1. Approval-gate first.** The primary function of this deliverable is to obtain County approval. Content should be clear and sufficient for County decision-makers to evaluate and approve the design direction — not so detailed as to foreclose final design flexibility, and not so vague as to prevent meaningful review.
Source: R-01, §3.3.2.

**2. Operational context drives design.** The WDMLRL site is an active regional landfill with heavy equipment operations (motor scraper class and similar). Landscape design must be compatible with operational realities: gravel driving surfaces, large turning radii, heavy vehicle loads, and ongoing site activities.
Source: R-01, §3.3.2 ("The final driving surface shall be gravel and designed to accommodate the weight and turning radius of large construction equipment, such as a motor scraper."); R-07 (Appendix C, site aerial showing active landfill context).

**3. Drainage integrity is non-negotiable.** Landscape design must not impede positive site drainage or alter drainage conditions on neighbouring properties. This constraint flows directly from the design requirements and must be addressed at the preliminary stage to avoid costly redesign later.
Source: R-01, §3.4 (SOW-0020, SOW-0021).

**4. PKG-005 is the civil grading authority.** Landscape planting, restoration, and landscape-specific site elements are PKG-007 scope. Site grading, drainage features, and driving surface layout are PKG-005 scope. The preliminary design should define this interface clearly and avoid designing over civil scope without coordination.
Source: Decomposition §6 PKG-007 ("Exclusions: Civil grading (PKG-005), construction execution (PKG-018).").

**5. County-owned scope boundaries.** Brushing/clearing and bulk cut/fill are County responsibilities and are excluded from the Proponent's landscape scope. The preliminary design should not assume these are Proponent-performed activities.
Source: R-01, §3.3.1 (SOW-0200, SOW-0201); Decomposition §4.

## Preliminary Design Success Criteria

Beyond the binary County approval gate, the following criteria provide a framework for evaluating the quality and sufficiency of the preliminary design. These are proposed internal benchmarks to guide the Landscape Architect and design team.

### Sufficiency Criteria

| Criterion | Description | Source / Basis |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Clarity of design intent** | The design concept is communicated clearly enough for non-specialist County decision-makers to evaluate the direction. | Principle 1 (Approval-gate first); R-01, §3.3.2 |
| **Requirement coverage** | All seven Specification requirements (REQ-007-01-001 through REQ-007-01-007) are addressed or explicitly deferred with rationale. | Specification.md Requirements |
| **Interface definition** | The PKG-005/PKG-007 scope boundary is explicitly documented, not implied. | Principle 4; REQ-007-01-005 |
| **Constraint acknowledgement** | Operational constraints (drainage, driving surface, topsoil, County-owned scope) are acknowledged in the design. | Principles 2, 3, 5; REQ-007-01-002 through REQ-007-01-004 |
| **Data gap transparency** | All assumptions and TBD items are documented, not asserted as facts. | Non-negotiable invariant (source-anchored with explicit assumptions) |
| **Cross-deliverable alignment** | Preliminary landscape design is consistent with peer preliminary deliverables (DEL-001-01, DEL-005-01) at interface points. | Procedure Step 4.3 |

### Quality Evaluation Framework

The following questions provide a qualitative rubric for internal review (Procedure Step 4). They supplement the checklist-style verification and address design quality rather than rule compliance alone.

1. **Fitness for purpose:** Does the design concept address the site's functional requirements (heavy equipment operations, drainage, access) while also meeting restoration/planting objectives?
2. **Proportionality:** Is the level of detail appropriate for a preliminary presentation — sufficient for County evaluation but not so detailed as to constrain final design?
3. **Coherence:** Do the design elements work together as an integrated site concept, or are they isolated responses to individual requirements?
4. **Defensibility:** Can every significant design decision be traced to a source requirement, site condition, or documented assumption?
5. **Downstream viability:** Does the preliminary concept provide a viable framework for detailed design development (DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03, DEL-007-04)?

Source: ASSUMPTION (best-effort quality framework). These criteria are not stated in the RFP or decomposition but are inferred from standard landscape architectural practice and the nature of a preliminary design gate. Enrichment ref: A-005, D-003.

## Requirement Priority Rationale

The Specification establishes a proposed priority ordering for requirements REQ-007-01-001 through REQ-007-01-007. The rationale for that ordering is as follows:

1. **County Approval Gate (REQ-007-01-001) is highest priority** because it is the mandatory contractual gate without which no downstream work proceeds. All other requirements exist in service of producing a design that passes this gate.

2. **Code Alignment (REQ-007-01-006) ranks second** because regulatory compliance is a non-negotiable obligation that constrains all design decisions. A design that violates codes cannot be approved regardless of other merits.

3. **Drainage (REQ-007-01-002) ranks third** because the RFP states it shall not alter neighbouring property drainage — this is both a design constraint and a potential liability issue with external parties.

4. **Driving Surface (REQ-007-01-003) ranks fourth** because it is an explicit functional requirement reflecting the site's primary operational purpose.

5. **Topsoil Stripping (REQ-007-01-004) ranks fifth** because, while it is an explicit construction requirement, its normative status has a conditional element (owner may have already performed it) that creates flexibility.

6. **Interface (REQ-007-01-005) and Objectives (REQ-007-01-007) rank lowest** because they are coordination and traceability requirements respectively — important for design coherence but not standalone compliance items.

**ASSUMPTION (best-effort ordering):** This priority ordering is inferred from the nature and source authority of each requirement, not explicitly stated in the RFP or decomposition. If requirements conflict during design, the Landscape Architect should consult this ordering as guidance and escalate unresolvable conflicts. Enrichment ref: C-001.

## Escalation Criteria

The documents for DEL-007-01 contain numerous TBD items and ASSUMPTION flags (see Datasheet TBD/Assumption Register). The following guidance helps the Landscape Architect discriminate between assumptions that are safe to proceed with and those that require resolution before work continues.

### Proceed with documented assumption when:
- The assumption is conservative (i.e., proceeding with the assumption does not foreclose options or create rework risk).
- The assumption relates to a detail that will be resolved at the final design stage (DEL-007-02 through DEL-007-04) with no impact on the preliminary concept direction.
- The assumption is consistent with standard industry practice and does not create liability exposure.
- The assumption is explicitly documented and flagged for later confirmation.

### Halt and seek input when:
- The assumption affects the scope boundary or extent of work (e.g., CONF-007-01-001 — landscape treatment extent). Proceeding with an incorrect scope assumption could lead to significant rework.
- The assumption relates to regulatory compliance (e.g., specific Alberta codes). Proceeding without confirming applicable codes could result in a non-compliant design direction.
- The assumption contradicts information from another discipline or deliverable. Cross-discipline conflicts should be resolved before embedding them in the preliminary concept.
- The assumption involves County expectations that, if incorrect, would cause the approval gate to fail.

### Escalation path:
1. Document the assumption and the uncertainty in the relevant production document.
2. Raise the item at the next project coordination meeting or directly with the project manager.
3. If the item is blocking and the coordination meeting is not imminent, contact the County PM (when identified) or the PKG-005 Civil Engineer (for interface items) directly.

Source: ASSUMPTION (best-effort escalation framework based on the nature of identified TBD/ASSUMPTION items). Not stated in sources. Enrichment ref: B-003.

## Considerations

**Site character and context.** The expansion area is situated to the east of the existing Old North Shop on a County-owned landfill site near Ferintosh, Alberta (SW 14-44-21-W4). The site aerial (Appendix C) shows the expansion area delineated within an active working yard. The site is in Alberta's central parkland region. Specific vegetation community, soil conditions, and microclimate data are not available in the RFP documents; these will depend on geotechnical and site investigation findings (DEL-008-01, DEL-008-02).

**Grading and topsoil.** Topsoil is to be stripped from all driving surfaces (Decomposition SOW-0075; D-010 notes this is assumed required). The preliminary design should address where stripped topsoil may be managed or reused on-site as part of landscape restoration areas, though the RFP does not specify this explicitly. ASSUMPTION: topsoil salvage and reuse approach is a reasonable design consideration for landscape restoration areas.

**Planting and restoration intent.** DEL-007-03 (Planting & Restoration Plans) is a downstream deliverable. The preliminary design should indicate the overall intent and extent of any planting or restoration areas without committing to final plant species, quantities, or specifications. The extent of restoration is not specified in the RFP beyond the general requirement to "grade and landscape the proposed lot" (SOW-0076, R-01, §3.3.2). ASSUMPTION: restoration scope is limited to the expansion area; extents beyond that are TBD.

**Schedule constraint.** All Work must be completed on or before December 31, 2026 (R-01, §3.1). The preliminary design should be submitted and approved early enough to allow final design, permit, and construction within this hard deadline. A realistic design schedule milestone for DEL-007-01 approval is TBD (to be confirmed by the project schedule, which is not yet established at this deliverable stage).

**Coordination with Natural Gas, Electrical, and Communication Tie-Ins.** Utility corridors (SOW-0080-0082) pass through or adjacent to the site. Landscape design should avoid conflicts with planned utility routes. ASSUMPTION: utility routing will be confirmed through coordination with PKG-005 and PKG-018.

## Trade-offs

| Trade-off | Option A | Option B | Considerations |
|-----------|----------|----------|---------------|
| Depth of preliminary presentation | High-level concept only (faster to produce, easier for County to redirect) | Detailed schematic with planting intent and layout (more information, but more effort before approval) | For an industrial facility with functional constraints, a clear concept with design principles and interface diagram is likely sufficient. Excessive detail at preliminary stage risks rework if County redirects. ASSUMPTION: County's primary interest is design direction and site coverage, not plant species at this stage. |
| Planting extent | Minimal (functional revegetation of disturbed areas only) | Enhanced landscape planting beyond revegetation | RFP does not specify aesthetic landscape goals; functional restoration compatible with landfill operations is most defensible. Any enhancements should be flagged as value-add and not assumed IN scope. |
| Interface documentation with civil | Informal (notes in presentation) | Formal interface diagram | Formal interface documentation reduces later coordination risk and is recommended given that PKG-005 and PKG-007 share a boundary. |
