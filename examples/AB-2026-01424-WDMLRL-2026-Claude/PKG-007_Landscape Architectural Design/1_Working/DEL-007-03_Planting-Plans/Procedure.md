# Procedure — DEL-007-03 Planting & Restoration Plans

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce the Planting & Restoration Plans drawing set (DEL-007-03) for the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition project. The output is a stamped IFC drawing set ready for use by the construction/installation team and referenced by the Landscape Specification (DEL-007-04).

Source: _CONTEXT.md; DECOMP §7 PKG-007; R-01 §1.0, §3.3.2.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Site Context and Ecological Assessment

1.1 Review the Appendix C aerial imagery (R-07) to understand the current site conditions: extent of disturbed areas, existing vegetation (tree line to north), and expansion area boundaries.

1.2 Conduct or commission a site ecological assessment to characterize:
- Existing soil conditions in and around the expansion area.
- Native plant communities present on or adjacent to the site (including the northern tree line).
- Invasive species presence (if any).
- Landfill-specific constraints on planting (e.g., proximity to waste cells, leachate/gas migration zones).

> Note: The RFP does not explicitly require a formal ecological assessment; this step is ASSUMPTION based on standard landscape practice. Confirm scope with project team.

1.3 Obtain DEL-007-02 approved Landscape Site Plans and PKG-005 civil grading drawings as the spatial base for plan development.

### Step 2 — Plant Palette Development

2.1 Develop a plant palette appropriate to:
- Central Alberta climate (hardiness zone — confirm: likely Canadian zone 3b–4a for the Ferintosh area; ASSUMPTION).
- Disturbed site conditions (landfill compound — compacted soils, dust, heavy equipment environment).
- Native and naturalized species preferences for restoration areas.
- Maintenance constraints (low-maintenance species appropriate for an operational landfill facility).

2.2 Organize the palette by zone/application:
- Restoration/revegetation zones (disturbed areas outside traffic surfaces).
- Interface/buffer zone adjacent to northern tree line.
- Foundation/building perimeter planting (if applicable).
- Seed mix specifications for broad area stabilization.

2.3 Document species using standard botanical nomenclature (genus, species, cultivar where applicable).

> Species selection is TBD — specific native plant species list to be determined by Landscape Architect with reference to applicable Alberta native plant guidelines.

### Step 3 — Restoration Strategy and Phasing Plan

3.1 Define restoration zones on the plan drawing, keyed to the plant palette developed in Step 2.

3.2 Develop a phasing plan that sequences planting and restoration activities relative to:
- Construction completion milestones (all Work by December 31, 2026 per R-01 §3.1).
- Alberta growing season windows (ASSUMPTION: planting typically late May–mid September).
- Practical sequencing (initial soil stabilization → seeding → containerized planting where appropriate).

3.3 Address the CONF-007-03-002 conflict (restoration phasing vs. December 31, 2026 deadline) with the County before finalizing phasing plan — confirm whether spring 2027 planting window is acceptable. See Guidance.md Conflict Table.

3.4 Document the restoration methodology including:
- Soil preparation requirements (amendment, loosening, import if needed).
- Seeding method (broadcast, hydroseeding, drill seeding) for each zone.
- Planting method and spacing for containerized/bare-root species.
- Initial establishment care requirements.

### Step 4 — Drawing Set Production

> **Drawing standards reference:** All drawings shall comply with project-level drawing standards (title block format, scale conventions, layer naming, file format). TBD — project team to provide or reference applicable project drawing standards and CAD standards. If no project-level standards exist, use standard landscape architectural drawing conventions and confirm with project team. (_SEMANTIC_LENSING.md C-003)

4.1 Prepare Planting Plan sheet(s):
- Base plan from DEL-007-02 Landscape Site Plans (spatial framework).
- Overlay planting zones with boundaries, zone codes, and plant callouts.
- Show individual plant locations (for tree/shrub-scale plantings) and zone boundaries (for mass planting/seeding areas).
- Include north arrow, scale bar, legend, and drawing border consistent with project drawing standards.

4.2 Prepare Plant Schedule:
- Tabular format listing: plant code, botanical name, common name, size/grade at planting, quantity, and notes.
- Consistent with plant callouts on plan sheets.

4.3 Prepare Planting Detail Sheet(s):
- Standard details for: tree planting pit, shrub planting pit, groundcover planting, erosion control seeding, mulching, staking (where required).
- Details consistent with DEL-007-04 specification requirements.

4.4 Prepare Restoration Phasing Diagram:
- Diagrammatic phasing plan showing Phase 1 (construction period), Phase 2 (initial seeding/planting at practical completion), Phase 3 (supplementary planting — timing TBD, pending CONF-007-03-002 resolution).

### Step 5 — Coordination and Integration Review

5.1 Overlay planting plan on PKG-005 civil grading drawings to confirm:
- No planting zones conflict with drainage swales, detention areas, or gravel driving surfaces.
- Planting does not encroach on civil infrastructure (utilities, pavements, pads).

5.2 Cross-check plant codes and zone designations with DEL-007-04 Landscape Specification draft — confirm all callouts are consistently named.

5.3 Coordinate with DEL-007-02 Landscape Site Plans for spatial consistency — confirm that hard/soft landscape boundaries are compatible.

5.4 Confirm stamp type for IFC drawings with project team (P.Eng. vs. RLA stamp — see CONF-007-03-001 in Guidance.md Conflict Table).

### Step 6 — County Approval and IFC Issue

6.1 Confirm DEL-007-01 Preliminary Landscape Design has received County approval before issuing planting plans for construction. (R-01 §3.3.2)

6.2 Issue drawing set for internal project team review and coordination.

6.3 Incorporate review comments and re-issue.

6.4 Apply professional stamp (P.Eng. or RLA — pending CONF-007-03-001 resolution).

6.5 Issue IFC Planting & Restoration Plans drawing set.
