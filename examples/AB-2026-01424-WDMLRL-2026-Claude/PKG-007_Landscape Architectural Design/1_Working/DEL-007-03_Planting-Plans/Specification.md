# Specification — DEL-007-03 Planting & Restoration Plans

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-007-03 covers the complete Planting & Restoration Plans drawing set for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project. Specifically, this deliverable addresses:

- Botanical and ecological framework for landscape implementation of the expansion area (Appendix C aerial, R-07).
- Plant palette development including species selection, zonation by site condition, and restoration methodology.
- Planting plan drawings showing plant locations, zones, quantities, and callouts referenced in the landscape specification (DEL-007-04).
- Restoration strategy for disturbed areas within and adjacent to the expansion footprint, consistent with the civil grading plan (PKG-005).
- Phasing plan for native vegetation establishment.
- Planting details and plant schedule sufficient for construction implementation.

Source: _CONTEXT.md (scope description); DECOMP §7 PKG-007 narrative; R-01 §1.0 (landscape architectural consulting services included in proponent scope); R-01 §3.3.2 ("Grade/landscape the proposed lot").

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Civil grading design — assigned to PKG-005 (Civil Design). (DECOMP §6 PKG-007 Exclusions)
- Construction execution of landscaping and sitework — assigned to PKG-018 (Sitework & Civil Construction). (DECOMP §6 PKG-007 Exclusions)
- Landscape specifications (material and workmanship requirements) — assigned to DEL-007-04 (Landscape Specification).
- Brushing and clearing of the project area — County forces responsibility. (R-01 §3.3.1)
- Bulk cut and fill — County forces responsibility. (R-01 §3.3.1)
- Aggregate supply — County/Landfill responsibility. (R-01 §3.3.1)

## Requirements

### REQ-007-03-001 — Landscape Design Completeness
The Planting & Restoration Plans shall constitute a complete landscape architectural design as required by SOW-0017.
Source: R-01 §1.0; DECOMP SOW-0017.

### REQ-007-03-002 — Integration with Site Plans
The planting plans shall be spatially coordinated with the Landscape Site Plans (DEL-007-02), using the approved site layout as the spatial framework for all planting zones and plant locations.
Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (upstream dependency: DEL-007-02 must be approved); _CONTEXT.md (relationship to package).

### REQ-007-03-003 — Integration with Civil Grading
Planting zones and restoration areas shall be sited and designed to be compatible with the civil grading plan (PKG-005), including positive drainage requirements and the constraint that grading shall not alter drainage conditions of neighboring properties.
Source: R-01 §3.4 (SOW-0020, SOW-0021); DECOMP §6 PKG-007 exclusion note.

### REQ-007-03-004 — Grade and Landscape the Proposed Lot
The landscape architectural design shall address grading and landscaping of the proposed lot in accordance with the Issued for Construction drawings.
Source: R-01 §3.3.2 ("Grade/landscape the proposed lot and construct the building as per the Issued for Construction drawings").

### REQ-007-03-005 — Plant Palette Development
The plans shall include a plant palette appropriate to the central Alberta climate and site conditions, with a preference for native vegetation where consistent with the restoration strategy.
Source: _CONTEXT.md ("native vegetation establishment"); _REFERENCES.md (native plant selection and restoration guidelines noted); ASSUMPTION: specific species requirements not explicitly stated in RFP; palette to be developed by Landscape Architect.

### REQ-007-03-006 — Plant Schedule
The drawing set shall include a plant schedule with botanical name, common name, size/grade at planting, quantity, and applicable notes for each plant species used.
Source: ASSUMPTION based on standard landscape drawing set conventions consistent with deliverable type (Drawing Set); location TBD in explicit RFP/standards text.

### REQ-007-03-007 — Restoration Strategy and Phasing
The plans shall document a restoration strategy for disturbed areas and a phasing plan for native vegetation establishment, including sequencing relative to construction completion.
Source: _CONTEXT.md ("sustainable restoration strategies and phasing for native vegetation establishment"); MEMORY.md (work items).

### REQ-007-03-008 — Planting Details
The drawing set shall include standard planting details (e.g., planting pit, staking, mulching, seeding sections) sufficient to guide installation.
Source: ASSUMPTION based on standard drawing set conventions; location TBD in explicit RFP/standards text.

### REQ-007-03-009 — IFC Drawing Standards
All Issued for Construction (IFC) drawings shall be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed in the Province of Alberta.
Source: R-01 §3.3.2; ASSUMPTION: applies to all IFC drawing sets across disciplines per SOW-0018; confirm applicability of P.Eng. vs. registered Landscape Architect stamp with project team — see Conflict Table in Guidance.md.

### REQ-007-03-010 — Downstream Specification Support
The planting plan callouts, plant codes, and zone designations shall be consistent with and referenced by the Landscape Specification (DEL-007-04).
Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (downstream: DEL-007-04 references planting callouts and plant details).

### REQ-007-03-011 — Guarantee Period
All planting and restoration work executed under these plans is subject to the 2-year guarantee period post-CCC, requiring rectification of defects within 10 days of written Owner instruction.
Source: R-01 §2.10; ASSUMPTION: guarantee applies to landscape installation work delivered under this design.

### REQ-007-03-012 — County Approval — Preliminary Design
A preliminary landscape design (DEL-007-01) must receive County approval before final planting plans are issued for construction.
Source: R-01 §3.3.2 ("Deliver Preliminary Design for the County project team to approve"); DECOMP DEL-007-01 relationship to DEL-007-03.

### REQ-007-03-013 — Erosion and Sediment Control (Interim)
The planting and restoration plans shall address interim erosion and sediment control measures for the period between construction disturbance and vegetation establishment, including identification of erosion-vulnerable areas and temporary stabilization methods.
Source: ASSUMPTION — no explicit RFP requirement identified; inferred from disturbed site context (active landfill compound, R-07 Appendix C aerial) and the gap between construction completion and vegetation establishment. REQ-007-03-003 addresses grading integration and REQ-007-03-007 addresses restoration phasing, but neither addresses interim erosion control. (_SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-002)

### REQ-007-03-014 — Vegetation Establishment Success Criteria
The planting and restoration plans shall define measurable performance criteria for vegetation establishment success, including survival rate percentage, coverage percentage, and assessment intervals (e.g., at 3-month, 1-year, and 2-year post-planting), to support verification of restoration outcomes during the 2-year guarantee period.
Source: ASSUMPTION — no explicit RFP requirement for quantitative success criteria identified; inferred from guarantee period obligation (R-01 §2.10) and the need for objective assessment of planting/restoration outcomes. TBD — specific threshold values to be proposed by Landscape Architect. (_SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-005, E-004)

### REQ-007-03-015 — Municipal/County Landscape Approvals
The planting and restoration plans shall comply with applicable Camrose County landscape bylaws, development permit conditions, and environmental setback requirements for the landfill site, if any.
Source: ASSUMPTION — no specific County landscape bylaws are cited in the RFP; TBD — Landscape Architect to confirm with County whether landscape bylaws, development permit conditions, or environmental setbacks apply to the landfill site beyond the preliminary design gate (REQ-007-03-012).
