# Guidance — DEL-007-02 Landscape Site Plans

---

## Purpose

DEL-007-02 Landscape Site Plans exists to translate the preliminary landscape design concept into a set of detailed, construction-ready drawings that define the spatial and technical framework for landscape implementation at the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition site.

The plans serve three primary functions within the project:
1. They establish the definitive site layout — building footprints, hardscape, circulation, site features — that all downstream landscape work (DEL-007-03 Planting & Restoration Plans, DEL-007-04 Landscape Specification) builds upon.
2. They provide the landscape-discipline input to cross-discipline coordination, particularly with civil grading and site construction (PKG-005, PKG-018).
3. They demonstrate to Camrose County that the grading and landscaping of the proposed lot will be executed as required by the IFC drawings and that applicable codes are met.

Source: _CONTEXT.md; DECOMP PKG-007; RFP §3.3.2.

## Principles

### P-1: Site Functionality Drives Design

The WDMLRL site is an active regional landfill maintenance facility. The primary design driver is operational functionality: heavy equipment access, safe circulation, appropriate surface materials, and positive drainage. Landscape aesthetic treatments, while potentially included, are secondary to function and should not compromise heavy equipment operations.

Source: RFP §3.3.2 (gravel surface for motor scraper class); App B (Shop) — site layout showing equipment-focused spatial organization; App C — aerial showing active landfill operational context.

### P-2: Integrate with Civil Grading — Do Not Duplicate

The Landscape Site Plans provide the landscape architect's site layout and grading framework. Civil grading engineering (positive drainage, drainage features, grading calculations) is the domain of PKG-005. The Landscape Architect must coordinate closely with the Civil Engineer to ensure the landscape plans are consistent with and complementary to the civil grading plan, without duplicating engineering decisions.

Source: DECOMP PKG-007 (explicit exclusion: civil grading in PKG-005); _DEPENDENCIES.md.

### P-3: Conceptual Drawing Is the Spatial Baseline

Appendix B (Shop) — the conceptual floor plan/site drawing — establishes the baseline spatial organization: building footprints (130' × 100' New North Shop, 105' × 40' Old North Shop), cement pad locations, gravel pad, overhead pole shed, metal racking, and the Wash Bay Mud Sump exterior location. The Landscape Site Plans must be consistent with this spatial baseline unless the design development process yields a County-approved revision.

Source: App B (Shop).

### P-4: IFC Standard Requires P.Eng. Stamp

All drawings must meet IFC standard and be stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta before issue for construction. The Landscape Architect should coordinate with the project engineer to satisfy this requirement. ASSUMPTION: Whether a Landscape Architect (LAA) registered seal is additionally required under Alberta professional practice rules should be confirmed.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; DECOMP SOW-0018.

### P-6: Drawing Set Quality as a Construction Document (Ref: A-005)

Beyond regulatory and coordination content, the Landscape Site Plans must function as effective construction documents for field use. The Landscape Architect should consider the following quality/performance objectives for the drawing set itself:

1. **Constructability:** Drawings should be readily interpretable by field personnel without requiring clarification requests. Layout dimensions, material callouts, and reference notes should be self-contained on each sheet where practical.
2. **Drawing Clarity:** Scale, line weights, annotation size, and symbol conventions should support field-scale printing (TBD print size) without loss of readability.
3. **Internal Consistency:** Dimensions, labels, and references should be cross-checked across all sheets in the set to eliminate contradictions.
4. **Downstream Usability:** DEL-007-03 and DEL-007-04 teams should be able to adopt the spatial framework from DEL-007-02 without ambiguity about zone boundaries, extents, or reference points.

**ASSUMPTION: These quality objectives are inferred from standard construction document practice for Drawing Set deliverables. Specific acceptance thresholds are TBD -- Landscape Architect / Project team to establish.**

Source: _CONTEXT.md (deliverable type: Drawing Set); standard construction document practice.

### P-5: Preliminary Design Approval Gates IFC Progress

The County must approve the Preliminary Landscape Design (DEL-007-01) before the Landscape Site Plans can be issued for construction. Design development of DEL-007-02 may proceed in parallel with DEL-007-01 approval, but IFC issue is gated on that approval.

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md; RFP §3.3.2.

## Considerations

### C-1: Grading and Drainage — Do Not Impact Neighbours

RFP §3.4 explicitly requires that the grading design not alter drainage conditions of neighbouring properties. The Landscape Site Plans must reflect this constraint and cross-reference the Civil Engineer's drainage design. Where landscape grading intent could affect off-site drainage, the Landscape Architect must flag this to the Civil Engineer.

Source: RFP §3.4; DECOMP SOW-0020, SOW-0021.

### C-2: Topsoil Stripping Extent

The RFP requires stripping topsoil from all driving surfaces (if not already performed by the Owner/County). The Landscape Site Plans should identify which areas are driving surfaces requiring topsoil stripping and include a notation clarifying Owner vs. Proponent responsibility. ASSUMPTION: The County may have partially completed brushing and clearing; the extent of pre-existing topsoil stripping should be confirmed at the mandatory site meeting or early in design development.

Source: RFP §3.3.1, §3.3.2; DECOMP SOW-0075, SOW-0200, SOW-0201.

### C-3: Aggregate Supply Is Not In Proponent Scope

The County/Landfill supplies aggregate/gravel. The Landscape Architect's drawings should not specify aggregate supply as a Proponent-delivered material for gravel driving surfaces and gravel pads. Construction specifications (DEL-007-04) should clearly note County-supplied aggregate.

Source: RFP §3.3.1; DECOMP SOW-0203.

### C-4: Heavy Equipment Turning Radius

The gravel driving surface must accommodate the turning radius of motor scraper-class heavy equipment. The Landscape Architect should confirm the required turning radius with the civil/structural team and ensure the site circulation layout shown on the plans is geometrically sufficient. Minimum turning radii for motor scraper class equipment are TBD from project-specific sources (no specific dimension provided in RFP).

**Action required (Ref: A-004):** Obtain minimum turning radius dimension for motor scraper-class equipment from the civil/structural team or equipment manufacturer data. Without a specific dimension, the driving surface layout cannot be verified for geometric adequacy. This TBD also affects Specification Verification (REQ-007-02-03 row, Ref: D-001).

Source: RFP §3.3.2, §3.4; DECOMP SOW-0077.

### C-5: Geotechnical Information Dependency

Landscape grading and surface treatment decisions may be influenced by geotechnical findings (DEL-008-01). The Landscape Architect should review the geotech report when available and confirm that landscape site plan assumptions about ground conditions, subgrade, and drainage are consistent with geotech findings.

**Contingency guidance (Ref: B-001):** If the geotechnical report (DEL-008-01) is unavailable or delayed when landscape design must proceed, the Landscape Architect should:
1. Document all geotechnically-sensitive assumptions explicitly on the drawings as "Subject to Geotech Confirmation."
2. Flag the dependency risk to the Project Manager with an assessment of schedule impact relative to the December 31, 2026 completion deadline.
3. Proceed with design development using conservative grading assumptions, with the understanding that revisions may be required upon receipt of the geotech report.
4. Identify which drawing elements are most sensitive to geotech findings (subgrade preparation, drainage assumptions, surface treatment) and plan for targeted revision rather than full redraw.

**ASSUMPTION: This contingency approach is inferred from standard design practice for geotechnically-dependent deliverables. Project Manager / Landscape Architect to confirm acceptable risk tolerance.**

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md; DECOMP SOW-0001; Procedure Step 5.

### C-6: Coordination with Wash Bay Mud Sump

The Wash Bay Mud Sump is located exterior to the building on the east side (App B). The Landscape Site Plans should show its location and ensure adequate access and clearance for excavator cleanout operations, as the sump design requires cleanout by excavator.

Source: App B (Shop); DECOMP SOW-0027b; RFP §3.4.

### C-9: Escalation Protocol for Cross-Discipline Coordination Conflicts (Ref: X-001)

When Step 4 coordination between Landscape and Civil disciplines reveals irreconcilable conflicts (e.g., landscape grading intent vs. civil drainage requirements, driving surface geometry disagreements), the following escalation path should be followed:

1. **Document the conflict** in the coordination review record with clear descriptions of each discipline's position and the impact on the project.
2. **Attempt discipline-level resolution** through a joint technical review meeting between the Landscape Architect and Civil Engineer.
3. **If unresolved after discipline-level review**, escalate to the Project Manager for a ruling, providing the conflict documentation and both disciplines' recommended resolutions.
4. **Record the ruling** and update affected drawings and documents accordingly.

**ASSUMPTION: This escalation protocol is inferred from standard cross-discipline coordination practice. Project Manager to confirm or provide project-specific escalation procedure.**

Source: Procedure Step 4; standard design coordination practice.

### C-10: County Approval Criteria for DEL-007-01 (Ref: X-004)

REQ-007-02-08 gates IFC issue on County approval of DEL-007-01 (Preliminary Landscape Design). However, neither the RFP nor available sources define what criteria the County evaluates for this approval. The Landscape Architect and Project Manager should:

1. **Proactively inquire** with the County (or County liaison) about the evaluation criteria for preliminary landscape design approval.
2. **Align DEL-007-02 design development** with anticipated approval conditions so that any County-directed changes to DEL-007-01 can be efficiently incorporated into DEL-007-02.
3. **Document the County's stated or inferred criteria** once known, and update Guidance and Specification accordingly.

TBD -- County approval criteria to be obtained. Source: Specification REQ-007-02-08; Guidance P-5; RFP §3.3.2.

### C-8: Building Dimensions Are Conceptual Only (Ref: E-002)

The building dimensions used throughout the document set (130' x 100' New North Shop, 105' x 40' Old North Shop, approx. 235' total east-west extent) are sourced exclusively from AB-2026-01424-Appendix B (Shop).pdf, which is a conceptual drawing. These are design-basis dimensions that have not been confirmed by survey or structural engineering. The Landscape Architect should:
1. Treat these dimensions as preliminary design-basis values subject to confirmation.
2. Coordinate with the Structural Engineer (PKG-002) and Surveyor (DEL-008-02) to obtain confirmed dimensions before IFC issue.
3. Flag any landscape site plan elements (setbacks, clearances, driving surface geometry) that are sensitive to changes in building footprint dimensions.

**ASSUMPTION: Confirmed dimensions will be available from structural design and site survey before IFC. If not, landscape plans issued with conceptual dimensions must be clearly marked as "Subject to Dimensional Confirmation."**

Source: AB-2026-01424-Appendix B (Shop).pdf; Datasheet Attributes (Expansion Area); Procedure Step 3.

### C-7: Completion Deadline Pressure

All Work must be complete by December 31, 2026. The Landscape Architect should target IFC issue of landscape plans early enough to allow procurement, mobilization, and construction completion within this deadline. Design schedule should be coordinated with overall project schedule.

Source: RFP §3.1; DECOMP SOW-0091, OBJ-002.

## Trade-offs

### T-1: Landscape Detailing Depth vs. Schedule

Given the December 31, 2026 completion deadline and the scope of the broader project, the Landscape Architect must balance producing thorough, detailed landscape site plans with the schedule constraint. Detailed planting design is deferred to DEL-007-03; DEL-007-02 should focus on site layout, hardscape, grading framework, and circulation — the minimum content needed for construction.

### T-2: Landscape Architect vs. Civil Engineer Scope Boundary

The boundary between landscape architectural site plans (DEL-007-02) and civil grading/driving surface drawings (PKG-005) is not precisely defined in the RFP. ASSUMPTION: The Landscape Architect leads spatial/landscape site planning; the Civil Engineer leads grading calculations, drainage engineering, and driving surface structural design. The two disciplines must agree on this boundary early in design development to avoid duplication or gaps.

Source: DECOMP PKG-007 (exclusion noted), PKG-005.

### T-3: IFC Stamp Requirement — Discipline Coordination

The requirement for a P.Eng. stamp on all IFC drawings may require the Landscape Architect to work under or alongside a licensed P.Eng. for drawing stamping. This is a schedule and coordination consideration, not a design issue. Confirm professional responsibility and stamp arrangement with project leadership early.

Source: RFP §3.3.2.

### T-4: Post-IFC Feedback Mechanism (Ref: E-005)

No lessons-learned or post-IFC feedback mechanism is currently defined for this deliverable. The Landscape Architect should consider establishing a construction feedback loop so that:
1. Field issues encountered during construction (PKG-018) that relate to landscape site plan adequacy are documented and fed back to the design team.
2. Design clarification requests (RFIs) related to DEL-007-02 content are tracked and their resolution incorporated into a lessons-learned register.
3. Insights from field experience inform future similar deliverables.

TBD -- Project Manager to determine whether a project-wide post-IFC feedback mechanism exists or whether one should be established for landscape deliverables.
