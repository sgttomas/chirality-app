# Procedure — DEL-007-02 Landscape Site Plans

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps for producing the Landscape Site Plans (DEL-007-02) for the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition project, from design initiation through Issued for Construction (IFC) drawing set delivery. The Procedure covers both the design production process (how to produce the deliverable) and key verification checkpoints.

## Steps

### Step 1: Mobilize and Gather Existing Site Information

1.1 Obtain and review all available source documents: RFP (especially §3.3.2 and §3.4), Appendix B (Shop conceptual drawing), Appendix C (Site Maps), and any site survey outputs from DEL-008-02.

1.2 Confirm with the project team the current status of County-completed site preparation (brushing, clearing, bulk cut and fill) to understand what existing site conditions the landscape plans must account for.

1.3 Attend or review notes from the mandatory site meeting (RFP §3.2, scheduled March 3, 2026 at the WDMLRL site) to observe existing building and available space for the addition.

1.4 Confirm the geotechnical report (DEL-008-01) status and expected completion date; flag any landscape grading assumptions that may need revision once geotech is available.

1.5 **Record (Ref: E-004):** Upon completing the source document review, produce the "Source Document Register" listed in the Records table, documenting which source documents (title, version/date, relevant sections) were reviewed as design basis for this deliverable.

Source: RFP §3.2; _DEPENDENCIES.md.

### Step 2: Establish Design Scope Boundary with Civil Engineer

2.1 Coordinate with the Civil Engineer responsible for PKG-005 (Civil Design) to agree on the scope boundary:
- Landscape Architect leads: spatial site layout, landscape envelope, hardscape zone extents, pad/surface locations, landscape grading intent, planting zone framework.
- Civil Engineer leads: grading calculations, drainage engineering, driving surface structural cross-section, stormwater management.

2.2 Document the agreed scope boundary in a brief coordination note and circulate to project manager. **Record:** Produce the "Scope Boundary Coordination Note" listed in the Records table. (Ref: C-003)

**ASSUMPTION:** This boundary is not explicitly defined in the RFP; the above represents a best-effort interpretation. Human confirmation required if the project team intends a different division of work. See Guidance Conflict Table CONF-007-02-02.

Source: DECOMP PKG-007, PKG-005.

### Step 3: Develop Design Concept — Design Development Phase

3.1 Using Appendix B (Shop) as the spatial baseline, develop the landscape site plan at design development level:
- Lay out building footprints (New North Shop 130' × 100'; Old North Shop 105' × 40') accurately on the site plan.
- Show gravel pad (north of New North Shop per App B), cement pad(s), overhead pole shed and metal racking (for spatial context), gravel driving surface extent, and Wash Bay Mud Sump exterior location (east of New North Shop per App B).
- Confirm overall site dimensions (approx. 235' total east–west per App B).
- Establish and show site circulation routes for heavy equipment (motor scraper class).
- Indicate topsoil stripping zones (all driving surfaces).
- Indicate grading intent consistent with civil grading (positive drainage direction).
- Establish planting and restoration zone extents (spatial framework for DEL-007-03).

3.2 Circulate design development drawings to project manager and Civil Engineer for review.

Source: App B (Shop); App C; RFP §3.3.2, §3.4; DECOMP SOW-0075–SOW-0079.

### Step 4: Coordinate with Civil Grading and Other Disciplines

4.1 When DEL-005-02 (Site Grading Plan) is available, overlay landscape site plan with civil grading plan and resolve any conflicts:
- Confirm grading does not alter drainage of neighbouring properties (RFP §3.4).
- Confirm positive site drainage direction is consistent across both drawing sets.
- Coordinate contour information and spot elevations at key landscape interface points.

4.2 Coordinate the Wash Bay Mud Sump location and access clearance with the structural and plumbing teams (DEL-011-05 Wash Bay Enclosure, DEL-014-04 Floor Drains).

4.3 Confirm site access routes are compatible with the project civil/sitework construction sequence (PKG-018).

4.4 **Record:** Produce the "Civil Coordination Review Record" listed in the Records table, documenting the overlay review, parties involved, items reviewed, conflict resolutions, and any open items with assigned owner and target date. (Ref: C-003)

Source: RFP §3.4; DECOMP SOW-0020, SOW-0021; _DEPENDENCIES.md.

### Step 5: Incorporate Geotechnical Findings

5.1 Upon receipt of the geotechnical report (DEL-008-01), review for any findings that affect landscape grading, surface material decisions, or subsurface drainage assumptions.

5.2 Update design development drawings if geotechnical findings require revision.

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md; DECOMP SOW-0001.

### Step 6: Prepare Issued for Construction (IFC) Drawing Set

6.1 Confirm that DEL-007-01 Preliminary Landscape Design has received County approval. Do not proceed to IFC issue without this confirmation.

6.2 Prepare final IFC drawing set:
- Finalize all drawing content per REQ-007-02-01 through REQ-007-02-10 (see Specification).
- Include drawing titles, scales, north arrow, legend, sheet index, and revision block.
- Include coordination notes cross-referencing civil grading and drainage drawings.
- Include topsoil stripping zone notation with Owner vs. Proponent responsibility clearly stated.
- Include driving surface notation confirming County-supplied aggregate.

**ASSUMPTION:** Anticipated drawing sheets: L-001 (Overall Landscape Site Plan), L-002 (Grading Framework/Drainage Indication), L-003 (Driving Surface & Pad Layout), L-004 (Site Details). Final sheet list to be confirmed by Landscape Architect.

6.3 Apply drawing revision tracking protocol (Ref: X-005):
- Assign revision numbers per the project revision numbering convention (TBD -- project team to establish or reference a project-wide revision control standard).
- Include a revision history block on each sheet documenting: revision number, date, description of change, and author/checker initials.
- Use revision clouds on revised sheets to highlight areas changed from previous revision.
- Maintain a drawing revision log as a project record.

**ASSUMPTION: Revision tracking conventions are inferred from standard drawing production practice. If a project-wide revision control standard exists, reference it here instead. TBD -- Project team / CAD standard to confirm.**

6.4 Submit IFC drawing set for P.Eng. review and stamp.

6.5 Confirm stamp and signature requirements: P.Eng. licensed in Alberta required on all IFC drawings. Confirm with project leadership whether LAA seal is also required. See Guidance Conflict Table CONF-007-02-01.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; DECOMP SOW-0018.

### Step 7: Internal Quality Review

7.1 Before issuing IFC drawings, conduct an internal quality review against:
- Specification requirements REQ-007-02-01 through REQ-007-02-10
- Drawing quality acceptance criteria (see Specification -- Drawing Quality Acceptance Criteria section) (Ref: F-003)
- Conceptual drawing consistency (AB-2026-01424-Appendix B (Shop).pdf)
- Site map consistency (AB-2026-01424-Appendix C - Site Maps.pdf)
- Civil drawing coordination (DEL-005-02, DEL-005-03)
- Downstream deliverable compatibility (DEL-007-03, DEL-007-04)

7.2 Document review findings and resolve any non-conformances before IFC issue.

7.3 **Sign-off authority (Ref: D-003):** The internal quality review shall be approved by TBD (role/person with authority to approve the QA review). **Action required:** Project Manager / QA lead to designate the sign-off authority for this deliverable's quality review.

**ASSUMPTION: Sign-off authority is typically the Landscape Architect (discipline lead) or a designated QA reviewer. Project team to confirm.**

### Step 8: Issue IFC Drawing Set

8.1 Issue IFC Landscape Site Plans with P.Eng. stamp to the project team and County as required by the contract.

8.2 Distribute to DEL-007-03 and DEL-007-04 teams as the spatial framework baseline.

8.3 Retain a record copy in the project document management system.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; DECOMP SOW-0018.
