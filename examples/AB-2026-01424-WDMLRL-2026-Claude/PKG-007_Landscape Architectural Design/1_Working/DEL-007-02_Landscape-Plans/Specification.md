# Specification — DEL-007-02 Landscape Site Plans

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-007-02 Landscape Site Plans is a Drawing Set produced by the Landscape Architect under PKG-007 — Landscape Architectural Design. It provides the comprehensive graphic representation of the landscape and site layout for the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition, covering:

- Site layout and spatial organization of the landscape envelope around the New North Shop addition and Old North Shop
- Site grading framework from a landscape architectural perspective (coordinated with Civil grading deliverables)
- Spatial representation of site hardscape elements: gravel driving surface, cement pads, gravel pad, site access and circulation
- Site amenities and landscape elements at design development and construction document (IFC) levels
- Integration framework for planting and restoration (detailed in DEL-007-03)
- Establishing the technical and spatial basis for DEL-007-03 (Planting & Restoration Plans) and DEL-007-04 (Landscape Specification)

Sources: _CONTEXT.md; DECOMP PKG-007; RFP §1.0.

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Civil grading engineering calculations and civil drainage design (scope of PKG-005: DEL-005-02, DEL-005-03)
- Construction execution of grading, driving surfaces, and pads (scope of PKG-018)
- Detailed planting plan and species palette (scope of DEL-007-03)
- Material and construction specifications for landscape elements (scope of DEL-007-04)
- Brushing and clearing (County responsibility — out of Proponent scope per RFP §3.3.1)
- Bulk cut and fill (County responsibility — out of Proponent scope per RFP §3.3.1)
- Aggregate/gravel supply (County/Landfill responsibility per RFP §3.3.1)

## Requirements

### REQ-007-02-01: Site Plan Content — Site Layout

The Landscape Site Plans shall graphically represent the full landscape and site layout of the project area, including:
- Building footprints (New North Shop addition and Old North Shop)
- Gravel driving surface area
- Cement pad location(s)
- Gravel pad location
- Wash Bay Mud Sump location (exterior, east of New North Shop)
- Overhead Pole Shed and Metal Racking (existing, shown for context)
- Site access routes and circulation

Source: RFP §3.3.2; App B (Shop) — conceptual floor plan/site drawing; App C — site aerial.

### REQ-007-02-02: Grading Framework Integration

The Landscape Site Plans shall show site grading intent sufficient for integration with the Civil Grading Plan (DEL-005-02), including:
- Indication of positive site drainage direction(s)
- Confirmation that grading design does not alter drainage conditions of neighbouring properties
- Coordination notes or cross-reference to Civil grading drawings

Source: RFP §3.4; DECOMP SOW-0020, SOW-0021.

### REQ-007-02-03: Gravel Driving Surface

The plans shall show the extent and layout of the gravel driving surface. The driving surface shall be designed to accommodate the weight and turning radius of large construction equipment (motor scraper class).

Source: RFP §3.3.2, §3.4; DECOMP SOW-0077.

Note: Aggregate/gravel supply is County/Landfill responsibility and is out of Proponent scope (DECOMP SOW-0203).

### REQ-007-02-04: Cement and Gravel Pads

The plans shall show the location and extent of cement pad(s) and gravel pad as indicated on conceptual drawings (Appendix B).

Source: App B (Shop); DECOMP SOW-0078, SOW-0079.

### REQ-007-02-05: Topsoil Stripping Indication

The plans shall indicate areas requiring topsoil stripping from all driving surfaces (if not already performed by Owner).

Source: RFP §3.3.2; DECOMP SOW-0075. **ASSUMPTION: Topsoil stripping extent is assumed to cover all driving surface areas shown on the site plan; confirmation required if Owner has already performed stripping.**

### REQ-007-02-06: IFC Drawing Standard — P.Eng. Stamp

All Issued for Construction (IFC) drawings produced under this deliverable shall be signed and stamped by a professional engineer (P.Eng.) licensed in the Province of Alberta.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; DECOMP SOW-0018.

**ASSUMPTION: The P.Eng. stamp requirement applies to the full IFC drawing set including landscape plans; the RFP §3.3.2 specifies this for all final design drawings. Confirmation of whether a registered Landscape Architect (LAA) seal is additionally required is TBD — Alberta landscape architectural regulatory requirements apply (location TBD in RFP).** See Guidance Conflict Table CONF-007-02-01 for proposed resolution path. (Ref: A-001)

### REQ-007-02-07: Design Development and Construction Documents

The Landscape Site Plans shall be produced at design development level for County approval (aligned with DEL-007-01 preliminary approval sequence) and progressed to IFC/construction document level.

Source: _CONTEXT.md (Design Development and Construction Document levels stated); RFP §3.3.2.

### REQ-007-02-08: Preliminary Design Approval Prerequisite

Landscape Site Plans shall not advance to IFC stage until the Preliminary Landscape Design (DEL-007-01) has received County approval.

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (declared upstream dependency); RFP §3.3.2 (preliminary design for County approval required).

### REQ-007-02-09: Coordination with Civil Design

The Landscape Site Plans shall be coordinated with the Civil Grading Plan (DEL-005-02), Drainage Plan (DEL-005-03), and Driving Surface & Pad Layout Plan (DEL-005-04). Conflicts or interface conditions shall be identified and resolved with the Civil Engineer.

Source: DECOMP PKG-007 (exclusion: civil grading in PKG-005); _DEPENDENCIES.md.

### REQ-007-02-10: Planting and Restoration Framework

The Landscape Site Plans shall establish the spatial framework (zones, edges, extent) within which DEL-007-03 Planting & Restoration Plans will develop planting and restoration detail.

Source: _CONTEXT.md; DECOMP PKG-007.
