# Guidance — DEL-002-09 Stair Details

---

## Purpose

DEL-002-09 provides the structural detail drawings that enable the construction team (PKG-011) to build the stair giving access to the overhead mezzanine storage level within the New North Shop. The stair is a direct program requirement: the mezzanine is intended for heavy storage (oil totes, oil pumping equipment), and access must be safe, code-compliant, and structurally adequate.

This deliverable is part of the coordinated structural drawing set for PKG-002. It supports OBJ-001 (code-compliant, fully functional facility) and OBJ-003 (complete, P.Eng.-stamped IFC documentation across all disciplines before construction begins).

Without this drawing set, the construction team cannot build the stair structure with confidence that it meets code, coordinates with the mezzanine framing, or achieves the Owner's intended use of the mezzanine.

---

## Principles

### P-1 — Integration with Mezzanine Framing
The stair structural details must be developed in direct coordination with DEL-002-05 (Mezzanine Framing Details). The stair top landing must connect to the mezzanine structure at a compatible point, with matching connection details and load assumptions. Develop both deliverables in parallel or sequence DEL-002-05 before DEL-002-09 to ensure the landing elevation, beam locations, and load paths are established before stair detailing begins.
- **Source (rationale):** OBJ-003 (coordinated design across disciplines); standard structural engineering practice; Decomposition PKG-002 structure.
- **[D-002] Sequencing rationale:** If DEL-002-05 is delayed and not available when stair detailing must begin, the Structural Engineer should (a) establish a preliminary mezzanine deck elevation and connection zone based on the best available information from DEL-002-03 (Structural Framing Plans), (b) document the assumed interface parameters as provisional design assumptions, and (c) plan for a coordination check and potential revision once DEL-002-05 is available. The risk of proceeding without DEL-002-05 is dimensional or connection incompatibility at the top landing; the risk of waiting is schedule delay to the overall PKG-002 drawing set. ASSUMPTION: The Structural Engineer will assess the schedule trade-off and document the decision.

### P-2 — Load Path Clarity
The stair dead load and live load must be clearly traced to the concrete structure below and/or the mezzanine framing above. If the stair is self-supporting (post-and-stringer type anchored at floor and landing), the base connections into the concrete slab and the top connections into the mezzanine must be fully detailed. If the stair is cantilevered or suspended, the load path to the primary structure must be explicit.
- **Source (rationale):** Code compliance requirement (R-01 §3.3.2, §3.4); heavy-storage mezzanine context (R-01 §3.4).

### P-3 — Heavy-Use Context
The mezzanine this stair serves is specified for heavy storage. The stair itself should be designed for industrial occupancy live loads consistent with the mezzanine's intended use (oil totes and pumping equipment imply frequent heavy-load handling). The structural engineer should not default to minimum residential or light commercial stair loading.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.4 ("Mezzanine storage above utility and parts room capable of handling heavy items such as several oil totes and oil pumping equipment"); Decomposition SOW-0033.
- **Note:** Specific live load value for stairs is to be determined by the Structural Engineer per applicable Alberta Building Code provisions. ASSUMPTION: Industrial/storage occupancy classification applies.

### P-4 — Concrete Anchorage Detail Quality
The shop is a concrete structure (R-01 §3.4). Stair base connections to the concrete slab are a critical interface. The drawing set should clearly show anchor bolt layout, base plate sizing, grout requirements, and any embedded plate details, and cross-reference DEL-002-08 (Steel Plate Embedment Plan) where floor embedments exist at the stair base.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.4 (concrete structure); R-04 (App B shows steel plates in floor at various locations); Decomposition DEL-002-08.

### P-5 — Code-First Design
The Alberta Building Code and Safety Codes govern minimum stair geometry (rise, run, width), handrail heights, guardrail requirements at the mezzanine landing, and structural load cases. Designing to code minimums should be explicitly verified rather than assumed. The drawings should call out which code edition and clauses are applied.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.3.2; R-01 §3.4.

## Considerations

### C-1 — Stair Location Confirmation
The Appendix B (Shop) conceptual floor plan shows a label "Stairs to Mezzanine" in the area between the Utility Room and the 50,000 L Water Storage tank, near the east side of the mezzanine footprint. This is a conceptual location only. The final stair location should be confirmed with the Architect (PKG-001) and mechanical/electrical engineers to avoid conflicts with equipment, plumbing runs, or electrical panels. ASSUMPTION: The Structural Engineer will confirm location with the design team during coordinated design.
- **Source:** R-04 (App B floor plan); R-01 §3.4 (design-build context).

### C-2 — Stair Material Selection
The RFP does not specify the stair construction material. Both structural steel open-riser stairs and concrete stairs are common in industrial concrete-structure buildings in Alberta. Key trade-off considerations:
- Steel stairs: faster fabrication and installation, lighter weight (favorable for mezzanine connection), easier field adjustment.
- Concrete stairs: more durable in industrial environment, better fire resistance, heavier (impacts foundation and mezzanine loading).
- ASSUMPTION: The Structural Engineer will select material based on structural efficiency, coordination with the mezzanine framing material (DEL-002-05), and local construction practice.

### C-3 — Guardrail at Mezzanine Landing
At the top of the stair, a guardrail system around the mezzanine opening and along exposed mezzanine edges is required by code. The structural engineer should detail the guardrail post connections to the mezzanine framing as part of this deliverable or clearly assign responsibility to DEL-002-05. Ambiguity between DEL-002-09 and DEL-002-05 regarding guardrail connection details at the mezzanine deck is a coordination risk.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.4 (Alberta Safety Codes); standard structural engineering practice.

### C-4 — Access Width for Equipment
Given the mezzanine is used for heavy storage items (oil totes, pumping equipment), consider whether the stair width needs to accommodate equipment handling (e.g., can workers carry equipment up the stair safely, or is a mechanical lift considered?). The RFP does not specify a mechanical lift; ASSUMPTION: stair is the primary access, and width should allow safe manual handling per Health and Safety requirements applicable to the site.
- **Source:** R-01 §3.4; R-01 §2.15 (Prime Contractor health and safety obligations).

### C-5 — Coordination with Electrical (Stair Lighting)
If the stair landing area requires dedicated lighting (code requirement in enclosed or semi-enclosed conditions), coordinate with PKG-004 (Electrical Design) to confirm luminaire locations do not conflict with structural members. This is a coordination note, not a structural design requirement for this drawing set.
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION — standard inter-discipline coordination; not explicitly stated in RFP for this deliverable.
