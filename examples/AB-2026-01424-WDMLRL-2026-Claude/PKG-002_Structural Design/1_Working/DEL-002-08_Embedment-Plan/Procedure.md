# Procedure — DEL-002-08 Steel Plate Embedment Plan

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce the Steel Plate Embedment Plan drawing set (DEL-002-08). It is oriented toward the Structural Engineer responsible for preparing and issuing this deliverable as part of the PKG-002 Structural Design package for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project.

The output of this procedure is a complete, P.Eng.-stamped drawing set ready for construction use (IFC status), documenting all embedded steel plate locations and embedment details in the New North Shop concrete floor slab.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Confirm Scope and Coordination Inputs

1.1 Obtain the final architectural floor plan (DEL-001-02) to establish the column grid, bay dimensions, and exact room/bay boundaries for the New North Shop addition.

1.2 Confirm bay dimensions with the Architect: repair bays (2 drive-through bays), wash bay, and Service Pit zone.

1.3 Obtain or confirm the following from other PKG-002 deliverables in progress:
   - Slab thickness and reinforcement layout (DEL-002-02)
   - Service Pit plan extents and structural walls (DEL-002-06)
   - Crane runway column base locations (DEL-002-07)

1.4 Obtain equipment load data from the project team or County: axle/track loads, contact patch dimensions, and vehicle operating patterns for tracked and packer-type equipment (motor scraper class and similar) intended to use the new shop. **TBD (A-004):** Identify specific source for equipment load data (County fleet records, manufacturer specifications, or Owner-provided data) and confirm availability timeline. The data source must be named before Step 2 can proceed.

1.5 Document all confirmed inputs; note any items received as TBD and record the assumed value used.

Source: Specification REQ-001, REQ-005, REQ-006; Guidance §C-04.

---

### Step 2 — Conduct Structural Load Analysis

2.1 Determine the governing load cases for steel plate design:
   - Vertical wheel/track loads from heavy equipment
   - Lateral scrubbing/turning loads for articulated equipment (motor scraper)
   - Dynamic impact factors as applicable per applicable code

2.2 Determine minimum steel plate thickness and surface area to resist the governing load cases with adequate bearing capacity on the concrete slab below.

2.3 Design the plate anchor system (headed studs, deformed bar anchors, or equivalent):
   - Anchor capacity in shear and tension per CSA A23.3 (ASSUMPTION: applicable)
   - Anchor layout to fit within slab reinforcement layout
   - Embedment depth compatible with slab thickness (from DEL-002-02)

2.4 Document all calculations in the Structural Calculation Package (DEL-002-10). Cross-reference the calculation sections to the drawing notes.

Source: Specification REQ-002, REQ-004, REQ-009; Guidance P-01, P-04, C-05.

---

### Step 3 — Determine Final Plate Locations

3.1 Using the Appendix B conceptual floor plan as reference, determine final plate locations:
   - Confirm which areas require protection (repair bays, wash bay; confirm Service Pit approach zones if applicable)
   - Adjust indicative locations from Appendix B based on engineering judgment and equipment path analysis
   - Identify all plates by plan reference (e.g., SP-01 through SP-n, or per drawing numbering convention)

3.2 Confirm no conflicts with:
   - Service Pit/pit walls and openings (DEL-002-06)
   - Crane runway column base plates (DEL-002-07)
   - Floor drain and sump drain locations (coordinate with plumbing discipline)
   - 50,000 L water storage footprint (confirm with DEL-001-02 and DEL-006-04)

3.3 Document the final plate count, zone assignments, and any deviations from the Appendix B conceptual layout with engineering rationale.

Source: Specification REQ-001, REQ-003, REQ-006; Guidance §C-01, §C-02, P-05.

---

### Step 4 — Prepare Plan Drawing(s)

4.1 Prepare a plan view drawing of the New North Shop floor at a scale appropriate for construction use (TBD by Structural Engineer; typically 1:100 or 1:50 for industrial floor plans).

4.2 Show all steel plates with:
   - Plan dimensions (length and width of each plate)
   - Dimensions to column grid lines or fixed reference points
   - Plate designation labels (e.g., SP-01, SP-02, etc.)
   - Reference to relevant detail drawings (e.g., "See Detail 1/S-XX")

4.3 Show and label adjacent floor elements for coordination context:
   - Service Pit/pit extent
   - Floor drains (for reference only — coordinate with plumbing drawings)
   - Column centerlines and bay lines

4.4 Include a general notes block on the plan sheet covering:
   - Applicable codes and standards
   - Steel plate material specification (grade and standard)
   - Required surface condition (flush tolerances)
   - Reference to calculation package

Source: Specification REQ-003, REQ-010; Guidance P-02.

---

### Step 5 — Prepare Detail Drawing(s)

5.1 Prepare cross-section detail(s) for each plate type (if multiple plate types/thicknesses are used, prepare a detail for each type):

   Detail shall show:
   - Plate width, thickness, and surface condition
   - Plate top elevation relative to finished floor (flush or specified tolerance)
   - Edge treatment (chamfer, radiused edge, or flush cut)
   - Anchor type, size, spacing, and embedment depth
   - Relationship to top reinforcement mat in slab
   - Concrete slab thickness callout (reference to DEL-002-02)
   - Weld symbols if plate-to-anchor welding is required

5.2 If the wash bay plate embedment differs from the main repair bay (e.g., due to drain slope requirement or different equipment loading), prepare separate details for each condition.

5.3 If any plates are adjacent to the Service Pit wall, prepare a detail showing the edge condition at the trench interface.

Source: Specification REQ-004; Guidance §C-01, §C-02, P-03.

---

### Step 6 — Internal Quality Review

6.1 Structural Engineer reviews all drawings against:
   - Load calculations in DEL-002-10 (consistency of design parameters)
   - Coordination drawings from DEL-002-02, DEL-002-06, DEL-002-07
   - Code requirements (Alberta Safety Codes, NBC, CSA standards)
   - RFP requirements (§3.4 — steel plating for tracked/packer equipment)

6.2 Drafting check:
   - All dimensions present and legible
   - No conflicting dimensions
   - Title block complete (project name, drawing number, date, revision)
   - North arrow and scale bar present
   - All detail callouts match detail sheet references

6.3 Resolve all internal review comments before proceeding to stamp.

### Step 7 — Issue for Review / Coordination (Pre-IFC)

7.1 Issue the drawing set to the design team for inter-discipline coordination review:
   - Architect (DEL-001-02) — confirm plate zones do not conflict with architectural floor features
   - Plumbing Engineer (DEL-006-03 / DEL-014-04) — confirm no plate conflicts with drain locations in wash bay and repair bays
   - Mechanical Engineer — confirm no plate conflicts with floor penetrations for exhaust or sump connections

7.2 Address all coordination comments. Document responses and any drawing revisions using a structured coordination comment log.

### Step 8 — P.Eng. Stamp and IFC Issue

8.1 Structural Engineer reviews the final coordinated drawing set.

8.2 Structural Engineer signs and stamps each drawing sheet with their Alberta P.Eng. seal.

8.3 Issue drawings at IFC status with appropriate revision designation (e.g., "IFC Rev 0" or per the project drawing management convention).

**Enrichment (X-003) — Drawing Revision Management Protocol:**
8.3a The following revision management protocol shall apply to this drawing set:
- Initial IFC issue shall be designated Rev 0.
- Subsequent revisions (e.g., post-geotech confirmation, post-coordination changes) shall follow the project drawing management convention. If no project convention exists, use sequential revision numbers (Rev 1, Rev 2, etc.).
- Each revision shall include: (a) a revision description in the title block revision table, (b) revision clouds or delta marks on affected drawings, (c) updated date and re-stamping by the P.Eng.
- A revision log shall be maintained documenting the reason for each revision, the date issued, and the distribution list.
- If design parameters change post-geotech (see Guidance C-04 and CONF-003), the revision shall be issued within TBD business days of receiving confirmed parameters. **TBD:** Define revision turnaround time.

8.4 Distribute IFC drawing set to:
   - General Contractor (for construction use — DEL-011-02)
   - County Project Manager (as required by RFP §3.3.2)
   - Project document control
   - **TBD (E-001):** Confirm whether additional parties require copies (e.g., building inspector for permit review, subtrades for steel plate fabrication/procurement). See Guidance — IFC Distribution Rationale section.

Source: Specification REQ-007; RFP §3.3.2.
