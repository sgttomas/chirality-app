# Specification — DEL-002-08 Steel Plate Embedment Plan

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers
DEL-002-08 is a structural engineering drawing set that provides the complete design information required to install steel plates embedded in the concrete floor slab of the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project.

The drawing set covers:
- Plan-view layout of all steel plate locations, dimensioned to building column grid or reference lines
- Embedment details (cross-sections) showing plate geometry, thickness, anchor/reinforcement connections, surface finish, and edge conditions
- Design notes and general requirements for steel plate embedments

This deliverable covers SOW-0012 (final structural design) as it relates to embedded floor elements.

### What This Deliverable Does Not Cover
- Structural design of the concrete slab itself (see DEL-002-02 Foundation Plan and DEL-002-10 Structural Calculation Package)
- Structural design of crane runway beams or crane support columns (see DEL-002-07 Crane Support Structure Details)
- Service pit/trench structural design (see DEL-002-06 Service Pit / Trench Structural Details)
- Procurement or installation of the steel plates (see DEL-011-02 Steel Plate Floor Embedments, PKG-011)
- Wash bay drainage or floor drain design (see DEL-006-03, DEL-014-04)
- Material specifications beyond what is shown on the drawings (full material specifications are in DEL-002-12 Structural Specification)

## Requirements

### REQ-001 — Locations Shown on Drawing
The Steel Plate Embedment Plan shall show all steel plate locations indicated in the Appendix B conceptual floor plan and any additional locations determined by the Structural Engineer through loading analysis.

Source: R-04 (App B conceptual floor plan — "Steel Plates in Floor as indicated"); RFP §3.4 — "Steel plating in concrete at strategic locations to accommodate track and packer type heavy equipment."

### REQ-002 — Strategic Placement for Heavy Equipment
Steel plates shall be located at positions that accommodate tracked and packer-type heavy equipment (including motor scraper-class vehicles) operating within the repair bays and wash bay.

Source: RFP §3.4.

### REQ-003 — Plan Dimensions
Plan drawings shall dimension steel plate locations with reference to established building column grid lines or other fixed reference points. Dimensions shall be sufficient for field layout by the general contractor without requiring engineering interpretation.

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard IFC drawing practice for Alberta structural drawings. Confirmation of dimensioning convention subject to Structural Engineer's judgment.

### REQ-004 — Embedment Detail Sections
Detail sections shall be provided showing, at minimum:
- Steel plate dimensions (length, width, thickness)
- Embedment depth and relationship to slab top surface
- Anchor/reinforcement connection (e.g., headed studs, deformed bar anchors, or equivalent)
- Surface condition (flush with slab finish, chamfer/edge treatment) — including measurable flush tolerance (e.g., +0/-X mm relative to finished concrete surface). **TBD (A-005):** Specific numeric tolerance to be specified by Structural Engineer. Guidance P-03 states plates must be "flush with or marginally proud of" finished surface, but no measurable acceptance criterion exists without this value.
- Any required weld symbols or material callouts

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard structural drawing practice for embedded plates in industrial concrete slabs. Specific detail requirements to be confirmed by Structural Engineer.

### REQ-005 — Concrete Slab Coordination
The embedment plan shall be coordinated with the concrete floor slab design (thickness, reinforcement, and pour sequence) as documented in DEL-002-02 (Foundation Plan) and DEL-002-10 (Structural Calculation Package).

Source: ASSUMPTION — inter-deliverable coordination requirement within PKG-002; consistent with decomposition structure.

### REQ-006 — Service Trench Conflict Avoidance
Steel plate locations shall not conflict with the service trench/pit as designed in DEL-002-06. Any steel plates adjacent to the service trench shall show clearance dimensions and edge conditions.

Source: ASSUMPTION — coordination requirement inferred from Appendix B floor plan showing service trench and adjacent steel plates in the same zone.

### REQ-007 — P.Eng. Stamp
All drawings forming this deliverable that are issued for construction (IFC) shall be signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer licensed to practice structural engineering in the Province of Alberta.

Source: RFP §3.3.2 — "All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta."

### REQ-008 — Code Compliance
The design of the steel plate embedments shall comply with all applicable codes and standards, including Alberta Safety Codes and the National Building Code of Canada (NBC) as adopted and amended in Alberta.

### REQ-008A — Thermal/Frost Considerations for Embedded Plates
**TBD (B-004):** The Structural Engineer shall address thermal and frost considerations for steel plates embedded in the heated shop concrete slab, including differential thermal expansion between steel and concrete. Datasheet Environmental Conditions notes "frost depth, thermal loading in concrete slab, and temperature cycling" as relevant factors, but no corresponding requirement existed in the Specification. The Structural Engineer should determine whether thermal effects require explicit design provisions or whether standard detailing is sufficient for this application.

Source: Datasheet Environmental Conditions; ASSUMPTION — gap identified via semantic lensing (B-004). Proposed authority: Structural Engineer.

### REQ-009 — Load Basis Documented
The design loading basis for each plate (or for the plate group) shall be documented in the Structural Calculation Package (DEL-002-10), referencing the equipment types the plates are designed to accommodate.

Source: ASSUMPTION — engineering documentation standard; consistent with OBJ-003 (complete P.Eng.-stamped IFC design documentation).

### REQ-010 — Drawing Format
Drawings shall be formatted for construction use and shall include:
- Title block with project name, deliverable ID (or drawing number), date, revision, and P.Eng. stamp area
- North arrow and scale
- Reference to related drawings (foundation plan, structural sections)

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard IFC drawing requirements consistent with RFP §3.3.2 and CCDC 14–2013 obligations.
