# Specification — DEL-002-06: Service Pit / Trench Structural Details

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-002-06 is a structural drawing set providing complete engineering detail for the service pit / service trench to be constructed within the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill, SW 14–44–21–W4, near Ferintosh, Alberta.

The drawing set shall include sufficient structural detail to:
- Define the geometry and dimensions of the below-grade pit/trench structure.
- Specify the reinforced concrete wall, floor, and cover system.
- Define all embedments, edge conditions, and pit-to-slab interfaces.
- Provide sufficient structural information for the General Contractor (PKG-011) to construct the service pit as part of the building structure package.
- Coordinate structural openings and interfaces for mechanical (ventilation), electrical (lighting), and plumbing (drainage) disciplines.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 (SOW-0028); App B (Shop) conceptual floor plan; Decomposition DEL-002-06 entry; SOW-0012 (final structural design).

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Mechanical ventilation design and specifications (PKG-003 / DEL-003 series).
- Electrical lighting design and specifications (PKG-004 / DEL-004 series).
- Plumbing drainage design within the pit (PKG-006 / DEL-006 series).
- Foundation design (DEL-002-11 Foundation Design Package — variable-price scope).
- Construction of the service pit (DEL-011-06 — General Contractor scope, PKG-011).
- Geotechnical investigation (DEL-008-01 — PKG-008).

## Requirements

### R-001 — Functional Requirement: Ventilated and Lighted Pit

**Requirement:** The service pit structural design shall accommodate the installation of a ventilation system and lighting system within the pit.

**Rationale:** RFP §3.4 states: "Ventilated and lighted service pit area for mechanics to perform servicing under equipment."

**Structural Implication:** The drawing set shall show structural openings, penetration locations, and blockout provisions for ventilation ductwork and electrical conduit/fixtures as coordinated with PKG-003 and PKG-004.

**Verification:** Drawing review — confirm penetrations and blockouts are shown and coordinated with mechanical and electrical discipline drawings.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 (SOW-0028).

---

### R-002 — Functional Requirement: Heavy Equipment Surcharge Loading

**Requirement:** The structural design shall account for the loads imposed by tracked and packer-type heavy equipment (minimum: motor scraper class) traversing and parking at or adjacent to the service pit opening at the shop floor level.

**Rationale:** RFP §3.4 specifies heavy equipment servicing in the pit; the building design includes tracked and packer equipment (RFP §3.4: "Steel plating in concrete at strategic locations to accommodate track and packer type heavy equipment"). App B confirms steel plates in the floor. The service pit cover/grating system and pit walls are exposed to these loads.

**TBD — Equipment Load Data Required:** Specific equipment load data (axle loads, track footprint, contact pressure) must be obtained from the Owner (Camrose County) before R-002 can be finalized. Without this data, the mandatory design practice for R-002 cannot be fully applied. See CON-002 in Guidance.md. (Lensing ref: A-002)

**Verification:** Structural calculation package (DEL-002-10) shall document load derivation and capacity checks for the pit cover system and pit walls at the floor interface.

**Source:** RFP §3.4; App B (Shop) floor plan notes.

---

### R-003 — Dimensional Requirement: Pit Geometry

**Requirement:** The pit dimensions shall be determined by the Structural Engineer to meet the operational program. The conceptual floor plan (App B) indicates a service trench of approximately 24 ft wide by 100 ft long within the 130' × 100' New North Shop footprint. Final dimensions are subject to structural design.

**Note:** The 24' x 100' dimensions are read from the App B conceptual drawing and are design targets only. The Structural Engineer shall confirm or adjust these dimensions based on structural analysis, geotech findings, and coordination with the Architect and other disciplines.

**Acceptance Criteria — Pit Depth (TBD):** Minimum pit depth (working clearance from pit floor to underside of equipment at floor level) shall be TBD -- to be established by the Owner based on the equipment types to be serviced. The Structural Engineer shall confirm the minimum clearance target against frost depth and geotech constraints. No performance threshold is currently stated. (Lensing ref: A-004)

**Verification:** Issued-for-Construction (IFC) drawings shall show fully dimensioned plan, sections, and details of the pit.

**Source:** App B (Shop) conceptual floor plan — labeled dimensions; RFP §3.4.

**ASSUMPTION:** CSA A23.3 — Design of Concrete Structures is the applicable standard in Alberta. Location TBD (standard not directly accessible in source documents).

### R-008 — Geotechnical Integration

**Requirement:** The structural design of the service pit shall incorporate geotechnical parameters (bearing capacity, groundwater level, frost depth, soil classification) from the Geotechnical Investigation Report (DEL-008-01).

**Verification:** Structural calculation package (DEL-002-10) shall reference the geotechnical report. If the geotechnical report is not yet available at time of drawing issue, design assumptions shall be explicitly stated on drawings with a note to confirm against geotechnical findings.

**Source:** RFP §4.8.2; Decomposition DEL-008-01; ASSUMPTION — standard structural design practice.

---

### R-009 — Waterproofing and Drainage Provisions

**Requirement:** The structural design shall include or accommodate waterproofing details and drainage provisions for the below-grade pit, appropriate to Alberta frost and groundwater conditions.

**Note:** The type and extent of waterproofing is TBD pending geotech findings and coordination with PKG-006 (Plumbing Design). The structural drawings shall identify where waterproofing is required and indicate the system type in general notes (system to be specified in coordination with other disciplines or by specification in DEL-002-12).

**Drainage Slope Requirement (Lensing ref: X-001):** The pit floor structural design shall incorporate a drainage slope direction and gradient, coordinated with PKG-006 (Plumbing Engineer). The slope direction, gradient (TBD), and drain/sump location are structural design decisions that shall be shown on the structural drawings. Procedure Step 4.2 identifies this coordination requirement. The slope direction and gradient are currently TBD pending plumbing coordination.

**Verification:** Drawings reviewed for waterproofing/drainage notation at pit base and walls. Drainage slope direction and gradient shall be shown on the pit floor slab detail.

**Source:** ASSUMPTION — standard below-grade structural design practice; geotech dependency; Procedure Step 4.2.

---

### R-010 — Coordination of Pit Access

**Requirement:** The structural drawing set shall show provisions for personnel access into and egress from the pit (stairs, ladder, or combined system), including any structural framing or embedments required to support the access element.

**Note:** The access configuration is TBD and subject to coordination with the Architect (PKG-001) and applicable code provisions for below-grade working areas.

**Verification:** IFC drawings show pit entry/egress structural details.

**Source:** ASSUMPTION — required for safe occupancy per Alberta OH&S Code; RFP §3.4 functional description.

---

### R-011 — Design Life

**Requirement:** The service pit structural design shall be consistent with the intended design life of the building. The Datasheet Conditions section states ASSUMPTION: 50-year building design life per NBC. If the pit design life differs from the building design life, this shall be documented and the implications for material durability, waterproofing system selection, and rebar cover shall be addressed.

**Note:** This requirement is currently an ASSUMPTION carried from the Datasheet. The Owner and/or Structural Engineer shall confirm the intended design life. Design life affects concrete durability requirements (exposure class per CSA A23.1 — ASSUMPTION: likely applicable), waterproofing system service life, and minimum rebar cover. (Lensing ref: D-001)

**Verification:** Design life stated in calculation package (DEL-002-10) and on drawing general notes; durability provisions (exposure class, cover, concrete mix) consistent with stated design life.

**Source:** Datasheet Conditions ("ASSUMPTION: consistent with 50-year building design life per NBC"); ASSUMPTION — standard structural practice.

---

### R-012 — Confined Space Compliance Determination

**Requirement:** The Structural Engineer, in coordination with an OH&S consultant (if required), shall determine whether the service pit qualifies as a "confined space" under the Alberta OH&S Code and document the determination.

**Note:** Guidance P-4 raises the confined space question. If the pit is classified as a confined space, additional design requirements may apply, including: minimum access dimensions, forced ventilation sizing, emergency egress provisions, and signage/labeling requirements. The compliance determination shall be documented and referenced in the structural drawing general notes.

**Verification:** Written determination of confined space classification, referenced in general notes on IFC drawings.

**Source:** ASSUMPTION — Alberta OH&S Code applicable (location TBD); Guidance P-4. (Lensing ref: A-003)

### Expected Drawing Sheet Content (ASSUMPTION — standard structural drawing set for a below-grade pit)

The drawing set for DEL-002-06 is expected to include (at minimum):

| Sheet Type | Content |
|---|---|
| Plan — Pit Layout | Overall pit plan showing dimensions, wall thickness, cover system extent, embedments, penetrations |
| Sections | Transverse and longitudinal sections through the pit showing wall height/depth, slab thickness, edge conditions, pit-to-floor-slab interface |
| Wall Details | Reinforcement details for pit walls (vertical and horizontal rebar, lap splices, hook conditions) |
| Floor Slab Detail | Pit floor slab reinforcement, drainage slope (if applicable), sump location |
| Cover/Grating System | Structural detail for pit cover (grating support angles, embedded plates, cover frame) |
| Edge Condition Details | Pit edge at shop floor slab — thickened slab, edge beam, or haunch as required |
| Access Detail | Structural framing or embedments for stairs/ladder |
| Penetration/Blockout Schedule | Coordinated penetrations for ventilation, electrical, plumbing |
| General Notes | Design standards, material specifications, concrete strength, reinforcement grade, geotechnical reference |
