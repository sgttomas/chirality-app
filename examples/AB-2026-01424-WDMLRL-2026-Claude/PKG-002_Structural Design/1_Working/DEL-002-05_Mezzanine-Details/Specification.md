## 1. Scope

### 1.1 What This Specification Covers

This specification governs the production of the **Mezzanine Framing Details** drawing set (DEL-002-05) for the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop (WDMLRL), Camrose County, Alberta.

The mezzanine framing details shall cover the structural design and detailing of:
- The overhead mezzanine structure spanning over the Parts Room, Utility Room, and Wash Bay in the New North Shop addition.
- Mezzanine framing members (beams, joists, columns, and bracing as required).
- Mezzanine deck or floor system.
- Mezzanine guardrails and edge protection.
- Stair structure providing access from the main floor to the mezzanine level.
- All connections, anchorages, and embedments into or onto the primary concrete structure.

## 2. Requirements

### 2.1 Functional Requirements

**REQ-DS-001** — The mezzanine structure shall span over the Parts Room, Utility Room, and Wash Bay in the New North Shop, as shown conceptually on the Appendix B floor plan.
Source: R-01 §3.4; R-04 (App B notes and floor plan).

**REQ-DS-002** — The mezzanine structure shall be load-bearing and capable of supporting heavy storage items, specifically oil totes and oil pumping equipment, in addition to standard occupancy live loads.
Source: R-01 §3.4 ("Mezzanine storage above utility and parts room capable of handling heavy items such as several oil totes and oil pumping equipment"); R-04 (App B notes: "Load Bearing Over Parts Room + Utility For Storage").

**REQ-DS-003** — Stair access to the mezzanine level shall be provided. The stair location is indicated conceptually on the Appendix B floor plan ("Stairs to Mezzanine" label near utility room / cistern area boundary).
Source: R-04 (App B floor plan).

**REQ-DS-004** — The mezzanine framing details shall be fully coordinated with the primary concrete structure (35' ceiling) of the New North Shop addition.
Source: R-01 §3.4 ("Concrete Structure (35' Ceiling)"); R-04 (App B notes).

**REQ-DS-005** — The mezzanine and stair design shall comply with Alberta Building Code (ABC) requirements for occupancy classification, live loads, guardrail height and strength, stair geometry, and all other applicable provisions.
Source: R-01 §3.3.2 ("The building design must adhere to all applicable building codes and regulations"); R-01 §3.3.2 ("The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes").

**REQ-DS-006** — The mezzanine framing details drawing set shall be included in the Issued for Construction (IFC) drawing set, signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.
Source: R-01 §3.3.2 ("All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta").

### 2.2 Design Requirements

**REQ-DS-007** — The mezzanine live load shall be determined by the Structural Engineer of Record based on the intended use (heavy storage: oil totes, pumping equipment) and shall meet or exceed the minimum live load requirements in the Alberta Building Code for the applicable storage occupancy. The design live load shall be explicitly stated on the drawings.
Source: R-01 §3.4; ASSUMPTION: ABC minimum storage live load provisions applicable (specific clause TBD — location TBD).

**REQ-DS-008** — All steel framing members, if used, shall be designed in accordance with CSA S16 — Design of Steel Structures (current edition per permit requirements).
Source: ASSUMPTION: likely applicable to industrial mezzanine steel framing in Alberta (location TBD — confirm code edition with permit authority).

**REQ-DS-009** — All connections to the concrete structure (column base plates, ledger connections, embeds) shall be designed in accordance with CSA A23.3 — Design of Concrete Structures (current edition per permit requirements).
Source: ASSUMPTION: location TBD — confirm with permit authority.

**REQ-DS-010** — Mezzanine guardrails shall meet ABC requirements for height (minimum 1,070 mm for industrial occupancies — ASSUMPTION: location TBD in ABC) and be designed to resist the applicable horizontal design load per ABC. **The specific horizontal design load value (e.g., 0.75 kN/m concentrated at top of rail, or as specified in the applicable ABC edition) shall be stated on the guardrail detail drawings** (X-003).
Source: ASSUMPTION based on NBC/ABC guardrail provisions (location TBD). The specific load value is TBD pending confirmation of the applicable ABC edition — Structural Engineer of Record to confirm per ABC and state the value explicitly on drawings.

**REQ-DS-011** — Stair geometry (rise, run, handrail height, landing dimensions) shall comply with ABC requirements for the occupancy classification.
Source: R-01 §3.3.2; ASSUMPTION: ABC stair geometry provisions applicable (location TBD).

**REQ-DS-012** — The mezzanine framing system shall accommodate required service penetrations (mechanical, plumbing, electrical) routed below or through the mezzanine, coordinated with PKG-003, PKG-004, and PKG-006. Penetrations shall not compromise structural integrity.
Source: ASSUMPTION based on multi-discipline coordination requirement; SOW-0012 final structural design.

### 2.3 Additional Design Requirements (Pass 3 Enrichment)

**REQ-DS-015** — Steel framing members within or above the wash bay zone shall be protected against corrosion per an applicable corrosion protection standard (e.g., CSA G189 or equivalent galvanizing/paint system specification). The corrosion protection approach shall be specified on the drawings (A-001).
Source: Guidance.md §3.3 identifies wash bay as a corrosive environment (R-01 §3.4 — wash bay / mud sump); Specification REQ-DS-008 references CSA S16 but does not address corrosion protection. ASSUMPTION: CSA G189 or equivalent is applicable — location TBD; confirm standard selection with Structural Engineer of Record.

**REQ-DS-016** — The mezzanine design shall explicitly address seismic design applicability. The Structural Engineer of Record shall determine whether seismic analysis is required for the mezzanine as a secondary structure within the primary concrete building, with reference to ABC seismic provisions. The determination shall be documented in the calculation package (DEL-002-10) and, if seismic design is required, the seismic design parameters shall be stated on the drawings. If seismic design is explicitly excluded, the exclusion rationale citing the applicable ABC clause shall be documented (A-002).
Source: REQ-DS-005 requires ABC compliance broadly; Datasheet §3 lists seismic zone as TBD. ASSUMPTION: seismic applicability determination is required per ABC for structural elements — specific clause TBD pending code edition confirmation.

**REQ-DS-017** — The Structural Engineer of Record shall address fire protection applicability for the steel mezzanine framing per the applicable ABC occupancy classification. If fire-rated protection is required, the fire protection system or rating shall be specified. If fire-rated protection is not required, the applicable ABC exemption shall be cited in the drawing general notes (C-001).
Source: Guidance.md §3.1 mentions fire protection as a consideration for steel framing (Option A disadvantages); no normative requirement in Pass 1/2 Specification addressed this. ASSUMPTION: fire protection applicability must be resolved per ABC occupancy classification — specific clause TBD.

**REQ-DS-018** — The mezzanine framing shall be designed to satisfy the applicable deflection limits per CSA S16 (for steel members) and/or ABC (for serviceability). Deflection limits shall be explicitly stated on the drawings (e.g., L/360 for live load, L/240 for total load, or as required by the governing code) (F-001).
Source: Procedure Step 3 action 7 mentions "check deflection limits per ABC / CSA S16" but no corresponding normative requirement existed in Pass 1/2. ASSUMPTION: deflection limits per CSA S16 Table 2 / ABC are applicable — specific values TBD pending code edition confirmation.

**REQ-DS-019** — Embed and anchor bolt locations for mezzanine column bases shall be verified and approved by the Structural Engineer of Record before the applicable concrete pour. The approval shall be documented (e.g., signed embed layout drawing or field verification record) (X-001).
Source: Guidance.md §2.2 recommends cast-in-place embeds; Procedure Step 7 mentions embed inspection before concrete pour; this item elevates the procedural check to a normative requirement. ASSUMPTION: cast-in-place embeds are the preferred connection method — if post-installed anchors are used instead, the approval sequence shall be adapted accordingly.

**REQ-DS-020** — The Structural Engineer of Record shall determine and document whether snow load transfer to the mezzanine framing applies. Although the mezzanine is internal to the building, the column load paths from the roof structure to the foundation may affect mezzanine column design. The determination shall be recorded in the calculation package (DEL-002-10) (E-002).
Source: Procedure Step 3 action 1 mentions "snow load transfer (if applicable)." The mezzanine is internal to a 35' building (R-01 §3.4), so direct snow load is unlikely, but roof load paths through shared columns must be evaluated. ASSUMPTION: determination is required — specific load path analysis TBD.

### 2.4 Deliverable Content Requirements

**REQ-DS-013** — The Mezzanine Framing Details drawing set shall include, at minimum:
- Mezzanine framing plan (plan view with member sizes, spacings, and dimensions).
- Mezzanine cross-sections sufficient to show clearances and structural depth.
- Framing connection details (beam-to-column, beam-to-wall/concrete, deck attachment).
- Stair framing details (stringers, treads, landings, handrail connections).
- Guardrail / edge protection details.
- Anchorage and base plate details at concrete structure interfaces.
- General notes identifying design basis, material grades, weld and bolt specifications, and applicable code references.
Source: ASSUMPTION — standard content for industrial mezzanine drawing set; consistent with IFC requirements per R-01 §3.3.2.

**REQ-DS-014** — All drawing sheets shall carry the Alberta P.Eng. stamp and signature of the Structural Engineer of Record.
Source: R-01 §3.3.2.
