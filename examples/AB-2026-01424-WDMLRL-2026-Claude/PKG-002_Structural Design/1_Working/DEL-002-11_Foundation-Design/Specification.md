# Specification — DEL-002-11 Foundation Design Package (Variable-Price)

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-002-11 covers the complete foundation design package for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition (the "Addition"), a new ~13,000 sq ft concrete structure (130 ft x 100 ft) with a 35-foot ceiling height, located at SW 14–44–21–W4, near Ferintosh, Alberta.

This deliverable addresses SOW-0019: "Design foundation (pricing negotiated post-geotech; estimate based on normal ground conditions)" (Source: Decomposition §3, SSOW Group C, SOW-0019).

The Foundation Design Package includes:
- Structural design of the building foundation system for the Addition
- Design basis document capturing assumed and confirmed geotechnical parameters
- Foundation plans and details sufficient to support IFC drawing production (DEL-002-02)
- Structural calculations supporting foundation sizing and bearing (DEL-002-10, which covers the broader calculation package including SOW-0019)
- Cost estimate basis document for variable-price negotiation (per RFP §4.8.2)

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Foundation construction (SOW-0023 — covered by DEL-010-01, PKG-010)
- Structural superstructure design beyond the foundation interface (DEL-002-03 through DEL-002-09)
- Geotechnical investigation (DEL-008-01, PKG-008)
- Civil grading and site drainage design (PKG-005)
- Renovation of the Old North Shop (PKG-017)
- Foundation design for any structure other than the Addition
- Foundation design for the existing Old North Shop or any renovation work (PKG-017) — scope boundary clarified to avoid confusion with shared-site or shared-foundation scenarios between the Addition and the Old North Shop renovation (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-001; Decomposition §7, PKG-017)

## Requirements

### REQ-001 — Design Basis: Normal Ground Conditions (Proposal Phase)
The foundation design estimate submitted with the Proposal shall be based on normal ground conditions without any deleterious subgrade material.
**Source:** RFP §4.8.2 — "The Proponent shall provide a cost estimate based on normal ground conditions, without any deleterious subgrade material."

### REQ-002 — Final Pricing Tied to Geotechnical Report
Final pricing for the foundation design and construction shall be negotiated with the successful Proponent upon availability of the complete geotechnical investigation report.
**Source:** RFP §4.8.2 — "Final pricing for the design/construction of the foundation will be negotiated with the successful Proponent when the complete geotechnical report is available."

### REQ-003 — Geotechnical Input
The foundation design shall incorporate findings from the geotechnical investigation (SOW-0001, DEL-008-01). If the geotech report is not yet available at the time of design, the Structural Engineer shall document all assumed geotechnical parameters and flag these explicitly for revision upon report receipt.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 — "A full complete geotechnical report will be required by the successful proponent to use during the design process."

### REQ-004 — Code Compliance
The foundation design shall adhere to all applicable Alberta building codes and regulations, including the Alberta Building Code (ABC) and National Building Code of Canada (NBC), as adopted and amended for Alberta, and the Alberta Safety Codes Act.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 — "The building design must adhere to all applicable building codes and regulations." RFP §3.3.2 — "The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes."

### REQ-005 — P.Eng. Stamp
All Issued for Construction (IFC) foundation drawings shall be signed and stamped by a professional engineer (P.Eng.) licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 — "All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta."

### REQ-006 — Structural Loads — Heavy Equipment Floor
The foundation design shall account for floor loads imposed by heavy tracked and packer equipment (minimum motor scraper class), consistent with the steel plate embedment locations shown on Appendix B (Shop). The Structural Engineer shall determine and document the design floor load value (in kPa or equivalent point load in kN) corresponding to the specified equipment class; the requirement is that the assumed load shall be not less than the heaviest equipment the Owner intends to operate on the floor.
**Source:** RFP §3.4 — "Steel plating in concrete at strategic locations to accommodate track and packer type heavy equipment." Appendix B (Shop) — steel plate embedment locations indicated on floor plan.

> **Enrichment note (X-002):** Strengthened to require the Structural Engineer to document a quantitative design load value. "Motor scraper class" is descriptive but different models have different weights; the requirement now mandates documentation of the assumed load. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-002; RFP §3.4)

### REQ-007 — Structural Loads — Crane
The foundation design shall account for reactions from two 5-tonne overhead cranes on trolley, including dynamic load factors per applicable CSA standards.
**Source:** RFP §3.4 — "2 – 5-tonne overhead cranes on a trolley." Appendix B (Shop) — crane locations shown.

### REQ-008 — Structural Loads — Mezzanine
The foundation design shall account for mezzanine loads; the mezzanine structure is load-bearing, capable of supporting heavy items including oil totes and pumping equipment, located above the parts room, utility room, and wash bay. The Structural Engineer shall document the assumed mezzanine design live load (in kPa) and justify this value based on the intended storage use.
**Source:** RFP §3.4 — "Mezzanine storage above utility and parts room capable of handling heavy items such as several oil totes and oil pumping equipment." Appendix B (Shop).

> **Enrichment note (X-003):** Strengthened to require documentation and justification of the assumed mezzanine live load, given that the mezzanine stores "heavy items including oil totes and pumping equipment" but no design live load is specified in the RFP. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-003; RFP §3.4)

### REQ-009 — Structural Loads — Service Pit / Trench
The foundation design shall account for the below-grade service pit/trench structure for under-equipment servicing, including retention of adjacent soil and any associated hydrostatic or surcharge loads.
**Source:** RFP §3.4 — "Ventilated and lighted service pit area for mechanics to perform servicing under equipment." Appendix B (Shop).

### REQ-010 — Cistern Load Interface
The foundation design shall account for the load imposed by the 50,000 L water storage cistern and its associated plumbing, whether the cistern is located within or adjacent to the building footprint.
**Source:** RFP §3.4 — "Minimum 50,000 L Cistern complete with the necessary plumbing." Appendix B (Shop) — cistern location indicated.

### REQ-011 — Wash Bay Interface
The foundation design shall account for the single enclosed wash bay (24 ft wide, motor scraper class), including floor slab drainage requirements and connection to the exterior mud sump.
**Source:** RFP §3.1, §3.4 — "Single enclosed bay dedicated for washing large equipment." Appendix B (Shop).

### REQ-012 — County Approval — Preliminary Design
Preliminary structural design (DEL-002-01, including preliminary foundation concept) shall be delivered to the County project team for approval prior to finalizing IFC foundation documents.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 — "Deliver Preliminary Design for the County project team to approve."

### REQ-013 — Coordination with IFC Drawing Set
The foundation design package shall be fully coordinated with all other IFC drawing disciplines (architectural, mechanical, electrical, civil, plumbing) before drawings are stamped and issued. Coordination shall include discipline-by-discipline review per Procedure.md Step 2.5 (architectural alignment, mechanical penetrations, plumbing interfaces, civil grading interface, crane base details), with evidence of coordination retained as a record.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 — "Complete final design... All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer."

> **Enrichment note (F-002):** Strengthened coordination requirement to specify discipline-by-discipline approach and evidence retention, aligning with the more detailed Procedure Step 2.5. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-002; Procedure.md Step 2.5)

### REQ-014 — Completion Deadline and Schedule Milestones
All design work (including IFC foundation drawings) shall be completed in sufficient time to support construction completion on or before December 31, 2026. The Structural Engineer and Project Manager shall identify specific milestone dates for: (a) proposal-phase foundation cost estimate readiness (tied to proposal submission date), and (b) IFC foundation drawings completion (tied to construction start for PKG-010 Foundation Construction). Milestone dates shall be documented in the project schedule.
**Source:** RFP §3.1 — "All Work pertaining to the Design Build... must be completed on or before December 31, 2026."

> **Enrichment note (D-002):** Strengthened to require identification of specific milestone dates rather than the generic "in sufficient time" phrasing. Actual dates remain TBD — to be determined by Project Manager and Structural Engineer based on project schedule. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-002; RFP §3.1)

### REQ-015 — Cost Estimate Submission (Proposal Phase) — ASSUMPTION
ASSUMPTION: The Foundation Design Package shall include or reference a variable-price cost estimate breakout (per RFP §4.8 Proposal Section 3.2 "Foundation Design & Construction") that is separate from the firm-price scope, clearly identified as a normal-conditions estimate.
**Source:** RFP §4.8.2; Decomposition §5 OBJ-006 — "Firm-price scope and variable-price foundation scope must both be managed within the contractual framework."

### REQ-016 — Settlement Analysis
The foundation design shall include settlement analysis demonstrating that total and differential settlement are within acceptable limits for the structure type (heavy industrial maintenance shop with overhead cranes, mezzanine, and below-grade service pit). Settlement limits shall be stated and justified by the Structural Engineer.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — derived from Procedure.md Step 2.3 item 2 ("Settlement analysis: confirm acceptable differential settlement") and standard structural engineering practice for this structure type. No explicit RFP requirement for settlement analysis was identified; however, settlement analysis is a standard requirement for foundation design under NBC/CSA A23.3 practice.

> **Enrichment note (C-003):** Added as a formal requirement. Procedure.md Step 2.3 already prescribes settlement analysis as a calculation task, but no corresponding Specification requirement existed. This requirement formalizes the Procedure's implied obligation. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md C-003; Procedure.md Step 2.3 item 2)

### REQ-017 — Concrete Materials and Methods
Concrete materials, mix design, placement, and testing for the foundation shall conform to CSA A23.1/A23.2 (Concrete Materials and Methods of Concrete Construction / Test Methods and Standard Practices for Concrete). The Structural Engineer shall specify the concrete exposure class, minimum compressive strength, and any special requirements (e.g., air entrainment for freeze-thaw resistance) appropriate for the project location and below-grade exposure conditions.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — derived from CSA A23.1/A23.2 being listed in the Standards table as applicable. RFP §3.3.2 requires adherence to all applicable building codes and regulations; CSA A23.1/A23.2 governs concrete materials for structures designed under CSA A23.3.

> **Enrichment note (F-001):** Added as a formal requirement. CSA A23.1/A23.2 was listed in the Standards table but had no corresponding requirement invoking it. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-001; Standards table — CSA A23.1/A23.2 row)

### REQ-018 — Assumption Tracking and Resolution
The Structural Engineer shall maintain a consolidated register of all ASSUMPTION items in the foundation design (including but not limited to: bearing capacity, frost depth, foundation type, groundwater condition, code editions, equipment loads). Upon receipt of the geotechnical report (DEL-008-01) and confirmation of other pending inputs, each assumption shall be formally validated, revised, or flagged for re-design. The assumption register shall be documented in the Design Basis Document.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — derived from the presence of numerous ASSUMPTION-tagged items across Datasheet.md and Specification.md, combined with REQ-003 (which requires flagging assumptions for revision). This requirement formalizes the tracking mechanism.

> **Enrichment note (X-004):** Added as a formal requirement to address the verification gap: no consolidated mechanism existed to track and resolve all ASSUMPTION items post-geotech. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-004; Datasheet.md Attributes — multiple ASSUMPTION entries; REQ-003)
