# Procedure — DEL-002-01 Preliminary Structural Design

---

## 1. Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to **produce** the Preliminary Structural Design deliverable (DEL-002-01) — a design presentation for submission to Camrose County for review and approval — and to manage the approval cycle. It is intended for use by the Structural Engineer and the Design-Build project team.

The output of this procedure is an approved preliminary structural design that authorizes the Structural Engineer to proceed with final structural design (DEL-002-02 through DEL-002-12).

## 3. Steps

### Step 1 — Review All Source Documents

1. Read and understand RFP §3.1, §3.3.2, §3.4 in full.
2. Review App B (Shop) conceptual floor plan in detail. Record all noted requirements and dimensions.
3. Confirm understanding of the 35 ft concrete structure requirement, two 5-tonne crane requirement, service pit, mezzanine, steel plate embedments, wash bay, and foundation variable-price basis.
4. Identify any ambiguities or gaps in the source documents. Record these as open items for resolution with the project team or for presentation to the County at the preliminary design review. (Refer to Guidance.md Conflict Table for known conflicts: CON-001, CON-002.)

**Verification:** Source review checklist or engineer's notes confirm all RFP design requirements have been reviewed.

---

### Step 2 — Establish Governing Code Framework

1. Identify the applicable edition of the National Building Code of Canada (NBC) and Alberta Building Code (ABC) for this project, as confirmed with the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ — Camrose County building department).
2. Identify applicable CSA standards (CAN/CSA A23.3, CAN/CSA S16, and others as required by the structural system chosen).
3. Identify Alberta Safety Code requirements relevant to structural work.
4. Document the code framework in the preliminary design narrative — this becomes the code commitment for all subsequent structural design work.

**Verification:** Code framework list included in preliminary design presentation document.

---

### Step 3 — Establish Structural System Concept

1. **Main building structural system:** Select and document the proposed structural system for the New North Shop Addition (e.g., tilt-up concrete, precast, cast-in-place). Justify the selection in terms of suitability for the 35 ft ceiling height, the 130 ft × 100 ft footprint, and construction schedule constraints (refer to Guidance §4.1).
2. **Structural grid:** Establish a preliminary column/wall grid consistent with the App B (Shop) floor plan, accommodating:
   - Two drive-through repair bays (24 ft wide openings)
   - Wash bay (24 ft wide)
   - Parts room (~400 sq ft), utility room, office
   - Service pit / trench location
   - Crane runway alignment (two 5-tonne cranes)
3. **Roof framing concept:** Propose a preliminary roof framing system spanning the building (material and approximate spanning direction TBD — refer to Guidance CON-002).
4. **Lateral load resistance system:** Identify the approach for wind and seismic lateral resistance (shear walls, bracing, moment frames — TBD based on system selection).

**Verification:** Preliminary structural system narrative drafted.

---

### Step 4 — Develop Conceptual Designs for Key Structural Elements

Develop each of the following to a conceptual/sketch level:

| Element | Key Design Actions |
|---|---|
| Crane runway support | Determine runway beam span concept; identify column/wall bracket arrangement for two 5-tonne cranes; confirm crane hook clearance under roof structure (ASSUMPTION: clearance verification requires knowing crane model — Structural Engineer to use standard 5-tonne crane dimensions as placeholder) |
| Service pit / trench | Establish pit plan dimensions consistent with App B (Shop); determine depth based on under-equipment servicing clearance requirements (ASSUMPTION: depth TBD; standard service pit depths range 1.2–1.5 m — Structural Engineer to propose); design preliminary wall and slab concept; note groundwater/drainage dependency on geotech |
| Mezzanine structure | Establish mezzanine plan extent (above parts room, utility room, wash bay); propose structural system (steel framing, concrete slab, composite — TBD); establish preliminary design live load (ASSUMPTION: Structural Engineer to propose; typical storage loads 4.8 kPa / 100 psf or as appropriate for oil tote/pump equipment weights) |
| Steel plate embedments | Mark preliminary embedment zones on structural floor plan consistent with App B (Shop) conceptual drawing; note plate sizing and embedment details as TBD at this stage (to be finalized in DEL-002-08) |
| Wash bay enclosure | Confirm structural integration of wash bay walls and roof with main building; confirm steel plate floor requirement; note drain opening provisions (coordinate with plumbing: SOW-0027b) |
| Stair structure | Identify stair location and preliminary structural concept (steel or concrete stair to mezzanine); confirm with architectural preliminary design |
| Entrance/exit door openings | Confirm structural framing at all overhead door and personnel door openings (lintels, headers) |
| Foundation concept | Describe proposed foundation type based on normal ground conditions assumption; state explicitly that this is a variable-price element subject to revision following geotechnical investigation (SOW-0001); identify any special foundation considerations for service pit, cranes, or mezzanine columns |

**Verification:** Preliminary sketches or annotated drawings exist for each element.

---

### Step 5 — Coordinate with Other Disciplines

1. Share preliminary structural grid and building section concept with the Architect (PKG-001) for cross-check with architectural floor plan (DEL-001-01).
2. Coordinate crane runway beam depth and ceiling height with the Mechanical Engineer (PKG-003) to confirm no conflicts with HVAC ductwork, makeup air units, or exhaust systems.
3. Coordinate service pit structural provisions with the Mechanical Engineer (ventilation) and Electrical Engineer (lighting) per SOW-0028.
4. Coordinate mezzanine slab provisions with Mechanical and Plumbing as needed.
5. Coordinate wash bay floor drain openings with Plumbing Engineer (SOW-0027b).
6. Identify any structural implications for Old North Shop renovation scope (mezzanine, washroom, locker/change room — SOW-0071–SOW-0073).

**Verification:** Coordination log or meeting notes confirm cross-discipline review has occurred. Additionally, coordination effectiveness shall be verified by one of the following (X-002):
- Coordination sign-off from each discipline lead confirming no unresolved structural interface issues, OR
- A coordination issues register with all items either resolved or explicitly deferred to final design stage with documented rationale.

> **Note (X-002):** Occurrence of coordination activities is necessary but not sufficient. The verification must confirm that coordination actually resolved issues or achieved alignment, not merely that meetings took place.

---

### Step 6 — Prepare Preliminary Design Presentation Package

Assemble the deliverable for County submission. Typical content (refer also to Specification §5.2):

1. **Cover page** — project name, deliverable ID (DEL-002-01), date, prepared by (Structural Engineer firm name). **P.Eng. seal/signature at preliminary stage: TBD — decision required (C-002).** Specification REQ-017 states P.Eng. seal is "Not applicable at preliminary stage" (applying only at IFC stage per SOW-0018). This Procedure step previously assumed a P.Eng. seal was required on the preliminary design, which contradicts the Specification. Resolution: Project Manager and Structural Engineer to confirm whether P.Eng. seal is required on the preliminary submission or only at IFC stage.
2. **Structural system narrative** — written description of proposed system, materials, load path, code framework.
3. **Preliminary floor plan** — annotated structural plan showing grid, columns/walls, crane runways, service pit, mezzanine extent, steel plate zones.
4. **Preliminary building sections** — at least one transverse and one longitudinal section confirming 35 ft ceiling height, crane clearance, mezzanine level.
5. **Foundation narrative** — conceptual foundation approach; variable-price statement; assumptions; geotechnical dependency.
6. **Key element summaries** — crane runway concept, service pit concept, mezzanine concept (with preliminary design load), steel plate embedment zones, wash bay structural integration.
7. **Inter-discipline coordination notes** — key interfaces and requirements noted.
8. **Open items / assumptions list** — all ASSUMPTIONs and TBDs requiring County ruling or future resolution (including CON-001 and CON-002 from Guidance.md Conflict Table).

**Verification:** Presentation package assembled and reviewed internally by Structural Engineer before submission.

---

### Step 7 — Internal Quality Review

1. Structural Engineer in responsible charge reviews all preliminary documents for completeness, consistency with RFP requirements, and internal consistency.
2. Verify all mandatory requirements (REQ-001 through REQ-017 in Specification.md) are addressed, even if only at conceptual level.
3. Confirm all known conflicts (CON-001, CON-002) are flagged in the presentation for County ruling.
4. Project Manager reviews for completeness relative to contract requirements.

**Verification:** Internal review sign-off by Structural Engineer in responsible charge.

---

### Step 8 — Submit to County for Approval

1. Submit the preliminary structural design presentation to Camrose County per the project communication protocol (via County project manager — SOW-0086, SOW-0087; County project manager identity confirmed at project award per SOW-0303).
2. Record the submission date.
3. Coordinate with other discipline preliminary designs (DEL-001-01 through DEL-006-01) to ensure a coordinated multi-discipline preliminary design package is presented to the County (ASSUMPTION: multi-discipline coordinated submission is preferred; submission timing to be confirmed by project team).

**Verification:** Submission confirmation received from County (email, letter, or meeting record).

---

### Step 9 — Manage County Review and Approval Cycle

1. Respond to County comments or questions regarding the preliminary structural design.
2. If revisions are required:
   - Revise the preliminary design to address County concerns.
   - Resubmit for approval.
   - Record all revision cycles.
3. Obtain written (or otherwise documented) County approval to proceed to final structural design.
4. Record County approval confirmation and date.

**Verification:** County written approval (or documented approval per project communication protocol) is on file. This is the gate condition for proceeding to final structural design (DEL-002-02 through DEL-002-12).
