## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce the Structural Specification (DEL-002-12) for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. The Structural Specification is a normative design document that establishes material, performance, and workmanship requirements for the structural systems of the New North Shop and related renovations to the Old North Shop.

The procedure is organized around the design-build delivery model under CCDC 14–2013, where the Proponent (Design-Builder) is responsible for completing the design. [Source: RFP §1.0, §2.7]

## Steps

### Step 1 — Confirm Scope and Identify Governing Standards

1.1 Review RFP §3.4 (Design Requirements) and Appendix B (conceptual floor plan) to confirm all structural elements in scope for DEL-002-12 (see Specification.md, Scope section).

1.2 Confirm which elements are excluded (foundation design — DEL-002-11; MEP; civil).

1.3 Confirm the applicable building code edition and Alberta Safety Code requirements with the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ). Record confirmed code edition in the specification cover sheet.

1.4 Compile the list of applicable standards (NBC, CSA A23.3, CSA S16, CMAA, Alberta Safety Codes Act). Update the Standards table in Specification.md with confirmed references.

### Step 2 — Obtain and Review Geotechnical Report

2.1 Obtain the completed geotechnical investigation report from PKG-008 (DEL-008-01).

2.2 Review geotechnical report for:
- Allowable bearing pressures
- Groundwater conditions
- Frost depth
- Soil classification
- Recommendations for foundation type

2.3 Record geotechnical parameters applicable to the superstructure-foundation interface in the Structural Calculation Package (DEL-002-10). Reference these values in the Structural Specification (DEL-002-12) as foundation performance requirements.

2.4 If geotechnical conditions reveal deleterious subgrade material, flag as a variable-price scope item per RFP §4.8.2 and coordinate with Owner/PM.

### Step 3 — Obtain Crane Manufacturer / Supplier Specifications

3.1 Identify the overhead crane supplier (2 × 5-tonne overhead cranes on trolley). Obtain manufacturer-provided data sheets including: rated capacity, bridge span, end truck wheel loads, runway gauge, and CMAA service class.

3.2 If crane supplier is not yet selected: prepare structural specification requirements for the crane runway based on preliminary assumed parameters. Include a note requiring the contractor to verify design against final crane data and resubmit for engineering review if crane selection changes.

[Source: RFP §3.4 "2 – 5-tonne overhead cranes on a trolley"]

### Step 4 — Establish Design Loads

4.1 Determine and document all applicable structural design loads in accordance with the applicable NBC edition:
- Dead loads (self-weight, superimposed)
- Live loads (floor, roof, occupancy — including mezzanine heavy storage)
- Snow loads (location: SW 14–44–21–W4, near Ferintosh, Alberta)
- Wind loads
- Seismic loads
- Crane runway loads (including impact factors per CMAA)
- Track/packer equipment loads at steel embedment locations
- Service pit edge loads (vehicle surcharge)
- Wash bay mud sump hydrostatic and surcharge loads

4.2 Document all load derivations in the Structural Calculation Package (DEL-002-10).

4.3 Confirm mezzanine design live load with Owner, given the intended storage of oil totes and oil pumping equipment.

### Step 5 — Prepare Specification Sections

5.1 Organize the Structural Specification using MasterFormat format (or equivalent). At minimum, the following divisions are anticipated: ASSUMPTION.

| Division | Content |
|---|---|
| Division 03 — Concrete | Cast-in-place concrete, reinforcing steel, formwork, floor slab, service pit, footings |
| Division 05 — Metals | Structural steel (crane runway girders, mezzanine framing if applicable), steel embedments, anchor bolts, miscellaneous metals |
| Division 31 — Earthwork (coordination reference only) | Reference to civil/geotechnical scope for earthwork; not structural-authored |

5.2 For each specification section, include:
- Part 1 — General (scope, references, submittals, quality assurance)
- Part 2 — Products (materials, fabrication requirements)
- Part 3 — Execution (installation, tolerances, inspection)

5.3 Ensure all specification requirements trace back to either RFP requirements (REQ-001 through REQ-012 in Specification.md) or applicable code clauses.

### Step 6 — Coordinate with Other Disciplines

6.1 Issue a structural coordination package to the Mechanical, Electrical, and Architectural design leads identifying:
- Structural penetrations required for crane electrical conduit
- Service pit ventilation duct openings through pit walls or floor
- Openings in mezzanine floor for equipment hatch (if required)
- Wash bay drain trench locations relative to floor slab design
- Structural framing heights for coordination with HVAC clearances

6.2 Record coordination items and resolution in the project coordination log.

6.3 **Verify coordination package delivery and closure:**
   - 6.3.1 Confirm that the structural coordination package (Step 6.1) was issued to all required disciplines (Mechanical, Electrical, Architectural, Civil, Plumbing).
   - 6.3.2 Record the date of issuance and the recipients in the coordination log.
   - 6.3.3 Set a response deadline (ASSUMPTION: 10 business days or as required by the project schedule).
   - 6.3.4 Track responses received and outstanding. Follow up on non-responses.
   - 6.3.5 Confirm that all coordination items are resolved or documented as open items with an agreed resolution path before proceeding to Step 8 (Internal Review).
   - 6.3.6 Close the coordination log entry for DEL-002-12 when all items are resolved.
   [Source: Lensing D-002]

6.4 Confirm Old North Shop mezzanine renovation scope with Architect (DEL-001-10 Renovation Plans). Obtain as-built information if available.

### Step 7 — Conduct Structural Assessment of Old North Shop Mezzanine

7.1 Conduct a site visit to the existing Old North Shop to assess the existing mezzanine structure.

7.2 Acquire as-built drawings of the Old North Shop using the following procedural pathway:
   - 7.2.1 Request as-built drawings from the Owner (Camrose County). Contact the Design-Build PM to initiate the request.
   - 7.2.2 If the Owner does not have as-built drawings, request from the original building contractor or architect (if known).
   - 7.2.3 Check municipal building records for filed construction drawings.
   - 7.2.4 If as-built drawings cannot be obtained from any source, explicitly record their absence in the structural assessment report and proceed with field measurements as the baseline (see Step 7.3).
   - 7.2.5 If as-built drawings are obtained, verify their accuracy against field conditions before relying on them for structural assessment.
   [Source: Lensing A-004]

7.3 Determine if the existing mezzanine can support the post-renovation loads or if upgrades are required.

7.4 Incorporate findings into the Structural Specification as renovation requirements.

### Step 8 — Internal Review and P.Eng. Signing

8.1 Complete the Structural Specification draft and submit for internal quality review within the Structural Engineer's firm.

8.2 Address review comments.

8.3 Issue for interdisciplinary review (share with Architect, Mechanical, Electrical, Civil, and Plumbing Engineers for coordination check).

8.4 Address coordination comments.

8.5 Structural Engineer (P.Eng., licensed in Alberta) reviews the final document and signs/stamps the Structural Specification as part of the IFC document package.

### Step 9 — Submit as Part of IFC Package

9.1 Assemble the Structural Specification with the full IFC drawing set (DEL-002-02 through DEL-002-09) and Structural Calculation Package (DEL-002-10).

9.2 Submit the complete PKG-002 IFC package to the Owner and to the AHJ as required for building permit.

[Source: RFP §3.3.2 — County representative must be present for all inspections and provided with inspection reports]
