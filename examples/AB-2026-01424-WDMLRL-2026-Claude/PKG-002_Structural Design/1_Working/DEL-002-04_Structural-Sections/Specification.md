# Specification — DEL-002-04 Structural Sections & Details

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-002-04 (Structural Sections & Details) is a Drawing Set forming part of the final Issued for Construction (IFC) structural drawing package for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. It provides cross-sectional views and construction-level detail drawings that convey structural member sizes, connection geometry, reinforcing patterns, embedment configurations, and interface conditions that cannot be fully communicated on plan drawings alone.

This deliverable covers:
- Structural cross-sections through the building shell (walls, roof, floor slab at critical locations)
- Typical and atypical section details for the concrete structure with 35 ft clear ceiling height
- Structural details for key components: crane runway/support interface, mezzanine framing connections, service pit/trench walls and floor slab, wash bay structure, steel plate embedments, stair structure
- Enlarged plan details and connection details as required by the structural design
- Referenced relationship to companion deliverables DEL-002-03 (Framing Plans), DEL-002-05 (Mezzanine), DEL-002-06 (Service Pit), DEL-002-07 (Crane Support), DEL-002-08 (Embedment Plan), DEL-002-09 (Stairs)

**Note:** The boundary between this deliverable and DEL-002-05 through DEL-002-09 is at the discretion of the Structural Engineer's drawing organization. Some details may be combined in this set or split into dedicated detail drawings. The key requirement is that all structural elements identified in SOW-0012 are fully detailed across the PKG-002 drawing set collectively.

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Foundation details (covered by DEL-002-02 Foundation Plan)
- Architectural section views not pertaining to structural members (covered by PKG-001)
- Mechanical, electrical, or plumbing penetration routing details (covered by PKG-003, PKG-004, PKG-006)
- Structural calculation narratives (covered by DEL-002-10 Calculation Package)
- Written material or product specifications (covered by DEL-002-12 Structural Specification)
- Civil/grading sections (covered by PKG-005)

## Requirements

### R-001 — IFC Drawing Standard
**Requirement:** All Structural Sections & Details drawings shall be issued as Issued for Construction (IFC) drawings, signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer (P.Eng.) licensed in the Province of Alberta.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-002 — Concrete Structure at 35 ft Clear Ceiling
**Requirement:** Sections shall reflect a concrete structural system with a minimum clear ceiling height of 35 ft in the main shop area.
**Source:** R-01 §3.4; R-04 Appendix B notes
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-003 — Main Shop Bay Widths
**Requirement:** Sections cutting through the repair bays shall show two drive-through repair bays, each 24 ft wide, consistent with App B floor plan.
**Source:** R-04 Appendix B floor plan; R-01 §3.1
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-004 — Wash Bay Structural Section
**Requirement:** Sections and details shall fully define the structural enclosure of the single enclosed wash bay (24 ft wide, motor scraper-sized) including overhead door opening, walls, roof integration, and steel plate floor.
**Source:** R-01 §3.1, §3.4; R-04 Appendix B
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-005 — Service Pit / Trench Structural Details
**Requirement:** Structural sections and details shall define the below-grade service pit/trench structure including retaining walls, floor slab, and any structural provisions for ventilation and lighting penetrations. Pit dimensions (width annotated ~24 ft, length ~100 ft per App B) shall be confirmed by design.
**Source:** R-01 §3.4; R-04 Appendix B ("Service Trench" annotation)
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Note:** Exact depth of service pit is TBD — not stated in RFP or App B. Depth shall be determined by the Structural Engineer based on functional requirements and geotech report.

### R-006 — Mezzanine Structural Details
**Requirement:** Sections and details shall define the load-bearing mezzanine structure above the parts room, utility room, and wash bay. The mezzanine shall be capable of supporting heavy items including oil totes and pumping equipment. Design live load shall be determined by the Structural Engineer to satisfy this functional requirement.
**Source:** R-01 §3.4; R-04 Appendix B
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Note:** Specific mezzanine live load value is TBD — the RFP states "heavy items (oil totes, pumping equipment)" without quantifying a design load. ASSUMPTION: Structural Engineer will establish a design live load (e.g., per NBCC Table 4.1.5.3 storage occupancy) sufficient for this application.

### R-007 — Stair Details
**Requirement:** Structural details for the stair providing access to the mezzanine shall be included in the PKG-002 drawing set (this deliverable or DEL-002-09).
**Source:** R-04 Appendix B ("Stairs to Mezzanine" annotation)
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-008 — Steel Plate Embedment Details
**Requirement:** Detail drawings shall show the type, size, and installation requirements for steel plates embedded in the concrete floor at strategic locations for tracked and packer-type heavy equipment. Locations shall be coordinated with DEL-002-08 Embedment Plan.
**Source:** R-01 §3.4; R-04 Appendix B (multiple "Steel Plate" annotations)
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Note:** Steel plate dimensions and thickness are TBD — not quantified in RFP or App B.

### R-009 — 5-Tonne Overhead Crane Support Structure Details
**Requirement:** Structural details shall define the crane runway beam, brackets, and column interface for supporting two 5-Tonne Overhead Cranes on trolley. Crane support details may appear in this deliverable or DEL-002-07.
**Source:** R-01 §3.4; R-04 Appendix B ("5 Ton Crane" x 2 annotated; "Gantry" is App B alias per Vocabulary Map D-001)
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Note:** Crane manufacturer and runway geometry are TBD — final details require crane supplier information (ASSUMPTION). Canonical term: "5-Tonne Overhead Crane" (lensing item B-004).

### R-010 — Code Compliance
**Requirement:** All structural sections and details shall comply with applicable Alberta building codes and Alberta Safety Codes in force at time of design. **The specific code edition(s) governing this deliverable shall be confirmed with the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) and recorded on the drawing general notes sheet prior to IFC issue.**
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2, §3.4
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Note:** Specific code editions are TBD — not stated in RFP. The current text says "applicable Alberta building codes" without identifying the edition, which creates compliance ambiguity. ASSUMPTION: Alberta Building Code (current edition) and referenced CSA standards (CSA A23.3 for concrete, CSA S16 for steel elements) apply. The Structural Engineer should confirm the specific edition with the AHJ (Camrose County / applicable Safety Codes authority) (lensing items A-001, A-003).

### R-011 — Coordination with Architectural and MEP Disciplines
**Requirement:** Structural sections shall be coordinated with architectural sections (PKG-001), mechanical penetrations (PKG-003), plumbing penetrations (PKG-006), and electrical conduit sleeves (PKG-004) to ensure all structural penetrations and sleeve requirements are captured in IFC drawings.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2 (coordinated final design); ASSUMPTION: standard multi-discipline IFC coordination requirement
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-012 — Preliminary Design County Approval
**Requirement:** Structural sections and details are final-design documents; they shall not be produced for IFC until the preliminary structural design (DEL-002-01) has received County approval.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-013 — Concrete Construction Method Selection (ASSUMPTION)
**Requirement:** The concrete construction method (cast-in-place, tilt-up, precast, or hybrid) shall be selected and documented as a mandatory design decision preceding detail drawing production. The selected method shall be confirmed jointly by the Structural Engineer and Design-Builder and recorded in the project design record before Phase 2 detail drawing commences.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — inferred from R-01 §3.4 ("concrete structure" without specifying method); Guidance P-03 identifies construction method as a prerequisite to detail types; Procedure Step 4 references this decision.
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION — lensing item A-002)
**Note:** No RFP clause explicitly mandates this as a formal requirement, but the construction method fundamentally determines the detail types produced in this deliverable. Elevated to a requirement based on its impact on downstream work.

### R-014 — Revision Management (ASSUMPTION)
**Requirement:** Drawing revisions shall be tracked using a defined revision numbering scheme. Each sheet shall carry a revision block recording revision number, date, description of change, and approval. The revision scheme shall be consistent across the PKG-002 structural drawing set.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — standard IFC drawing practice; Procedure Step 17 references "IFC revision designation (e.g., Rev. 0 IFC)" but no governing requirement was identified in R-01 or other accessible sources.
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION — lensing item X-003)
**Note:** Revision management is essential for an exhaustive implementation record, particularly given multi-discipline coordination and potential two-stage issue approach (Guidance T-02).

### R-015 — Foundation Interface Verification (ASSUMPTION)
**Requirement:** Structural sections showing wall and column elements shall include the top-of-foundation reference elevation and the interface condition between superstructure and foundation. These elements shall be explicitly cross-referenced to DEL-002-02 (Foundation Plan) to confirm dimensional and detailing consistency at the interface.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — inferred from Guidance C-05 ("interface must be explicitly cross-referenced between the two deliverables"); standard structural drawing practice.
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION — lensing item X-004)
**Note:** Without this requirement, a binding decision on interface completeness between DEL-002-04 and DEL-002-02 cannot be made.

**Note (lensing item F-004):** The following criteria define what constitutes a "complete" DEL-002-04 set for appraisal purposes. Without this definition, readiness assessment is subjective.

A complete DEL-002-04 drawing set is one where:

1. **Sheet coverage:** All sheet types listed above (or their equivalent in companion deliverables DEL-002-05 through DEL-002-09) are present in the PKG-002 drawing set collectively.
2. **Section cut coverage:** Every section cut line shown on DEL-002-03 (Structural Framing Plans) has a corresponding section sheet in this deliverable or a cross-reference to the relevant companion deliverable.

5. **Drawing index:** A complete sheet list / drawing index accompanies the issued set.
