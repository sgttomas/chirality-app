# Procedure — DEL-002-02 Foundation Plan

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce the Foundation Plan drawing set (DEL-002-02) for the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill. It covers: input gathering, preliminary design, interdisciplinary coordination, final design based on geotechnical data, IFC drawing production, review, and issue.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Mobilize and Gather Inputs

1.1 Obtain all prerequisite documents listed above that are currently available (RFP, Appendix B, site survey when available).

1.2 Confirm the column grid and structural bay layout from the conceptual floor plan (Appendix B — Shop). Note: New North Shop is 130 ft wide x 100 ft deep with bay widths of 24 ft, 18 ft (multiple), 16 ft, and 24 ft (wash bay) as shown on App B.

1.3 Contact the crane supplier to obtain preliminary crane runway beam reactions (vertical wheel loads, horizontal side thrust, longitudinal braking) for two 5-tonne overhead cranes. These are required for column and footing design. (Crane supplier selection may be TBD — use conservative preliminary loading for preliminary design.) **Sequencing note:** Clarify whether preliminary (conservative) crane loads are sufficient for the preliminary foundation design (Step 2) or whether confirmed supplier data is a hard prerequisite before Step 2 can begin. If preliminary loads are acceptable for Step 2, establish a target date by which confirmed crane supplier data must be received to support final design (Step 6). (Source: Lensing item A-004.)

1.4 Contact the plumbing, mechanical, and electrical engineers to obtain:
- **Plumbing:** cistern type and location (buried, semi-buried, or slab-mounted), sump drain locations in repair bays and wash bay, below-slab plumbing rough-in locations.
- **Mechanical:** service pit ventilation duct routing and dimensions affecting pit structural sizing.
- **Electrical:** service pit lighting and power requirements, and any embedded conduit requirements in the slab-on-grade. (Source: Guidance C-3; RFP §3.4 — "ventilated and lighted service pit"; Lensing item F-002.)

1.5 Contact the civil engineer to confirm the slab-on-grade finish elevation and the exterior grade elevation at the wash bay mud sump.

Source: RFP §3.3.2 (fully coordinated design); Decomposition PKG-002.

### Step 2 — Establish Preliminary Foundation Design (Normal Ground Conditions)

2.1 Develop a preliminary column grid consistent with the conceptual bay layout and the architectural program.

2.2 Estimate column loads based on the structural system (35 ft concrete structure, crane loads, mezzanine loads, roof loads). Use conservative tributary area assumptions.

2.3 Prepare a preliminary foundation type selection using the normal-ground-conditions assumption (RFP §4.8.2):
- Assume conventional spread footings and/or continuous grade beams.
- Assume frost depth appropriate for central Alberta (ASSUMPTION: approximately 1.2 m to 1.5 m below final grade — confirm per applicable code edition and local AHJ guidance; specific value TBD).
- Assume adequate bearing capacity for normal soils (ASSUMPTION — specific value TBD pending DEL-008-01).

2.4 Document all preliminary assumptions in writing. These assumptions will form the basis of the variable-price cost estimate required by RFP §4.8.2 and will be incorporated into the foundation drawing general notes.

Source: RFP §4.8.2; ASSUMPTION (standard preliminary design practice).

### Step 3 — Produce Preliminary Foundation Plan Drawings

3.1 Prepare preliminary foundation plan drawings sufficient to support the Preliminary Structural Design presentation (DEL-002-01) and County review/approval.

3.2 Preliminary drawings must show at minimum:
- Column grid with column designations.
- Approximate footing locations and types.
- Service pit location and preliminary extents.
- Steel plate embedment locations (per App B — Shop).
- Cistern slab/foundation provision.
- Wash bay location and mud sump interface.
- Mezzanine column/wall support locations.
- Crane column locations.

3.3 Submit preliminary drawings as part of DEL-002-01 (Preliminary Structural Design) for County approval.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; Decomposition DEL-002-01.

### Step 4 — Obtain County Approval of Preliminary Design

4.1 Present the Preliminary Structural Design (DEL-002-01) to the County project team for review and approval as required by RFP §3.3.2.

4.2 Incorporate County review comments into the preliminary foundation design.

4.3 Record County approval in writing before proceeding to IFC drawing production.

Source: RFP §3.3.2.

### Step 5 — Receive and Integrate Geotechnical Report

5.1 Receive the completed Geotechnical Investigation & Report (DEL-008-01) from the Geotechnical Engineer.

5.2 Review the following data from the geotechnical report:
- Soil stratigraphy and bearing capacity.
- Groundwater depth and any dewatering requirements.
- Frost depth recommendation.
- Foundation type recommendation.
- Any special conditions (e.g., expansive soils, deleterious subgrade material, settlement risk).

5.3 Assess whether geotechnical findings are consistent with the normal-ground-conditions assumption used in the preliminary design. If conditions differ materially:
- Update the foundation type, bearing size, depth, and/or reinforcement as required.
- Initiate the post-geotechnical cost reconciliation process with Camrose County per RFP §4.8.2 and the CCDC 14–2013 contractual mechanism.

5.4 Update the preliminary foundation design to reflect confirmed geotechnical parameters.

Source: RFP §4.8.2; Decomposition DEL-008-01, SOW-0019, OBJ-006.

### Hold Point — Geotech Integration Confirmation (between Step 5 and Step 6)

**HOLD POINT:** Before proceeding to IFC drawing production (Step 6), the Structural Engineer must confirm in writing that:
1. The geotechnical report (DEL-008-01) has been received and reviewed.
2. All foundation parameters (bearing capacity, frost depth, foundation type, soil conditions) have been updated in the design to reflect geotechnical findings.
3. Any material deviations from the normal-ground-conditions assumption have been identified and communicated to the Design-Builder project manager for cost reconciliation action per RFP §4.8.2.

This hold point implements Guidance Principle P-1 (Geotech-First, then IFC) and prevents premature IFC production before geotechnical integration is confirmed. The hold-point sign-off should be documented and filed with the project management records. (Source: Guidance P-1; Lensing item C-003.)

### Step 6 — Produce Final Foundation Plan Drawings (IFC)

6.1 Produce final IFC-quality foundation plan drawing set incorporating:
- Final column grid.
- All footing types, dimensions, elevations, and reinforcement (per structural calculations DEL-002-10).
- Grade beams (if applicable) — dimensions, elevations, reinforcement.
- Slab-on-grade — thickness, reinforcement, control joint layout, and embedded items.
- Service pit — plan extents, structural provisions (detailed further in DEL-002-06).
- Steel plate embedment locations (coordinated with DEL-002-08).
- Cistern foundation provisions.
- Crane column footing designations (detailed further in DEL-002-07).
- Mezzanine column/wall support footings.
- Wash bay slab-edge detail and mud sump interface.
- Sump drain rough-in openings (coordinated with plumbing).
- General structural notes: concrete strength (f'c — TBD), reinforcing grade, concrete cover, applicable codes, geotechnical report reference.
- Variable-price scope note referencing RFP §4.8.2 assumptions.

6.2 Confirm all embedments, penetrations, and openings against interdisciplinary drawings (architectural, mechanical, plumbing, electrical).

6.3 Confirm column grid consistency with all other PKG-002 structural deliverables.

Source: RFP §3.3.2, §3.4, §4.8.2; App B (Shop); Specification requirements R-001 through R-012.

### Step 7 — Structural Engineer Review and Stamp

7.1 The Structural Engineer (P.Eng. — Alberta) reviews the final foundation plan drawings for completeness, accuracy, and compliance with applicable codes.

7.2 The Structural Engineer signs and stamps all drawing sheets with their professional seal.

7.3 The signed/stamped drawings are issued as the IFC Foundation Plan set.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; Alberta Engineering and Geoscience Professions Act (ASSUMPTION: applicable; location TBD).

### Step 8 — Issue for Construction and Submit for Building Permit

8.1 Issue the stamped IFC Foundation Plan drawing set to the construction team (PKG-010 — Foundation Construction) and to the building permit authority (AHJ). **Note:** The AHJ identity for building permit review at this site (SW 14-44-21-W4) is TBD — Design-Builder to confirm whether the AHJ is Camrose County Safety Codes Officer or a provincial authority prior to permit submission. (Source: Specification R-005 note; Lensing item A-003.)

8.2 Provide the foundation plan drawings to the County project manager as required by RFP §3.3.2.

8.3 Ensure the foundation plan is included in the building permit application package (DEL-009-02).

Source: RFP §3.3.2; Decomposition DEL-009-02.
