# Specification — DEL-002-02 Foundation Plan

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-002-02 is the Foundation Plan drawing set for the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill, Camrose County, Alberta. It provides all Issued for Construction (IFC) foundation drawings required to support:

- Construction of the building foundation system (footings, grade beams, slab-on-grade, and related elements) for the ~13,000 sq ft concrete-structure maintenance shop addition with a 35 ft clear ceiling height.
- Foundation accommodation of: two 5-tonne overhead cranes, below-grade service pit (also referred to as "service trench" in some sources), mezzanine structure (over Parts Room, Utility Room, and Wash Bay), steel plate embedments at indicated floor locations, wash bay mud sump, and 50,000 L cistern.
- Variable-price scope: the foundation design must be compatible with post-geotechnical pricing reconciliation under the CCDC 14–2013 contractual framework (SOW-0019, OBJ-006).

Source: RFP §3.1, §3.3.2, §3.4, §4.8.2; App B (Shop); Decomposition §7 PKG-002.

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Structural framing plans, sections, and details above the foundation level (covered by DEL-002-03 through DEL-002-09).
- Geotechnical investigation and report (covered by DEL-008-01 — prerequisite).
- Foundation construction (covered by DEL-010-01 — PKG-010).
- Structural calculations (covered by DEL-002-10).
- Variable-price Foundation Design Package post-geotech (covered by DEL-002-11).
- Architectural, mechanical, electrical, civil, or plumbing design.

## Requirements

### R-001 — IFC Drawing Stamp
**Statement:** All Issued for Construction drawings in this set must be signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer (P.Eng.) licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Verification:** Confirm engineer stamp and signature on each sheet prior to issue. Verification must include confirmation that the stamping engineer holds a current licence issued by APEGA (Association of Professional Engineers and Geoscientists of Alberta), including confirmation of Alberta licence number. (Source: RFP §3.3.2 — "licensed to practice in the province of Alberta".)

### R-002 — Preliminary Design Approval
**Statement:** A Preliminary Structural Design (DEL-002-01) must be reviewed and approved by the County project team before Foundation Plan drawings are issued for construction.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Verification:** Confirm County approval of DEL-002-01 on record before IFC issue.

### R-003 — Geotechnical Data Integration
**Statement:** The final foundation design must be based on geotechnical investigation results (DEL-008-01). Preliminary drawings may be prepared using normal ground conditions assumption; however, foundation type, bearing capacity, and depth must be confirmed against the geotechnical report before IFC issue.
**Source:** RFP §4.8.2; Decomposition SOW-0019
**Priority:** Mandatory for IFC; preliminary drawings may use normal-ground-conditions assumption.
**Verification:** Confirm geotechnical report (DEL-008-01) has been received and foundation parameters updated accordingly.

### R-004 — Foundation System Adequacy for Structural Loads
**Statement:** The foundation must be designed to safely support all gravity, lateral, and dynamic loads from the superstructure, including:
- Two 5-tonne overhead cranes on trolley (crane column reactions).
- Mezzanine structure over Parts Room, Utility Room, and Wash Bay (load-bearing, capable of storing heavy items such as oil totes and oil pumping equipment).
- Steel plate embedments at floor locations serving track and packer-type heavy equipment.
- Service pit structure.
- 50,000 L cistern (dead load contribution and any uplift consideration).
- Concrete superstructure with 35 ft clear ceiling height.

**Source:** RFP §3.4; App B (Shop) notes
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Verification:** Structural calculations (DEL-002-10) confirm adequacy for all listed load cases.

### R-005 — Code Compliance
**Statement:** The foundation design must comply with all applicable building codes and regulations, including Alberta Safety Codes, as required by the RFP.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 ("The building design must adhere to all applicable building codes and regulations"), §3.3.2 ("The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes")
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Note — Specific Code Editions:** The specific editions of the Alberta Building Code (ABC) and National Building Code of Canada (NBC) governing this project are not identified in accessible sources. The RFP and Standards table both reference "current edition" without identifying the specific code edition year. **ASSUMPTION:** current editions applicable at time of permit application are required. Structural Engineer to confirm applicable edition(s) with the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) prior to commencing design, and record the confirmed edition(s) on the drawing general notes sheet. (Source: RFP §3.3.2; Lensing item A-001.)
**Note — AHJ Identity:** The Authority Having Jurisdiction for building permit review at this site (SW 14-44-21-W4) has not been identified in accessible sources. TBD — Design-Builder to confirm whether the AHJ is Camrose County Safety Codes Officer or a provincial authority. (Source: Lensing item A-003.)
**Verification:**
- **Leading verification:** Structural Engineer to complete a code compliance self-review checklist prior to building permit submission, confirming that the foundation design addresses all applicable code provisions for the confirmed edition(s). (ASSUMPTION — standard design practice; addresses verification gap identified by lensing item F-001.)
- **Lagging verification:** Building permit issued by AHJ confirms code compliance acceptance.

### R-006 — Service Pit Structural Design
**Statement:** The foundation plan must include structural provisions for the below-grade service pit (for mechanics to work under equipment) shown in the conceptual floor plan. The pit must be ventilated and lit; structural design must accommodate pit walls, floor, and any cover/grating system.
**Terminology note:** The canonical term for this element is "service pit" per the project vocabulary (Decomposition §2). Some source documents use "service trench" interchangeably; both refer to the same below-grade structure.
**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Ventilated and lighted service pit area for mechanics to perform servicing under equipment"); App B (Shop) — floor plan shows service pit
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Verification:** Service pit structural elements shown on foundation plan and confirmed in DEL-002-06 (Service Pit / Trench Structural Details).

### R-007 — Steel Plate Embedment Locations on Foundation Plan
**Statement:** The foundation plan (or coordinated embedment plan DEL-002-08) must show the location and structural provisions for steel plate embedments in the concrete floor slab. Locations must accommodate track and packer-type heavy equipment.
**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Steel plating in concrete at strategic locations to accommodate track and packer type heavy equipment"); App B (Shop) plan — steel plate locations indicated
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Verification:** Steel plate locations shown on plan; confirmed against DEL-002-08 (Embedment Plan).

### R-008 — Wash Bay Mud Sump Coordination
**Statement:** The foundation plan must coordinate with the exterior wash bay mud sump location. The sump is exterior to the building on the wash bay side.
**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Mud sump from wash bay on exterior of building for clean out with excavator"); App B (Shop) — plan shows Wash Bay Mud Sump exterior
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Verification:** Mud sump location and foundation interface shown on foundation plan or referenced drawing.

### R-009 — Variable-Price Scope Identification
**Statement:** The Foundation Plan drawing set must clearly identify the variable-price scope elements associated with SOW-0019 (foundation design/construction) to support post-geotech pricing reconciliation. Foundation design assumptions used in the preliminary estimate must be documented.
**Source:** RFP §4.8.2; Decomposition SOW-0019, OBJ-006
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Verification:** Drawing notes or general notes sheet identifies variable-price assumptions and references the post-geotech reconciliation mechanism.

### R-010 — Slab-on-Grade Provisions
**Statement:** The concrete slab-on-grade must accommodate: sump drains in repair bays, steel plate embedments, service pit opening, cistern provisions, and wash bay drainage connections. Slab thickness and reinforcement are TBD pending geotechnical report and structural calculations.
**Source:** RFP §3.4; App B (Shop); Decomposition SOW-0012
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Acceptance Criteria (TBD):** Minimum slab thickness range, reinforcement criteria, and/or reference to a design standard for industrial floor loading are TBD — to be established by the Structural Engineer based on equipment loading from the Datasheet (track and packer-type heavy equipment per RFP §3.4) and geotechnical report (DEL-008-01). The slab must be designed for concentrated loads from heavy tracked equipment (e.g., motor scraper class per RFP §3.4), not typical light-duty loading. (Source: RFP §3.4; Lensing item A-005.)
**Verification:** Slab specifications (including thickness, reinforcement, and design load basis) appear on foundation plan drawings; confirmed by DEL-002-10 structural calculations demonstrating adequacy for all load cases including concentrated equipment loads.

### R-011 — Coordination with Other Structural Deliverables
**Statement:** The Foundation Plan must be coordinated with all other structural drawing deliverables (DEL-002-03 through DEL-002-09) to ensure column grid, bearing locations, embedments, and openings are consistent.
**Source:** Decomposition PKG-002 deliverable list; RFP §3.3.2 (coordinated design)
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Verification:** Cross-check column grid and embedment locations against framing plans (DEL-002-03), details (DEL-002-04), mezzanine (DEL-002-05), service pit (DEL-002-06), crane supports (DEL-002-07), embedment plan (DEL-002-08).

### R-012 — Coordination with Other Disciplines
**Statement:** The Foundation Plan must be coordinated with: architectural floor plans (DEL-001-02), mechanical/plumbing plans (cistern slab opening, floor drain rough-ins), and civil/site grading (DEL-005-02) for slab elevation and drainage compatibility.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (fully coordinated final design across all disciplines); OBJ-003
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Verification:** Interdisciplinary coordination review completed before IFC issue.

### R-013 — Drawing Revision Control
**Statement:** Each IFC drawing sheet must include a revision block containing: revision number, date, and description of changes. All revisions to issued drawings must be tracked through this mechanism.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION (standard drawing practice); Lensing item X-003. Documentation section already anticipates a "Revision log" but no verification requirement was established.
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Verification:** Drawing sheet review — confirm revision block is present on each sheet and contains required fields.
