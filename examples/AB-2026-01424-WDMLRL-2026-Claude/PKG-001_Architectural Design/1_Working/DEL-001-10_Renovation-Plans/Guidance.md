## Purpose

DEL-001-10 exists to provide the architectural renovation drawing set that documents the design intent for the Old North Shop portion of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project. The Old North Shop is the existing building on site; while the primary project deliverable is the New North Shop addition (approx. 13,000 sq ft), the County has identified five distinct renovation scope items within the Old North Shop that must be completed as part of the same Design-Build contract.

These renovation drawings are the architectural record that communicates what is to be built (or rebuilt) to the construction team (PKG-017), the trade subcontractors, and the inspection authority. They must be produced by the Architect of Record, coordinated with all other design disciplines, and issued as part of the IFC drawing package before construction of the renovation scope commences.

Source: RFP §3.1, §3.3, §3.4; Decomposition §5 OBJ-001, OBJ-003; Decomposition §7 PKG-001, PKG-017.

### Long-term Facility Vision (Lensing item E-001)

All documents treat the Old North Shop renovation as a current scope requirement, but none address whether the renovated spaces have a defined service life or are intended as permanent improvements to the facility. This matters because it affects design decisions: material quality, infrastructure sizing (plumbing, electrical), and the level of finish appropriate for the renovation areas. If the renovated Old North Shop spaces are intended as interim use (e.g., temporary facilities during a future phase), a different design approach may be warranted than if they are permanent.

The County Project Manager should confirm whether the renovated Old North Shop spaces (kitchenette, mezzanine, washroom/locker room) are intended as permanent long-term improvements or interim facilities. This determination should be made before the Preliminary Design submission. (Source: Lensing item E-001 -- all four production documents; no source addresses long-term intent.)

## Principles

### 1. Renovation drawings are design-build output, not prescriptive client documents

The RFP states the County has a concept in mind and that this is a design/build project (RFP §3.4: "The County only has a concept in mind; therefore, it will be a design/build project"). The renovation drawings produced under DEL-001-10 are therefore the Architect's design response to the Owner's programmatic requirements — not reproductions of Owner-issued construction documents. The Architect retains design responsibility for how the requirements are met.

Source: RFP §3.4; CCDC 14–2013 (referenced in RFP §2.7; full text not reviewed — location TBD).

### 2. Coordination with demolition is a prerequisite

DEL-001-09 (Old North Shop Demolition Plans) covers the demolition scope for SOW-0070, SOW-0071, and SOW-0072. The renovation plans must be predicated on what the demolition plans show as remaining after demolition. The renovation drawing set should reference or reproduce the post-demolition existing conditions as its base. Discrepancies between the demolition scope and the renovation base conditions would constitute a design coordination failure.

Source: Decomposition §7 PKG-001 — DEL-001-09 and DEL-001-10 are companion deliverables.

### 3. The Old North Shop renovation is functionally connected to the New North Shop addition

Appendix B (Shop) shows the Old North Shop and New North Shop as a connected, combined facility. The renovation of the Old North Shop — particularly the mezzanine and washroom/locker room zone — must be spatially and functionally compatible with the new building. The Architect should consider circulation, egress, and fire separation between the existing and new portions when designing the renovations.

Source: App B (Shop) — combined floor plan showing 235 ft total building length, Old North Shop at west end (approx 105 ft width).

### 4. The five renovation scope items span multiple functional zones

Each SOW item is distinct but the items are spatially clustered:
- SOW-0070 (kitchenette) and SOW-0071 (mezzanine) are likely related or adjacent (the coffee room mezzanine is above the kitchenette zone or adjacent — see App B, west section of Old North Shop showing "2nd Level Mezzanine Coffee Room" adjacent to what appears to be the kitchenette/wash station area).
- SOW-0072 (washroom renovation), SOW-0073 (new locker room), and SOW-0074 (urinal expansion) are functionally co-located and should be planned as a single washroom/change room complex.

Source: App B (Shop) — plan shows "Wash Station," "2nd Level Mezzanine Coffee Room," and "Washroom" in the east-central section of the Old North Shop plan.

### 5. Preliminary design approval gates IFC production

The RFP requires Preliminary Design to be submitted for County approval before the final IFC design is completed (RFP §3.3.2). The renovation scope should be included in the Preliminary Design submission to secure County sign-off on the programmatic interpretation before proceeding to full IFC drawings.

Source: RFP §3.3.2.

## Considerations

### Existing Conditions Uncertainty

The RFP and Appendix B provide only a conceptual plan of the Old North Shop. The actual existing conditions (wall construction, structural framing, mezzanine structure, existing mechanical/plumbing/electrical rough-ins, floor levels, door/window sizes) are not documented in the available source materials. The Proponent was expected to attend a mandatory site meeting (RFP §3.2 — Tuesday, March 3, 2026) to observe existing conditions. As-built or measured drawings of the Old North Shop are not available in the accessible source materials for this deliverable.

**Implication for drawings:** The renovation plans must be based on actual field-verified conditions, not solely on the conceptual Appendix B plan. The Architect should conduct an existing conditions survey of the Old North Shop prior to drafting renovation drawings.

Source: RFP §3.2 (mandatory site meeting); App B (Shop) — labeled "conceptual drawing."

**Minimum as-built information threshold (Lensing item B-002):** The following minimum as-built information should be obtained before renovation design can proceed to the design intent stage (Procedure Step 2):
- Verified overall building dimensions (length, width, floor-to-floor and floor-to-ceiling heights)
- Mezzanine structural framing type, span, and condition assessment
- Existing washroom dimensions, fixture locations, and plumbing rough-in positions
- Existing kitchenette area boundaries and services (water, drain, electrical)
- Existing door and window inventory (locations, sizes, conditions)
- Existing mechanical/plumbing/electrical infrastructure serving renovation areas
- Photographic documentation of all renovation areas

ASSUMPTION: The above list defines the sufficiency threshold for proceeding with design. If any item cannot be obtained during the field survey, the Architect should document what is missing and assess whether design can proceed with stated assumptions or whether additional investigation is required. (Source: Guidance.md Considerations -- Existing Conditions Uncertainty; Procedure.md Step 1.)

### Structural Coordination for Mezzanine

The mezzanine renovation (SOW-0071) may involve changes to the mezzanine structure (floor, framing, guard rails, stair). The Appendix B note for the New North Shop references "Load Bearing Over Parts Room + Utility For Storage" — the Old North Shop mezzanine has a similar load-bearing function (coffee room use). Any structural modifications must be coordinated with PKG-002 (Structural Design). The renovation plan drawings should clearly identify any structural elements to be designed or verified by others.

Source: App B (Shop) — mezzanine noted; Decomposition §7 PKG-002.

### Washroom and Laundry Services — Plumbing Coordination

The washroom renovation (SOW-0072), urinal addition (SOW-0074), and laundry services in the locker room (SOW-0073) all involve plumbing tie-ins to the existing or new service system. The existing Old North Shop's plumbing configuration is not documented in the accessible source materials. The renovation plans must indicate plumbing rough-in locations and be coordinated with the Mechanical/Plumbing drawings (PKG-003/PKG-004).

The RFP references a 2,000 US Gallon septic holding tank (potentially removal and relocation of existing) for the overall project (RFP §3.4). The impact on the existing Old North Shop septic/drain connections is TBD.

Source: RFP §3.4; Decomposition SOW-0073 (laundry services).

The renovation design should address accessible washroom requirements if the renovation triggers accessibility compliance per the code. Confirm with the Authority Having Jurisdiction.

(Source: Guidance.md Considerations -- Accessibility; Specification.md Requirements section.)

### Fire Separation Between Old North Shop and New North Shop (Lensing item X-001)

Guidance Principle 3 mentions fire separation in passing but does not develop it as a design consideration. The Old North Shop (existing) and New North Shop (addition) are connected, and the renovation of the Old North Shop -- particularly at the mezzanine level and the washroom/locker room zone -- must address fire separation requirements under the applicable Alberta Building Code. Key considerations include:

- Whether a fire separation is required between the existing building and the new addition, and at what fire-resistance rating
- How the mezzanine renovation interacts with any required fire separation at the junction between old and new buildings
- Whether the washroom/locker room zone, which is near the junction, requires fire-rated enclosure or separation
- Egress path compliance through the connected building zones

These questions are code-dependent and require the confirmed code edition (see Specification REQ-006, Lensing items A-001/C-001) and AHJ input. The Architect of Record or a code consultant should address fire separation as a primary design consideration before the Preliminary Design submission.

Source: App B (Shop) — combined floor plan; Guidance Principle 3; ASSUMPTION: fire separation is likely applicable under Alberta Building Code for a renovation connected to a new addition. (Source location TBD for specific code clause.)

### Construction Phasing and Temporary Conditions (Lensing item X-002)

Guidance Trade-off 2 identifies that renovation occurs alongside new construction but no document currently provides phasing direction. The renovation drawings should address or reference the following:

- Whether the existing Old North Shop will remain occupied/operational during renovation, or whether it will be vacated
- If occupied, what temporary conditions (barriers, temporary services, dust control, noise mitigation) should be noted on the renovation drawings
- Sequencing considerations: whether renovation work should precede, follow, or run concurrently with the New North Shop construction
- Whether phasing notes or temporary conditions should appear on the renovation plan drawings or be addressed in a separate phasing plan

ASSUMPTION: Phasing direction is a design-build coordination decision. The design-build team should establish phasing and document any temporary conditions on the renovation drawings or in a project-wide phasing plan. The County should be consulted on occupancy requirements during construction. (Source: RFP §3.1 site context; Guidance Trade-off 2.)

### Kitchenette Size Constraint

The RFP specifies the kitchenette area as 250 sq ft (RFP §3.4). This is a programmatic size established by the Owner. The Architect should lay out the kitchenette to achieve approximately 250 sq ft of functional kitchenette space. Whether this is the total room area or the net functional area is not specified — ASSUMPTION: 250 sq ft is the gross kitchenette room area.

Source: RFP §3.4.

## Trade-offs

### Trade-off 1: Extent of Renovation vs. Replacement

For SOW-0071 (mezzanine) and SOW-0072 (washroom), the scope describes "renovation" of existing elements. The extent of renovation (cosmetic refresh vs. full rebuild) is a design-build decision. Retaining more of the existing fabric reduces cost and program time but may limit the quality of the finished product. Full replacement delivers a better integrated result but has higher cost and schedule impact.

ASSUMPTION: The design-build team should select the renovation extent that achieves the Owner's operational objectives within budget and schedule constraints. No budget figure is available in the accessible source documents to guide this trade-off.

Source: RFP §3.4 (conceptual intent); CCDC 14–2013 (stipulated price contract — location TBD).

**Evaluation framework gap (Lensing item C-002):** This trade-off currently identifies the renovation-vs-replacement decision but provides no evaluation framework for making it. A defensible competence standard for the "extent of renovation" decision should include criteria such as:
- Condition assessment results from the field survey (Step 1) — what is the current state of the mezzanine structure, washroom finishes, kitchenette area?
- Code compliance implications — does retaining existing fabric trigger code upgrade requirements that full replacement would avoid?
- Service life expectations — what is the expected remaining useful life of retained elements vs. the facility design life?
- Cost-benefit analysis — does the stipulated price contract provide a framework for evaluating renovation extent?

ASSUMPTION: The design-build team should develop evaluation criteria for the renovation extent decision and document the rationale for the chosen approach. The County Project Manager may have expectations about renovation quality that should inform this decision. (Source: Guidance.md Trade-off 1; RFP §3.4.)

### Trade-off 2: Sequencing Renovation Alongside New Construction

The Old North Shop renovations (PKG-017) occur within the existing building while the New North Shop addition is being constructed adjacent. Sequencing must account for occupancy of the existing shop during construction and safe access for workers. This may affect the renovation drawing package's phasing notes and temporary conditions shown on drawings.

Source: RFP §3.1 (site context); RFP §3.2 (existing building to be observed at mandatory site meeting).

### Trade-off 3: Washroom/Locker Room Cluster — Expansion Direction

The expansion of the existing washroom to add a urinal (SOW-0074) and a new locker/change room (SOW-0073) requires available floor area within or adjacent to the existing washroom. Appendix B shows limited space in the Old North Shop section. The direction of expansion (into adjacent spaces, into the mezzanine below zone, or via a new addition to the Old North Shop) is a design decision with architectural, structural, and schedule implications.

Source: App B (Shop) — existing washroom location shown; available adjacent space is TBD based on field survey.
