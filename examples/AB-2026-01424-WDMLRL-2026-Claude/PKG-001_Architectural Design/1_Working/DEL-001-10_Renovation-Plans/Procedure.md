## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and issue the Old North Shop Renovation Plans drawing set (DEL-001-10). It covers the workflow from existing conditions assessment through final Issued for Construction (IFC) document release, and identifies the verification checkpoints and records required at each stage.

The procedure addresses the drawing production process only. Construction execution of the renovation scope (SOW-0070 through SOW-0074) is governed by the PKG-017 construction deliverables.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Attend Mandatory Site Meeting and Conduct Existing Conditions Survey

1.1 Attend the mandatory site meeting (RFP §3.2 — Tuesday, March 3, 2026, 10:00 a.m. MST, SW-14-44-21-W4, Alberta). Record attendance.

1.2 During or following the site meeting, conduct a field survey of the Old North Shop to document existing conditions:
- Measure and record overall building dimensions (length, width, ceiling heights at lower level and mezzanine)
- Record existing washroom size, location, and configuration
- Record existing mezzanine/coffee room size, structural framing type, and condition
- Record existing kitchenette area size and location
- Record existing door and window locations, sizes, and conditions
- Record existing mechanical/plumbing rough-in locations (floor drains, supply lines, fixtures)
- Record existing electrical panel location and branch circuits serving renovation areas
- Photograph all areas to be renovated

1.3 Produce existing conditions base drawings (CAD/BIM) of the Old North Shop for use as the renovation drawing base.

Source: RFP §3.2; Guidance § Existing Conditions Uncertainty.

**Verification checkpoint (strengthened per Lensing item A-004):** This is a formal pass/fail gate, not an informal checkpoint. Before proceeding to Step 2, the following conditions must be verified and signed off by the Architect of Record:
- [ ] Existing conditions field survey is complete for all renovation areas
- [ ] Existing conditions base drawings (CAD/BIM) are produced and dimensionally accurate
- [ ] Base drawings have been reviewed and approved by the Architect of Record
- [ ] Drawing numbering convention and title block standards are established (Lensing item C-004)
- [ ] Any gaps in the existing conditions survey are documented with stated assumptions

If any item above cannot be satisfied, the gate does not pass and Step 2 shall not commence until the deficiency is resolved or a documented exception is approved by the Architect of Record. (Source: Procedure.md Step 1 Verification checkpoint; Lensing item A-004.)

### Step 2 — Establish Renovation Design Intent for Each SOW Item

2.1 **SOW-0070 — Kitchenette Renovation (250 sq ft)**
- Layout kitchenette to achieve approximately 250 sq ft (gross room area — ASSUMPTION; confirm with Owner if net area is intended)
- Establish program: counters, cabinetry, sink, appliance locations, ventilation
- Coordinate plumbing rough-in with PKG-003/PKG-004 Mechanical/Plumbing
- Coordinate electrical with PKG-005 Electrical

2.2 **SOW-0071 — Mezzanine Renovation**
- Establish extent of renovation: finishes, floor surface, guards/railings, stair upgrade (if required), ceiling
- Coordinate structural assessment/modifications with PKG-002 Structural if any structural changes are required
- Confirm live load capacity of mezzanine for coffee room use with Structural Engineer

2.3 **SOW-0072 — Washroom Renovation**
- Establish extent of renovation: new finishes, fixture replacement or upgrade, accessibility compliance review
- Coordinate plumbing rough-in with PKG-003/PKG-004
- Coordinate with SOW-0073 and SOW-0074 (washroom and locker room are co-located)

2.4 **SOW-0073 — New Locker/Change Room with Laundry**
- Establish locker/change room layout: lockers, benches, laundry appliance space (washer/dryer — ASSUMPTION: residential-type laundry pair or commercial unit)
- Identify available floor area within or adjacent to existing washroom for expansion
- Coordinate plumbing for laundry connections with PKG-003/PKG-004
- Identify direction of spatial expansion (from field survey findings — Step 1.2)

2.5 **SOW-0074 — Washroom Expansion with Urinal**
- Incorporate urinal fixture in washroom renovation plan
- Confirm plumbing connection feasibility with Mechanical/Plumbing design team
- Coordinate with SOW-0072 washroom renovation (same space)

Source: RFP §3.4; Decomposition §3-I; App B (Shop).

### Step 3 — Produce Preliminary Design Drawings

3.1 Draft preliminary renovation floor plan(s) incorporating the design intent from Step 2 for all five SOW items.

3.2 Ensure preliminary design is at sufficient detail to:
- Communicate the renovation layout and program to the County
- Identify major items requiring structural, mechanical, and electrical input
- Flag any conflicts or constraints requiring County direction (e.g., expansion direction for locker room, extent of mezzanine renovation)

3.3 Coordinate the preliminary renovation drawings with:
- DEL-001-09 Demolition Plans team (confirm demolition-renovation boundary)
- PKG-002 Structural (mezzanine, any new footings for locker room)
- PKG-003/PKG-004 Mechanical/Plumbing (kitchenette, washroom, laundry)
- PKG-005 Electrical (lighting, power, ventilation in renovated areas)

3.4 Submit Preliminary Design to the County project manager for approval (RFP §3.3.2 — "Deliver Preliminary Design for the County project team to approve").

**Verification checkpoint:** Written or recorded County approval of Preliminary Design received before proceeding to Step 4.

Source: RFP §3.3.2.

---

### Step 4 — Produce IFC Renovation Drawing Set

4.1 Advance preliminary drawings to full IFC (Issued for Construction) quality based on County-approved preliminary design and discipline coordination inputs.

4.2 The IFC renovation drawing set shall include at minimum:

| Sheet Content | Description |
|---|---|
| Existing Conditions Plan | Post-demolition base plan of Old North Shop (or reference to DEL-001-09) |
| Renovation Floor Plan(s) | New layout for all five SOW items, dimensioned and annotated |
| Enlarged Plans | Enlarged washroom / locker room plan (ASSUMPTION: required for plumbing coordination and code compliance detail) |
| Interior Elevations — Kitchenette | Elevation(s) of kitchenette showing cabinetry and equipment layout |
| Interior Elevations — Washroom/Locker Room | Elevation(s) of washroom and locker room renovation areas |
| Sections | Building sections through renovation areas as required to show mezzanine relationship and vertical clearances |
| Notes and General Information | Code references, material specifications by reference to DEL-001-11, coordination notes |

ASSUMPTION: The exact sheet list is subject to the Architect's professional judgment based on field survey findings and the complexity of the renovation. Additional sheets may be required.

4.3 Ensure all renovation drawings are cross-referenced to:
- DEL-001-07 Door & Window Schedule (for any new or modified doors/windows in renovation areas)
- DEL-001-08 Finish Schedule (for renovation area finishes)
- DEL-001-11 Architectural Specification (for material and workmanship requirements)

4.4 Conduct internal quality review of drawings prior to submission for stamp using the following checklist items (Lensing item A-005 — QA checklist content defined):

**Internal QA Checklist:**
- [ ] All dimensions verified against field survey base drawings
- [ ] Code compliance confirmed: egress paths, accessibility (if triggered — see Guidance Considerations), fire separation (see Guidance Considerations -- Fire Separation)
- [ ] Coordination confirmed: structural drawings (PKG-002) for mezzanine and any structural modifications
- [ ] Coordination confirmed: mechanical/plumbing drawings (PKG-003/PKG-004) for kitchenette, washroom, and laundry services
- [ ] Coordination confirmed: electrical drawings (PKG-005) for lighting, power, and ventilation in renovation areas
- [ ] Cross-references verified: DEL-001-07 (Door & Window Schedule), DEL-001-08 (Finish Schedule), DEL-001-11 (Architectural Specification)
- [ ] Coordination with DEL-001-09 (Demolition Plans) verified: post-demolition conditions match renovation base plan
- [ ] Drawing numbering convention and title block standards applied consistently across all sheets
- [ ] All notes, abbreviations, and symbols consistent with project standards
- [ ] All TBD items from the design process resolved or documented as exceptions
- [ ] Discipline coordination sign-offs obtained (see Specification REQ-009 verification, Lensing item X-004)

(Source: Procedure.md Step 4.4 and Records table; Lensing item A-005 — QA checklist was referenced but not defined.)

4.5 Submit drawings to P.Eng. (and Architect of Record as applicable) for review, seal, and signature (RFP §3.3.2).

Source: RFP §3.3.2, §3.4; Decomposition DEL-001-10 row; REQ-001 through REQ-009.

### Step 5 — Issue IFC Drawing Set

5.1 Issue the stamped IFC renovation drawing set to:
- County project manager
- General Contractor / Construction team (PKG-017)
- All trade subcontractors whose work involves the renovation areas
- Building permit authority (Camrose County development/building permit)

5.2 Ensure the IFC issue is recorded in the project document control log.

5.3 Submit drawings as required for building permit application (RFP §3.3.2 — "Obtaining a development permit and building permit from the County, and all applicable Safety Code Permits" is the Proponent's responsibility).

**Permit sequencing note (Lensing item D-001):** Clarify the building permit application timing relative to IFC drawing production and issuance to the construction team. Two approaches are possible:
- **Option A (Permit-first):** Submit drawings to AHJ for plan review and obtain building permit approval before issuing IFC drawings to the construction team. This ensures compliance confirmation before construction begins but may delay the construction schedule.
- **Option B (Concurrent):** Submit drawings to AHJ for plan review concurrently with issuing IFC drawings to the construction team for mobilization/procurement, with construction commencement contingent on permit approval.

ASSUMPTION: The appropriate sequencing depends on Camrose County's plan review timeline and the project schedule. The design-build team should confirm the AHJ's expected plan review duration and select the permit sequencing approach accordingly. Record the selected approach here once determined. (Source: Procedure.md Step 5.3; RFP §3.3.2.)

Source: RFP §3.3.2.
