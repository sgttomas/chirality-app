# Guidance — DEL-001-11 Architectural Specification

---

## Purpose

The Architectural Specification (DEL-001-11) exists to translate the County's design intent — expressed conceptually in the RFP and Appendix B drawings — into binding, normative requirements that the Design-Builder and subcontractors must satisfy during construction. It is the primary document through which the Architect communicates materials performance expectations, finish standards, and code compliance obligations across the architectural scope of the project.

This deliverable supports two project objectives:
- **OBJ-001** — Deliver a code-compliant, fully functional maintenance shop addition and associated renovations satisfying the County's operational program. (Source: Decomposition §5)
- **OBJ-003** — Produce complete, P.Eng.-stamped IFC design documentation across all disciplines before construction begins. (Source: Decomposition §5)

ASSUMPTION (best-effort mapping): These objectives are associated with DEL-001-11 because the decomposition's objective-to-deliverable table maps OBJ-001 and OBJ-003 to PKG-001 deliverables including DEL-001-11 explicitly. (Source: Decomposition §7, PKG-001 table.)

## Principles

### P-1: Operational Function Drives Specification Choices

The facility is a heavy-equipment maintenance shop servicing large landfill machinery (motor scrapers, tracked equipment, packer-type vehicles). Specification choices must support industrial-grade performance: high live loads, durable floor surfaces, large clearance heights, industrial ventilation, and corrosion-resistant finishes where applicable. The RFP emphasis on steel floor plating, 35 ft ceiling height, and 5-tonne overhead cranes signals that the Architect should specify materials capable of handling these conditions. (Source: RFP §3.1, §3.4, App B)

### P-2: Coordination with Structural Engineer is a Hard Dependency

The Architectural Specification does not govern structural performance, but it must be fully coordinated with the Structural Specification (DEL-002-12). Items such as mezzanine live loads, steel plate embedment details, and floor slab thickness affect both architectural and structural scope. Contradictions between the two specifications must be resolved before IFC issuance. (Source: RFP §3.3.2, SOW-0018)

### P-3: Renovation Scope Requires Existing Conditions Survey

The Old North Shop renovation items (SOW-0070–SOW-0074) are described at a high level in the RFP and conceptual drawings. The Architect must conduct or review an existing conditions survey of the Old North Shop before finalizing renovation specification requirements. Specifying finishes or performance requirements for renovation scope without existing conditions data risks conflict with as-built conditions. **This principle is operationalized in Procedure Step 2; a verification gate confirming survey completion should precede specification drafting for renovation scope (see C-003).** (Source: RFP §3.4, App B)

### P-4: Code Compliance is Non-Negotiable

The RFP states explicitly that the building design must adhere to all applicable Alberta building codes, regulations, and Alberta Safety Codes. The Architect must identify the applicable editions of these codes and integrate all relevant requirements into the specification. No waiver or substitution may be assumed. (Source: RFP §3.3.2)

### P-5: Design-Build Context Allows Architectural Latitude — Within Program Limits

The County has stated "the County only has a concept in mind; therefore, it will be a design/build project" (RFP §3.4). This means the Architect has latitude in specifying materials and approaches beyond the minimum program, but must satisfy all stated design requirements as non-negotiable minimums. Departures from the conceptual drawings (App B) must be justified and coordinated with the County.

## Considerations

### C-1: Floor Specification — Steel Plates and Concrete

The RFP requires steel plates embedded in the concrete floor at strategic locations for tracked and packer-type heavy equipment (RFP §3.4, App B). The Architectural Specification must define or cross-reference:
- Steel plate material grade and surface finish (coordinate with Structural Engineer)
- Plate dimensions and layout (coordinate with App B drawing locations)
- Transition detailing between plated and unplated floor areas
- Concrete floor finish specification for non-plated areas (industrial-grade, minimum requirements TBD per code and operational needs)

The County is responsible for supplying welding equipment specifications (RFP §3.4); the Architect should confirm this information is received before finalizing receptacle and plate location specifications.

### C-2: Wash Bay Specification

The enclosed wash bay must accommodate motor scraper-sized equipment (RFP §3.1, §3.4). Specifying bay width as 24 ft (App B) is necessary but not sufficient — the Architect should also address:
- Overhead door height (TBD — must clear motor scraper profile; specific height not stated in sources)
- Floor drain and sump connections (coordinate with plumbing and civil)
- Wall and floor surface finish resistant to wash water and detergents
- Mud sump connection to exterior (coordinate with Civil, DEL-005-07)

ASSUMPTION: Overhead door height will need to be confirmed with the County and specified based on equipment clearance requirements. No height is stated in RFP or App B.

### C-3: Mezzanine and Locker/Change Room Relationship

The Old North Shop renovation scope includes a new locker/change room with laundry services (SOW-0073) and a 2nd-level coffee room mezzanine renovation (SOW-0071). The App B drawing shows the Old North Shop mezzanine as "2nd Level Coffee Room." The Architect must clarify:
- Whether the new locker/change room is within the Old North Shop or in the new addition
- Whether the renovated 2nd-level mezzanine above the Old North Shop becomes the locker/change room or remains a coffee room
- The relationship between the new and old mezzanines

ASSUMPTION: The new locker/change room is constructed within or adjacent to the Old North Shop, separate from the mezzanine renovation, based on the separation of SOW-0071 and SOW-0073 in the decomposition.

### C-4: IFC Stamping Requirement

All IFC drawings must be signed/stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta (RFP §3.3.2, SOW-0018). In Alberta, architectural drawings for this scale and type of project may also require an Architect of Record (registered with AAAL — Alberta Association of Architects). The Architect should confirm their registration obligations and whether dual-stamp requirements apply. (ASSUMPTION: AAAL registration likely required for commercial/industrial building of this size; specific requirements TBD from regulatory authority.)

**TBD (C-002):** Confirm whether AAAL registration is required and whether dual-stamp (P.Eng. + Architect) requirements apply for this project type and scale. This affects ARCH-005 and the entire IFC issuance process. PROPOSAL: Architect to confirm with AAAL and APEGA.

### C-5: Kitchenette Renovation (SOW-0070)

The RFP specifies renovation of the 250 sq ft kitchenette area. The Architectural Specification should address finishes, fixture locations, and any plumbing coordination. Detailed scope for the kitchenette (e.g., cabinetry, countertops, appliances) is not stated in sources and will be TBD pending existing conditions survey and County input.

### C-6: Guarantee Period Implications

The guarantee period covers the construction period plus two years post-CCC (RFP §2.10). The Architectural Specification should specify materials and systems consistent with this guarantee obligation. Where manufacturer warranties are required, the specification should confirm that warranty terms align with the guarantee period.

**Rationale for material warranty alignment (F-004):** The 2-year post-CCC guarantee period (Datasheet Conditions; RFP §2.10) creates a binding obligation to rectify defects within 10 days of Owner instruction (SOW-0121). Material and system specification choices should be informed by this guarantee obligation as follows:
- Materials with manufacturer warranties shorter than the guarantee period create coverage gaps that transfer defect risk to the Design-Builder.
- The Specification should include warranty alignment requirements: specified materials and systems should carry manufacturer warranties of at least the construction period plus 2 years, or the Design-Builder must accept the coverage gap risk.
- Critical systems (roofing, envelope waterproofing, mechanical equipment) should be evaluated for warranty-guarantee alignment as part of specification review.

ASSUMPTION: This rationale is directional guidance; specific warranty alignment requirements should be established by the Architect based on industry practice and system-by-system risk assessment.

### C-7: Building Envelope and Thermal Performance (B-004)

Guidance T-1 notes that insulation and vapour barrier specifications must be appropriate for concrete construction in an Alberta climate. The Specification should address building envelope systems (Division 07 scope) including:
- Insulation type and R-value requirements per Alberta Building Code energy provisions
- Vapour barrier specification appropriate for concrete wall and roof assemblies
- Weather barrier / air barrier systems
- Roof membrane and drainage

These requirements are captured as ARCH-015 in the Specification. Procedure Step 4 (4.2, Division 07) identifies Thermal and Moisture Protection as a required specification division with "specific systems TBD." The Architect should specify envelope systems after confirming the applicable ABC energy code requirements and concrete construction details with the Structural Engineer.

## Trade-offs

### T-1: Concrete Structure vs. Pre-Engineered Steel Building

The RFP explicitly requires a concrete structure with 35 ft ceiling (RFP §3.4, App B). This is not a trade-off available to the Design-Builder — concrete is specified. The Architect should note that this structural choice affects thermal performance, anchoring details, and finish systems differently than a pre-engineered steel building would. Insulation and vapour barrier specifications must be appropriate for concrete construction in an Alberta climate. See Consideration C-7 and Specification ARCH-015 for building envelope requirements.

### T-2: Specification Depth vs. Design-Build Flexibility

A prescriptive specification reduces Design-Builder risk and provides greater Owner certainty, but may constrain cost-effective solutions. A performance-based specification provides Design-Builder flexibility but shifts acceptance risk to the inspection phase. For this project, the RFP approach is mixed: some elements are prescriptive (steel plates, cistern size, crane tonnage) while others are performance-described (high-recovery heating, load-bearing mezzanine). The Architectural Specification should maintain this distinction — be prescriptive where the RFP is prescriptive, performance-based where the RFP is not.

### T-3: Renovation Specification Under Uncertainty

Renovation scope (SOW-0070–SOW-0074) is difficult to specify without existing conditions data. Writing overly detailed specifications before the existing building is surveyed risks producing inapplicable requirements. The recommended approach is to issue preliminary specifications flagged as "subject to existing conditions survey" and update prior to IFC.

## Conflict Resolution Process (E-003)

> **Note:** When the human provides rulings for conflicts in the Conflict Table above, the following process should be followed to maintain document integrity:

1. **Record the ruling:** Update the "Human Ruling" column in the Conflict Table with the decision, date, and authority.
2. **Propagate to affected documents:**
   - Update Specification.md requirements and verification entries affected by the ruling.
   - Update Datasheet.md attributes or conditions affected by the ruling.
   - Update Procedure.md steps if the ruling changes procedural requirements.
3. **Maintain audit trail:** Each ruling should note:
   - Conflict ID resolved
   - Decision made (including any conditions or caveats)
   - Date of ruling
   - Authority (who made the decision)
   - Documents and sections updated as a result
4. **Cross-reference:** Each updated section in Specification, Datasheet, or Procedure should reference the Conflict ID and ruling that triggered the change.

ASSUMPTION: This conflict resolution process is directional guidance. The Architect and Project Manager should establish the formal change management process per project requirements.
