# Procedure — DEL-001-11 Architectural Specification

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps for **producing** the Architectural Specification document (DEL-001-11) as a complete, IFC-ready deliverable. It covers the sequence of activities the Architect follows from project initiation through final specification issue, consistent with the design-build contract requirements under CCDC 14-2013 and the County's RFP.

This procedure also includes brief notes on how the Architectural Specification is **used** by downstream parties (Design-Builder, subcontractors, inspectors).

## Steps

### Step 1: Project Initiation and Scope Confirmation

1.1 Confirm the architectural program against the RFP §3.1 and §3.4 requirements and Appendix B floor plan.
1.2 Confirm which Alberta Building Code edition and Safety Codes apply to this project. Obtain applicable code documents.
1.3 Confirm AAAL (Alberta Association of Architects) registration obligations and P.Eng. stamping requirements for this project type. (ASSUMPTION: likely required; specific obligations TBD from regulatory authority. See Guidance C-4, item C-002.)
1.4 Confirm County-owner scope split (aggregate supply vs. cement and prep) with the County project representative. (Note: use standardized term "aggregate" per E-004 terminology normalization; confirm specific material term with County.)
1.5 Attend mandatory site meeting (RFP §3.2 — site meeting required on March 3, 2026). Record attendance and document any site-specific observations relevant to architectural scope.
1.6 Note open items to be resolved: overhead door heights (B-001), locker/change room location, washroom urinal scope split (see Conflict Table in Guidance.md, CONF-001), utility room and office space minimum areas (B-002).

### Step 2: Existing Conditions Survey — Old North Shop

2.1 Coordinate with the surveyor and general contractor to obtain as-built information for the Old North Shop (105 ft x 40 ft existing building).
2.2 Document existing conditions of:
   - Kitchenette area (250 sq ft, SOW-0070)
   - 2nd-level mezzanine / coffee room (SOW-0071)
   - Washroom below mezzanine (SOW-0072)
   - Existing washroom facilities (SOW-0074)
   - Space available for new locker/change room (SOW-0073)
2.3 Record all findings; flag discrepancies between existing conditions and Appendix B conceptual drawing.
2.4 Document baseline data for the Old North Shop per Datasheet "Existing Conditions Baseline" section (E-002): floor area per space, structural condition, existing mechanical/electrical/plumbing systems, available space for locker/change room.

**Verification gate (C-003):** Before proceeding to Step 4 (Draft Specification) for renovation scope items (ARCH-040 through ARCH-044), confirm that the existing conditions survey has been completed and findings documented. If the survey is incomplete, renovation scope specification sections shall be drafted as "subject to existing conditions survey" placeholders only. See Guidance P-3.

### Step 3: Preliminary Design Coordination

3.1 Prepare Preliminary Architectural Design (DEL-001-01) for County approval per SOW-0010a.
3.2 Coordinate with structural, mechanical, electrical, plumbing, civil, and landscape disciplines during preliminary design.
3.3 Confirm mezzanine loads (in conjunction with Structural Engineer) to support specification of mezzanine structure and access.
3.4 Confirm wash bay and repair bay overhead door heights based on motor scraper equipment profile (confirm with County). Record confirmed heights for Datasheet update (B-001).
3.5 Obtain County approval of Preliminary Architectural Design before proceeding to final specification. Record the form and content of the approval (see D-001).

### Step 4: Draft Architectural Specification

4.1 Organize the specification using CSC MasterFormat or equivalent division structure. (ASSUMPTION: CSC MasterFormat is the standard for Alberta construction specifications; confirm with the County if a different format is required. See D-002 for status clarification.)
4.2 Address the following minimum specification divisions relevant to architectural scope:
   - Division 01: General Requirements (reference only — may be in contract or separate division)
   - Division 03: Concrete (floor slab specification, concrete mix design, steel plate embedment) — coordinate with Structural
   - Division 05: Metals (steel floor plate material, surface finish) — coordinate with Structural
   - Division 06: Wood, Plastics, Composites (if applicable — TBD based on interior scope)
   - Division 07: Thermal and Moisture Protection (insulation, vapour barrier, weather barrier, roofing — specific systems TBD per Alberta Building Code energy provisions and concrete construction requirements; see Guidance C-7 and Specification ARCH-015; ASSUMPTION: critical for Alberta climate)
   - Division 08: Openings (doors, frames, hardware, overhead doors — including repair bay 24 ft overhead doors, wash bay overhead door, parts room 6 ft roll-up door, entrance/exit doors)
   - Division 09: Finishes (floor, wall, and ceiling finishes for all spaces — industrial-grade in shop; TBD for office, washroom, kitchenette, locker room)
   - Division 10: Specialties (toilet accessories, lockers — TBD for washroom and locker room scope)
   - Division 11: Equipment (TBD — coordinate with County for Owner-supplied items)
4.3 For renovation scope (SOW-0070–SOW-0074), draft specification sections covering:
   - Demolition and selective removal requirements (coordinate with DEL-001-09)
   - Renovation finishes for kitchenette, mezzanine, and washroom areas
   - Locker/change room construction and laundry services rough-in
   - Washroom expansion for urinal (coordinate with plumbing; subject to CONF-001 resolution)
4.4 Flag all TBD items requiring additional information from the County, existing conditions survey, or discipline engineers.
4.5 Determine building occupancy classification per Alberta Building Code (ARCH-008). Record classification and applicable code sections. This determination drives plumbing fixture counts (ARCH-023 / F-001), fire separation, egress, and accessibility requirements (ARCH-009 / E-001).

### Step 5: Inter-Discipline Coordination Review

5.1 Circulate draft specification to Structural, Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing engineers for coordination review.
5.2 Confirm that architectural requirements do not conflict with:
   - Structural specification (steel plate grades, mezzanine loads, floor slab thickness)
   - Mechanical specification (service pit ventilation, wash bay exhaust, heating)
   - Electrical specification (service pit lighting, welding receptacle locations)
   - Plumbing specification (washroom, cistern, wash bay drains, locker room laundry)
5.3 Document and resolve all interdisciplinary conflicts before finalizing specification. Document the coordination mechanism used (meeting, written review, or shared model) and retain evidence of coordination per ARCH-007 and X-003.
5.4 Update Conflict Table in Guidance.md with any unresolved items requiring human ruling.

### Step 6: Code Review

6.1 Review drafted specification against applicable Alberta Building Code requirements (confirmed edition per A-001 and C-001).
6.2 Confirm occupancy classification (per Step 4.5) and applicable building requirements for the maintenance shop addition.
6.3 Confirm Alberta Safety Codes compliance for applicable systems (coordinate with discipline engineers for mechanical and electrical Safety Code items).
6.4 Confirm egress requirements for mezzanine level (stair configuration, guard/handrail requirements).
6.5 Confirm washroom facility requirements under Alberta Building Code (minimum fixture counts per occupancy classification and occupant load; accessibility requirements per ARCH-009 and E-001).
6.6 Document code compliance rationale. Include clause-level traceability to the confirmed ABC edition (C-001).

### Step 7: Final Review and Issuance

7.1 Conduct internal quality review of specification against the RFP §3.4 design requirements checklist (see Verification section of Specification.md).
7.2 Confirm that all minimum design requirements from RFP §3.4 are addressed.
7.3 Obtain P.Eng. stamp on all IFC documents per RFP §3.3.2 and SOW-0018. Confirm AAAL stamp requirements if applicable (C-002).
7.4 Issue specification as part of the IFC document package.
7.5 Submit building permit application to Camrose County permit authority (A-004). Track permit application status and schedule permit inspections.
7.6 Submit all inspection requests through the County project representative per RFP §3.3.2.
