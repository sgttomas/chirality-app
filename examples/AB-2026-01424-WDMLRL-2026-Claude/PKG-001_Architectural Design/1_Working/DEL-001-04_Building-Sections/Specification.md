## Scope

### Included

This specification governs the production of the Building Sections drawing set (DEL-001-04) for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition (New North Shop). Building Sections are vertical cut drawings that expose the internal spatial organization, structural relationships, floor-to-ceiling relationships, floor level conditions (including the below-grade service pit), mezzanine levels, overhead crane clearances, and wall/roof assembly interfaces of the proposed addition.

The drawing set shall provide sufficient information for:
- Structural coordination (slab, pit, mezzanine, roof, crane runways)
- Mechanical and electrical routing coordination (ceiling plenum, mezzanine clearances)
- Door and opening verification at section locations
- Construction of the building in accordance with IFC requirements

### Excluded

- Floor plans (DEL-001-02)
- Exterior elevations (DEL-001-03)
- Interior elevations (DEL-001-05)
- Reflected ceiling plans (DEL-001-06)
- Structural section and detail drawings (DEL-002-04)
- Service pit structural details (DEL-002-06)
- Mezzanine framing details (DEL-002-05)
- Renovation drawings for the Old North Shop (DEL-001-09, DEL-001-10), except where renovation interfaces with the addition are shown for context

### REQ-010 — Coordination with Other Disciplines

**Statement:** Building section drawings shall be coordinated with:

| Discipline | Interface |
|---|---|
| Structural (PKG-002) | Concrete structure, slab, pit, mezzanine framing, crane runway beams, roof structure |
| Mechanical (PKG-003) | HVAC equipment clearances, exhaust routing, makeup air penetrations |
| Electrical (PKG-004) | Crane power routing, high bay light mounting heights |

**Acceptance criteria for coordination review (ASSUMPTION):**
- A clash is defined as a spatial conflict between elements shown on the architectural section drawing and confirmed elements on a discipline drawing (structural, mechanical, or electrical).
- Clashes shall be categorized by severity: (a) hard clash (physical overlap), (b) clearance clash (insufficient clearance for maintenance or operation), (c) workflow clash (sequencing conflict for construction).
- "Unresolved" means a clash has been identified but no agreed resolution exists at the time of IFC review.
- At IFC issue, zero hard clashes and zero clearance clashes shall remain unresolved. Workflow clashes may remain open if documented with an agreed resolution path.

> **TBD (X-004):** The acceptance criteria above are ASSUMPTION-based. The Architect should confirm or revise these definitions and thresholds. Proposed authority: Architect. (Source: Specification Verification table, REQ-010 row.)
