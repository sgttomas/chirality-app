# Procedure — DEL-001-03 Exterior Elevations

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps for producing the Exterior Elevations drawing set (DEL-001-03) as part of the final architectural design for the New North Shop Addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop. It covers the workflow from prerequisite inputs through to IFC-ready drawing submission, including coordination checkpoints and verification.

The Exterior Elevations are produced by the Architect and form part of the IFC drawing package (SOW-0011, SOW-0018) to be signed/stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Confirm Preliminary Design Approval

Verify that DEL-001-01 (Preliminary Architectural Design) has received written County approval before committing to final elevation geometry.

- Check: County approval documentation is on file.
- If not approved: do not advance to final exterior elevations; present preliminary elevations as part of DEL-001-01 for County review.

Source: R-01 §3.3.2 ("Deliver Preliminary Design for the County project team to approve").

---

### Step 2 — Establish Elevation Reference Framework

2.1 Set the finished floor elevation (FFE) datum for the New North Shop Addition, coordinated with:
  - Civil grading plan (DEL-005-02) for approach grades and site drainage
  - Foundation design (DEL-002-02) for slab-on-grade or raised floor condition

2.2 Confirm or establish compass orientation of the building on site (coordinated with civil and site plan). Assign elevation tags (e.g., North Elevation, South Elevation, East Elevation, West Elevation) consistent with floor plan (DEL-001-02) orientation.

2.3 Confirm overall building footprint dimensions: 130' × 100' addition as indicated on conceptual drawings (R-04), subject to any adjustments from structural grid or site constraints.

Source: R-04 (Appendix B); R-01 §3.1, §3.4.

---

### Step 3 — Obtain Structural Height Parameters

3.1 Coordinate with Structural Engineer (PKG-002) to obtain:
  - Required interior clear height under crane hook (consistent with 35' ceiling height requirement — R-01 §3.4)
  - Crane rail elevation above FFE
  - Top-of-wall / parapet height
  - Mezzanine level elevation
  - Service pit/trench depth (for reference — shown in section, may appear on elevation at openings)
  - Wall construction depth (structural wall thickness affects exterior face dimension)

3.2 Confirm crane support column locations that may be visible as structural bays on the exterior elevation.

Source: R-01 §3.4; R-04; DEL-002-07 (Crane Support Structure Details).

**Note:** If structural information is not yet available, produce elevation with 35' ceiling height dimension as the governing constraint and mark crane-related heights as "COORD — see structural" pending structural confirmation.

---

### Step 4 — Draft All Exterior Elevation Views

Produce a minimum of four elevation drawings (one per face of the addition). The following content must be included on each applicable elevation face:

**All faces:**
- Finished floor elevation (FFE) reference line
- Finished grade line at the building face (from civil grading)
- Top of wall / parapet line with dimension from FFE
- Overall building width dimension (plan dimension as seen on that face)
- Exterior material keynotes (referenced to Finish Schedule DEL-001-08; use "TBD" if not yet selected)
- Drawing title, scale, sheet number

**Face with drive-through repair bay doors (ASSUMPTION: north face — verify on site):**
- Two overhead door openings — 24' wide (verify clear opening with structural — see CONF-001 in Guidance.md)
- Overhead door type symbol / tag (referenced to Door & Window Schedule DEL-001-07)
- Bay spacing dimensions
- Separation element between bays (column, pilaster, or wall segment) shown

**Face with wash bay door:**
- One overhead door opening — width TBD (size for motor scraper — see Guidance.md C-02)
- Overhead door type symbol / tag (DEL-001-07)
- Exterior mud sump location indicated on this face with a note referencing plumbing drawings (PKG-006)

**Face(s) with pedestrian doors:**
- Entrance door location and tag (DEL-001-07) — labeled as "ENTRANCE"
- Exit door location and tag (DEL-001-07) — labeled as "EXIT"
- Separate entrance and exit are required; must be shown as distinct openings (R-01 §3.1)
- Door sill height at FFE

**Face with parts room roll-up door:**
- Roll-up door opening — 6' wide (R-01 §3.4)
- Tag referenced to DEL-001-07

**Mezzanine level:**
- Indicate mezzanine floor level on relevant elevation faces where mezzanine occurs (above parts room, utility room, and wash bay — R-04)

**Interface face (junction with existing Old North Shop):**
- Show existing building (Old North Shop — 40' high or as existing) versus new addition height
- Interface condition (expansion joint / shared wall / connection detail) — TBD, per Architect's design decision; mark with a detail bubble referencing DEL-001-04 or DEL-002-04 if a structural detail governs

**Note regarding "40' high or as existing" for Old North Shop (Ref: Lensing Item X-003, Guidance CONF-003):** The "40'" figure appearing in this step for the existing building height is unconfirmed and may be a plan dimension (40' building depth) rather than a height. Do not rely on this figure for the interface elevation until the existing building height is confirmed by an existing condition survey. Use "height TBD — pending existing condition survey" until confirmed.

Source: R-01 §3.1, §3.4; R-04; all REQ items in Specification.md.

---

### Step 4A — Obtain County Design Direction on Exterior Materials (Ref: Lensing Item F-003)

4A.1 Before proceeding to final material keynoting in Step 4 (or as early as practicable), request the County's design direction on exterior materials from the Owner through the County project manager (R-01 §3.1).

4A.2 Document the Owner's direction (material type, colour preferences, quality expectations) in the project record.

4A.3 If no direction is received by the time elevation drafting begins, proceed with "TBD — see Finish Schedule" keynotes per REQ-008, and flag this as an open item in the coordination log.

Source: R-01 §3.3.2 (Owner involvement in design); Specification REQ-008; Guidance T-01.

---

### Step 5 — Coordinate Exterior Penetrations with MEP Engineers

5.1 Obtain from Mechanical Engineer (PKG-003):
  - Location and size of makeup air louver(s)
  - Location and size of air exhaust terminations
  - Location and size of equipment exhaust penetrations
  - Location of gas meter / regulator if exterior-mounted

5.2 Obtain from Electrical Engineer (PKG-004):
  - Location of electrical service entry / meter base on exterior wall
  - Any exterior conduit or panel locations

5.3 Obtain from Plumbing Engineer (PKG-006):
  - Confirmed exterior mud sump location and size
  - Bulk water fill connection location on exterior wall
  - Any other exterior plumbing penetrations

5.4 Show all confirmed exterior penetrations and equipment on the relevant elevation faces with keynotes. Use "COORD — see [discipline] drawings" for any items not yet confirmed.

Source: R-01 §3.4; SOW-0035–0044; OBJ-003 (fully coordinated design).

---

### Step 6 — Coordinate Finished Grade Lines with Civil Engineer

6.1 Obtain finished grade elevations at each face of the building from the Civil Engineer (DEL-005-02 Site Grading Plan).

6.2 Plot finished grade line on each exterior elevation.

6.3 Verify that any accessible entrance door has an accessible approach path (Alberta Building Code requirement — specific threshold TBD pending occupancy classification determination).

6.4 Confirm that positive drainage is maintained away from the building on all faces (R-01 §3.4).

Source: R-01 §3.4; SOW-0015, SOW-0020; DEL-005-02.

---

### Step 8 — Inter-Discipline Coordination Issue

8.1 Issue the draft Exterior Elevations to all discipline leads (Structural, Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing, Civil) for coordination review.

8.2 Collect and respond to all comments. Document coordination decisions in the project record.

8.3 Update elevation sheets to reflect coordination outcomes.

Source: OBJ-003 (fully coordinated design across disciplines); R-01 §3.3.2.

---

### Step 8A — Revision Control (Ref: Lensing Item D-002)

8A.1 Establish a drawing revision log for the Exterior Elevations sheets. Each revision shall be assigned a sequential revision letter (Rev A, Rev B, etc.) with:
- Revision date
- Reason for revision (e.g., "Coordination response — structural heights updated," "County review comment — door location revised")
- Affected sheets/areas

8A.2 Maintain the revision log throughout the production process (Steps 4 through 12). Each time elevation sheets are updated following a coordination review (Step 8), County review (Step 9), or AHJ deficiency response (Step 12), increment the revision and record the change.

8A.3 The revision log forms part of the project record (see Records table — "Revision history").

Source: Standard drawing production practice; Records table requirement.

---

### Step 9 — County Preliminary Review (if required before IFC)

9.1 If the project schedule includes a County review milestone between preliminary and IFC stages, issue the coordinated Exterior Elevations to the County project manager for review.

9.2 Incorporate any County-directed changes.

Source: R-01 §3.3.2; SOW-0086 (weekly meetings and coordination with County project manager).

---

### Step 10 — Finalize and Prepare for IFC

10.1 Complete all drawings to IFC standard, including final dimensions, all keynotes, all coordination notes resolved.

10.2 Confirm that the Exterior Elevations are consistent with all other IFC drawings in the PKG-001 set (floor plans, sections, schedules) and with the IFC sets of PKG-002 through PKG-006.

10.2A **Cross-package consistency verification checkpoint (Ref: Lensing Item X-004):** Before proceeding to P.Eng. stamp, conduct a specific verification confirming that the Exterior Elevation drawings are consistent with all other IFC packages (PKG-002 Structural, PKG-003 Mechanical, PKG-004 Electrical, PKG-005 Civil, PKG-006 Plumbing). This checkpoint verifies:
- Structural: wall heights, opening sizes, crane support visible elements consistent with PKG-002 IFC drawings
- Mechanical: louver/exhaust penetration locations consistent with PKG-003 IFC drawings
- Electrical: service entry location consistent with PKG-004 IFC drawings
- Civil: finished grade lines consistent with PKG-005 IFC drawings
- Plumbing: mud sump and bulk water fill locations consistent with PKG-006 IFC drawings
Record the cross-package consistency check result in the coordination issue log.

10.3 Prepare drawing sheets for P.Eng. signature and stamp.

Source: R-01 §3.3.2 ("All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta").

---

### Step 11 — P.Eng. Review and Stamp

11.1 Submit the complete IFC drawing set (including Exterior Elevations) to the Engineer of Record / P.Eng. for review.

11.2 The P.Eng. reviews the drawings for code compliance and coordination.

11.3 The P.Eng. signs and stamps each sheet of the IFC set.

11.4 No construction shall commence from drawings that have not been stamped.

Source: R-01 §3.3.2; SOW-0018.

---

### Step 12 — Building Permit Submission

12.1 Include the stamped Exterior Elevations as part of the building permit application package submitted to Camrose County (R-01 §3.3.2; SOW-0006).

12.2 Respond to any deficiency comments from the building permit authority (AHJ) relating to the exterior elevations.

12.3 Update drawings if required by AHJ.

12.4 **Post-permit revision re-verification (Ref: Lensing Item E-003):** When drawings are updated in response to AHJ deficiency comments (Steps 12.2-12.3), the following re-verification steps shall be performed to confirm that post-permit changes do not introduce new inconsistencies:
- Re-run applicable items from the internal QC checklist (Step 7) that are affected by the AHJ-directed change
- Re-confirm inter-discipline coordination (Step 8) for any changes that affect MEP, structural, or civil interfaces
- Record the revision per Step 8A revision control process
- If changes are substantive (affect building geometry, opening sizes, or material designations), re-submit to the P.Eng. for confirmation that the stamp remains valid (Step 11)

Source: R-01 §3.3.2; SOW-0006; DEL-009-02 (Building Permit Application & Approval).
