# Specification — DEL-001-03 Exterior Elevations

---

## Scope

### What this deliverable covers

DEL-001-03 is the Exterior Elevations drawing set for the New North Shop Addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop. It is one component of the final architectural design package (SOW-0011) and is part of the Issued for Construction (IFC) drawing set.

The Exterior Elevations drawing set shall depict all exterior faces of the New North Shop Addition including:

- All exterior wall elevations (all four faces, minimum)
- Exterior door and overhead door locations, sizes, and types
- Exterior fenestration (windows, if any) locations and sizes
- Finished floor level and finished grade level reference datums
- Overall building height dimensions to top of wall/parapet and roof ridge (as applicable)
- Ceiling height callout (35') correlated to interior finished floor elevation
- Exterior material and finish designations (referenced to Finish Schedule DEL-001-08)
- Mud sump location and exterior features of wash bay drainage system
- Bulk water fill exterior connection location
- Mezzanine level indication on relevant faces
- Relationship / interface with existing Old North Shop at shared wall or junction (on renovation boundary face)
- Exterior gravel pad and cement pad extents where they interface with the building face (coordination with civil and floor plan drawings)

## Requirements

### REQ-001 — All Exterior Faces Depicted
All exterior-facing walls of the New North Shop Addition shall be drawn at a minimum of one elevation view per face. Faces shall be labelled by compass orientation or elevation tag consistent with the floor plan (DEL-001-02).
Source: R-01 §3.3.2 (complete final design); SOW-0011. Verification: drawing completeness review.

### REQ-002 — Building Height and Ceiling Height
The exterior elevations shall show the overall building height from finished floor elevation (FFE) to top of wall/parapet or roof peak. The 35' interior ceiling height requirement shall be reflected in the exterior wall height depicted on the elevations.
Source: R-01 §3.4 ("Concrete structure 35' ceiling"), R-04 (Appendix B building notes). Verification: dimension check against Section drawings (DEL-001-04).

### REQ-003 — Overhead Door Openings
All overhead door openings shall be shown on the elevations with labeled widths. Drive-through repair bay overhead door openings shall reflect 24' bay widths as shown on conceptual floor plan. Wash bay overhead door opening shall be sized to accommodate motor scraper-class equipment. **Note:** The wash bay overhead door currently lacks specific minimum height and width dimensions; the statement "sized to accommodate motor scraper-class equipment" is insufficiently precise for construction — specific dimensions must be confirmed by Architect based on County equipment specifications (Ref: Lensing Item A-002; see also Guidance C-02). The 24' repair bay dimension is subject to CONF-001 resolution (bay width vs. door opening width).
Source: R-01 §3.1 (two drive-through bays), §3.4 (single wash bay for motor scraper), R-04. Verification: cross-check against Floor Plans (DEL-001-02) and Door & Window Schedule (DEL-001-07).

### REQ-004 — Parts Room Roll-Up Door
The exterior elevation shall show the 6-foot-wide roll-up door for the parts storage room (~400 sq ft).
Source: R-01 §3.4 ("400 square foot secure parts storage area containing a 6 foot wide roll-up door"). Verification: dimension shown on elevation and confirmed in DEL-001-07.

### REQ-005 — Separate Entrance and Exit Doors
Separate entrance and exit pedestrian doors shall be shown on the exterior elevations with their locations clearly distinguished.
Source: R-01 §3.1 ("The proposed building shall have separate entrance/exit doors"). Verification: at least two distinct pedestrian door locations shown; confirmed in DEL-001-07.

### REQ-006 — Exterior Mud Sump
The exterior mud sump associated with the wash bay drainage system shall be depicted on the elevation of the face on which it occurs. It shall be shown as an exterior feature accessible for excavator cleanout.
Source: R-01 §3.4 ("Mud sump from wash bay on exterior of building for clean out with excavator"), R-04. Verification: mud sump shown on correct elevation face; location coordinated with plumbing drawings (PKG-006).

### REQ-007 — Finished Grade Reference
Finished grade levels at each face of the building shall be indicated on the exterior elevations to allow assessment of accessible entry conditions and drainage slope.
Source: R-01 §3.4 (positive site drainage requirement, grading design); SOW-0015, SOW-0020. Verification: finished grade lines shown on all elevation faces; coordination note with civil grading plan (DEL-005-02).

### REQ-008 — Material and Finish Identification
Exterior cladding materials, wall finishes, and roof materials shall be identified on the elevations using keynotes or material symbols, cross-referenced to the Finish Schedule (DEL-001-08). If materials have not yet been selected, the elevation shall use a keynote referencing "TBD — see Finish Schedule."
Source: R-01 §3.3.2 (complete final architectural design); SOW-0011. Verification: all wall and roof surfaces keyed; Finish Schedule referenced.

### REQ-009 — P.Eng. Stamp on IFC Set
The Exterior Elevations drawing sheet(s), as part of the IFC drawing set, shall be signed and stamped by a professional engineer (P.Eng.) licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta prior to construction.
Source: R-01 §3.3.2 ("All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta"). Verification: stamp and signature present on each sheet of the IFC set.

### REQ-010 — Alberta Building Code Compliance
The exterior design depicted on the elevations shall comply with the Alberta Building Code (edition in force at time of building permit application) for the applicable occupancy classification. This includes requirements for exterior wall construction, fire resistance ratings (where applicable), and accessibility provisions at entry points.
Source: R-01 §3.3.2, §3.4 ("The building design must adhere to all applicable building codes and regulations"), §3.4 ("The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes"). Verification: building permit authority review; code compliance register (DEL-009-04).

**Verification gap (Ref: Lensing Items A-003, F-002):** The verification approach for REQ-010 currently relies on general "building permit authority review" without enumerating specific compliance checks for:
- Exterior wall construction type (based on confirmed occupancy classification — see Datasheet, occupancy class TBD)
- Fire resistance rating for exterior walls where proximity to property lines or adjacent buildings may trigger code requirements (see also REQ-NEW-01 below)
- Accessible entrance provisions at exterior doors (specific acceptance criteria TBD pending occupancy classification determination)
These specific checks should be enumerated once occupancy classification is confirmed by AHJ or code consultant.

### REQ-011 — Coordination with Structural Drawings
Exterior elevations shall be coordinated with the structural drawings (PKG-002) to ensure wall height, crane support structure height, mezzanine level, and opening sizes are consistent. Any structural elements visible on the exterior (e.g., pilasters, exposed concrete) shall be shown.
Source: SOW-0011, SOW-0012; OBJ-003 (fully coordinated design across disciplines). Verification: cross-check with DEL-002-03 (Structural Framing Plans) and DEL-002-04.

### REQ-012 — Relationship to Existing Old North Shop
The elevation face where the New North Shop Addition connects to or abuts the existing Old North Shop (105' × 40' existing building) shall show the interface condition, including any height transition, shared wall, expansion joint, or separation. This face shall be coordinated with the Renovation Plans (DEL-001-10) and Demolition Plans (DEL-001-09).
Source: R-01 §3.1, R-04 (combined plan shows 105' existing + 130' new addition = 235' total). Verification: interface condition shown on relevant elevation face; no unresolved gaps between new and existing building depicted.

**Acceptance criteria gap (Ref: Lensing Item D-001):** The current verification statement ("no unresolved gaps between new and existing building depicted") is circular — the gap is unresolved if the design decision itself is unresolved. Acceptance criteria should specify what constitutes a resolved interface condition, including:
- Interface type identified (expansion joint, shared wall, structural connection, or full separation)
- Height transition shown with dimensions if existing building height differs from new addition
- Weather seal / waterproofing detail referenced or noted
- Structural connection or separation detail referenced (cross-ref DEL-002-04)
These criteria are TBD pending Architect / Structural Engineer design decision and existing condition survey.

### REQ-NEW-01 — Exterior Wall Fire Resistance Rating (ASSUMPTION: may be required)
Where proximity to property lines or adjacent buildings triggers fire resistance requirements under the Alberta Building Code, the exterior wall assembly and fire resistance rating shall be identified on the elevations. The existing Old North Shop adjacency may trigger fire separation requirements.
Source: ASSUMPTION — Guidance P-07 identifies this as applicable where proximity conditions exist; R-01 §3.3.2, §3.4 (general code compliance). Specific ABC clause: location TBD. (Ref: Lensing Item F-001)
Verification: TBD — code consultant or Architect to confirm applicability based on occupancy classification and site plan.

### REQ-NEW-02 — Fenestration (Windows) — Explicit Statement Required
The exterior elevations shall either show window locations and sizes or explicitly state that no windows are provided in the design. The current documents are silent on fenestration; for an industrial building this may be intentional but must be explicitly stated to avoid ambiguity.
Source: ASSUMPTION — no RFP or conceptual drawing evidence of windows identified; explicit statement needed for completeness. Specification scope line 3 mentions "Exterior fenestration (windows, if any)" but no requirement addresses this. (Ref: Lensing Item B-004)
Verification: fenestration shown on elevations, or "no windows" statement in Specification scope and Datasheet.

### REQ-NEW-03 — Bulk Water Fill Exterior Connection
The bulk water fill exterior connection location shall be shown on the appropriate elevation face, coordinated with plumbing drawings (PKG-006).
Source: R-01 §3.4 (bulk water fill requirement); Specification scope already lists this item; Datasheet lists "Bulk water fill connection: Exterior location TBD." No corresponding requirement existed previously. (Ref: Lensing Item X-002)
Verification: TBD — Plumbing Engineer / Architect to confirm location; connection shown on elevation with keynote referencing plumbing drawings.

### REQ-NEW-04 — Foundation / Wall Base Coordination Note
The exterior elevations shall include a coordination note or detail reference addressing how the wall base condition varies with foundation type (TBD pending geotechnical investigation, DEL-008-01). The finished floor elevation datum (REQ-002, REQ-007) depends on foundation design.
Source: Datasheet Construction table — foundation type TBD (R-01 §4.8.2, Decomposition SOW-0019); Procedure Step 2 depends on FFE datum. (Ref: Lensing Item C-001)
Verification: coordination note present on elevation drawings referencing structural/geotechnical design for wall base condition.
