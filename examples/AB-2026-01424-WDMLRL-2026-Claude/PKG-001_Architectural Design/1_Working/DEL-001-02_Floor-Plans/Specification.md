# Specification — DEL-001-02 Architectural Floor Plans

---

## Scope

### What this deliverable covers

DEL-001-02 is the Architectural Floor Plans drawing set for the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop project. It covers:

- Final architectural floor plan(s) for the addition (~13,000 sq ft useable area; 130' × 100' building footprint per conceptual drawing)
- All major spaces, programmatic elements, dimensions, column grid, door/opening locations, and floor features (steel plates, drains, service pit location) for the addition
- Coordination-level representation of the Old North Shop plan where required for interface purposes (ASSUMPTION — extent to be confirmed with Architect; primary renovation drawing set for the Old North Shop is DEL-001-09 and DEL-001-10)
- Site orientation context as needed (north arrow, adjacency to Old North Shop, exterior mud sump location)

### What this deliverable excludes

- Exterior elevations (DEL-001-03)
- Building sections (DEL-001-04)
- Interior elevations (DEL-001-05)
- Reflected ceiling plans (DEL-001-06)
- Door and window schedules (DEL-001-07)
- Finish schedules (DEL-001-08)
- Old North Shop demolition and renovation plans (DEL-001-09, DEL-001-10)
- Architectural specification (DEL-001-11)
- Structural, mechanical, electrical, civil, and plumbing drawings (PKG-002 through PKG-006)
- Preliminary architectural design presentation (DEL-001-01)

## Requirements

### REQ-001 — Building Area
The Addition floor plan shall depict a useable area of approximately 13,000 sq ft.
> Source: RFP §1.0, §3.1; Decomp SOW-0011

### REQ-002 — Building Footprint
The building footprint shall be based on the conceptual drawing dimensions of 130' × 100'.
> Source: R-04 (Appendix B — Shop)

### REQ-003 — Ceiling Height Reference
Floor plans shall be coordinated with the 35' ceiling height as the governing vertical constraint for all space planning, equipment placement, and crane runway coordination.
> Source: RFP §3.4; R-04

### REQ-004 — Repair Bays
The floor plan shall show two (2) drive-through repair bays, each 24' wide.
> Source: RFP §3.1; R-04; Decomp SOW-0025

### REQ-005 — Wash Bay
The floor plan shall show one (1) enclosed wash bay, 24' wide, sized for motor scraper-class equipment. The wash bay shall be shown as a separate enclosed space with an overhead door.
> Source: RFP §3.4; R-04; Decomp SOW-0027a

### REQ-006 — Wash Bay Drainage / Mud Sump
The floor plan shall show wash bay floor drains and the connection path to the exterior mud sump. The exterior mud sump shall be shown outside the building envelope adjacent to the wash bay.
> Source: RFP §3.4; R-04; Decomp SOW-0027b

### REQ-007 — Parts Storage Room
The floor plan shall show a parts storage room of approximately 400 sq ft with a 6' wide roll-up door.
> Source: RFP §3.4; Decomp SOW-0029

### REQ-008 — Utility Room
The floor plan shall show a utility room. Exact dimensions are TBD by Architect consistent with program and mechanical/plumbing equipment requirements.
> Source: RFP §3.1; R-04; Decomp SOW-0030

### REQ-009 — Office Space
The floor plan shall show an office space. Exact dimensions are TBD by Architect.
> Source: RFP §3.1; R-04; Decomp SOW-0031

### REQ-010 — Washroom
The floor plan shall show a washroom within the addition.
> Source: RFP §3.1

### REQ-011 — Industrial Wash Station
The floor plan shall show a separate industrial wash station (wash sink) location within the addition.
> Source: RFP §3.1; Decomp SOW-0050

### REQ-012 — Service Pit / Trench
The floor plan shall show the below-grade service pit/trench location. The service pit shall be ventilated and lighted per design requirements.
> Source: RFP §3.4; R-04; Decomp SOW-0028

### REQ-013 — Mezzanine
The floor plan shall show the mezzanine level as an overlay or separate plan, spanning over the parts room, utility room, and wash bay. The mezzanine structure shall be load-bearing for heavy items (oil totes, pumping equipment).
> Source: RFP §3.4; R-04; Decomp SOW-0032, SOW-0033

### REQ-014 — Stairs to Mezzanine
The floor plan shall show stair access to the mezzanine.
> Source: R-04; Decomp SOW-0034

### REQ-015 — Steel Plates in Floor
The floor plan shall show the locations of embedded steel plates in the concrete floor. Steel plates shall be at strategic locations to accommodate tracked and packer-type heavy equipment.
> Source: RFP §3.4; R-04; Decomp SOW-0024

### REQ-016 — 50,000 L Cistern / Water Storage
The floor plan shall show the location of the 50,000 L water storage cistern within the addition.
> Source: RFP §3.4; R-04; Decomp SOW-0041

### REQ-017 — Overhead Cranes (Coordination)
The floor plan shall show the crane runway/rail footprint for the two (2) 5-tonne overhead cranes on trolley, for coordination with the structural design.
> Source: RFP §3.4; R-04; Decomp SOW-0067

### REQ-018 — Welding Station
The floor plan shall show the welding station location within the main shop area.
> Source: R-04; RFP §3.4; Decomp SOW-0039

### REQ-019 — Entrance / Exit Doors
The floor plan shall show separate entrance and exit door locations for the addition.
> Source: RFP §3.1; Decomp SOW-0026

### REQ-020 — Forced Air Makeup Air (Coordination)
The floor plan shall show or reference mechanical equipment space required for the forced air makeup air system, for coordination with the mechanical design.
> Source: R-04; Decomp SOW-0037
> **ASSUMPTION:** Extent of mechanical coordination shown on architectural floor plan versus mechanical drawings to be confirmed by Architect.

### REQ-021 — Code Compliance (Alberta Building Code)
The floor plan design shall comply with all applicable provisions of the Alberta Building Code and Alberta Safety Codes Act, including but not limited to occupancy classification, means of egress, accessible design (where required), and spatial clearances.
> Source: RFP §3.3.2, §3.4; Decomp SOW-0008, SOW-0009

### REQ-022 — IFC Stamp
The floor plan drawing set, when issued for construction, shall be signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer (P.Eng.) licensed in the Province of Alberta.
> Source: RFP §3.3.2; Decomp SOW-0018
> **NOTE:** The RFP requires P.Eng. stamp on IFC drawings. It is ASSUMPTION that the Architect of Record may also require architectural stamp (AANB or equivalent). Confirm with project team. **See Guidance.md Conflict Table CONF-003 for full analysis of this ambiguity.** **TBD: Owner and AHJ to clarify whether P.Eng. stamp requirement (RFP ss3.3.2) applies to architectural drawings or only engineering discipline drawings** (Lensing item A-002).

### REQ-023 — County Approval (Preliminary Stage)
The preliminary architectural design (DEL-001-01) must receive County approval before final floor plan drawing set production is complete and issued for construction.
> Source: RFP §3.3.2; Decomp SOW-0010a

### REQ-024 — Coordinate with Old North Shop Renovation
The floor plan shall show the interface between the New North Shop addition and the Old North Shop (105' × 40' existing building) to the extent necessary for construction coordination.
> Source: R-04

### REQ-025 — Fire Separation Identification
The floor plan shall identify fire-rated separations between distinct occupancy groups (e.g., industrial shop vs. office vs. storage), where required by the Alberta Building Code for mixed-occupancy buildings. Fire separation ratings shall be noted on the floor plan.
> Source: Procedure.md Step 1.3, Step 7.1; Alberta Building Code (ASSUMPTION: likely applicable — edition and specific clause references TBD); Lensing item C-001.
> **NOTE:** This requirement is conditional on the building's occupancy classification and whether mixed-use fire separations are triggered under the applicable ABC edition. The Architect shall confirm during code analysis (Procedure Step 1).

### REQ-026 — Emergency Shower / Eyewash Station Location
The floor plan shall show the location of the emergency shower and/or eyewash station if required by applicable occupational health and safety regulations for the facility's intended operations.
> Source: Procedure.md Step 6.1 (plumbing coordination — "emergency shower location (DEL-006)"); Decomp SOW-0050; Lensing item F-002.
> **ASSUMPTION:** The need for an emergency shower/eyewash station is assumed based on industrial shop operations (welding, chemical handling). Confirm requirement with the Owner and applicable OH&S regulations. If not required, this REQ may be removed.

### REQ-027 — Ceiling Fan Coordination
The floor plan shall show or reference ceiling fan locations for the six (6) ceiling fans in the main shop area, for coordination with the mechanical design.
> Source: Procedure.md Step 6.1; Decomp SOW-0040; Lensing item E-001.
> **ASSUMPTION:** Ceiling fans are referenced in the decomposition (SOW-0040) and in Procedure Step 6.1, but no corresponding normative requirement existed in the Specification. The Architect shall confirm ceiling fan count and placement with the Mechanical Engineer.
