# Guidance — DEL-001-06 Reflected Ceiling Plans

---

## Purpose

The Reflected Ceiling Plans (RCPs) exist to communicate, in a single coordinated drawing set, the ceiling geometry, height conditions, and all ceiling-mounted elements for the New North Shop addition and Old North Shop renovation. The RCP is the primary drawing through which:

1. The architect records and communicates ceiling height decisions (particularly the 35' structural concrete ceiling, the mezzanine soffits, and the lower-ceiling ancillary rooms).
2. Lighting and ceiling fan layout — as established in consultation with the electrical engineer — is graphically fixed and made enforceable for construction.
3. Overhead crane rail alignment, forced-air mechanical unit positions, and exhaust fan locations are spatially fixed at the ceiling plane, enabling all trades to coordinate their ceiling-level work from a single document.

Without complete, coordinated RCPs, the ceiling plane for a building of this complexity (35' industrial concrete structure, two 5-ton cranes, high-bay LED lighting, dedicated wash bay, ventilated service pit, overhead mezzanine) cannot be built or inspected consistently.

*Source: OBJ-001, OBJ-003; RFP §3.1, §3.3.2, §3.4*

---

## Principles

### P-01: The RCP is a coordination output, not a stand-alone design

The RCP does not independently design the lighting, mechanical, or structural ceiling systems. It records the coordinated positions of elements designed by other disciplines. The architect must obtain confirmed layouts from the electrical engineer (lighting, fans, exhaust), the mechanical engineer (makeup air units, ventilation), and the structural engineer (crane beams, mezzanine framing) before finalizing the RCP. Issuing RCPs that conflict with discipline drawings is a construction risk.

*Source: OBJ-003; RFP §3.3.2 (coordination obligation)*

### P-02: The 35' ceiling height is a fixed program requirement

The 35-foot structural ceiling height for the main shop is a stated design requirement and is not subject to value-engineering reduction without owner approval. This height was programmed to accommodate the two 5-ton overhead cranes and large heavy equipment (e.g., motor scrapers). The RCP must reflect this height and flag any conditions where penetrations, mechanical equipment, or crane rail clearances might reduce effective working clearance.

*Source: RFP §3.4 ("Concrete structure 35' ceiling"); App B (Shop) legend*

### P-03: Lighting quantities and types are established in the conceptual electrical drawing

The App B (Electrical) drawing establishes minimum lighting intent: 5 rows × 4 high bay fixtures (150W LED, 22,000 lumens) for the main shop, and 2 rows × 3 high bay fixtures for the wash bay. These are conceptual but represent the owner's minimum expectation. The electrical engineer may adjust spacing or quantities in the final design; any changes must be reflected on both the electrical plans and the RCP. The RCP must be consistent with the electrical plans at IFC issue.

*Source: App B (Electrical) — Electrical Notes*

### P-04: Overhead crane rails are a dominant ceiling element

The two 5-ton overhead crane rails traverse the full length of the main shop bay. Their structural depth, end stops, and clearance envelopes are significant ceiling-level features that must be shown on the RCP. The RCP should show the crane rail centerlines and the structural corbel or beam locations. Crane clearance below the hook must be confirmed with the structural engineer and reflected in the RCP annotations.

*Source: RFP §3.4 ("2 – 5-tonne overhead cranes on a trolley"); App B (Shop) — "5 TON CRANE" annotations*

### P-05: Mezzanine level creates a two-tier ceiling condition

The overhead mezzanine over the Parts Room, Utility Room, and Wash Bay creates a lower ceiling condition at the main floor in those areas. The RCP must clearly differentiate this zone from the full-height 35' main shop area. The mezzanine soffit height is TBD (to be set by architect and structural engineer). The mezzanine must be shown as a distinct ceiling plane with its own height annotation.

*Source: App B (Shop) legend ("Overhead Mezzanine over Parts Room/Utility Room/Wash Bay"); RFP §3.4*

### P-06: The wash bay has a distinct lighting and structural condition

The wash bay is a separately enclosed space (conceptual drawing shows 24' dimension at wash bay side). It has its own lighting regime (2 rows × 3 high bay fixtures) and will have an exterior mud sump for drainage. Ceiling conditions in the wash bay — particularly as they relate to drainage slope, wash-down splash protection for fixtures, and vehicle clearance at the overhead door — may differ from the main shop. These conditions should be annotated separately on the RCP.

*Source: App B (Shop) — "WASH BAY" and 24' annotation; App B (Electrical) — Wash Bay lighting; RFP §3.4 ("Single enclosed bay dedicated for washing large equipment"); RFP §3.1 ("one wash-bay")*

## Considerations

### C-01: LED High Bay Fixture Mounting Height

At 35' ceiling height, fixture mounting height and minimum footcandle levels at the working plane should be confirmed by a lighting designer or electrical engineer. 150W LED fixtures at 22,000 lumens are specified in the conceptual drawings; the final design must confirm this achieves required illumination levels per applicable codes.

*Source: App B (Electrical) — "5 Rows of 4 High Bay Lights Main Shop (150 Watt LED 22,000 Lumens)"*

### C-02: Crane Beam Depth Reduces Available Clearance

At a 35' structural ceiling, the depth of the crane beam/runway girder system will reduce the bottom-of-hook height below 35'. The RCP should show or annotate the crane rail bottom elevation as confirmed by the structural engineer. This is important for equipment clearance verification.

*Source: App B (Shop); RFP §3.4 — ASSUMPTION: crane rail depth will consume 1–3 ft of the 35' height; exact dimension TBD from structural*

### C-03: Forced Air Makeup Unit Locations Require Mechanical Confirmation

The conceptual drawing notes "Forced Air Makeup" as a ceiling feature. The number, size, and precise ceiling locations of makeup air units must be confirmed by the mechanical engineer (DEL-003). These units may be large enough to appear prominently on the RCP and may conflict with crane travel or lighting if not coordinated.

*Source: App B (Shop) legend ("Forced Air Makeup"); RFP §3.4 ("High recovery heating system," "High volume air exchanger")*

### C-04: Exhaust Hose Drop Points in Repair Bays

The RFP requires exhaust hoses and fans for heavy equipment in the repair bays. The ceiling drop point locations for retractable exhaust hoses must be determined in coordination with the mechanical engineer and shown on the RCP. These are a health and safety requirement.

*Source: RFP §3.4 ("Exhaust hoses and fans for heavy equipment in the repair bays")*

### C-05: Ventilation at Welding Station

The welding station at the north end of the main shop (per App B) requires dedicated exhaust/ventilation. The RCP should show the ceiling-level exhaust fan at the welding station. This is distinct from general shop ventilation.

*Source: RFP §3.4 ("Ventilation at welding station"); App B (Electrical) — "E" (exhaust fan) symbol at welding station*

### C-06: Security Camera and Antenna Wiring Rough-In

The electrical design notes wiring for security cameras and antenna (2-way radios). Camera mounting locations are typically shown on RCPs for interior mounted units. While not structural ceiling elements, the architect should coordinate ceiling rough-in locations for camera brackets with the electrical engineer.

*Source: App B (Electrical) — "Wiring for Security Cameras," "Antenna Wire for 2 Way Radios"*

### C-07: Escalation Path for Unresolved Coordination Conflicts *(added per E-001)*

When interdisciplinary coordination conflicts cannot be resolved at the design team level (e.g., structural clearance vs. mechanical unit placement, crane rail vs. lighting position), the conflict shall be escalated. Recommended escalation path: design team lead to project principal to County project manager. The escalation path and responsible parties are TBD pending confirmation from the project principal.

*Source: Guidance P-01 ("Issuing RCPs that conflict with discipline drawings is a construction risk"); Procedure B-04 ("Flag unresolved conflicts for design team review"); _SEMANTIC_LENSING E-001*
*ASSUMPTION: Escalation to County project manager is consistent with RFP §3.3.2 (County project manager involvement in inspections and approvals).*

### C-07b: Handling Scope Changes During RCP Production *(added per E-002)*

If scope changes or new information are received during RCP production (e.g., structural design changes, owner-requested additions, mechanical equipment changes) that affect ceiling-plane content already drawn, the architect should: (1) assess the impact on the current RCP drawings, (2) update the coordination log with the change and its source, (3) re-coordinate with affected disciplines, and (4) revise the RCP accordingly. Significant scope changes that affect the project schedule should be flagged to the project principal. Specific change management procedures are TBD per project-level protocols.

*Source: Standard design practice; _SEMANTIC_LENSING E-002*
*ASSUMPTION: No explicit scope change procedure exists in source materials for this deliverable.*

### C-08: Renovation Areas — Existing Condition Verification

The Old North Shop renovation areas (washroom, locker room, 2nd-level mezzanine/coffee room) require field verification of existing ceiling conditions before the architect can complete RCPs for those areas. Existing ceiling heights, structural conditions, and any existing ceiling-mounted elements must be recorded.

*Source: App B (Shop) legend ("Old North Shop Mezzanine to be renovated and the washroom below renovated along with new locker room")*

### C-09: Minimum Acceptable Quality for RCP Drawings *(added per C-004)*

RCP drawings shall be legible at the printed scale, with all ceiling-mounted elements distinguishable from one another and all annotations (height callouts, fixture designations, cross-references) readable without magnification. Symbol sizes and line weights shall be appropriate for the chosen drawing scale. This is a minimum quality expectation; specific pass/fail criteria for print quality are TBD per project drawing standards.

*Source: Standard drawing practice; _SEMANTIC_LENSING C-004*
*ASSUMPTION: No explicit quality threshold was stated in source materials; this articulates the implied minimum.*

### C-10: Drawing Scale Selection

The main shop at 130' × 100' is a large floor plate. At 1:100 metric scale, the plan fits on a standard D-size sheet. Smaller rooms (office, parts room, utility room) may require a supplemental larger-scale RCP (e.g., 1:50) to show fixture layout legibly.

*ASSUMPTION: Standard drawing scales for Canadian construction practice apply; exact scale selection is the architect's judgment.*

### C-11: Measurement Unit Convention *(added per B-003)*

The RCP documents and drawings currently use imperial units exclusively (feet, sq ft) for dimensions (e.g., "35 feet," "130' x 100'," "400 sq ft"), while the Standards table references metric-based codes (NBC, NECB). A project-level convention for measurement units on RCP drawings should be established. Options: (a) imperial throughout with metric equivalents where required by code, (b) metric throughout, or (c) dual units. The convention should be consistent with the overall project drawing standards. Convention: TBD.

*Source: Datasheet Drawing Set Scope and Construction (imperial units); Specification Standards table (metric code references); _SEMANTIC_LENSING B-003*

### C-12: Drawing Numbering Convention *(added per X-001)*

A drawing numbering convention for RCP sheets within the overall project drawing package should be established or referenced from the project-level drawing standards. Procedure Step C-01 references "sheet numbering per project drawing index" and the Documentation section lists "Drawing sheet index / title block information" as TBD. The convention should specify: sheet numbering prefix (e.g., "RCP-" or "A-" series), sequence logic, and relationship to the overall drawing index. Convention: TBD per project drawing standards.

*Source: Procedure Step C-01; Specification Documentation — Anticipated Artifacts; _SEMANTIC_LENSING X-001*

## Trade-offs

| Trade-off | Option A | Option B | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| RCP sheet organization | One composite sheet for all spaces | Separate sheets by zone (main shop, ancillary rooms, renovation) | Separate sheets preferred for legibility given the scale difference between the 35' main shop and the lower ancillary rooms — ASSUMPTION |
| Crane rail representation on RCP | Show crane rail as hidden/dashed line with elevation note | Show crane rail as a distinct ceiling plane element with section reference | Dashed with elevation note is standard practice and less cluttered — ASSUMPTION |
| Mezzanine soffit ceiling annotation | Note on RCP with arrow and height value | Separate enlarged RCP of mezzanine level | Both required; main-level RCP shows soffit, and a mezzanine-level RCP shows the mezzanine floor/ceiling — ASSUMPTION |
