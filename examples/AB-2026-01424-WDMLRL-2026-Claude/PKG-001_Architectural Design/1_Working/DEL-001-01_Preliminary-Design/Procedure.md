# Procedure — DEL-001-01 Preliminary Architectural Design

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and deliver DEL-001-01: the Preliminary Architectural Design presentation for Camrose County approval, as required by SOW-0010a (R-01, §3.3.2).

The procedure covers the activities from project mobilization through County approval of the preliminary design. It does not cover final design production (DEL-001-02 through DEL-001-11).

## Steps

### Step 1 — Project Mobilization and Information Gathering

1.1 Review all accessible RFP documents: R-01 (full), R-02 (Addendum 1), R-03 (Appendix A — forms), R-04 (Appendix B — Shop conceptual), R-05 (Appendix B — Electrical), R-06 (Appendix B — Plumbing), R-07 (Appendix C — Site Maps).

1.2 Attend the mandatory site meeting (March 3, 2026, 10:00 a.m. MST, SW-14-44-21-W4, Alberta) per R-01, §3.2. Failure to attend results in disqualification.

**Site Meeting Confirmation Checklist** (lensing item D-002):
- [ ] Observe existing building (Old North Shop) and available site space for the addition
- [ ] Confirm County's preferred format for the preliminary design presentation (see Guidance C-7, C-13)
- [ ] Confirm County project manager contact and approval routing mechanism (see Step 7.3a)
- [ ] Identify any site constraints not captured in the RFP documents
- [ ] Confirm number of elevations expected for preliminary presentation (see Guidance C-13)
- [ ] Discuss County review process: evaluation criteria, conditional approval possibility, revision cycle expectations (see Guidance C-12)
- [ ] Confirm geotechnical investigation timeline and impact on preliminary design (DEL-008-01)
- [ ] Discuss acceptance tolerance for ~13,000 sq ft area target (see Guidance C-9)

1.3 Obtain geotechnical and survey data as it becomes available from PKG-008 (DEL-008-01 and DEL-008-02). Note any constraints affecting building placement, foundation approach, or grading.

1.4 Review the electrical (R-05) and plumbing (R-06) conceptual appendices to understand interdisciplinary spatial requirements that affect the architectural layout.

1.5 Establish interdisciplinary coordination protocol with Structural, Mechanical, Electrical, Civil, Plumbing, and Landscape Architectural teams (DEL-002-01 through DEL-007-01 are concurrent preliminary deliverables).

**Coordination Protocol (to be confirmed at project mobilization):**
- Meeting frequency: ASSUMPTION: Weekly or bi-weekly coordination meetings during preliminary design phase. TBD: Confirm and commit to a defined coordination schedule or reference a project coordination plan (lensing item A-004).
- Meeting format: TBD (in-person, virtual, or hybrid)
- Coordination log: Architect to maintain a coordination log recording items raised, disposition, and responsible party
- The successful Proponent shall coordinate inspections, weekly meetings, and billing through the County project manager (R-01, §3.1).

---

### Step 2 — Develop Preliminary Floor Plan(s)

2.1 Produce a preliminary floor plan for the New North Shop addition based on the conceptual layout (R-04, App B) and RFP design requirements (R-01, §3.4).

The floor plan shall show, at minimum:
- Overall building dimensions (~130 ft × 100 ft addition per conceptual plan; to be confirmed from site survey)
- Two crane bay positions with 5-tonne overhead crane notation
- Enclosed wash bay with notation of mud sump location (exterior)
- Ventilated and lighted service pit (location per conceptual plan)
- Parts storage room (~400 sq ft, 6 ft roll-up door)
- Utility room (with 50,000 L cistern footprint)
- Office space
- Washroom / locker / change room with laundry
- Mezzanine outline above parts room and utility room (and wash bay per App B note)
- Work bench locations (north and south walls per App B)
- Welding station location (northeast per App B)
- Steel plate locations in floor (consistent with App B)
- Separate entrance/exit door locations
- Gantry locations where shown

2.2 Produce a preliminary floor plan or sketch plan for the Old North Shop renovation showing:
- 250 sq ft kitchenette renovation area
- 2nd Level mezzanine / coffee room renovation zone
- Washroom renovation including urinal addition
- New locker/change room with laundry
- Relationship to the New North Shop addition

2.3 Show the relationship between the Old North Shop (existing, ~105 ft × 40 ft) and the New North Shop addition (~130 ft × 100 ft) on a combined site/floor plan.

---

### Step 3 — Develop Building Section(s) and/or Elevation(s)

3.1 Produce at minimum one building section showing:
- 35 ft clear ceiling height
- Crane clearance zone
- Mezzanine level
- Service pit depth (TBD -- not specified in accessible sources; obtain from structural or operational requirements. See Datasheet, Building Program. Lensing item B-001.)

3.2 ASSUMPTION: One or two elevations (e.g., north face and east face) showing overall building massing and entrance/exit door locations are appropriate for a preliminary presentation of this type. Confirm with County project manager.

---

### Step 4 — Prepare Room/Area Schedule

4.1 Produce a room/area schedule listing each space, its target area, and a cross-reference to the RFP §3.4 requirement it satisfies.

4.2 Confirm total useable area is approximately 13,000 sq ft (R-01, §1.0, §3.1).

---

### Step 5 — Interdisciplinary Coordination Review

5.1 Circulate preliminary floor plan(s) and section(s) to Structural, Mechanical, Electrical, Civil, and Plumbing engineers for preliminary coordination review.

Items to confirm at this stage:
- Crane bay column grid (Structural)
- Cistern location and clearances (Plumbing, Structural)
- Service pit structural implications (Structural)
- Welding station exhaust routing (Mechanical)
- Three-phase power service entry point (Electrical)
- Wash bay and mud sump drainage connection (Civil, Plumbing)
- Foundation approach status relative to geotechnical data (Structural)

5.2 Incorporate coordination feedback into the preliminary design. Document any unresolved coordination items as TBD for the County presentation.

5.3 **Record coordination outcomes** (lensing item X-005): For each discipline reviewed, record:
- Items raised and disposition (resolved, deferred, or TBD)
- Agreement or signoff from the discipline lead (e.g., email confirmation, meeting minutes signature, or coordination log entry)
- Any items that remain unresolved and will be noted as open in the preliminary presentation

ASSUMPTION: A coordination log maintained by the Architect (see Step 1.5) serves as the record of interdisciplinary agreement. A formal signoff sheet is recommended but TBD -- confirm whether the project requires a formal coordination signoff form or whether meeting minutes suffice.

---

### Step 6 — Prepare Design Narrative

6.1 Prepare a brief design narrative for the County presentation covering:
- Project description and design intent
- Summary of how the design meets the RFP program (R-01, §3.1, §3.4)
- Key design assumptions (including geotechnical dependency and any open items; reference Assumption Register in Guidance)
- Code framework acknowledgment: applicable Alberta Building Code edition, Alberta Safety Codes, and building occupancy classification (REQ-012, REQ-012a -- specific editions: location TBD)
- Environmental and hazardous materials acknowledgment where applicable (REQ-012b)
- Any proposed departures from the conceptual drawing with rationale (reference Departure Log in Guidance)

---

### Step 6a — Internal QA/QC Review (prior to submission)

6a.1 Conduct an internal quality review of the preliminary design package before submission to the County. This step verifies the package against the Specification requirements and RFP program before the external approval gate (lensing item F-004).

**QA/QC Checklist:**
- [ ] All spaces from RFP §3.4 are shown on the floor plan (cross-check against REQ-003 and room/area schedule)
- [ ] Area calculation confirms approximately 13,000 sq ft useable area (REQ-002)
- [ ] 35 ft clear ceiling height shown on building section (REQ-004)
- [ ] Crane bay positions shown (REQ-005)
- [ ] Wash bay and mud sump shown (REQ-006)
- [ ] Steel plate locations indicated (REQ-007)
- [ ] Separate entrance/exit doors shown (REQ-008)
- [ ] Mezzanine shown on plan and section (REQ-009)
- [ ] Old North Shop renovation areas labeled (REQ-010)
- [ ] Cistern location shown (REQ-012c)
- [ ] Code framework acknowledged in design narrative (REQ-012)
- [ ] Occupancy classification stated (REQ-012a)
- [ ] Environmental/hazmat considerations acknowledged (REQ-012b)
- [ ] Geotech/survey dependency noted with pending items listed (REQ-013)
- [ ] Design departures from conceptual drawing flagged with rationale (Guidance P-1, Departure Log)
- [ ] Assumptions listed in design narrative match Assumption Register (Guidance)
- [ ] Interdisciplinary coordination items resolved or documented as TBD (Step 5)
- [ ] Terminology consistent (service pit, not service trench -- see Guidance VN-001)

6a.2 Record the internal review date, reviewer(s), and any items requiring correction. Correct identified issues before proceeding to Step 7.

---

### Step 7 — Assemble and Submit Preliminary Design Presentation

7.1 Compile the preliminary design presentation package. ASSUMPTION: Package includes floor plan(s), section(s)/elevation(s), room/area schedule, and design narrative.

7.2 Format the presentation in the County's preferred format confirmed in Step 1.2 (see Guidance C-7).

7.3 Submit the preliminary design presentation to the Camrose County project manager for review and approval.

7.3a **Submission Routing Protocol** (lensing item F-003):
- **Recipient:** Camrose County project manager (identity confirmed at project award per R-01, §3.1; contact confirmed at site meeting per Step 1.2)
- **Transmission method:** TBD -- confirm with County (e.g., email with PDF attachment, physical delivery, project portal upload)
- **Receipt acknowledgment:** Request written acknowledgment of receipt from the County project manager (email reply, signed receipt, or portal timestamp)
- **Copies:** TBD -- confirm whether copies are required for other County representatives

7.4 Record the submission date, method, and receipt acknowledgment.

---

### Step 8 — County Review and Approval

8.1 Respond to County questions or requests for revision arising from the preliminary design review. Revise the presentation as necessary.

8.2 Obtain written County approval of the preliminary architectural design before proceeding to final design documentation (REQ-001).

8.3 Record the County approval (written confirmation) in the project record.
