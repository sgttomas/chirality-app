# Guidance — DEL-001-01 Preliminary Architectural Design

---

## Purpose

DEL-001-01 exists to fulfill SOW-0010a: deliver a preliminary architectural design to Camrose County for approval before the Design-Builder proceeds to final design documentation (R-01, §3.3.2).

The preliminary design serves two interconnected purposes:

1. **Owner approval gate.** The County must approve the preliminary design before the Architect advances to IFC drawing production. This protects both the County and the Design-Builder: the County confirms the design direction satisfies its operational program; the Design-Builder confirms the design is buildable and constructable within scope.

2. **Interdisciplinary coordination baseline.** The preliminary architectural design is the primary spatial framework against which the structural (DEL-002-01), mechanical (DEL-003-01), electrical (DEL-004-01), civil (DEL-005-01), plumbing (DEL-006-01), and landscape architectural (DEL-007-01) preliminary designs are coordinated. The Architect leads this coordination role for PKG-001 as the coordinating discipline for IFC drawing tracking (Decomposition, D-008).

This document provides directional guidance to the Architect producing DEL-001-01. It is not a normative specification; for requirements, see Specification.md.

## Principles

### P-1: Design Fidelity to the Conceptual Program

The RFP provides a conceptual floor plan (R-04, App B) and a detailed list of design requirements (R-01, §3.4). The preliminary design must be faithful to this program. Where the Architect proposes departures from the conceptual layout for constructability, code compliance, or functional reasons, those departures must be explicitly flagged to the County in the presentation with a clear rationale.

**Rationale:** Under CCDC 14–2013, the Design-Builder holds design responsibility, but the Owner has approved a conceptual direction. Unannounced departures risk rework and approval delays.

### P-2: Sufficient Detail for County Approval — No More, No Less

The preliminary design presentation should provide the County with enough information to make an informed approval decision. It is not the IFC drawing set. Excessive detail at this stage may cause confusion between preliminary and final deliverables; insufficient detail may prevent meaningful County review.

**ASSUMPTION: The following content is likely appropriate for the preliminary presentation:** floor plan(s), one or more building sections or elevations, a room/area schedule, and a brief design narrative. This assumption should be confirmed with the County project manager at the mandatory site meeting (R-01, §3.2) or early project kickoff.

### P-3: Code Compliance Acknowledged, Not Yet Detailed

The Alberta Building Code and Alberta Safety Codes govern the final building design. At the preliminary stage, the Architect should acknowledge the applicable code framework and identify any known design constraints (e.g., occupancy classification, exit requirements, ceiling height relative to code) but need not produce a full code compliance matrix. The full matrix belongs with the IFC drawing set.

**Rationale:** Premature code-resolution effort at the preliminary stage can lock in design decisions before geotechnical and survey data (DEL-008-01, DEL-008-02) are available, increasing rework risk.

### P-4: Geotechnical and Survey Dependency

The preliminary architectural design proceeds in advance of the geotechnical report (DEL-008-01). Foundation type is variable-price and will be reconciled post-geotech (Decomposition, OBJ-006, D-009). The Architect should note this dependency explicitly in the preliminary presentation and avoid committing to foundation assumptions in the architectural program that could conflict with geotech findings.

### P-5: Renovation Scope Integration

The Old North Shop renovation (kitchenette 250 sq ft, mezzanine, washroom urinal addition, new locker/change room with laundry) must be addressed in the preliminary design alongside the New North Shop addition. These are connected functionally and spatially. The preliminary presentation should show the relationship between the existing building and the addition clearly.

## Considerations

### C-1: Building Orientation and Site Integration

The conceptual plan (R-04, App B) shows the New North Shop to the north of the Old North Shop, with the building oriented along an east-west axis (130 ft north face). The relationship to the gravel pad, cement pad, wash bay, and mud sump to the east and the overhead pole shed/metal racking to the west is shown in the conceptual drawing. The Architect should verify this orientation against the site maps (R-07, Appendix C — Site Maps) and the results of the preliminary site survey (DEL-008-02).

**Note:** R-07 (Site Maps) was not directly read in preparing this document. The Architect should consult R-07 for site orientation, expansion area boundaries, and property constraints.

### C-2: Crane Bay Design Implications

Two 5-tonne overhead cranes on trolley (R-01, §3.4) are a dominant driver of the structural design and ceiling height. The 35 ft clear ceiling (R-01, §3.4; R-04, App B) is directly tied to crane clearance requirements. The Architect must coordinate crane bay dimensions, column spacing, and structural wall/frame layout with the Structural Engineer (DEL-002-01) at the preliminary stage. Crane bay width dimensions shown on the conceptual plan (~60 ft combined per plan annotation) should be confirmed with the structural and crane supplier.

### C-3: Wash Bay and Mud Sump Relationship

The wash bay (single enclosed bay for large equipment) and the exterior mud sump accessible by excavator (R-01, §3.4) are architecturally and civilly linked. The mud sump location relative to the building envelope must be coordinated with the Civil Engineer (DEL-005-01) and Plumbing Engineer (DEL-006-01) at the preliminary stage.

### C-4: Mezzanine Structural Loading

The mezzanine over the parts room and utility room is load-bearing for heavy items (oil totes, oil pumping equipment — R-01, §3.4). This is a structural loading consideration that the Architect must communicate clearly to the Structural Engineer in the preliminary coordination. The architectural design should not resolve structural loads but should flag the intended use for structural input.

### C-5: Steel Plate Locations

Steel plates in the floor are shown at multiple locations in the conceptual plan (R-04, App B) to accommodate track and packer type heavy equipment (R-01, §3.4). The Architect should include a schematic indication of steel plate locations in the preliminary floor plan, consistent with the conceptual drawing, recognizing that final sizing and embedment details are structural scope.

### C-6: Electrical and Mechanical Coordination Points

Key architectural coordination items for the preliminary stage:
- **Welding station location and exhaust ventilation**: The welding station is shown at the northeast corner (R-04, App B). Exhaust routing is Mechanical scope (DEL-003-01) but the architectural layout must accommodate it.
- **Service pit**: Ventilated and lighted; location shown on plan (R-04, App B). Pit dimensions and structural implications are Structural scope but architectural layout drives placement.
- **50,000 L cistern**: Located in or adjacent to the utility room per conceptual plan. Cistern footprint and ceiling clearance have architectural space-planning implications.

### C-7: Presentation Format

The preliminary design is a "Design Presentation" type deliverable (Decomposition, PKG-001, DEL-001-01). The format is not prescribed by accessible sources. ASSUMPTION: A PDF or plotted drawing presentation suitable for County project team review is appropriate. TBD: Confirm the County's preferred format for the preliminary design presentation -- PDF, plotted drawings, digital model, or other (lensing item A-005). This is a key evaluative driver for whether the deliverable meets County expectations. The Architect should confirm the format at the mandatory site meeting (R-01, §3.2) or project kickoff.

### C-8: Heating System Specification Clarity

RFP §3.4 states "High recovery heating system" and App B notes "Forced Air Makeup." It is unclear whether the RFP prescribes a specific system type (forced-air makeup unit) or a performance requirement (high recovery of heated air). This distinction affects the Architect's design latitude and coordination with the Mechanical Engineer (DEL-003-01).

**Guidance:** At the preliminary stage, the Architect should note the RFP language and coordinate with the Mechanical Engineer to determine whether the heating system is prescriptive or performance-based. If the Architect proposes an alternative system meeting the performance intent, this should be flagged as a departure from the conceptual program per Principle P-1.

**Source:** R-01, §3.4; R-04, App B (lensing item E-002).

### C-9: Area Target Basis and Derivation

The ~13,000 sq ft useable area target (R-01, §1.0, §3.1) is used across all four documents as a primary design parameter, but its basis or derivation is not explained in accessible sources. TBD: Is this figure driven by equipment count, operational need, budget, or site constraints? Understanding the basis helps the Architect evaluate design trade-offs (e.g., whether the target is a minimum, maximum, or approximate center). The Architect should confirm with the County whether there is flexibility around this number and what acceptance tolerance applies.

**Source:** R-01, §1.0, §3.1 (lensing item C-003).

### C-10: Electrical and Plumbing Appendix Implications

Procedure Step 1.4 requires review of R-05 (Appendix B -- Electrical) and R-06 (Appendix B -- Plumbing) to understand interdisciplinary spatial requirements affecting the architectural layout. Neither Datasheet nor Guidance currently extracts data or architectural implications from these appendices. If R-05 or R-06 contain space-planning constraints (e.g., electrical panel locations, transformer pad, plumbing chase routing, emergency shower placement per R-06), these should be incorporated into the preliminary architectural layout.

**Guidance:** The Architect should review R-05 and R-06 during project mobilization and record any architectural space-planning implications. If no constraints are found, record that finding.

**Source:** R-05, R-06 (not read in detail -- TBD); Procedure Step 1.4 (lensing item E-001).

### C-11: Existing Old North Shop Structural Condition

The renovation scope addresses the Old North Shop interior (kitchenette, mezzanine, washroom, locker room -- R-01, §3.4; R-04, App B), and the preliminary design must show the relationship between the existing building and the new addition (Guidance P-5). However, there is no mention of the existing building's structural condition or interface requirements at the connection point between old and new structures.

**Guidance:** The Architect should identify whether the existing Old North Shop structural condition imposes constraints on the connection to the new addition (e.g., load transfer, wall demolition for connection, differential settlement). This information may not be available until the geotechnical report (DEL-008-01) and preliminary survey (DEL-008-02) are complete. At the preliminary stage, record the connection interface as a design assumption requiring confirmation.

**Source:** R-04, App B (shows old and new buildings adjacent); R-01, §3.4 (renovation scope). ASSUMPTION: Structural condition assessment of the existing building is TBD (lensing item B-004).

### C-12: County Review Process and Revision Management

REQ-001 requires "County written approval" of the preliminary design, and Trade-off T-1 addresses depth vs. speed. However, there is no guidance on what constitutes a successful County review outcome versus a conditional approval versus a rejection, or how revision cycles should be managed.

**Guidance:** The Architect should discuss with the County project manager at the site meeting or project kickoff:
- What are the County's evaluation criteria for the preliminary design?
- Is a conditional approval possible (approval with noted revisions)?
- How many revision cycles are anticipated, and what is the expected turnaround time?
- Who within the County has final approval authority?

**Source:** R-01, §3.3.2 (approval gate); R-01, §3.1 (County project manager coordination). TBD: County review process details not specified in accessible sources (lensing item D-003).

### C-13: Number of Elevations for Preliminary Presentation

Procedure Step 3.2 notes "ASSUMPTION: One or two elevations" for the preliminary presentation, with no resolution path beyond "confirm with County." The number and orientation of building elevations affects scope and effort.

**Guidance:** The Architect should confirm with the County how many elevations are appropriate for preliminary review. As a minimum, a north elevation (primary face of the New North Shop) and an east or west elevation (showing the old-to-new relationship) would likely be sufficient. If the County requires all four elevations, this increases preliminary design effort.

**Source:** Procedure Step 3.2 (ASSUMPTION). TBD: Confirm with County project manager (lensing item X-001).

### C-14: Environmental and Hazardous Materials Handling

The building will contain oil totes (mezzanine), oil pumping equipment, a wash bay with drainage, welding operations, and sump drains (R-01, §3.4; R-04, App B). These operations may generate hazardous materials or effluent subject to provincial environmental regulations beyond the Alberta Building Code. At the preliminary stage, the Architect should coordinate with mechanical and plumbing disciplines to acknowledge applicable environmental or hazardous materials handling requirements.

**Source:** R-01, §3.4; R-04, App B. ASSUMPTION: Applicable provincial environmental regulations (e.g., Alberta EPEA) are TBD -- specific regulatory references not identified in accessible sources (lensing item C-002).

### VN-001: Service Pit (canonical term)

The below-grade service access feature is referred to inconsistently across documents:

- Specification uses "ventilated/lighted service pit" (REQ-003)

- Decomposition Vocabulary Map establishes **"Service Pit"** as the canonical term, with "Service Pit" as a synonym

**Resolution:** Adopt **"service pit"** as the single canonical term across all four documents, consistent with the Decomposition Vocabulary Map. Where the conceptual drawing (R-04, App B) labels it as "SERVICE TRENCH," note this as a drawing-specific label.

**Source:** Decomposition Vocabulary Map; R-01, §3.4 ("Ventilated and lighted service pit area"); R-04, App B ("SERVICE TRENCH") (lensing item E-003).

## Trade-offs

### T-1: Depth vs. Speed at Preliminary Stage

Producing a highly detailed preliminary design accelerates the final design phase but increases rework risk if the County requests significant changes at the approval gate. A schematic-level presentation (floor plan + sections + program) minimizes rework exposure while satisfying the approval gate requirement.

**Proposed approach (ASSUMPTION):** Schematic-level presentation sufficient for County approval; advance to IFC drawings only after written County approval.

### T-2: Foundation Approach at Preliminary Stage

The conceptual plan implies a concrete slab-on-grade foundation (consistent with a concrete structure). The geotechnical report (DEL-008-01) may require modifications to the foundation approach, and foundation pricing is variable-price (Decomposition, D-009). The Architect should note the pending geotechnical determination in the preliminary presentation rather than committing to a specific foundation type.

### T-3: Renovation Scope Depth

The Old North Shop renovation is a smaller component of the overall design effort. At the preliminary stage, schematic plans indicating the renovation areas are sufficient. Detailed demolition and renovation drawings (DEL-001-09, DEL-001-10) are final design deliverables.
