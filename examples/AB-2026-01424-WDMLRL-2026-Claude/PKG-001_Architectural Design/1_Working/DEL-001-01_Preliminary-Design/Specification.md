# Specification — DEL-001-01 Preliminary Architectural Design

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-001-01 covers the production of a Preliminary Architectural Design presentation for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition (the "New North Shop") for submission to Camrose County for approval, as required by SOW-0010a (R-01, §3.3.2).

The Preliminary Architectural Design establishes:
- overall building massing, footprint, and spatial organization,
- room programming and functional layout,
- key architectural elements sufficient for County project team review and approval,
- design intent sufficient to enable coordination with other preliminary discipline designs (structural, mechanical, electrical, civil, plumbing, landscape architectural — DEL-002-01 through DEL-007-01).

The preliminary design addresses both the **New North Shop addition** (~13,000 sq ft useable area) and the **Old North Shop renovation** scope (kitchenette, mezzanine, washroom, locker/change room with laundry).

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Final (IFC) architectural drawings and specifications — covered by DEL-001-02 through DEL-001-11.
- Structural calculations or structural design — covered by PKG-002.
- Mechanical, electrical, civil, plumbing, or landscape design — covered by PKG-003 through PKG-007.
- Permit applications — covered by PKG-009.
- Construction — covered by PKG-010 through PKG-018.
- P.Eng. stamps — required on IFC drawings (final design stage); not required on preliminary presentation.

**Statement:** The Preliminary Architectural Design must be submitted to the Camrose County project team for review and approval before the Architect proceeds to final design documentation.
**Source:** R-01, §3.3.2 ("Deliver Preliminary Design for the County project team to approve"); SOW-0010a.
**Verification:** County written approval or sign-off on record. ### REQ-002 — Useable Area
**Statement:** The addition design shall target approximately 13,000 square feet of useable area.
**Source:** R-01, §1.0, §3.1.
**Verification:** Area calculation or scaled floor plan demonstrating compliance with target. TBD: Acceptance tolerance around "approximately 13,000 sq ft" is not defined in accessible sources (e.g., +/-5%, +/-10%). The Architect should propose an acceptable range for County confirmation (lensing item F-002). See also Guidance C-9 for rationale on the area target basis.

### REQ-003 — Building Program Completeness
**Statement:** The preliminary design shall include all spaces specified in RFP §3.1 and §3.4, at minimum: two drive-through bays (ASSUMPTION: count inferred from conceptual plan), enclosed wash bay, ventilated/lighted service pit, parts storage room (~400 sq ft with 6 ft roll-up door), utility room, office space, washroom/locker/change room with laundry, and mezzanine storage.
**Source:** R-01, §3.1, §3.4; R-04, App B.
**Verification:** Room schedule or program matrix cross-referenced against RFP §3.4 requirements.

### REQ-004 — Concrete Structure and Ceiling Height
**Statement:** The building structural concept shall be a concrete structure with a 35 ft clear ceiling height.
**Source:** R-01, §3.4; R-04, App B ("Concrete Structure (35' Ceiling)").
**Verification:** Section or elevation in the preliminary presentation showing 35 ft clear height.
**Note:** Structural design is within PKG-002 scope; the Architect must convey this requirement in the preliminary presentation to enable structural coordination.

### REQ-005 — Overhead Cranes (Spatial Accommodation)
**Statement:** The building layout shall accommodate two (2) 5-tonne overhead cranes on a trolley.
**Source:** R-01, §3.4; R-04, App B.
**Verification:** Preliminary floor plan showing crane bay locations consistent with the conceptual drawing.

### REQ-006 — Wash Bay
**Statement:** The design shall include a single enclosed bay dedicated for washing large equipment (e.g., motor scraper), with a mud sump exterior to the building accessible by excavator.
**Source:** R-01, §3.4; R-04, App B.
**Verification:** Wash bay shown on floor plan with mud sump location indicated.

### REQ-007 — Steel Plates in Floor
**Statement:** The architectural floor plan shall indicate the locations of steel plates embedded in the concrete floor to accommodate track and packer type heavy equipment, consistent with the conceptual drawing.
**Source:** R-01, §3.4; R-04, App B ("Steel Plates in Floor as indicated").
**Verification:** Steel plate locations indicated on preliminary floor plan.

### REQ-009 — Mezzanine Storage
**Statement:** The design shall include overhead mezzanine storage above the parts room and utility room, load-bearing, capable of handling heavy items such as several oil totes and oil pumping equipment.
**Source:** R-01, §3.4; R-04, App B ("Overhead Mezzanine over Parts Room/Utility Room/Wash Bay"; "Load Bearing Over Parts Room + Utility For Storage").

### REQ-010 — Old North Shop Renovation
**Statement:** The preliminary design shall address renovation of the Old North Shop including: (a) renovation of the 250 sq ft kitchenette area, (b) mezzanine renovation, (c) washroom renovation including addition of urinal, and (d) new locker/change room with laundry.
**Source:** R-01, §3.4; R-04, App B ("Old North Shop Mezzanine to be renovated and the washroom below renovated along with new locker room").
**Verification:** Renovation scope shown on preliminary floor plan; areas labeled.

ASSUMPTION: Current Alberta Building Code (ABC) edition and associated Safety Codes apply as of 2026. ### REQ-012a — Building Occupancy Classification
**Statement:** The preliminary design shall identify the building occupancy classification under the Alberta Building Code as a fundamental design parameter affecting exits, fire separation, and structural requirements.
**Source:** R-01, §3.3.2 (code compliance); Alberta Building Code (not directly accessible -- location TBD).
**Verification:** Occupancy classification stated in the design narrative of the preliminary presentation.
**Note:** ASSUMPTION: The building is likely Group F Division 2 or 3 under the Alberta Building Code (maintenance shop / industrial occupancy). The Architect must confirm the applicable classification. This classification is a binding compliance threshold that was not captured in the original requirements (identified via lensing item C-001).

### REQ-012b — Environmental and Hazardous Materials Considerations
**Statement:** The preliminary design shall acknowledge environmental and hazardous materials handling requirements applicable to a maintenance shop facility, including but not limited to: oil storage (oil totes on mezzanine), oil pumping equipment, wash bay drainage and effluent, welding fume exhaust, sump drain contents, and fuel/solvent storage if applicable.
**Source:** R-01, §3.4 (oil totes, oil pumping equipment, wash bay, welding station, sump drains); R-04, App B (layout showing oil storage, wash station areas).
**Verification:** TBD -- preliminary design narrative should acknowledge applicable environmental or hazardous materials regulations; detailed compliance at IFC stage.
**Note:** ASSUMPTION: A maintenance shop handling petroleum products, welding operations, and wash bay effluent is subject to provincial environmental regulations beyond the Alberta Building Code. Specific regulations are not identified in accessible sources (location TBD). Identified via lensing item C-002 as a regulatory coverage gap.

### REQ-012c — Cistern Location Compatibility
**Statement:** The preliminary floor plan shall demonstrate that the building layout accommodates the minimum 50,000 L water storage cistern, including adequate space for cistern access, plumbing connections, and ceiling clearance within the utility room or designated location.
**Source:** R-01, §3.4 ("Minimum 50,000 L Cistern complete with the necessary plumbing"); R-04, App B (cistern shown adjacent to utility room).
**Verification:** Floor plan showing cistern footprint allocation within building layout; coordination note with Plumbing Engineer (DEL-006-01) confirming spatial adequacy.
**Note:** The 50,000 L cistern is a major space-planning item. This requirement was not individually called out in the original requirements; REQ-003 lists program spaces but cistern accommodation was implicit. Identified via lensing item X-002.

### REQ-013 — Coordination with Geotechnical and Survey Data
**Statement:** The preliminary architectural design shall be informed by, or acknowledge dependency on, the geotechnical investigation (DEL-008-01) and preliminary site survey (DEL-008-02).
**Source:** R-01, §3.3.2; Decomposition SOW-0001, SOW-0002.
**Verification:** Design notes shall include: (a) acknowledgment of geotechnical and survey data status, (b) list of pending dependencies (e.g., geotech results, survey confirmation of building footprint), and (c) statement of foundation approach held open pending geotech (lensing item X-003). ASSUMPTION: This level of acknowledgment is appropriate for the preliminary stage.
