# Guidance — DEL-001-09 Old North Shop Demolition Plans

---

## Purpose

DEL-001-09 exists to provide the Architect's formal demolition drawing set for the three renovation areas within the existing Old North Shop. These drawings serve multiple downstream functions:

1. **Construction coordination** — They define precisely what is to be removed, so the general contractor executing PKG-017 (Existing Building Renovation) can scope and sequence demolition work without ambiguity.
2. **Permitting support** — Demolition drawings are typically required by the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) as part of the building permit application (DEL-009-02). (ASSUMPTION — standard practice; confirm with Camrose County / AHJ.)
3. **Design-to-construction handoff** — They bridge the gap between the existing building condition (as-found) and the renovation design shown in DEL-001-10, ensuring the renovation plans are grounded in accurate documentation of what is being removed.
4. **Objective alignment** — This deliverable directly supports OBJ-001 (code-compliant, fully functional renovations) and OBJ-003 (complete P.Eng.-stamped IFC documentation). (Source: Decomposition §5 and §7.)

## Principles

### P-001 — Document Before Demolish
Demolition plans should reflect verified existing conditions, not assumed ones. The Architect should confirm existing wall layouts, fixture locations, and structural elements through field verification or available as-built records before finalizing demolition drawings. Assumptions about existing conditions that are later found to be incorrect can propagate into the renovation design (DEL-001-10) and cause construction cost impacts.
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION — standard professional practice; not explicitly stated in RFP

### P-002 — Delineate Scope Boundaries Precisely
Demolition drawings must clearly distinguish: (a) elements to be fully removed, (b) elements to be partially removed or modified, and (c) elements to remain. In a renovation context where the contractor may be working around active or adjacent spaces, ambiguity about demolition limits is a significant risk.
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION — standard demolition drawing convention

### P-003 — Coordinate Structural Demolition Early
The mezzanine renovation (SOW-0071) may involve structural elements (mezzanine floor/framing above the washroom and mezzanine coffee room). Any structural elements within the demolition scope must be coordinated with the Structural Engineer (PKG-002) before the demolition drawings are issued. Structural demolition details, if any, will be part of PKG-002, not DEL-001-09.
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION — inferred from Appendix B (Shop) showing mezzanine noted as "2nd Level Mezzanine Coffee Room" above the existing Old North Shop area; structural mezzanine is part of PKG-002 scope

### P-004 — Align with Renovation Plans Throughout
DEL-001-09 (Demolition Plans) and DEL-001-10 (Renovation Plans) are complementary documents that together define the full extent of change to the Old North Shop. They should be developed in tandem by the same Architect, with cross-checks at each milestone to ensure what is shown as demolished in DEL-001-09 is consistent with the new construction shown in DEL-001-10.
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION — standard architectural practice; supported by RFP §3.3.2 (complete, coordinated final design obligation)

### P-005 — IFC Stamping Obligation
All drawings in this set must be stamped by a P.Eng. at the IFC stage per RFP §3.3.2. The design team should identify at the outset whether the Architect of Record holds a P.Eng. designation, or whether a P.Eng. must co-stamp the drawings. This affects the workflow and approval chain.
- **Source:** RFP §3.3.2 — "All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta."

## Considerations

### C-001 — Existing Building Survey / As-Built Information
The RFP provides only a conceptual floor plan (Appendix B / R-04). No as-built drawings of the existing Old North Shop are referenced in the available sources. The Architect should determine whether existing as-built drawings are available from Camrose County, or whether a field survey is required before producing demolition drawings. Inaccurate base plan data is a common source of change orders in renovation projects.

**Contingency for materially different field conditions (Enrichment: E-001):** If field conditions materially differ from the R-04/Appendix B conceptual plan (e.g., walls in different positions, additional elements discovered, different room configurations), the response strategy should include: (1) document the discrepancies in writing with photographs, (2) reassess the demolition scope against the renovation design intent for DEL-001-10, (3) notify the County project manager and the design-build team of scope implications, and (4) if discrepancies are significant enough to affect the renovation program, escalate to the project manager for a scope confirmation decision before proceeding with demolition design. TBD — the Architect should establish a threshold for "material difference" (e.g., dimensional variance > X% or discovery of structural elements not shown on R-04).
- **Source:** R-04 (Appendix B — Shop); ASSUMPTION — no other existing-building documentation visible in sources. **Proposed authority:** Architect.

### C-002 — Scope Interface with SOW-0073 and SOW-0074
SOW-0073 (new locker/change room with laundry) and SOW-0074 (expand washroom to include urinal) are also renovation scope items for the Old North Shop, but they are not listed under DEL-001-09. The Architect must determine whether demolition associated with SOW-0073/0074 should be shown in this deliverable or handled within DEL-001-10.
- **Source:** Decomposition §7 — DEL-001-09 lists SOW-0070/0071/0072; DEL-001-10 lists SOW-0070/0071/0072/0073/0074; R-04 (Appendix B — Shop) shows washroom and mezzanine proximity

### C-003 — Phasing and Building Occupancy
The RFP does not specify whether the Old North Shop will remain in use during renovation. If the building remains operational (e.g., ongoing landfill maintenance operations), demolition sequencing may be constrained by the Owner's operational needs. The Architect should flag this to the project manager for confirmation with Camrose County.
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION — RFP is silent on occupancy during renovation; standard precaution

### C-004 — Hazardous Materials
Existing buildings of uncertain age may contain hazardous materials (e.g., asbestos-containing materials, lead paint). The RFP does not reference any hazardous materials survey. The design team should determine whether a hazardous materials assessment is required before demolition design is finalized. If hazardous materials are present, the demolition scope and procedure may be subject to additional regulatory requirements under Alberta Occupational Health and Safety (OHS) legislation.

**Clarification (Enrichment: A-001):** The current language across Guidance C-004 and Procedure Step 1.3 uses hedging ("if required", "may contain") without establishing a clear decision threshold for when the hazardous materials assessment becomes mandatory vs. discretionary. The design team should establish an explicit decision rule: e.g., if the building predates a specific date (TBD -- confirm threshold with AHJ / Alberta OHS), then assessment is mandatory; otherwise, a documented risk evaluation by the Architect suffices to waive the assessment. Without such a threshold, different team members may reach different conclusions about whether to proceed with or without the assessment.
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION — standard due-diligence consideration; not addressed in available RFP sources. **Proposed authority:** AHJ / Alberta OHS legislation.

### C-005a — Salvage and Reuse Disposition (Enrichment: X-001)
Procedure Step 2.1 references "remove and salvage (if any)" as a demolition category, but Guidance provides no direction on when salvage applies or how the decision is made. The Architect should consult with the Owner (Camrose County) to determine whether any demolished elements (e.g., fixtures, cabinetry, materials) require salvage, reuse, or Owner-directed disposition. If salvage applies, the demolition drawings should note salvage items separately from items to be disposed of, and the Procedure should include a step for obtaining the Owner's salvage directive before finalizing demolition categories.
- **Source:** Procedure.md Step 2.1 (demolition categorization); ASSUMPTION — standard Owner consultation practice for renovation projects. **Proposed authority:** Owner / Camrose County.

Demolition of the kitchenette (SOW-0070), mezzanine coffee room (SOW-0071), and washroom (SOW-0072) will likely involve removal of mechanical (plumbing, HVAC) and electrical elements in those areas. These demolition elements are typically coordinated between architectural and MEP disciplines. Confirm with PKG-003/PKG-004/PKG-006 teams what MEP demolition is within their drawing scope vs. shown on architectural demolition drawings.

## Trade-offs

### T-001 — Breadth vs. Precision of Demolition Scope
**Trade-off:** A broader demolition scope on the drawings (showing more elements as "to be removed") provides more flexibility for the contractor but may create unnecessary waste and cost. A more precise scope reduces waste but increases change-order risk if conditions in the field differ from the drawings.
**Guidance:** At the IFC stage, prefer precision grounded in verified field conditions. At the preliminary design stage, it is acceptable to show a slightly broader scope with a note that limits will be confirmed in field.

**Evaluation framework (Enrichment: D-003):** The trade-off above lacks a concrete metric or threshold for judging whether the deliverable has achieved adequate scope precision at each stage. Consider the following stage-gated evaluation approach:
- **Preliminary design stage:** Acceptable if all demolition extents are shown to the nearest identifiable boundary (wall line, column grid line, or fixture group) and any unverified extents are noted as "subject to field confirmation." The metric is: zero unverified boundaries without a TBD annotation.
- **IFC stage:** Acceptable if all demolition extents are shown to dimensions verified by field survey data (per REQ-010 field verification sign-off) and no element remains categorized as TBD. The metric is: zero TBD demolition extents on IFC-issued sheets.
TBD — the Architect should confirm whether these thresholds are appropriate for this project or whether project-specific criteria apply.
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION — professional practice guidance. **Proposed authority:** Architect.

### T-002 — Combined vs. Separate Drawing Sheets
**Trade-off:** The three renovation areas (kitchenette, mezzanine, washroom) could be shown on a single demolition plan sheet (efficient, lower cost) or on separate sheets per area (clearer, but more documents).
**Guidance:** Given the relatively small scale of the Old North Shop (105' × 40') and the three discrete areas, a single annotated floor plan with clear area boundaries and a separate detail or enlarged plan per area is likely sufficient. This is an Architect's judgment call.
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION — standard practice; not prescribed in RFP
