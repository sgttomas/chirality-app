# Specification — DEL-001-09 Old North Shop Demolition Plans

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-001-09 is a Drawing Set deliverable produced by the Architect under PKG-001 (Architectural Design). It covers demolition plans for the three renovation areas of the existing Old North Shop:

1. **Kitchenette area** — approximately 250 sq ft (SOW-0070; RFP §3.4)
2. **Mezzanine (2nd-level coffee room)** — area TBD from field measurement (SOW-0071; R-04/Appendix B)
3. **Washroom below mezzanine** — area TBD from field measurement (SOW-0072; R-04/Appendix B)

These drawings document existing conditions and the extent of demolition/removal required to enable the renovations defined in DEL-001-10 (Old North Shop Renovation Plans) and constructed under PKG-017.

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Renovation construction drawings (covered by DEL-001-10)
- Demolition of any portion of the Old North Shop not enumerated above (SOW-0073 locker/change room and SOW-0074 washroom expansion are renovation scope, but their demolition extent must be coordinated with DEL-001-10; not independently scoped here unless confirmed)
- Structural demolition details (coordinated with PKG-002 / Structural Engineer)
- Mechanical or electrical demolition drawings (coordinated with PKG-003, PKG-004, PKG-006 disciplines)
- New North Shop addition drawings (covered by other DEL-001 deliverables)

## Requirements

### REQ-001 — Coverage of All Three Renovation Areas
The demolition plan drawing set shall address all three renovation areas: kitchenette (SOW-0070), mezzanine (SOW-0071), and washroom (SOW-0072).
- **Source:** Decomposition §7 PKG-001, DEL-001-09 entry; RFP §3.4
- **Verification:** All three areas identified and annotated on drawings

### REQ-002 — Existing Conditions Documentation
Drawings shall document existing-condition elements within each renovation area sufficient to establish demolition scope, including (at minimum): walls/partitions, doors, and fixtures to be removed.
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION — standard demolition drawing convention; supported by RFP §3.3.2 (complete design obligation) and IFC drawing requirements
- **Verification:** Architect confirms coverage of all existing elements within renovation extents

### REQ-003 — Demolition Extent Clearly Delineated
Drawings shall use standard graphic conventions (e.g., dashed or screened lines, "DEMO" notation, or equivalent legend) to distinguish elements to be demolished from elements to remain.
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION — standard drawing convention; not explicitly stated in RFP
- **Verification:** Legend present; demolish/remain elements clearly differentiated

### REQ-004 — Coordination with Renovation Plans
Demolition plans shall be coordinated with DEL-001-10 (Old North Shop Renovation Plans) such that items shown for demolition align with the renovation design intent.
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION — coordination requirement implicit in design-build delivery; consistent with RFP §3.3.2 requirement for complete final design
- **Verification:** Architect cross-check between DEL-001-09 and DEL-001-10

### REQ-005 — IFC Stamping
All Issued for Construction (IFC) drawings shall be signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer (P.Eng.) licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.
- **Source:** RFP §3.3.2 — "All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta."
- **Note:** The RFP specifically requires P.Eng. stamp. Whether an Architect's seal is sufficient or whether a structural P.Eng. must co-stamp demolition drawings is TBD — confirm with permitting authority. **Action required before IFC issue:** This ambiguity must be resolved prior to IFC production (see Guidance CONF-001 and Procedure Step 3.4). The current documents surface the conflict but lack a mechanism to force resolution before IFC issue. (Enrichment: A-002)
- **Verification:** Stamp and signature present on issued drawings

### REQ-006 — Code Compliance
The demolition design shall adhere to all applicable Alberta building codes, regulations, and Alberta Safety Codes.
- **Source:** RFP §3.3.2; §3.4 — "building design must adhere to all applicable building codes and regulations" and "all Alberta Safety Codes"
- **Verification:** Code review completed; no unresolved non-compliances at IFC issue

### REQ-007 — Preliminary Design Approval
A preliminary design shall be delivered to the County for approval before proceeding to IFC.
- **Source:** RFP §3.3.2 — "Deliver Preliminary Design for the County project team to approve"
- **Verification:** County approval documented before IFC issue

### REQ-008 — Kitchenette Area — Minimum Area
The kitchenette renovation area covered by demolition drawings shall correspond to the approximately 250 sq ft area stated in the RFP.
- **Source:** RFP §3.4 — "Renovate 250 square foot kitchenette area located in the existing building"
- **Verification:** Demolition extent confirmed against 250 sq ft boundary on drawings

### REQ-009 — Permit-Readiness of Drawing Set
The demolition drawing set shall be suitable for submission as part of the building permit application (DEL-009-02). Drawings shall contain sufficient information for AHJ review of demolition scope and compliance.
- **Source:** Guidance.md Purpose item 2 (permitting support function); Datasheet.md Construction section (DEL-009-02 relationship); RFP §3.3.2 (complete design obligation). ASSUMPTION — standard practice that demolition drawings support permit applications.
- **Verification:** Architect confirms drawing set meets AHJ submission requirements prior to permit filing (see also F-004 verification entry below)
- **(Enrichment: F-002)**

### REQ-010 — Field Verification Sign-Off
The Architect shall formally confirm that field survey data has been incorporated into the base plan before proceeding to preliminary demolition design. This sign-off ensures that drawings are based on verified existing conditions rather than solely on R-04 conceptual plan data.
- **Source:** Procedure.md Step 1.2 (field survey); REQ-002 (existing conditions documentation). ASSUMPTION — standard quality control practice for renovation projects.
- **Verification:** Documented sign-off that field data has been incorporated into base plan (see Procedure Phase 1 gate)
- **(Enrichment: X-002)**

### REQ-011 — Revision Control
Drawing sheets shall include revision control (revision number, date, and description of changes) as a formal requirement. Revision history shall be maintained on each drawing sheet title block.
- **Source:** Specification.md Documentation section ("Drawing title block with... revision history"); Procedure.md Verification table ("Drawing revision history current" row). ASSUMPTION — standard drawing management practice; formalizing existing implicit expectation.
- **Verification:** Each drawing sheet includes populated revision block with current revision information
- **(Enrichment: X-003)**

### REQ-012 — Records Management
The project team shall create and retain the records identified in Procedure.md Records section. This requirement establishes the normative basis for record retention referenced by the Procedure.
- **Source:** Procedure.md Records section (nine record types defined). ASSUMPTION — standard project documentation practice; provides normative basis for audit evidence.
- **Verification:** All records identified in Procedure.md Records section are created and filed per project filing conventions
- **(Enrichment: X-005)**
