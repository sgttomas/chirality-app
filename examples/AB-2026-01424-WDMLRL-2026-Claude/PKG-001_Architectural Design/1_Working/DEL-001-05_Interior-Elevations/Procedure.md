# Procedure — DEL-001-05 Interior Elevations

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce the Interior Elevations drawing set (DEL-001-05) for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition — New North Shop. It covers the Architect's workflow from information gathering through IFC issuance, including coordination checkpoints with other disciplines and required County review gates.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Establish Drawing Set Scope and Room List

1.1 Using the approved floor plan (DEL-001-02) and the Appendix B conceptual drawing (R-04), compile a complete list of all interior spaces requiring elevation coverage:
- Main repair bay (north side) — north, south, east, west walls
- Main repair bay (south side, if distinct) — as applicable
- Wash bay — all four walls
- Parts room — all four walls (including roll-up door wall)
- Utility room — all four walls (including water storage wall and mezzanine stair wall)
- Office — all four walls
- Washroom / locker / change room — all four walls (new scope and Old North Shop renovation area)
- Welding station zone — relevant walls (if not covered in main bay elevation)
- Service trench — both long walls and end walls
- Mezzanine level — perimeter guardrail elevation and storage area walls (as applicable)

1.2 Identify which rooms have repetitive conditions and prepare a repetition matrix to reduce redundant sheets while maintaining full documentation.

1.3 Assign drawing numbers per Architect's numbering convention.

**Verification:** Room list reviewed against floor plan; all spaces accounted for.

---

### Step 2 — Gather Vertical Datum and Coordination Information

2.1 Extract finished floor elevation (FFE) datum from building sections (DEL-001-04) and structural drawings.

2.2 Obtain from structural engineer (PKG-002):
- Underside of crane rail elevation (repair bays and wash bay) — reference to DEL-002-07.
- Mezzanine deck top-of-slab elevation — reference to DEL-002-05.
- Service trench depth below FFE — reference to DEL-002-06.
- Steel plate top-of-plate elevation at floor — reference to DEL-002-08.

2.3 Obtain from mechanical engineer (PKG-003):
- Approximate wall penetration zones for exhaust duct (welding station, repair bay exhaust hoses, wash bay ventilation).
- Make-up air unit soffit clearance (affects effective clear height shown in elevation).

2.4 Obtain from plumbing engineer (PKG-006):
- Washroom fixture rough-in dimensions (floor drain, wall-hung fixtures if applicable, laundry connection heights).
- Utility room: cistern connection heights and access requirements.
- Wash bay: floor drain slope direction reference and wall penetration for mud sump connection.

2.5 Record all received coordination information with date and source contact. Flag any items not yet received as TBD.

**Verification:** Coordination log complete; all TBD items identified with responsible party and expected date.

---

### Step 3 — Establish Interior Finish Zones

3.1 In coordination with the Architect's finish strategy (DEL-001-08 Finish Schedule), assign finish zones to each wall surface visible in interior elevations:
- Heavy industrial / concrete/CMU — main repair bays, wash bay.
- Light industrial — parts room, utility room.
- Office/administrative — office, washroom/change room.
- Renovation — Old North Shop washroom/locker area (match or upgrade existing finish level as directed).

3.2 Assign finish keynote symbols consistent with DEL-001-08.

3.3 Coordinate base condition (slab edge, cove base, or none) per space type.

**Verification:** All finish zones assigned and cross-checked against DEL-001-08 Finish Schedule.

---

### Step 3A — Building Inspection Coordination Planning (added per lensing item A-003)

3A.1 Review R-01 §3.3.2 inspection coordination requirements: "The County project representative must be present for all inspections and shall be provided with a copy of all complete inspection reports."

3A.2 Identify which building inspections are anticipated for the scope of work shown on interior elevations (e.g., framing inspection, barrier-free compliance, fire-separation, pre-close-in). The specific inspection list is TBD — Architect to confirm with AHJ and County project representative.

3A.3 Note inspection hold points on the IFC drawing set where construction must pause for inspection before finishes are applied (e.g., note on washroom/locker room elevations: "Inspection required before close-in — coordinate with County project representative per R-01 §3.3.2").

3A.4 Coordinate inspection scheduling requirements with the project manager and County project representative. Source: R-01 §3.3.2; Datasheet Conditions ("Inspection Coordination").

**Verification:** Inspection coordination plan documented; hold point notes identified for inclusion on IFC drawings.

---

### Step 4 — Draft Interior Elevation Drawings

**Readiness Gate (added per lensing item D-002):** Before proceeding to Step 4, confirm that the following critical coordination inputs from Step 2 have been received or have a documented expected date:
- Crane rail underside elevation from PKG-002 / DEL-002-07 (critical for repair bay and wash bay elevations)
- Mezzanine deck top-of-slab elevation from PKG-002 / DEL-002-05 (critical for mezzanine-level elevations)
- Service trench depth from PKG-002 / DEL-002-06 (critical for service trench wall elevations)

If any of these three critical inputs are still TBD with no expected resolution date, the Architect should escalate to the project manager before proceeding to drafting. Non-critical inputs (finish selections, receptacle locations) may remain TBD at drafting start and be added during coordination review (Step 5).

4.1 For each room and wall, draw interior elevation in CAD/BIM to scale. Minimum information per elevation view:
- Floor finish top-of-material line.
- Wall height to underside of structure or ceiling (as applicable).
- All openings: doors (with swing direction note), roll-up doors (head and sill heights), windows (if any), pass-through openings.
- All built-in elements visible in elevation: work benches, shelving (height and depth), wash station, gantry rails (reference).
- Finish keynotes.
- Dimension strings: floor-to-head at doors; floor-to-top-of-bench; floor-to-crane-rail (in repair bays); floor-to-mezzanine-deck.
- Penetration locations (MEP) shown as dashed circles/rectangles with discipline cross-reference.
- Section cut reference symbols where building sections cross the elevation view.

4.2 For service trench walls: show trench depth, ventilation penetration zones, light fixture rough-in zones (shown as coordination reference), and drain locations.

4.3 For mezzanine perimeter: show guardrail height reference (building code minimum — ASSUMPTION: 1,070 mm per NBC; confirm with AHJ), stair opening, and deck edge condition.

4.4 For parts room roll-up door: show full door opening (6 ft = 1,829 mm wide), head height, and door box clearance above head.

4.5 For wash bay: show crane rail reference elevation, floor drain position reference, wall penetration for mud sump drain, and exhaust/ventilation penetration zones.

4.6 For Old North Shop washroom/locker renovation: show existing conditions (to remain) and new/revised conditions clearly differentiated per project drawing convention.

**Verification:** Internal drawing QA check by Architect's project team before issuing for coordination.

---

### Step 5 — Issue for Coordination Review

5.1 Issue draft interior elevations to all discipline sub-consultants (structural, mechanical, electrical, plumbing, civil) and to the County project representative for coordination review.

5.2 Conduct coordination meeting (or written review) to resolve:
- Penetration locations: confirm MEP penetration positions match architectural elevations.
- Equipment clearances: confirm crane rail height is consistent with structural design and does not conflict with mechanical equipment.
- Finish boundaries: confirm finish transitions are constructable and code-compliant.
- Renovation scope: confirm Old North Shop washroom elevation is consistent with DEL-001-10 Renovation Plans.

5.3 Record all review comments and resolutions in a coordination log.

5.4 Update interior elevation drawings to reflect resolved comments.

5.5 Flag any unresolved items as TBD in the drawing set with a coordination note. Add unresolvable conflicts to the Conflict Table in Guidance.md.

**Verification:** Coordination log shows all comments addressed or tracked. No outstanding unresolved items without TBD notation.

---

### Step 5A — Coordination Resolution Quality Gate (added per lensing item F-004)

5A.1 Before proceeding to County approval check (Step 6) and IFC stamping (Step 7), review the coordination log from Step 5 and confirm:
- All coordination comments have been resolved (drawing updated) or tracked as TBD with responsible party and expected resolution date.
- No unresolved items affect life-safety or structural integrity (if any do, they must be resolved before IFC).
- The coordination log fields (date, discipline, item, resolution, status, responsible party) are complete for every entry.

5A.2 Obtain written or electronic sign-off from each discipline lead confirming their coordination review is complete. Record sign-off in the coordination log.

5A.3 If any coordination comments remain unresolved and cannot be tracked as TBD (e.g., conflicting information from two disciplines), escalate to the project manager and add to the Conflict Table in Guidance.md.

**Verification:** Coordination log shows all items resolved or tracked; discipline sign-offs recorded. This step satisfies the verification method for REQ-012 (Specification.md).

---

### Step 6 — County Preliminary Approval Confirmation

6.1 Confirm that County project team approval of the preliminary architectural design (DEL-001-01) has been documented before proceeding to IFC stamp.

6.2 If preliminary approval has not been received, do not issue IFC drawings. Report status to project manager.

6.3 **Building permit application timing (added per lensing item D-001):** Confirm the building permit application status relative to IFC issuance. Per R-01 §3.3.2, the Proponent is responsible for "obtaining a development permit and building permit from the County." The timing of permit application relative to IFC drawing issuance is TBD — the Architect / Owner should confirm whether:
- (a) Building permit application is submitted with IFC drawings (permit application accompanies IFC issuance), or
- (b) Building permit is obtained before IFC drawings are issued (permit precedes IFC), or
- (c) IFC drawings are issued first and permit application follows (IFC precedes permit).
Record the confirmed approach in the project record. Source: R-01 §3.3.2; Specification REQ-002 verification ("permit issuance by the County").

**Verification:** Written County approval on file. Permit application timing confirmed and documented.

---

### Step 7 — Issue for Construction (IFC)

7.1 Conduct final internal QA review of all interior elevation sheets against the checklist below. **QA review requirements (per lensing item X-005):**
- The QA review shall be conducted by a qualified member of the Architect's project team who is independent of the primary drafter (i.e., a different individual reviews than the one who produced the drawings).
- The reviewer shall complete the IFC Readiness Checklist (below) and record findings.
- Any findings shall be recorded with: item description, severity (critical / minor), resolution, and date resolved.
- The completed checklist and findings record shall be filed in the project record.

7.2 Submit drawing set to the Architect of Record / Professional Engineer of Record for stamp and signature.

7.3 Issue stamped IFC drawing set to:
- County project representative.
- General contractor / construction team.
- All sub-consultants for record.

7.4 File a copy of the stamped IFC set in the project record (PKG-001 Architectural Design / 0_References or equivalent).

**Verification:** Each IFC sheet bears a valid Alberta P.Eng. stamp and/or Architect stamp (per CFT-001-05-03 resolution) and wet or electronic signature per applicable Alberta professional legislation (ASSUMPTION: current Act versions applicable).

---

### Step 8 — Post-IFC Revisions and As-Built Reconciliation (added per lensing item A-004)

8.1 If field conditions during construction differ from the IFC interior elevation drawings (e.g., penetration locations shifted, wall layout modified, equipment clearances revised), the Architect shall:
- Issue revised interior elevation sheets with a revision cloud and revision number.
- Update the Drawing Revision Register.
- Distribute revised sheets to the County project representative, general contractor, and affected sub-consultants.

8.2 At project completion, reconcile interior elevation drawings with as-built conditions if required by the contract (CCDC 14-2013) or by the County. As-built reconciliation scope and format are TBD — Architect to confirm with the County and per contract requirements.

8.3 File final as-built or record set drawings in the project record.

**Verification:** Drawing Revision Register updated for all post-IFC revisions. As-built reconciliation completed per contract requirements (if applicable).
