# Guidance — DEL-001-05 Interior Elevations

---

## Purpose

DEL-001-05 Interior Elevations exists to provide the Architect's orthographic interior views of all principal spaces within the New North Shop addition and the associated Old North Shop renovation areas. These drawings serve as the primary reference for:

- Confirming vertical geometry (wall heights, header heights, equipment clearances) not fully legible from floor plans or sections alone.
- Communicating interior finish intent, built-in feature locations, and opening conditions to contractors and trades.
- Providing the spatial context needed by mechanical, electrical, and plumbing sub-consultants to locate penetrations, equipment, and fixtures on actual wall surfaces.
- Supporting code compliance review by the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) by showing door/opening configurations, guardrail locations, and accessible design features in vertical view.

This deliverable supports OBJ-001 (code-compliant, fully functional facility) and OBJ-003 (complete, P.Eng.-stamped, fully coordinated IFC documentation) as part of the PKG-001 Architectural Design package. Source: Decomp §5 (objectives), §7 (DEL-001-05 row explicitly lists OBJ-001 and OBJ-003); R-01 §3.3.2.

---

## Principles

### 1. Completeness of Spatial Coverage
Every room or zone that has distinct interior conditions — wall finishes, built-in elements, openings, or equipment interface conditions — should have at least one interior elevation view. For large repetitive bays (main repair bays), a representative elevation with a clear note of repetition is acceptable provided differences between bays are captured. Source: Decomp SOW-0011; R-01 §3.1.

### 2. Coordination Takes Priority Over Speed
The interior elevations are among the last architectural drawings developed before IFC issuance, and they integrate information from all other disciplines. Issue for coordination review before stamping. Unresolved penetration locations, equipment clearances, or finish boundaries are better captured as TBD notes in the drawing set than resolved incorrectly. Source: Decomp OBJ-003; R-01 §3.3.2.

### 3. Industrial Character of the Spaces
The New North Shop is a heavy industrial maintenance facility with 35 ft ceilings, 5-tonne overhead cranes, a ventilated service pit, and a wash bay designed for motor scrapers. Interior elevations for industrial bays should focus on functional geometry (crane rail elevation, door header heights, trench wall conditions, penetration locations) rather than finish detailing, which is minimal in industrial areas. Source: R-01 §3.4; R-04 (App B).

### 4. Renovation Scope Integration
The Old North Shop washroom and locker room renovation is architecturally linked to the new addition. Interior elevations for this renovation zone should be produced alongside the new building's elevations to ensure consistent finish language and plumbing coordination. Source: R-01 §3.4; R-04 (App B, "Old North Shop Mezzanine to be renovated and the washroom below renovated along with new locker room").

**Renovation Scope Boundary for Interior Elevations (per lensing item E-001):** The precise boundary of which Old North Shop rooms/walls require interior elevations under DEL-001-05 is TBD. R-01 §3.4 and R-04 confirm that the washroom below the mezzanine and the new locker room are in scope. It is unclear whether the mezzanine level above (2nd-level coffee room renovation per R-04) also requires interior elevations under this deliverable or is covered separately. The Architect should define the renovation scope boundary at preliminary design and confirm whether any mezzanine-level renovation areas require interior elevation views within DEL-001-05 or are deferred to DEL-001-10 Renovation Plans. Source: R-01 §3.4; R-04 (App B); Specification.md scope ("includes renovation scope for the Old North Shop washroom and locker room area where interior elevations are required").

### 5. Hierarchy of Drawing Information
Interior elevations reference and are subordinate to:
- Floor plans (DEL-001-02) for room identities and horizontal locations.
- Building sections (DEL-001-04) for vertical datum and overall height relationships.
- Structural drawings (PKG-002) for crane rail, mezzanine, service pit, and embedment conditions.
Interior elevations are the primary documents for:
- Wall finish callouts (keyed to DEL-001-08 Finish Schedule).
- Interior door/opening conditions (keyed to DEL-001-07 Door & Window Schedule).
- Millwork, shelving, and work bench reference heights.

## Considerations

### Crane Clearance in Main Repair Bays
The two 5-tonne overhead cranes on trolley dictate a critical underside-of-crane-rail elevation within the 35 ft clear ceiling height. The interior elevations for the repair bays must show this reference elevation and confirm adequate hook height above the floor for the intended equipment (motor scraper, heavy landfill equipment). The structural engineer's crane support design (DEL-002-07) and the mechanical engineer's make-up air unit locations (PKG-003) both compete for height in this zone. Coordinate early. Source: R-01 §3.4; R-04 (App B, "2 × 5 Ton Cranes").

### Service Pit / Trench Depth and Ventilation
The Service Pit is described as "ventilated and lighted" (R-01 §3.4). The trench wall interior elevations should show the trench depth (TBD — to be determined at final design based on mechanical vehicles serviced), ventilation duct penetration locations (for coordination with PKG-003), and lighting fixture rough-in positions (for coordination with PKG-004). The structural trench walls are designed by the structural engineer (DEL-002-06); the Architect shows the finished interior face with finish callouts.

### Mezzanine Loading Reference
The mezzanine over the parts room, utility room, and wash bay is specifically noted as capable of handling heavy items including "several oil totes and oil pumping equipment" (R-01 §3.4). The interior elevation should clearly show the mezzanine deck height above the floor level, guardrail height, and stair opening location. The structural loading capacity is the structural engineer's responsibility (DEL-002-05), but the Architect's interior elevations must be consistent with the structural design.

### Wash Bay Mud Sump Connection
The wash bay has an exterior mud sump (R-04, App B; R-01 §3.4). The interior elevation of the wash bay floor/wall interface should show the floor drain connection reference and the wall penetration for the mud sump drain — both for coordination with the plumbing engineer (PKG-006) and to confirm the wash bay floor slopes correctly toward the drain. Floor slope is a civil/architectural coordination item.

### Parts Room Roll-Up Door Head Clearance
The 6 ft wide roll-up door into the parts storage area (R-01 §3.4) is an interior roll-up door. The interior elevation must confirm head clearance for the door box/curtain, and whether the mezzanine above the parts room (if the roll-up door is on the mezzanine-side wall) affects this clearance.

### Washroom / Locker Room — Accessible Design
ASSUMPTION: Alberta building code accessible design requirements (barrier-free path of travel, accessible washroom stall dimensions) are likely applicable given this is a new building with washroom facilities, subject to code. The Architect should confirm applicable code provisions with the AHJ. The interior elevations for the washroom/locker room should reflect accessible dimensions if required.

**Note:** This assumption has been elevated to a tracked requirement (REQ-013) in Specification.md per lensing item C-001, given that barrier-free requirements are a compulsory compliance baseline for new buildings with washroom facilities under Alberta code. The Architect / AHJ must confirm specific provisions; until confirmed, REQ-013 status is TBD.

### Sump Drains in Repair Bays
The RFP specifies "sump drains in the repair bays" (R-01 §3.4). While this is primarily a plumbing/civil item, the interior elevation of the bay floor/wall interface may need to show the drain locations if they affect floor finish layout or base condition — coordinate with PKG-006.

### Inspection Report Scope for Interior Elevations
Per R-01 §3.3.2, "The County project representative must be present for all inspections and shall be provided with a copy of all complete inspection reports." The Procedure Records table lists "Inspection Report Copies" as a deliverable record. For the interior elevations scope specifically, the anticipated inspections are TBD but may include (per lensing item E-004):
- Framing inspections (wall framing for office, washroom, locker room, parts room partitions — if framed construction)
- Barrier-free compliance inspection (washroom/locker room accessible features, if required by AHJ)
- Firewall or fire-separation inspections (if interior walls have fire-rating requirements per code)
- Pre-drywall or pre-close-in inspection (to verify penetration locations match drawings before finishes are applied)

The Architect's role in these inspections is TBD — typically the Architect of Record coordinates inspection scheduling and reviews reports for conformance with IFC drawings. The County project representative attends all inspections per R-01 §3.3.2. Source: R-01 §3.3.2; Datasheet Conditions ("Inspection Coordination").

### Steel Plate Locations at Floor Level
The floor steel plates "at strategic locations to accommodate track and packer type heavy equipment" (R-01 §3.4, R-04) are structural embedments (DEL-002-08). However, if any steel plate edges are adjacent to a wall condition shown in interior elevation, the elevation should note the plate edge condition and finish transition.

## Trade-offs

| Trade-off | Option A | Option B | Guidance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Coverage depth for industrial bays | Full four-wall elevations for each bay | Representative elevation with repetition note | For identical repetitive conditions, a representative elevation with clear annotation is acceptable and reduces drawing production time without loss of information. |
| Level of detail for penetrations | Show all penetrations on architectural elevation sheets | Show penetration zones only; refer to MEP drawings for precise locations | ASSUMPTION: For IFC, show penetration locations coordinated with MEP disciplines on architectural elevations; precise sizing and detailing on MEP drawings. Coordinate positions must agree. |
| Finish level in shop areas | Full finish schedule callout on each elevation | Finish callout by zone on floor plan; elevations reference zone | Either approach is acceptable; consistency with DEL-001-08 Finish Schedule is the requirement. |
| Renovation scope drawings (Old North Shop) | Separate sheet within this drawing set | Separate sub-set cross-referenced to DEL-001-10 | ASSUMPTION: Including Old North Shop washroom/locker interior elevations within this drawing set (DEL-001-05) is the most efficient approach. Consistent with scope of SOW-0011 and renovation descriptions in R-01 §3.4. |

## Examples

The Appendix B (Shop) conceptual floor plan (R-04) provides the primary spatial reference for interior space configuration. Key features observable from the floor plan that inform interior elevation content:

- Main bays: two large bays with two 5-tonne cranes each; work benches at north wall; welding station at northeast; steel plates at multiple locations in both bays.
- Parts room: 16 ft width (per R-04 dimension); with roll-up door connecting to main bay; shelving on interior walls.
- Utility room: 50,000 L water storage; stairs to mezzanine; washroom adjacent.
- Wash bay: 24 ft wide (per R-04 dimension); gantry and work bench at east wall; steel plates at floor; mud sump exterior connection.
- Office: located between parts room and main bay entrance area; desk shown in R-04.
- Wash station: at the interface between the main bay area and parts/office area.

**Note:** Dimensions from R-04 (App B) are conceptual and subject to revision at final design. Do not treat conceptual dimensions as IFC-ready without Architect verification.
