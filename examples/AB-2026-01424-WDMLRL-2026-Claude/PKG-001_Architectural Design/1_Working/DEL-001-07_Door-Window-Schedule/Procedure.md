# Procedure — DEL-001-07 Door & Window Schedule

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce the Door & Window Schedule for DEL-001-07, from information gathering through IFC-ready completion. The schedule is a design deliverable within PKG-001 (Architectural Design) and must be complete before the IFC drawing set is issued (SOW-0018).

The procedure also covers the use of the completed schedule by the construction team: the schedule is a reference document used throughout the construction phase for procurement, installation, and inspection of all door and window units.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Conduct Code Analysis

**Responsible:** Architect

1.1 Determine the occupancy classification(s) of all spaces in the New North Shop Addition and Old North Shop renovation areas under the Alberta Building Code.

1.2 Identify required fire separations between occupancies. Determine which door openings fall within fire-rated separations and what fire rating is required for each.

1.3 Determine egress requirements: minimum door widths, swing direction, panic hardware requirements, number of required exits.

1.4 Determine natural light and ventilation requirements for occupied spaces (office, washroom, locker/change room). Determine whether windows are required by code.

1.5 Document findings in a code compliance summary (to be incorporated into DEL-009-04 Code Compliance Register and DEL-001-11 Architectural Specification).

**Output:** Code compliance analysis — informs all subsequent steps.

---

### Step 2 — Confirm Program with Owner

**Responsible:** Architect (with Project Manager facilitating Owner communication)

2.1 Confirm equipment fleet specifications with Owner (Camrose County / Darren King — Manager, per RFP §1.3.4). Obtain transport height of motor scraper and other large equipment to be driven into repair bays and wash bay. This determines minimum overhead door heights for D-OHD-01, D-OHD-02, D-OHD-03.

2.2 Confirm the intended arrangement of separate entrance/exit doors (SOW-0026): Are these two separate door assemblies? Are they on different walls? What are the security and access control requirements?

2.3 Confirm whether windows are desired by Owner in any areas of the Addition or renovation, beyond what code requires.

2.4 Confirm keying strategy and security requirements for all personnel doors.

2.5 Document Owner responses in meeting minutes or written correspondence for record (supports SOW-0086 weekly coordination and SOW-0089 quality control).

**Output:** Confirmed program information for door heights, entrance/exit arrangement, window policy, and security.

---

### Step 3 — Develop Door and Window Tag List

**Responsible:** Architect

3.1 Based on confirmed floor plans (DEL-001-02) and code analysis (Step 1), compile a complete list of all door and window openings in the project scope. Include:
- All openings in the New North Shop Addition
- All new or modified openings in the Old North Shop renovation areas

3.2 Assign a unique tag to each opening following the Architect's office numbering convention. (Draft tags from Datasheet.md — D-OHD-01 through D-RENO-02 — may be adopted or revised per office standard.)

3.3 Cross-check the tag list against the floor plans to ensure every marked opening on the drawings has a corresponding schedule entry, and every schedule entry corresponds to a marked opening on the drawings.

3.4 Identify all motorized doors. Flag these for electrical coordination (Step 5).

**Output:** Complete, cross-checked tag list.

---

### Step 4 — Populate the Schedule

**Responsible:** Architect

4.1 For each tagged door opening, populate all schedule columns:
- Tag / Mark
- Quantity
- Location (room name and wall)
- Type (sectional overhead, roll-up, hinged, sliding, etc.)
- Nominal width
- Nominal height (confirmed from Owner for overhead doors; code minimum for egress doors; Architect judgment for interior doors)
- Frame material
- Glazing (if any)
- Operation (manual, motorized, self-closing, etc.)
- Fire label / fire rating (from code analysis — Step 1)
- Hardware group (from hardware matrix — Step 4.2)
- Finish / paint
- Remarks (e.g., "SOW-0026 entrance door," "motor scraper clearance required," "coordinate electrical — SOW-0060")

4.2 Develop hardware groups. Assign hardware sets based on door function, security zone, accessibility requirements, and fire rating:
- Hardware Group A (example): Heavy-duty industrial overhead door operator — for OHD doors
- Hardware Group B (example): Heavy-duty exterior personnel door set — for entrance/exit doors (panic hardware if required by code)
- Hardware Group C (example): Standard interior commercial door set — for office, utility room, washroom
- Actual hardware groups to be defined per Architect's office standard and code requirements

4.3 For each tagged window opening (if any), populate the window schedule with equivalent columns: tag, location, type, width, height, glazing type, U-value, operation, finish, remarks.

4.4 If no windows are required (code analysis confirms not required and Owner does not want them), the window schedule shall state: "No windows — not required by code and not included in project scope per Owner direction. [date confirmed]"

**Output:** Draft Door & Window Schedule with all columns populated or marked TBD.

---

### Step 4A — Draft Completeness Check (Gate)

**Responsible:** Architect

**[Enriched per D-002: Steps 4 and 5 previously flowed without a confirmation gate; there was no checkpoint to confirm the draft schedule is internally complete before coordinating with other disciplines.]**

Before proceeding to engineering coordination (Step 5), verify the draft schedule is internally complete:

4A.1 Confirm all known door openings have been assigned tags and populated in the schedule (no missing entries from the tag list developed in Step 3).

4A.2 Confirm all available data columns are populated with either confirmed values or explicit TBD entries with justification.

4A.3 Confirm hardware groups have been assigned (at minimum as preliminary groupings).

4A.4 Confirm window schedule status is resolved (either populated or documented as "not required" per code analysis and Owner direction).

4A.5 If any critical data is missing that would prevent meaningful engineering coordination (e.g., no door sizes at all), resolve before proceeding to Step 5.

**Output:** Draft schedule confirmed internally complete and ready for external coordination.

---

### Step 5 — Coordinate with Engineering Packages

**Responsible:** Architect (coordinating with Structural, Electrical, Mechanical Engineers)

5.1 **Structural coordination:** Provide the complete list of all door and window openings with confirmed rough opening sizes to the Structural Engineer (PKG-002). Obtain confirmation that each opening is structurally feasible and that the structural drawings reflect correct header/lintel design for each opening.

5.2 **Electrical coordination:** Provide the list of all motorized doors (D-OHD-01, D-OHD-02, D-OHD-03, D-RUD-01 if motorized, and any others) with location, operator specifications, and power requirements to the Electrical Engineer (PKG-004). Confirm that SOW-0060 coverage is complete. Ensure that each motorized door appears in the Electrical package load schedule and panel schedules (DEL-004-06).

5.3 **Mechanical coordination:** Confirm that overhead doors in repair bays are reflected in the HVAC design (PKG-003) — large overhead door openings in an Alberta climate represent significant heat loss and makeup air demand. Ensure the mechanical design accounts for door-open scenarios.

5.4 Document coordination exchanges and any resulting changes to the schedule or drawings.

**Output:** Coordinated schedule confirmed with structural and electrical packages; discrepancies resolved.

---

### Step 6 — Internal Quality Review

**Responsible:** Architect (QA/QC review by senior staff or second reviewer)

6.1 Verify that every door and window mark on the floor plans (DEL-001-02), building sections (DEL-001-04), interior elevations (DEL-001-05), and reflected ceiling plans (DEL-001-06) appears in the schedule.

6.2 Verify that door heights are adequate for the intended use:
- Overhead doors: confirm motor scraper clearance (Step 2 result)
- Egress doors: confirm minimum clear width per Alberta Building Code
- Interior doors: confirm minimum height for accessibility where required

6.3 Verify fire ratings are assigned to all openings in fire-rated separations.

6.4 Verify that all motorized doors are flagged for electrical coordination.

6.5 Verify that hardware groups are complete and consistent.

6.6 Verify that all renovation-area door openings are included and correctly designated.

6.7 Resolve any TBD items remaining from Step 4 and Step 5.

**Output:** Quality-reviewed schedule, ready for IFC issue.

---

### Step 7 — Issue for Construction (IFC)

**Responsible:** Architect / Professional of Record

7.1 Incorporate the completed Door & Window Schedule into the IFC architectural drawing set per the Architect's drawing organization standard.

7.2 Obtain professional stamp and signature from the Architect of Record (or P.Eng. as applicable under Alberta regulations) on the IFC drawing sheets containing the schedule.

7.3 Issue the IFC drawing set to the County for approval per the approval process established at project award (SOW-0086, weekly coordination with County project manager).

7.4 Provide the IFC set to the General Contractor for construction. The General Contractor shall use the Door & Window Schedule as the primary reference for door and window procurement and installation.

**Output:** Stamped, issued IFC Door & Window Schedule drawing sheet(s).

---

### Step 8 — Construction Phase Use

**Responsible:** General Contractor, with Architect oversight

8.1 The General Contractor shall use the Door & Window Schedule as the primary procurement reference. Each door and window unit shall be ordered per the specifications in the schedule.

8.2 For overhead doors (D-OHD-01 through D-OHD-03), the General Contractor shall coordinate electrical rough-in for operators with the Electrical Contractor before concrete walls and floors are poured (SOW-0060).

8.3 Shop drawings for overhead doors, roll-up doors, and hardware shall be submitted to the Architect for review before fabrication or procurement. **[Enriched per X-004: acceptance criteria for shop drawing review were previously undefined.]** The Architect shall review shop drawings against the following acceptance criteria:

- Submitted door dimensions (width, height, thickness) match the schedule within allowable manufacturing tolerances
- Fire rating label matches the specified rating (where applicable)
- Hardware and operator specifications match the schedule requirements
- Material and finish match the schedule specifications
- Structural attachment details are consistent with the structural design (coordination with PKG-002)

Shop drawings shall be stamped "Reviewed," "Reviewed as Noted," "Revise and Resubmit," or "Rejected" per the Architect's office standard. The Architect should establish an expected turnaround time for shop drawing review (TBD -- typically 10-15 business days for industrial projects).

8.4 At installation, the General Contractor shall verify that each installed unit matches the schedule specifications (type, size, fire label, hardware group) before requesting inspection.

8.5 Any field changes to door or window openings require written Architect approval and a schedule revision before implementation. The following changes constitute a formal revision trigger requiring a new schedule revision issue: **[Enriched per X-003: revision trigger criteria were previously undefined.]**

- Any change to door type, size (width or height), or fire rating after IFC issue
- Any change to hardware group assignment
- Addition or deletion of a door or window opening
- Any change to operator type or power requirements for motorized doors
- Any change required by the AHJ during permit review or inspection

Minor field adjustments that do not affect the above parameters (e.g., correction of a room name typo) do not require a formal revision but shall be documented in the drawing revision block notes.
