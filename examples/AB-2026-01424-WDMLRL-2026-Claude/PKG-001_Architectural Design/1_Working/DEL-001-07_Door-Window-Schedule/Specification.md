# Specification — DEL-001-07 Door & Window Schedule

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-001-07 is the Architect's Door & Window Schedule for the 2026 West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill (WDMLRL) Maintenance Shop Addition. It covers:

- All door openings in the New North Shop Addition (~13,000 sq ft, concrete structure, 35' ceiling), including:
  - Drive-through repair bay sectional overhead doors
  - Wash bay sectional overhead door
  - Parts storage room roll-up door
  - Separate entrance and exit doors required by RFP §3.1 (SOW-0026)
  - All interior doors serving office, utility room, washroom, and other rooms within the Addition
- Door openings in the Old North Shop renovation areas (washroom renovation SOW-0072; new locker/change room SOW-0073), to the extent that new or modified door openings are part of the renovation scope
- Window openings (if any) in the Addition and in the renovation areas, as determined by the Architect under Alberta Building Code requirements

This schedule is a design-phase deliverable within PKG-001 (Architectural Design) and is part of the Issued for Construction (IFC) drawing set (SOW-0018).

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Structural design of door/window openings and headers (DEL-002 — Structural Design package)
- Electrical power wiring to overhead door operators (DEL-004, DEL-015 — Electrical packages; SOW-0060)
- Hardware procurement (construction phase; schedule defines hardware groups only)
- Mechanical louvers or ventilation openings that are not classified as doors or windows under the building code
- Doors in the Old North Shop existing areas not affected by the renovation scope

## Requirements

### REQ-DW-001 — Separate Entrance/Exit Doors

The New North Shop Addition shall be provided with separate entrance and exit doors, as required by RFP §3.1 (SOW-0026). The schedule shall identify these doors by tag, location, type, size, hardware group, and fire rating.

**Source:** RFP §3.1; SOW-0026 (R-01, location confirmed)

### REQ-DW-002 — Repair Bay Overhead Doors

The two drive-through repair bays shall each have a sectional overhead door of minimum 24' nominal width. The door height shall provide minimum clearance equal to the tallest equipment transport height plus a safety margin (TBD -- numeric criterion to be established once the Owner provides equipment fleet dimensions). **[Enriched per C-002: the height acceptance threshold was absent; a numeric criterion is required once equipment dimensions are confirmed.]** **PROPOSAL:** Add numeric height criterion once equipment dimensions are confirmed.

**Source:** App B conceptual drawing (R-04) — 24' bay widths shown; RFP §3.1 (drive-through bays); SOW-0025

### REQ-DW-003 — Wash Bay Overhead Door

The wash bay enclosure shall be provided with a sectional overhead door of minimum 24' nominal width. The door height shall accommodate motor scraper-class equipment, with a minimum clearance equal to the motor scraper transport height plus a safety margin (TBD -- numeric criterion to be established once the Owner provides equipment dimensions). **[Enriched per C-002: height acceptance threshold was absent; same criterion approach as REQ-DW-002.]**

**Source:** App B conceptual drawing (R-04) — 24' wash bay width shown; RFP §3.4 (single enclosed bay for motor scraper); SOW-0027a

### REQ-DW-004 — Parts Storage Room Roll-Up Door

The parts storage room shall be provided with a roll-up door of minimum 6 feet (6') nominal width. The height shall be determined by the Architect per operational and code requirements.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("400 square foot secure parts storage area containing a 6 foot wide roll-up door"); SOW-0029 (R-01, confirmed)

### REQ-DW-005 — Schedule Completeness

The Door & Window Schedule shall enumerate all door and window openings in the Addition and in the renovation scope areas. For each opening, the schedule shall include at minimum:

- Door/Window tag (unique identifier)
- Location / room description
- Type (e.g., sectional overhead, roll-up, hinged, sliding, glazed)
- Nominal size (width × height)
- Frame material
- Glazing (for windows and glazed doors)
- Operation (e.g., manual, motorized)
- Fire rating (if applicable)
- Hardware group designation
- Finish
- Remarks / special notes

**REQ-DW-005a — Schedule Presentation Standard:** The Door & Window Schedule drawing sheet(s) shall be legible at the issued drawing size, shall follow a consistent tabular format, and shall include cross-references to the architectural floor plans (DEL-001-02) identifying each opening location. The format shall be consistent with the Architect's office standard for IFC drawing sets. **[Enriched per C-004: REQ-DW-005 listed required data columns but did not address drawing sheet quality, format consistency, or cross-referencing convention.]** **PROPOSAL:** Specification or Guidance should address schedule presentation standards.

**Source:** Standard architectural schedule practice (ASSUMPTION — no schedule template specified in RFP); consistent with IFC drawing set requirements (SOW-0018)

### REQ-DW-006 — Alberta Building Code Compliance

All doors and windows shall be designed and specified in compliance with all applicable Alberta building codes and regulations, including but not limited to requirements for:

- Means of egress (exit door widths, swing direction, hardware)
- Fire separations and fire-rated assemblies (where occupancy separation requires rated openings)
- Accessibility — The Architect shall determine and explicitly state whether accessibility requirements apply to the Addition based on the occupancy classification under the Alberta Building Code. If accessibility applies, the specific requirements (accessible hardware, clear widths, thresholds, maneuvering clearances) shall be identified and incorporated into the schedule. **[Enriched per C-001: the previous qualifier "where applicable" was ambiguous and could lead to the requirement being overlooked; the Architect must make an affirmative determination.]**
- Natural light and ventilation for occupied spaces (relevant to window placement)

**REQ-DW-006a — Fire Rating Determination Methodology:** The Architect shall determine fire ratings for all door openings using a documented methodology referencing the Alberta Building Code occupancy classification process. The method shall identify the occupancy classification(s) of spaces on each side of the opening and apply the corresponding fire separation requirements. The fire rating determination shall be documented and traceable. **[Enriched per A-001: REQ-DW-006 required ABC compliance but did not prescribe the method for determining fire ratings.]** Source: Specification REQ-DW-006; Guidance Principles §5 (Fire Rating Determination). **PROPOSAL:** Architect to specify fire rating determination method per ABC.

**REQ-DW-006b — Code Analysis Scope and Acceptance:** The code compliance analysis (Procedure Step 1) shall address all occupancy classifications within the project scope, all fire separations between occupancies, all egress paths, and all accessibility determinations. The analysis is considered sufficient when each of these elements has been explicitly addressed and documented. **[Enriched per D-001: REQ-DW-006 required compliance but had no acceptance standard for the analysis process itself.]** Source: Specification REQ-DW-006; Procedure Step 1. **PROPOSAL:** Add proficiency standard for code analysis deliverable.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 ("building design must adhere to all applicable building codes and regulations"); SOW-0008; SOW-0009 (R-01)

### REQ-DW-007 — IFC Drawing Stamp

The Door & Window Schedule, as part of the IFC architectural drawing set, shall be signed and stamped by a professional engineer (or architect, as applicable under Alberta regulations) licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.

**ASSUMPTION:** The RFP §3.3.2 requirement for P.Eng. stamping of all IFC drawings is interpreted to apply to the full drawing set including schedules. Alberta practice may allow the architect of record to seal architectural drawings; the applicable professional designation (architect vs. engineer) is TBD pending confirmation of project team qualifications.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0018 (R-01)

### REQ-DW-008 — Overhead Door Power Coordination

The schedule shall identify all overhead doors and motorized roll-up doors requiring electrical power connections, so that this information can be coordinated with the Electrical package (PKG-004/DEL-004, PKG-015/DEL-015; SOW-0060). For each motorized door, the schedule shall include or reference the following operator specifications to support electrical coordination:

- Operator type (e.g., chain hoist, trolley, jackshaft)
- Operator voltage and phase
- Operator horsepower or power rating
- Control requirements (e.g., push button, key switch, remote)

**[Enriched per X-002: Procedure Step 5.2 references "operator specifications and power requirements" but the Specification did not require these as schedule data fields; adding them supports electrical coordination under SOW-0060.]** **PROPOSAL:** Add operator specification fields to REQ-DW-005 column list or REQ-DW-008. TBD values are acceptable until the Architect selects specific operators.

**Source:** SOW-0060 ("Provide power for all overhead doors"); Decomposition §3 Group G (R-DECOMP); Procedure Step 5.2

### REQ-DW-009 — Renovation Scope Door Openings

New or modified door openings in the Old North Shop renovation areas (washroom renovation — SOW-0072; new locker/change room — SOW-0073) shall be included in the schedule. Existing doors to be retained without modification are excluded unless they require upgrading to meet code.

**Source:** SOW-0072, SOW-0073; Decomposition §7 DEL-001-09, DEL-001-10 (R-DECOMP); RFP §3.4

### REQ-DW-010 — Mezzanine Access Door (Conditional)

If the mezzanine design and applicable code requirements (fall protection, fire separation, or egress) require a door at the mezzanine stair landing or guard enclosure, that opening shall be enumerated in the schedule with all required attributes. The Architect shall determine whether a mezzanine access door is required as part of the code analysis (REQ-DW-006). If not required, this requirement is inapplicable and shall be recorded as such.

**[Enriched per C-003: Guidance raised the possibility of a mezzanine landing door but there was no corresponding requirement in Specification.]** **PROPOSAL:** Architect to determine if mezzanine door is required and add to requirements if so.

**Source:** App B (R-04) — mezzanine stairs shown (SOW-0034); Guidance Considerations — Mezzanine Access
