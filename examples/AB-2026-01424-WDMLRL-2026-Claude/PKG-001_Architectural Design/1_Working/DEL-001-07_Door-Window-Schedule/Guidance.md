# Guidance — DEL-001-07 Door & Window Schedule

---

## Purpose

The Door & Window Schedule is a standard architectural deliverable that provides a tabulated reference for every door and window opening in the building. Its purpose in this project context is threefold:

1. **Design communication:** It ensures that every opening is explicitly identified, sized, typed, and specified — preventing ambiguity between architectural, structural, mechanical, and electrical packages during design coordination.
2. **Construction coordination:** The schedule is the primary reference for the General Contractor (PKG-011, PKG-012, PKG-017) and subcontractors when ordering, installing, and inspecting door and window units. It eliminates reliance on reading dimensions from floor plans.
3. **Regulatory compliance tracking:** Egress requirements, fire separation ratings, and accessible hardware must all be traceable to specific doors. The schedule is the instrument for that traceability during building permit review and inspections.

For DEL-001-07 specifically, the schedule must be completed as part of the IFC drawing set (SOW-0018) and must reflect all decisions made in the final architectural design (SOW-0011) and the renovation design (SOW-0070–SOW-0074).

Source: RFP §3.1, §3.3.2, §3.4 (R-01); Decomposition DEL-001-07 (R-DECOMP)

## Principles

### 1. Industrial Building Character

The WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition is a concrete industrial facility with a 35' ceiling height designed to accommodate very large heavy equipment, including motor scrapers. Door selection must reflect this:

- Overhead sectional doors in the repair bays and wash bay must be sized not merely for vehicle clearance but for the specific equipment classes operated at this landfill site. Motor scraper height is the controlling dimension for the wash bay door. The Architect should confirm equipment dimensions with the Owner.
- Hardware and finish specifications must be durable for a heavy industrial environment (exposure to grit, grease, water, and vehicle traffic near door openings).
- Standard residential or light-commercial door hardware groups are not appropriate for this project.

**Hardware Selection Criteria for Industrial Use:** When selecting industrial-grade hardware, the Architect should evaluate the following criteria to ensure suitability for this facility type. **[Enriched per A-005: the previous statement that light-commercial hardware is inappropriate did not provide criteria for what constitutes adequate industrial-grade hardware.]**

- **Load rating:** Overhead door operators must be rated for the door weight at the specified size (24' wide doors at TBD height represent substantial weight; operators must handle repeated cycling under load).
- **Corrosion resistance:** Hardware exposed to wash bay moisture, road salt residue, and industrial chemicals should be specified with corrosion-resistant finishes (galvanized, stainless steel, or epoxy-coated as appropriate).
- **Cycle life:** Industrial overhead doors in an active maintenance shop will experience significantly higher cycle counts than commercial applications. Specify operators and springs rated for high-cycle use (minimum cycle rating TBD by Architect based on anticipated usage frequency).
- **Impact resistance:** Personnel door hardware in vehicle traffic areas should be heavy-duty rated to withstand incidental contact from equipment and materials handling.

**PROPOSAL:** Guidance should outline hardware selection criteria for industrial use. Source: RFP §3.1, §3.4 (R-01); Guidance Principle 1.

Source: RFP §3.1, §3.4; App B (R-01, R-04)

### 2. Separation of Entrance and Exit

The RFP explicitly requires separate entrance and exit doors for the Addition (§3.1, SOW-0026). This is a programmatic requirement, not merely a code requirement. The Architect must design a circulation pattern that physically separates pedestrian entry flow from exit flow — relevant for safety in an active vehicle workshop environment.

**ASSUMPTION:** The intent of §3.1 is operational safety (workers not crossing vehicle paths); exact locations and door types for the entrance and exit doors are an Architect judgment call not resolved by available sources.

Source: RFP §3.1; SOW-0026 (R-01)

### 3. Coordination with Structural Package

Door and window openings in a concrete structure require structural headers, lintels, or framed openings. Every opening in the schedule must be coordinated with DEL-002 (Structural Design) to ensure structural adequacy. Opening sizes should be confirmed jointly with the Structural Engineer before the IFC drawings are issued.

Key coordination points:
- Overhead door rough opening sizes (24' wide minimum) require beam or transfer structure above
- Any penetrations through concrete walls for windows or pedestrian doors require coordination
- The 35' concrete wall system constrains where openings can be placed without affecting structural integrity

Source: RFP §3.4, App B; SOW-0012 (R-01, R-04, R-DECOMP)

### 4. Coordination with Electrical Package

All motorized overhead doors and motorized roll-up doors require electrical power (SOW-0060). The Electrical Engineer (PKG-004) must be provided a complete list of motorized doors, with location, operator voltage, and circuit requirements. The Door & Window Schedule is the primary source document for this coordination.

Source: SOW-0060; Decomposition §3 Group G (R-DECOMP)

### 5. Fire Rating Determination

The Architect must assign fire ratings to door openings based on:
- The occupancy classification(s) of the spaces on either side of each opening
- Alberta Building Code fire separation requirements between occupancies (e.g., between the shop floor and office/washroom areas)
- Egress corridor requirements (if any corridor is designated as a means of egress)

No fire ratings are determinable from the conceptual drawings (R-04) or the RFP. The Architect must conduct a full code analysis.

Source: REQ-DW-006 (Specification.md); RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0008 (R-01)

## Considerations

### Window Requirements

No windows are shown or mentioned in the RFP or Appendix B conceptual drawings. However, the Architect should evaluate:

- Alberta Building Code requirements for natural light in the office space
- Alberta Building Code requirements for natural ventilation in the washroom (if mechanical ventilation is not provided)
- Site security implications of windows in an industrial facility at a remote landfill location
- Thermal performance: windows in a concrete industrial building in a cold Alberta climate represent significant thermal bridging and heat loss risk; if included, high-performance glazing is warranted

**ASSUMPTION:** If windows are not required by code and are not desired by the Owner, a "no windows" approach is architecturally defensible for this occupancy type. The Architect should confirm with the Owner.

### Overhead Door Thermal Performance

The overhead doors (D-OHD-01, D-OHD-02, D-OHD-03) represent the largest thermal bridges and air infiltration risk points in the building envelope. At 24' width and TBD height (potentially 16'+ openings), each overhead door is a substantial thermal weak point in a facility operating in Alberta's cold climate with a 35' ceiling height (significant air volume to heat). The Architect should consider: **[Enriched per D-003: Guidance discussed thermal performance for windows but not for overhead doors, which represent a much larger thermal impact.]**

- **Insulated door panels:** Specify insulated sectional overhead door panels with a minimum R-value appropriate for the Alberta climate zone. Polyurethane-injected steel panels are typical for industrial applications.
- **Air infiltration at seals:** Weather seals at the bottom, sides, and header of overhead doors are critical. Air infiltration through large overhead door perimeters can dominate the building's heating load when doors are closed.
- **Door-open scenarios:** When overhead doors are open for equipment movement, the entire bay volume is exposed. The mechanical design (PKG-003) must account for makeup air and rapid recovery. The Architect should coordinate with the Mechanical Engineer on door-open duration assumptions.
- **U-value specification:** Consider specifying a maximum U-value for overhead door assemblies. TBD -- Architect to determine appropriate thermal performance targets in coordination with HVAC design.

This consideration is relevant to the overall energy performance of the facility and should be coordinated with DEL-003 (Mechanical Design) and the energy requirements under the Alberta Building Code.

Source: RFP §3.4 (R-01) -- 35' ceiling, motor scraper equipment; Guidance Principles §1 (Industrial Building Character); Procedure Step 5.3 (Mechanical coordination).

### Renovation Door Openings (Old North Shop)

The renovation of the Old North Shop washroom (SOW-0072) and the construction of the new locker/change room (SOW-0073) will require door openings that must be coordinated with the existing structure. The Architect must:
- Confirm existing wall construction (frame, block, or concrete) to determine header/lintel requirements
- Confirm existing door rough openings and whether they can be reused, modified, or must be replaced
- Include these doors in the schedule with a clear "RENOVATION — EXISTING" or "RENOVATION — NEW" designation

This information requires a site investigation and is not determinable from the conceptual drawings.

Source: SOW-0072, SOW-0073; App B (R-04)

### Mezzanine Access

The conceptual drawing (App B) shows stairs to the mezzanine (SOW-0034). If the mezzanine access requires a door (e.g., a landing door or guard enclosure), this opening must be included in the schedule. The drawing does not show a door at the mezzanine stair landing.

**ASSUMPTION:** A mezzanine landing door may or may not be required depending on the mezzanine design and the applicable code requirements for fall protection. This is a judgment call for the Architect.

Source: App B (R-04); SOW-0032, SOW-0034

### Wash Station Area

The Appendix B floor plan shows a "Wash Station" in the interior of the New North Shop (SOW-0050 — separate industrial wash sink). If the wash station is enclosed with walls, a door opening may be required. The conceptual drawing does not show enclosure walls around the wash station.

**ASSUMPTION:** The wash station is an open fixture installation, not an enclosed room. No additional door is assumed. If the Owner intends an enclosed wash station room, this must be confirmed and added to the schedule.

**TBD — OWNER CONFIRMATION REQUIRED (X-001):** Confirm with the Owner whether the wash station (SOW-0050) will be enclosed. If enclosed, a door opening must be added to the schedule. This question should be addressed during Step 2 (Confirm Program with Owner) of the Procedure. Source: Guidance Considerations — Wash Station Area; SOW-0050.

## Dimensional Notation Convention

**[Enriched per B-003: Minor inconsistency was identified between Datasheet (feet-and-inches notation, e.g., "24'", "6'") and Specification (mixed notation, e.g., "24'", "6 feet (6')"). A consistent convention should be established to prevent drift in downstream documents.]**

The following convention is recommended for all four deliverable documents and the final schedule drawing:

- **Imperial notation** shall be used throughout, consistent with standard Alberta architectural practice for this building type.
- Dimensions shall be expressed in feet and inches using the standard notation: feet (') and inches ("). Example: 24'-0" x 16'-0".
- Where a dimension is stated in text, the numeric notation shall follow in parentheses on first use. Example: "six feet (6'-0")".
- The Architect's office standard for dimensional notation on the IFC drawing set shall govern the final schedule drawing format.

**PROPOSAL:** Guidance should define unit convention for the schedule. Source: Datasheet Door Schedule table; Specification REQ-DW-004.

- Specification REQ-DW-007 states: "professional engineer (or architect, as applicable)"

- If the project team includes a licensed Alberta Architect, the Architect of Record would typically seal architectural drawings (including schedules).
- If the project team operates under a P.Eng. as the professional of record for all disciplines, the P.Eng. seals.
- The CCDC 14-2013 design-build contract form and Alberta professional practice regulations govern which designation applies.

**PROPOSAL:** Guidance should clarify the applicable professional designation for this project. The human should confirm which professional designation applies and both Specification REQ-DW-007 and Procedure Step 7.2 should be updated for consistency.
