# Procedure
## DEL-009-02 | Building Permit Application & Approval

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and obtain the Building Permit Application & Approval for the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition. It covers: pre-application engagement with Camrose County building authority, application package assembly, submission, follow-up through approval, permit conditions documentation, and inspection coordination during construction — all as required by R-01, §3.3.2 and SOW-0006.

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Pre-Application Engagement

**Step 1: Confirm Development Permit Approval (Gate Check)**
- Verify that DEL-009-01 (Development Permit) has been issued and is in effect.
- Retrieve and review the development permit document, including all attached conditions.
- Do not proceed to Step 2 until this gate is cleared.
- *Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md; REQ-009-02-02*

**Step 2: Engage Camrose County Building Authority**
- Contact Camrose County building services to:
  - Obtain the current building permit application form(s) and submission checklist.
  - Confirm specific submission requirements for a ~13,000 sq ft concrete commercial/industrial structure.
  - Confirm whether a pre-application meeting or consultation is available.
  - Confirm the permit fee structure (for County payment coordination — SOW-0202).
  - Confirm expected review and processing timeline (record in Datasheet — Attributes "Processing timeline").
  - Clarify the relationship between the building permit and Safety Code permits (see CONF-009-02-01 and CONF-009-02-02 in Guidance.md).
  - Confirm minimum advance notice period required for County representative attendance at inspections (record for Phase 5 scheduling).
  - Confirm whether environmental assessment, heritage overlay, or other regulatory overlays apply to this site (Lensing ref: F-002).
- Document all information received from the authority.
- **Acceptance criteria for Step 2 completion:** Step 2 is complete when: (a) application form(s) and submission checklist have been obtained, (b) submission requirements for this building type have been confirmed, (c) expected processing timeline has been recorded, and (d) the building permit / Safety Code permit coordination question (CONF-009-02-01) has been clarified. If any of (a)-(d) remain unresolved after engagement, document as TBD with a follow-up action and escalate to the Project Manager before proceeding to Phase 2. (Lensing ref: A-004 — acceptance criteria added for go/no-go between Phase 1 and Phase 2.)
- *Source: R-01, §3.3.2; SOW-0006; Guidance — Considerations*

**Step 3: Coordinate County Permit Fee Arrangement**
- Notify the County project manager of the permit fee amount (once available from Step 2).
- Confirm the County's fee payment process and timing.
- Exclude permit fee costs from any Proponent cost tracking.
- *Source: R-01, §3.3.1; SOW-0202*

### Phase 2 — Application Package Assembly

**Step 4: Confirm IFC Drawing Set Completeness**
- Verify that IFC drawing sets are complete and available across all required disciplines:
  - Architectural (PKG-001 deliverables)
  - Structural (PKG-002 deliverables)
  - Mechanical/HVAC (PKG-003 deliverables)
  - Electrical (PKG-004 deliverables)
  - Civil / Site Grading (PKG-005 deliverables, including site grading plan for drainage — SOW-0015, SOW-0020, SOW-0021)
  - Plumbing (PKG-006 deliverables)
- Confirm all drawings are signed and stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta.
- *Source: R-01, §3.3.2; SOW-0018; REQ-009-02-03*

**Step 5: Review Development Permit Conditions for Impact**
- Review all conditions from DEL-009-01 for any requirements that affect the building permit application content (e.g., design modifications, setback conditions, site constraints).
- Incorporate applicable conditions into the application package.
- *Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md; _CONTEXT.md (Success Criteria)*

**Step 6: Assemble Building Permit Application Package**
- Complete the application form(s) obtained in Step 2.
- Compile all required documents per the authority's submission checklist, including but not limited to:
  - Completed application form
  - IFC drawing sets (all disciplines, stamped)
  - Site grading plan
  - Specifications (as required by authority — TBD)
  - Supporting calculations (as required by authority — TBD)
  - Development permit copy / conditions (as may be required)
- *Source: R-01, §3.3.2; REQ-009-02-03, REQ-009-02-04, REQ-009-02-05*

**Step 7: Internal Review of Application Package**
- Conduct internal review of the assembled application package for completeness against the authority's submission checklist.
- Conduct explicit pre-submission code compliance review: confirm that all design drawings address applicable Alberta building codes (SOW-0008) and Safety Codes (SOW-0009). Document the review findings as a Pre-submission Code Compliance Review record. (Lensing ref: A-003 — pre-submission compliance check added to avoid circular reliance on permit issuance as the sole verification of code compliance.)
- Obtain sign-off from the Project Manager before submission.
- *Source: REQ-009-02-04, REQ-009-02-05*

### Phase 3 — Submission and Authority Review

**Step 8: Submit Application to Camrose County Building Authority**
- Submit the complete application package to Camrose County building services.
- Obtain confirmation of receipt (submission date, tracking reference, or receipt number). Record the tracking reference in the Datasheet — Attributes "Submission tracking reference" field. (Lensing ref: E-001)
- Notify the County project manager of the submission.
- *Source: R-01, §3.3.2; SOW-0006*

**Step 9: Manage Authority Review and Respond to Requests**
- Monitor application status with the building authority.
- Respond promptly to any requests for additional information, drawing revisions, or clarifications.
- Log all communications with the building authority in the project records.
- Escalate delays to the County project manager via weekly meetings (SOW-0086).
- Monitor against schedule checkpoints defined in Specification Verification (REQ-009-02-11). (Lensing ref: X-003)
- *Source: R-01, §3.1 (schedule constraint); SOW-0086*

**Step 9A: Handle Application Rejection or Major Revision Request**
- If the building authority rejects the application or requests major revisions (beyond minor clarifications/additional information addressed in Step 9):
  1. Document the rejection or revision request in full, including the authority's stated reasons and required changes.
  2. Assess the scope and impact of the required revisions on the design documentation and the project schedule (escalate to Project Manager and design team leads immediately).
  3. Coordinate with the design team to address all required revisions.
  4. Reassemble the application package incorporating all required changes (return to Step 6 or Step 7 as appropriate).
  5. Re-submit the revised application to the building authority (return to Step 8).
  6. Update the schedule monitoring checkpoints (Specification REQ-009-02-11 verification) to reflect revised timeline.
- **Responsible parties:** Project Manager (coordination and escalation), Design Team (technical revisions), Building Authority (clarification of requirements).
- (Lensing ref: F-003 — re-submission workflow added for rejection/major revision scenario.)
- *Source: ASSUMPTION — re-submission workflow is standard industry practice for permit applications; no specific Camrose County re-submission process documented in accessible sources.*

### Phase 4 — Permit Receipt and Conditions Documentation

**Step 10: Receive and Review Issued Building Permit**
- Upon approval, receive the issued building permit from Camrose County.
- Review all permit conditions, restrictions, and inspection requirements attached to the permit.
- Retain original permit document on site and in project records.
- *Source: SOW-0006; REQ-009-02-01*

**Step 11: Document and Distribute Permit Conditions**
- Prepare a Permit Conditions Summary documenting all conditions attached to the issued permit.
- Evaluate the cost and schedule impact of permit conditions on the construction program (see Guidance — Considerations: Permit Conditions — Cost and Schedule Impact). (Lensing ref: C-003)
- Enter all permit conditions into DEL-009-04 (Code Compliance Register & Inspection Log).
- Distribute relevant conditions to the construction team for incorporation into site instructions and construction procedures.
- *Source: _CONTEXT.md (Success Criteria); _DEPENDENCIES.md (downstream DEL-009-04); REQ-009-02-09*

**Step 12: Confirm Construction Commencement Gate**
- Confirm in writing (or in weekly meeting minutes) that the building permit is issued before authorizing construction mobilization.
- *Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md; REQ-009-02-10*

### Phase 5 — Inspection Coordination (During Construction)

**Step 13: Identify Required Inspections Under Permit**
- Review the issued building permit and building authority requirements to identify all mandatory inspection stages (see Specification — Anticipated Mandatory Inspection Stages table for preliminary list; confirm with authority upon permit issuance).
- Develop an inspection schedule aligned with the construction schedule.
- *Source: R-01, §3.3.2; SOW-0084*

**Step 14: Schedule Inspections with County Representative**
- For each required inspection, provide advance notice to the County project manager to ensure the County representative can be present.
- Minimum advance notice period: TBD — confirm with Camrose County project manager per Step 2 engagement and Guidance Principle 4. (Lensing ref: X-004)
- Coordinate inspection timing with the construction schedule and the authority's availability.
- *Source: R-01, §3.3.2; SOW-0084; REQ-009-02-07*

**Step 15: Submit Inspection Requests**
- Submit all inspection requests to the building authority (or Safety Codes Officer) as required by the permit.
- *Source: R-01, §3.3.2; SOW-0084; REQ-009-02-06*

**Step 16: Attend Inspections and Obtain Reports**
- Coordinate Proponent site access for the inspector.
- Ensure County project representative is in attendance.
- Receive copies of all completed inspection reports.
- *Source: R-01, §3.3.2; SOW-0084, SOW-0085; REQ-009-02-07, REQ-009-02-08*

**Step 17: Distribute Inspection Reports to County**
- Provide copies of all completed inspection reports to the County project manager.
- File original inspection reports in DEL-009-04 (primary record location); retain reference copies in DEL-009-02 folder. (Lensing ref: E-002 — primary vs. reference copy distinction clarified.)
- *Source: R-01, §3.3.2; SOW-0085; REQ-009-02-08*

**Step 18: Address Deficiencies or Failed Inspections**
- If an inspection identifies deficiencies or fails, document the deficiencies, implement corrective action, and schedule a re-inspection.
- Track corrective actions in DEL-009-04.
- *Source: R-01, §3.3.2; SOW-0084, SOW-0085; REQ-009-02-09 (conditions tracking)*

**Step 19: Permit Closeout and Archival**
- Upon completion of all mandatory inspection stages and resolution of all deficiencies:
  1. Confirm with the building authority that all required inspections have been completed and the permit can be considered fulfilled / closed out.
  2. Obtain any final occupancy or completion documentation from the authority (if applicable).
  3. Compile all permit-related records (application package, issued permit, conditions summary, inspection reports, correspondence) into the project record system.
  4. Confirm that all inspection records in DEL-009-04 are complete and reconciled with the DEL-009-02 folder reference copies.
  5. Record the permit closeout date and confirmation in _STATUS.md (via appropriate pipeline mechanism).
- (Lensing ref: X-002 — closeout/archival step added to complete the permit lifecycle.)
- *Source: ASSUMPTION — permit closeout is standard practice; specific Camrose County closeout process TBD.*
