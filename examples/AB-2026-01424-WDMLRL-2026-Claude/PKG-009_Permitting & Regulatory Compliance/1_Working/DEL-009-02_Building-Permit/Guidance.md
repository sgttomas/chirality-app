# Guidance
## DEL-009-02 | Building Permit Application & Approval

---

## Purpose

DEL-009-02 exists to satisfy SOW-0006: the Design-Builder's contractual obligation to obtain a building permit from Camrose County for the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition (R-01, §3.3.2). The building permit is the legal authorization to construct the building and is a hard gate: construction cannot commence until the permit is issued. It is also a critical path item — delay in permit acquisition compresses the available construction window against the December 31, 2026 completion deadline (R-01, §3.1).

Within the project structure, this deliverable sits in PKG-009 (Permitting & Regulatory Compliance) and is sequentially downstream of DEL-009-01 (Development Permit). It is a prerequisite for the entire construction phase (PKG-010 through PKG-018) and feeds permit conditions into DEL-009-04 (Code Compliance Register & Inspection Log). Supporting OBJ-001 (code-compliant, functional delivery) and OBJ-002 (completion on schedule) — ASSUMPTION: package-level objective mapping per decomposition §7 and §9 Objective Coverage Matrix.

## Principles

**1. Sequential logic must be honoured.**
The building permit follows the development permit. Attempting to submit a building permit application before the development permit is secured risks rejection or delay by the authority. Confirm DEL-009-01 approval before initiating application assembly (source: _DEPENDENCIES.md — declared upstream dependency).

**2. Complete design is a non-negotiable input.**
The Camrose County building authority will require IFC drawings stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta (R-01, §3.3.2; SOW-0018). Submitting incomplete or preliminary drawings may result in rejection and re-submission delays. Design completeness — across all six disciplines (architectural, structural, mechanical/HVAC, electrical, civil/site grading, plumbing) — is the primary schedule risk for this deliverable.

**3. Permit fees are the County's responsibility.**
The Proponent shall exclude permit fee costs from its proposal and shall not pay permit fees on behalf of the County (R-01, §3.3.1; SOW-0202). Budget or payment coordination with the County should be established in advance of application submission.

**4. County representative engagement is embedded in the permit process.**
All inspections conducted under the building permit require the County project representative to be present (R-01, §3.3.2; SOW-0084). Inspection scheduling must account for County availability. Advance notice to the County project manager is essential to avoid inspection delays. The minimum advance notice period required for County representative attendance should be confirmed with the Camrose County project manager and documented for scheduling purposes. ASSUMPTION: a minimum of TBD business days advance notice is likely required; confirm with authority. (Lensing ref: X-004 — rationale: without a defined notice period, inspection scheduling cannot be reliably planned, risking construction delays.)

**5. Permit conditions are binding on construction.**
All conditions attached to the issued building permit must be documented and incorporated into construction procedures (source: _CONTEXT.md, Success Criteria). Conditions are not merely administrative — they may affect materials, sequencing, or inspection hold points. The cost and schedule impact of permit conditions on the construction program should be evaluated upon receipt of the permit, as conditions may impose additional expenses (e.g., specialized materials, additional inspections) or constrain the construction sequence. (Lensing ref: C-003 — cost and schedule impact evaluation added.) Feeding conditions into DEL-009-04 is the mechanism for tracking compliance.

**6. Permit lifecycle extends beyond initial issuance.**
If the construction timeline extends beyond the building permit's validity period, or if design changes occur after permit issuance, the permit may require amendment, renewal, or extension. The building permit lifecycle should be monitored throughout construction. ASSUMPTION: Camrose County has a defined process for permit amendments and extensions; specific requirements TBD — confirm with building authority. (Lensing ref: X-001 — permit amendment/renewal scenario acknowledged.)

## Considerations

### Schedule Risk: Critical Path Position
The building permit is on the critical path to construction commencement. The project completion deadline is December 31, 2026 (R-01, §3.1). Given that:
- DEL-009-01 (Development Permit) must precede this application,
- Design IFC documents must be complete before submission,
- Building authority review and approval takes an indeterminate time (TBD — authority processing SLAs not stated in accessible sources),

the Project Manager should identify the target application submission date early and work backward from the construction start date needed to achieve December 31, 2026 completion.

**Schedule impact quantification:** The processing timeline for building authority review is TBD. ASSUMPTION: typical municipal building permit processing for a commercial/industrial structure of this scale in Alberta may range from 4 to 12 weeks, but this varies significantly by jurisdiction and application complexity — **location TBD; confirm with Camrose County building authority.** The Project Manager should bracket the expected review duration (best case / expected / worst case) and model the impact on the December 2026 deadline. If the worst-case processing duration plus remaining construction duration exceeds the available window, early escalation to the County project manager is warranted. (Lensing ref: A-005, D-002 — schedule impact quantification added; the 4-12 week range is an ASSUMPTION based on general Alberta municipal practice, not Camrose County-specific data.)

### Application Package Assembly Complexity
A building permit application for a ~13,000 sq ft concrete structure with 35' ceiling height (R-01, §3.1, §3.4), mechanical/electrical/plumbing systems, overhead cranes (SOW-0067), service pit (SOW-0028), wash bay (SOW-0027a), septic (SOW-0043), and cistern (SOW-0041) is substantive. Assembly requires drawing sets and supporting documents from multiple design disciplines. Coordination with all design sub-consultants (architectural, structural, mechanical, electrical, civil, plumbing) is necessary.

### Alberta Safety Codes Framework
Alberta issues building permits under the Safety Codes Act framework, administered by accredited municipalities or Safety Codes Officers. Camrose County is the issuing authority (R-01, §3.3.2). The exact application process, required forms, and submission requirements should be confirmed directly with Camrose County building services as a first step. ASSUMPTION: standard Alberta Safety Codes building permit process applies; specific procedural requirements not reproduced in accessible sources.

### Relationship to Safety Code Permits (DEL-009-03)
Building permit and Safety Code permits (DEL-009-03) are related but distinct. In Alberta, discipline-specific Safety Code permits (electrical, gas, plumbing, etc.) are often issued separately from the building permit. The Project Manager should clarify with Camrose County whether the building permit encompasses all Safety Code disciplines or whether separate Safety Code permit applications are required in parallel. This affects coordination sequencing with DEL-009-03. See Conflict Table below.

### Inspection Coordination
Inspections required under the building permit must be coordinated so that the County project representative can attend (SOW-0084). The County project manager identity will be confirmed at project award (R-01, §3.1; SOW-0303 — TBD). Inspection coordination should be included in the weekly meeting agenda (SOW-0086) to ensure no inspection is scheduled without County availability.

**Minimum notice period:** The minimum advance notice period required for County representative attendance at inspections is TBD. The Proponent should confirm this with the Camrose County project manager at the earliest opportunity and document the agreed notice period. The inspection scheduling approach (see Procedure Phase 5) depends on this lead time. ASSUMPTION: a formal notice period (likely measured in business days) will be required; without it, inspection delays are a schedule risk. (Lensing ref: X-004 — rationale for notice period guidance added.)

### Development Permit Conditions Flow-Through
Conditions attached to DEL-009-01 (Development Permit) may affect the building permit application content — for example, site boundary setbacks, landscaping requirements, or design modifications imposed by the development authority. Review DEL-009-01 conditions carefully before assembling the building permit package.

### Permit Conditions — Cost and Schedule Impact
Upon receipt of the issued building permit, the Project Manager should evaluate the cost and schedule impact of all attached conditions on the construction program. Permit conditions may require specialized materials, additional inspections, modified construction sequencing, or hold points that affect the critical path. This evaluation should be documented and communicated to the construction team and the County project manager as part of the permit conditions distribution process (Procedure Step 11). (Lensing ref: C-003 — consideration for cost/schedule impact of permit conditions added; source: _CONTEXT.md Success Criteria states "All permit terms incorporated into construction procedures," which implies an evaluation of impact.)

### Permit Amendment, Renewal, or Extension
If the construction timeline extends beyond the building permit's validity period, or if significant design changes occur after permit issuance that affect code compliance, the building permit may require formal amendment or renewal. The process and requirements for permit amendment at Camrose County are TBD — confirm with the building authority. The Project Manager should monitor the permit validity period throughout construction and initiate amendment proceedings proactively if needed. (Lensing ref: X-001 — permit lifecycle scenario acknowledged.)
