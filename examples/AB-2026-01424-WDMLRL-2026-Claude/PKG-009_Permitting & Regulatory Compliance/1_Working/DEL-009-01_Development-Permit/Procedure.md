# Procedure — DEL-009-01 | Development Permit Application & Approval

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps required to produce and manage the Development Permit Application & Approval for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. The procedure covers application preparation, submission to Camrose County, coordination through the review process, and receipt and recording of the approved permit.

Source: R-01 §3.3.2; Decomp SOW-0005; _CONTEXT.md.

## Steps

> Note: Steps below describe the process to produce and obtain the development permit. Step sequencing is based on standard Alberta development permit practice and RFP obligations. Specific authority requirements, timeline, and form content are TBD and must be confirmed at project mobilization.

### Step 1 — Identify and Contact the Regulatory Authority
1.1 Confirm the specific permitting office within Camrose County responsible for development permit issuance for the project site (SW 14–44–21–W4).

1.2 Identify the authority's designated contact for the application.

1.3 Initiate pre-application consultation with the authority. This step is a committed procedural action (see Guidance, Pre-Application Consultation). The consultation should aim to resolve the following TBD items: application form and checklist, processing timeline, fee coordination process, minimum design requirements for application, specific applicable Alberta Building Code and Safety Code identifiers, and whether concurrent development/building permit applications are accepted. If pre-application consultation is not pursued, document the rationale for the decision.

- **Record:** Regulatory authority contact information; pre-application consultation notes (documenting resolution of TBD items listed above); or decision record if consultation not pursued.
- Source: R-01 §1.3.4 (County contact: Darren King, Manager WDMLRL — owner's representative, not necessarily the permit office); R-01 §2.22 (governing law: Alberta)
- Enrichment: Step 1.3 strengthened from advisory to committed per A-004 from _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md; TBD resolution list added per A-001, A-002, B-001

### Step 2 — Obtain Application Requirements
2.1 Obtain the current development permit application form from Camrose County.

2.2 Obtain the checklist of required supporting documents (site plan, drawings, descriptions, fees) from the authority.

2.3 Confirm the fee payment process — note that fees are paid by Camrose County directly (not by the Proponent). Coordinate with the County's project manager to ensure fees do not delay the application.

2.4 Confirm processing timelines and any submission deadlines required to meet the December 31, 2026 construction completion. Update the Datasheet processing timeline field and establish escalation thresholds per Guidance (Escalation Triggers for Schedule Delays) once the authority's stated timeline is known.

- **Record:** Application form; submission checklist; fee process confirmation; processing timeline; established escalation thresholds.
- Source: R-01 §3.3.1 (fee payment by County); R-01 §3.1 (completion deadline)
- Enrichment: Escalation threshold establishment added per B-003 from _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md

### Step 3 — Prepare Application Package
3.1 Compile project information required for the application:
   - Project name, legal land description (SW 14–44–21–W4), and civic address (if applicable)
   - Description of proposed development (new maintenance shop addition, ~13,000 sq ft; renovation of existing Old North Shop)
   - Site plan showing proposed building footprint, setbacks, access, drainage, and relationship to existing structures
   - Building use description (commercial/industrial maintenance shop)
   - Building dimensions and height (35' ceiling per R-01 §3.4)

3.2 Confirm that the design information available at the time of submission is sufficient to meet the authority's minimum requirements. If not, assess whether a pre-application consultation (Step 1.3) can resolve gaps.

3.3 Prepare and assemble all required forms and supporting documents per the authority's checklist (obtained in Step 2.2).

3.4 Conduct formal internal review of completed application package before submission (per REQ-009-01-013). The review shall confirm:
   - Completeness against the authority's checklist
   - Completeness against baseline content items (site plan, legal land description, building description, access information) even if authority checklist is not yet available
   - Consistency between site plan and other project documentation
   - Correct legal land description and project information
   - Sign off on the internal review record

- **Record:** Draft application package; internal review sign-off record.
- Source: R-01 §3.3.2; R-01 §3.4 (design requirements including building dimensions); R-01 §3.1 (site description)
- ASSUMPTION: Specific application content requirements are those standard to Camrose County development permit process, to be confirmed in Step 2.
- Enrichment: Step 3.4 formalized as a requirement-linked verification step per C-003 from _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md

### Step 4 — Submit Application
4.1 Submit the completed application package to the appropriate Camrose County permitting office.

4.2 Obtain a submission receipt or acknowledgment from the authority.

4.3 Record the submission date and expected review timeline.

4.4 Notify the County project manager of the submission, consistent with weekly coordination obligations.

- **Record:** Submission receipt / acknowledgment; submission date; estimated review completion date.
- Source: R-01 §3.3.2; R-01 §3.1 (coordination via County project manager)

### Step 5 — Manage Review Process
5.1 Respond promptly to any authority requests for additional information, clarification, or resubmission.

5.1.1 **Resubmission Procedure (if deficiency notice received):** Upon receipt of a deficiency notice or request for resubmission from the authority:
   - (a) Log the deficiency notice in the correspondence log with the date received and items identified.
   - (b) Assess the impact of the deficiency on the project schedule; if the revised timeline threatens the construction completion deadline, escalate per Step 5.2 and Guidance (Escalation Triggers for Schedule Delays).
   - (c) Prepare the revised or supplementary materials to address each identified deficiency.
   - (d) Conduct internal review of revised materials for completeness before resubmission (per REQ-009-01-013).
   - (e) Resubmit to the authority and obtain a revised submission receipt/acknowledgment.
   - (f) Update the expected review timeline and notify the County project manager of the resubmission.

- **Record:** Deficiency notice (original); schedule impact assessment; revised submission package; resubmission receipt; updated timeline.
- Source: R-01 §3.3.2; Guidance (Pre-Application Consultation trade-off regarding deficiency notice likelihood)
- Enrichment: Resubmission sub-step added per X-001 from _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md to formalize the deficiency response workflow

5.2 Track review status and escalate to the County project manager if processing delays threaten the project schedule. Apply escalation triggers and thresholds established in Guidance (Escalation Triggers for Schedule Delays) and confirmed during Step 2.4.

5.3 Coordinate County representative availability for any required site visits or meetings during the review process.

- **Record:** All authority correspondence (requests, responses); status log; escalation records (if triggered).
- Source: R-01 §3.3.2

### Step 6 — Receive Permit and Record Conditions
6.1 Upon approval, obtain the issued development permit document from Camrose County.

6.2 Review the approved permit for any conditions attached to the approval.

6.3 Document all permit conditions. Assess whether any conditions require material design changes or have schedule impact (per REQ-009-01-014). If so, document the design change implications and schedule impact assessment.

6.4 Communicate permit conditions to (per REQ-009-01-012):
   - The County project manager
   - The relevant design discipline leads (if conditions affect design)
   - DEL-009-04 Code Compliance Register & Inspection Log (for conditions tracking)
   - Relevant construction packages (if conditions affect physical work)
   - Obtain acknowledgment of receipt from each downstream recipient.

6.5 File the original approved permit in the project document management system.

- **Record:** Approved development permit (original); conditions record; design change/schedule impact assessment (if triggered); distribution confirmation with acknowledgments; downstream condition distribution confirmation.
- Source: _CONTEXT.md (Scope: Conditions and requirements tracking; Permit approval documentation); Decomp DEL-009-04 relationship
- Enrichment: Condition impact assessment added per D-003; acknowledgment requirement added per F-003 from _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md

### Step 7 — Coordinate Inspections (Ongoing)
7.1 Submit all inspection requests to the authority as required throughout the permit-related inspection process.

7.2 Notify the County project representative with a minimum of TBD business days advance notice for all scheduled inspections to ensure their attendance. The minimum lead time is to be confirmed through coordination with the County project representative (see Guidance P-3).

7.3 Obtain copies of all completed inspection reports.

7.4 Distribute inspection report copies to the County.

7.5 File inspection reports in the project document management system and log them in DEL-009-04.

- **Record:** Inspection request submissions; inspection report copies; distribution records.
- Source: R-01 §3.3.2; Decomp SOW-0084, SOW-0085
- Enrichment: Lead time placeholder added per E-002 from _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md

> Note: "Archive per contract" refers to CCDC 14-2013 retention requirements as applicable. Guarantee period is construction period plus 2 years following Construction Completion Certificate (CCC), per R-01 §2.10.2. The distinction between "archive per contract" and "2-year guarantee period minimum" retention standards is as follows: the permit document itself and conditions imposed by the authority have regulatory significance that extends beyond project closeout and therefore carry the longer retention; supporting process records (correspondence, checklists, etc.) follow the standard contractual archive requirement.
