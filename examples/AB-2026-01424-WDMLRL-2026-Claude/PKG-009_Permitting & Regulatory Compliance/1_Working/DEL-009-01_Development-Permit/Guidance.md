# Guidance — DEL-009-01 | Development Permit Application & Approval

---

## Purpose

The Development Permit Application & Approval (DEL-009-01) exists because the Proponent is contractually obligated under the RFP to obtain a development permit from Camrose County as a prerequisite to commencing construction of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition.

Source: R-01 §3.3.2 ("Obtaining a development permit and building permit from the County"); Decomp SOW-0005, OBJ-001, OBJ-002.

The development permit is a foundational regulatory gate. Its absence would legally preclude construction activities on the subject site. Its conditions, once issued, may impose design or construction constraints that must flow downstream into DEL-009-04 (Code Compliance Register) and into construction execution packages.

The permit fee is explicitly outside Proponent scope — the County pays permit fees. The Proponent's scope is the work of obtaining the permit (application preparation, submission, authority coordination, and approval management), not the fee payment.

---

## Principles

### P-1: Obtain Early — Critical Path Sensitivity
The development permit sits at the front of the construction critical path. Any delay in permit issuance directly delays the ability to begin construction, compressing the available build window before the December 31, 2026 completion deadline. Permit activities should be initiated at the earliest practicable time after contract award.

- Source: R-01 §3.1 (completion deadline); Decomp OBJ-002

### P-2: Fee Exclusion is Mandatory
The Proponent must exclude development permit, building permit, and Safety Code permit fees from its proposal. The County pays these fees directly. Failure to exclude may result in proposal irregularity.

- Source: R-01 §3.3.1; Decomp SOW-0202

### P-3: County Presence at Inspections is Non-Negotiable
The County project representative must be present at all inspections. The Proponent must schedule inspections with sufficient lead time to ensure County availability. Copies of all inspection reports must be provided to the County. Minimum scheduling lead time for County representative attendance is TBD — to be confirmed through coordination with County project representative.

- Source: R-01 §3.3.2; Decomp SOW-0084, SOW-0085
- Enrichment: Lead time clarification added per E-002 from _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md

### P-4: Design Must Meet Alberta Building and Safety Codes
The permit application will be reviewed against applicable Alberta building codes and Safety Codes. The building design, as it evolves through the design packages (PKG-001 through PKG-007), must maintain ongoing compliance. Non-compliance discovered late will risk permit denial or conditional approval requiring costly redesign.

- Source: R-01 §3.3.2; Decomp SOW-0008, SOW-0009

### P-5: All IFC Drawings Require P.Eng. Stamp (Alberta)
The final design drawings submitted for the permit (and for construction) must be signed and stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.

- Source: R-01 §3.3.2 ("All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta"); Decomp SOW-0018

## Considerations

### Regulatory Authority Identity
The development permit authority is Camrose County. The RFP identifies Darren King (Manager, West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill) as the owner's project contact (R-01 §1.3.4). However, the specific municipal permit department contact and application process are TBD — the Proponent should confirm the correct permitting office within Camrose County administration at project mobilization.

### Relationship to Building Permit
The development permit (DEL-009-01) must precede the building permit (DEL-009-02). The Proponent should clarify with Camrose County whether these permits are processed simultaneously or strictly sequentially, and whether a single combined application is accepted. This sequencing has schedule implications.

- ASSUMPTION: Development permit must be obtained or at minimum substantially approved before building permit application is submitted. This is the conventional Alberta municipal practice but the specific Camrose County process is TBD.

**Concurrent Permit Application Strategy (enrichment per E-001):** If Camrose County allows concurrent or overlapping processing of development and building permit applications, the Proponent should evaluate whether submitting both in parallel could reduce the overall permit timeline. Key considerations for this decision include: (a) whether the authority's process allows the building permit review to begin before development permit issuance, (b) the risk of rework if the development permit is issued with conditions that affect the building permit application, and (c) the fee payment coordination implications for the County. Until the authority's process is confirmed, the assumption remains that permits are processed sequentially. This is flagged as TBD for the Proponent to resolve during initial authority contact (Procedure Step 1).

- Source for concurrent question: Guidance (original Considerations); R-01 §3.3.2 (both permits required)
- Label: ASSUMPTION — strategic implications inferred from standard practice; Camrose County's specific process is TBD

### Design Readiness for Application
The permit application will require a level of design detail sufficient for the authority to assess land use compatibility. This typically does not require full IFC-level design but does require site plan, building footprint, height, use, and access information. The Proponent should confirm minimum design requirements with the authority at pre-application consultation.

- ASSUMPTION — specific application design requirements not stated in available sources; derived from standard Alberta development permit practice.

### Pre-Application Consultation
Engaging the regulatory authority for a pre-submission meeting or consultation is common practice and can reduce review cycles. The RFP does not preclude this; the Proponent should evaluate this as a committed procedural step rather than an optional consideration. The rationale for commitment: pre-application consultation directly addresses several TBD items (application form, checklist, processing timeline, fee coordination, code identifiers) that block multiple requirements. If the Proponent decides not to pursue pre-application consultation, the decision and rationale should be documented.

- ASSUMPTION — not explicitly required or prohibited by sources.
- Enrichment: Strengthened from advisory "should consider" to a committed procedural recommendation with documented opt-out per A-004 from _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md. The operational commitment is reflected in Procedure Step 1.3 (updated to match this guidance).

### Permit Conditions as Downstream Constraints
The development permit may be issued with conditions (e.g., landscaping requirements, access restrictions, drainage management requirements). These conditions must be:
1. Recorded in this deliverable's correspondence log.
2. Tracked in DEL-009-04 (Code Compliance Register & Inspection Log).
3. Communicated to the relevant construction packages if they affect physical work.
4. Acknowledged by the receiving deliverable owners to confirm receipt and understanding.

The mechanism for downstream condition flow is now governed by REQ-009-01-012 in Specification. See also Procedure Step 6.4.

- Enrichment: Item 4 (acknowledgment) added per F-003 from _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md.

### County Pays Permit Fees
Camrose County is paying the development permit fees directly (R-01 §3.3.1). The Proponent should coordinate with the County's project manager to understand the payment mechanism to avoid application delays caused by outstanding fees.

### Escalation Triggers for Schedule Delays
During the permit review period (Procedure Step 5), the Proponent must monitor processing status and escalate if delays threaten the project schedule. The following decision thresholds and triggers should be applied:

1. **Processing timeline exceeded:** If the authority's stated processing timeline (once obtained per Procedure Step 2.4) is exceeded by more than TBD business days, escalate to the County project manager.
2. **No substantive response:** If no substantive authority response (receipt acknowledgment, deficiency notice, or approval) is received within TBD business days of submission, the Proponent should initiate follow-up with the authority and notify the County project manager.
3. **Schedule compression threshold:** If the remaining construction window after permit issuance falls below the minimum duration required by the construction schedule (TBD — to be determined during project scheduling), escalate immediately to the County project manager and assess schedule recovery options.

Specific numeric thresholds are TBD and should be established during project mobilization based on the authority's stated processing timeline and the project's construction schedule.

## Trade-offs

| Trade-off | Option A | Option B | Consideration | Decision Criteria |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Application Timing | Submit early with preliminary design information | Wait for more complete design before submitting | Early submission reduces schedule risk but may require resubmission if design changes; late submission compresses the build window. Given the December 31, 2026 hard deadline, earlier submission is generally preferred. | Early submission is preferred when: (a) preliminary design is sufficient for authority's minimum requirements (confirm at pre-application consultation), and (b) the risk of design changes requiring resubmission is assessed as low. Late submission is justified only when: (a) design changes are known to be pending that would materially affect the application, or (b) the authority has indicated that incomplete applications result in longer overall processing times than waiting for complete information. Threshold: TBD — to be refined after pre-application consultation confirms authority's minimum requirements and processing implications. |
| Pre-Application Consultation | Conduct pre-application meeting with authority | Submit cold without consultation | Pre-application consultation adds a step but typically reduces formal review time and the likelihood of a deficiency notice requiring resubmission. | |
| Permit Scope | Single development permit for entire project | Multiple permits for phased scope | ASSUMPTION — likely a single permit for the full addition and renovation scope, but to be confirmed with the authority. A single permit is simpler administratively. Phased permits may be warranted if: (a) the authority requires separate permits for the new construction and the renovation, or (b) phasing enables earlier construction start on one component while the other is still under review. The cost/schedule trade-off of phased permitting should be assessed during pre-application consultation. | Single permit is the default assumption unless the authority indicates otherwise. Phased permits should be considered if the authority advises it or if project scheduling analysis shows a phased start provides schedule benefit exceeding the additional administrative effort. |
