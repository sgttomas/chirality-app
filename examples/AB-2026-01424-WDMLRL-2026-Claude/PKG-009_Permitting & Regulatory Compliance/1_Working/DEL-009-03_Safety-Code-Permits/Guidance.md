# Guidance
## DEL-009-03 | Safety Code Permits

---

## Purpose

DEL-009-03 exists to ensure the Proponent lawfully secures all Safety Code permits required to design, construct, and commission the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition in Alberta. The deliverable is a contractual obligation (RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0007) and functions as a gate: construction cannot lawfully commence or proceed without the relevant permits being in place.

Beyond compliance, this deliverable establishes the Proponent's Prime Contractor designation (RFP §2.15) — a legal status under Alberta OH&S legislation that assigns site safety accountability to the Proponent for the full project duration. Failure to manage this designation properly exposes the Proponent to regulatory and legal liability.

This deliverable supports OBJ-001 (Project Delivery Success) and OBJ-002 (Regulatory Compliance) and is a direct dependency of the construction phase and DEL-009-04 (Code Compliance Register & Inspection Log).

## Principles

1. **Obtain early, not just on time.** Safety Code permits must precede related construction activities. The permit acquisition process — audit, application, authority review, inspection scheduling — has lead times that may exceed the Proponent's direct control. Begin the safety code audit immediately upon contract award and engage authorities early.

2. **Alberta Safety Codes is a multi-authority system.** In Alberta, Safety Codes permits are issued by accredited agencies or municipalities under the Safety Codes Act. Different permit categories (electrical, fire, gas, plumbing, building, elevator, etc.) are handled by different accredited bodies. ASSUMPTION: The project will require at minimum electrical and building safety code permits; fire-related permits, OH&S-specific approvals, gas, plumbing, and elevator/crane permits are also anticipated given the building systems described in RFP §3.4 (three-phase power, 2-5 tonne overhead cranes, cistern, septic, natural gas tie-in, wash bay, ventilation, welding stations). The specific authorities and permit categories must be confirmed by the safety code audit.

3. **Fee payment is the County's responsibility.** The RFP explicitly excludes permit fee costs from the Proponent's scope (RFP §3.3.1). Proponent proposals must not include these costs. However, the Proponent is responsible for all process activities (applications, coordination, inspections).

4. **Prime Contractor designation carries legal weight.** Under Alberta OH&S legislation, "Prime Contractor" is a formal legal designation that places overarching safety responsibility on the designated party whenever multiple employers are present on a worksite. The Proponent must not treat this as administrative formality. The Prime Contractor Designation form issued by Camrose County must be completed and maintained throughout the project (RFP §2.15; Decomposition SOW-0083).

5. **Coordinate with design disciplines.** Safety Code permit applications are dependent on finalized design documentation (especially IFC drawings signed by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta — RFP §3.3.2). Safety code requirements should inform design decisions, not simply be applied after the fact. Early coordination between the permit manager, structural/mechanical/electrical engineers, and the fire/safety authorities reduces rework.

6. **County visibility is a contractual requirement.** The County project representative must be present at all inspections. This is not optional. Inspection scheduling must account for County availability, and inspection reports must be transmitted to the County promptly (RFP §3.3.2).

## Considerations

### Regulatory lead times
Safety Code authorities in Alberta may have processing queues. Applications should be submitted as early as the design allows — ideally before design is fully complete for preliminary consultations, and with complete applications as soon as IFC drawings are available. ASSUMPTION: Lead times for permit reviews could range from a few days to several weeks depending on authority workload; exact timelines are TBD.

### Schedule risk from permit delays
The project has a hard completion deadline of December 31, 2026 (RFP §3.1; REQ-009-03-09). Safety Code permits gate construction, meaning any permit delay directly compresses the construction window. **ASSUMPTION: If typical authority processing timelines range from days to several weeks per permit category, and multiple categories must be obtained (potentially sequentially for inspections), cumulative delay risk is material to OBJ-001 (Project Delivery Success).** The relationship between the December 31, 2026 deadline and authority processing timelines should be characterized during the safety code audit (Phase 1 of Procedure), and intermediate schedule milestones should be established to detect slippage early (see Specification REQ-009-03-09 verification). The project schedule should include buffer time for authority processing and potential re-submissions. Exact schedule risk quantification is TBD pending audit results and project schedule development.

### Building type complexity
The project involves an approximately 13,000 sq ft industrial maintenance shop addition with features that attract multiple safety code disciplines: three-phase power, 2-5 tonne overhead cranes on a trolley, a ventilated wash bay, service pit, high-recovery heating, natural gas tie-in, plumbing (cistern, septic, wash bay drainage, sump drains), bulk water fill, welding stations with ventilation, and heavy equipment access (RFP §3.4). Each of these systems may trigger separate Safety Code permit streams. In particular:
- **Gas**: Natural gas tie-in requires gas safety code permits (ASSUMPTION: anticipated applicable).
- **Plumbing**: Cistern (50,000 L), septic holding tank (2,000 US gallon), sump drains, and wash bay drainage trigger plumbing safety code requirements (ASSUMPTION: anticipated applicable).
- **Elevator/Crane**: The 2-5 tonne overhead cranes on trolley may fall under elevator/crane safety code provisions (ASSUMPTION: anticipated applicable; to be confirmed by audit).

### Interaction with DEL-009-01 and DEL-009-02
Safety Code permits are parallel to, but operationally distinct from, the development permit (DEL-009-01) and building permit (DEL-009-02). All three permit streams may be in flight simultaneously. The building permit process often incorporates some safety code reviews; **TBD: Confirm with Camrose County and the relevant Safety Codes accredited agency whether the building permit application process covers some Safety Code categories or if separate applications are required.** This question is material to the permitting workflow and timeline. The answer should be documented and used to update Procedure Phase 3 accordingly.

### Integration with the OH&S program
The Prime Contractor designation and site safety obligations connect this deliverable directly to DEL-019-01 (Prime Contractor Designation & OH&S Program). There is an informational dependency: the OH&S plan and site safety program (DEL-019-01) inform the safety conditions that permit authorities may evaluate. Confirm the interface between these two deliverables with the Project Manager and Safety Officer. See Conflict Table C-009-03-02 for the boundary clarification needed.

### Compliance register linkage
Once permits are issued, ongoing compliance tracking, inspection logging, and certification management shift to DEL-009-04 (Code Compliance Register & Inspection Log). Ensure clean handoff of permit documentation to the team responsible for DEL-009-04.

### Post-permit condition stewardship
After Safety Code permits are issued, they may carry conditions that must be satisfied during construction and prior to occupancy. The stewardship of these conditions requires:
- **Tracking**: Each permit condition should be logged (e.g., in DEL-009-04 or a permit conditions register) with the responsible party and required evidence of compliance.
- **Responsibility**: The Project Manager is accountable for condition compliance; the Safety Officer and relevant trade supervisors are responsible for execution on specific conditions related to their disciplines.
- **Interface with DEL-009-04**: The Code Compliance Register should include a section for permit conditions, not only permit issuance status. Condition compliance evidence should be collected and filed as part of the inspection and compliance record.
- **ASSUMPTION**: Post-permit conditions will exist; the nature and number of conditions are TBD pending permit issuance. Procedure Step 14 addresses integration of conditions into operations; this guidance supplements that step with accountability and tracking mechanisms.

## Trade-offs

| Trade-off | Option A | Option B | Consideration |
|-----------|----------|----------|---------------|
| Timing of safety code audit | Conduct broad audit early (pre-design) to inform design | Conduct targeted audit once design is further developed | Early audit reduces risk of design rework; later audit is more precise but compresses permit timeline. ASSUMPTION: early audit is preferred given the December 31, 2026 deadline. |
| Single vs. multiple permit agents | Use a single permit expediter for all Safety Code streams | Manage each authority relationship separately | A permit expediter experienced in Alberta Safety Codes can reduce coordination burden. **Decision criteria (ASSUMPTION):** Consider (a) whether the number of anticipated permit categories (6+ per RFP §3.4 building systems) justifies a dedicated expediter, (b) cost of expediter vs. internal project management time, (c) whether the expediter has established relationships with the specific accredited agencies serving Camrose County, (d) schedule risk tolerance given the December 31, 2026 deadline. Cost/availability of expediter are TBD. Proposed authority for this decision: Project Manager. |
| Sequential vs. parallel permitting | Obtain permits sequentially | Pursue all applicable permits in parallel | Parallel permitting is preferred to meet the December 31, 2026 deadline, but requires more coordination effort. |
