# Procedure
## DEL-009-03 | Safety Code Permits

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and manage the Safety Code Permits deliverable for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition (WDMLRL). It covers the process from initial safety code identification through permit application, inspection coordination, and final certification — fulfilling the Proponent's obligations under RFP §2.15, §3.3.2, and SOW-0007.

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Safety Code Audit (Pre-Application)

**Step 1: Confirm applicable Safety Code categories**
- Engage a Safety Codes Council accredited agency (or Camrose County's designated authority) to identify all Safety Code permit categories applicable to the project.
- ASSUMPTION: Categories to investigate include, but may not be limited to: Building, Electrical, Fire, Gas, Plumbing, and Elevator/Crane (for 2-5 tonne overhead cranes on trolley per RFP §3.4). Additional categories may apply based on building systems described in RFP §3.4.
- Document all identified permit categories and the responsible accredited agency for each (per REQ-009-03-12).
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; RFP §3.4; Decomposition SOW-0007.

**Step 2: Identify permit application requirements per category**
- For each identified permit category, obtain the application form, required documentation list, and processing timeline from the relevant authority.
- Record estimated lead times for each permit.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; ASSUMPTION re: process details (authority-specific requirements TBD).

**Step 3: Develop Safety Code Permit schedule**
- Integrate permit lead times into the project schedule to confirm that all permits can be obtained before the related construction activities commence and within the December 31, 2026 completion deadline.
- Establish intermediate schedule milestones: (1) safety code audit complete, (2) all permit applications submitted, (3) all permits issued. Specific dates are TBD pending project schedule development (see Specification REQ-009-03-09 verification).
- Flag any critical-path permit streams to the project manager.
- Source: RFP §3.1 (deadline); ASSUMPTION re: scheduling methodology.

---

### Phase 2 — Prime Contractor Designation

**Step 4: Complete Prime Contractor Designation form**
- Obtain the Prime Contractor Designation form from Camrose County.
- Complete and execute the form; ensure it covers the full project duration.
- Submit to County for acknowledgment.
- Source: RFP §2.15; Decomposition SOW-0083.

**Step 5: Communicate Prime Contractor obligations to the site team**
- Distribute the executed Prime Contractor Designation to the Safety Officer and site supervisors.
- Confirm that all subcontractors are aware of the Prime Contractor's site safety authority and OH&S obligations.
- Source: RFP §2.15; ASSUMPTION re: internal communication process.

---

### Phase 3 — Permit Applications

**Step 6: Prepare permit application packages**
- For each identified Safety Code permit category, assemble the required application package.
- Ensure IFC drawings (signed/stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta) are available before submitting applications that require them. Note: IFC drawing availability timeline is TBD — coordinate with design discipline leads to confirm expected availability dates.
- Confirm that permit fees are billed directly to Camrose County (or that the County's payment mechanism is in place).
- Source: RFP §3.3.1, §3.3.2.

**Step 7: Submit permit applications**
- Submit all permit applications to the respective authorities.
- Record submission dates, application reference numbers, and expected response timelines.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2.

**Step 8: Track application status and respond to authority queries**
- Monitor the status of each application.
- Respond promptly to any requests for additional information from authorities.
- **Escalation criteria for processing delays:**
  - If an authority has not acknowledged receipt of a permit application within **5 business days** of submission, follow up directly with the authority. **ASSUMPTION: 5-day threshold is a reasonable default; adjust based on authority-specific guidance obtained in Step 2.**
  - If an authority has not issued a decision (approval, request for additional information, or rejection) within the estimated processing timeline obtained in Step 2 (plus a **5 business day buffer**), escalate to the Project Manager.
  - Upon escalation, the Project Manager shall: (a) contact the authority for a status update and revised timeline, (b) assess impact on intermediate schedule milestones and the December 31, 2026 deadline, (c) determine whether contingency actions are needed (e.g., parallel submission to alternative accredited agency if applicable, re-sequencing of construction activities).
  - If delays risk the December 31, 2026 completion deadline, the Project Manager shall notify the County project manager and document the risk in the project risk register.
- Source: ASSUMPTION re: tracking and escalation process.

---

### Phase 4 — Inspection Coordination

**Step 9: Schedule inspections**
- Upon authority notification of inspection readiness, schedule all required inspections.
- Notify the County project representative of each inspection date and time with sufficient advance notice to ensure County attendance.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2.

**Step 10: Facilitate inspections**
- Ensure site is prepared and relevant personnel are available for each inspection.
- Confirm County project representative is present at each inspection.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2.

**Step 11: Distribute inspection reports**
- Upon receipt of each completed inspection report from the authority, provide a copy to the County project representative promptly.
- File originals in the project permit record.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2.

**Step 12: Address deficiencies or hold points**
- If an inspection results in a deficiency notice or hold, implement the required corrective action.
- Reschedule re-inspection and repeat Steps 9-11.
- **Re-inspection closure criteria:**
  - A re-inspection is considered passed when the authority issues a written clearance or pass notification for the specific deficiency.
  - **Maximum re-inspection cycles**: If the same deficiency is not cleared after **two (2) re-inspections**, escalate to the Project Manager for root-cause review and authority consultation. **ASSUMPTION: two cycles is a reasonable threshold; adjust based on authority-specific practices.**
  - **Escalation if deficiencies persist**: The Project Manager shall (a) engage the relevant design discipline or trade contractor to resolve the underlying issue, (b) request a pre-inspection consultation with the authority if available, (c) document the deficiency history in the project risk register and notify the County project manager if the deficiency risks schedule milestones.
- Source: ASSUMPTION re: deficiency management process and closure criteria.

---

### Phase 5 — Permit Receipt and Compliance Confirmation

**Step 13: Receive and record issued permits**
- Upon issuance of each Safety Code permit, record the permit number, issuing authority, accredited agency, issue date, and any conditions attached.
- Confirm that all conditions are understood and actionable.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2.

**Step 14: Integrate permit conditions into construction operations**
- Communicate any permit conditions to the construction team and Safety Officer.
- Ensure conditions are tracked and met throughout the relevant construction phase.
- Log each permit condition in the compliance tracking system (DEL-009-04 or permit conditions register) with the responsible party and required evidence of compliance. See Guidance, Post-permit condition stewardship, for accountability and tracking mechanisms.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; ASSUMPTION re: integration process.

**Step 15: Hand off to DEL-009-04 (Code Compliance Register & Inspection Log)**
- Provide all permit certificates, inspection reports, and compliance documentation to the team responsible for DEL-009-04.
- Confirm the compliance register reflects all issued permits.
- Source: Decomposition PKG-009; _REFERENCES.md (DEL-009-04 relationship).

| Issued Safety Code Permit Certificates | Original or certified copies of each issued permit | Project Manager |

| Transmittal Records | Evidence that inspection reports were provided to County | Project Manager |
| Deficiency Clearance Records | Authority clearances for any deficiencies identified during inspections (written pass/clearance per Step 12 criteria) | Project Manager |
| Permit Condition Compliance Records | Documentation confirming all permit conditions have been addressed, with responsible party and evidence per condition | Project Manager / Safety Officer |

Record ownership is aligned with the Specification Documentation table: the Project Manager is the primary owner for all permit-related records, with the Safety Officer sharing ownership for the Prime Contractor Designation Form and Permit Condition Compliance Records where OH&S-specific expertise is required.
