# Specification
## DEL-009-04 | Code Compliance Register & Inspection Log

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-009-04 is the centralized, project-wide Code Compliance Register & Inspection Log for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition (AB-2026-01424). It consolidates all regulatory compliance requirements, permit conditions, inspection scheduling and outcomes, deficiency documentation, and corrective actions for the full project lifecycle — from permit issuance through construction completion and project closeout.

The register covers:
- All conditions attached to the Development Permit (input from DEL-009-01)
- All conditions attached to the Building Permit (input from DEL-009-02)
- All requirements from Safety Code Permits (input from DEL-009-03)
- All inspections required by the above permits and regulatory authorities
- All deficiencies and corrective actions arising from inspections
- Compliance evidence documentation supporting audit and closeout

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Permit applications, fee payments, and permit-issuance activities (covered by DEL-009-01, DEL-009-02, DEL-009-03)
- Payment of development permit, building permit, and Safety Code permit fees — these are Camrose County responsibility (RFP §3.3.1)
- Design compliance verification activities (covered by respective design packages PKG-001 through PKG-007)
- General construction quality control (covered by DEL-019-04)
- Commissioning records (covered by PKG-020)

## Requirements

### REQ-001 — Inspection Request Submission
**Statement:** The Proponent (Design-Builder) shall submit all inspection requests to the relevant regulatory authorities.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 — "Submit all inspection requests."
**Priority:** Mandatory.
**Verification:** Inspection request log showing submission records for all required inspections.

### REQ-002 — County Representative at Inspections
**Statement:** The County project representative must be present for all inspections. The Proponent is responsible for coordinating inspection timing to enable County attendance.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 — "The County project representative must be present for all inspections."
**Priority:** Mandatory.
**Verification:** Inspection log includes confirmation of County representative attendance at each inspection event.

### REQ-003 — Inspection Reports to County
**Statement:** Copies of all completed inspection reports shall be provided to the County.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 — "shall be provided with a copy of all complete inspection reports."
**Priority:** Mandatory.
**Verification:** Inspection log includes date and method of report delivery to County for each completed inspection.

### REQ-004 — Building Code Compliance (Design)
**Statement:** The building design must adhere to all applicable Alberta building codes and regulations.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0008) — "Ensure building design adheres to all applicable Alberta building codes and regulations."
**Priority:** Mandatory.
**Note:** The register tracks compliance status; the underlying design obligation rests with the design disciplines (PKG-001 through PKG-007).
**Verification:** Register records applicable code references and associated inspection outcomes confirming compliance. Each code compliance item in the register shall have a defined pass/fail criterion linked to the specific code section or inspection standard, such that a positive compliance determination requires either (a) a signed inspection pass from the relevant authority, or (b) documented evidence that the specific code requirement has been met. *(Enrichment: A-003 — strengthened compliance determination criteria.)*

### REQ-005 — Safety Code Compliance (Design and Construction)
**Statement:** The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0009) — "The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes."
**Priority:** Mandatory.
**Note:** The register tracks Safety Code compliance items derived from DEL-009-03. Specific Safety Codes applicable to this project are TBD pending permit issuance.
**Verification:** Register records Safety Code permit conditions and tracks inspection outcomes for each. Each Safety Code compliance item shall have a defined pass/fail criterion: a positive compliance determination requires a signed inspection pass or certificate of compliance from the relevant Safety Codes authority for the applicable discipline. A deficiency finding triggers the corrective action workflow per REQ-007. *(Enrichment: A-003 — strengthened compliance determination criteria.)*

### REQ-006 — Permit Conditions Consolidated
**Statement:** All conditions attached to the Development Permit, Building Permit, and Safety Code Permits shall be compiled into the register.
**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Scope — "Centralized register of all permits and conditions"); logical requirement from SOW-0008, SOW-0009, SOW-0084, SOW-0085.
**Priority:** Mandatory.
**Note:** ASSUMPTION — this consolidation requirement is inferred from the deliverable type and scope description. The RFP does not use the term "register" explicitly; however, SOW-0084 and SOW-0085 imply a tracking function.
**Verification:** Register contains a row or entry for every condition listed in DEL-009-01, DEL-009-02, and DEL-009-03. Additionally, a code identification completeness check shall be performed to verify that the upstream permits (DEL-009-01, DEL-009-02, DEL-009-03) have captured all applicable codes for the project scope. ASSUMPTION: This verification may require consultation with regulatory authorities to confirm no applicable codes were omitted during the permitting process. *(Enrichment: C-001 — added verification for code identification completeness.)*

### REQ-007 — Deficiency Documentation and Resolution Tracking
**Statement:** All deficiencies discovered during inspections shall be documented, tracked, and resolved. During the guarantee period, deficiency rectification is required within 10 days of the Owner's written instruction.
**Source:** RFP §2.10.4 (Owner notification of defects); RFP §2.10.6 (10-day rectification obligation); _CONTEXT.md (Scope — "Deficiency documentation and resolution").
**Priority:** Mandatory.
**Verification:** Deficiency log shows each deficiency with discovery date, responsible party, resolution action, and close-out confirmation.

### REQ-008 — Compliance Evidence Documentation
**Statement:** Evidence of compliance (inspection reports, certificates, sign-offs) shall be documented and retained.
**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Scope — "Compliance evidence documentation"); RFP §2.14.3 (CCC requires confirmation of compliance with contract documents).
**Priority:** Mandatory.
**Verification:** Register file contains retrievable compliance evidence for each inspected item. Acceptable forms of evidence include: (a) signed inspection certificate or pass sheet from the regulatory authority; (b) authority letter confirming compliance; (c) signed inspection report with explicit pass determination; or (d) photo documentation with dated inspector sign-off where the authority's standard process produces photographic records. TBD: Confirm with regulatory authorities whether additional evidence forms are required for specific inspection types. *(Enrichment: F-002 — defined evidence sufficiency criteria.)*

### REQ-009 — Audit Trail Maintenance
**Statement:** A complete audit trail shall be maintained for all compliance activities throughout the project lifecycle.
**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Success Criteria — "Complete audit trail maintained").
**Priority:** Mandatory.
**Note:** ASSUMPTION — specific audit trail format and retention requirements are not stated in the RFP. The format will be determined during register design.
**Verification:** All entries in the register are date-stamped and attributed to the person who created or modified the entry (i.e., the register operator who recorded the data). For inspection outcomes, the attribution chain shall identify both the register operator and the inspector/authority who issued the determination. *(Enrichment: E-004 — clarified meaning of "attributed.")*

### REQ-010 — Zero Non-Compliance Incidents
**Statement:** The register shall be operated proactively to achieve zero non-compliance incidents with permit conditions and inspection requirements.
**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Success Criteria — "Zero non-compliance incidents").
**Priority:** Aspirational / operational standard.
**Note:** ASSUMPTION — this is a success criterion, not a design requirement that can be verified in the register structure alone. The register enables this outcome but does not guarantee it.
**Verification:** Register shows no unresolved non-compliance items at project closeout.

### REQ-011 — Periodic Register Audit *(New — Enrichment: A-004, D-003)*
**Statement:** The register shall be subject to periodic audit during construction to verify completeness, accuracy, and currency of all entries.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — inferred from the need for proactive register quality assurance during construction; no single RFP clause mandates register self-audit, but the project success criteria (_CONTEXT.md — "Complete audit trail maintained") and the zero non-compliance objective imply that gaps should be detected before closeout.
**Priority:** Mandatory.
**Verification:** Audit records confirming that a register completeness and accuracy review has been conducted at the defined frequency. The audit shall verify that (a) all triggered inspections have been requested and tracked, (b) all deficiencies have been logged and assigned, and (c) all inspection reports have been delivered to County.
**Responsible Party:** Project Manager.
**Frequency:** TBD — ASSUMPTION: monthly during active construction or at each major construction milestone, whichever is more frequent. The human should confirm the appropriate cadence.

### REQ-012 — Mid-Lifecycle Regulatory Change Incorporation *(New — Enrichment: C-002)*
**Statement:** The register shall include a defined process for incorporating newly discovered regulatory obligations or mid-construction code amendments into the tracking framework.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — inferred from the need for full regulatory coverage across the project lifecycle. The existing requirements assume a static set of permit conditions loaded at permit issuance; however, regulatory changes or additional authority requirements may arise during construction.
**Priority:** Mandatory.
**Verification:** Register includes a documented process for adding new regulatory items. Any mid-lifecycle additions are logged with date, source of new obligation, and responsible party. The register audit (REQ-011) shall verify that known regulatory changes have been incorporated.

### REQ-013 — Readiness Gate for Register Population *(New — Enrichment: F-003)*
**Statement:** Before permit conditions are loaded into the register (Phase 2 commencement), a formal readiness verification shall confirm that the register template is approved, the filing structure is established, and the communication protocol with the County is confirmed.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — inferred from the risk that loading conditions into an incomplete or unapproved template could produce tracking errors. Procedure Phase 1 outputs are listed but no formal gate exists.
**Priority:** Mandatory.
**Verification:** Signed readiness checklist confirming: (a) register template approved by Project Manager; (b) filing structure created and accessible; (c) County project representative identity confirmed; (d) communication protocol documented and agreed.

### REQ-014 — Register Operational Quality Metrics *(New — Enrichment: F-004)*
**Statement:** Quantitative quality metrics (KPIs) shall be defined and tracked for register operation to enable proactive management of compliance performance.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — inferred from the zero non-compliance objective (_CONTEXT.md Success Criteria) and the need for intermediate operational indicators. REQ-010 states "zero non-compliance" as aspirational but no measurable intermediate metrics exist.
**Priority:** Recommended.
**Metrics (ASSUMPTION — to be confirmed by Project Manager):**
- Inspection request lead time: target TBD days advance notice to authority
- County notification compliance rate: 100% of inspections with advance notification per agreed protocol
- Inspection report delivery time: TBD business days from inspection completion to County delivery
- Deficiency resolution time: average days from discovery to resolution (construction phase); within 10 days of written instruction (guarantee period, per RFP §2.10.6)
- Register completeness ratio: percentage of triggered inspections that have been requested and tracked vs. total required per inspection schedule matrix
**Verification:** KPI dashboard or periodic summary report produced at each register audit (REQ-011). Trends reported to Project Manager.
