## Report: DEL-011-03 Drive-Through Repair Bays Analysis

**Deliverable Identity:**
- **Name:** Drive-Through Repair Bays
- **ID:** DEL-011-03
- **Package:** PKG-011 Building Structure & Envelope
- **Discipline:** General Contractor (Structural/Envelope)
- **Type:** Construction | **Status:** SEMANTIC_READY
- **Covers:** SOW-0025 | **Supports:** OBJ-001

**Scope Summary:**
DEL-011-03 covers the construction of two drive-through repair bays within the New North Shop addition at West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill. The deliverable includes:
- Structural bay enclosure elements (walls, roof integration) as part of concrete superstructure
- Overhead door frames, sills, and structural rough openings (one pair per bay)
- Overhead door units, tracks, hardware, safety devices, and weather seals
- Electrical power rough-in connections for door operators
- Coordination interfaces with steel plate embedments (DEL-011-02), exhaust systems (PKG-013), sump drains (PKG-014), and electrical (PKG-015)

**Key Attributes:**
- 2 drive-through bays (separate entrance/exit doors per bay)
- Nominal bay width: 24 feet (TBD clarity on structural vs. clear opening dimension)
- Bay configuration: Drive-through with overhead motorised doors
- Ceiling clearance: 35-foot concrete structure
- Equipment target: Heavy landfill equipment including motor scraper class
- Climate: Alberta cold climate (heated interior with insulated doors anticipated)
- Floor: Concrete slab with embedded steel plates for tracked equipment protection

- **Blocking upstream:** DEL-011-01 (Superstructure), DEL-011-02 (Steel Embedments), DEL-010-01 (Foundation), building/safety permits (DEL-009-02, DEL-009-03), architectural/structural design drawings (DEL-001-02, DEL-002-03/04/07/08)

**Key Quality Observations:**

1. **Dimension Ambiguity (Lensing B-002):** The 24-foot bay width dimension lacks clarity on whether it represents nominal structural bay width (centerline-to-centerline), rough opening width, or clear opening width. IFC drawings must establish authoritative definition and account for wall thickness/column allowances.

2. **Equipment Clearance TBD (Lensing A-001, F-001):** Motor scraper class sizing (height with raised attachments, width, turning radius) requires confirmation from County fleet data and must inform final door opening dimensions in IFC drawings.

3. **Door Insulation R-value (Lensing A-005):** R-value selection for door panels should be informed by Alberta climate zone data and energy modelling. Specific selection criteria TBD pending design team input.

4. **Floor Drainage Acceptance Criteria (Lensing D-001):** Measurable acceptance criteria for floor drainage slope (percentage, direction, ponding test requirements) must be defined in IFC drawings rather than subjective assessment.

5. **Exhaust System Coordination Hold (Lensing X-002):** A defined coordination checkpoint is required between structural provisions (DEL-011-03) and mechanical rough-in (DEL-013-04) to confirm exhaust penetrations/supports are correctly located before installation proceeds.

6. **Long-Lead Procurement Risk (Lensing D-002):** Industrial overhead doors typically require 8-16 week lead times. Procurement timing must be incorporated as a critical-path item aligned to December 31, 2026 completion deadline.

7. **Door Safety Devices (REQ-011-03-015):** Motorised industrial overhead doors require obstruction sensors and auto-reverse mechanisms per safety code expectations.

8. **Lifecycle Maintenance Access (Lensing C-003):** Door type selection (sectional vs. rolling) should consider accessibility for torsion spring and operator servicing at 35-foot ceiling height.
