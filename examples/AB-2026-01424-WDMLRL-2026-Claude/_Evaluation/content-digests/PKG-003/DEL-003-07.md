### Scope

**What it covers:**
1. Materials and equipment specifications for all mechanical systems (heating, ventilation, makeup air, air exchange, exhaust)
2. Installation requirements and standards per Alberta Safety Codes
3. Performance criteria (capacities in BTU/hr, CFM, air change rates, capture velocities)
4. Coordination requirements across structural, electrical, plumbing, and architectural disciplines

**What it excludes:**
- Plumbing systems (PKG-006), electrical systems (PKG-004), structural design (PKG-002)
- Construction installation work (PKG-013 — references this specification as primary contractual requirement)
- Ceiling fan electrical power circuits (PKG-004 responsibility; mechanical covers placement and airflow function)

### Document Kit Summary

The specification is structured across five primary documents:

1. **Datasheet.md:** Identity, building context (13,000 sq ft industrial maintenance shop, 35 ft ceiling height, 2 drive-through bays, wash bay, service pit), mechanical systems identified (heating, makeup air, air exchange, equipment exhaust, welding exhaust, service pit ventilation, 6 ceiling fans, general building exhaust, mechanical controls), design parameters (heating type, makeup air capacity, air exchanger volume, exhaust coverage, natural gas tie-in, three-phase power).

2. **Specification.md:** Normative requirements (REQ-M-001 through REQ-M-007+) covering heating systems, forced-air makeup air, air exchangers, equipment exhaust, and associated systems. Multiple parameters marked TBD pending DEL-003-06 calculations (heating capacity, makeup air volume, air change rates, exhaust capacity).

3. **Guidance.md:** Design principles (P-1: occupant safety drives ventilation design; P-2: 35 ft ceiling height creates thermal stratification requiring ceiling fans; P-3: "high-recovery heating" signals owner's efficiency intent; P-4: design-build latitude requires performance-based specs, not prescriptive products; P-5: critical cross-discipline coordination). Open design decisions flagged (e.g., whether heat recovery from air exchanger should be mandatory vs. encouraged).

4. **Procedure.md:** Step-by-step workflow for producing the specification — systems inventory, design load calculations (Step 2), equipment selection (Step 3), coordination reviews, compliance verification, and finalization to IFC package state.

### Quality Observations

1. **TBD Proliferation:** Numerous performance values (heating capacity, makeup air volume, air change rates, capture velocities, ceiling height thermal stratification analysis) are explicitly marked TBD pending DEL-003-06 calculations. Estimating precision is limited until calculations finalize.

2. **Design Latitude vs. Specification Constraint:** Specification leverages design-build framework (CCDC 14–2013) by defining minimum performance criteria rather than prescriptive products, preserving design-builder flexibility while protecting owner outcomes.

3. **Estimating Impact:** Four BLOCKING dependencies (DEL-003-06, DEL-003-05, DEL-001-02, PKG-013) gate meaningful scope/cost estimation. Five companion drawing deliverables (DEL-003-02/03/04) and installation package (PKG-013) are dependent on this specification's finalization.

4. **Open Design Decisions:** Heat recovery status (mandatory vs. encouraged in air exchanger) flagged as unresolved in Guidance P-3; ceiling fan electrical/mechanical responsibility boundary pending MEP coordination (Guidance CONF-M-01); general building exhaust fan scope boundary coordination noted in Guidance CONF-M-02.

5. **Safety-Critical Scope:** Ventilation system adequacy for equipment bay exhaust, welding emissions, and below-grade pit CO prevention is life-safety-class, not comfort-class, requiring rigorous compliance with Alberta OH&S Code and industrial ventilation standards.

6. **Coordination Criticality:** Mechanical systems create extensive interfaces across structural (ductwork penetrations, equipment supports, crane clearance), electrical (power supply, VFD controls, CO sensors, ceiling fan circuits), plumbing (wash bay drainage, cistern utility room), and architectural (louver placement, utility room layout) disciplines.
