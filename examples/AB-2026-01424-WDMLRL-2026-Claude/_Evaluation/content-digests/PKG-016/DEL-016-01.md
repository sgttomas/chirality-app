### Identity

- **Package:** PKG-016 (Crane & Lifting Equipment)
- **SOW Reference:** SOW-0067
- **Responsible Party:** Crane Contractor
- **Objectives Supported:** OBJ-001 (fully functional shop), OBJ-004 (mechanical systems), OBJ-005 (electrical systems)

### Scope
This deliverable covers the procurement and installation of two 5-tonne capacity overhead cranes on trolley within the New North Shop. Work includes equipment sourcing from qualified crane suppliers, installation on corbel-supported structural runway, connection to dedicated electrical circuits, and commissioning.

**Design Parameters (per Addenda 3 & 4):**
- Hook height: 26' (above floor; constrained by 35' building ceiling)
- Maximum runway bay spacing: 25 feet
- Support method: Corbel-supported on side walls (not free-standing)
- Power supply: Three-phase

### Quality Observations

1. **TBD Parameters:** Crane span, hook height, voltage/amperage, applicable standards, Alberta regulations, and duty class remain unresolved and gate procurement. These must be finalized before RFQ issuance.

2. **Coordination Loop Risk:** DEL-016-01 receives crane span from structural design (DEL-002-07) but must provide crane load data back to structural engineer. This bidirectional interface requires early engagement; sequential design will create rework risk.

3. **Regulatory Clarity:** Alberta Safety Code specific regulation sections (e.g., Elevating Devices and Amusement Rides Regulation applicability) must be confirmed with Authority Having Jurisdiction before procurement proceeds.

4. **Duty Class Decision:** Configuration choice (single vs. double girder, shared vs. independent runways, duty class C vs. D) has significant cost and structural implications. This decision cannot remain a ASSUMPTION through procurement — requires County input.

5. **Timeline Criticality:** Given 8–12 week lead times and December 31, 2026 deadline, procurement milestones must be established working backward from required installation date and tracked as critical-path items. Delay in RFQ issuance will compress installation schedule and commissioning window.

6. **Documentation Completeness:** All engineered components (structural anchorage, load certifications, shop drawings, installation details) must be certified by Alberta P.Eng. per RFP §3.3.2.
