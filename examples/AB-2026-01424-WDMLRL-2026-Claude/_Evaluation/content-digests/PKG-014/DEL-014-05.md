### Scope

The deliverable covers:
- Procurement of one (1) code-compliant emergency shower unit (combination shower/eyewash per assumption; see Guidance CF-002)
- Complete installation with labour, materials, and equipment
- Water supply rough-in from the cistern-fed internal service water distribution system (DEL-014-01)
- Drain connection to floor drain below the unit (DEL-014-04)
- Functional testing and commissioning
- All required Safety Code permits and inspections

**Excludes:** Cistern/distribution system (DEL-014-01), floor drain (DEL-014-04), electrical work (if required; assigned to PKG-015), and OH&S program administration (DEL-019-01).

Resolution responsibility assigned to plumbing designer with escalation path if unresolved by IFC stage.

2. **Standards Hierarchy Ambiguity:** Specification declares Alberta OH&S Code Part 16 as governing authority, but source document not accessible. Specification requires confirmation at IFC stage and escalation to Owner/Alberta OH&S authority if unresolved.

3. **Life-Safety Criticality:** Emergency shower is operational-readiness safety equipment with zero-tolerance design principle (Principle P-1). Non-functionality creates regulatory compliance risk and worker safety exposure.

4. **Upstream Dependency Coupling:** Unit operability depends entirely on cistern fill level and distribution pump status (P-3). Guidance notes potential facility shutdown risk and recommends considering backup water supply or portable eyewash station during cistern unavailability.

5. **10-Second Accessibility Requirement:** P-2 references ANSI/ISEA Z358.1 10-second accessibility rule (~15–16 m unobstructed travel) to ensure worker reach-time during emergency. Conceptual drawing placement near south shop area; IFC design must confirm adequate facility-wide coverage.

6. **Tepid Water as Mandatory Design Element:** P-4 flags cold water risk (hypothermia, premature flushing termination in Alberta climate) requiring thermostatic mixing valve confirmation or documented supply temperature adequacy.
