### Quality Observations

1. **Compliance Risk — Hard Gate:** Bid security omission automatically invalidates the proposal. There is no discretion for the Owner to waive. This is a critical path item requiring early surety engagement (2–5 business days minimum for underwriting).

2. **Bid Amount Dependency:** Bid bond face value cannot be finalized until the cost estimate is complete. The final bid amount is a hard prerequisite (DEP-021-01-006, BLOCKING).

3. **Surety Jurisdiction and Address:** Surety must be licensed in Alberta and carry an Alberta address (or Alberta representative agent address) on the Consent of Surety. Non-compliance with this requirement creates automatic rejection risk.

4. **Dual Instruments:** Both the bid bond (or certified cheque) and the Consent of Surety are required. These are separate, distinct deliverables. Both must be executed and submitted simultaneously.

5. **Physical Delivery Requirement:** Proposals must be delivered in a sealed physical envelope to Camrose County. No faxed or electronic submissions are accepted. Courier/delivery timing is critical (April 2, 2026, 2:00:00 p.m. MST deadline).

6. **Post-Award Liability:** Upon award, the proponent has 21 days to sign the contract and provide required post-award security and insurance. Failure triggers forfeiture and financial liability up to the bid security amount. This contingency must be factored into bid decision-making.

7. **Addendum Acknowledgment:** Addendum No. 1 (February 19, 2026) introduced §4.11. Appendix A acknowledgment form must be completed and submitted. Failure to acknowledge may result in disqualification.

8. **Downstream Handoff:** Upon award, surety identity, Consent of Surety terms, underwriting documentation, and final contract price must transfer to DEL-021-02 (Performance & Payment Bonds). Early surety relationship establishment reduces friction at contract execution.
