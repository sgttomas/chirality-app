### Scope
The deliverable covers conducting a full geotechnical field investigation at the proposed Maintenance Shop Addition site (SW 14–44–21–W4, expansion area shown in RFP Appendix C) and producing a complete report documenting soil conditions, bearing capacity, settlement analysis, and foundation recommendations. The report is required to support foundation design (PKG-002) and enable post-investigation negotiation of final foundation pricing under RFP §4.8.2.

**Out of scope:** Foundation design, foundation construction, civil/site grading design, preliminary survey, construction survey, as-built survey, brushing/clearing/bulk cut and fill (County responsibility), and environmental assessment (TBD confirmation required for landfill context).

### Quality Observations
1. **Critical path status confirmed**: Investigation is a hard predecessor to foundation design and pricing negotiation; delays directly affect project completion deadline of December 31, 2026.
2. **Variable-price commercial mechanism**: The report's assessment of "normal ground conditions" vs. "deleterious subgrade material" (REQ-004, RFP §4.8.2) is the contractual trigger for foundation price adjustment; this makes the report both technical and commercial in nature.
3. **Scope ambiguities requiring resolution**:
   - Structural load data from PKG-002 must be obtained before investigation program finalization (Step 2.2, item B-001 in semantic lensing)
   - Definition of "deleterious subgrade material" and "normal ground conditions" criteria must be established with Owner before report finalization (Guidance item X-003)
   - Groundwater monitoring approach (single-reading vs. extended monitoring) requires decision and rationale documentation (item B-002)
   - Report transmission format (hard copy/electronic, number of copies) must be confirmed with Owner (item X-004)
   - Environmental assessment applicability for subsurface investigation on active landfill site must be confirmed (item C-002)
4. **Site context complexities**: Active landfill operations, County site preparation activities (brushing/clearing/cut-fill), and mandatory site coordination requirements necessitate careful sequencing and access management.
5. **Service pit consideration**: Below-grade service pit structure (RFP §3.4; SOW-0028) requires geotechnical analysis of excavation, lateral earth pressure, and groundwater ingress (now formalized in REQ-011).
