## Report: DEL-006-06 Fixture Schedule Analysis

### Deliverable Identity and Context

**DEL-006-06 — Plumbing Fixture Schedule** is a normative engineering artifact for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project (AB-2026-01424-WDMLRL). The deliverable is the responsibility of the Plumbing Engineer within PKG-006 (Plumbing Design) and traces directly to:

- **SOW-0016** (Schedule Production)
- **OBJ-003** (Produce P.Eng.-stamped IFC design documentation)
- **OBJ-004** (Install and commission plumbing systems to operational readiness)

The project scope spans both new construction (New North Shop, ~13,000 sq ft) and renovations to the Old North Shop, with a completion deadline of December 31, 2026.

---

### Document Kit Summary

The deliverable package contains eight core documents:

1. **_CONTEXT.md** — Establishes the deliverable identity, responsible parties, scope, and anticipated artifacts (fixture tables, equipment specs, manufacturer selections, calculations, code compliance notes, drawing cross-references).

2. **_DEPENDENCIES.md** — Registers 15 active dependencies (3 structural anchors + 12 execution dependencies). Key blocking gates include:
   - DEL-001-02 (Architectural Floor Plans) — fixture locations depend on room layouts
   - DEL-001-10 (Old North Shop Renovation Plans) — renovation scope quantities blocked without plans
   - DEL-006-01 (Preliminary Plumbing Design approval) — approval gate before IFC issue
   - Downstream: PKG-014 (Plumbing Installation) — installation pricing blocked without the schedule

3. **_REFERENCES.md** — Points to two primary sources:
   - **R-01:** AB-2026-01424-WDMLRL RFP.pdf (main specification, §1.0, §3.4)
   - **R-06:** Appendix B (Plumbing).pdf (conceptual plumbing layout)

4. **Datasheet.md** — Provides identification, building context, and preliminary fixture inventory derived from the conceptual drawing (App B-Plumbing). Identifies fixtures by location (main bay, wash bay, washroom, utility room) and cross-references to SOW items (SOW-0041 through SOW-0074).

5. **Specification.md** — Defines scope, requirements (REQ-001 through REQ-004 shown in sample), and exclusions. Key requirement: the schedule must be internally consistent with all other plumbing design documents and serve as the single source of fixture truth.

6. **Guidance.md** — Establishes four design principles:
   - The schedule is the single source of truth for fixture identity (consistency with all design documents)
   - Industrial context drives fixture selection (durability, serviceability, function over aesthetics)
   - Completeness includes both new construction and renovation scope
   - Emergency shower is safety-critical (ANSI/ASSE 1009 compliance)

7. **Procedure.md** — Describes nine steps for producing the schedule, including establishment of fixture inventory from the conceptual drawing, reconciliation against RFP/SOW requirements, and coordinate checks.

8. **_SEMANTIC.md** — Applies Chirality Semantic Algebra framework defining orientation matrices (normative/operative/evaluative by guiding/applying/judging/reviewing) and conceptualization matrices (necessity/sufficiency/completeness/consistency across data/information/knowledge/wisdom levels).

### Key Quality Observations

**Completeness and Scope Clarity:**
- The deliverable clearly delineates scope boundaries (what is included in the fixture schedule vs. what belongs to concurrent deliverables like DEL-006-02, DEL-006-03, DEL-006-04).
- Fixture inventory is explicitly anchored to both the conceptual drawing (App B-Plumbing) and RFP/SOW requirements.
- Renovation scope is integrated (Old North Shop washroom, urinal, laundry services).

**Coordination and Dependencies:**
- 15 active dependencies mapped with confidence levels and impact classes (BLOCKING, ADVISORY, INFO).
- Dependencies balance internal PKG-006 concurrency (DEL-006-02, 03, 04, 07) with external architectural gates (DEL-001-02, DEL-001-10).
- Downstream handoff to PKG-014 (Installation) is flagged as BLOCKING.

**Design Assumptions and TBDs:**
- Multiple critical design decisions are explicitly marked TBD: floor drain types/materials, sump drain configuration (trench vs. point), washroom fixture count in New North Shop, laundry equipment specifications.
- Building occupancy classification (Alberta Building Code Group F Division 2/3) is flagged as TBD — affects code-required minimum fixture counts.
- Accessibility requirements for industrial washrooms require verification by the engineer of record.

**Semantic Framework Integration:**
- The _SEMANTIC.md file anchors the deliverable within Chirality Semantic Algebra, providing orientation and conceptualization matrices to support quality assurance and cross-discipline reviews.
