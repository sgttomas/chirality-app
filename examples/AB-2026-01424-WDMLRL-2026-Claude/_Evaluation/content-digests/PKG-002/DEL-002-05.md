### Scope - Description, Acceptance Criteria, SOW IDs, OBJ IDs
**Description:** Mezzanine Framing Details — Drawing Set deliverable covering structural design and detailing of the overhead mezzanine structure spanning Parts Room, Utility Room, and Wash Bay in the New North Shop addition. Encompasses framing members, deck/floor system, guardrails, edge protection, stair structure, and all connections/anchorages into the primary concrete structure.

**Acceptance Criteria:** IFC-stamped drawing set signed by Professional Engineer licensed in Alberta; compliance with ABC requirements; demonstrated coordination with primary concrete structure; stated design live load for heavy storage use case; detailed stair and guardrail design.

### Document Kit Summary - Datasheet/Specification/Guidance/Procedure
- **Datasheet.md:** Descriptive inventory of mezzanine location/extent (Parts Room ~400 sq ft, Utility Room, 24'-wide Wash Bay), structural characteristics (load-bearing for oil totes and pumping equipment, TBD framing system), and preliminary dead load estimates by candidate system; multiple TBD attributes requiring Owner confirmation of live load and equipment arrangement (B-001).

- **Specification.md:** Normative requirements REQ-DS-001 through REQ-DS-011 governing scope (mezzanine span over three rooms, load-bearing for heavy storage, stair access, ABC compliance, IFC stamping), exclusions table (main framing, foundations, service pit, crane structure, etc. covered by other deliverables), and design requirements (live load determination, CSA S16/A23.3 code compliance, guardrail height/load per ABC, stair geometry).

- **Guidance.md:** Directional principles explaining heavy-storage design intent (oil totes ~1,000 kg each), concrete-dominant building interface strategy (preference for cast-in-place embeds), stair/guardrail as life-safety elements, and framing system selection considerations (Steel Option A vs. Concrete Option B; steel favored for speed and flexibility).

- **Procedure.md:** Operational steps 1–7 for production, starting with conflict resolution (CF-DS-001 stair scope boundary, CF-DS-002 mezzanine extent over wash bay), Owner confirmation of live load and clearance requirements, code edition confirmation, and multi-discipline coordination with Architect, Plumbing, Mechanical, and Electrical.
