# Procedure — DEL-014-04 Floor Drains & Sump Drains

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps the Plumbing Contractor shall follow to produce and deliver the floor drain and sump drain installation for the New North Shop addition (DEL-014-04). It covers pre-installation coordination, material procurement and submittals, installation, inspection, and closeout. The procedure applies to both SOW-0045 (sump drains in repair bays) and SOW-0046 (floor drains throughout the shop).

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Design Coordination and Procurement

**Step 1.1 — Review IFC Plumbing Drawings**
Upon receipt of IFC plumbing drawings (DEL-006-03), the Plumbing Contractor shall review drain locations, invert elevations, pipe slopes, drain body specifications, and venting requirements. Confirm that all drain locations shown on the drawings are consistent with the conceptual drawing (App B — Plumbing, R-06). Flag any conflicts or missing information to the Plumbing Engineer before proceeding.

**Step 1.2 — Prepare and Submit Product Submittals**
Prepare and submit manufacturer data sheets, cut sheets, and load-rating documentation for:
- All floor drain bodies and grates (including heavy-duty grates for repair bays and wash bay)
- Sump drain bodies and grates
- Trap types (integral or separate P-trap as specified)
- Trap primers or trap seal devices (if specified by design)

Submit to Plumbing Engineer for review and acceptance before ordering materials.

**Step 1.3 — Confirm Scope Boundary with DEL-018-05 Contractor**
Before commencing wash bay drain rough-in, confirm in writing with the General Contractor (DEL-018-05) the exact scope boundary for the wash bay drain system: where the Plumbing Contractor's work ends and the civil drainage / mud sump work begins. Document the agreed interface point. Source: Guidance CFT-014-04-01; Specification Scope section "Scope Boundary — Wash Bay Drain (Normative)."

**Step 1.4 — Coordinate Oil/Grease Interceptor Determination**
Before procurement, coordinate with the Plumbing Engineer (PKG-006) to confirm whether an oil/grease interceptor is required upstream of the septic connection per Specification REQ-014-04-11. Obtain written determination and document the outcome. If an interceptor is required, ensure it is included in the procurement scope (Step 1.5). Source: Guidance Conflict Table CFT-014-04-02; Specification REQ-014-04-11. [Enrichment: A-004 — oil/grease interceptor coordination step.]

**Step 1.5 — Procure Materials**
Order all approved drain bodies, grates, traps, and associated fittings (and oil/grease interceptor if required per Step 1.4). Verify lead times against the concrete pour schedule; drain bodies must be on-site before the floor slab is poured.

---

### Phase 2 — Underground Rough-In (Before Slab Pour)

> **HOLD-POINT:** Do not commence Phase 2 until IFC plumbing drawings (DEL-006-03) have been received and reviewed per Step 1.1. Source: Specification REQ-014-04-04; Procedure Prerequisites. [Enrichment: C-003 — hold-point enforcement.]

**Step 2.1 — Coordinate Pre-Pour Meeting**
Hold a pre-pour coordination meeting with the General Contractor (PKG-011) to confirm:
- All drain body locations (horizontal position)
- Finished floor elevation for each drain
- Pipe routing, trench locations, and any conflicts with structural embedments (steel plates, footings)

The County project representative shall be notified and invited to attend this coordination meeting per Specification REQ-014-04-09 ("present for all inspections"). Obtain sign-off from General Contractor confirming drains are correctly positioned before concrete placement. Document the sign-off. [Enrichment: X-004 — County PM presence normalized to include pre-pour coordination.]

**Step 2.2 — Excavate Trenches and Install Underground Drainage Piping**
Excavate trenches for underground drain piping as shown on IFC plumbing drawings. Install all underground drain piping including:
- Main drain runs from sump/floor drain bodies to building drain/vent manifold
- Connections to septic system (for shop floor and sump drains)
- Connection stub for wash bay drain to exterior mud sump (per agreed scope boundary, Step 1.3)
Maintain specified pipe slopes (per IFC drawings; minimum slope per National Plumbing Code of Canada, edition TBD).

**Step 2.3 — Set Drain Bodies**
Set all drain bodies to finished floor elevation as shown on IFC drawings, within the tolerance specified in REQ-014-04-03 (TBD per IFC drawings, or +/- 3mm default if not specified). Brace or secure drain bodies to prevent movement during concrete placement. Protect drain openings (plug or cover) to prevent concrete entry during the pour.

**Step 2.4 — Underground Rough-In Inspection**
Prior to covering underground piping or pouring concrete, arrange for the Safety Code plumbing rough-in inspection. Ensure the County project representative is present per Specification REQ-014-04-09. Obtain signed inspection report. **Do not pour concrete slab until rough-in inspection is passed and documented.**

---

### Phase 3 — Slab Pour Coordination

**Step 3.1 — Monitor During Concrete Pour**
Be present (or have a designated representative on site) during the concrete floor pour to:
- Verify drain bodies remain at correct elevation throughout pour
- Protect drain bodies and exposed piping from concrete damage
- Remove plugs/covers from drain openings only after concrete has cured sufficiently

**Step 3.2 — Verify Drain Elevations After Pour**
After concrete has cured, verify that all drain tops are flush with or at the correct depression below finished floor elevation per IFC drawings, within the tolerance specified in REQ-014-04-03. Document any deviations for corrective action.

---

### Phase 4 — Above-Ground Rough-In and Venting

**Step 4.1 — Install Above-Ground Drain/Vent Piping**
Install all above-ground drain and vent piping connecting floor/sump drains to the building drain/vent stack and vent-through-roof system, as shown on IFC plumbing drawings. Install trap primers or trap seal devices at locations identified by the Plumbing Engineer per Specification REQ-014-04-07.

**Step 4.2 — Install Drain Grates**
Install all drain grates. Confirm grate type (heavy-duty in repair bays and wash bay; standard in washroom, emergency shower area, and other non-vehicle-traffic areas) matches IFC drawing specifications and approved submittals.

---

### Phase 5 — Testing and Final Inspection

**Step 5.1 — Pressure/Water Test**
Test the entire drain/vent system in accordance with National Plumbing Code of Canada requirements (edition TBD — hydrostatic or air test, per code requirement applicable at time of construction). Document test method, test pressure, duration, and result.

**Step 5.2 — Final Plumbing Inspection**
Arrange for Safety Code final plumbing inspection. Ensure County project representative is present per Specification REQ-014-04-09. Obtain signed inspection report. Address any deficiencies before requesting re-inspection.

**Step 5.3 — Functional Water Test**
Conduct a functional water-flow test of all installed floor and sump drains. The County project representative shall be notified and invited to attend per Specification REQ-014-04-09 ("present for all inspections"). [Enrichment: X-004 — County PM presence normalized to include functional test.]

Test procedure:
- Pour water into each drain and confirm it flows freely without backup or pooling
- Quantitative acceptance criteria: TBD — the Plumbing Engineer shall specify maximum drain clearance time and minimum test water volume. Until specified, a minimum of 10 litres of water shall be poured into each drain and shall clear completely within a reasonable time with no residual pooling. **ASSUMPTION:** Specific quantitative thresholds (e.g., maximum clearance time in seconds) are to be established by the Plumbing Engineer or per NPC requirements. [Enrichment: X-003 — quantitative criteria framework for functional test; marked TBD pending Plumbing Engineer specification.]
- Confirm trap seals are established in all drains
- Confirm no sewer gas odour is present at any drain location

**Step 5.4 — Pipe Slope Verification**
Verify all drain piping is installed at minimum slopes per IFC drawings and National Plumbing Code requirements. Document slope measurements for each pipe run. Source: Specification REQ-014-04-04; NPC (edition TBD). [Enrichment: F-004 — pipe slope verification step added.]

---

### Phase 6 — Closeout

**Step 6.1 — Prepare As-Built Mark-Ups**
Mark up the IFC plumbing drawings to show actual installed locations of all drain bodies, invert elevations, pipe sizes and materials, and any deviations from the issued design. Submit to the Plumbing Engineer for review.

**Step 6.2 — Compile Documentation Package**
Assemble and submit all closeout documentation (see Records section below).

**Step 6.3 — Commissioning Readiness Verification**
Before engaging the Commissioning Agent (DEL-020-01), verify all of the following:
- All Safety Code inspections (rough-in and final) are passed and documented
- All functional tests (Step 5.1, 5.3, 5.4) are completed and documented
- All as-built mark-ups are prepared (Step 6.1)
- All records in the Records table are assembled or in progress

This readiness check confirms that DEL-014-04 is ready for inclusion in the building systems commissioning scope. Document the readiness confirmation. [Enrichment: F-003 — commissioning readiness gate.]

**Step 6.4 — Coordinate with Commissioning Agent**
Ensure drain systems are included in the building systems commissioning scope (DEL-020-01). Provide any required access or demonstrations to the commissioning agent.

## Verification

| Check | Method | Acceptance Criterion |
|---|---|---|
| Drain locations match IFC drawings | Field survey against IFC drawing coordinates | All drains within tolerance per IFC drawings; no location omissions |
| Drain bodies at correct elevation | Survey after pour | Drain top at finished floor level (or designed depression) +/- tolerance per IFC drawings (TBD; default +/- 3mm per REQ-014-04-03) |
| Underground rough-in inspection passed | Safety Code inspection report | Signed and approved inspection report; no outstanding deficiencies |
| All drains drain freely | Functional water test (Step 5.3) | No backup; water clears drain within specified time (TBD per Plumbing Engineer); no pooling |
| Trap seals established | Functional test; no odour | No sewer gas odour present at any drain |

| Final plumbing inspection passed | Safety Code final inspection report | Signed and approved inspection report; all deficiencies resolved |
| Wash bay scope boundary confirmed | Written agreement on file | Signed scope boundary document between Plumbing Contractor and DEL-018-05 contractor |
