# Specification — DEL-014-04 Floor Drains & Sump Drains

---

## Scope

### Included

This deliverable covers the supply and installation of all floor drains and sump drains within the New North Shop addition, as follows:

- **SOW-0045:** Sump drains in the repair bays (R-01 S3.4; Decomposition SOW-0045).
- **SOW-0046:** Floor drains throughout the shop as shown on the plumbing drawing (App B — Plumbing; Decomposition SOW-0046).
- All drain bodies, grates, traps, trap primers (if required), and connection piping from drain outlet to the building's drain/vent system.
- Setting of drain bodies to correct floor elevation in coordination with the concrete floor pour.
- All rough-in, final connection, and testing of the drain/vent tie-ins.
- Drain at the emergency shower wash station (interface with DEL-014-05).
- Drain(s) in the renovated washroom area (interface with DEL-014-06 and renovation scope).

### Excluded

- Exterior mud sump construction and associated civil drainage from wash bay to mud sump — that scope belongs to DEL-018-05 (Wash Bay Drainage & Mud Sump, PKG-018 — General Contractor).
- Design of the drain/vent system — governed by DEL-006-03 (Drain & Vent Plans, PKG-006 — Plumbing Engineer); installation shall conform to IFC drawings when issued.
- Supply and installation of the septic holding tank — governed by DEL-014-02 (Septic System).
- Supply and installation of plumbing fixtures (sinks, toilets, urinals) — governed by DEL-014-06.
- Supply and installation of the emergency shower head/eye wash unit — governed by DEL-014-05.
- Concrete floor construction and steel plate embedments — governed by PKG-011.

### Scope Boundary — Wash Bay Drain (Normative)

The scope boundary between DEL-014-04 (Plumbing Contractor) and DEL-018-05 (General Contractor, civil/site) for the wash bay drainage path shall be formally defined in the IFC plumbing and civil drawings. Until IFC drawings resolve this, the scope boundary remains TBD and shall be confirmed by written agreement between the two contractors before installation commences. See Guidance Conflict Table CFT-014-04-01. Source: Decomposition SOW-0046/DEL-014-04 vs. SOW-0027b/DEL-018-05. [Enrichment: D-001 — elevated from Conflict Table entry to normative scope statement.]

## Requirements

### REQ-014-04-01 — Floor Drain Installation per Drawing
**Source:** SOW-0046 (Decomposition); R-06 (App B — Plumbing)
Floor drains shall be installed throughout the shop at the locations shown on the Issued-for-Construction plumbing drawings. Until IFC drawings are issued, the conceptual plumbing drawing (App B) shall be used as the minimum location reference showing: four (4) floor drains in the main shop / repair bay area, one (1) floor drain in the wash bay, and one (1) drain in the washroom area.

### REQ-014-04-02 — Sump Drain Installation in Repair Bays
**Source:** SOW-0045 (Decomposition); R-01 S3.4 ("Sump drains in the repair bays")
Sump drains shall be installed in each repair bay. Quantity, sizing, and type shall be per IFC plumbing drawings. **ASSUMPTION:** Each drive-through repair bay (two bays, each approx. 24' wide per App B) receives at least one sump drain; final count per design.

### REQ-014-04-03 — Drain Elevation and Floor Coordination
**Source:** ASSUMPTION (standard construction practice)
All drain bodies shall be set to the correct finished floor elevation prior to concrete placement. The Plumbing Contractor shall coordinate with the General Contractor (PKG-011 concrete scope) on drain placement and elevation before the floor slab is poured. Installation elevation tolerance: TBD — to be specified by the Plumbing Engineer in IFC drawings, or per industry standard practice (e.g., +/- 3mm from design elevation). If IFC drawings do not specify a tolerance, the Plumbing Contractor shall request clarification from the Plumbing Engineer before installation. [Enrichment: C-004 — added elevation tolerance requirement; marked TBD pending IFC specification.]

### REQ-014-04-04 — Connection to Drain/Vent System
**Source:** SOW-0046; R-06; ASSUMPTION (standard plumbing practice)
All floor and sump drains shall be connected to the building's drain/vent system as shown on IFC plumbing drawings (DEL-006-03). Venting shall be provided in accordance with the National Plumbing Code of Canada (edition TBD — see REQ-014-04-05 and Standards table) and applicable Alberta Safety Codes.

> **Note:** The applicable edition of the National Plumbing Code of Canada is not identified in the RFP. The Plumbing Engineer (PKG-006) shall confirm the applicable NPC edition before procurement and installation proceed. This confirmation shall be documented. [Enrichment: A-002 — NPC edition clarification mechanism.]

### REQ-014-04-05 — Code Compliance
**Source:** R-01 S3.3.2 ("The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes"); S3.3.2 ("The building design must adhere to all applicable building codes and regulations")
All floor drain and sump drain installations shall comply with:
- Alberta Safety Codes Act and applicable Safety Code standards (plumbing)
- National Plumbing Code of Canada (edition TBD — to be confirmed by Plumbing Engineer per REQ-014-04-04 note)
- Alberta Building Code (applicability TBD — to be confirmed by AHJ / Safety Code Officer; see Standards table)
- Any local Camrose County amendments or requirements (TBD — to be confirmed with Camrose County / AHJ before construction; see F-002 note below)

> **TBD (F-002):** Confirm whether Camrose County has any local plumbing amendments or additional requirements beyond provincial Safety Codes. This must be resolved before construction commences. Source: Specification REQ-014-04-05; _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-002. [Enrichment: F-002 — TBD tracking for Camrose County amendments.]

### REQ-014-04-06 — Load Capacity in Heavy Equipment Areas
**Source:** R-01 S3.4 ("Steel plating in concrete at strategic locations to accommodate track and packer type heavy equipment"); App B (repair bays, wash bay)
Drain grates and frames installed in repair bays and the wash bay shall be rated for the wheel loads and track loads of heavy equipment (e.g., motor scrapers). Minimum load rating: TBD — to be specified by the Plumbing Engineer or Structural Engineer in IFC design (e.g., minimum H-20 loading per AASHTO or equivalent load class). If IFC drawings do not specify a minimum load class, the Plumbing Contractor shall request clarification from the Plumbing Engineer before procurement. [Enrichment: A-003 — added mechanism to resolve load rating acceptance threshold.]

### REQ-014-04-07 — Trap Requirements
**Source:** ASSUMPTION (standard plumbing practice / code requirement)
All floor and sump drains shall be provided with a trap (P-trap or integral trap as appropriate) to prevent sewer gas entry into the occupied building. Trap primers or trap seal protection shall be provided where drains are subject to infrequent use and evaporation risk, as required by code. The Plumbing Engineer shall identify which specific drain locations require trap primers or trap seal protection devices during IFC design, considering that drains in infrequently used areas (e.g., emergency shower area, underused bays) are at higher evaporation risk. Specific trap type TBD per IFC design. Source: ASSUMPTION (standard plumbing practice); NPC (location TBD); Guidance Principle 4. [Enrichment: X-001 — clarified that specific trap primer locations must be identified by the Plumbing Engineer.]

### REQ-014-04-08 — Wash Bay Drain Interface
**Source:** App B — Plumbing (wash bay floor drain shown); Decomposition SOW-0027b, DEL-018-05
The wash bay floor drain shall be connected to the wash bay drainage system that routes to the exterior mud sump (DEL-018-05). The exact connection point between the plumbing contractor's scope (DEL-014-04) and the civil contractor's scope (DEL-018-05) shall be defined in the IFC plumbing and civil drawings, or by written scope boundary agreement if IFC drawings do not resolve the interface. See Scope section "Scope Boundary — Wash Bay Drain (Normative)" above. **ASSUMPTION:** The Plumbing Contractor installs the interior wash bay drain body and connection piping to the building's drainage system; the civil contractor constructs the exterior mud sump and connecting drainage from the building exterior.

### REQ-014-04-09 — Permits and Inspections
**Source:** R-01 S3.3.2
All plumbing rough-in and final inspections required under Alberta Safety Codes shall be arranged by the Plumbing Contractor. The County project representative shall be present for all inspections and shall receive a copy of all completed inspection reports.

### REQ-014-04-10 — Completion Deadline
**Source:** R-01 S3.1
All work under DEL-014-04 shall be complete on or before December 31, 2026.

### REQ-014-04-11 — Oil/Grease Interceptor Determination (NEW)
**Source:** Guidance Conflict Table CFT-014-04-02; ASSUMPTION (provincial environmental/plumbing regulations for heavy equipment maintenance facilities)
The Plumbing Engineer (PKG-006) shall determine whether an oil/grease interceptor is required upstream of the septic connection per the National Plumbing Code of Canada and Alberta environmental regulations, and the determination shall be documented. If required, the interceptor shall be included in the IFC plumbing design (DEL-006-03). This requirement is added because the Conflict Table identifies oil/grease interceptor ambiguity (CFT-014-04-02), and a prescriptive requirement is needed to mandate the design determination. [Enrichment: A-001 — new requirement to formalize oil/grease interceptor design determination.]

The Plumbing Contractor performing work under DEL-014-04 shall hold a valid Alberta plumbing trade license/certification as required by the Alberta Safety Codes Act. **ASSUMPTION:** The CCDC 14-2013 Design-Build contract may address contractor qualifications; this requirement is stated here for completeness. If the contract already addresses this, this requirement is satisfied by contractual compliance. [Enrichment: C-002 — contractor qualification requirement.]

### REQ-014-04-13 — Emergency Shower Drain Sizing (NEW)
**Source:** Guidance Examples section (ASSUMPTION — industry practice); interface with DEL-014-05
The emergency shower area drain shall be sized to handle the full flow rate of the emergency shower/eye wash unit. **ASSUMPTION:** Typical emergency shower flow rate is 75.7 L/min per ANSI Z358.1; actual flow rate TBD per the emergency shower unit selected (DEL-014-05). The Plumbing Engineer shall confirm drain sizing in IFC design. [Enrichment: B-003 — added emergency shower drain sizing signal as a normative requirement.]
