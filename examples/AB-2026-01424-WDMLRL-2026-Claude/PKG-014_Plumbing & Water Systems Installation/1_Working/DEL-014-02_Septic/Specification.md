# Specification — DEL-014-02: Septic System

---

## Scope

### What this deliverable covers

DEL-014-02 covers the complete supply, delivery, and installation of a new 2,000 US gal (minimum) septic holding tank and the removal of the existing septic tank at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition site (SW 14–44–21–W4, near Ferintosh, Alberta).

Scope includes:
- Supply of a new 2,000 US gal (minimum) septic holding tank (Source: RFP §3.4; SOW-0043)
- Delivery of the tank to site
- Installation of the tank at the location shown on the conceptual plumbing drawing (Appendix B — Plumbing, R-06), confirmed by IFC plumbing drawings (DEL-006-05)
- Connection of the tank to the building's wastewater drainage network per IFC plumbing drawings
- Removal (demolition) of the existing septic tank from site (Source: SOW-0043; D-002)
- All necessary bedding, backfill, and surface restoration associated with tank installation and existing tank removal

### What this deliverable excludes

- Relocation of the existing septic tank to any new location on site — OUT of scope per Decision D-002 (Decomposition Decision Log)
- Plumbing engineering design (covered by PKG-006, DEL-006-05)
- Electrical alarm/monitoring systems for septic — TBD: confirm whether a high-level alarm is code-required for holding tanks under Alberta Safety Codes; if required, scope assignment and coordination with PKG-015 must be resolved (ASSUMPTION: may be code-required; see Guidance § Considerations — Alarm / Monitoring) [B-004]
- Permit fees (paid by Camrose County per RFP §3.3.1)
- Site bulk cut and fill (County forces per RFP §3.3.1)
- Aggregate supply (County forces per RFP §3.3.1)

## Requirements

### REQ-014-02-01: Tank Supply and Capacity
The Plumbing Contractor shall supply a new septic holding tank with a minimum capacity of 2,000 US gal.
- Source: RFP §3.4; SOW-0043 (Decomposition)
- Verification: Material submittal confirming tank capacity rating prior to installation.

### REQ-014-02-02: Tank Installation Location
The tank shall be installed at the location indicated on the IFC plumbing drawings (DEL-006-05). The conceptual location is shown on the north side of the New North Shop building per Appendix B (Plumbing).
- Source: R-06 (Appendix B — Plumbing drawing); DEL-006-05 (location TBD pending IFC design)
- Verification: Field inspection confirming installation location consistent with IFC drawings.

### REQ-014-02-03: Existing Tank Removal
The existing septic tank shall be removed (demolished and disposed of or decommissioned). Removal and disposal shall comply with the Alberta Environmental Protection and Enhancement Act (EPEA) and the Alberta Waste Control Regulation, including lawful disposal of tank contents and tank structure. Relocation is explicitly out of scope per Decision D-002. ASSUMPTION: EPEA and Waste Control Regulation are the governing regulations for septic tank decommissioning and disposal; specific clause-level requirements TBD pending confirmation by environmental consultant or County regulatory liaison [C-001].
- Source: SOW-0043; D-002 (Decomposition Decision Log); EPEA (ASSUMPTION — location TBD)
- Verification: Site inspection confirming existing tank has been removed; disposal documentation demonstrating compliance with EPEA and Waste Control Regulation; regulatory sign-off if required by Authority Having Jurisdiction [C-002].

### REQ-014-02-04: Connection to Facility Wastewater System
The new septic holding tank shall be connected to the facility's wastewater drainage network per the IFC plumbing drawings (DEL-006-05, DEL-006-03). At minimum, the following acceptance criteria shall apply:
- All gravity connections shall maintain the minimum pipe slope specified in the IFC plumbing drawings (TBD pending DEL-006-05 design completion) [D-001].
- Connection method (e.g., solvent weld, mechanical coupling, rubber gasketed) shall be as specified in the IFC plumbing drawings [D-001].
- A leak/pressure test shall be performed on all new connections. Test parameters: TBD — the Plumbing Engineer of Record shall specify the test method (hydrostatic or air test), test pressure, hold duration, and pass/fail criteria per applicable Alberta Plumbing Code requirements [X-004]. ASSUMPTION: hydrostatic test is standard practice for below-grade sanitary connections.
- Source: ASSUMPTION based on deliverable function (_CONTEXT.md); connection details and test parameters TBD pending IFC plumbing design (DEL-006-05)
- Verification: Leak/pressure test with documented parameters, results, and pass/fail determination; County project representative to be present.

### REQ-014-02-05: Code Compliance
All work shall comply with applicable Alberta Safety Codes, Alberta Plumbing Code, and any municipal/county requirements. The specific Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) for plumbing and septic code compliance at this site shall be confirmed prior to permit application. ASSUMPTION: the AHJ is likely the Camrose County Safety Codes Officer or a designated agency under Alberta Municipal Affairs; specific identity TBD [A-003].
- Source: RFP §3.3.2 ("The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes")
- Verification: Safety Code permit sign-off by the identified AHJ; inspection records.
- PROPOSAL: Owner/County to confirm AHJ identity [A-003].

### REQ-014-02-06: IFC Drawing Basis
Installation shall be executed in accordance with P.Eng.-stamped Issued for Construction (IFC) plumbing drawings. Installation shall not commence prior to IFC drawing approval.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2 ("All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta")
- Verification: Confirmed IFC drawings on file before installation begins.

### REQ-014-02-07: Inspections
The Contractor shall submit all inspection requests. The County project representative must be present for all inspections and shall be provided with copies of all completed inspection reports.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2
- Verification: Inspection request logs; copies of all inspection reports provided to Owner.

### REQ-014-02-08: Warranty
All materials and workmanship shall be warranted for the guarantee period: the construction period plus two (2) years following the date noted on the Construction Completion Certificate (CCC). The warranty start date shall be documented, and a deficiency notification process shall be established for the warranty period per CCDC 14 warranty provisions.
- Source: RFP §2.10.2; CCDC 14–2013 (warranty provisions)
- Verification: CCC issuance; warranty documentation submitted at project closeout; warranty start date recorded; deficiency notification process confirmed with contract administrator [X-005].
- PROPOSAL: Contract administrator to confirm CCDC 14 warranty enforcement provisions [X-005].

### REQ-014-02-09: Completion Deadline
All work shall be completed on or before December 31, 2026.
- Source: RFP §3.1
- Verification: CCC issued on or before December 31, 2026.

### REQ-014-02-10: Minimum Tank Burial Depth and Frost Protection
The septic holding tank shall be installed at a minimum burial depth sufficient to provide frost protection in accordance with the applicable Alberta Plumbing Code requirements and the geotechnical investigation report. ASSUMPTION: frost depth in central Alberta (near Ferintosh) typically exceeds 1.5 m; minimum installation depth and any insulation requirements TBD pending geotechnical report and Plumbing Engineer of Record confirmation [X-002].
- Source: ASSUMPTION based on central Alberta climate conditions; geotechnical report TBD; Alberta Plumbing Code (specific clause TBD — location TBD)
- Verification: Confirmation that installation depth meets or exceeds geotechnical and code-specified frost protection requirements; field measurement documented prior to backfill.
- PROPOSAL: Geotechnical report + Plumbing Engineer of Record to specify [X-002].

### REQ-014-02-11: Backfill and Compaction
All backfill and compaction around the new tank and in the existing tank excavation shall comply with the geotechnical engineer's recommendations and the IFC plumbing drawings (DEL-006-05). Compaction testing or engineer inspection shall be performed to verify backfill quality. ASSUMPTION: compaction standard and testing requirements TBD pending geotechnical investigation [F-002].
