# Procedure — DEL-014-02: Septic System

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps required to produce and verify the complete installation of the septic system for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition, in accordance with SOW-0043, RFP §3.4, and the IFC plumbing design (DEL-006-05). The procedure covers both the installation of the new 2,000 US gal septic holding tank and the removal of the existing septic tank.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Pre-Construction Coordination

1.1 Confirm IFC plumbing drawings (DEL-006-05) are approved and on file. Do not begin excavation or installation until drawings are available.
(Source: Specification REQ-014-02-06; RFP §3.3.2)

1.2 Confirm the existing septic tank's location, condition, and service connections using the site survey and available record drawings. Identify any active connections that must remain in service until the new system is ready.

1.3 Submit all inspection requests to the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) and notify the County project representative. County representative must be present for all inspections. (Source: RFP §3.3.2; Specification REQ-014-02-07)

1.4 Confirm disposal/decommissioning plan with applicable Alberta regulatory authority for existing tank contents and structure.

1.5 Coordinate with DEL-014-04 (Floor Drains & Sump Drains) and DEL-014-06 (Plumbing Fixtures) contractors to sequence work and confirm pipe routing and connection points before excavation. (Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md)

1.6 Confirm vacuum truck access path to the new tank location is unobstructed in the site layout (coordinate with PKG-018 Sitework if required).

1.7 **Service continuity planning [D-002]:** Determine whether the existing septic system has any active service connections that must remain operational during the transition period between existing tank decommissioning and new tank commissioning. If active connections exist, develop a temporary wastewater management plan (e.g., temporary holding, portable sanitary facilities, or phased disconnection/reconnection) to maintain facility wastewater service continuity. Coordinate the plan with the Owner and affected contractors (DEL-014-04, DEL-014-06). If no active connections exist (i.e., existing system is already inactive), document this finding and proceed with removal as a site clearing activity. (Source: Guidance § Principles — Principle 2; ASSUMPTION: service continuity should be addressed prior to removal [D-002].)

---

### Step 2 — Existing Septic Tank Removal

2.1 Photograph the existing septic tank and surrounding area before commencing removal work (concealed-work documentation [E-002]). Arrange for licensed vacuum truck contractor to pump out remaining contents of the existing septic tank. Dispose of contents per applicable Alberta environmental regulations (EPEA and Waste Control Regulation — see Specification REQ-014-02-03 [C-001]) and confirm disposal with documentation.

2.2 Excavate around the existing tank as required for safe removal. Follow Alberta OHS Code, Part 32 — Excavating (ASSUMPTION: applicable excavation safety standard; specific edition TBD — location TBD [A-004]) and confined space requirements (if applicable). Ensure excavation protection (shoring, sloping, or trench box) is in place per the standard before personnel enter the excavation.

2.3 Disconnect all service connections from the existing tank. Cap or plug open pipe ends to prevent contamination.

2.4 Remove the existing septic tank structure from the excavation. Dispose of tank material per EPEA and Waste Control Regulation requirements (see Specification REQ-014-02-03). Obtain and retain disposal documentation. (Source: SOW-0043; D-002 — relocation is OUT of scope)

2.5 Backfill and compact the excavation from the existing tank removal per geotechnical requirements and IFC drawings. Restore surface to match surrounding conditions.

---

### Step 3 — New Holding Tank Installation

3.1 Excavate the installation trench/pit at the location shown on the IFC plumbing drawings (DEL-006-05). Comply with Alberta OHS Code, Part 32 — Excavating (ASSUMPTION: applicable excavation safety standard [A-004]). Ensure excavation protection is in place per the standard before personnel enter. Note: conceptual location is the north side of the New North Shop building per Appendix B (Plumbing), R-06.

3.2 Prepare bedding layer per IFC drawing requirements and geotechnical recommendations. (Bedding specification TBD — to be confirmed in DEL-006-05)

3.3 Deliver the new 2,000 US gal septic holding tank to site. Confirm tank matches approved material submittal (capacity, material, configuration).

3.4 Lower tank into excavation using appropriate lifting equipment. Ensure tank is level and correctly oriented per IFC drawings (access manway location, pipe stub-out orientation).

3.5 Set tank on prepared bedding. Confirm invert elevations for all inlet/outlet connections match IFC drawings.

3.6 Connect inlet piping from the building's sanitary drainage system per IFC drawings (DEL-006-03, DEL-006-05). Ensure proper slope for gravity flow. (Connection details TBD per IFC design)

3.7 Install access manway, riser(s), and cover per IFC drawings to final grade. Access must be located to allow vacuum truck pump-out without entering the building or obstructed area.

3.8 **HOLD POINT [F-003]:** Before backfilling, notify the County project representative for inspection of the tank placement, orientation, invert elevations, and connections in the open excavation. Do not proceed with backfill until the County representative has inspected and authorized continuation. Photograph the open excavation with tank in place as concealed-work documentation [E-002].

3.9 Backfill and compact around tank per IFC drawings and geotechnical requirements (see Specification REQ-014-02-11). Avoid over-compaction against tank walls; follow manufacturer requirements. Restore surface.

3.10 Install any required high-level alarm or monitoring system if required by Alberta Safety Codes or as specified in IFC drawings. Coordinate electrical connections with PKG-015 (Electrical & Low-Voltage Installation). (Source: ASSUMPTION — specific requirement TBD pending code confirmation)

---

### Step 4 — Testing and Commissioning

4.1 **WITNESS POINT [F-003]:** Notify the County project representative prior to conducting the leak/pressure test. Conduct a hydrostatic (leak) test or equivalent approved test on the installed tank and all new connections per applicable Alberta plumbing code requirements. Test parameters (method, pressure, hold duration, pass/fail criteria) shall be as specified by the Plumbing Engineer of Record per applicable code [X-004]. Document test results including test parameters, date, results, and witness information. Photograph test setup as concealed-work documentation [E-002].
(Source: Specification REQ-014-02-04; ASSUMPTION — test method TBD per code and engineer direction)

4.2 Verify all pipe connections have proper slope and are free of obstructions. Inspect accessible cleanouts.

4.3 Confirm tank access manway is accessible and serviceable at final grade.

4.4 Confirm alarm or monitoring system (if installed) is functional.

4.5 Submit inspection reports and test records to the County project representative. County representative must be present for final inspection. (Source: RFP §3.3.2; Specification REQ-014-02-07)

4.6 Obtain Safety Code permit sign-off from the AHJ.

---

### Step 5 — Downstream Coordination Confirmation

5.1 Confirm with DEL-014-04 (Floor Drains & Sump Drains) contractor that drain connections to the septic system are complete and tested.

5.2 Confirm with DEL-014-06 (Plumbing Fixtures) contractor that fixture connections to the septic system are complete and functional.

5.3 Conduct a whole-system functional check: run water from fixtures and floor drains, confirm drainage flows to holding tank, and confirm no leaks.

---

### Step 6 — Closeout Documentation

6.1 Compile and submit the following to the Owner/County project representative:
- Material submittal (tank data sheet confirming 2,000 US gal capacity)
- Inspection request logs
- All completed inspection reports (copies)
- Hydrostatic/leak test records (including test parameters, results, and pass/fail determination [X-004])
- Compaction test records or geotechnical engineer field inspection sign-off [F-002]
- Photographic documentation at key concealed-work milestones (existing tank before removal, open excavation before backfill, new tank placement before backfill, completed connections) [E-002]
- Existing tank disposal documentation (vacuum pump-out receipt, tank disposal records — demonstrating compliance with EPEA and Waste Control Regulation [C-001])
- As-built mark-ups or record drawings — TBD: confirm requirement under CCDC 14 contract provisions; ASSUMPTION: likely required for buried infrastructure [F-004]
- Warranty documentation (2-year guarantee covering materials and workmanship from CCC date; warranty start date documented [X-005])

6.2 Confirm all items are incorporated into the project closeout package (PKG-020 Commissioning & Closeout).
