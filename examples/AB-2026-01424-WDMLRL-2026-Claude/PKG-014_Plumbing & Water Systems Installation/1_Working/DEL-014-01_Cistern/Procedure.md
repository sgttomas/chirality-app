> **Interpretation Note:** This procedure describes the steps required to **produce** (procure, install, and commission) the DEL-014-01 cistern and distribution deliverable. It is not an operation and maintenance manual for end-users. Operation and maintenance instructions are an anticipated closeout artifact (see Specification Documentation) to be prepared by the Plumbing Contractor and/or equipment manufacturers.

---

## Purpose

This procedure governs the execution sequence for supply, installation, testing, and commissioning of the 50,000L cistern and internal service water distribution system. Following this procedure ensures the cistern deliverable satisfies SOW-0041 and SOW-0042, meets OBJ-001 and OBJ-004 objectives, and is ready for final inspection and CCC issuance by the project completion deadline of December 31, 2026.

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Design Coordination and Submittal

**Step 1.1 — Confirm cistern and pump specifications**
- Obtain IFC plumbing drawings from PKG-006 (DEL-006-04).
- Confirm cistern volume (minimum 50,000L), pump capacity (minimum 100 LPM), and filling connection diameter (2.5 inches) match RFP §3.4 requirements.
- Submit equipment shop drawings and data sheets for cistern and pump to the County project representative for review.
- Source: RFP §3.4; SOW-0041; SOW-0042

**Step 1.2 — Confirm interface dimensions with bulk water fill system**
- Coordinate with DEL-014-03 (Bulk Water Fill System) contractor to confirm the 2.5-inch fill connection coupling type and external inlet location are compatible.
- Source: RFP §3.4; Guidance Considerations (Coordination with Bulk Water Fill System)

**Step 1.3 — Confirm pump electrical requirements**
- Provide pump motor electrical specifications (voltage, amperage, starter type) to the electrical contractor (PKG-015/DEL-015-01).
- Confirm a dedicated electrical circuit is included in the electrical design.
- Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (DEL-015-01); ASSUMPTION

**Step 1.4 — Obtain plumbing permit**
- Submit plumbing permit application to the applicable Alberta Safety Codes authority.
- Confirm County project representative is notified and will be present for all inspections.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; DEL-009-03

---

### Phase 2 — Procurement

**Step 2.1 — Procure cistern vessel**
- Procure cistern with rated volume of minimum 50,000L, complete with all necessary fittings, access manholes, and inlet/outlet connections per IFC drawings.
- Confirm delivery schedule allows placement in building before building envelope closes (if applicable).
- Source: RFP §3.4; SOW-0041; ASSUMPTION re: sequencing

**Step 2.2 — Procure distribution pump and accessories**
- Procure pump rated at minimum 100 LPM with 2.5-inch cistern filling connection.
- Procure all distribution piping, valves, pressure-reducing equipment, and appurtenances per IFC drawings.
- Source: RFP §3.4; SOW-0042

---

### Phase 3 — Installation

**Step 3.1 — Coordinate cistern placement with general contractor**
- Confirm building construction stage is suitable for cistern installation (structural slab complete, walls at appropriate stage per IFC drawings).
- Place cistern vessel in the utility room / designated location per IFC plumbing drawings.
- Source: R-06 (Plumbing Drawing); IFC drawings (PKG-006)

**Step 3.2 — Install cistern and piping rough-in**
- Connect cistern inlet (bulk water fill supply from DEL-014-03) per IFC drawings.
- Install all distribution piping from cistern to service points (water taps, pressure washer reel, and all other points per IFC drawings).
- Install all pipe supports, hangers, valves, and drain provisions per IFC drawings and applicable code.
- Source: SOW-0041; RFP §3.4; IFC drawings

**Step 3.3 — Install pump**
- Install distribution pump per manufacturer's instructions and IFC drawings.
- Connect pump to cistern outlet piping per IFC drawings.
- Connect pump to electrical supply circuit (provided by PKG-015).
- Source: SOW-0042; RFP §3.4

**Step 3.4 — Install freeze protection measures**
- Install insulation and/or heat trace on all piping and components susceptible to freezing, per IFC plumbing design.
- Source: ASSUMPTION — required for Alberta climate; specific method TBD in IFC design

**Step 3.5 — Install 2.5-inch cistern filling connection**
- Install and label the 2.5-inch cistern filling connection at the external fill inlet location per IFC drawings and in coordination with DEL-014-03.
- Source: RFP §3.4; SOW-0041; SOW-0042

**Step 3.6 — Install overflow provisions and backflow prevention (Lensing Items F-002, F-001, F-003)**
- Install cistern overflow mechanism (overflow pipe, high-level alarm, or both) per IFC plumbing design.
- Route overflow discharge to appropriate drain (floor drain system, DEL-014-04) per IFC drawings.
- Install backflow prevention device(s) per IFC plumbing design and applicable Alberta plumbing code provisions.
- Verify overflow and backflow prevention provisions are installed per IFC drawings.
- Source: ASSUMPTION — standard practice for bulk water storage installations and Alberta plumbing code; specific provisions TBD in IFC design. See Specification REQ-014-01-011, REQ-014-01-012.

---

### Phase 4 — Inspection and Testing

**Step 4.1 — Alberta Safety Code plumbing inspection**
- Notify Safety Code officer and County project representative of readiness for inspection.
- Arrange inspection; ensure County representative is present.
- Provide inspection report to County.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; DEL-009-03

**Step 4.2 — Pressure test distribution piping**
- Conduct pressure test on all distribution piping per applicable Alberta Safety Code / plumbing code requirements.
- TBD: specific test pressure value, hold duration, and allowable pressure drop to be confirmed per applicable code (Lensing Item A-005).
- Document test results.
- Source: ASSUMPTION: required by Alberta plumbing code (location TBD); standard practice

**Step 4.3 — Functional test of pump**
- Verify pump starts and operates correctly.
- Verify pump achieves minimum 100 LPM flow at the cistern outlet.
- Document test results.
- Source: REQ-014-01-002; ASSUMPTION: commissioning test required per OBJ-004

### Phase 4A — Flushing and Disinfection (Lensing Item F-004)

**Step 4A.1 — Flush distribution piping**
- After successful pressure test and before commissioning, flush all distribution piping to remove construction debris, solder flux residue, and other contaminants.
- Flushing shall be performed per standard plumbing practice for new water distribution systems.
- If the system is classified as potable (pending resolution of water quality classification, see Guidance Conflict Table CONF-014-01-01), disinfection shall also be performed per applicable Alberta plumbing code provisions.
- Document flushing (and disinfection, if applicable) results.
- Source: ASSUMPTION — flushing is standard practice for new water distribution systems prior to placing in service; specific Alberta plumbing code provisions TBD (**location TBD**). **Proposed authority: P.Eng. / Alberta Plumbing Code.**

---

### Phase 5 — Commissioning

**Step 5.1 — Fill cistern and verify distribution**
- Fill cistern to operating level using bulk water fill system (DEL-014-03).
- Confirm fill via 2.5-inch connection operates correctly without leaks.
- Activate pump and confirm water distribution to all service points (water taps, pressure washer reel, and other points per IFC drawings).
- **Lensing Item F-003:** During commissioning, test overflow provision function: confirm overflow mechanism activates at the intended level and discharges safely to the designated drain.
- Source: OBJ-004; DEL-020-01 (Building Systems Commissioning); ASSUMPTION

**Step 5.2 — Commissioning sign-off**
- Complete commissioning checklist in coordination with DEL-020-01 (Building Systems Commissioning agent).
- Provide commissioning documentation to County project representative.
- Source: OBJ-004; DEL-020-01

---

### Phase 6 — Closeout

**Step 6.1 — Prepare as-built documentation**
- Mark up plumbing drawings to reflect actual installed conditions.
- Provide as-built plumbing drawings to County.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; ASSUMPTION: standard closeout deliverable

**Step 6.2 — Provide O&M documentation**
- Compile and provide operation and maintenance (O&M) data for cistern and pump (manufacturer manuals, warranty cards, maintenance schedules).
- Source: ASSUMPTION: standard closeout deliverable; OBJ-001 operational readiness

**Step 6.3 — Conduct pre-CCC deficiency inspection (Lensing Item X-003)**
- Conduct a proactive pre-CCC deficiency inspection of the cistern and distribution system.
- Document all deficiencies and confirm resolution before CCC application.
- Source: ASSUMPTION: standard practice for CCC handover. See Specification Verification (REQ-014-01-009).

**Step 6.4 — Confirm completion for CCC**
- Confirm all punch list items are resolved.
- Confirm cistern and distribution system is accepted as complete for inclusion in Construction Completion Certificate (CCC) application.
- Source: RFP §2.14; DEL-020-02 (Final Inspection & CCC)
