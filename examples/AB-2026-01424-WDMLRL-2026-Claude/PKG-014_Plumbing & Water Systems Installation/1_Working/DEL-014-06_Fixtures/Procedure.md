# Procedure — DEL-014-06: Plumbing Fixtures

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce (design-coordinate), procure, and install the plumbing fixtures for DEL-014-06 — specifically the water taps, pressure washer reel, and industrial wash station within the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop.

The procedure covers activities from initial design coordination through to commissioning sign-off. It is oriented toward the Plumbing Contractor and Project Manager as the executing parties.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Participate in Preliminary Plumbing Design Review

**Who:** Plumbing Contractor, Plumbing Engineer (PKG-006), Project Manager

**What:**
1. Review the conceptual plumbing drawing (R-06) to understand the intended locations and types of fixtures.
2. Participate in the preliminary plumbing design review (DEL-006-01) to identify any installation constraints (mounting surfaces, rough-in clearances, supply/drain routing limitations).
3. Flag the open question regarding hot water supply for the industrial wash station (see CONF-014-06-01 in Guidance.md). Confirm resolution path with the Plumbing Engineer.
4. Confirm scope boundary for the pressure washer unit vs. the pressure washer reel (see CONF-014-06-02 in Guidance.md).
5. Confirm the number and type of water taps required at each location.

**Output:** Preliminary design comments submitted; open items logged on the project issue register.

---

### Step 2 — Review and Accept IFC Plumbing Drawings

**Who:** Plumbing Contractor, Project Manager

**What:**
1. Receive IFC plumbing drawings from the Plumbing Engineer (DEL-006-02, DEL-006-03) and Fixture Schedule (DEL-006-06).
2. Verify drawings are signed and stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta (R-01 §3.3.2).
3. Confirm all fixtures from DEL-014-06 scope (water taps, pressure washer reel, industrial wash station) are addressed in the Fixture Schedule.
4. Review rough-in dimensions, connection sizes, and mounting heights for all fixtures.
5. Confirm coordination with Electrical Contractor for pressure washer reel location (70A stub-out alignment).
6. Confirm no conflicts with floor drain rough-in (DEL-014-04).
7. Forward any drawing discrepancies to the Plumbing Engineer for resolution before procurement.

**Output:** IFC drawings accepted or comments issued; coordination issues resolved.

---

### Step 3 — Procure Fixtures

**Who:** Plumbing Contractor

**What:**
1. **Submittal review (X-004):** Before procurement, prepare and submit fixture submittals / shop drawings to the Plumbing Engineer and Project Manager for review and approval. Submittal package shall include manufacturer data sheets, rough-in dimensions, material certifications, and warranty information for each fixture type. Do not proceed to procurement until submittals are approved.
2. Procure all fixtures per the approved Plumbing Fixture Schedule (DEL-006-06) and approved submittals:
   - Water taps (type and quantity per IFC drawings)
   - Pressure washer reel assembly (retractable; water supply connection per IFC drawings)
   - Industrial wash station (heavy-duty, industrial grade; ASSUMPTION — stainless steel or approved equivalent)
3. Procure associated trim, valves, supply stops, drain assemblies, and mounting hardware per IFC drawings.
4. Procure hot water heating equipment if required for industrial wash station (pending CONF-014-06-01 resolution; TBD).
5. Confirm delivery schedule aligns with installation readiness (upstream prerequisites complete).

**Output:** Fixtures and associated materials on site or staged for delivery.

---

### Step 4 — Coordinate Site Readiness with Upstream Trades

**Who:** Project Manager, Plumbing Contractor

**What:**
1. Confirm with the General Contractor (PKG-011, PKG-012) that walls, blocking, and backing are installed at all fixture mounting locations.
2. Confirm with DEL-014-01 Plumbing Contractor that the water supply distribution rough-in is complete and capped at fixture stub-out locations.
3. Confirm with DEL-014-02 / DEL-014-04 Plumbing Contractor that sanitary drain rough-in is complete and available at the industrial wash station location.
4. Confirm with the Electrical Contractor (PKG-015) that the 70A electrical stub-out for the pressure washer reel is in place and identified (if pressure washer unit is Contractor-supplied; TBD per CONF-014-06-02).
5. Obtain confirmation of building permit and Safety Code permit validity for the plumbing installation.

6. Complete and obtain sign-off on the **Coordination Checklist** (F-003): a formal document recording confirmation from each upstream trade (DEL-014-01, DEL-014-02, DEL-014-04, PKG-015) that their prerequisites are complete and the site is ready for fixture installation. Each trade representative should sign or initial the checklist. Retain this checklist as a project record (see Records table).

**Output:** Site readiness confirmation documented via signed Coordination Checklist; any outstanding items escalated to the Project Manager.

---

### Step 5 — Install Fixtures

**Who:** Licensed Plumbing Contractor

**What:**

#### 5a — Water Taps
1. Install water tap(s) at locations per IFC plumbing drawings.
2. Connect to supply stub-outs from the cistern distribution system (DEL-014-01).
3. Install isolation valve upstream of each tap.
4. Secure taps to wall or surface mount per IFC rough-in dimensions.
5. Test for leaks at connection points.

#### 5b — Pressure Washer Reel
1. Install retractable pressure washer reel mounting bracket at wall or overhead location per IFC drawings.
2. Mount reel assembly per manufacturer instructions and IFC drawings.
3. Connect hose reel supply inlet to the cistern distribution supply stub-out.
4. Install isolation valve and pressure regulator (if specified) upstream of reel.
5. Extend supply hose and confirm free retraction.
6. Leave pressure washer unit electrical connection to Electrical Contractor (PKG-015) unless within Plumbing Contractor scope (TBD per CONF-014-06-02).

#### 5c — Industrial Wash Station
1. Set industrial wash station in position per IFC rough-in drawings.
2. Connect hot and cold supply lines to supply stop valves (ASSUMPTION — pending hot water source confirmation per CONF-014-06-01).
3. Connect drain outlet to the building sanitary drain/DWV system (DEL-014-02 connection).
4. Install P-trap, tail piece, and drain strainer per code requirements.
5. Secure sink to wall/counter mounting per manufacturer instructions and IFC drawings.
6. If a point-of-use water heater is required, install per IFC drawings and manufacturer instructions; coordinate electrical connection with PKG-015.

#### 5d — Backflow Prevention (C-003)
1. Determine whether backflow prevention devices (vacuum breakers, check valves, or other devices) are required at fixture supply connections per applicable plumbing code. ASSUMPTION: backflow prevention may be required given the cistern-fed (non-municipal) water source; specific requirements TBD pending AHJ / Plumbing Engineer confirmation.
2. If required, install backflow prevention devices at each fixture supply connection per IFC drawings and code requirements.
3. If not required per AHJ determination, document the exemption basis.

#### 5e — General
1. All connections shall be made with appropriate materials for the system (CPVC, copper, or approved equivalent per Alberta plumbing code; material type per IFC drawings).
2. Cap or protect all rough-in openings that are not yet connected.
3. Clean up all installation debris.

**Output:** All fixtures installed, connected, and visually confirmed against IFC drawings.

---

### Step 5.5 — As-Built Verification Gate (X-003)

**Who:** Plumbing Contractor, Project Manager

**What:**
1. Before proceeding to pressure testing, perform a discrete verification gate to confirm all installed fixture locations match IFC drawings.
2. Prepare an as-built markup showing actual installed locations, connection sizes, and any deviations from IFC drawings.
3. If deviations are found, document and resolve with the Plumbing Engineer before proceeding to testing.
4. Obtain Project Manager sign-off that installation is complete and matches design intent.

**Output:** As-built markup prepared; installation confirmed to match IFC drawings; authorization to proceed to testing.

---

### Step 6 — Pressure Test and Pre-Commissioning Check

**Who:** Plumbing Contractor

**What:**
1. Pressure test supply connections per Alberta Safety Code requirements (A-005). Test parameters:
   - Test pressure: TBD -- ASSUMPTION: minimum 1.5x operating pressure or as specified by AHJ. Specific test pressure value to be confirmed by Plumbing Engineer / AHJ.
   - Hold duration: TBD -- ASSUMPTION: observe all joints for minimum 15 minutes under test pressure, or as specified by applicable code clause (X-005). Specific duration to be confirmed by Plumbing Engineer / AHJ.
   - Acceptance criteria: no visible leaks, no pressure drop exceeding TBD threshold during hold period.
2. Open each tap and pressure washer reel supply valve; verify flow and no leaks at all joints.
3. Run water through the industrial wash station; confirm drain flow to sanitary system.
4. Inspect all drain connections for leaks under flow conditions. Observe for minimum duration per applicable code requirement (TBD).
5. Confirm that all supply isolation valves operate correctly.
6. If backflow prevention devices were installed (Step 5d), verify proper function.
7. Document any deficiencies; rectify before inspection.

**Output:** Pressure test and pre-commissioning checklist completed; deficiencies resolved.

---

### Step 7 — Safety Code Inspection

**Who:** Plumbing Contractor, Project Manager, AHJ Inspector, County Representative

**What:**
1. Submit inspection request per R-01 §3.3.2.
2. Notify the County project representative; County must be present at all inspections (R-01 §3.3.2).
3. Make installation accessible and documentation available for the inspector.
4. Accompany AHJ inspector and address any deficiencies identified.
5. Obtain and retain copy of the completed inspection report; provide copy to County (R-01 §3.3.2).

**Output:** Passed Safety Code inspection; inspection report obtained and distributed.

---

### Step 8 — Commissioning Support

**Who:** Plumbing Contractor, Commissioning Agent (PKG-020)

**What:**
1. Support the building systems commissioning agent (DEL-020-01) during commissioning of the plumbing system.
2. Demonstrate operational function of all fixtures:
   - Water flow from all taps
   - Pressure washer reel extension, retraction, and pressurized water delivery
   - Industrial wash station hot and cold flow (if applicable) and drain operation
3. Confirm no leaks under operational conditions.
4. Sign off on commissioning checklist items for DEL-014-06 fixtures.

**Output:** Commissioning sign-off for DEL-014-06 fixtures recorded in PKG-020 commissioning record.
