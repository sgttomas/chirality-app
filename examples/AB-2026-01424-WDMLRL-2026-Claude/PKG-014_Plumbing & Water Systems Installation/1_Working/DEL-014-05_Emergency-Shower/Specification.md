# Specification — DEL-014-05 Emergency Shower

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

This specification governs the supply and installation of one (1) emergency shower station in the New North Shop of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. The scope includes:

- Procurement of a code-compliant emergency shower unit (combination shower/eyewash ASSUMPTION; see Guidance CF-002 for ruling)
- All labour, materials, and equipment for complete installation
- Water supply rough-in from the cistern-fed internal service water distribution system (DEL-014-01)
- Drain connection to the floor drain below the unit (DEL-014-04)
- Functional testing and commissioning
- All associated Safety Code permits and inspections

Source: SOW-0048 (Decomposition SSOW §F); App B-Plumb (R-06); RFP §3.3.2

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- The cistern and water distribution system (DEL-014-01)
- The floor drain below the unit (DEL-014-04)
- Electrical work, if any alarm or indicator light is required (scope to be confirmed by plumbing designer — ASSUMPTION: electrical interface, if needed, is scope of PKG-015). See Datasheet Attributes row "Electrical interface" for tracking.
- OH&S program administration (DEL-019-01)

## Requirements

### Standards Hierarchy (Enriched per F-001)

Where requirements in this section cite numeric thresholds or performance criteria from ANSI/ISEA Z358.1, these values are stated as ASSUMPTION pending confirmation of applicable standards. **If Alberta OH&S Code Part 16 specifies a different or more stringent requirement than ANSI/ISEA Z358.1, the Alberta OH&S Code requirement shall govern.** This hierarchy applies to all requirements below, including REQ-ES-004, REQ-ES-005, REQ-ES-006, REQ-ES-007, and REQ-ES-009.

Source: RFP §3.3.2 (Alberta Safety Codes Act is the governing legislative framework); REQ-ES-001 (hierarchy statement).

### ASSUMPTION Resolution Path (Enriched per C-001)

Multiple requirements below are marked ASSUMPTION because the governing standards (ANSI/ISEA Z358.1 and Alberta OH&S Code Part 16) are not available in accessible sources. The following resolution path applies:

- **Responsible party:** Plumbing designer
- **Resolution milestone:** IFC design issuance
- **Action:** Confirm applicability of each standard and update numeric thresholds accordingly
- **Escalation:** If not confirmed by IFC issuance, escalate to the Owner and Alberta OH&S authority for determination

This resolution path applies to: REQ-ES-001, REQ-ES-004, REQ-ES-005, REQ-ES-006, REQ-ES-007, REQ-ES-009, REQ-ES-010.

### REQ-ES-001 — Unit Supply and Compliance
The emergency shower unit shall be supplied new, free of defects, and shall comply with all applicable Alberta Safety Codes and standards in effect at the time of installation.

Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0009), §3.4; OBJ-001.

ASSUMPTION: Unit shall meet ANSI/ISEA Z358.1 requirements for emergency shower and eyewash equipment (location TBD in accessible sources). If Alberta OH&S Code Part 16 specifies a different or additional standard, that requirement shall govern (see Standards Hierarchy above).

**Equipment Selection Criteria (Enriched per A-005):** The unit shall meet the following minimum quality expectations in addition to code compliance:
- Listed or certified to the applicable standard by a recognized testing laboratory (ASSUMPTION: per ANSI/ISEA Z358.1; location TBD in accessible sources)
- Constructed of corrosion-resistant materials suitable for an industrial shop environment
- Minimum expected service life: TBD (confirm with plumbing designer or Owner preference)
- Manufacturer shall be an established supplier of emergency safety equipment (ASSUMPTION: industry standard expectation)

### REQ-ES-002 — Location
The emergency shower shall be installed at the location indicated on the IFC plumbing drawings. Based on the conceptual plumbing drawing (App B-Plumb, R-06), this location is near the south wall of the main shop area, in proximity to the wash station and roll door.

Source: App B-Plumb (R-06).

Note: Exact coordinates are subject to IFC drawing finalization. If the IFC drawing relocates the unit, the IFC drawing governs.

### REQ-ES-003 — Water Supply Connection
The emergency shower shall be connected to the cistern-fed internal service water distribution system supplied under DEL-014-01.

Source: App B-Plumb (R-06); RFP §3.4 (water distribution system); _DEPENDENCIES.md (upstream dependency: DEL-014-01).

### REQ-ES-004 — Water Flow Rate
The unit shall deliver water at a flow rate sufficient to meet the applicable standard requirement.

ASSUMPTION: Minimum 75.7 LPM (20 USgpm) per ANSI/ISEA Z358.1 (location TBD in accessible sources). If Alberta OH&S Code Part 16 specifies a different minimum, that value shall govern (see Standards Hierarchy above). Exact minimum flow rate to be confirmed by the plumbing designer in the IFC design and shall be verified during commissioning testing.

**Acceptance criterion (Enriched per A-003):** Flow rate measured at the shower head during commissioning shall be equal to or greater than 75.7 LPM (20 USgpm) (ASSUMPTION per ANSI/ISEA Z358.1; location TBD in accessible sources), or the minimum specified by the confirmed applicable standard, whichever is more stringent. The numeric threshold shall be updated once the applicable standard is confirmed (see ASSUMPTION Resolution Path above).

### REQ-ES-005 — Tepid Water Delivery
ASSUMPTION: The unit shall deliver tepid water (per applicable standard, nominally 16-38 deg C / 60-100 deg F) for the required minimum flush duration.

Source: ANSI/ISEA Z358.1 (ASSUMPTION; location TBD in accessible sources); Alberta OH&S Code Part 16 (ASSUMPTION; location TBD in accessible sources). If Alberta OH&S Code Part 16 specifies a different tepid range, that range shall govern (see Standards Hierarchy above).

Note: Because this is a cistern-fed non-potable water system in an Alberta climate, the plumbing designer must confirm whether a tempering valve or thermostatic mixing valve is required. This is a design decision to be resolved in the IFC plumbing drawings. See Guidance.md Conflict Table entry CF-001.

**Verification threshold (Enriched per F-002):** The acceptance range for tepid water temperature is 16-38 deg C (ASSUMPTION per ANSI/ISEA Z358.1; location TBD in accessible sources). This range shall be updated to the confirmed authoritative numeric range once the applicable standard is verified. Temperature shall be measured at the shower head discharge point during the commissioning flow test.

### REQ-ES-006 — Flush Duration Capacity
ASSUMPTION: The water supply system shall be capable of delivering water to the emergency shower for a minimum of 15 continuous minutes without interruption.

Source: ANSI/ISEA Z358.1 (ASSUMPTION; location TBD in accessible sources). Capacity is dependent on cistern fill level and distribution pump capacity (DEL-014-01). If Alberta OH&S Code Part 16 specifies a different minimum duration, that value shall govern (see Standards Hierarchy above).

### REQ-ES-007 — Activation
The emergency shower shall be activated by a single, clearly operable mechanism (ASSUMPTION: stay-open foot pedal or pull rod). The activation mechanism shall not require the use of hands to maintain flow.

ASSUMPTION: Per ANSI/ISEA Z358.1 (location TBD in accessible sources). If Alberta OH&S Code Part 16 specifies additional activation requirements, those shall also apply (see Standards Hierarchy above).

### REQ-ES-008 — Drain Connection
A floor drain shall be provided below the emergency shower unit. The drain connection is covered under DEL-014-04.

Source: App B-Plumb (R-06) — drain symbol shown beneath emergency shower on conceptual drawing.

### REQ-ES-009 — Clearance Zone
ASSUMPTION: A minimum clear access zone of 1.0 m radius shall be maintained around the emergency shower unit at all times after installation, consistent with OH&S Code requirements (location TBD in accessible sources). If Alberta OH&S Code Part 16 specifies a different clearance dimension, that value shall govern (see Standards Hierarchy above).

The Plumbing Contractor shall coordinate with the General Contractor to ensure no obstructions are placed within this zone.

### REQ-ES-010 — Signage
ASSUMPTION: The emergency shower location shall be identified with high-visibility signage visible from all directions of approach, per Alberta OH&S Code requirements (location TBD in accessible sources).

Signage type and content to be confirmed in the IFC design package.

### REQ-ES-011 — Permits and Inspections
All plumbing work associated with this deliverable shall be performed under the applicable Alberta Safety Code permits. The Plumbing Contractor shall submit all inspection requests; the County project representative must be present at all inspections and shall receive copies of all completed inspection reports.

Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0007, SOW-0084, SOW-0085).

### REQ-ES-012 — Workmanship and Materials Warranty
All materials furnished and work performed shall be guaranteed for the construction period plus a two (2) year warranty period following the Construction Completion Certificate (CCC).

Source: RFP §2.10 (SOW-0120, SOW-0121).

### REQ-ES-013 — National Plumbing Code Compliance (Enriched per C-002)
All plumbing work associated with this deliverable shall comply with the National Plumbing Code of Canada (current edition adopted in Alberta), in addition to the Alberta Safety Codes Act and associated codes.

Source: ASSUMPTION — NPC is listed in the Standards table as "likely applicable" (location TBD in accessible sources). The plumbing designer shall confirm specific NPC clauses applicable to emergency shower installations and update this requirement with clause-level references at IFC stage.

Note: This requirement is added to close the traceability gap between the Standards table (which lists NPC) and the Requirements section (which previously did not reference NPC).
