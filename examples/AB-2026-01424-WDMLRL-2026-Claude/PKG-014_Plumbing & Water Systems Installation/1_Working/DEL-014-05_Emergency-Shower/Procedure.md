# Procedure — DEL-014-05 Emergency Shower

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and deliver the Emergency Shower installation (DEL-014-05) — from pre-installation planning through commissioning, testing, and records submission. It is intended for use by the Plumbing Contractor and the project management team.

The procedure also includes a brief operational activation procedure at Step 10, to support commissioning testing and Owner orientation.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Review IFC Drawings and Equipment Submittal

1.1 Obtain and review the IFC plumbing drawings for the emergency shower. Confirm:
- Unit location matches App B-Plumb conceptual drawing intent (south shop area near wash station) or reflects any approved design change.
- Supply pipe size, routing, and connection point to DEL-014-01 distribution system.
- Drain connection to floor drain (DEL-014-04).
- Tepid water delivery method (thermostatic mixing valve, or documented confirmation that supply temperature is adequate without one — see Guidance CF-001).
- Unit type: combination shower/eyewash or shower-only (see Guidance CF-002 and Guidance Terminology Note).

Source: App B-Plumb (R-06); REQ-ES-002; REQ-ES-003.

1.2 Review the equipment submittal/data sheet for the emergency shower unit. Confirm the unit is listed or approved to the applicable standard (ASSUMPTION: ANSI/ISEA Z358.1 or equivalent Alberta-accepted standard; location TBD in accessible sources). Verify the submittal contains the required data points per Specification Verification table (REQ-ES-001 row).

1.3 Record any discrepancies between drawings, submittal, and site conditions. Raise with plumbing designer before proceeding.

---

### Step 2 — Confirm Permit and Inspection Schedule

2.1 Confirm that the Alberta Safety Code plumbing permit covering this work has been obtained.

2.2 Coordinate with the County project representative and Safety Code Authority (ASSUMPTION: Alberta Municipal Affairs or applicable authority having jurisdiction) to schedule rough-in inspection and final inspection.

Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0007, SOW-0084, SOW-0085).

---

### Step 3 — Mark Out Location and Clearance Zone

3.1 Mark the unit installation location on the floor per IFC drawings.

3.2 Mark the minimum clearance zone (ASSUMPTION: 1.0 m radius; confirm per applicable standard — location TBD in accessible sources) around the installation point.

3.3 Coordinate with the General Contractor to ensure no obstructions, permanent fixtures, or stored materials will encroach on the clearance zone.

Source: REQ-ES-009; Guidance C-5.

---

### Step 4 — Install Supply Branch Piping

4.1 Install the supply branch from the distribution main (DEL-014-01) to the emergency shower rough-in location.

4.2 Install the thermostatic mixing valve (if required per IFC design — see Guidance CF-001) in the supply branch.

4.3 Install all pipe supports, hangers, and fittings per IFC drawings and applicable codes.

4.4 Ensure the supply branch is located in a heated zone or freeze-protected zone (see Guidance C-3).

4.5 Cap the supply branch at the rough-in point. Do not connect the unit until rough-in inspection is complete.

Source: REQ-ES-003; REQ-ES-005; Guidance C-3.

---

### Step 5 — Rough-In Inspection

5.1 Request rough-in inspection from the Safety Code authority.

5.2 Ensure the County project representative is present at the inspection.

5.3 Obtain and retain a copy of the completed inspection report.

5.4 Address any deficiencies identified. Re-inspection required before proceeding if any deficiencies are flagged.

**HOLD POINT (Enriched per D-001):** Do not proceed to Step 6 (unit installation) until the rough-in inspection has passed with no outstanding deficiencies. This is a code-critical gate — the transition from rough-in to unit installation requires formal inspection clearance. The hold point is released when: (a) the rough-in inspection report shows no outstanding deficiencies, and (b) the inspection report is signed off by the Safety Code authority. If deficiencies are identified, the re-inspection must also clear before this hold point is released.

Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0084, SOW-0085); REQ-ES-011.

---

### Step 6 — Install Emergency Shower Unit

6.1 Install the emergency shower unit per manufacturer instructions and IFC drawings.

6.2 Connect the supply branch to the unit inlet.

6.3 Confirm the floor drain (DEL-014-04) is in place below the unit and unobstructed.

6.4 If the unit is a combination shower/eyewash (see Guidance CF-002 and Terminology Note), connect both shower head and eyewash/face wash connections per manufacturer instructions.

6.5 Install all required signage (ASSUMPTION: high-visibility sign identifying the emergency shower location, per Alberta OH&S Code requirements; location TBD in accessible sources). Confirm signage type and content with project team and/or plumbing designer.

Source: REQ-ES-007; REQ-ES-008; REQ-ES-010; Guidance C-1.

---

### Step 7 — Pressure Test and Leak Check

7.1 With the supply system charged (DEL-014-01 distribution system pressurized), inspect all connections at the emergency shower unit for leaks.

7.2 Confirm that the thermostatic mixing valve (if installed) is functioning and delivering within the required temperature range (ASSUMPTION: 16-38 deg C; confirm per applicable standard — location TBD in accessible sources).

Source: REQ-ES-003; REQ-ES-005.

---

### Step 8 — Final Safety Code Inspection

8.1 Request final plumbing inspection from the Safety Code authority.

8.2 Ensure the County project representative is present.

8.3 Obtain and retain a copy of the completed inspection report.

8.4 Address any final deficiencies. Re-inspection required before commissioning sign-off.

Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0084, SOW-0085); REQ-ES-011.

---

### Step 8A — Pre-Commissioning Readiness Checkpoint (Enriched per D-002)

Before proceeding to the commissioning flow test (Step 9), confirm all of the following readiness conditions are met:

| Readiness Condition | Verification | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Final Safety Code inspection passed (Step 8) with no outstanding deficiencies | Inspection report on file, signed off | Confirm |
| DEL-014-01 (Cistern & Distribution) fully operational — cistern filled, distribution pump running, system pressurized | Written confirmation from DEL-014-01 responsible party (see Guidance C-7) | Confirm |
| DEL-014-04 (Floor drain below unit) clear and functional | Visual inspection | Confirm |
| Thermostatic mixing valve operational (if installed per CF-001) | Temperature check per Step 7.2 | Confirm |
| All deficiencies from Steps 5 and 8 resolved | Deficiency log reviewed | Confirm |

**This is a formal go/no-go gate.** Do not proceed to Step 9 until all readiness conditions are confirmed. The Plumbing Contractor and project management team shall jointly sign off on readiness. Record the sign-off date and any conditions or exceptions.

Source: Guidance P-1; REQ-ES-003; REQ-ES-011.

---

### Step 9 — Commissioning — Functional Flow Test

9.1 Confirm that DEL-014-01 (cistern and distribution pump) is operational and supplying water to the distribution system.

9.2 Confirm that the floor drain below the unit (DEL-014-04) is clear and functional.

9.3 Activate the emergency shower using the normal activation mechanism (ASSUMPTION: single-action stay-open valve — foot pedal or pull rod).

9.4 Verify:
- The activation mechanism operates with a single action and does not require the use of hands to maintain flow (REQ-ES-007).
- Water flows from the shower head (and eyewash head, if combination unit — see Guidance CF-002) (REQ-ES-004).
- Flow rate is measured and confirmed at or above the minimum required rate (ASSUMPTION: 75.7 LPM / 20 USgpm per ANSI/ISEA Z358.1 — location TBD; confirm with IFC specification; if Alberta OH&S Code specifies a different minimum, that value governs per Standards Hierarchy) (REQ-ES-004).
- Water temperature is measured and confirmed in the tepid range (ASSUMPTION: 16-38 deg C; update per confirmed standard) at the shower head discharge point (REQ-ES-005).
- Flow continues uninterrupted for a minimum of 15 minutes (REQ-ES-006).
- Drain handles flow without flooding (Guidance C-4).

9.5 Deactivate the unit (confirm the shut-off is positive and water flow stops).

9.6 Record all test results: flow rate, temperature, duration, activation test pass/fail, drain performance. Retain records.

Source: REQ-ES-004 through REQ-ES-008; Specification Verification table.

---

### Step 10 — Owner Orientation

10.1 Demonstrate the emergency shower activation procedure to the County project representative or designated facility staff:
- Identify the unit location and signage.
- Demonstrate activation.
- Describe the clearance zone and access requirements.
- Explain the recommended periodic testing protocol (ASSUMPTION: weekly activation test and annual performance test per Alberta OH&S Code; location TBD in accessible sources).
- Inform the Owner of lifecycle cost considerations (see Guidance T-4) and recommended maintenance budget items.

10.2 Provide the Owner with:
- Manufacturer's operation and maintenance instructions.
- Recommended testing and maintenance schedule.
- Copies of all inspection reports and commissioning test records.
- Thermostatic mixing valve documentation (if installed per CF-001 — see Specification Documentation item 7).

Source: Guidance C-6; Guidance T-4; REQ-ES-012; OBJ-007.

---

### Step 11 — Close-Out Review (Enriched per E-002)

11.1 Conduct a formal close-out review after Owner orientation (Step 10) is complete:
- Confirm all records listed in the Records section below have been produced and submitted to the County project manager.
- Review the deficiency resolution log — confirm all deficiencies from Steps 5, 8, and 8A have been resolved and documented.
- Capture any lessons learned during the installation and commissioning process (e.g., coordination issues with DEL-014-01, unexpected site conditions, equipment substitutions) for project close-out documentation.
- Confirm warranty documentation is in place per REQ-ES-012 and Specification Verification table.

11.2 Record the close-out date and sign-off by the Plumbing Contractor representative and the project management team.

Source: ASSUMPTION — industry best practice for life-safety equipment close-out; REQ-ES-011; REQ-ES-012.

## Records

The following records shall be produced and retained by the Plumbing Contractor, with copies submitted to the County project manager:

1. Alberta Safety Code plumbing permit (copy)
2. Rough-in inspection report (copy)
3. Final plumbing inspection report (copy)
4. Equipment submittal / data sheet for the emergency shower unit
5. Commissioning test record: flow rate, temperature, duration, activation check, drain performance
6. Manufacturer's operation and maintenance instructions (submitted to Owner)
7. As-built / record notation on plumbing drawings confirming installed location, supply pipe size and routing, pipe materials, valve locations (including thermostatic mixing valve if installed), clearance zone dimensions, and drain connection
8. Thermostatic mixing valve documentation (if installed per CF-001): manufacturer data sheet, settings/setpoint documentation, and maintenance instructions
9. Warranty documentation: signed warranty certificate or contractor warranty letter per Specification Verification table (REQ-ES-012 row)
10. Deficiency resolution log (all deficiencies from inspections, with resolution dates)
11. Close-out review sign-off record (Step 11)

Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0084, SOW-0085, SOW-0093); Specification Documentation section; REQ-ES-011; REQ-ES-012.
