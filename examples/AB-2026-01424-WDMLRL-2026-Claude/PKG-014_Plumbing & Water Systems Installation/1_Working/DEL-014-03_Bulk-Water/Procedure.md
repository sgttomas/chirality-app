## Purpose

This procedure governs the installation of the Bulk Water Fill System (DEL-014-03) at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. It describes the sequence of activities required to produce and verify the installed system, from pre-installation prerequisites through commissioning and records submission. This procedure does not replace the IFC plumbing drawings produced under PKG-006; installation must conform to those drawings.

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Preparation and Layout

**Step 1.1 — Site Survey and Layout**
Confirm the exterior fill point location against the IFC drawings (referencing Appendix B conceptual drawing as context). Mark pump mounting location (inside utility room or as designed), fill line routing, and exterior wall penetration point. Flag any conflicts with structural, mechanical, or electrical elements.

**Step 1.2 — Wall Penetration Coordination**
Coordinate the exterior wall sleeve location and size with the General Contractor. Wall penetration through the concrete structure (35' ceiling building) must be pre-sleeved during construction if not already provided. Confirm sleeve size and elevation.

**Step 1.3 — Material Staging**
Receive, inspect, and stage all materials: pump, piping, fittings, valves, wall sleeve, fill connection hardware, freeze protection components (heat trace cable, insulation, enclosure as specified), supports and hangers.

---

### Phase 2 — Rough-In

**Step 2.1 — Wall Sleeve Installation**
Install or verify the wall sleeve for the fill line penetration. Seal sleeve to prevent air infiltration, moisture ingress, and pest entry, per IFC specifications.

**Step 2.2 — Pump Rough-In**
Set pump on mounting pad/frame in the designated location (utility room or as shown on IFC drawings). Confirm pump orientation relative to fill line routing and cistern connection. Do not final-connect until piping is complete.

**Step 2.3 — Fill Line Installation**
Install fill line piping from pump outlet to exterior fill connection point, following IFC drawings. Include all specified isolation valves, check valves, backflow prevention device, and drain provisions. Support piping at intervals per code and drawing specifications.

**Step 2.4 — Cistern Inlet Connection**
Install the fill line connection to the cistern (interface with DEL-014-01). Coordinate connection point, fitting type, and isolation valve with DEL-014-01 scope. **Interface ownership: document in writing which deliverable (DEL-014-03 or DEL-014-01) owns each component at the cistern interface, including the cistern-side connection fitting and isolation valve. This documentation shall be signed by both the Plumbing Contractor foreman (or designee responsible for each deliverable) and filed with the PM. TBD -- Plumbing Contractor + PM to resolve and document scope split before rough-in begins.** *(Semantic Lensing A-004)*

**Step 2.5 — Exterior Fill Point Installation**
Install exterior fill connection hardware (coupling, cap, bollard protection, enclosure or cover as specified). Confirm fill point is accessible to water delivery vehicles at grade level.

**Step 2.6 — Freeze Protection Installation**
Install specified freeze protection measures: heat trace cable (if specified), insulation, heated enclosure, or drain-back provisions. Follow manufacturer installation instructions for heat trace.

**Step 2.7 — Electrical Stub-Out**
Provide electrical conduit stub to pump motor location per coordination with Electrical Contractor. Plumbing Contractor is responsible for confirming stub-out location; electrical connection is under DEL-015-04.

**Step 3.2 — Deficiency Correction**
Correct any deficiencies identified at rough-in inspection before proceeding. Document corrections.

---

### Phase 4 — Completion

**Step 4.1 — Final Connections**
Make final connections: pump to piping, electrical (by Electrical Contractor under DEL-015-04), cistern fill line. Confirm all joints are complete, sealed, and supported.

**Step 4.2 — Pressure Test** **[MANDATORY HOLD POINT]**
Pressure test the fill line per Alberta Plumbing Code requirements. Record test pressure, duration, and result. Repair any leaks; re-test until passing. **TBD — specific test pressure (kPa/psi), hold duration, and pass/fail criteria to be confirmed from applicable Alberta Plumbing Code clause or plumbing engineer specification.** *(Semantic Lensing F-001)*

**This is a mandatory hold point: do not proceed to final inspection (Step 4.3) until the pressure test has passed.** *(Semantic Lensing E-002)*

**Step 4.3 — Final Plumbing Inspection** **[MANDATORY HOLD POINT]**
Request final Safety Codes plumbing inspection. County project representative must be notified and present at inspection per RFP S3.3.2. Notification shall follow the protocol established in Prerequisites. File inspection report.

**This is a mandatory hold point: do not proceed to Phase 5 (Commissioning) until the final inspection has passed.** *(Semantic Lensing E-002, X-002)*

**Step 4.4 — System Flush**
Flush the fill line to remove debris before operational testing. Dispose of flush water appropriately.

---

### Phase 5 — Commissioning

**Step 5.1 — Operational Test — Pump Start**
With power supplied by Electrical Contractor, start pump and confirm correct rotation direction. Observe for unusual noise, vibration, or leakage. Follow pump manufacturer's commissioning checklist.

**Step 5.2 — Flow and Performance Test**
Operate the pump through the external fill mode. Measure achieved flow rate and compare against design specification. Record results. Confirm the system can deliver water from the exterior fill point through to the cistern.

**Step 5.3 — Backflow Prevention Verification**
Test backflow prevention device per manufacturer instructions and applicable code. Record test result.

**Step 5.4 — Freeze Protection Function Check**
Verify heat trace power (if applicable), thermostat setpoint, and continuity of insulation coverage. Confirm drain-back valves (if specified) operate correctly.

**Step 5.5 — Integration Test with DEL-014-01**
Confirm that the bulk fill system can deliver water into the cistern as designed. Coordinate this test with DEL-014-01 commissioning. Confirm cistern level responds to pump operation. **Overfill prevention: before running this test, confirm cistern level monitoring/overflow protection status (per Prerequisites). If no overflow protection is in place, assign a dedicated observer to monitor cistern level and stop the pump before overfill. Record cistern level before and after test.** *(Semantic Lensing C-003)*

**Step 5.6 — Building Systems Commissioning (DEL-020-01)**
Confirm readiness for and participate in the overall building systems commissioning (DEL-020-01). Provide commissioning agent with test records and pump submittals. This step supports OBJ-004 traceability (see Specification REQ-014-03-12).

---

### Phase 6 — Closeout

**Step 6.1 — As-Built Drawings**
Mark up IFC drawings to reflect as-installed condition. All deviations from IFC shall be red-lined. As-built mark-ups shall be signed and dated by the site foreman, with RFI/change order references for field changes. Submit to Engineer of Record or PM for filing.

**Step 6.2 — O&M Manual**
Compile and submit Operations and Maintenance manual covering: pump operation, maintenance schedule, seasonal winterization procedure (including step-by-step drain-down and spring re-commissioning), troubleshooting guide, and manufacturer contact information. **Note:** The winterization protocol is a lifecycle-critical operational concern for this system -- see Guidance C-8. Ensure the manual is clear enough for Owner's operations staff to execute without specialized plumbing knowledge.

**Step 6.3 — Warranty Documentation**
Submit manufacturer warranties for pump and major components.

**Step 6.4 — Punch List Clearance**
Address all punch list items identified during final inspection and commissioning. Obtain written confirmation of punch list clearance from Owner representative.
