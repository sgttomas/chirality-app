# Procedure — DEL-011-02 Steel Plate Floor Embedments

**Document Type:** Procedure (operational)

**Revision:** R1 — 2026-02-26 (Pass 3 enrichment)

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps required to produce the DEL-011-02 Steel Plate Floor Embedments deliverable: from procurement and pre-installation coordination through to installation, inspection, and documentation closeout. It is oriented toward the General Contractor responsible for execution under PKG-011.

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Design Coordination and Procurement

**Step 1: Confirm scope and method with structural engineer**
- Obtain DEL-002-08 (Steel Plate Embedment Plan) and DEL-002-12 (Structural Specification).
- Confirm embedment method (cast-in-place vs. surface-mounted). ASSUMPTION: cast-in-place; see Guidance CON-011-02-01.
- Confirm plate material grade, thickness, dimensions for each plate zone (repair bays and wash bay), anchorage type and specifications, and surface finish requirements.
- Confirm quantitative flush tolerance value (+/- mm) from DEL-002-08; if not stated, request explicit written tolerance from structural engineer of record. [Lensing ref: A-005]
- Confirm whether wash bay plates are within this scope per resolution of CON-011-02-03.
- Confirm corrosion protection requirements for wash bay zone plates specifically. [Lensing ref: F-004]
- Confirm service life expectation with Owner/structural engineer. [Lensing ref: C-003]
- Record confirmation in project file.

**Step 2: Procure steel plates and anchorage hardware**
- Purchase steel plates per DEL-002-12 material specifications.
- Obtain mill certificates for each heat/lot of plate material.
- Procure anchorage hardware (headed studs, deformed bar anchors, or equivalent) per DEL-002-08 details.
- Verify material receipt against order and IFC drawings; note any discrepancies.

**Step 3: Mobilize and qualify welding subcontractor (if anchorage requires welding)**
- Confirm welding company certification per CSA W47.1 (ASSUMPTION: required; verify in DEL-002-12).
- Confirm welding procedures and welding inspector arrangements per DEL-002-12 requirements.
- Confirm applicable welding acceptance standard (CSA W59 or W47.1) for anchorage welds. [Lensing ref: A-003]

**Step 4: Confirm construction schedule interface with DEL-011-01 slab pour**
- Coordinate with concrete slab foreman: identify the scheduled date(s) for repair bay and wash bay floor pours.
- Confirm that steel plate installation will be completed and the pre-pour inspection hold point will be scheduled before each pour date.
- Notify County project representative of planned pour schedule and inspection dates.
- Confirm escalation protocol for DEL-002-08 drawing delay (see Guidance — Drawing Availability escalation path). [Lensing ref: X-001]

---

### Phase 2 — Installation (Cast-in-Place Method, ASSUMPTION)

**Step 5: Lay out plate positions in formwork**
- Set out plate centre lines and perimeter limits per DEL-002-08 dimensions and coordinates.
- Mark plate positions on the reinforcing steel or formwork to confirm layout against IFC drawings before any plates are placed.
- Verify bay width dimensions (24' per Appendix B, R-04) align with drawing dimensions. Note: verify unit convention consistency between Appendix B (imperial) and IFC drawings (confirm if metric or imperial). [Lensing ref: B-003]

**Step 6: Position steel plates and set elevation**
- Place steel plates in the marked positions.
- Set plate top surface elevation to achieve flush finish with finished concrete floor surface within tolerance per DEL-002-08 (TBD: confirm specific +/- mm tolerance value). Use temporary supports or levelling shims as required. [Lensing ref: A-005, E-001]
- Confirm plates are level and plumb in both directions.

**Step 7: Install anchorage system**
- Install headed studs, deformed bar anchors, or equivalent as shown on DEL-002-08.
- Perform stud or anchorage welding per DEL-002-12 welding procedure.
- Welding inspector to inspect all welds per applicable CSA standard (TBD: W59 or W47.1 per DEL-002-12); record welding inspection report. [Lensing ref: A-003]

**Step 8: Pre-pour inspection hold point**
- Notify structural engineer and County project representative that installation is ready for pre-pour inspection.
- Structural engineer (or delegate) and County representative to inspect using the following formalized checklist: [Lensing ref: X-002, X-004]

  **Pre-Pour Inspection Checklist:**
  | Check Item | Acceptance Criterion | Method | Pass/Fail |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Plate positions vs. IFC drawing locations | Within positional tolerance per DEL-002-08 (TBD: +/- X mm) | Field measurement against IFC coordinates | |
  | Plate top surface elevation | Within flush tolerance per DEL-002-08 (TBD: +/- X mm of finished floor level) | Laser level or straightedge survey | |
  | Anchorage count and type | All anchors per DEL-002-08 detail installed; correct type and size | Visual count and size verification | |
  | Weld visual acceptance | Per applicable CSA standard (TBD: W59 or W47.1 per DEL-002-12) — no cracks, porosity, undercut, or incomplete fusion per standard's visual acceptance criteria | Certified welding inspector visual inspection | |
  | Plate identification | Each plate traceable to mill certificate | Tag or marking verification | |

- Obtain written sign-off from structural engineer and County representative before authorizing concrete pour. (TBD: Clarify County representative's authority — see Guidance Considerations — County Representative Authority.) [Lensing ref: X-005]
- Do not proceed to pour until sign-off is received. This is a mandatory hold point. (Source: Specification REQ-011-02-06; Guidance Principle 4)

---

### Phase 3 — Concrete Pour and Finishing

**Step 8a: Protect plate surfaces during pour** [Lensing ref: D-002]
- Prior to concrete pour, apply temporary protection to plate top (wearing) surfaces to prevent concrete bonding, splash damage, or slurry contamination. TBD: Confirm protection method with structural engineer — options include:
  - Masking tape or adhesive film over plate top surfaces.
  - Form release agent applied to plate top surfaces.
  - Removable sheet covers secured to plate edges.
- ASSUMPTION: Plate top surfaces are the wearing surface and must remain clean and free of bonded concrete after pour; protection method to be confirmed with structural engineer as coating/treatment compatibility must be verified.

**Step 9: Coordinate concrete pour**
- Confirm concrete mix design and pour sequence with concrete subcontractor (coordination with DEL-011-01 scope).
- Vibration/consolidation plan requirements: [Lensing ref: F-003]
  - The concrete subcontractor shall prepare a vibration/consolidation plan that addresses the embedment zones specifically.
  - The plan shall define vibrator exclusion zones, reduced vibration intensity zones, or alternative consolidation methods (e.g., external vibration, self-consolidating concrete) near plate edges and anchorage systems.
  - TBD: The GC shall review and approve the vibration plan in coordination with the structural engineer before the pour. Responsible party for plan preparation: concrete subcontractor. Approval: GC + structural engineer.
- Monitor plate positions and elevations during pour; do not permit pour to proceed if plates shift from inspection-approved positions.

**Step 10: Finish concrete around embedments**
- Finish concrete surface to specified finish around and flush with plate surfaces.
- Remove temporary plate surface protection (from Step 8a) after concrete has set sufficiently to avoid contamination; clean plate surfaces as needed. [Lensing ref: D-002]
- Check plate surface elevation relative to finished floor after initial set; document any deviation.
- If any plate is found out of tolerance: immediately notify structural engineer for disposition. Do not proceed with subsequent work on that bay until engineer disposition is received.

---

### Phase 4 — Inspection, Documentation, and Closeout

**Step 11: Post-pour inspection**
- After concrete has reached sufficient cure — TBD: minimum cure period per DEL-011-01 concrete specification or applicable standard (ASSUMPTION: CSA A23.1 may specify minimum cure period; cross-reference with DEL-011-01 concrete specification for specific duration, e.g., 7-day or 28-day strength requirement before inspection loading). [Lensing ref: A-004]
  - Visually inspect all embedded plates for surface flush, damage, or edge cracking in the concrete.
  - Conduct straightedge or laser level survey to confirm plate surface flush within tolerance per DEL-002-08 (TBD: +/- X mm). [Lensing ref: A-005]
- Document results; photograph all plate locations.

**Step 12: Safety code inspection**
- Submit inspection request to Safety Codes Officer per applicable Alberta Safety Code permit requirements.
- Ensure County project representative is present for inspection (RFP R-01 §3.3.2).
- Obtain copy of inspection report; provide copy to County project representative.

**Step 13: Load rating certification**
- Coordinate with structural engineer of record (P.Eng.) to obtain load rating certification for the installed embedments. [Lensing ref: F-001]
- Certification shall confirm installed embedments meet the design load requirements per DEL-002-10.
- TBD: Confirm whether certification is by calculation-based analysis only or includes physical load testing. [Lensing ref: D-003]
- File certification with project documentation.

**Step 14: Compile and submit installation documentation**
- Assemble the full documentation package per Specification Documentation table:
  - Steel embedment installation documentation (as-built locations, dimensions).
  - Welding and connection inspection reports (per applicable CSA standard). [Lensing ref: A-003]
  - Load rating certification (P.Eng.-sealed). [Lensing ref: F-001]
  - Mill certificates / material test reports.
  - Safety code inspection sign-offs.
  - Pre-pour inspection sign-off from structural engineer and County representative.
  - Photographs — pre-pour and post-pour. [Lensing ref: X-003]
  - As-built drawings (mark-up of DEL-002-08 drawings). [Lensing ref: F-002]
  - Pre-pour inspection checklist (completed, from Step 8). [Lensing ref: X-002]
- Submit to County project representative.

### Step 15: Warranty-Period Monitoring Setup [Lensing ref: E-003]
- TBD: Establish warranty-period inspection protocol per Specification REQ-011-02-10:

  - Inspection criteria: plate surface condition, flush alignment, edge cracking, anchorage integrity, corrosion (especially in wash bay zone).
  - Defect reporting procedure: per RFP R-01 §2.10.6 (10-day rectification upon written notice).
