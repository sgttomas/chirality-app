## Scope

### Included
This specification governs the supply and installation of steel plate embedments cast into the concrete floor slab of the repair bays and wash bay within the New North Shop building addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop, Camrose County, Alberta. It covers:

- Procurement of structural steel plates to engineered dimensions.
- Positioning, anchorage, and embedment of steel plates within the concrete floor slab system.
- Coordination with concrete formwork, reinforcing steel, and slab pour activities.
- Inspection, documentation, and load rating certification of the completed embedment installation.

**Scope boundary — wash bay:** Wash bay steel plate embedments are included in this deliverable scope per Appendix B (R-04) floor plan and SOW-0024. However, the wash bay scope boundary with SOW-0027a (Wash Bay Enclosure, PKG-011) and SOW-0027b (Wash Bay Drainage, PKG-018 / DEL-018-05) requires confirmation — see Guidance CON-011-02-03. [Lensing ref: D-001]

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md; RFP R-01 §3.4; SOW-0024 (Decomposition §3, §8).

### Excluded
- Structural design and engineering calculations for embedments (covered by PKG-002, DEL-002-08, DEL-002-10).
- Structural specification for steel (covered by DEL-002-12).
- Concrete superstructure slab design and construction (covered by DEL-011-01, except the interface zone where plates are cast in).
- Equipment installation on embedments (covered by downstream packages, PKG-012 and PKG-016).
- Wash bay drainage and mud sump construction (covered by PKG-018, DEL-018-05).

## Requirements

### REQ-011-02-01: Scope of Supply
The General Contractor shall supply and install steel plate embedments in the repair bay floors and wash bay floor at the locations, sizes, and details shown on the Issued for Construction (IFC) structural drawings (DEL-002-08).

**Source:** RFP R-01 §3.4 ("Steel plating in concrete at strategic locations to accommodate track and packer type heavy equipment"); Appendix B (R-04) floor plan notation.

### REQ-011-02-02: Design Compliance
All steel plate embedments shall be installed strictly in accordance with the IFC structural drawings (DEL-002-08) and structural specification (DEL-002-12) issued by the structural engineer of record, P.Eng.-stamped and licensed to practice in Alberta.

**Source:** RFP R-01 §3.3.2.

### REQ-011-02-03: Equipment Load Compatibility
The embedments shall be designed and installed to accommodate tracked and packer-type heavy equipment. The specific design load values (uniformly distributed loads, point loads, dynamic amplification factors) shall be as specified in the structural calculation package (DEL-002-10).

**Source:** RFP R-01 §3.4; SOW-0024.
**Note:** Specific load values are TBD pending issuance of DEL-002-10. See Datasheet Design Load Category Table (Placeholder) for expected load categories. [Lensing ref: B-001]

### REQ-011-02-04: Material Grade
Steel plate material grade shall be as specified by the structural engineer in DEL-002-12. ASSUMPTION: Structural steel conforming to CAN/CSA G40.20/G40.21 or ASTM A36 equivalent is likely applicable; grade to be confirmed on IFC documents. [Lensing ref: A-002 — material grade is a TBD pending DEL-002-12 issuance]

### REQ-011-02-05: Plate Surface and Finish
The top surface of each embedded steel plate shall be flush with the finished concrete floor surface within the tolerance per DEL-002-08. TBD: A quantitative flush tolerance value (e.g., +/- X mm) shall be confirmed on DEL-002-08 or specified by the structural engineer; if not stated on IFC drawings, the GC shall request explicit written tolerance from the engineer of record before proceeding with installation. [Lensing ref: A-005, E-001]

ASSUMPTION: Flush installation is required to permit unobstructed equipment travel and to prevent edge loading of the concrete at plate perimeters.

### REQ-011-02-06: Anchorage to Slab
Steel plates shall be anchored to the reinforced concrete slab using a mechanical anchorage system (headed studs, deformed bar anchors, or equivalent) as detailed on DEL-002-08. Anchorage type, size, spacing, and welding requirements are TBD pending IFC drawings.

**Embedment method clarification:** ASSUMPTION: cast-in-place method (plates installed before concrete pour). This assumption drives the entire construction sequence. If surface-mounted plates were intended, the specification and procedure requirements differ substantially. Confirm method with structural engineer at earliest opportunity. See Guidance CON-011-02-01. [Lensing ref: A-001]

### REQ-011-02-07: Sequence — Upstream Dependency
Installation of steel plate embedments shall not commence until:
(a) The concrete floor slab design is complete and IFC drawings (DEL-002-08) are issued.
(b) The slab formwork and reinforcing steel are in place and ready for the steel plate placement step.

**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md (upstream: DEL-011-01 Superstructure). Note: for cast-in-place embedments (ASSUMPTION), plates are installed prior to the pour, not after cure; the upstream dependency governs sequencing relative to the overall concrete deck work package — specifically, formwork and rebar must be ready for plate placement before the pour. [Lensing ref: B-004 — clarified to be consistent with cast-in-place sequence]

### REQ-011-02-08: Code Compliance
All work shall comply with:
- Alberta Safety Codes Act and applicable Safety Code Permits.
- Alberta Building Code (ABC) — edition in force at time of building permit issuance (TBD: confirm specific edition number against actual building permit when obtained). [Lensing ref: C-001]
- CSA A23.1/A23.2 (Concrete Materials and Methods of Concrete Construction) — applicable to the concrete interface zone where plates are embedded. [Lensing ref: C-002]
- All applicable Alberta Safety Codes (RFP R-01 §3.3.2).

ASSUMPTION: CSA W47.1 (Certification of Companies for Fusion Welding of Steel) and/or CSA W59 (Welded Steel Construction) apply to stud welding or anchorage welding. TBD: Confirm which specific standard (W47.1, W59, or both) governs acceptance/rejection of anchorage welds; structural engineer to specify in DEL-002-12. The welding standard selection determines the acceptance criteria for the pre-pour hold point welding inspection. [Lensing ref: A-003]

### REQ-011-02-09: Permit and Inspection Coordination
The General Contractor shall submit all required inspection requests. The County project representative must be present for all inspections and shall receive copies of all completed inspection reports (RFP R-01 §3.3.2).

### REQ-011-02-10: Warranty
All materials and workmanship are covered under the two (2) year guarantee period following the Construction Completion Certificate (CCC) date (RFP R-01 §2.10.2). Defects identified during the guarantee period shall be rectified within ten (10) days of written Owner notice (RFP R-01 §2.10.6).

### REQ-011-02-11: Completion Deadline
All work under DEL-011-02 shall be completed in time to support the project completion deadline of December 31, 2026 (RFP R-01 §3.1; OBJ-002).
