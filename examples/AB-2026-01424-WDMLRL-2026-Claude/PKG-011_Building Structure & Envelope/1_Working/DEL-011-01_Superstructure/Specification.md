# Specification: DEL-011-01 Concrete Superstructure

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-011-01 covers the construction of the concrete superstructure for the New North Shop Addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill, including:

- The complete concrete structural frame (walls, columns, beams, and roof structure) achieving a 35 ft minimum interior clear ceiling height
- The concrete floor slab with embedded steel plates at locations shown on structural drawings
- The ventilated and lighted below-grade service pit/trench
- The load-bearing mezzanine structure above the parts room, utility room, and wash bay
- The mezzanine access stair
- Structural crane runway supports for two 5-tonne overhead cranes
- Structural rough openings and framing for overhead doors on repair bays and wash bay (see vocabulary note in Guidance regarding "overhead doors" scope boundary)
- Separate entrance/exit door assemblies
- Structural provisions (slab penetration, structural support if required) for the 50,000L water cistern located inside the building footprint (cistern supply/install under DEL-014-01; see CONF-011-01-001 in Guidance Conflict Table). Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-001; App B.

**SOW Reference:** SOW-0022 (Construct concrete structure with 35' ceiling height). Source: Decomposition §3-D, §7 PKG-011; RFP §3.4, App B.

## Requirements

### REQ-011-01-01: Structural System and Ceiling Height
The superstructure shall be a concrete structure achieving a minimum 35 ft interior clear ceiling height throughout the main shop area. Note: Whether "concrete structure" prescriptively requires all-concrete or permits hybrid steel/concrete systems (e.g., steel roof on concrete walls) is TBD -- to be confirmed by the Structural Engineer of Record. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-001)
- **Source:** RFP §3.4; App B (note: "Concrete Structure (35' Ceiling)")
- **Verification:** Dimensional survey at substantial completion confirming >= 35 ft clear height; structural engineer sign-off on IFC drawings. Dimensional tolerance to be specified by Structural Engineer of Record in IFC drawings. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md B-002)

### REQ-011-01-02: Building Footprint
The superstructure shall be constructed to a footprint of approximately 130 ft x 100 ft, providing a useable floor area of approximately 13,000 sq ft. Note: The 13,000 sq ft figure refers to the New North Shop addition only (130 ft x 100 ft), consistent with App B dimensions; confirmation with County required (see CONF-011-01-002 in Guidance Conflict Table). (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md E-003)
- **Source:** RFP §3.1 ("approximately 13000 square feet"); App B (conceptual plan dimensions 130' x 100' for new shop area)
- **Verification:** As-built survey (DEL-008-04). Dimensional tolerance for building footprint TBD per Structural Engineer of Record in IFC drawings. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md B-002)

### REQ-011-01-03: Structural Design Compliance
All structural construction shall be executed in conformance with P.Eng.-stamped Issued for Construction (IFC) drawings. The IFC drawings must be signed/stamped by a Professional Engineer licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.
- **Source:** RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0018
- **Verification:** Confirmation that IFC drawings are stamped before construction begins; copy of drawings on file. Explicit verification checkpoint in Procedure Phase 1 confirms P.Eng. stamp presence and documents it before first concrete placement. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-001)

### REQ-011-01-04: Applicable Codes and Regulations
The superstructure shall comply with all applicable Alberta building codes and regulations, and all Alberta Safety Codes. Specific Safety Code permits applicable to superstructure scope (building, electrical, plumbing, gas, etc.) to be confirmed by Building Inspector / Safety Codes Officer and enumerated in DEL-009-03 permit register. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-003)
- **Source:** RFP §3.3.2, §3.4; SOW-0008, SOW-0009
- **Verification:** Building permit issued; successful code inspections documented in DEL-009-04

### REQ-011-01-05: Concrete Floor Slab with Steel Plate Embedments
The concrete floor slab shall include embedded steel plates at strategic locations as indicated on the structural drawings (IFC), designed to accommodate tracked and packer-type heavy equipment. Floor slab finish (e.g., hard trowel, burnished, broom) TBD per architectural/structural design team specification in IFC documents. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md C-002)
- **Source:** RFP §3.4; App B (steel plates shown on floor plan at multiple bays); SOW-0024
- **Verification:** Location and installation of steel plates confirmed against IFC embedment plan (DEL-002-08) during inspection. Note: DEL-002-08 is the design drawing (embedment plan); DEL-011-02 is the field installation tracking/documentation. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-005)

### REQ-011-01-06: Wash Bay Structural Enclosure
Note: Per decomposition, wash bay structural enclosure (walls, roof, overhead door opening, steel plate floor) is assigned to DEL-011-05 (SOW-0027a). This requirement should be reviewed for scope reassignment or confirmed as intentional overlap. See CONF-011-01-003 in Guidance Conflict Table. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md B-003; Decomposition SOW-0027a -> DEL-011-05)

The structural frame shall include coordination with the wash bay structural enclosure (DEL-011-05) including structural integration points for overhead door opening, structural walls, and roof integration.
- **Source:** RFP §3.1 ("single enclosed bay dedicated for washing"); RFP §3.4; App B; SOW-0027a
- **Verification:** Dimensional verification confirming structural opening dimensions, wall tie-in points, and roof integration meet IFC requirements; inspection sign-off documenting specific items verified. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-002)

### REQ-011-01-07: Service Pit/Trench
A below-grade service pit/trench shall be constructed within the superstructure, designed and sized to allow mechanics to perform servicing under heavy equipment. The pit shall include provisions for ventilation and lighting.
- **Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Ventilated and lighted service pit area"); App B (service trench shown on floor plan, 24 ft dimension); SOW-0028
- **Verification:** Inspection prior to backfill/topping; dimensional check against structural detail (DEL-002-06). Additionally, verification that ventilation duct openings/sleeves and lighting conduit sleeves are installed per MEP coordination drawings (DEL-003/DEL-005 series) before concrete placement. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-004)

### REQ-011-01-08: Load-Bearing Mezzanine Structure
The superstructure shall include a load-bearing mezzanine level above the parts room, utility room, and wash bay, capable of supporting heavy items including oil totes and oil pumping equipment. Specific design loads shall be established by the Structural Engineer of Record and reflected in the structural calculation package (DEL-002-10).
- **Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Mezzanine storage above utility and parts room capable of handling heavy items such as several oil totes and oil pumping equipment"); App B ("Load Bearing Over Parts Room + Utility For Storage"); SOW-0032, SOW-0033
- **Verification:** Structural calculations approved by P.Eng.; inspection at mezzanine framing stage

### REQ-011-01-09: Mezzanine Access Stair
The superstructure shall include a stair providing access from the main floor to the mezzanine level.
- **Source:** App B ("Stairs to Mezzanine"); SOW-0034
- **Verification:** Code compliance inspection for stair geometry (riser height, tread depth — TBD per applicable building code edition); inspection sign-off documenting specific code compliance items checked. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-002)

### REQ-011-01-10: Crane Runway Structural Supports
The superstructure shall include integral structural runway support beams and connections to carry two 5-tonne overhead cranes on trolleys. The structural capacity of the crane supports shall be confirmed by the Structural Engineer of Record in accordance with the crane manufacturer's requirements (crane supply under DEL-016-01). Crane runway alignment tolerances (span, elevation, levelness) shall be documented from the crane manufacturer's specifications (DEL-016-01) and incorporated into IFC drawings before runway construction. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-001)
- **Source:** RFP §3.4 ("2 -- 5-tonne overhead cranes on a trolley"); App B ("5 TON CRANE" x 2); SOW-0067; DEL-002-07
- **Verification:** Structural calculations reviewed; crane runway elevation and alignment verified against manufacturer's specified tolerances before crane installation. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-001)

### REQ-011-01-11: Structural Rough Openings for Overhead Doors
The superstructure shall include structural rough openings and framing for overhead doors on the two drive-through repair bays (each 24 ft wide per conceptual drawing) and on the enclosed wash bay. Note: This requirement covers the structural rough openings only; overhead door supply and installation is under DEL-011-03 (repair bays) and DEL-011-05 (wash bay). See Guidance vocabulary note on "overhead doors." (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md C-003)
- **Source:** RFP §3.1, App B (repair bay widths shown as 24'); SOW-0025; DEL-001-07 (door schedule TBD)
- **Verification:** Dimensional inspection against door schedule confirming rough opening dimensions, lintel/header structural capacity per IFC drawings, and plumbness/levelness within IFC-specified tolerances. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-002)

### REQ-011-01-12: Entrance/Exit Doors
The superstructure shall provide separate entrance/exit door assemblies as indicated on the architectural drawings.
- **Source:** RFP §3.1 ("separate entrance/exit doors"); SOW-0026
- **Verification:** Inspection against architectural door/window schedule (DEL-001-07) confirming door rough opening dimensions, frame alignment, and hardware compatibility. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-002)

### REQ-011-01-13: Inspection Coordination
All structural inspections shall be submitted by the Contractor. The County project representative must be present at all inspections and receive copies of all completed inspection reports.
- **Source:** RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0084, SOW-0085
- **Verification:** Inspection coordination log maintained in DEL-009-04

### REQ-011-01-14: Completion Deadline
All superstructure construction work shall be substantially complete in sufficient time to allow downstream PKG-011, PKG-012, PKG-013, PKG-014, PKG-015, PKG-016, PKG-017, and PKG-020 work to be completed on or before December 31, 2026. Intermediate schedule milestones or duration targets for superstructure completion TBD per Project Manager -- to demonstrate sufficient progress toward OBJ-002. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-003)
- **Source:** RFP §3.1 ("All Work... must be completed on or before December 31, 2026"); SOW-0091; OBJ-002
- **Verification:** Milestone schedule monitoring; project manager confirmation

### REQ-011-01-15: Guarantee
All materials and workmanship in the superstructure shall be guaranteed for the construction period plus a 2-year period following issuance of the Construction Completion Certificate (CCC). Defects identified during the guarantee period shall be rectified within 10 days of Owner's written instruction.
- **Source:** RFP §2.10; SOW-0120, SOW-0121
- **Verification:** CCC issued; warranty tracking under DEL-021-05

### REQ-011-01-16: Concrete Compressive Strength
Minimum concrete compressive strength (f'c) shall be as specified by the Structural Engineer of Record in the structural calculation package (DEL-002-10) and reflected in IFC specifications. Until f'c is established, this value is TBD. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md C-001)
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION: CSA A23.1/A23.2 governs concrete strength requirements; specific f'c per DEL-002-10 (location TBD)
- **Verification:** Concrete cylinder test results (lab reports) confirming compressive strength meets or exceeds specified f'c; delivery tickets confirming mix design compliance

### REQ-011-01-17: Concrete Surface Quality
Acceptance criteria for concrete surface defects (honeycombing, cold joints, cracking) TBD -- to be specified per CSA A23.1 surface defect classification or per IFC specification. No concrete surface quality threshold is currently defined beyond the implicit "per IFC." (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-005)
- **Source:** ASSUMPTION: CSA A23.1 applicable for surface quality classification (location TBD)
- **Verification:** Visual inspection of concrete surfaces against IFC/CSA-specified acceptance criteria; defect documentation and disposition records

### REQ-011-01-18: Cistern Structural Provisions
The superstructure shall include structural provisions for the 50,000L water cistern located inside the building footprint (shown on App B near stairs to mezzanine), including slab penetrations, structural support provisions, and any required reinforcement. Specific provisions TBD per coordination between structural design (DEL-002 series) and plumbing design (DEL-014-01). See CONF-011-01-001 in Guidance Conflict Table. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-001; App B)
- **Source:** App B (cistern shown inside building footprint); Decomposition PKG-014
- **Verification:** Review of IFC structural drawings confirming cistern provisions are coordinated with plumbing IFC; inspection of structural provisions before concrete placement
