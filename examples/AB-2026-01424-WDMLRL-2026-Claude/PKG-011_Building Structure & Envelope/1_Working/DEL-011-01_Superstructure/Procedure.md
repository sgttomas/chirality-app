# Procedure: DEL-011-01 Concrete Superstructure

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and execute the Concrete Superstructure for the New North Shop Addition (SOW-0022). It covers the production sequence from pre-construction coordination through structural installation, inspection, and documentation closeout.

The procedure applies to the General Contractor and their structural subcontractor(s) responsible for DEL-011-01 under PKG-011. Note: Structural subcontractor qualification expectations (e.g., COR certification, relevant concrete work experience) should be addressed during pre-construction coordination -- see Guidance C9. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md E-002)

---

## Steps

### Phase 1 -- Pre-Construction Coordination

**Step 1: Verify P.Eng. Stamp on IFC Drawings**
- Before any concrete placement or structural work commences, verify that the structural IFC drawing set bears a valid P.Eng. stamp from a Professional Engineer licensed in Alberta.
- Document the verification: record the drawing set number, date, P.Eng. name and stamp number, and date of verification.
- Retain the documented verification record as a project file.
- Source: REQ-011-01-03; RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0018; _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-001.

**Step 2: Confirm IFC Drawing Coordination**
- Conduct a pre-construction coordination review with the Structural, Architectural, Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing engineers.
- Confirm that all embedded items (conduit sleeves, drain sleeves, anchor bolts, steel plate embedment locations, service pit geometry, cistern structural provisions, ventilation/lighting sleeves for service pit) are shown on the structural IFC drawings and are consistent with all other discipline IFC drawings.
- Confirm that crane manufacturer alignment tolerances (span, elevation, levelness) are incorporated into the structural IFC drawings per DEL-002-07.
- Document coordination outcomes.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0012; Guidance P4, P5, C4, C5; _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-001, X-004, F-001.

**Step 3: Confirm Foundation Interface**
- Obtain foundation as-built survey (from DEL-010-01 records).
- Compare foundation as-built to structural IFC drawing requirements (bearing conditions, anchor bolt layout, column locations).
- Identify and resolve any discrepancies with the Structural Engineer of Record before proceeding.
- Source: Guidance P3; _DEPENDENCIES.md.

**Step 4: Schedule Inspections with County and Classify Hold Points**
- Notify the County project representative of the planned inspection schedule for key structural stages (reinforcement, service pit, floor slab, mezzanine framing, structural frame, building envelope).
- Classify each inspection as either a **Hold Point** (construction may not proceed until inspection passes) or a **Witness Point** (construction may proceed if inspector is notified and fails to attend). See classification table below.
- Confirm County representative availability for each inspection date.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0084; Guidance C6; _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-002.

**Inspection Classification Table (PROPOSAL -- TBD per Owner/Inspector confirmation):**

| Inspection | Proposed Classification | Rationale |
|---|---|---|

| Step 13: Floor slab reinforcement and embedments | Hold Point | Embedded items are permanent after pour |
| Step 17: Structural frame | Hold Point | Primary structural system must be verified before enclosure |
| Step 20: Mezzanine framing | TBD (Hold Point or Witness Point) | Depends on mezzanine structural significance |
| Step 23: Building envelope / structural completion | Witness Point | ASSUMPTION: Envelope closure is inspectable after fact |

(Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-002)

**Step 5: Submit Inspection Requests**
- Submit all required inspection requests to the Authority Having Jurisdiction (building inspector) as required by the building permit conditions.
- Coordinate so that both code inspector and County representative can attend jointly where possible.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0084, SOW-0085.

### Phase 2 -- Subgrade and Formwork Preparation

**Step 6: Set Layout and Control Points**
- Establish building layout using construction survey (DEL-008-03 or GC's layout crew working from survey control).
- Set column, wall, and service pit layout based on IFC drawings.
- Source: ASSUMPTION: standard construction practice; SOW-0003.

**Step 7: Service Pit Excavation and Forming**
- Excavate service pit/trench to dimensions shown on structural drawings (DEL-002-06).
- Install reinforcing steel and formwork for pit walls and floor.
- Inspect for conduit/drain sleeve placement per MEP coordination drawings. Specifically verify that ventilation duct openings/sleeves (PKG-013) and lighting conduit sleeves (PKG-015) are installed per MEP coordination drawings before concrete placement. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-004)
- Source: RFP §3.4; App B (service trench shown); SOW-0028.

**Step 8: First Inspection -- Service Pit Reinforcement (HOLD POINT)**
- Request and receive reinforcement inspection for service pit before concrete placement.
- Verify ventilation and lighting sleeve/duct placement per MEP coordination drawings.
- County representative present at inspection.
- Record inspection report.
- Note: This is a proposed Hold Point -- construction shall not proceed past this step until inspection passes. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-002)
- Source: SOW-0084, SOW-0085; REQ-011-01-07.

**Step 9: Place Service Pit Concrete**
- Place concrete in service pit walls and floor per IFC specifications.
- Verify concrete mix design matches IFC/structural specification (f'c per REQ-011-01-16).
- Conduct concrete cylinder tests per CSA A23.1/A23.2 (ASSUMPTION).
- Retain concrete delivery tickets for mix design traceability. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-002)
- For cold-weather placement: implement cold-weather concreting provisions per construction plan (see Guidance C10, P-14). (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-004)
- Source: ASSUMPTION: standard practice.

---

### Phase 3 -- Floor Slab

**Step 10: Prepare Subgrade for Floor Slab**
- Compact subgrade to requirements established in structural/civil IFC drawings.
- Install vapour barrier and insulation if required per IFC drawings.
- Source: ASSUMPTION: standard practice for industrial concrete slab on grade.

**Step 11: Set Steel Plate Embedments**
- Position and secure steel plates in the floor slab forms at locations shown on the structural embedment plan (DEL-002-08, design authority).
- Steel plates must be levelled, braced against displacement during pour, and inspected before concrete placement.
- Field installation tracking and documentation under DEL-011-02. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-005)
- Source: RFP §3.4; App B (steel plates shown at multiple locations); SOW-0024; REQ-011-01-05.

**Step 12: Set Floor Slab Reinforcement and Embedded Items**
- Place floor slab reinforcing steel per IFC drawings.
- Set all floor penetration sleeves for plumbing drains (DEL-014-04), electrical conduits (DEL-015), and any other MEP penetrations.
- Set anchor bolts for columns, crane rails, and other structural attachments.
- Set cistern structural provisions (slab penetrations, reinforcement, structural support provisions) per coordination with DEL-014-01. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-001)
- Source: IFC drawings (all disciplines); Guidance P4.

**Step 13: Second Inspection -- Floor Slab Reinforcement and Embedments (HOLD POINT)**
- Request and receive reinforcement/embedment inspection before floor slab pour.
- Verify steel plate embedment locations against DEL-002-08 embedment plan.
- Verify cistern structural provisions per coordination drawings.
- County representative present.
- Record inspection report.
- Note: This is a proposed Hold Point -- construction shall not proceed past this step until inspection passes. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-002)
- Source: SOW-0084, SOW-0085.

**Step 14: Pour Floor Slab**
- Place and finish concrete floor slab per IFC specifications.
- Verify concrete mix design matches IFC/structural specification (f'c per REQ-011-01-16).
- Conduct cylinder tests per CSA A23.1/A23.2 (ASSUMPTION).
- Retain concrete delivery tickets for mix design traceability. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-002)
- Achieve specified finish (TBD per architectural/structural specifications -- see REQ-011-01-05 and Specification note on floor finish; ASSUMPTION: hard trowel finish for industrial use). (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md C-002)
- For cold-weather or hot-weather placement: implement applicable provisions per construction plan (see Guidance C10). (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-004)
- Source: RFP §3.4; App B; ASSUMPTION.

**Step 15: Cure Floor Slab**
- Apply curing compound or wet cure per IFC specifications and CSA A23.1 curing requirements (ASSUMPTION).
- For cold-weather conditions: maintain minimum curing temperature per construction plan provisions (see Guidance C10). (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-004)
- Do not apply loads until concrete has reached minimum specified compressive strength (f'c per REQ-011-01-16 -- TBD per structural design). Minimum curing duration TBD per CSA A23.1 requirements and structural engineer's specification. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md C-004)
- Source: ASSUMPTION: CSA A23.1 curing requirements.

**Step 15A: Strip Formwork**
- Remove formwork from floor slab and any formed elements only after concrete has achieved minimum strength for form removal as specified in IFC documents or per CSA A23.1 requirements (ASSUMPTION).
- Inspect formed concrete surfaces after stripping for defects (honeycombing, cold joints, cracking) per REQ-011-01-17 acceptance criteria (TBD).
- Document formwork removal date and concrete age/strength at removal.
- Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-004; ASSUMPTION: CSA A23.1 formwork removal requirements.

### Phase 4 -- Structural Frame Erection

**Step 16: Erect Structural Frame (Walls/Columns)**
- Erect concrete structural frame (tilt-up panels, precast, or cast-in-place columns/walls per structural system selected -- TBD per DEL-002 design).
- Ensure crane runway corbels/brackets are positioned to IFC specified elevations and alignments per crane manufacturer tolerances.
- For cast-in-place elements: retain concrete delivery tickets and conduct cylinder tests. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-002)
- Source: RFP §3.4; SOW-0022; REQ-011-01-01; Guidance C1.

**Step 17: Third Inspection -- Structural Frame (HOLD POINT)**
- Request structural frame inspection.
- County representative present.
- Record inspection report.
- Note: This is a proposed Hold Point -- construction shall not proceed past this step until inspection passes. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-002)
- Source: SOW-0084, SOW-0085.

**Step 17A: Strip Frame Formwork (if cast-in-place)**
- If the structural frame uses cast-in-place concrete, remove formwork only after concrete has achieved minimum strength for form removal as specified in IFC documents or per CSA A23.1 requirements (ASSUMPTION).
- Inspect formed concrete surfaces after stripping for defects per REQ-011-01-17 acceptance criteria (TBD).
- Document formwork removal date and concrete age/strength at removal.
- Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-004; ASSUMPTION: CSA A23.1 formwork removal requirements.

**Step 18: Install Crane Runway Beams**
- Install crane runway beams/rails to elevations and alignments specified in DEL-002-07 and crane manufacturer's requirements.
- Survey runway alignment (span, elevation, levelness) against manufacturer's specified tolerances before proceeding.
- Document survey results and compare against tolerance criteria.
- Source: RFP §3.4; App B; SOW-0067; REQ-011-01-10; Guidance P5; _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-001.

**Step 19: Install Mezzanine Framing**
- Construct mezzanine structure (beams, joists, deck) above parts room, utility room, and wash bay per DEL-002-05.
- Confirm mezzanine framing meets design live load requirements (f'c and design load per DEL-002-10).
- Install mezzanine access stair.
- Source: RFP §3.4; App B; SOW-0032, SOW-0033, SOW-0034; REQ-011-01-08, REQ-011-01-09.

**Step 20: Fourth Inspection -- Mezzanine Framing (TBD Classification)**
- Request mezzanine framing inspection.
- County representative present.
- Record inspection report.
- Note: Classification as Hold Point or Witness Point TBD per Owner/Inspector confirmation. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-002)
- Source: SOW-0084, SOW-0085.

**Step 21: Construct Roof Structure**
- Erect roof structure to achieve 35 ft minimum interior clear height.
- Integrate roof structure with crane runway and wall system (see Guidance C8 for roof system considerations).
- Install roof deck and sheathing.
- Source: RFP §3.4; SOW-0022; REQ-011-01-01; _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md E-001.

**Step 22: Install Building Envelope Cladding**
- Install exterior wall cladding and roof membrane/covering per architectural and structural IFC drawings.
- Install entrance/exit door assemblies (SOW-0026; REQ-011-01-12).
- Install structural rough openings and framing for overhead doors on repair bays and wash bay (REQ-011-01-11). Note: Overhead door supply/install is under DEL-011-03 (repair bays) and DEL-011-05 (wash bay) -- see Guidance vocabulary note. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md C-003)
- Source: RFP §3.1; App B; SOW-0025, SOW-0026, SOW-0027a.

**Step 23: Fifth Inspection -- Building Envelope/Structural Completion (WITNESS POINT)**
- Request structural completion / building envelope inspection.
- County representative present.
- Record inspection report.
- Note: This is a proposed Witness Point. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-002; ASSUMPTION)
- Source: SOW-0084, SOW-0085.

### Phase 5 -- Dimensional and Load Verification

**Step 24: Verify Ceiling Height**
- Conduct dimensional survey to confirm 35 ft minimum clear ceiling height is achieved throughout main shop area.
- Record measurements with dimensional tolerance per IFC specification.
- Source: REQ-011-01-01; RFP §3.4; _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md B-002.

**Step 25: Verify Building Footprint**
- Confirm that as-built footprint is consistent with IFC drawings (~130 ft x 100 ft).
- Record dimensional tolerance per IFC specification.
- Coordinate with as-built survey (DEL-008-04).
- Source: REQ-011-01-02; App B; _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md B-002.

**Step 26: Structural Engineer Load Verification**
- Obtain written confirmation from Structural Engineer of Record that:
  - Mezzanine design load capacity is adequate for the intended use (oil totes, pumping equipment).
  - Crane runway load capacity and alignment meet crane manufacturer requirements (per manufacturer's specified tolerances).
- Source: REQ-011-01-08, REQ-011-01-10; Guidance C3; _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-001.

**Step 27: Concrete Surface Quality Inspection**
- Inspect all concrete surfaces for defects (honeycombing, cold joints, cracking) per REQ-011-01-17 acceptance criteria (TBD per IFC/CSA A23.1 classification).
- Document any defects and disposition (accept, repair, reject) with reference to acceptance criteria.
- Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-005; ASSUMPTION: CSA A23.1 applicable.

---

### Phase 6 -- Documentation and Handoff

**Step 28: Compile Concrete Test Records and Delivery Tickets**
- Collect all concrete cylinder test results, slump test records, and delivery tickets.
- Confirm all test results meet or exceed specified f'c (per REQ-011-01-16).
- Retain all concrete delivery tickets as quality records for mix design traceability. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-002)
- Source: ASSUMPTION: CSA A23.1/A23.2 requirements; REQ-011-01-04.

**Step 29: Compile Structural Installation Documentation**
- Assemble field records of concrete pours (volumes, dates, mix designs, admixtures).
- Assemble all structural inspection reports.
- Assemble photo record of key stages (steel plate embedments, service pit before topping, mezzanine framing, crane runway installation, completed frame).
- Assemble formwork removal records.
- Source: _CONTEXT.md Anticipated Artifacts; Specification Documentation table.

**Step 30: Prepare Structural As-Built Drawings**
- Prepare red-line markup of IFC drawings showing actual constructed conditions (field deviations, as-built dimensions, any approved modifications).
- Note: As-built survey (DEL-008-04) covers building footprint confirmation. As-built structural drawings are the contractor's field record of actual construction versus IFC design.
- Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-003; ASSUMPTION: standard construction practice.

**Step 31: Confirm County Copies of Inspection Reports**
- Verify that the County project representative has received copies of all completed structural inspection reports.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0085.

**Step 32: Notify Downstream Trades**
- Confirm to the Project Manager that the superstructure is ready for downstream trade access (mechanical, electrical, plumbing, crane installation, interior fit-out).
- Source: Decomposition PKG-011 downstream dependencies; OBJ-002.
