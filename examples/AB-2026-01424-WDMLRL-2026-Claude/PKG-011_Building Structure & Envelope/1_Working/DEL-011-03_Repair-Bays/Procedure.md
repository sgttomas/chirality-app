## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce (construct) the two drive-through repair bays as required by SOW-0025 within the New North Shop addition. It covers the construction sequence from pre-construction readiness through to handoff of the repair bay structural and door scope to the General Contractor's completion package.

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Design Coordination and Pre-Construction

**Step 1.1 — Confirm Repair Bay Scope from IFC Drawings**
Review IFC structural drawings (DEL-002-03, DEL-002-04) and architectural drawings (DEL-001-02, DEL-001-07) to confirm:
- Bay clear width dimensions (nominal 24' per Appendix B; confirm whether this represents nominal structural bay width or clear opening width per IFC definition -- Lensing B-002)
- Bay door rough opening widths and heights (confirm equipment clearance envelope dimensions: height with raised attachments, width, length, and turning radius for motor scraper class -- Lensing F-001)
- Column and wall locations bounding each bay
- Door lintel/header structural requirements
- Interface with overhead crane runway beams (DEL-002-07)
- Construction tolerances for door rough openings per manufacturer requirements (Lensing A-004)
- Applicable Alberta Building Code edition confirmed from permit documentation (Lensing A-003)

Record any discrepancies between IFC drawings and conceptual Appendix B dimensions. Raise with design team for resolution before proceeding.

**Step 1.2 — Coordinate with Downstream Trades**
Prior to framing door rough openings, confirm requirements with:
- PKG-013 (DEL-013-04 -- Exhaust System): locations and sizes of any ceiling or wall penetrations or attachment points for exhaust hose systems in repair bays. **Establish coordination hold point:** structural provisions for exhaust system must be confirmed and signed off by both General Contractor and mechanical contractor before closing ceiling or roof assembly. (Lensing X-002)
- PKG-014 (DEL-014-04 -- Sump Drains): sump pit locations and any structural slab requirements (pit framing, slope direction, slope percentage per civil/structural engineer specification). Confirm whether oil/water separator is required by Alberta environmental regulations and, if so, confirm impact on sump design before concrete is poured. (Lensing C-001, D-001)
- PKG-015 (DEL-015-04 -- Equipment Power): conduit stub-in locations for overhead door operator electrical feeds
- PKG-016 (DEL-016-01 -- Overhead Cranes): confirm crane runway beam elevation and any clearance constraints affecting door height; confirm whether crane travel coverage extends over both repair bays (see Guidance Conflict Table CT-001)
- Fire separation requirements: confirm with design team whether fire separation is required between repair bays and adjacent spaces (parts room, wash bay, storage) per Alberta Building Code/Fire Code. (Lensing F-002)

Document coordination outcomes and confirm with General Contractor project manager.

**Step 1.3 — Procure Overhead Doors**
Issue purchase order for overhead doors based on IFC door schedule (DEL-001-07) and confirmed rough opening dimensions. Confirm:
- Quantity: 4 doors total (2 doors x 2 bays -- one entry and one exit per bay)
- Type, width, height, insulation R-value, and operator specifications per IFC door schedule (TBD)
- Safety devices: obstruction sensors and auto-reverse mechanism per REQ-011-03-015 (Lensing A-002)
- Weather seals: head, jamb, and floor seals per REQ-011-03-016 (Lensing C-002)
- Operator voltage and amperage consistent with PKG-015 circuit sizing (SOW-0060)
- Delivery schedule consistent with construction programme
- Warranty documentation: confirm manufacturer warranty period meets or exceeds project guarantee period (construction period plus 2-year warranty per RFP S2.10.2) (Lensing F-003)

**Procurement timing (Lensing D-002):** Industrial overhead doors typically have 8-16 week lead times (ASSUMPTION). Procurement must be initiated no later than TBD weeks before the scheduled installation date, anchored to a specific project schedule milestone, to ensure delivery does not impact the December 31, 2026 completion deadline. The General Contractor shall confirm required lead time with the selected manufacturer and incorporate this constraint into the project schedule. The procurement milestone date should be identified in the project schedule as a critical-path item.

**Source:** RFP S3.1; SOW-0025; ASSUMPTION: 4 doors for 2 drive-through bays.

### Phase 2 — Structural Rough-In (Door Frames and Openings)

**Step 2.1 — Lay Out Bay Door Rough Openings**
Using IFC drawings and surveyor-established reference points (DEL-008-03 construction survey), mark out:
- Centerlines of each bay
- Door rough opening extents (width and height) at each end of each bay
- Sill and jamb locations

Verify layout against superstructure column locations. Confirm with site supervisor before forming or placing any concrete associated with door jambs or sills.

**Step 2.2 — Construct Door Frame Structural Elements**
Construct door jambs, sills, and headers / lintels per IFC structural drawings. Ensure:
- Header/lintel capacity is per structural design (P.Eng. specification)
- Anchor bolts, embeds, or other door frame attachment hardware are placed accurately per IFC drawings
- Door frame dimensions match the IFC door schedule within manufacturer-specified tolerances for plumb, square, and dimensional accuracy (tolerances TBD pending manufacturer selection and IFC issuance -- Lensing A-004)
- Conduit sleeves for overhead door operator wiring are placed at this stage (coordinate with PKG-015)

**Step 2.3 — Confirm Slab Drainage Slope**
Verify that the concrete floor in each repair bay is sloped toward the intended sump drain location(s) per IFC and plumbing drawings (coordinate with PKG-014 DEL-014-04). Floor slope shall be confirmed before final floor finishing. Coordinate with plumbing contractor on sump pit rough-in timing.

**Acceptance criteria (Lensing D-001):** Floor drainage slope percentage and direction must be measured and verified against the IFC/civil drawing specification (slope %, direction, and ponding test requirements as defined by the civil/structural engineer). Subjective assessment ("provisions are in place") is not sufficient; measurable slope verification is required.

**Step 2.4 — Confirm Exhaust System Provisions**
Install any blocking, backing, roof penetration sleeves, or overhead mounting provisions required by the mechanical contractor (PKG-013) for exhaust hose drops and fan units.

**Coordination hold point (Lensing X-002):** Before closing ceiling or roof assembly, obtain formal sign-off from the PKG-013 mechanical contractor confirming that all structural provisions for exhaust system installation are correctly located and adequate. Record the hold point sign-off in coordination records. This gate ensures provisions are not missed prior to mechanical rough-in.

---

### Phase 3 — Overhead Door Installation

**Step 3.1 — Inspect Rough Openings Prior to Door Installation**
Before door installation commences, inspect:
- Rough opening dimensions (width, height, plumb, square) against door manufacturer's published installation tolerances (Lensing A-004)
- Structural frame integrity (headers, jambs, sills)
- Conduit stubs for operator power present and capped
- Floor slab complete and cured in door threshold area

Document inspection and obtain sign-off from General Contractor superintendent.

**Step 3.2 — Install Overhead Doors**
Install overhead doors per manufacturer's installation instructions and IFC drawings. Install:
- Door panels or curtain (sectional or rolling, per specification)
- Tracks and guides
- Springs (torsion or extension, per design)
- Door operator units
- Safety devices (obstruction sensors, auto-reverse) per REQ-011-03-015 (Lensing A-002)
- Weather seals (head, jamb, and floor seals) per REQ-011-03-016 (Lensing C-002)

**Step 3.3 — Connect Operator Power**
Coordinate with PKG-015 electrical contractor to connect overhead door operators to electrical circuits (SOW-0060). Confirm:
- Circuit voltage and amperage consistent with operator specifications
- Wiring complete and circuits energised before functional testing

**Step 3.4 — Commission and Test Each Door**
Cycle each overhead door through a minimum of three complete open/close cycles. Verify:
- Door travels full open and full closed without binding, racking, or deviation
- Operator stops and reverses correctly on obstruction (safety device functional test per REQ-011-03-015)
- Safety devices (obstruction sensors, auto-reverse) function as specified
- Door seals (head, jamb, floor) contact surfaces correctly when closed (REQ-011-03-016 verification)
- Door insulation panels intact and correctly installed

Record test results for each door.

---

### Phase 4 — Inspections

**Step 4.1 — Request Required Inspections**
Submit all required inspection requests per the building permit and Safety Code permit conditions. Notify County project representative of all inspections in advance (RFP S3.3.2).

Inspections to be requested (specific inspection types TBD per permit conditions; the following are ASSUMPTION based on typical Alberta construction):
- Structural framing inspection (door frames, lintels) -- prior to concealment
- Rough-in inspection (conduit, drain provisions) -- prior to concealment
- Final building inspection -- upon completion

**Step 4.2 — Accompany Inspectors**
Ensure County project representative is present at all inspections (RFP S3.3.2; SOW-0084). Provide access and relevant IFC drawings to inspectors.

**Step 4.3 — Obtain and File Inspection Reports**
Obtain copies of all completed inspection reports. Provide copies to County project representative (SOW-0085). File in project records.

**Step 4.4 — Rectify Deficiencies**
If inspections identify deficiencies:

- **During construction phase:** Rectify within the timeframe specified by the inspector. ASSUMPTION: specific timeframe is per inspector authority under Alberta Safety Codes; the General Contractor should confirm the expected rectification timeframe at the time of inspection and document it. (Lensing X-003)
- **During guarantee period (post-CCC):** Rectify within 10 days of the Owner's written instruction to perform the work, per RFP S2.10.6.

Re-inspect as required.

**Note (Lensing X-003):** The construction-phase and guarantee-period rectification timeframes are governed by different authorities. During construction, the inspector's authority applies. During the guarantee period, the 10-day contractual requirement per RFP S2.10.6 applies. These are not interchangeable.

### Phase 5 — Documentation and Handoff

**Step 5.1 — Prepare Repair Bay Structural Installation Documentation**
Compile:
- Record of IFC drawing revisions used during construction
- Materials records (concrete, steel, door specifications, door manufacturer data sheets)
- Inspection reports
- Deficiency correction records (if any)
- As-built markups showing actual door rough opening dimensions and locations (Lensing E-002)

**Step 5.2 — Prepare Bay Door and Track Installation Records**
Compile:
- Overhead door manufacturer installation records
- Operator commissioning records
- Functional test records (three cycles per door per Step 3.4), including safety device and weather seal verification results
- Warranty documentation from door and operator manufacturer, confirming warranty period meets or exceeds project guarantee period per REQ-011-03-017 (Lensing F-003)

**Step 5.3 — Prepare Functional Testing Report**
Produce a functional testing report summarising the door operation test results (Step 3.4) and confirming all four overhead doors are operational, including:
- Safety device verification results for each door (REQ-011-03-015)
- Weather seal contact verification for each door (REQ-011-03-016)

**Step 5.4 — Compile Coordination Hold Point Records**
Compile sign-off records from:
- PKG-013 exhaust system provisions hold point (Step 2.4 / Lensing X-002)
- PKG-014 sump drain provisions coordination (Step 2.3)
- PKG-015 electrical coordination (Step 3.3)
- PKG-016 crane clearance coordination (Step 1.2)

**Step 5.5 — Handoff to General Contractor Completion Package**
Pass all documentation (Steps 5.1-5.4) to the project completion package for inclusion in:
- CCC documentation (DEL-020-02)
- As-built survey input (DEL-008-04)
- Guarantee period records (DEL-021-05)
