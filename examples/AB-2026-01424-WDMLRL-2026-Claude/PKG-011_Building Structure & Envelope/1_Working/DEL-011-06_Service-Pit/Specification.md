# Specification — DEL-011-06 Service Pit/Trench

---

## Scope

### In Scope
This deliverable covers the complete construction of the service pit/trench within the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill site, including:

- Excavation of the below-grade pit/trench void within the building footprint
- Structural concrete construction of pit walls, floor slab, and integration with the building floor system
- Waterproofing or damp-proofing at the pit base and wall/slab interface *(added per Lensing Item C-002)*
- Lining and finishing of the pit interior surfaces
- Installation of safety railings and edge protection at the pit perimeter
- Installation of pit cover/grating system (if required — scope status TBD, see Conflict Table C-011-06-04 in Guidance) *(enriched per Lensing Item E-001)*
- Rough-in provisions for ventilation (mechanical coordination with PKG-013)
- Rough-in provisions for lighting (electrical coordination with PKG-015)
- Rough-in provisions for pit floor drainage (plumbing coordination with PKG-014) — scope boundary TBD, see Conflict Table C-011-06-04 in Guidance *(enriched per Lensing Item E-002)*
- All associated labour, materials, and equipment required to achieve a completed, functional service pit/trench

**SOW Reference:** SOW-0028 — "Construct ventilated and lighted service pit/trench for under-equipment servicing" (RFP §3.4, App B)

### Out of Scope
- Structural design and engineering drawings for the pit (DEL-002-06 — PKG-002 Structural Design)
- Mechanical ventilation equipment installation (PKG-013)
- Electrical lighting fixture installation and wiring (PKG-015)
- Building foundation (PKG-010)
- Concrete superstructure above grade (DEL-011-01)
- Wash bay drainage and mud sump (PKG-018)
- Interior fit-out of adjacent spaces (PKG-012)

**Note on pit floor drainage scope:** The current Specification scope listing includes rough-in provisions for pit floor drainage. However, the scope boundary between this deliverable and PKG-006 (Plumbing Design) / PKG-014 (Plumbing & Water Systems Installation) for pit floor drainage is not explicitly defined in sources. This is recorded as Conflict Table item C-011-06-04 in Guidance. *(Per Lensing Item E-002)*

## Requirements

### REQ-011-06-01 — Function
The service pit/trench shall provide a below-grade working area that allows mechanics to stand and perform servicing operations on the underside of heavy maintenance equipment positioned above the pit opening.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 — "Ventilated and lighted service pit area for mechanics to perform servicing under equipment." | Decomposition SOW-0028.

### REQ-011-06-05 — Code Compliance
The service pit/trench construction shall comply with all applicable Alberta Safety Codes, including the Alberta Building Code and the Alberta Occupational Health and Safety (OHS) Code.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 ("The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes").

**Specific OHS requirements:**
- **Excavation safety (Alberta OHS Code Part 32):** Excavation and trenching safety measures are mandatory during construction. The contractor shall comply with Part 32 requirements for shoring, benching, or sloping of excavation walls based on soil conditions and excavation depth. This is an enforced regulatory mandate, not discretionary. *(Strengthened per Lensing Item C-001 — WeakStatement: normative document was less specific than operative document for excavation safety.)*
- **Confined space (Alberta OHS Code Part 5):** ASSUMPTION — the pit/trench may be classified as a confined space under Alberta OHS Code Part 5. **This classification requires confirmation** by the project safety officer against the Part 5 definition criteria. If classified as a confined space, mandatory entry programs, atmospheric monitoring, and rescue provisions apply during both construction and operational occupancy. If not classified as a confined space, this ASSUMPTION shall be removed and the associated compliance requirements shall not apply. *(Enriched per Lensing Item A-003 — TBD_Question: classification determination has material impact on compliance obligations.)*
- **Fall protection (Alberta OHS Code Part 9):** Fall protection provisions are required at the pit perimeter per Alberta OHS Code Part 9.
