# Procedure — DEL-011-06 Service Pit/Trench

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps required to construct and complete the service pit/trench for the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill. It covers the production of the physical deliverable — from pre-construction prerequisites through excavation, structural concrete construction, finishing, safety feature installation, and final verification — and identifies the records that must result.

## Steps

> **Note:** Steps below describe production of the service pit/trench. Specific dimensional values, material grades, and reinforcing details are TBD pending IFC structural drawings (DEL-002-06). No construction step shall proceed without the applicable IFC drawings being in force.

### Step 1 — Confirm IFC Drawings and MEP Rough-In Requirements

1.1 Obtain and review IFC structural drawings for the service pit/trench (DEL-002-06) from the structural engineer of record.

1.2 Confirm pit dimensions (depth, width, length), wall and floor construction details, reinforcing schedule, and interface with building slab from IFC drawings. Note the stated dimensional tolerances (see Specification REQ-011-06-09). *(Enriched per Lensing Item X-001.)*

1.3 Obtain confirmed rough-in requirements from mechanical contractor (ventilation sleeves/duct openings, per PKG-013) and electrical contractor (conduit, junction boxes, per PKG-015) and plumbing contractor (floor drain locations and connections, per PKG-014).

1.4 Confirm cover/railing system design and any embedded anchors or edge plates required from IFC structural/architectural drawings. Confirm whether a pit cover/grating system is required (see Specification REQ-011-06-11). *(Enriched per Lensing Item E-001.)*

1.5 Confirm waterproofing/damp-proofing details at pit base and wall/slab interface from IFC structural drawings (see Specification REQ-011-06-12). *(Added per Lensing Item C-002.)*

1.6 Record all confirmed requirements before proceeding.

**Verification:** IFC drawings on site; written confirmation of MEP rough-in requirements received from trade contractors; waterproofing details confirmed.

---

### Step 2 — Site Preparation and Layout

2.1 Confirm the building foundation (PKG-010) is complete in the area of the pit, and that the superstructure frame (DEL-011-01) sequencing permits safe pit excavation.

2.2 Establish the pit excavation boundary per IFC drawings. Mark layout on the site/slab subgrade.

2.3 Confirm all underground utilities within the excavation zone have been located and cleared. Underground utility locates before excavation are required per Alberta One-Call regulations and Alberta OHS Code Part 32. For this specific pit (excavation within the building footprint), the GC should confirm with the Owner and plumbing/electrical design teams whether underground utilities exist in the excavation zone. *(Enriched per Lensing Item F-002 — regulatory basis for utility locates now stated; see Guidance for full rationale.)*

2.4 Erect temporary barriers and safety signage around the excavation zone per Alberta OHS Code Part 32.

**Verification:** Layout checked against IFC drawings by GC superintendent; underground utilities cleared per Alberta One-Call confirmation.

---

### Step 3 — Excavation

3.1 Excavate to the plan dimensions and depths shown on the IFC structural drawings. Excavation tolerances per IFC drawings (ASSUMPTION: +/-25 mm is typical for structural concrete work if IFC drawings do not state a tolerance — confirm with structural engineer per Specification REQ-011-06-09). *(Enriched per Lensing Item X-001 — tolerance linked to Specification requirement.)*

3.2 Slope, bench, or shore excavation walls as required per Alberta OHS Code Part 32, based on soil classification and excavation depth. Comply with specific Part 32 requirements for shoring, benching, or sloping — this is a mandatory safety requirement, not discretionary. *(Enriched per Lensing Item C-001 — excavation safety language strengthened to match normative requirement.)*

3.3 Remove and dispose of excavated material per the project's waste management plan. ASSUMPTION: excavated material is clean soil/fill; if contaminated material is encountered, stop and notify Owner.

3.4 Proof-roll or inspect the excavation subgrade. Report any soft, wet, or unsuitable subgrade conditions to the structural engineer before proceeding.

**Verification:** Excavation depth and plan dimensions surveyed/measured against IFC drawings (tolerance per IFC or confirmed with structural engineer); OHS-compliant excavation wall support in place; subgrade inspection complete.

---

### Step 4 — Formwork and MEP Rough-In

4.1 Install formwork for the pit walls and floor slab per IFC structural drawings.

4.2 Install all MEP rough-ins within formwork before concrete placement:
- Electrical conduit, pull strings, and junction box rough-ins for lighting (coordinate with PKG-015 contractor)
- Ventilation sleeve openings or duct stub connections (coordinate with PKG-013 contractor)
- Floor drain body and piping rough-ins (coordinate with PKG-014 contractor) — scope boundary for drain rough-in TBD per Guidance Conflict Table C-011-06-04 *(enriched per Lensing Item E-002)*
- Any anchor bolts, edge plates, or embedded items for the cover/railing system

4.3 Install reinforcing steel per IFC structural drawings and the structural engineer's rebar schedule.

4.4 Obtain structural engineer's inspection/approval of reinforcing and formwork before concrete placement (ASSUMPTION: required per standard Alberta structural inspection practice for structural concrete elements).

**Verification:** MEP trade confirmation that rough-ins are correctly placed; structural engineer inspection sign-off on reinforcing; formwork inspection complete.

---

### Step 5 — Concrete Placement — Pit Walls and Floor

5.1 Place concrete to the mix design and placement requirements specified in the IFC structural drawings or project concrete specification (concrete mix design, slump, air content, pour sequence per DEL-002-06 / project quality plan). *(Enriched per Lensing Item A-002 — explicit linkage to DEL-002-06 and project concrete specification.)*

5.2 Consolidate concrete using internal vibration per standard practice.

5.3 Cure concrete per specification requirements (ASSUMPTION: minimum 7-day wet cure or as specified on IFC drawings, appropriate for Alberta climate conditions during construction period).

5.4 Protect concrete from freezing during cold-weather placements. Cold-weather concrete protection is required when ambient temperature is below +5 degrees C. Apply protection measures per CSA A23.1 cold-weather concreting requirements or ACI 306 (Guide for Cold Weather Concreting), as applicable. ASSUMPTION: applicable given Alberta climate — confirm whether the project concrete specification (via DEL-002-06 or project quality plan) includes cold-weather requirements, or whether CSA A23.1 provisions apply by default under the Alberta Building Code. See Guidance — Cold-Weather Concrete Protection for decision framework. *(Enriched per Lensing Item D-001 — normative basis and decision framework now referenced.)*

5.5 Strip formwork after achieving sufficient cure per structural engineer's direction.

**Verification:** Concrete test cylinders taken per project quality plan. Testing frequency: TBD — confirm per project quality plan or CSA A23.1 requirements (ASSUMPTION: minimum one set of cylinders per pour, tested at 7 and 28 days for compressive strength — this is a placeholder; the project quality plan or CSA A23.1 should be cited as the governing requirement for test frequency). *(Enriched per Lensing Item X-002 — Normalization: ASSUMPTION label on test frequency clarified; governing source identified.)* Placement logs completed; cold-weather protection log maintained if applicable.

---

### Step 6 — Pit Lining and Finishing

6.1 Apply pit interior lining/finishing to walls and floor as specified in IFC drawings (material and finish TBD).

6.2 ASSUMPTION: if a sealed or coated concrete finish is specified (e.g., epoxy or cementitious coating), prepare substrate per product manufacturer's instructions (grinding, cleaning, priming) before application.

6.3 Complete waterproofing or damp-proofing at the pit base and wall/slab interface per IFC structural drawing details and Specification REQ-011-06-12. *(Enriched per Lensing Item C-002 — now linked to normative requirement.)*

6.4 Inspect finished surfaces for defects (spalling, honeycombing, cracks) and repair as required per structural engineer's direction. Verify acceptance criteria per Specification REQ-011-06-10 (surface flatness, coating thickness, adhesion — criteria TBD pending material selection). *(Enriched per Lensing Item C-003 — acceptance criteria linked.)*

**Verification:** Visual inspection of finished surfaces against acceptance criteria (TBD per REQ-011-06-10); waterproofing installation verified per manufacturer requirements; material product data sheets on file; any repair work documented.

---

### Step 7 — Safety Railing, Edge Protection, and Cover Installation

7.1 Install safety railings and edge protection at the pit perimeter per the IFC drawings and Alberta OHS Code Part 9 fall protection requirements.

7.2 Railings shall achieve the required height and load resistance per Alberta OHS Code Part 9 (**location TBD** — code text not accessible in sources). ASSUMPTION: top rail at 1,070 mm (42 in.) height minimum, mid-rail at approximately half top-rail height, and toe board — confirm per IFC drawings and applicable OHS Code Part 9 requirements. *(Enriched per Lensing Item F-001 — benchmarks now linked to Specification REQ-011-06-06.)*

7.3 Removable sections or covers shall be installed at the equipment entry end of the pit per IFC drawings.

7.4 Install pit cover or grating system per IFC drawings and Specification REQ-011-06-11. The cover system is required (ASSUMPTION — see REQ-011-06-11 for status). Verify cover type, load rating, and functional operation. *(Enriched per Lensing Item E-001 — conditional "if specified" language replaced with reference to requirement; ASSUMPTION status noted.)*

**Verification:** Railing height (ASSUMPTION: minimum 1,070 mm per Alberta OHS Code Part 9) and load resistance checked; mid-rail and toe board present; cover/grating system installed and functional; installation report completed.

---

### Step 8 — Final Inspection and Commissioning Coordination

8.1 Conduct GC self-inspection of the completed pit against IFC drawings and specification requirements (REQ-011-06-01 through REQ-011-06-12). Compile a deficiency list and correct all items before requesting Owner/Authority inspections.

8.2 Request building inspection by the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) per RFP §3.3.2. Provide a copy of the inspection report to the Owner.

8.3 Coordinate with PKG-013 (Mechanical) and PKG-015 (Electrical) contractors to confirm that ventilation and lighting systems are commissioned and operational within the pit. Verify against measurable performance criteria (ventilation: air changes/contaminant levels per REQ-011-06-02; lighting: lux level per REQ-011-06-03) — acceptance thresholds TBD. *(Enriched per Lensing Items F-003, D-002.)*

8.4 Coordinate with PKG-014 (Plumbing) contractor to confirm floor drain(s) are connected, functional, and tested. Note: scope boundary for drain work TBD per Guidance Conflict Table C-011-06-04. *(Enriched per Lensing Item E-002.)*

8.5 Confirm the completed pit is available for downstream use by PKG-012 (Interior Construction & Fit-Out) and operational maintenance activities.

8.6 Complete as-built dimensional survey of the completed pit (see Specification Documentation item 5 and Guidance — As-Built Survey Rationale for confirmation status). *(Added per Lensing Item X-003.)*

**Verification:** AHJ inspection passed and report on file; ventilation and lighting operational (acceptance thresholds TBD); drains tested; Owner notified of completion; as-built dimensions recorded.
