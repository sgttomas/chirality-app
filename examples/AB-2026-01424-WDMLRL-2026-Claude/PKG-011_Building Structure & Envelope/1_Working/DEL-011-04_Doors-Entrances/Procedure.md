# Procedure — DEL-011-04 Entrance/Exit Doors

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps required to produce and close out the DEL-011-04 construction deliverable: supply and installation of separate pedestrian entrance/exit doors, frames, and hardware for the New North Shop addition, as required by SOW-0026 (RFP §3.1, R-01).

The procedure covers: receiving design inputs, procurement, pre-installation checks, installation, commissioning, and records closeout.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Receive and Review IFC Design Documents

1.1 Obtain the issued-for-construction Door & Window Schedule (DEL-001-07) and Architectural Specification (DEL-001-11) from the Architect (PKG-001).

1.2 Review door schedule for: door type, size (width × height), frame type, hardware set designation, fire-rating requirements (if any), accessibility provisions, and any special requirements (e.g., vision panels, kick plates, weather stripping specification).

1.3 Confirm that all entrance/exit door openings shown on IFC drawings correspond to structural openings to be provided under DEL-011-01. Flag any discrepancy to the Architect before proceeding.

1.4 Identify any submittals required by the Architectural Specification (shop drawings, product data, samples). Initiate submittal process per the project submittal schedule.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2, R-01; DEL-001-07; DEL-001-11; ASSUMPTION for submittal process.

---

### Step 2 — Submittal and Approval

2.1 Prepare and submit shop drawings and/or product data for door assemblies (door leaf, frame, hardware) as required by Architectural Specification DEL-001-11.

2.2 Obtain Architect's review and approval (or approved-as-noted response) before ordering materials.

2.3 Retain submittal records (submission, response, any revisions).

**Source:** ASSUMPTION: standard design-build construction process; Specification DEL-001-11 will govern specific submittal requirements (TBD).

**Note (Submittal obligation):** The conditionality of this step ("if required") has been removed. The Specification Documentation table lists shop drawings/submittals as an anticipated artifact, and standard design-build practice requires submittal review prior to procurement. If DEL-001-11 explicitly waives the submittal requirement for standard door assemblies, this step may be reduced in scope but should still be documented. TBD — confirm with DEL-001-11. [Enriched per D-001; source: Procedure.md Step 2, Specification Documentation table]

---

### Step 3 — Material Procurement

3.1 Procure door assemblies (door leaves, frames, hardware) in accordance with the approved submittals and Architectural Specification (DEL-001-11).

3.2 Confirm lead times for door frames, specialty hardware, and any fire-rated assemblies (if applicable) against the project schedule to ensure delivery before required installation date.

3.3 Upon receipt, inspect delivered materials against the approved submittals and purchase orders. Receiving inspection criteria shall include: [Enriched per D-002]
- Verify door model, size, and finish match the approved submittal for each scheduled opening
- Inspect door leaves, frames, and hardware for shipping damage (dents, bends, scratches, broken glazing if applicable)
- Verify fire-rating labels are present and legible on fire-rated assemblies (if applicable)
- Confirm hardware sets are complete per the approved schedule
- Document any damage or non-conformance and initiate replacement if required

**Source:** ASSUMPTION: standard procurement process consistent with RFP §3.3.2 and project schedule requirements; receiving inspection criteria per standard construction practice.

---

### Step 4 — Pre-Installation Verification of Structural Openings

4.1 Confirm with DEL-011-01 (Concrete Superstructure) superintendent that all structural openings intended for entrance/exit doors are complete.

4.2 Measure each opening for:
- Clear width and height against Door & Window Schedule dimensions
- Plumb and level (vertical and horizontal)
- Square (diagonal measurement check)
- Condition of opening edges (no protrusions, broken concrete, or reinforcement that would interfere with frame setting)

4.3 Document measurements. If any opening is out of tolerance per the IFC drawings and Architectural Specification, notify the Architect and structural team before proceeding. Note: Specific numeric tolerances for frame plumb, level, and square are TBD — refer to DEL-001-11 and/or door manufacturer's installation tolerances when available. [Enriched per A-002]

4.4 Verify rough-in for any electrical services at door locations (electric strikes, hold-open devices, access control) has been completed by PKG-015, if applicable.

**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md (DEL-011-01 upstream); ASSUMPTION for measurement protocol.

---

### Step 5 — Door Frame Installation

5.1 Install door frames per manufacturer's instructions and Architectural Specification (DEL-001-11), including:
- Setting frame in opening
- Shimming and securing frame to achieve plumb, level, and square condition
- Anchoring frame to structural opening per specification (anchor type and spacing TBD in DEL-001-11)
- Grouting or packing frame as required

5.2 For fire-rated assemblies: install in strict accordance with the ULC listing and manufacturer's instructions. Do not modify fire-rated components without Architect/Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) approval.

5.3 **HOLD POINT — Frame Installation Inspection:** Inspect installed frames before hanging door leaves. Verify plumb, level, square, and that frame dimensions match the door leaf dimensions. This inspection shall be documented with sign-off before proceeding to Step 6 (door hanging). A non-conforming frame found after door hanging requires costly rework. [Enriched per D-003; source: standard construction QA practice]

**Source:** ASSUMPTION: standard hollow metal door frame installation practice; specific requirements TBD in DEL-001-11.

---

### Step 6 — Door Leaf and Hardware Installation

6.1 Hang door leaves on frames per manufacturer's instructions.

6.2 Install hardware per Door & Window Schedule (DEL-001-07) hardware sets:
- Hinges (check specified hinge count and weight rating)
- Door closers
- Locksets / latches / panic hardware (if required for egress)
- Weather stripping (head, jambs, and door bottom)
- Threshold
- Any vision panels, kick plates, or other accessories

6.3 For accessible doors: verify hardware is lever-style or otherwise operable with a closed fist (per NBC/ABC accessibility requirements); verify threshold height does not exceed the applicable maximum.

**Source:** REQ-011-04-03 (Alberta Safety Code); REQ-011-04-04 (Accessibility); DEL-001-07; DEL-001-11.

---

### Step 7 — Caulking and Weatherproofing

7.1 Apply caulking at frame perimeter (exterior and interior faces) per specification (DEL-001-11). Caulk type and colour TBD in DEL-001-11.

7.2 Inspect weather stripping continuity around the full door perimeter. Confirm no visible gaps when door is in the closed and latched position.

7.3 Confirm threshold is properly sealed to the floor slab to prevent air and water infiltration.

**Source:** REQ-011-04-05 (Weather performance); ASSUMPTION: standard cold-climate construction practice.

---

### Step 8 — Operational Testing and Adjustment

8.1 Test each installed door assembly:
- Open and close freely through full range of travel without binding, dragging, or sticking
- Latch engages positively when door is closed
- Lock operates correctly (both inside and outside)
- Door closer returns door to closed and latched position from any open position
- Panic hardware (if installed) operates correctly
- Weather stripping seals without excessive resistance
- Door does not rattle in frame when closed

8.2 Adjust hinges, strikes, closers, and hardware as needed to achieve correct operation.

8.3 Record the result of testing for each door assembly (pass/adjust/fail).

**Quantified pass/fail criteria (TBD):** The following measurable criteria should be established when DEL-001-11 and manufacturer data are available: [Enriched per A-005]
- Maximum door closer return time (full open to latched): TBD seconds (per manufacturer specification)
- Maximum acceptable gap dimension for weather stripping (daylight test): TBD mm
- Maximum operating force for accessible hardware: per NBC/ABC accessibility requirements (typically 22 N for interior, TBD for exterior)
- Door leaf clearance to frame: TBD mm per manufacturer's installation guide
- Proposed authority: DEL-001-11 / door manufacturer installation manual

**Source:** REQ-011-04-07 (Operational testing); RFP §2.14.1, R-01.

---

### Step 8A — Temporary Protection of Installed Door Assemblies

8A.1 If remaining construction activities will continue after door installation (e.g., welding, concrete work, painting, mechanical/electrical rough-in), install temporary protection on installed door assemblies to prevent damage from: [Enriched per C-003]
- Welding spatter
- Concrete dust and debris
- Paint overspray
- Impact from material handling and equipment movement

8A.2 Protection measures may include protective film, cardboard sheathing, or other methods appropriate to the construction activities in progress. Remove protection only at final cleaning/handover.

8A.3 Inspect protected doors periodically and document any damage discovered during the construction period.

**Source:** ASSUMPTION: standard construction practice for protection of finish elements installed before project completion. Specific protection requirements TBD per project sequencing.

### Step 9 — Safety Code Inspection

9.1 Submit inspection request to the Safety Codes Officer (SCO) as required for the building permit and Safety Code permits.

9.2 Ensure the County project representative is present for all inspections (RFP §3.3.2, R-01).

9.3 Provide the County project representative with a copy of all completed inspection reports.

9.4 Address any deficiencies identified by the SCO and obtain re-inspection sign-off before claiming completion of this deliverable.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2, R-01.
