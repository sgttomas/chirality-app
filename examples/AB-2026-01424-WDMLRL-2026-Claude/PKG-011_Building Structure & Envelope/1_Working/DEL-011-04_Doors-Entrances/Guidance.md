# Guidance — DEL-011-04 Entrance/Exit Doors

---

## Purpose

DEL-011-04 exists to satisfy SOW-0026 — the requirement that the New North Shop addition be provided with separate pedestrian entrance/exit doors, as explicitly stated in RFP §3.1 (R-01): "The proposed building shall have separate entrance/exit doors."

This deliverable distinguishes pedestrian access from the large vehicle overhead doors (DEL-011-03, DEL-011-05) that dominate the building envelope. Separate entrance/exit doors serve personnel circulation, emergency egress, and code-required means of egress for an industrial occupancy with a 35' ceiling height and approximately 13,000 sq ft floor area. Operational doors in this deliverable are a prerequisite for downstream PKG-012 Interior Construction & Fit-Out to proceed (per declared dependency in _DEPENDENCIES.md).

The deliverable supports OBJ-001: deliver a code-compliant, fully functional maintenance shop addition that passes all applicable inspections (Decomposition §5, R-01).

---

## Principles

**1. Design-build sequencing: construction follows Architectural documents.**
This is a design-build project (CCDC 14–2013). The General Contractor does not independently determine door specifications — door types, sizes, quantities, hardware, and any fire-rating requirements are established by the Architect in DEL-001-07 (Door & Window Schedule) and DEL-001-11 (Architectural Specification). The construction deliverable (DEL-011-04) executes those decisions. Construction procurement and installation must wait for IFC documents from PKG-001.
Source: RFP §3.3.2 ("construct the building as per the Issued for Construction drawings"), R-01.

**2. Separate from overhead vehicle doors.**
The RFP explicitly calls for "separate" entrance/exit doors (RFP §3.1, R-01). The conceptual floor plan (Appendix B, R-04) shows the building has large overhead vehicle doors (24' wide bays for repair bays; 24' wash bay door) and a parts room roll-up door (6' wide, per SOW-0029, Decomposition §4). The pedestrian entrance/exit doors in this deliverable are a distinct element serving personnel movement, not vehicle access. This separation matters for egress planning, energy performance, security, and code compliance. [Corrected per B-003: previously stated "16'" — source confirms 6' per SOW-0029]

**3. Alberta Safety Code compliance is non-negotiable.**
RFP §3.3.2 states explicitly: "The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes." (R-01, p. 12.) All door installations must pass Safety Code inspections. The County project representative must be present for all inspections (RFP §3.3.2).

**4. Cold-climate performance.**
The project site is in central Alberta (near Ferintosh, SW 14–44–21–W4). Door assemblies, weather stripping, thresholds, and seals must be appropriate for Alberta's cold climate. ASSUMPTION: industrial-grade weather sealing is expected; specific thermal performance values will be confirmed in the Architectural Specification (DEL-001-11).

**5. Upstream dependency is a hard sequencing constraint.**
Door frames cannot be installed until structural openings are complete (DEL-011-01 Concrete Superstructure). Premature installation risks damage to door frames, misalignment, and rework. Verify opening dimensions, plumb, and level before commencing door installation.

## Considerations

**Quantity and placement of entrance/exit doors:**
The RFP and conceptual drawing (Appendix B, R-04) do not explicitly specify the number of pedestrian entrance/exit doors or their precise locations. The Appendix B floor plan does not dimension or label pedestrian entrance/exit doors separately from the overhead doors shown. The exact configuration is TBD pending the IFC Architectural Door & Window Schedule (DEL-001-07). ASSUMPTION: at minimum, the building will require at least one accessible main entrance and at least one secondary means of egress to satisfy the Alberta Building Code for an industrial occupancy of this size.

**Accessibility:**
Alberta Building Code (NBC as adopted) requires barrier-free (accessible) provisions for commercial/industrial buildings. At minimum, the principal entrance is typically required to be accessible (compliant clear opening width, level threshold or low threshold, operable hardware). ASSUMPTION: the Architect (PKG-001) will confirm accessibility provisions in DEL-001-07/DEL-001-11. The General Contractor should flag any site condition that may prevent compliant threshold installation.

**Fire rating:**
If the Architect's design includes fire compartmentation (e.g., between the main shop area and other occupancies such as the office, parts room, utility room, or washroom), some entrance/exit door assemblies may be required to be fire-rated (ULC-listed fire door assemblies with rated frames and hardware). ASSUMPTION: this is likely applicable for doors separating different occupancy groups or fire compartments. Specific requirements TBD in DEL-001-11.

**Hardware durability for industrial use:**
The building is a heavy equipment maintenance shop used by landfill operations staff. Door hardware (hinges, closers, locksets) should be specified for heavy-duty industrial use to withstand frequent use and harsh environmental conditions (dust, temperature extremes, potential for impact by equipment movement). ASSUMPTION: the Architect will specify appropriate hardware grade; General Contractor should flag if specified hardware is unsuitable for the operational environment.

**Coordination with electrical scope (PKG-015):**
If any entrance/exit doors are specified with electronic access control (card reader, electric strike, electromagnetic hold-open, etc.), the electrical rough-in must be coordinated with PKG-015 Electrical & Low-Voltage Installation. ASSUMPTION: electronic access control is possible given that security is not ruled out by the RFP; this is TBD in design documents.

**Relationship to Door & Window Schedule (DEL-001-07):**
DEL-001-07 (Architectural, PKG-001) explicitly covers SOW-0026 in addition to SOW-0011 (Decomposition §7). This is the primary design document governing this construction deliverable. The General Contractor should ensure DEL-001-07 is received and reviewed before ordering or fabricating door assemblies.

**Coordination with building envelope air/vapour barrier (PKG-011 envelope scope):**
Door frame perimeter caulking (this deliverable, Procedure Step 7) must integrate with the overall building envelope air barrier and vapour barrier system. This is a common coordination gap in construction where the door installer's caulking must tie into the envelope contractor's air barrier detailing to maintain continuity. Responsibility for the air barrier transition at door frames should be clearly assigned between this deliverable and the PKG-011 envelope scope. TBD — coordinate with PKG-011 envelope design / DEL-001-11. [Enriched per X-001; source: Guidance.md Considerations section, standard construction coordination practice]

**Energy code applicability (NECB):**
Exterior pedestrian doors in a heated industrial building in Alberta may be subject to National Energy Code of Canada for Buildings (NECB) or Alberta energy code requirements for thermal transmittance and air leakage. This should be confirmed when DEL-001-11 is issued. See also Specification Standards table. [Enriched per C-002; source: Specification.md Standards table, ASSUMPTION: NECB may apply]

**Installer qualifications:**
The Procedure assigns all door/frame/hardware installation work to the General Contractor, but does not address whether specific trade qualifications or manufacturer certifications are required. For fire-rated assembly installation, ULC listing requirements may mandate manufacturer-certified installers. ASSUMPTION: the General Contractor should verify whether journeyman carpenter or manufacturer certification is required, particularly for any fire-rated assemblies. TBD — proposed authority: DEL-001-11 / ULC listing requirements. [Enriched per F-002; source: Procedure.md Steps 5-6, Guidance.md]

**Guarantee scope — hardware adjustment vs. maintenance:**
The 2-year guarantee (REQ-011-04-08) covers "materials and workmanship," but doors in cold-climate industrial buildings commonly require seasonal re-adjustment due to normal settling and thermal movement. The boundary between warranty-covered adjustment and owner maintenance is unclear. This should be clarified in the context of CCDC 14-2013 warranty provisions. TBD — proposed authority: CCDC 14-2013 warranty provisions. [Enriched per F-003; source: Specification.md REQ-011-04-08]

## Trade-offs

| Trade-off | Option A | Option B | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Door material: steel vs. fibreglass | Hollow metal steel (heavy-duty, industry standard for industrial) | Fibreglass (better thermal performance, lighter) | ASSUMPTION: hollow metal steel is typical for industrial applications in Alberta; final decision by Architect in DEL-001-07 |
| Number of entrance/exit doors | Minimum per code (egress only) | Additional doors for operational convenience (multiple entry points for large workforce or equipment access corridors) | TBD by Architect; operational program should drive this decision |
| Closer type: surface-mounted vs. concealed | Surface-mounted (easier maintenance, heavy-duty options) | Concealed (cleaner appearance) | ASSUMPTION: surface-mounted preferred for industrial maintenance shop environment |
