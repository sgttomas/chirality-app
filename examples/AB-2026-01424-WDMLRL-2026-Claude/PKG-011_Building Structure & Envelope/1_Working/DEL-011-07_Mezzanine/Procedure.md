# Procedure — DEL-011-07 Mezzanine Structure & Stairs

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce (construct) the mezzanine structural frame, stairs, guardrails, and associated platform elements for the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project, in accordance with the P.Eng.-stamped IFC structural drawings, applicable Alberta Safety Codes, and the CCDC 14–2013 contract.

---

## Steps

### Step 1 — Confirm Structural Readiness of Supporting Elements
1. Verify that the concrete superstructure (DEL-011-01) is in place and sufficiently cured in the zones where mezzanine framing will bear or connect.
2. Confirm that any embedded anchor bolts, plates, or connection hardware specified in the IFC mezzanine framing drawings are correctly located, plumb/level, and match the IFC drawing coordinates.
3. Document condition of supporting elements with photographs and field notes.
4. If discrepancies are found between as-built superstructure and IFC drawing assumptions, notify the Structural Engineer of Record before proceeding. Do not proceed with mezzanine framing until discrepancies are resolved.

**Verification:** Field inspection sign-off; discrepancy log (if applicable).

---

### Step 2 — Procure Mezzanine Structural Materials
1. Procure structural steel framing members, connection hardware, deck materials, stair components, guardrail systems, and anchorage materials per the IFC structural drawings (DEL-002-05 and DEL-002-09) and specifications.
2. Verify material certifications (mill certs or equivalent) as required by the Structural Specification (DEL-002-12).
3. Confirm material delivery schedule aligns with the construction programme to meet the December 31, 2026 completion deadline. (Source: RFP §3.1)

**Verification:** Material receiving records; mill certificate file.

---

### Step 3 — Install Mezzanine Structural Frame
1. Set out and install mezzanine structural framing per IFC drawings (DEL-002-05), including:
   - Primary beams / girders bearing on or connecting to the concrete superstructure
   - Secondary framing members
   - Any columns or posts (if required by structural design)
   - Connection plates, bolts, welds as specified
2. Maintain plumb, level, and alignment tolerances per IFC drawing specifications. TBD — specific tolerances per structural specification (DEL-002-12). **Note (enriched per A-004):** Before commencing framing installation, confirm that the applicable tolerances are documented in the IFC package or structural specification (DEL-002-12). If tolerances are not stated in the IFC package, request them from the Structural Engineer of Record before proceeding. Do not proceed with framing without confirmed tolerances. Typical structural steel erection tolerances (e.g., plumb within +/- 1:500 of column height, beam level within +/- 3 mm) may serve as interim guidance — ASSUMPTION per standard practice; confirm with DEL-002-12.
3. Do not place mezzanine deck or permit loads on the mezzanine frame until the Structural Engineer of Record or designated inspector has inspected primary framing connections (if required by IFC documents or Safety Codes Officer).

**Step 3a — Mandatory Hold-Point: Load-Path Verification (added per A-002)**
4. After primary framing is installed and before proceeding to deck installation (Step 4), conduct a formal load-path verification:
   - The Structural Engineer of Record or designated inspector shall verify that the as-installed framing connections, bearing conditions, and load paths conform to the IFC structural drawings (DEL-002-05).
   - Document the verification with a signed inspection record.
   - Do not proceed to Step 4 until the load-path verification hold-point is cleared.
   - (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-002; Specification R-02 verification; ASSUMPTION: hold-point requirement derived from standard structural construction practice for load-bearing platforms.)

**Verification:** Field inspection by QC and, if required, by Safety Codes Officer; framing photograph record; load-path verification sign-off (Step 3a).

---

### Step 4 — Install Mezzanine Deck
1. Install mezzanine floor deck system per IFC structural drawings. Deck type (steel deck with concrete topping, or other) TBD per structural engineer — refer to DEL-002-05.
2. If concrete topping is specified, place and cure concrete per IFC specifications. Do not apply loads until concrete achieves specified compressive strength. **Acceptance criteria (enriched per D-002):** Compressive strength achievement shall be confirmed by one of the following methods as specified in the IFC structural specification (DEL-002-12): (a) cylinder break test (field-cured or lab-cured cylinders per CSA A23.1), (b) maturity method, or (c) Structural Engineer of Record sign-off based on submitted test data. The method and required strength value are TBD per DEL-002-12. Do not proceed with loading until the Structural Engineer of Record or designated inspector confirms the required strength has been achieved. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-002; CSA A23.1 — **location TBD**; ASSUMPTION: verification method derived from standard concrete practice.)
3. Confirm deck surface condition and level prior to handoff to PKG-012 (Interior Construction & Fit-Out) for any finish treatment. Note: scope boundary between structural deck and wearing surface is TBD — see Guidance.md Conflict Table CFT-011-07-02.

**Verification:** Concrete test reports (if applicable); concrete strength confirmation record (per D-002); field inspection sign-off; level survey.

---

### Step 5 — Install Stairs
1. Install stair stringers, treads, risers (if closed-riser), and landing structure per IFC stair drawings (DEL-002-09).
2. Confirm stair geometry (rise, run, width) complies with Alberta Safety Code requirements. TBD — specific dimensions per IFC drawings and Safety Codes Officer confirmation.
3. Confirm stair connection to mezzanine deck and to the building floor below is per IFC drawings.
4. Do not install handrails on stair until stair structure is confirmed level, plumb, and per drawings.

**Verification:** Dimensional check against IFC drawings; Safety Codes Officer inspection.

---

### Step 6 — Install Guardrails, Handrails, and Edge Protection
1. Install guardrails at all open edges of the mezzanine platform per IFC drawings and Alberta Safety Code requirements.
2. Install handrails on both sides of the stair run (ASSUMPTION: both sides required; confirm with Safety Codes Officer and IFC drawings).
3. Confirm guardrail height, post spacing, and load capacity per Alberta Safety Code requirements. TBD — specific requirements per Safety Codes Officer.
4. Install any required chain-gates or removable sections at mezzanine access openings per IFC drawings (if applicable — see Guidance.md Considerations re material handling access method, B-003).

**Verification:** Dimensional and load check; Safety Codes Officer inspection.

---

### Step 7 — Structural Inspection and Load Capacity Certification
1. Notify the County project representative prior to the structural inspection, as required. (Source: RFP §3.3.2 — County representative must be present at all inspections)
2. Arrange Safety Codes Officer inspection of the completed mezzanine structure and stairs.
3. Obtain load capacity certification from the Structural Engineer of Record or approved inspector confirming the mezzanine as-built meets the specified design load, in the format specified in Specification R-02 (enriched per F-001). (Source: _CONTEXT.md anticipated artifact: "Load capacity certification and inspections")
4. Provide copies of all completed inspection reports to the County. (Source: RFP §3.3.2 — SOW-0085)
5. Resolve any inspection deficiencies before proceeding to the next construction phase or formal handoff. **Deficiency tracking (enriched per F-003):** Maintain a deficiency register (punch list) for any inspection items requiring correction. Each deficiency entry shall record: (a) deficiency description, (b) location, (c) date identified, (d) responsible party for correction, (e) date corrected, and (f) verification of correction. Track all deficiencies to closure before CCC readiness sign-off (Step 8). The deficiency register format should align with the Project Quality Control Plan (DEL-019-04). (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-003; QC Plan DEL-019-04; ASSUMPTION: deficiency register format derived from standard construction QC practice.)

**Verification:** Inspection report copies filed; load capacity certification on record; deficiency register closed out (per F-003).

---

### Step 8 — Final Self-Inspection and Handoff Readiness
1. Conduct a final General Contractor self-inspection of the complete mezzanine structure and stairs (framing, deck, stairs, guardrails, connections) against the IFC drawings and specification checklist.
2. Confirm cleanliness and tidiness of the mezzanine zone in preparation for PKG-012 Interior Construction & Fit-Out work. (Source: RFP §2.14.1)
3. Notify PKG-012 team that structural mezzanine is ready for downstream fit-out activities (flooring finishes per _DEPENDENCIES.md downstream handoff).
4. Prepare and submit as-built documentation per Specification R-10 (enriched per E-002). As-built documentation is required regardless of whether deviations from IFC drawings occurred.
5. Confirm deficiency register (per F-003) is fully closed out before CCC readiness sign-off.

**Verification:** Self-inspection checklist completed; as-built documentation submitted; deficiency register closed.

## Records

The following records shall be produced and retained as part of this deliverable:

1. **Mezzanine structural installation documentation** — field records, photographs, and inspection notes confirming installation per IFC drawings. (Source: _CONTEXT.md anticipated artifacts)
2. **Stair and railing installation records** — dimensional records and photos confirming stair and guardrail installation. (Source: _CONTEXT.md anticipated artifacts)
3. **Load capacity certification and inspections** — formal certification from Structural Engineer of Record or approved inspector, in the format specified in Specification R-02 (enriched per F-001). (Source: _CONTEXT.md anticipated artifacts)
4. **Safety compliance testing reports** — Safety Codes Officer inspection reports for mezzanine structure and stairs. (Source: _CONTEXT.md anticipated artifacts; RFP §3.3.2)
5. **Material certifications** — mill certificates or equivalent for structural steel and concrete (if applicable).
6. **Welder qualification records** — CWB certification records for structural steel welders (per F-002), if structural steel welding is required.
7. **As-built documentation** — verified record of as-built conditions per Specification R-10 (enriched per E-002), submitted to Owner within 30 days of construction completion.
8. **Deficiency register** — punch list tracking all inspection deficiencies to closure (per F-003).
9. **Load-path verification record** — signed inspection record from Step 3a hold-point (per A-002).
10. **Concrete strength confirmation record** — test reports or engineer sign-off confirming compressive strength achievement (per D-002), if concrete topping is used.
11. **Copies to County** — all completed inspection reports to be provided to the County as required by RFP §3.3.2.
