# Specification — DEL-011-07 Mezzanine Structure & Stairs

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-011-07 covers the design-build construction of the mezzanine structural frame, stair access, railings, and associated platforms within the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project.

Specifically, this deliverable covers:
- Construction of the overhead mezzanine storage structure above the parts room, utility room, and wash bay (SOW-0032)
- Provision of a load-bearing mezzanine capable of storing heavy items such as oil totes and oil pumping equipment (SOW-0033)
- Construction of stairs providing access to the mezzanine level (SOW-0034)
- Guardrails, edge protection, and other safety elements associated with elevated platform access (ASSUMPTION: required by Alberta Safety Codes; extent TBD per structural design)

**Scope boundary note (enriched per D-001):** The structural mezzanine deck (bare slab / steel deck) is the deliverable endpoint of DEL-011-07. The question of whether a wearing surface or non-structural finish is included in this deliverable or falls to PKG-012 (Interior Construction & Fit-Out) is unresolved. See Guidance.md Conflict Table CFT-011-07-02 for the proposed resolution (PKG-011 provides structural deck to bare/structural finish; PKG-012 provides any non-structural wearing surface). This boundary must be confirmed by the Project Manager before construction. (Source: Specification Scope, Guidance Conflict Table CFT-011-07-02, Procedure Step 4)

### What This Deliverable Does Not Cover

- Foundation construction (PKG-010 — DEL-010-01)
- Main concrete superstructure (PKG-011 — DEL-011-01; upstream dependency)
- Steel plate floor embedments (PKG-011 — DEL-011-02; upstream dependency)
- Mezzanine flooring finishes and interior fit-out (PKG-012 Interior Construction & Fit-Out — downstream; scope boundary TBD per CFT-011-07-02)
- Structural engineering design drawings and calculations for the mezzanine (PKG-002 — DEL-002-05 Mezzanine Framing Details, DEL-002-09 Stair Details; design is a prerequisite)
- Lighting on or under the mezzanine (PKG-015 Electrical & Low-Voltage Installation)
- Plumbing elements in the mezzanine zone (PKG-014)
- Old North Shop mezzanine renovation (PKG-017 — DEL-017-02; separate deliverable)

## Requirements

### R-01: Mezzanine Location
The mezzanine structure shall be positioned overhead above the parts room, utility room, and wash bay of the New North Shop addition, consistent with the conceptual layout shown in Appendix B (Shop).

**Source:** RFP §3.4; App B (Shop) floor plan. **Verification:** Confirm against IFC structural drawings prior to construction.

---

### R-02: Load-Bearing Capability
The mezzanine structure shall be load-bearing and capable of handling heavy items, including multiple oil totes and oil pumping equipment.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Mezzanine storage above utility and parts room capable of handling heavy items such as several oil totes and oil pumping equipment"); App B note ("Load Bearing Over Parts Room + Utility For Storage"); Decomposition SOW-0033.

**Design Load:** TBD — specific design live load (kPa) shall be determined by the Structural Engineer of Record in the IFC structural drawings (DEL-002-05) and must meet or exceed the load demands of multiple oil totes and associated equipment. The Structural Engineer's determination of the design live load is binding for this requirement. ASSUMPTION: live load will likely exceed the minimum 2.4 kPa industrial storage floor load under NBCC; Guidance.md Examples section notes 4.8–7.2 kPa (100–150 psf) as a general industrial range for heavy storage mezzanines, but the actual design value must be confirmed by the Structural Engineer of Record. (Enriched per A-001: strengthened prescriptive force by making engineer's determination binding and cross-referencing the industrial range from Guidance.)

**Load Capacity Certification (enriched per F-001):** The load capacity certification required under this requirement shall be issued in writing by the Structural Engineer of Record or an approved third-party inspector, referencing the as-built configuration and confirming compliance with the IFC design loads. The certification document shall identify: (a) the design live load, (b) the as-built structural system verified, (c) a statement of compliance or non-compliance, and (d) the certifying engineer's P.Eng. stamp and signature. (Source: _CONTEXT.md anticipated artifact: "Load capacity certification and inspections"; ASSUMPTION: attestation format derived from standard engineering practice; specific format TBD per Structural Engineer of Record.)

**Verification:** Load capacity certification required per format above; structural engineer's stamp on IFC drawings confirms design compliance; mandatory hold-point for load-path verification prior to permitting operational loads (see Procedure Step 3a).

### R-03: Stair Access
Stairs shall be constructed to provide access to the mezzanine level.

**Source:** RFP App B (Shop) floor plan ("Stairs to Mezzanine"); Decomposition SOW-0034.

**Configuration:** Single stair run; location shown on App B floor plan near the utility room / water storage zone. Exact configuration (width, rise/run, handrail arrangement) TBD per IFC structural drawings (DEL-002-09) and Alberta Safety Codes.

**Verification:** Physical inspection confirming stair installation complete per IFC drawings; Safety Codes Officer inspection acceptance.

### R-04: Code Compliance
The mezzanine structure and stairs shall comply with all applicable Alberta building codes, regulations, and Alberta Safety Codes.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 ("The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes"); RFP §3.3.2 ("The building design must adhere to all applicable building codes and regulations").

**Applicable Codes:** Alberta Building Code (ABC); Alberta Safety Codes Act and applicable Safety Codes (edition current at time of permit issuance). ASSUMPTION: National Building Code of Canada (NBC) forms the base code adopted by Alberta; specific edition TBD per permit authority. **Note (enriched per A-003):** Specific code clause references applicable to the mezzanine (e.g., ABC/NBC Part 4 structural design provisions, Part 3 means of egress for stairs and guardrails) should be confirmed by the Structural Engineer of Record and Safety Codes Officer at permit stage and recorded in this section. Until confirmed, clause-level references remain TBD. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-003; Alberta Building Code / NBC — **location TBD**.)

**Verification:** Building permit and Safety Code permit coverage; Safety Codes Officer inspection sign-off.

### R-05: P.Eng.-Stamped IFC Drawings as Prerequisite
Construction of the mezzanine structure shall not commence prior to receipt of P.Eng.-stamped Issued for Construction (IFC) structural drawings covering mezzanine framing details (DEL-002-05) and stair details (DEL-002-09).

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 ("All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta").

**Verification:** Confirm IFC drawing package with P.Eng. stamp is on file before construction proceeds.

### R-06: Integration with Concrete Superstructure
The mezzanine structural frame shall be designed and installed to transfer loads to the main concrete superstructure (DEL-011-01) and/or utilize steel embedment attachment points (DEL-011-02) as required by the Structural Engineer of Record.

**Source:** ASSUMPTION — derived from building system context (concrete structure per RFP §3.4, App B); connection details TBD per structural engineer.

**Verification:** IFC structural drawing review; field inspection of connection installation.

---

### R-07: Guardrails and Edge Protection
Guardrails, handrails, and edge protection shall be installed at all open sides of the mezzanine platform and on both sides of the stair run where required by applicable Alberta Safety Codes.

**Source:** ASSUMPTION — Alberta Safety Codes require fall protection / guardrails at elevated work platforms; specific code clause TBD per Safety Codes Officer review.

**Verification:** Safety Codes Officer inspection; field verification against IFC drawings.

---

### R-08: Completion Deadline
All construction work associated with this deliverable shall be completed on or before December 31, 2026.

**Source:** RFP §3.1 ("All Work pertaining to the Design Build... must be completed on or before December 31, 2026").

**Verification:** Construction completion documentation; CCC readiness sign-off.

### R-09: Guarantee Period
All materials and workmanship for the mezzanine structure and stairs are guaranteed for the construction period plus 2 years following the Construction Completion Certificate (CCC).

**Source:** RFP §2.10.2. Defects to be rectified within 10 days of Owner's written instruction (RFP §2.10.6).

**Verification:** Contractual; tracked via CCC and guarantee period obligations under CCDC 14–2013. **Note (enriched per X-003):** A proactive structural inspection of the mezzanine framing connections and deck should be conducted prior to guarantee period expiry (CCC + 24 months) to identify latent defects such as connection loosening, deflection, or deck cracking. Findings shall be documented and any defects addressed within the guarantee period. TBD — confirm whether the contract administrator or Structural Engineer of Record performs this inspection per CCDC 14-2013 provisions. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-003; CCDC 14-2013 — **location TBD**; ASSUMPTION: proactive guarantee-period inspection is standard practice for load-bearing structures.)

### R-10: As-Built Documentation (added per E-002)
As-built documentation shall be prepared for the mezzanine structure and stairs and submitted to the Owner within 30 days of construction completion. As-built documentation shall identify: (a) any deviations from IFC drawings, (b) confirmation that the as-built configuration matches IFC drawings where no deviations occurred, and (c) a record of field-verified connection details. This requirement applies regardless of whether deviations occurred, to provide a verified conformance record confirming the as-built condition matches the design intent.

**Source:** ASSUMPTION — derived from standard construction practice and contract requirements (CCDC 14-2013 — **location TBD**); _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md E-002 identified that the prior as-built documentation was conditional only on deviations occurring (Procedure Step 8 item 4), leaving no verified conformance record when the as-built matched IFC. (Source: Procedure.md Step 8 item 4; Specification.md Documentation section.)

**Verification:** As-built documentation package submitted to Owner; reviewed by Structural Engineer of Record if deviations noted.
