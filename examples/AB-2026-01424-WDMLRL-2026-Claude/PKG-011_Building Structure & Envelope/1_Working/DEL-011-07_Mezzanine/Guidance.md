# Guidance — DEL-011-07 Mezzanine Structure & Stairs

---

## Purpose

DEL-011-07 exists to deliver a load-bearing mezzanine storage level within the New North Shop addition, spanning above the parts room, utility room, and wash bay. The mezzanine is a specifically called-out program element in the RFP because the County requires elevated, heavy-load-capable storage for operational materials — primarily oil totes and pumping equipment — that must be accessible but kept off the main shop floor.

The stairs exist to provide safe, code-compliant personnel access to the mezzanine storage level.

This deliverable is part of PKG-011 Building Structure & Envelope and represents one of the more operationally specific structural elements: unlike the superstructure (DEL-011-01), which is a general capacity element, the mezzanine is a defined functional space with a stated use and load requirement.

**Supports OBJ-001:** Deliver a code-compliant, fully functional maintenance shop addition that satisfies the County's operational program. (Source: Decomposition §5, §8 — SOW-0032, SOW-0033, SOW-0034 -> OBJ-001)

### Value Rationale (enriched per C-003)

The mezzanine is a net-positive investment for the project because it recovers usable floor space in the main shop area by relocating heavy storage items (oil totes, pumping equipment) to an elevated level. Without the mezzanine, these items would occupy ground-floor space needed for vehicle maintenance operations. The mezzanine avoids the need for a larger building footprint to accommodate the same storage capacity, which would increase both construction cost and land-use footprint. The County's operational need for accessible heavy-item storage is explicitly stated in the RFP (§3.4), making this a program-driven requirement rather than a design optimization. (Source: RFP §3.4; App B; ASSUMPTION: value-engineering rationale derived from standard practice for industrial mezzanines and the stated program.)

## Principles

### 1. Function-First Structural Design
The mezzanine's structural design must be driven by its stated function: heavy-item storage (oil totes, pumping equipment). The structural engineer should size the mezzanine floor system for a design live load comfortably exceeding the actual expected loads, with appropriate safety factors per the applicable code. Do not under-design to save material — the County has explicitly stated a need for heavy-item storage capability. (Source: RFP §3.4; App B)

### 2. Integration with the Concrete Superstructure
The New North Shop addition is a concrete structure with a 35' ceiling (RFP §3.4; App B). The mezzanine frame must integrate cleanly with this superstructure. Connections, bearing conditions, and load paths should be clearly defined in the IFC structural drawings (DEL-002-05) before any field work begins. The upstream dependency on DEL-011-01 (Superstructure) and DEL-011-02 (Steel Embedments) must be satisfied before mezzanine framing proceeds.

### 3. Safety Code Compliance is Non-Negotiable
The RFP explicitly requires adherence to Alberta Safety Codes (RFP §3.3.2). The mezzanine and stair design must be reviewed and accepted by the Safety Codes Officer. Stair geometry (rise, run, width), guardrail heights, and edge protection must meet code requirements. This is a code compliance matter, not a preference.

### 4. IFC Drawings Lead Construction
Per RFP §3.3.2, all IFC drawings must be P.Eng.-stamped before construction. The mezzanine and stair structural details (DEL-002-05 and DEL-002-09) are prerequisites for DEL-011-07 construction. The General Contractor must not begin mezzanine framing without approved IFC documents in hand.

### 5. Scope Boundary Clarity
The mezzanine structural frame and stairs fall under PKG-011 (this deliverable). Mezzanine flooring finishes and fit-out are downstream in PKG-012. The General Contractor must coordinate handoff with the interior fit-out team to avoid scope gaps (e.g., who provides the mezzanine deck surface vs. who provides the wearing surface or non-slip treatment). This boundary is not explicitly resolved in the RFP — see Conflict Table below.

## Considerations

### Span and Layout
The App B floor plan shows the mezzanine spanning above the parts room (~400 sq ft), utility room, and wash bay (24' wide). This is a multi-zone span. The structural engineer will need to determine whether the mezzanine frame is supported by the concrete walls between these rooms, by dedicated columns, or by the perimeter walls only. Column placement (if any) affects the floor plan of the spaces below and should be coordinated with the architectural team early. (Source: App B floor plan; RFP §3.4)

### Wash Bay Span
The wash bay is specifically mentioned as one of the zones beneath the mezzanine (App B: "Overhead Mezzanine over Parts Room/Utility Room/Wash Bay"). The wash bay services motor-scraper-sized equipment — very large vehicles. The structural engineer must confirm that any mezzanine support columns or framing within the wash bay zone do not conflict with wash bay vehicle access and overhead door operation. (Source: App B floor plan; RFP §3.1, §3.4)

ASSUMPTION: The mezzanine may not extend over the full wash bay bay width, or may be designed with long-span framing to avoid interference with the wash bay overhead door and vehicle clearance envelope. This should be confirmed in preliminary structural design.

**Value implications of mezzanine extent over wash bay (enriched per D-003):** The resolution of CFT-011-07-01 (mezzanine extent over wash bay) has direct value implications for the County. A full-span mezzanine over the wash bay maximizes elevated storage capacity (County value: more storage, fewer ground-floor constraints). A partial-span mezzanine preserves maximum wash bay operational clearance for motor-scraper-sized equipment (County value: unrestricted wash bay operations). Resolving this conflict early — ideally at preliminary design review — preserves both values by allowing the structural engineer to optimize the framing approach (e.g., long-span with no columns vs. partial coverage) before detailed design is committed. Deferring the decision risks late-stage redesign costs. (Source: RFP §3.4; App B; ASSUMPTION: value analysis derived from project context.)

### Load Path to Foundation
Oil totes (typically 1,000 L / ~1,000 kg each when full) and pumping equipment are heavy concentrated loads. The load path from the mezzanine frame to the superstructure and then to the foundation must be engineered to ensure the foundation (DEL-010-01) can accommodate the additional point loads. Foundation design should be informed by mezzanine loading requirements. This is a coordination item between PKG-002 (Structural Design) and PKG-010 (Foundation). (Source: ASSUMPTION — derived from project structure)

### Stair Location
App B shows the stair near the utility room / 50,000L water storage area. The stair must be positioned to provide clear access without conflicting with the cistern (DEL-014-01) or other utility equipment in that zone. Exact stair placement should be confirmed during architectural/structural coordination. (Source: App B floor plan)

### Guardrail and Fall Protection
The mezzanine platform edge and open sides of the stair require guardrails. These must meet Alberta Safety Code requirements. The design should consider:
- Loading dock / material handling access (will forklifts or pallet jacks access the mezzanine? If so, chain-gates or removable sections may be needed instead of fixed rails)
- ASSUMPTION: Materials are hand-carried or manually placed; forklift access to the mezzanine is not indicated in the RFP

**Material handling access question (enriched per B-003):** TBD — confirm with the Owner (Camrose County) whether forklift or mechanical lift access to the mezzanine is required or whether manual handling is assumed. This operational intent question affects: (a) guardrail design (fixed rails vs. chain-gate or removable sections per R-07), (b) floor deck load requirements (point loads from forklifts differ from distributed storage loads per R-02), and (c) the stair vs. ramp access decision (R-03). Until confirmed, the ASSUMPTION of manual handling governs. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md B-003; RFP §3.4 — no forklift access indicated; ASSUMPTION.)

### Warranty Obligations
Structural workmanship and materials are covered under the 2-year guarantee period post-CCC (RFP §2.10). Any deflection, settlement, or connection failures discovered during this period must be rectified within 10 days of Owner's written instruction (RFP §2.10.6).

## Assumption Resolution Governance (added per C-001)

Multiple requirements and attributes across the production documents are tagged **ASSUMPTION** (e.g., structural system, floor deck type, guardrail code clause, stair geometry, live load range, CSA standards applicability). This section establishes the governance process for resolving these assumptions:

1. **Who confirms:** Each ASSUMPTION is associated with a **Proposed Authority** (identified in the Enrichment Logs and Conflict Table). The Proposed Authority is the party or source expected to provide the confirming information. Common authorities for this deliverable include:
   - Structural Engineer of Record (structural system, loads, connections, CSA standards)
   - Safety Codes Officer (code clause applicability, stair geometry, guardrail requirements)
   - Owner / Camrose County (operational intent, material handling method)
   - Project Manager (scope boundaries, schedule)

2. **When confirmed:** Assumptions should be resolved at the following project stages:
   - **Preliminary design review** — structural system, mezzanine extent (CFT-011-07-01), material handling method (B-003)
   - **IFC drawing issuance** — design loads, stair dimensions, guardrail details, connection details, material specifications
   - **Permit stage** — code clause references (A-003), Safety Code requirements

3. **How recorded:** When an ASSUMPTION is confirmed or rejected, update the relevant production document (Datasheet, Specification, Guidance, or Procedure) by:
   - Replacing the ASSUMPTION tag with the confirmed value and its source
   - Recording the confirmation in the document's Enrichment Log or a dedicated resolution log
   - If an assumption is rejected, update the affected requirements and note the impact

(Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md C-001; ASSUMPTION: governance process derived from standard design-build practice; specific project-level procedures TBD per Project Manager / Design-Build team.)
