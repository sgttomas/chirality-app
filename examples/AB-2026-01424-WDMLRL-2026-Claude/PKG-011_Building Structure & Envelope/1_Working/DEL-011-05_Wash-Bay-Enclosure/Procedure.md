# Procedure — DEL-011-05 Wash Bay Enclosure

**Deliverable ID:** DEL-011-05
**Deliverable Name:** Wash Bay Enclosure
**Package:** PKG-011 Building Structure & Envelope
**Revision:** Draft P3 — 2026-02-26

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and install the Wash Bay Enclosure as a construction deliverable within the New North Shop addition. The procedure covers the full arc from design confirmation through physical construction to inspection acceptance. It is oriented to the General Contractor as the responsible party.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Review IFC Drawings and Coordination Package

1.1 Obtain the current revision of IFC architectural and structural drawings for the wash bay enclosure from the design team.

1.2 Confirm the following on IFC drawings before any work proceeds:
- Wash bay clear opening width: 24 ft (REQ-011-05-002)
- Overhead door width and height (REQ-011-05-003) — **door height must be confirmed; it is TBD in the RFP** [A-001]
- Wash bay clear ceiling height — accounting for mezzanine floor structure above (REQ-011-05-006a) [C-001]
- Steel plate location(s) and embedment details (REQ-011-05-005; refer to DEL-002-08)
- Wall construction type and materials [B-003]
- Floor drainage slope direction and minimum gradient (REQ-011-05-005a; coordinate with plumbing design) [F-002]

1.3 Obtain confirmed drain sleeve locations and floor drainage slope from plumbing subcontractor (coordination for DEL-018-05 / SOW-0027b). Document agreed locations and slope in a coordination sketch or RFI response before proceeding.

1.4 Confirm conduit stub-out or sleeve locations for electrical rough-in (overhead door operator, lighting) with electrical subcontractor (PKG-015) per the electrical coordination protocol. Document confirmation. [X-002]

1.5 Record all coordination confirmations. Note any discrepancies between IFC drawings and site conditions and resolve via RFI/Architect direction before proceeding.

1.6 If IFC drawings are unavailable for specific elements at the time work is scheduled to begin, initiate the IFC delay contingency protocol: submit RFI to design team identifying the missing information, notify the contract administrator, and do not proceed with affected elements until direction is received. Document the delay and its cause in the coordination log. [D-001]

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; REQ-011-05-008.

---

### Step 2 — Prepare Wash Bay Floor (Slab on Grade or Structural Slab)

2.1 Confirm foundation and subgrade are complete and approved at the wash bay location (coordinate with DEL-010-01 completion).

2.2 Place formwork for the wash bay floor slab, consistent with IFC structural drawings. Incorporate the required drainage slope (direction and minimum gradient per plumbing IFC drawings) into the formwork layout. Confirm that steel plate embedment elevations are coordinated with the sloped slab surface so that plates remain at the correct elevation relative to the finished floor. [F-002]

2.3 Install drain sleeves, pipe stubs, and any cast-in-place drain bodies at confirmed locations per plumbing IFC drawings. Confirm locations with plumbing subcontractor before any concrete is placed. Record as-placed locations.

2.4 Install steel plate embedment(s) per the structural embedment plan (DEL-002-08). Confirm plate size, thickness, material grade, and anchor specifications with Structural Engineer before placement. Record plate locations and elevations. [B-001]

2.5 Install any electrical conduit stubs through slab as required by electrical IFC drawings, per the electrical coordination protocol. [X-002]

2.6 **HOLD POINT — Pre-Pour Embedded Items Inspection.** Before proceeding with the concrete pour, all embedded items (steel plates, drain sleeves, electrical conduit stubs) shall be inspected and accepted. **Sign-off authority: GC site superintendent and Camrose County inspector** (per RFP §3.3.2 — County representative must be present at all inspections). Do not proceed with the pour until sign-off is obtained and documented. [C-003]

2.7 Pour concrete slab per IFC structural specifications. Minimum concrete cure duration before loading or further construction: TBD — refer to structural specification or concrete mix design for minimum cure time. If no specification is available, obtain direction from Structural Engineer before proceeding. [A-004]

**Source:** RFP §3.4, App B (R-04), REQ-011-05-004, REQ-011-05-005, REQ-011-05-005a, REQ-011-05-009.

---

### Step 3 — Construct Wash Bay Walls and Overhead Door Opening

3.1 Erect wash bay enclosure walls per IFC architectural and structural drawings, consistent with the overall building structure. Wall construction type and material per IFC drawings (TBD at draft stage — see Datasheet). [B-003]

3.2 Form and construct the overhead door rough opening at the correct location and to the correct dimensions (width 24 ft; height per IFC drawings — confirmed from Step 1.2). Install structural header or lintel above the door opening as required by structural drawings.

3.3 Install any wall penetrations, sleeves, or blockouts for:
- Drainage pipe connection to exterior mud sump (east wall, coordinate with DEL-018-05)
- Electrical conduit for overhead door operator and wall-mounted items (coordinate with PKG-015 per electrical coordination protocol) [X-002]

3.4 Tie walls into the existing superstructure (DEL-011-01) per structural IFC drawings. Coordinate with the superstructure construction team.

**Source:** RFP §3.1, SOW-0027a, REQ-011-05-002, REQ-011-05-004.

---

### Step 4 — Roof Integration and Mezzanine Structural Provisions

4.1 Construct the roof over the wash bay integrated with the overall New North Shop roof system per IFC drawings. Ensure the wash bay roof provides a weathertight enclosure.

4.2 Install structural provisions (connections, bearing elements) at the wash bay walls to receive the mezzanine framing (DEL-011-07). Confirm connection details with Structural Engineer before installation. Do not proceed with mezzanine framing installation under this deliverable — those activities are in DEL-011-07. Note: the precise interface boundary between DEL-011-05 structural provisions and DEL-011-07 mezzanine framing is subject to Conflict Table C-02 resolution.

4.3 Ensure any roof penetrations required for HVAC or ventilation (PKG-013) are blocked out per mechanical IFC drawings. Coordinate with mechanical subcontractor.

4.4 If gantry is confirmed by Owner/equipment design team: install any structural provisions required for gantry mounting in enclosure walls and/or roof per IFC drawings. [B-002, X-003]

**Source:** RFP §3.4, App B (R-04), REQ-011-05-004, REQ-011-05-006.

---

### Step 5 — Install Overhead Door and Hardware

5.1 Install the overhead door assembly (door panel, tracks, springs, and motorized operator — ASSUMPTION: motorized [A-002]) per manufacturer's instructions and IFC drawings.

5.2 Install header and jamb framing consistent with the structural drawings and door manufacturer's requirements.

5.3 Ensure rough-in for electrical door operator is in place (coordinate with PKG-015 for final connection per electrical coordination protocol). [X-002]

5.4 Test the door for smooth operation, correct seating at the sill, and proper weathersealing.

**Source:** RFP SOW-0027a, REQ-011-05-003.

---

### Step 6 — Coordinate with MEP Trades for Final Rough-In

6.1 After enclosure shell is complete, confirm with plumbing subcontractor (DEL-018-05 / PKG-014) that all drain sleeve locations are accessible and correctly positioned.

6.2 Confirm with electrical subcontractor (PKG-015) that all conduit stubs and rough-in points for wash bay lighting and door operator are correctly positioned and accessible, per the electrical coordination protocol. [X-002]

6.3 Document any deviations from IFC drawings and obtain Architect/Engineer direction before closing in any elements. Record all deviations in the as-built documentation. [X-001]

**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md, REQ-011-05-009.

---

### Step 7 — Self-Inspection Before County Inspection

7.1 Conduct a complete self-inspection of the wash bay enclosure against the IFC drawings and the requirements in this document. Use the Verification table in Specification.md as the checklist.

7.2 Confirm all coordination items with MEP subcontractors are complete or documented.

7.3 Conduct pre-CCC quality inspection of elements most likely to require warranty claims during the guarantee period: overhead door mechanism and operator, weatherseals at door sill/jambs and wall/roof junctions, steel plate embedment integrity. Document findings. [X-004, D-002]

7.4 Prepare inspection request for County project manager per RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0084). The County representative must be present at all inspections.

7.5 Correct all identified deficiencies before County inspection.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2, §2.14, SOW-0084, SOW-0085.

---

### Step 8 — County Inspection and Sign-Off

8.1 Coordinate and attend County inspector and/or building official inspection of the wash bay enclosure, including:
- Structural framing and floor slab inspection (before close-in)
- Overhead door inspection
- Building envelope inspection
- Code compliance inspection (Alberta Building Code applicable parts [A-003], Safety Codes)
- OH&S compliance confirmation [F-001]

8.2 Provide copies of all completed inspection reports to County project manager per SOW-0085.

8.3 Rectify any deficiencies identified at inspection promptly. Re-inspect as required.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2, SOW-0084, SOW-0085.
