## Conditions

### Trigger / Timing
- Closeout documentation compilation is initiated concurrent with final construction phases and continues through final inspection and CCC issuance. (Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md, Dependency Notes)
- Lien holdback (10% of contract value, per RFP §2.13.2) is released only upon submission of the three explicitly required documents (SOW-0094, SOW-0095, SOW-0096) together with a signed CCC. (Source: R-01 §2.13.2)

### Prerequisite States (Upstream Dependencies)

| Dependency | Deliverable | Required Input | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Commissioning complete | DEL-020-01 | Commissioning reports for archive | _DEPENDENCIES.md |
| Final inspection complete, CCC issued | DEL-020-02 | CCC and inspection records | _DEPENDENCIES.md |
| QC records available | PKG-019 (DEL-019-04) | Quality control documentation | _DEPENDENCIES.md |
| Warranty documents available | PKG-021 (DEL-021-05) | Guarantee period obligation documents | _DEPENDENCIES.md |

### Governing Contract Conditions
- Contract Value: TBD — to be recorded once known. The lien holdback is 10% of this value, making it essential for financial tracking in the holdback release process. (Source: R-01 §2.13.2; Lensing Item B-001)
- Guarantee Period: Construction period plus two (2) years following CCC issuance. (Source: R-01 §2.10.2)
- Deficiency rectification during guarantee period: within 10 days of Owner's written instruction. (Source: R-01 §2.10.6)
- Deficiency Holdback: Owner may retain an amount equal to estimated value of outstanding deficiencies; released upon correction and Owner approval. (Source: R-01 §2.11)
- Lien holdback: 10% of value of Work performed, per Prompt Payment and Construction Lien Act (Alberta); released upon completion, correction of deficiencies, and submission of required closeout documentation. (Source: R-01 §2.13.2)
