# Procedure — DEL-020-03: Closeout Documentation
**Project:** 2026 Design Build — West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition
**Deliverable ID:** DEL-020-03_Closeout-Docs
**Document Type:** Procedure (operational)
**Revision:** R1 — 2026-02-26 (4_DOCUMENTS Pass 3 — Semantic Lensing enrichment)

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps for the Project Manager to produce and assemble the closeout documentation package for DEL-020-03. The procedure covers:

1. The three contractually mandatory documents required for lien holdback release (SOW-0094, SOW-0095, SOW-0096 per RFP §2.13.2).
2. The broader closeout documentation compilation described in _CONTEXT.md (ASSUMPTION: as-built drawings, O&M manuals, warranty documentation, commissioning reports, inspection records, and final project archive).

This procedure governs production of the deliverable by the Project Manager. It does not govern the Owner's actions (CCC issuance is the Owner's responsibility per RFP §2.14.3).

**Terminology note:** Throughout this procedure, "final completion," "project completion," and "CCC issuance" all refer to the same event: the Owner's signing of the Construction Completion Certificate. See Specification Terminology table for formal definitions.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Confirm CCC Issuance and Deficiency Resolution
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**Timing:** Immediately upon receiving CCC from Owner (expected concurrent with DEL-020-02 completion)

1.1 Confirm that the Owner (Camrose County) has issued the signed Construction Completion Certificate (CCC). Obtain a copy for the project archive.
- Source: RFP §2.14.3; RFP §2.13.2.1
- Record: CCC date — this date initiates the two (2)-year guarantee period. (Source: RFP §2.10.2)

1.2 Confirm that all known deficiencies have been corrected and approved by the Owner. Confirm that any Deficiency Holdback amounts are tracked separately from lien holdback. (Source: RFP §2.11)

1.3 Confirm that the Deficiency Holdback (if any) is being addressed in parallel through the deficiency correction process. TBD: confirm whether unresolved Deficiency Holdback blocks lien holdback release (see Guidance — Deficiency Holdback vs. Lien Holdback; Lensing Item X-004).

**Output of Step 1:** Confirmed CCC on file; deficiency status documented; CCC date recorded for guarantee period calculation.

---

### Step 2 — Prepare Payment Claim Letter (SOW-0094)
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**Source:** RFP §2.13.2.2

2.1 Draft a letter on company letterhead, addressed to Camrose County (Owner), stating that the Proponent has submitted all claims for payment related to the contract.

2.2 Confirm with the finance/accounting team that all invoices and payment claims have in fact been submitted. Do not sign the letter until this is verified.

2.3 Have the letter signed by an authorized representative of the Proponent (i.e., a principal or officer with authority to bind the company).

2.4 Retain a signed copy for the project archive.

**Output of Step 2:** Signed Payment Claim Letter ready for submission.

---

### Step 3 — Obtain WCB Letter of Clearance (SOW-0095)
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**Source:** RFP §2.13.2.3

3.1 Submit a clearance request to WCB Alberta for the Proponent and confirm clearance for all subcontractors engaged on the project.
- ASSUMPTION: WCB clearance must cover the project account and all workers/subcontractors; confirm with WCB Alberta regarding scope of coverage required for this contract.

3.2 Confirm the WCB account is in good standing with no outstanding premiums or assessments.

3.3 Receive the Letter of Clearance from WCB Alberta. Confirm that:
- The letter is dated after CCC issuance or within an acceptable proximity to the submission date. (TBD: confirm acceptable dating window per CCDC 14-2013 and WCB Alberta requirements — Lensing Item A-003)
- The letter covers the correct project and account.

3.4 **Subcontractor Coverage Verification (Lensing Item F-003):** Verify that the WCB clearance covers not only the prime Proponent but also all subcontractors engaged on the project. If WCB Alberta issues separate clearances per subcontractor, obtain clearance letters for each. If a consolidated clearance is available, confirm it explicitly covers all subcontractor accounts.
- ASSUMPTION: Scope of WCB coverage requirement (prime only vs. all subcontractors) is TBD — confirm with WCB Alberta and per contract terms.

3.5 Retain copies for the project archive.

**Output of Step 3:** WCB Letter of Clearance on file (covering prime Proponent and all subcontractors as applicable) and ready for submission.

---

### Step 4 — Execute Statutory Declaration (SOW-0096)
**Responsible:** Project Manager / Legal Counsel
**Source:** RFP §2.13.2.3

4.1 Confirm with legal counsel the required form and content for a Statutory Declaration under the governing Alberta statute (ASSUMPTION: Alberta Oaths Act or equivalent — TBD: confirm specific statute; Lensing Item D-001).

4.2 Compile a complete list of all suppliers, Subcontractors, labourers, and other parties who performed work or supplied materials for the project.

4.3 Confirm with each party (or through payment records) that all amounts owing have been paid in full and no outstanding claims exist.

4.4 Prepare the Statutory Declaration stating that there are no outstanding claims for payment related to the project from any of the parties identified.

4.5 Have the declaration executed (sworn/affirmed and notarized or commissioned) by an authorized representative of the Proponent before a commissioner of oaths or notary public.
- ASSUMPTION: Standard Statutory Declaration form should be reviewed by legal counsel to ensure it satisfies CCDC 14-2013 and Alberta lien legislation requirements.

4.6 Retain the original executed declaration for submission; retain a copy for the project archive.

**Output of Step 4:** Executed Statutory Declaration ready for submission.

---

### Step 5 — Compile As-Built Drawings (ASSUMPTION — broader scope per _CONTEXT.md)
**Responsible:** Project Manager (compilation/coordination); design disciplines and surveyor (preparation)
**Source (ASSUMPTION):** _CONTEXT.md (Success Criteria); DEL-008-04 (As-Built Survey)

5.1 Request final as-built drawings from each design discipline (Architectural, Structural, Mechanical, Electrical, Civil, Plumbing, Landscape) confirming all field modifications have been captured.
- ASSUMPTION: As-built drawings are prepared by the respective licensed engineers/architects; PM coordinates receipt and completeness review.

5.2 Receive as-built survey from the Surveyor (DEL-008-04). Confirm survey reflects final constructed conditions. (Source: Decomposition §7, PKG-008)

5.3 Review as-built drawings and survey for completeness. Flag any discipline with outstanding or incomplete as-built documentation. Use the discipline enumeration from Specification REQ-006-V as a checklist.

5.4 Confirm format requirements with Owner (paper, digital, or both; CAD files vs. PDF). (TBD — see MEMORY.md, Question 1; Lensing Item A-004)

5.5 Organize as-built drawing set in the project archive. Confirm all sheets are present, properly stamped (where applicable), and indexed.

**Output of Step 5:** Complete as-built drawing set in project archive. (TBD: format confirmed with Owner)

---

### Step 6 — Compile Operations and Maintenance Manuals (ASSUMPTION — broader scope per _CONTEXT.md)
**Responsible:** Project Manager (collection/organization)
**Source (ASSUMPTION):** _CONTEXT.md (Success Criteria); industry standard practice

6.1 Collect O&M manuals from trade subcontractors and equipment suppliers for all major building systems, including at minimum:
- HVAC/ventilation and high-recovery heating (PKG-013)
- Plumbing, cistern (50,000L), septic system, bulk water fill (PKG-014)
- Electrical systems, three-phase power distribution, lighting (PKG-015)
- Two (2) x 5-tonne overhead cranes on trolley (PKG-016)
- Wash bay drainage system and mud sump (PKG-018)
- Any other equipment with manufacturer operation/maintenance requirements

6.2 Review each manual against the quality acceptance criteria defined in Specification REQ-007:
- Completeness (installation, operation, maintenance, troubleshooting, spare parts)
- Relevance to the specific equipment installed (not generic product literature)
- Legibility and language (ASSUMPTION: English language required)

6.3 If a manual is deficient (missing required sections, generic rather than project-specific, illegible), return to the subcontractor/supplier for correction before acceptance.

6.4 Organize manuals by building system or discipline (TBD: confirm preferred organization with Owner; see Guidance Trade-offs).

6.5 Index manuals in the final archive.

**Output of Step 6:** Organized O&M manual set, indexed and archived. (TBD: organization confirmed with Owner)

### Step 7 — Organize Warranty and Supporting Documentation (ASSUMPTION — broader scope per _CONTEXT.md)
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**Source (ASSUMPTION):** _CONTEXT.md (Success Criteria); _DEPENDENCIES.md (PKG-021)

7.1 Receive warranty and guarantee period documentation from PKG-021 (DEL-021-05), including:
- Performance Bond documentation
- Labour & Material Payment Bond documentation
- Guarantee period obligations record
- Insurance certificates (for reference)

7.2 Obtain commissioning reports from DEL-020-01. Confirm all systems are documented as commissioned.
- Note: DEL-020-03 compiles these reports into the archive; it does not produce them. (Source: Lensing Item X-005)

7.3 Obtain final inspection records and QC documentation from DEL-020-02 and PKG-019 (DEL-019-04).
- Note: DEL-020-03 compiles these records into the archive; it does not produce them. (Source: Lensing Item X-005)

7.4 Organize all warranty, commissioning, and inspection documentation in the project archive.

7.5 Record the CCC issuance date and calculate the end date of the two (2)-year guarantee period (CCC date + 2 years). Document this clearly in the archive index. (Source: RFP §2.10.2)
- Rationale: This record provides the Owner with a clear reference point for determining whether defect reports fall within the guarantee period obligation window, supporting enforcement of the Proponent's two-year guarantee obligation. (Source: Guidance — Guarantee Period Implications; Lensing Item D-003)

**Output of Step 7:** Warranty, commissioning, and inspection documentation organized in archive with guarantee period end date documented.

### Step 8 — Establish Final Project Archive
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**Source (ASSUMPTION):** _CONTEXT.md (Success Criteria); MEMORY.md (Questions 3, 4, 5)

8.1 Assemble all components of the final project archive:
- [ ] Signed CCC (from DEL-020-02)
- [ ] Payment Claim Letter (Step 2)
- [ ] WCB Letter of Clearance (Step 3)
- [ ] Statutory Declaration (Step 4)
- [ ] As-built drawings and as-built survey (Step 5) — TBD: format
- [ ] O&M manuals — all major systems (Step 6)
- [ ] Warranty and guarantee period documentation (Step 7)
- [ ] Commissioning reports (Step 7)
- [ ] Final inspection records and QC documentation (Step 7)
- [ ] All inspection reports and regulatory permits (from PKG-009)
- [ ] Contract documents (CCDC 14-2013 executed) — for reference
- [ ] Guarantee period end date record (Step 7.5)

> **Note:** The Master Closeout Artifact Checklist in Specification Documentation section may be used as an operational checklist for this step. (Lensing Item B-003)

8.2 Create an archive index identifying all documents with the following minimum fields: document title, document category, responsible originator, date received/created, revision status, and storage location. (Source: Specification REQ-009-V; Lensing Item F-002)

8.3 Confirm archive storage location with Owner. (TBD — see MEMORY.md, Question 4)

8.4 Confirm access and security protocols for the archive, including backup and disaster recovery provisions where applicable. (TBD — see MEMORY.md, Question 5; Guidance P-04 on digital preservation)

8.5 Transfer archive to Owner (or to designated storage location). Obtain written acknowledgment of receipt from the Owner or Owner's representative.

**Output of Step 8:** Complete project archive transferred to Owner with receipt confirmed.

### Step 9 — Submit Lien Holdback Release Package to Owner
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**Source:** RFP §2.13.2

9.1 Assemble the lien holdback release submission package:
- Signed CCC (from Owner — confirms prerequisite is met)
- Payment Claim Letter (Step 2)
- WCB Letter of Clearance (Step 3)
- Statutory Declaration (Step 4)

9.2 Submit the package to Camrose County (Owner / Owner's representative — TBD: specific recipient; Lensing Item E-001). Confirm the submission date is on or before December 31, 2026. (Source: RFP §3.1; OBJ-002)

9.3 Obtain written acknowledgment of receipt of the submission package from the Owner.

9.4 Track the payment timeline. (ASSUMPTION: Owner payment is due within 28 days of receiving a proper invoice per RFP §2.13.2; TBD: confirm payment schedule per CCDC 14-2013 terms — Lensing Item E-004)

**Output of Step 9:** Lien holdback release package submitted; written acknowledgment received; payment tracked.
