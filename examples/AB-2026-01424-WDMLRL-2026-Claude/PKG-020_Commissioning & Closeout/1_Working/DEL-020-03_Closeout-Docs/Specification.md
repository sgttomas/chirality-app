## Scope

### In Scope
This deliverable encompasses the collection, preparation, organization, and submission of all closeout documentation required to:
1. Release the lien holdback under RFP §2.13.2 (the three mandatory submission documents: payment claim letter, WCB Letter of Clearance, Statutory Declaration).
2. Formally establish the complete project record following Construction Completion Certificate (CCC) issuance.

The explicit minimum scope of this deliverable, as defined by the decomposition, comprises:
- **SOW-0094**: Letter from successful Proponent confirming all payment claims submitted (RFP §2.13.2.2)
- **SOW-0095**: WCB Letter of Clearance (RFP §2.13.2.3)
- **SOW-0096**: Statutory Declaration of no outstanding claims (RFP §2.13.2.3)

The broader organizational scope (per _CONTEXT.md) additionally includes compilation and handover of: as-built drawings, operations and maintenance manuals, warranty documentation, inspection records, commissioning reports, final financial reconciliation, and final project archive. These are treated as ASSUMPTION: part of the Project Manager's closeout coordination responsibilities unless the human confirms their explicit assignment to this deliverable.

### Out of Scope
- Issuance of the Construction Completion Certificate (CCC) — this is the Owner's (Camrose County's) responsibility and is covered under DEL-020-02. (Source: R-01 §2.14.3)
- Preparation of as-built design drawings — ASSUMPTION: responsibility of the respective design discipline packages (PKG-001 through PKG-007), coordinated by the Project Manager.
- Production of operations and maintenance manuals — ASSUMPTION: responsibility of trade subcontractors and equipment suppliers; PM compiles and organizes.
- Production of commissioning reports — responsibility of DEL-020-01. DEL-020-03 compiles these into the archive but does not produce them. (Source: Decomposition §7 PKG-020; Lensing Item X-005)
- Production of final inspection records — responsibility of DEL-020-02. DEL-020-03 compiles these into the archive but does not produce them. (Source: Decomposition §7 PKG-020; Lensing Item X-005)
- Execution of guarantee period obligations (defect rectification) — covered under DEL-021-05 (PKG-021). (Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md)
- Warranty/guarantee period cost obligations — covered under PKG-021. (Source: Decomposition §6)
- Payment of permit fees — Owner responsibility. (Source: R-01 §3.3.1)

## Terminology

The following terms are used throughout this deliverable's documents. Consistent usage is required across all four documents per Lensing Item B-004.

| Term | Definition | Source |
|---|---|---|
| CCC (Construction Completion Certificate) | The formal certificate issued by the Owner (Camrose County) upon satisfactory completion of all construction Work, including resolution of deficiencies, following the final inspection. Issuance of the CCC is a prerequisite for lien holdback release. | R-01 §2.14.3 |
| CCC Date | The date on which the Owner signs the CCC. This date initiates the two (2)-year guarantee period. | R-01 §2.10.2, §2.14.3 |
| Final Completion | The state at which all Work under the contract, including correction of deficiencies, has been completed and accepted by the Owner. For purposes of this deliverable, "final completion" is evidenced by CCC issuance. | R-01 §2.14; ASSUMPTION: CCDC 14-2013 may define this term more precisely — location TBD |
| Project Completion | Used interchangeably with "final completion" in this document set. Refers to the same event as CCC issuance for all practical purposes within DEL-020-03. | Lensing Item B-004 — normalization |
| Lien Holdback | 10% of the value of Work performed, retained by the Owner per the Alberta Prompt Payment and Construction Lien Act (PPACLA) and RFP §2.13.2. Released upon submission of the three mandatory closeout documents (SOW-0094, SOW-0095, SOW-0096) and a signed CCC. | R-01 §2.13.2 |
| Deficiency Holdback | A separate holdback equal to the estimated value of outstanding deficiencies, retained by the Owner until deficiencies are corrected and approved. Distinct from the lien holdback. | R-01 §2.11 |

## Requirements

### REQ-001: Payment Claim Letter (SOW-0094)
**Requirement:** The Project Manager shall prepare and submit a letter, signed by an authorized representative of the successful Proponent, stating that all claims for payment related to the contract have been submitted to the Owner.
**Source:** R-01 §2.13.2.2; Decomposition SOW-0094
**Trigger:** Submission is required before the lien holdback (10% of Work value) will be released by the Owner.
**Verification:** REQ-001-V: Letter is present in closeout package, correctly addressed, signed, and dated.

### REQ-002: WCB Letter of Clearance (SOW-0095)
**Requirement:** The Project Manager shall obtain and submit a Letter of Clearance from the Workers' Compensation Board (WCB) of Alberta confirming the Proponent is in good standing.
**Source:** R-01 §2.13.2.3; Decomposition SOW-0095
**Trigger:** Required before lien holdback release.
**Verification:** REQ-002-V: WCB Letter of Clearance is present, issued by WCB Alberta, and dated after CCC issuance (or within an acceptable window relative to the submission date — TBD: confirm acceptable dating window per CCDC 14-2013 and WCB Alberta requirements; Lensing Item A-003).

### REQ-003: Statutory Declaration (SOW-0096)
**Requirement:** The Project Manager shall obtain and submit a Statutory Declaration executed by the successful Proponent stating that there are no outstanding claims for payment related to the project from suppliers, Subcontractors, labourers, or any other parties affected by the Work.
**Source:** R-01 §2.13.2.3; Decomposition SOW-0096
**Trigger:** Required before lien holdback release.
**Legal Form (Lensing Item D-001):** The Statutory Declaration must comply with the governing Alberta statute for sworn declarations. ASSUMPTION: The Alberta Oaths Act (or equivalent) governs the form and execution requirements for Statutory Declarations. The specific statutory reference and form requirements are TBD — legal counsel to confirm. The declaration must be executed (sworn/affirmed) before a commissioner of oaths or notary public. (Source: R-01 §2.13.2.3; ASSUMPTION: Alberta Oaths Act — location TBD)
**Verification:** REQ-003-V: Statutory Declaration is present, properly executed (notarized/commissioned as required by the governing Alberta statute — TBD: confirm specific statute), and covers all subcontractors and suppliers.

### REQ-004: Submission Timing and Completeness (OBJ-002)
**Requirement:** All required closeout documents (REQ-001 through REQ-003, at minimum) shall be submitted on or before December 31, 2026, the contractual completion deadline.
**Source:** R-01 §3.1; Decomposition OBJ-002
**Schedule Risk Note (Lensing Item X-003):** If CCC issuance (REQ-005) occurs late in December 2026, there may be insufficient time to prepare and submit the remaining closeout documents before the December 31 deadline. Guidance Considerations (Timing and Schedule Pressure) addresses this risk. The PM should plan for contingency: initiate preparation of all non-CCC-dependent documents (REQ-001, REQ-002, REQ-003) in advance so that only the final assembly and submission step depends on CCC issuance.
**Verification:** REQ-004-V: Submission date of closeout package is confirmed to be on or before December 31, 2026.

### REQ-005: Signed CCC as Prerequisite Context (upstream)
**Requirement (constraint):** The lien holdback release package is processed by the Owner only after a signed CCC is issued by the Owner following final inspection. (Source: R-01 §2.13.2.1, §2.14.3) This is a sequencing constraint on DEL-020-03, not a document produced by this deliverable.
**Verification:** REQ-005-V: Confirm CCC has been issued (from DEL-020-02) prior to submitting final lien holdback release package.
