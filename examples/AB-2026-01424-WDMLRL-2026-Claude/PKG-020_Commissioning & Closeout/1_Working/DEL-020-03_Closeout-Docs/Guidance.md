**Document Type:** Guidance (directional)

## Purpose

DEL-020-03 serves two distinct but related functions within the project closeout:

**Function 1 — Contractual Release:** The three explicitly required documents (payment claim letter, WCB Letter of Clearance, Statutory Declaration) are the contractual prerequisites for the Owner to release the lien holdback (10% of contract value) under RFP §2.13.2 and the Alberta Prompt Payment and Construction Lien Act. Without these documents, the final payment to the Proponent is withheld. This makes DEL-020-03 a critical financial completion milestone.

**Function 2 — Project Record and Handover (ASSUMPTION):** The broader closeout documentation package (as-built drawings, O&M manuals, warranty documents, inspection records, commissioning reports, and final archive) establishes the permanent record of the project for Camrose County's ongoing facility operations and warranty administration. This function is described in _CONTEXT.md and is consistent with standard design-build closeout practice but is not explicitly captured in the SOW mapping for this deliverable. (Source: _CONTEXT.md; ASSUMPTION: industry standard closeout practice)

Together, these functions support OBJ-002 (timely completion of all work by December 31, 2026) and OBJ-006 (lien holdback release as part of the CCDC 14-2013 contract financial settlement).

## Principles

### P-01: Sequence Dependency — Do Not Submit Prematurely
The three mandatory closeout documents (SOW-0094-0096) are meaningless if submitted before construction is genuinely complete and all deficiencies resolved. The RFP is explicit: lien holdback is released only upon completion of the entire project, including correction of deficiencies, and after the Owner issues the signed CCC (RFP §2.13.2). The Project Manager must confirm DEL-020-02 is complete and the CCC issued before triggering the final submission package.

### P-02: Statutory Declaration Requires Legal Precision
The Statutory Declaration (SOW-0096) is a formal legal instrument. It must cover all suppliers, Subcontractors, labourers, and other parties affected by the Work (RFP §2.13.2.3). ASSUMPTION: It must be commissioned or notarized in accordance with Alberta's Oaths Act or equivalent (TBD: confirm governing statute — Lensing Item D-001). The PM should engage legal counsel to ensure proper form and execution. Using a generic template without legal review is a risk.

### P-03: WCB Clearance Has a Lead Time
WCB Alberta does not issue clearance letters instantaneously. ASSUMPTION: The PM should initiate the WCB clearance request several weeks before anticipated CCC issuance to avoid delays in the final payment release. The clearance must be current at the time of submission.

### P-04: The Broader Archive Serves Long-Term Operations
While the broader archive (O&M manuals, as-builts, etc.) does not directly unlock payment, it is essential for the Owner's ongoing operation and warranty administration of the facility. ASSUMPTION: A disorganized or incomplete archive creates operational risk for Camrose County and may create ambiguity during guarantee period defect resolution. The PM should treat archive quality as a professional obligation even if not contractually enforced in detail.

**Long-Term Digital Preservation (Lensing Item E-002):** The project archive will serve Camrose County for the two (2)-year guarantee period and beyond (facility lifespan). When selecting archive formats and storage methods, the PM should consider:
- **Format longevity:** PDF/A is preferred for long-term archival over standard PDF, as it is designed for document preservation. (ASSUMPTION: industry archival best practice)
- **Backup and redundancy:** At least one backup copy of the digital archive should be maintained at a separate location from the primary storage. (ASSUMPTION: standard records management practice)
- **Disaster recovery:** The archive storage plan should address how records would be recovered in the event of physical or digital loss. (TBD: confirm with Owner whether disaster recovery requirements apply)
- **Access continuity:** If the archive is stored digitally, ensure the file formats remain accessible to Camrose County without proprietary software dependencies where possible.

(Source: Lensing Item E-002; ASSUMPTION: general archival and records management best practice — not sourced from accessible project documents)

### P-05: Coordinate Collection Early
Documentation for the closeout archive (commissioning reports, inspection records, O&M manuals) is generated throughout the project. ASSUMPTION: The PM should establish a documentation collection process beginning at construction phase, not only at project end. Late-stage collection of O&M manuals from subcontractors can be difficult once subcontractors have demobilized.

## Considerations

### Timing and Schedule Pressure
The project has a hard completion deadline of December 31, 2026 (RFP §3.1; OBJ-002). Closeout documentation compilation must be planned as a parallel activity during final construction, not as a sequential afterthought. The critical path item for payment release is the sequence: all deficiencies resolved -> Owner issues CCC (DEL-020-02) -> closeout documents submitted -> lien holdback released.

**Late-CCC Contingency (Lensing Item X-003):** If CCC issuance occurs late in December 2026, there may be insufficient time to prepare and submit the remaining documents before the December 31 deadline. To mitigate this risk:
- Prepare REQ-001 (Payment Claim Letter), REQ-002 (WCB Letter of Clearance), and REQ-003 (Statutory Declaration) in advance, so that only the final assembly depends on CCC issuance.
- Initiate the WCB clearance request well in advance (see P-03).
- Have the Statutory Declaration form reviewed by legal counsel in advance; execution can occur once all subcontractor payments are confirmed.
- The broader archive (REQ-006 through REQ-009) can be substantially assembled before CCC issuance since these are compilation activities.
(Source: Specification REQ-004, REQ-005; Lensing Item X-003)

### Scope Ambiguity: Explicit SOW vs. Broader _CONTEXT.md Description
There is a gap between the decomposition's explicit SOW mapping (SOW-0094-0096 only) and the broader scope described in _CONTEXT.md (which includes as-built drawings, O&M manuals, warranty docs, commissioning reports, and final archive). The PM should seek clarification from the Owner at project award regarding:
- What specific documents are required in the final archive handover.
- Whether as-built drawing compilation is explicitly within the PM's scope or delegated to the design team.
- What format and media are required for the archive.
(See Conflict Table below for the formal capture of this ambiguity.)

### ASSUMPTION-Tagged Requirements: Prescriptive Authority (Lensing Items A-001, C-001)
Requirements REQ-006 through REQ-009 in the Specification are tagged as ASSUMPTION. This creates an apparent contradiction: they use normative "shall" language (implying binding requirements) while simultaneously disclaiming their authority (via ASSUMPTION tags).

**Rationale for inclusion as requirements (Lensing Item C-001):** These items are included in the Specification because _CONTEXT.md explicitly describes them as part of the deliverable scope and lists them as success criteria. While the decomposition's SOW mapping (SOW-0094-0096) does not directly reference them, they represent the organizational and compilation function that gives the three mandatory documents their operational context. A closeout documentation deliverable that provides only the three lien-release letters without the supporting archive would be technically compliant with the narrow SOW mapping but would fail the broader success criteria defined in _CONTEXT.md.

**Practical interpretation:** Treat REQ-001 through REQ-005 as binding contractual obligations. Treat REQ-006 through REQ-009 as presumed scope items that require human confirmation to elevate to full requirement status. Until confirmed, they carry prescriptive direction (the PM should plan to fulfill them) but not contractual enforceability. (See Conflict Table CON-001)

### As-Built Drawing Responsibility
The _CONTEXT.md lists as-built drawings as a success criterion for this deliverable. However, the as-built survey is mapped to DEL-008-04 (PKG-008), and as-built design drawings are typically prepared by the respective design disciplines. ASSUMPTION: The PM's role here is coordination and compilation, not preparation. The PM must confirm which parties are responsible for preparing (vs. compiling) each as-built drawing set. (Source: MEMORY.md, Question 2; Decomposition §7, PKG-008)

### O&M Manual Sourcing
Operations and maintenance manuals must be sourced from the appropriate parties:
- HVAC/mechanical systems: Mechanical subcontractor / equipment suppliers (ASSUMPTION)
- Electrical systems: Electrical subcontractor / equipment suppliers (ASSUMPTION)
- Overhead cranes (2 x 5-tonne): Crane supplier (ASSUMPTION; crane procurement under PKG-016)
- Cistern and water systems: Mechanical subcontractor / supplier (ASSUMPTION)
- Septic system: Plumbing subcontractor / supplier (ASSUMPTION)
- Wash bay drainage and mud sump: Plumbing/mechanical subcontractor (ASSUMPTION; PKG-018)
The PM should identify and contractually require manual submission from each relevant subcontractor at subcontract execution, not at project end. (Source: ASSUMPTION; industry practice)

**Subcontract Clause Guidance (Lensing Item X-001):** To ensure O&M manuals are received, the PM should include explicit manual submission clauses in trade subcontracts. At minimum, each subcontract for a major building system should include:
- A clause requiring the subcontractor to provide complete O&M manuals for all equipment and systems installed, meeting the quality acceptance criteria in Specification REQ-007.
- A submission deadline tied to substantial completion of the subcontractor's scope (not project completion).
- A holdback or payment milestone tied to manual submission where contractually feasible.
(Source: Lensing Item X-001; ASSUMPTION: standard construction contract management practice — not sourced from accessible project documents)

### Deficiency Holdback vs. Lien Holdback
Two separate holdback mechanisms exist under the RFP:
- **Lien Holdback (10%)**: Governed by the Prompt Payment and Construction Lien Act (Alberta); released per §2.13.2 upon submission of the three closeout documents and signed CCC. (Source: R-01 §2.13.2)
- **Deficiency Holdback**: Separate amount equal to estimated outstanding deficiency value; retained until deficiencies are corrected and approved by Owner. (Source: R-01 §2.11)

**Interaction between holdbacks (Lensing Item X-004):** The RFP describes these as distinct mechanisms. TBD: confirm whether unresolved deficiency holdback blocks lien holdback release or whether the two mechanisms operate independently. The RFP §2.13.2 conditions lien holdback release on "completion of the Work" and submission of the required documents; if "completion" requires all deficiencies to be resolved, then the deficiency holdback must effectively be at zero before lien holdback release can proceed. The PM should clarify this with legal counsel or the Owner at project award. (Source: R-01 §2.11, §2.13.2; Lensing Item X-004; ASSUMPTION: interpretation pending)

The PM must distinguish between these in financial tracking. Both may be outstanding simultaneously.

### Guarantee Period Implications
The guarantee period runs for the construction period plus two (2) years following the CCC date (RFP §2.10.2). During this period:
- Faulty materials or defective workmanship must be rectified. (RFP §2.10.3)
- Defect rectification must be carried out within 10 days of Owner's written instruction, except in emergencies. (RFP §2.10.6)
- Costs of guarantee-period remediation are borne by the Proponent. (RFP §2.10.7)
The closeout archive should clearly document the CCC issuance date and the end date of the guarantee period. ASSUMPTION: Warranty documentation from PKG-021 should be cross-referenced to the CCC date.

**Value of documenting guarantee period dates (Lensing Item D-003):** Recording the CCC date and calculated guarantee period end date in the archive index serves a specific operational purpose for the Owner: it provides a clear reference point for determining whether a defect reported during the guarantee period falls within the Proponent's obligation window. In any dispute about whether a warranty claim is timely, the documented CCC date establishes the start of the guarantee period. Without this clear record, the Owner may face difficulty enforcing the two-year guarantee obligation. (Source: RFP §2.10.2; Lensing Item D-003; ASSUMPTION: standard warranty administration practice)
