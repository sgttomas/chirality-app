## Purpose

This procedure describes the operational steps for conducting the final inspection of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition and obtaining the signed Construction Completion Certificate (CCC) from the Owner (Camrose County). Steps are ordered sequentially and reflect the contractual requirements in RFP SS2.14, SS2.10, SS2.11, and SS3.1.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Prepare Inspection Checklist
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**When:** Before commencing self-inspection

1. Develop a comprehensive inspection checklist organized by discipline (architectural, structural, mechanical, electrical, plumbing, civil/site). (Enrichment: A-001)
2. Cross-reference checklist items against: IFC drawings (confirmed current per Prerequisites), RFP SS3.3 and SS3.4 design requirements, commissioning reports (DEL-020-01), and all applicable Alberta Safety Code requirements. (Enrichment: F-003)
3. Include site condition items: cleanliness, site tidying, gravel driving surfaces, drainage, and landscaping.
4. Confirm the checklist with key site supervisors and trade leads.

**Verification:** Completed checklist draft reviewed and signed by Project Manager.
**Source:** RFP SS2.14.1 (basis); ASSUMPTION for checklist format specifics.

---

### Step 2 — Conduct Proponent Self-Inspection
**Responsible:** Project Manager (with site supervisors and trade leads as appropriate)
**When:** After all construction and commissioning work is complete

1. Walk all portions of the Work against the inspection checklist prepared in Step 1.
2. Verify that every item has been completed and is functioning as required by the Contract documents.
3. Confirm the Works are in a clean and tidy condition. (Source: RFP SS2.14.1)
4. Document any items that are incomplete, deficient, or require attention — record in the deficiency log with severity classification (critical/major/minor per Guidance). (Enrichment: D-003)
5. Prioritize and assign remediation tasks for all identified deficiencies.
6. Coordinate remediation with responsible trades and subcontractors.
7. Re-walk all remediated items to confirm correction before proceeding to Step 3; document re-inspection outcomes (deficiency reference, re-inspection date, finding, approver) in re-inspection records. (Enrichment: B-002)

**Verification:** Signed self-inspection checklist with all items completed or deficiencies logged and resolved; re-inspection records on file for remediated items.
**Source:** RFP SS2.14.1

---

### Step 3 — Confirm Readiness for Owner Inspection
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**When:** After self-inspection and remediation complete

1. Review the deficiency log — confirm all identified deficiencies are either corrected or have a documented remediation plan acceptable for Owner awareness.
2. Obtain written confirmation from the DEL-020-01 responsible party (Commissioning Agent / PM) that commissioning is complete and commissioning reports are available for Owner review. (Enrichment: F-002)
3. Confirm all Safety Code permit inspections are complete and approved — obtain written confirmation from Safety Codes officer or equivalent documentation. (See Specification REQ-020-02-012; Enrichment: A-004)
4. Confirm as-built / IFC drawings have been verified current against actual construction. (Enrichment: F-003)
5. Confirm the site is in clean and tidy condition. (Source: RFP SS2.14.1)
6. Confirm with the County project manager what threshold of outstanding deficiencies (if any) is acceptable at the time of Owner final inspection (see Conflict Table in Guidance — CONF-020-02-01; use deficiency severity classification as framework for discussion; Enrichment: D-003).

**Verification:** Project Manager sign-off on readiness; written commissioning completion confirmation on file; confirmation from County project manager that Owner inspection can be scheduled.
**Source:** RFP SS2.14.1-2.14.2

---

### Step 4 — Submit Written Request for Final Inspection
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**When:** After readiness confirmed in Step 3

1. Prepare a formal written request to the Owner (Camrose County) requesting that a final inspection of the Works be carried out.
2. Include in the request (Enrichment: X-001):
   a. Reference to project / contract
   b. Statement that the Works are complete and in a clean and tidy condition
   c. Request for the Owner to schedule a final inspection
   d. Contact for coordination (County project manager channel)
3. Submit the written request through the appropriate channel (coordinate through County project manager per RFP SS3.1), observing any advance notice period confirmed in Prerequisites. (Enrichment: D-002)
4. File a copy of the request in the correspondence record.

**Verification:** Written request sent and copy filed; confirmation of receipt from County project manager or Owner.
**Source:** RFP SS2.14.2, SS3.1

---

### Step 5 — Facilitate Owner Final Inspection
**Responsible:** Project Manager (Proponent facilitator); Owner (Camrose County) conducts inspection
**When:** As scheduled by Owner after receipt of written request

1. Coordinate inspection date and time through the County project manager. (Source: RFP SS3.1)
2. Ensure the County project representative is present at the inspection. (Source: RFP SS3.3.2)
3. Facilitate Proponent representation at the inspection (Project Manager or delegate to accompany Owner inspector throughout).
4. Provide Owner inspector with access to all areas, systems, and documentation requested (IFC drawings confirmed current, commissioning reports, Safety Code inspection records, QC records).
5. Record all Owner observations, comments, and identified deficiencies during the inspection walkthrough; assign severity classification to each deficiency. (Enrichment: D-003)
6. Provide County project representative with a copy of the complete inspection report. (Source: RFP SS3.3.2)

**Verification:** Owner inspection completed; inspection report produced; County project representative copy distributed.
**Source:** RFP SS2.14.3, SS3.1, SS3.3.2

---

### Step 6 — Document and Remediate Inspection Deficiencies
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**When:** Immediately following Owner final inspection

1. Compile the full deficiency list from the Owner inspection into the deficiency log, including severity classification (critical/major/minor). (Enrichment: D-003)
2. For each deficiency, confirm: description, location, responsible party, target remediation date, severity classification.
3. Notify responsible trades and subcontractors of deficiency remediation requirements.
4. For each deficiency, carry out remediation within **ten (10) days of the Owner's written instruction** to perform the work (except emergencies). (Source: RFP SS2.10.6)
5. Document remediation evidence appropriate to deficiency category: structural/safety items require re-inspection report or trade certification; cosmetic items require photographic evidence and Owner sign-off (ASSUMPTION for evidence categorization; Enrichment: X-002).
6. Notify the Owner in writing of each deficiency correction for Owner approval.
7. Track Owner approval of each corrected deficiency in the deficiency log.
8. Document re-inspection outcomes for each remediated deficiency (deficiency reference, re-inspection date, finding, approver). (Enrichment: B-002)
9. Note: The Owner may retain a Deficiency Holdback equal to the estimated value of outstanding deficiencies from payments due until deficiencies are corrected and approved. (Source: RFP SS2.11.1-2.11.2)

**Verification:** All deficiencies in the log are either: Owner-approved corrected (with re-inspection records on file), or documented in a written remediation plan with timeline accepted by the Owner.
**Source:** RFP SS2.10.4, SS2.10.6, SS2.11

---

### Step 6a — Handle Owner Refusal of CCC or Additional Non-Compliance (Enrichment: B-003)
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**When:** If the Owner declines to issue CCC after inspection or identifies additional non-compliance beyond the initial deficiency list

1. Request written explanation from the Owner for the reasons CCC was not issued. (ASSUMPTION: standard practice)
2. Review the additional non-compliance items identified by the Owner against the original deficiency list.
3. Determine whether the additional items are: (a) items missed during self-inspection, (b) new items arising from remediation work, or (c) disagreements about compliance interpretation.
4. For items in categories (a) and (b): add to deficiency log and remediate per Step 6.
5. For items in category (c): document the Proponent's position in writing and escalate through the County project manager for resolution. (ASSUMPTION: escalation path per contract dispute provisions in CCDC 14-2013 -- location TBD)
6. Once all additional items are resolved, return to Step 7 (Obtain Signed CCC).

**Verification:** All additional non-compliance items addressed; written explanation from Owner on file; escalation records on file if applicable.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION: not explicitly addressed in RFP; inferred from standard construction practice.

---

### Step 7 — Obtain Signed CCC
**Responsible:** Project Manager (Proponent); Owner (Camrose County) issues CCC
**When:** After Owner final inspection confirms full compliance with Contract documents

1. Confirm with the Owner that all compliance requirements have been met (per Step 6 and Step 5 outcomes).
2. Owner issues the Construction Completion Certificate (CCC) — signed by the Owner after confirming the Proponent has complied fully with the Contract documents. (Source: RFP SS2.14.3)
3. Obtain the signed CCC document.
4. Record the CCC issuance date — this date establishes the start of the 2-year guarantee period. (Source: RFP SS2.10.2; Enrichment: X-004 — terminology normalized)
5. File the signed CCC in the project record.
6. Forward the signed CCC to DEL-020-03 (Closeout Documentation) compilation as a required component of the lien holdback release documentation package. (Source: RFP SS2.13.2.1; _DEPENDENCIES.md)

**Verification:** Signed CCC document on file with issuance date recorded.
**Source:** RFP SS2.14.3, SS2.13.2.1

---

### Step 8 — Produce Consolidated Inspection Outcome Summary (Enrichment: X-003)
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**When:** After CCC received (or after final deficiency resolution if CCC was delayed)

1. Compile a consolidated inspection outcome summary document that aggregates:
   a. Self-inspection results (Step 2 checklist summary)
   b. Owner inspection findings (Step 5 inspection report summary)
   c. Deficiency status (all deficiencies: identified, remediated, re-inspected, Owner-approved)
   d. CCC decision (issued / date / any conditions)
2. File the consolidated summary in the project record.
3. Forward the consolidated summary to DEL-020-03 (Closeout Documentation) for inclusion in the closeout documentation package.

**Verification:** Consolidated inspection outcome summary produced, filed, and forwarded to DEL-020-03.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION: required for traceability to DEL-020-03.

---

### Step 9 — Notify Downstream and Close Out DEL-020-02
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**When:** Immediately after CCC received and consolidated summary produced

1. Notify the DEL-020-03 (Closeout Documentation) responsible party that the CCC has been obtained and the consolidated inspection outcome summary is available.
2. Confirm the Deficiency Holdback status with the Owner — which deficiencies (if any) remain outstanding and what holdback amount applies.
3. Continue monitoring and resolving any outstanding deficiencies (if any) until the Deficiency Holdback is fully released. (Source: RFP SS2.11.4)
4. File all DEL-020-02 records (checklists, inspection report, deficiency log, re-inspection records, consolidated summary, correspondence, CCC) in the project archive.
5. Update DEL-020-02 status as appropriate.

**Verification:** All records filed; DEL-020-03 notified; Deficiency Holdback status documented.
**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md; RFP SS2.11, SS2.13.2
