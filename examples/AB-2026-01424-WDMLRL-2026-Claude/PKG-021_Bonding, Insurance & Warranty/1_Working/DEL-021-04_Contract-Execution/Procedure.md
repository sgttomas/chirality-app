# Procedure — DEL-021-04: CCDC 14-2013 Contract Execution

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and execute the CCDC 14-2013 Design-Build Stipulated Price Contract package for the 2026 Design Build of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. It covers all activities from receipt of award notification through to issuance of fully executed contract copies and establishment of contract administration procedures.

Successful completion of this procedure is the gate condition for construction commencement (REQ-021-04-02; RFP §2.7).

## Steps

### Step 1 — Receive and Acknowledge Award Notification

1.1 Upon receipt of written contract award notification from Camrose County, record the award date. This date starts the 10-day bonding clock (RFP §2.12.3).

1.2 Acknowledge receipt to the County in writing.

1.3 Identify and confirm the County project manager (RFP §3.1; SOW-0303).

1.4 Obtain the County project manager's contact information for contract administration coordination.

1.5 Confirm with the County whether a separate Notice to Proceed (NTP) will be issued after contract execution, or whether the executed contract itself authorizes construction commencement. See Guidance Considerations (NTP Relationship) for context.

**Records:** Award notification letter (filed); written acknowledgement sent; County PM contact recorded; NTP process confirmed.

---

### Step 2 — Confirm Internal Signing Authority

2.1 Identify the authorized representative(s) of the Design-Builder who will execute the contract.

2.2 If the Design-Builder is a corporation: confirm names and titles of duly authorized officers per corporate signing authority documentation (RFP §4.3.2).

2.3 Obtain and file corporate authorization documentation (e.g., corporate resolution, signing authority certificate) as evidence.

**Records:** Corporate signing authority documentation filed.

---

### Step 3 — Prepare Contract Execution Package

3.1 Obtain the CCDC 14-2013 standard form document (location TBD — obtain from CCDC or project legal counsel).

3.2 Prepare the draft contract, populating all required fields:
- Party names: Camrose County (Owner) and Design-Builder (legal name, address)
- Project description: 2026 Design Build of West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition, SW 14-44-21-W4, near Ferintosh, Alberta
- Contract price: firm stipulated price for all scope except foundation (from accepted proposal)
- Foundation pricing: noted as variable; subject to post-geotech negotiation (RFP §4.8.2)
- Completion date: December 31, 2026 (RFP §3.1)
- Guarantee period: construction period plus 2 years post-CCC (RFP §2.10.2)

3.3 Prepare the Contract Price Schedule and Fee Payment Schedule with milestones per RFP §4.8.1. Ensure payment terms reflect: invoices every ≤31 days; payment within 28 days; 10% lien holdback per Prompt Payment and Construction Lien Act (RFP §2.13.2).

3.4 Prepare a separate Foundation Pricing placeholder/schedule identifying the variable-price element and confirming it will be negotiated upon receipt of the complete geotechnical report (RFP §4.8.2; Decomposition SOW-0304).

3.5 Assemble all Proposal Response Forms (Appendix A): Addenda Received Form, Proposal Consent Form, Contractor H&S Pre-qualification documentation (RFP §4.9).

3.6 Prepare Supplementary Conditions to CCDC 14-2013 if any project-specific modifications have been agreed (REQ-021-04-26). If no modifications are required, prepare a written record documenting that the standard form is used without amendment.

3.7 Review complete draft package with project legal counsel. Legal counsel identity and review process: TBD (see Prerequisites).

3.8 Confirm with the County whether the contract execution will be by physical (wet-ink) signatures or electronic signatures. See Guidance Considerations (Electronic vs. Physical Execution) for context.

**Records:** Draft contract package prepared and reviewed; legal review record; execution format confirmed.

---

### Step 4 — Confirm Bonds and Insurance

4.1 Confirm with the surety company that Performance Bond (50% of contract price; RFP §2.12.1.1) and Labour & Material Payment Bond (50% of contract price; RFP §2.12.1.2) are ready for issuance. Confirm surety is licensed in Alberta (RFP §2.12.2).

4.2 Confirm that bond forms are acceptable to the Owner and compatible with the CCDC 14-2013 contract documents (RFP §2.12.2). ASSUMPTION: Confirm with County prior to execution meeting.

4.3 Confirm with insurance broker that all required coverages are in force:
- Automobile/CGL: ≥$5,000,000 inclusive (RFP §4.2.1)
- Workers' Compensation: per Alberta law (RFP §4.2.2)
- Professional Liability (E&O): ≥$5,000,000 (RFP §4.2.3)
- Employer's Liability: ≥$5,000,000 per employee per accident (RFP §4.2.4)
- County named as additional insured on all policies except E&O (RFP §4.2.6; SOW-0103)
- 30-day written cancellation notice clause included (RFP §4.2.6)

4.4 Obtain insurance certificates and/or written documentation satisfactory to the County (RFP §4.2.6).

4.5 See Conflict Table C-021-04-01 (Guidance.md): resolve with County whether bonds must be physically delivered at signing or may follow within the 10-day window.

**Records:** Bond confirmation from surety; insurance certificates obtained; bond timing resolution recorded.

---

### Step 5 — Execute the Contract

5.1 Schedule the contract execution meeting with Camrose County. Confirm the County's authorized signatory.

5.2 At execution, assemble the complete contract package:
- Signed CCDC 14-2013 contract form (executed by authorized representatives of both parties)
- Supplementary Conditions (modifications or documentation of no modifications) (REQ-021-04-26)
- Signed Contract Price Schedule and Fee Payment Schedule
- Foundation Pricing Schedule / placeholder
- Performance Bond certificate
- Labour & Material Payment Bond certificate
- Insurance certificates / evidence documents
- Prime Contractor Designation Form (County-issued; RFP §2.15) — completed and signed
- Addenda Received Form (Appendix A)
- Proposal Consent Form (Appendix A)
- Contractor H&S Pre-qualification documentation (RFP §4.9)

5.3 Both parties execute the contract form. Verify:
- Design-Builder authorized representative(s) have signed with name and title printed
- County authorized signatory has signed
- All dates are correct
- Contract price matches accepted proposal

5.4 Confirm that bond delivery is complete (or confirm timeline to delivery, per resolution of Conflict C-021-04-01).

**Records:** Executed contract originals; countersigned by both parties.

---

### Step 6 — Foundation Price Negotiation (Deferred Step)

6.1 This step is deferred until the complete geotechnical report is available (DEL-008-01).

6.2 Upon receipt of the geotechnical report, initiate negotiation with the County for final foundation design and construction pricing per RFP §4.8.2.

6.3 Document the agreed foundation price in a contract amendment or supplementary pricing schedule, executed by both parties.

6.4 File executed foundation pricing amendment with contract records.

**Records:** Geotechnical report; foundation price negotiation records; executed foundation pricing amendment.

---

### Step 7 — Establish Contract Administration Procedures

7.1 Prepare and distribute a Contract Administration Procedures document covering the minimum content items per Specification REQ-021-04-23:
- (a) Weekly meeting schedule and format (RFP §3.1)
- (b) Invoice submission process and cycle (≤31-day intervals; RFP §2.13.2)
- (c) Inspection request and reporting process (RFP §3.3.2)
- (d) Change order process (per CCDC 14-2013 general conditions; TBD pending access to standard form — see Specification TBD Tracking item A-002)
- (e) Deficiency notification and rectification process (10-day requirement; RFP §2.10.6)
- (f) Deficiency holdback administration (RFP §2.11)
- (g) Lien holdback administration (RFP §2.13.2)
- (h) Document control and records management (using identified records management system — see Prerequisites)
- (i) County project manager communication protocols
- (j) Insurance renewal tracking: define schedule and responsibility for monitoring insurance certificate expiry dates, obtaining renewal certificates, and re-verifying coverage compliance throughout the project and guarantee period

7.2 For any items where specific procedures cannot be defined at contract execution, document as TBD with a resolution timeline.

7.3 Confirm contract administration procedures with the County project manager.

**Records:** Executed or acknowledged Contract Administration Procedures document.

---

### Step 8 — Distribute Executed Contract

8.1 Prepare the contract distribution list. Confirm with County at award. Anticipated minimum recipients:

| Recipient | Copy Type | Quantity |
|---|---|---|
| Camrose County (Owner) | TBD (original or certified) | TBD |
| Design-Builder project management team | TBD (original or certified) | TBD |
| Surety company | TBD (certified copy anticipated) | TBD |
| Design-Builder insurance broker | TBD (certified copy anticipated) | TBD |
| Additional parties | TBD | TBD |

8.2 Distribute executed contract copies (originals or certified copies as agreed) to all parties on the confirmed distribution list.

8.3 Retain at least one original executed contract set in the Design-Builder's project records.

8.4 Log distribution: record recipient, copy type (original/certified), and date of delivery.

**Records:** Contract distribution log; confirmation of receipt from all parties.
