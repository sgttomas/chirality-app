## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and maintain the Insurance Package (DEL-021-03) for the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition Design-Build project. The procedure covers procurement of required policies, documentation, submission to the County, and ongoing maintenance of coverage through project completion.

## Steps

### Phase 1: Pre-Procurement (Upon Award Notification)

**Step 1 — Confirm Award and Trigger Insurance Procurement**

Upon receipt of written contract award notification from Camrose County, the Project Manager shall immediately notify Finance and the designated insurance broker that insurance procurement is required. Reference RFP §4.2 in all broker instructions.

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Success Criteria); RFP §4.2.

**Step 2 — Brief Insurance Broker on RFP Requirements**

Provide the insurance broker with:
- A copy of RFP §4.2 (all sub-sections)
- Confirmation of the project scope (design-build, landfill-adjacent site, Alberta)
- The legal name of Camrose County for additional insured endorsements
- Any other parties to be named as additional insured (confirm with County at contract negotiation)
- Policy requirement: 30-day written cancellation notice to County

Document all instructions provided to the broker in the Broker Instructions and Correspondence Log (see Records — [E-001]).

**Source:** RFP §4.2.1–§4.2.6.

**Step 3 — Obtain and Review Insurance Quotes**

Finance shall obtain quotes for all required coverage lines:

| Coverage | Target Limit |
|---|---|
| Automobile / Bodily Injury / Property Damage (with sub-coverages per §4.2.1.1–§4.2.1.6) | $5,000,000 inclusive |
| Workers' Compensation (WCB) | Per Alberta statute |
| Professional Liability — E&O | $5,000,000 |
| Employer's Liability | $5,000,000 per employee |
| Other as directed by County | TBD |

Review quotes to confirm all sub-coverages are included before binding. Pay particular attention to: environmental liability sub-coverage (§4.2.1.5) given landfill-adjacent site, and independent subcontractors sub-coverage (§4.2.1.2).

Document all broker communications regarding quotes in the Broker Instructions and Correspondence Log.

**Source:** RFP §4.2.1–§4.2.5.

**Step 4 — Bind Policies and Confirm Policy Conditions**

Upon Finance approval of quotes, instruct the broker to bind all required policies. Before accepting final policy documents, confirm the following in each applicable policy:

- [ ] Coverage limit meets or exceeds RFP minimums
- [ ] All required sub-coverages are endorsed (for §4.2.1 policy)
- [ ] Camrose County named as additional insured (all policies except E&O)
- [ ] Cross-liability / severability of interests clause included
- [ ] 30-day written cancellation notice to County confirmed as policy condition

**Source:** RFP §4.2.6.

---

### Phase 2: Documentation and Submission

**Step 5 — Obtain Certificates of Insurance**

Request certificates of insurance from the broker for all policies. ASSUMPTION: Standard ACORD 25 or equivalent forms are used. Certificates must clearly show:
- Insured name (Proponent / Design-Builder legal name)
- Camrose County as certificate holder and additional insured
- Policy numbers, effective and expiry dates
- Coverage limits

**Source:** RFP §4.2.6 (evidence of coverage); ASSUMPTION for ACORD form (not prescribed in RFP).

**Step 6 — Obtain Additional Insured Endorsements**

Request formal additional insured endorsements (not merely certificate notations) from the broker for each applicable policy. Confirm endorsements reference Camrose County and any other parties designated by the County.

**Source:** RFP §4.2.6.

**Step 7 — Obtain WCB Good Standing Certificate**

Request a WCB Good Standing Certificate from the Alberta Workers' Compensation Board confirming the Proponent's WCB account is in good standing. This serves as evidence of WCB coverage compliance.

**Source:** RFP §4.2.2; SOW-0102.

### Phase 3: Ongoing Maintenance

**Step 11 — Track Policy Renewal Dates**

Finance shall log all policy effective and expiry dates. Set internal reminders not less than 60 days before each policy expiry to initiate renewal. ASSUMPTION: 60-day lead time is sufficient for standard commercial renewals; adjust if the broker advises a longer lead time.

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Success Criteria — insurance maintained throughout project duration); ASSUMPTION for 60-day lead time.

**Step 12 — Submit Renewal Certificates to County**

Upon renewal of any policy, obtain updated certificates and endorsements and submit to Camrose County without delay. Do not allow a coverage gap.

**Source:** RFP §4.2.6; _CONTEXT.md (Success Criteria).

**[A-004] Step 12A — Document Mid-Project Policy Changes**

Any mid-project policy amendments, insurer changes, coverage modifications, or endorsement additions (beyond standard renewals) shall be documented in the Policy Lifecycle Audit Log (see Records). This log provides an audit trail for regulatory review and ensures that all stakeholders are aware of coverage changes. For each policy change event, record:

- Date of change
- Policy affected
- Nature of change (amendment, insurer change, coverage modification, cancellation/replacement)
- Reason for change
- County notification date (if applicable under 30-day cancellation notice requirement)
- Updated certificate/endorsement reference

**Source:** RFP §4.2.6 (cancellation notice requirement implies audit trail for policy changes); ASSUMPTION for audit log mechanism (not explicitly required by RFP — standard practice for regulatory audit preparedness).

**Step 13 — Monitor Subcontractor Insurance (if required)**

ASSUMPTION: If the Proponent requires subcontractors to carry their own insurance (as is standard Alberta commercial construction practice), establish a subcontractor insurance compliance register. Collect certificates of insurance and additional insured endorsements from each subcontractor before they commence work on site. Review and log expiry dates.

**Source:** RFP §4.2.1.2 (independent subcontractors sub-coverage); ASSUMPTION for subcontractor flow-down (location TBD).

**Step 14 — Respond to County Requests for Evidence**

If the County requests written documentation of insurance coverage at any time during the project (§4.2.6), provide current certificates and endorsements within a commercially reasonable time. ASSUMPTION: 5 business days is a reasonable response time unless the County specifies otherwise.

**Source:** RFP §4.2.6; ASSUMPTION for response time.
