## Purpose

DEL-021-02 exists to satisfy the Owner's mandatory surety bonding requirements as a condition of contract execution under CCDC 14–2013. The Performance Bond protects the Owner against non-performance by the Design-Builder; the Labour & Material Payment Bond protects subcontractors and material suppliers against non-payment by the Design-Builder.

This deliverable is a pre-construction gate: the contract cannot be lawfully executed and Work cannot commence until both bonds are in place. Failure to deliver bonds within the 10-day window (RFP §2.12.3) risks forfeiture of bid security and potential loss of the contract award.

**Source for purpose:** RFP §2.12 (Surety Bonding), §2.7 (Contract Agreement), Decomposition OBJ-008 (Commercial & Legal Compliance).

## Principles

### 1. Bonds Are a Contract Prerequisite, Not a Post-Award Option
The RFP explicitly requires bonds "at the time of execution of the Contract Agreement" (RFP §2.12.1) and within 10 days of award notification (RFP §2.12.3). This is a hard deadline. The Project Manager must initiate surety engagement immediately upon award notification — not after contract review.

### 2. Leverage the Existing Surety Relationship
The Consent of Surety required at bid submission (DEL-021-01, Addendum 1 §4.11) establishes the surety relationship before award. This relationship should be activated immediately for performance bonding. Switching surety companies post-award introduces timeline risk.

**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md (declared upstream dependency on DEL-021-01); Addendum 1 §4.11.

### 3. Bond Amounts Are Fixed Percentages — Confirm Contract Price First
Both bonds are 50% of the Contract price (RFP §2.12.1.1, §2.12.1.2). The Contract price is not known until award. The Project Manager must confirm the final stipulated price under CCDC 14–2013 before directing the surety to issue bonds at the correct face value.

### 4. Surety Must Be Alberta-Licensed
The surety company must be licensed to conduct business in Alberta. This is a non-negotiable eligibility criterion (RFP §2.12.2). Proponents should confirm their surety's Alberta licensing before finalizing the surety selection. The existing bid bond surety should already satisfy this requirement if Consent of Surety was properly provided at bid.

### 5. Bond Forms Must Be Owner-Acceptable
The Owner (Camrose County) must accept the bond forms used (RFP §2.12.2). ASSUMPTION: CCDC standard bond forms (A312 or equivalent) are likely acceptable given the CCDC 14–2013 contract framework. However, this should be confirmed with the Owner or their representative (architect/consultant) before bond execution to avoid rejection.

## Considerations

### Timeline Compression Risk
The 10-day window from award notification to bond delivery (RFP §2.12.3) is tight if surety arrangements have not been prepared in advance. The following actions should be completed before award to avoid delays:
- Surety pre-qualification for the project
- Consent of Surety obtained (done via DEL-021-01 at bid)
- Draft bond forms reviewed for Owner acceptability

### Coordination with Contract Execution (DEL-021-04)
Performance and Payment Bonds must be in place before or concurrent with contract execution (RFP §2.12.1: "at the time of his execution of the Contract Agreement"). The Project Manager must coordinate closely with the entity responsible for DEL-021-04 to ensure bond execution and contract signing are sequenced correctly.

**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md (declared dependency: DEL-021-04 depends on DEL-021-02).

### Bond Amount Calculation
At 50% of Contract price each, the Performance Bond and Payment Bond together represent 100% of the Contract price in aggregate surety exposure. For a project of this scope (design-build of a ~13,000 sq ft maintenance shop addition), the absolute bond amounts are TBD pending contract award. ASSUMPTION: Bond premium costs should have been included in the Proponent's bid pricing.

### Insurance vs. Bonding Distinction
Insurance (DEL-021-03) and bonding (DEL-021-02) are distinct and complementary risk instruments. Insurance protects against third-party loss events; bonds protect the Owner against contractor default or non-payment. Both are required under PKG-021 and neither substitutes for the other.
