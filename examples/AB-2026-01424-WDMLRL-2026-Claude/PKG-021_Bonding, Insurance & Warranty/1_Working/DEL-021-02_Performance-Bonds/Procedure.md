## Steps

### Step 1 — Receive and Date-Stamp Award Notification
**Responsible:** Project Manager
**Trigger:** Receipt of written award notification from Camrose County
**Action:**
1. Record the date of award notification in writing.
2. Calculate the 10-day bond delivery deadline (Day 0 = notification date; deadline = Day 10).
3. Notify Finance immediately to initiate surety engagement.
4. **Enrichment (Lensing Item X-001):** Contact surety company (same as DEL-021-01 Consent of Surety) to confirm continued engagement and willingness to issue performance/payment bonds at the anticipated project value. If surety confirms, proceed. If surety declines or is non-responsive, escalate immediately per Step 4A.

**Note:** The 10-day clock begins at notification, not at contract signing. Do not wait for contract review to begin this step.
**Source:** RFP §2.12.3

---

### Step 2 — Confirm Contract Price
**Responsible:** Project Manager / Finance
**Action:**
1. Confirm the final stipulated Contract price under CCDC 14–2013 with the Owner.
2. Calculate 50% bond amount for each bond:
   - Performance Bond = 50% x Contract Price = TBD
   - Labour & Material Payment Bond = 50% x Contract Price = TBD
3. Document confirmed amounts for surety instruction.

**Source:** RFP §2.12.1.1, §2.12.1.2

---

### Step 3 — Confirm Surety Company and Bond Form
**Responsible:** Finance
**Action:**
1. Contact the surety company used for the Consent of Surety (DEL-021-01).
2. Confirm the surety holds a valid license to conduct business in Alberta.
   - Obtain written confirmation of Alberta licensing (Certificate of Authority or equivalent).
3. **Enrichment (Lensing Item C-001):** Obtain confirmation of surety company financial capacity/rating. ASSUMPTION: Industry practice suggests a minimum A.M. Best rating of A- (or equivalent Canadian rating). Confirm acceptable rating standard with Owner if required.
4. Confirm with the Owner (or Owner's architect/representative) which bond forms are acceptable:
   - ASSUMPTION: CCDC A312 Performance Bond and Payment Bond forms are likely acceptable. Confirm before execution.
   - If the Owner specifies a proprietary form, obtain that form from the Owner.
   - **Enrichment (Lensing Item F-002):** Obtain Owner's written acceptance of bond forms in a specified format (formal letter, email confirmation, or countersignature). Record the format and date of acceptance.
5. Obtain draft bond forms from the surety for internal review.

**Source:** RFP §2.12.2; Guidance (Trade-off: Bond Form Selection)

---

### Step 3A — Owner Rejection of Bond Form (Enrichment — Lensing Item C-003)
**Responsible:** Finance / Project Manager
**Trigger:** Owner (or Owner's representative) does not accept the proposed bond form in Step 3.
**Action:**
1. Request from the Owner the specific bond form required, or the specific deficiencies in the proposed form.
2. Re-engage surety company to issue bonds on the Owner-specified form (if available) or to modify the proposed form.
3. If the surety cannot accommodate the Owner's required form, notify the Project Manager and escalate per Step 4A.
4. Reassess the 10-day timeline. If the bond form revision process extends the timeline, notify the Owner in writing of the anticipated delay and request an extension if necessary.
5. Once a revised bond form is accepted, return to Step 4.

**Source:** Derived from RFP §2.12.2 (forms acceptable to Owner); Lensing Item C-003.

---

### Step 4 — Execute Performance Bond and Payment Bond
**Responsible:** Finance (coordinating with Surety and Project Manager)
**Action:**
1. Direct surety to issue the Performance Bond at the confirmed amount (50% of Contract price).
2. Direct surety to issue the Labour & Material Payment Bond at the confirmed amount (50% of Contract price).
3. Review executed bonds before delivery:
   - Confirm face amounts match confirmed Contract price x 50% (ASSUMPTION: exact to the dollar, no rounding — Lensing Item D-001; confirm rounding convention with surety if needed)
   - Confirm Principal is correctly identified (Design-Builder entity name)
   - Confirm Obligee is correctly identified (Camrose County)
   - Confirm surety company name and signature/seal are present
   - Confirm bond form is Owner-acceptable (per Step 3 confirmation)
4. Obtain original executed bond instruments.
5. Record bond numbers (Performance Bond Number, Payment Bond Number) for Datasheet and contract register.

**Source:** RFP §2.12.1.1, §2.12.1.2, §2.12.2

---

### Step 4A — Contingency / Escalation (Enrichment — Lensing Item A-004)
**Responsible:** Project Manager / Finance
**Trigger:** Any of the following occur during Steps 1–4:
- Surety company declines or is unable to issue bonds within the 10-day timeline
- Surety company's Alberta license cannot be confirmed
- Surety financial rating does not meet requirements
- Owner rejects proposed bond forms and no resolution is reached in time (see Step 3A)
- Contract price cannot be confirmed in time to calculate bond amounts

**Action:**
1. Immediately notify the Project Manager (if Finance-initiated) or Finance (if PM-initiated).
2. Assess whether the issue can be resolved within the remaining days of the 10-day window.
3. If resolution is possible within timeline:
   - Engage alternative surety company (if surety-related issue). Confirm new surety holds valid Alberta license and meets financial capacity requirements.
   - Re-obtain Owner confirmation of bond form acceptability with new surety's forms.
4. If resolution is NOT possible within timeline:
   - Notify the Owner (Camrose County) in writing of the delay, explaining the cause and proposed resolution timeline.
   - Request a reasonable extension of the bond delivery deadline (note: the RFP states 10 days — any extension is at the Owner's discretion and may not be granted).
   - Document all communications for the project file.
5. Record the escalation event, actions taken, and outcome in the project file.

**Source:** Derived from RFP §2.12.3 (10-day deadline); Lensing Item A-004. ASSUMPTION: Escalation procedure based on standard project management practice — specific escalation protocol not stated in accessible RFP text.

---

### Step 5 — Deliver Bonds with Contract Agreement
**Responsible:** Project Manager / Finance
**Action:**
1. Assemble both executed bonds with the CCDC 14–2013 Contract Agreement documents.
2. Deliver or coordinate delivery of both bonds to Camrose County along with the executed Contract Agreement.
3. Document the delivery date. Confirm delivery is within 10 days of award notification (Step 1 deadline).
4. Obtain written acknowledgement or receipt of bond delivery from the Owner where possible.

**Source:** RFP §2.12.1 ("at the time of his execution of the Contract Agreement"), §2.12.3

---

### Step 6 — Retain Copies and Update Contract File
**Responsible:** Project Manager / Finance
**Action:**
1. Retain certified copies of both executed bonds in the project file.
2. Attach bond copies to the project's CCDC 14–2013 Contract Agreement file.
3. Record bond details (surety, bond numbers, face amounts, execution dates) in project contract register.
4. Distribute copies per the distribution list in Specification Documentation (minimum: Owner original, Proponent file copy; Architect copy TBD per Owner direction — Lensing Item F-003).
5. Close out DEL-021-02 by updating _STATUS.md per pipeline protocol.

**Source:** Derived from project documentation best practice; specific retention requirements not stated in accessible RFP text — ASSUMPTION based on standard commercial practice.
