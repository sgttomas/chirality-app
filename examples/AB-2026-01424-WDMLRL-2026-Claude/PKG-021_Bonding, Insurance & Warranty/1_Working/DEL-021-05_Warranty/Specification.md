## Terminology Note

The RFP uses the term "guarantee period" (RFP §2.10) while common industry usage and several sections of these production documents use "warranty period." These terms are synonymous in the context of this deliverable. The authoritative term from the RFP is "guarantee period"; "warranty period" is used interchangeably throughout these documents for readability. Where precision is required for audit or contractual purposes, prefer "guarantee period" as the governing term. (Source: RFP §2.10 heading; Semantic Lensing item E-002)

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-021-05 covers the full administration of the guarantee period obligations for the 2026 Design Build of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. It encompasses:

- Establishment and documentation of the guarantee period commencement date (CCC date) and expiry date.
- Tracking, notification management, and remediation scheduling for all deficiencies and defects identified by the Owner during the guarantee period.
- Monitoring of Contractor performance against the 10-day rectification obligation.
- Management of the Deficiency Holdback — retention, valuation, ledger maintenance, and release upon deficiency correction.
- Coordination with performance bond and insurance coverage that remains in effect during the warranty period.
- Post-completion inspection activities during the warranty period.
- Final warranty expiration management and documentation.

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Construction-phase deficiency management (covered by PKG-020 Commissioning & Closeout).
- Issuance of the Construction Completion Certificate (CCC) — covered by DEL-020-02.
- Performance bond and insurance issuance and maintenance — covered by DEL-021-02 and DEL-021-03 respectively.
- Contract execution and establishment of warranty terms — covered by DEL-021-04.
- Lien holdback administration during construction (governed by RFP §2.13.2 and Alberta's Prompt Payment and Construction Lien Act, which is a separate mechanism from the Deficiency Holdback under §2.11).

## Requirements

### REQ-021-05-01: Guarantee Coverage (SOW-0120)

**Requirement:** The Contractor shall guarantee all materials furnished and Work performed during the construction period and during the warranty period.

**Source:** RFP §2.10.2; SOW-0120 (Decomposition §8 Scope Ledger).

**Detail:**
- The guarantee period includes the construction period AND the two (2) years following the construction completion date noted on the CCC. (RFP §2.10.2)
- Neither final progress payment nor the CCC relieves the Contractor of responsibility for faulty materials or defective workmanship. (RFP §2.10.1)
- Faulty materials shall be replaced; defects and failures occurring during the two (2) year guarantee period shall be rectified and approved by the Owner in accordance with the Contract Documents. (RFP §2.10.3)
- If deemed necessary by the Owner, replacement of all or a portion of the Work may be required. (RFP §2.10.3)

**Warranty Period End-Trigger (Semantic Lensing item A-001):** The RFP establishes the guarantee period as two years post-CCC (RFP §2.10.2) but does not explicitly define the end-trigger mechanism -- i.e., whether the period terminates by calendar expiry alone, upon final inspection, or upon Owner written release. TBD -- the normative end-trigger must be established. This affects enforceability of final holdback release (see REQ-021-05-04 and Guidance OI-005). **ASSUMPTION: Owner written approval of final deficiency clearance triggers holdback release and formal period conclusion, but calendar expiry governs the scope of guarantee coverage.**

### REQ-021-05-02: Defect Rectification Timeline (SOW-0121)

**Requirement:** The Contractor shall carry out all guarantee work within ten (10) days of the Owner's written instruction to perform the Work, except as provided for emergencies.

**Source:** RFP §2.10.6; SOW-0121 (Decomposition §8 Scope Ledger).

**Detail:**
- The Owner shall notify the Contractor immediately (by any available means) upon observation or discovery of a deficiency or defect, and confirm notification in writing. (RFP §2.10.4)
- After the 10-day period expires without Contractor action, the Owner may take whatever action is necessary to have the Work done. (RFP §2.10.6)
- All costs resulting from guarantee work, whether performed by the Contractor, his representative, or the Owner, shall be borne by the Contractor. (RFP §2.10.7)
- The Contractor is liable to the Owner for all expenses, losses, or damages incurred as a result of faulty materials, defective workmanship, or failure to meet guarantee requirements. (RFP §2.10.8)
- RFP §2.10.9: The guarantee article does not restrict the liability of the Contractor in any way.

**Defect Classification (Semantic Lensing item A-002):** The RFP treats all defects uniformly under the 10-day rectification rule and does not establish a defect classification framework (safety-critical / functional / cosmetic). TBD -- determine whether to adopt a classification framework or explicitly confirm uniform treatment. If a classification framework is adopted, it must be agreed at warranty period commencement and reflected in the Deficiency Log schema (see Guidance OI-001). **ASSUMPTION: All defects are treated uniformly under the 10-day rule unless the Owner establishes a classification framework at warranty period commencement.**

### REQ-021-05-03: Emergency Work Authorization

**Requirement:** In the event that work must be done immediately to prevent serious damage, injury, or loss of life, the Owner may perform or cause to be performed the necessary Work and shall notify the Contractor accordingly.

**Source:** RFP §2.10.5.

**Detail:**
- Emergency work may be executed without the standard 10-day notice requirement.
- Cost of emergency guarantee work is still borne by the Contractor. (RFP §2.10.7)

### REQ-021-05-04: Deficiency Holdback Administration

**Requirement:** The Project Manager shall maintain records of the Deficiency Holdback — including amounts retained, deficiencies outstanding, and amounts released — throughout the guarantee period.

**Source:** RFP §2.11.

**Detail:**
- The Owner is entitled to retain from any payment an amount equal to the estimated value of outstanding deficiencies or defects as noted by the Owner (the "Deficiency Holdback"). (RFP §2.11.1)
- The Deficiency Holdback is retained until deficiencies/defects are corrected and approved by the Owner. (RFP §2.11.2)
- If the Contractor fails to correct deficiencies during the guarantee period, the Owner may withdraw from the Deficiency Holdback sufficient to correct all such deficiencies and defects. (RFP §2.11.3)
- When the Contractor corrects all deficiencies occurring during the guarantee period, the Owner shall pay all remaining Deficiency Holdback funds to the Contractor. (RFP §2.11.4)

**Holdback Valuation Methodology (Semantic Lensing item D-001):** RFP §2.11.1 states the Owner may retain "an amount equal to the estimated value" of outstanding deficiencies, but does not prescribe the estimation methodology. TBD -- a method for estimating deficiency value must be established (e.g., reference to original contract unit prices, independent cost estimates, or Owner's reasonable judgment). This operationalizes the holdback mechanism and enables consistent ledger entries. See Guidance OI-006.
