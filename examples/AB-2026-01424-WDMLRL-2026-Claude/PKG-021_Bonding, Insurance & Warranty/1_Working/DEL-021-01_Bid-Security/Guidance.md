## Purpose

Bid security exists to protect the Owner (Camrose County) from the risk that a successful proponent will decline to execute the contract after submission. By requiring a financial instrument equal to 10% of the bid amount, the Owner has recourse if the proponent refuses or fails to sign the CCDC 14–2013 contract or provide required post-award security and insurance within 21 days of contract presentation.

From the proponent's perspective, this deliverable:
- Demonstrates financial standing and commitment to project execution.
- Satisfies a mandatory proposal compliance requirement — omission automatically invalidates the bid (Add. 1 §4.11).
- Introduces a forfeiture risk if the proponent wins but fails to execute the contract.

This deliverable supports OBJ-008 (satisfy all bonding, insurance, and warranty obligations). [Source: Decomposition — OBJ-008 mapping, PKG-021]

## Principles

### P1: Bid Security Is a Hard Compliance Gate
The RFP is unambiguous: omission of the certified cheque or bid bond "shall invalidate the bid and shall be cause for rejection of the bid" (Add. 1 §4.11). There is no discretion reserved to the County to waive a missing bid security instrument. Treat this deliverable as the highest-priority item in the bid submission package.

### P2: The Consent of Surety Is a Separate, Mandatory Instrument
The bid bond or certified cheque and the Consent of Surety are two distinct requirements. Both must be present. The Consent of Surety provides the Owner with assurance that the same surety is committed to issuing the post-award performance and payment bonds (DEL-021-02). Engage the surety early enough to obtain both instruments before the submission deadline.

### P3: Surety Must Be Licensed in Alberta and Carry a Provincial Address
The RFP requires the surety to be "licensed to do business in the Province of Alberta" and requires the surety's Alberta address (or that of its Alberta representative agent) to appear on the Consent of Surety document (Add. 1 §4.11). A surety that is nationally recognized but lacks Alberta licensure, or that omits the required address, would create a compliance deficiency.

### P4: The 21-Day Window Creates a Hard Post-Award Deadline
If awarded, the proponent has 21 days from contract presentation to sign the CCDC 14–2013 and provide required security and insurance (Add. 1 §4.11). Failure results in forfeiture and potential financial liability. Bid preparation should include a contingency plan for rapid contract execution, not just bid submission.

### P5: Bid Bond vs. Certified Cheque — Operational Tradeoff
The RFP permits either form. A bid bond is the standard commercial practice for projects of this scale (ASSUMPTION: based on common practice for publicly tendered design-build projects in Alberta; no explicit RFP guidance on preference). A certified cheque ties up liquid funds until the bid is decided. A bid bond is generally preferred for proponents with an established surety relationship, as it avoids cash outlay while satisfying the requirement.

## Considerations

### Surety Lead Time
Obtaining a bid bond and Consent of Surety requires the surety to underwrite the proponent's financial capacity. This process is not instantaneous. ASSUMPTION: Surety underwriting for a project of this scale typically requires 2–5 business days at minimum, with more time needed if the proponent does not have an existing surety credit facility. Initiate surety contact immediately.

### Bid Amount Uncertainty
The bid security amount is 10% of the bid amount (Add. 1 §4.11). The bid amount itself is not known until the cost estimate is finalized. The bid bond must therefore be sized based on the final bid figure. ASSUMPTION: The bid bond is typically executed in a "not to exceed" or exact face-value format; coordinate with the surety on their preferred approach once the bid estimate is finalized.

### Addendum Acknowledgment
Addendum No. 1 (dated Feb 19, 2026) introduced the bid security requirement. Proponents must acknowledge receipt of this addendum using Appendix A (RFP §4.1). Failure to acknowledge may constitute non-compliance. Ensure the Appendix A form is completed and included in the proposal package.

### Physical Submission Requirement
The bid and its accompanying bid security must be delivered in a sealed physical envelope to:

> Camrose County, Attention: Darren King, 3755 – 43 Ave, Camrose, AB, T4V 3S8

No faxed or electronically submitted proposals are accepted (RFP §1.3.1). Allow sufficient courier/delivery time to ensure receipt before 2:00:00 p.m. MST on April 2, 2026. Late deliveries will not be evaluated and will be returned unopened.

### Withdrawal Window
If the bid security has been submitted but the proponent wishes to withdraw, the proposal can be modified or withdrawn up to 30 minutes before closing time (2:00:00 p.m. MST on April 2, 2026). After that window closes, the proposal — and its bid security — are irrevocable for 30 days (RFP §2.4, §2.16).

### Downstream Dependency
The same surety that issues the Consent of Surety for bid security will typically issue the post-award Performance Bond and L&M Payment Bond (DEL-021-02). Early surety engagement establishes this relationship and reduces friction at contract execution.

### DEL-021-01 to DEL-021-02 Handoff (Enrichment: E-002)
Upon contract award, the bid security deliverable transitions to the performance bonding phase (DEL-021-02). The following information and artifacts must be transferred from the bid security process to performance bonding:
- **Surety identity and contact:** The surety company, agent, and contact details used for the Consent of Surety.
- **Consent of Surety terms:** A copy of the executed Consent of Surety, which represents the surety's commitment to issue performance and payment bonds.
- **Underwriting documentation:** Financial statements and bonding capacity information already provided to the surety during bid security underwriting.
- **Contract award notification:** The date of contract presentation, which triggers the 21-day execution window (Add. 1 §4.11).
- **Bid amount and contract price:** The final contract price, which determines performance bond amounts (50% each per RFP §2.12).

### Surety Underwriting Contingency (Enrichment: F-003)
If surety underwriting is denied or delayed beyond the submission deadline, the proponent must have a contingency plan:
- **Fallback to certified cheque:** The RFP permits a certified cheque as an alternative to a bid bond (Add. 1 §4.11). If the surety cannot complete underwriting in time, a certified cheque equal to 10% of the bid amount can be substituted. This ties up liquid capital but satisfies the bid security requirement.
- **Alternative surety:** If the primary surety declines underwriting, an alternative Alberta-licensed surety may be engaged. Time permitting, this should be explored before defaulting to a certified cheque.
- **Decision trigger:** ASSUMPTION: If the surety has not confirmed underwriting approval by TBD [recommend at least 3–5 business days before the April 2 deadline], the Project Manager and Finance lead should escalate and activate the certified cheque contingency.

### Bid Amount Finalization Timing (Enrichment: X-002)
The bid bond face value must equal exactly 10% of the bid amount (Add. 1 §4.11), which means the bid bond cannot be finalized until the cost estimate is complete. This creates a critical-path dependency:
- The cost estimate (Proposal Section 3.0) must be finalized before the surety can issue the bid bond with the exact face value.
- ASSUMPTION: Surety processing of the final bid bond (after underwriting is already approved) typically requires 1–2 business days for bond issuance.
- **Recommended milestone:** The bid amount should be finalized no later than TBD [recommend at least 3–5 business days before the April 2, 2026 deadline] to allow sufficient time for bond issuance, executive sign-off, and physical assembly of the submission package.
- If the bid amount is expected to change until very close to the deadline, coordinate with the surety on their process for last-minute bond amount adjustments (some sureties permit a "not to exceed" approach during underwriting, with exact amount confirmed at issuance).

[Source: Add. 1 §4.11; RFP §4.8; ASSUMPTION for timeline recommendation]
