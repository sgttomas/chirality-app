## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and submit the Bid Security Package for the 2026 Design Build of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition (Camrose County RFP). It is addressed to the Project Manager and Finance lead responsible for bid security coordination and submission.

Successful completion of this procedure satisfies:
- SOW-0097: Provide 10% bid bond or certified cheque with proposal submission
- SOW-0098: Provide Consent of Surety with proposal submission
- REQ-021-01-001 through REQ-021-01-013 (see Specification.md)

## Steps

### Phase 1: Surety Engagement

**Step 1.1 — Select and Contact Alberta-Licensed Surety**
Contact a surety company that is:
- Licensed to do business in the Province of Alberta, and
- Has a physical Alberta address or an Alberta representative agent whose address can appear on the Consent of Surety document.

[Source: Add. 1 §4.11]

Provide the surety with:
- Project name and RFP reference (AB-2026-01424-WDMLRL-2026-Claude)
- Owner: Camrose County
- Anticipated bid amount range (update to final amount as soon as known)
- RFP closing date: April 2, 2026, 2:00:00 p.m. MST
- Contract form: CCDC 14–2013 Design-Build Stipulated Price Contract

**Step 1.2 — Initiate Surety Underwriting**
Provide the surety with the financial documentation required for underwriting. ASSUMPTION: The following is a typical underwriting documentation checklist for construction surety bonds in Alberta. Specific documents required depend on the surety's underwriting process and the proponent's existing credit facility. Confirm with the selected surety which items are required. [Enrichment: F-001]

Typical underwriting documentation (ASSUMPTION — confirm with surety):
- Most recent 2–3 years of audited or reviewed financial statements
- Current work-in-progress (WIP) schedule
- Bonding capacity letter or existing credit facility documentation (if available)
- Corporate/company profile and organizational chart
- Project experience summaries (relevant completed projects of similar scope/value)
- Bank reference letter
- Completed surety application form (provided by the surety)

TBD: If the proponent has an existing surety credit facility, most of this documentation may already be on file. Confirm with the surety what updated or supplementary information is needed for this specific bid.

**Step 1.3 — Confirm Surety Can Issue Both Instruments**
Confirm in writing that the selected surety will issue:
1. The bid bond (or confirm alternative if using certified cheque), and
2. The Consent of Surety.

Both are required simultaneously for submission.

---

### Phase 2: Bid Bond / Certified Cheque Preparation

**Step 2.1 — Determine Instrument Type**
Decide whether to submit a bid bond or a certified cheque.
- Bid bond: preferred if a surety credit facility is available (avoids cash outlay). [ASSUMPTION: industry practice]
- Certified cheque: fallback if surety bond cannot be obtained in time. Ensure the cheque is payable to "Camrose County of the Province of Alberta."

[Source: Add. 1 §4.11]

**Step 2.2 — Finalize Bid Amount**
Obtain the final bid amount from the cost estimate team (Proposal Section 3.0 — Cost Estimate). The bid security face value must equal exactly 10% of this amount.

[Source: Add. 1 §4.11; RFP §4.8]

**Step 2.3 — Execute Bid Bond or Obtain Certified Cheque**

*If bid bond:*
- Provide the final bid amount to the surety.
- Surety prepares and executes the bid bond instrument.
- Review the executed bid bond for accuracy: verify face value, proponent name, owner name (Camrose County), project description, and expiry/irrevocability period.

*If certified cheque:*
- Obtain a certified cheque from the proponent's bank.
- Confirm: payable to "Camrose County of the Province of Alberta," amount = 10% of bid, date is current.

[Source: Add. 1 §4.11]

**Step 2.4 — Obtain Internal Executive Sign-Off**
Present the executed bid bond (or certified cheque) to the proponent's authorized executive/officer for review and approval before inclusion in the proposal package. Document this approval in writing.

[Source: ASSUMPTION — derived from _CONTEXT.md success criteria: "Obtain executive sign-off on bid bond"]

---

### Phase 3: Consent of Surety Preparation

**Step 3.1 — Request Consent of Surety from Surety Company**
Instruct the surety to prepare the Consent of Surety. Confirm the document will include:
- The surety company's Alberta address OR the Alberta representative agent's address.
- Reference to the project and the proponent.
- Surety's commitment to issue performance and payment bonds upon contract award (as required by CCDC 14–2013 and RFP §2.12).

[Source: Add. 1 §4.11]

**Step 3.2 — Review Consent of Surety Document**
Upon receipt from the surety, review the Consent of Surety to verify:
- Alberta address is present and legible.
- Surety company name and signature are present.
- Project name and proponent name are correctly stated.
- Document is consistent with the bid bond (same surety, same proponent, same project).

[Source: Add. 1 §4.11]

**Step 4.3 — Go/No-Go Decision Gate (Enrichment: X-003)**
Before sealing the proposal envelope, the Project Manager (or designated authorized person) must complete a mandatory go/no-go review. This is a formal decision point, not a best-practice suggestion.

**Go/No-Go Checklist:**

| Check | Pass Criteria | Status |
|-------|--------------|--------|
| Bid bond or certified cheque enclosed | Instrument present, amount = 10% of bid, correct payee | [ ] Pass / [ ] Fail |
| Consent of Surety enclosed | Present, Alberta address visible, surety name matches bond | [ ] Pass / [ ] Fail |
| Surety licensed in Alberta | Registry confirmation obtained | [ ] Pass / [ ] Fail |
| Addendum No. 1 acknowledged (Appendix A) | Form completed and signed | [ ] Pass / [ ] Fail |

| Forfeiture language reviewed | Bond instrument consistent with Add. 1 §4.11 terms | [ ] Pass / [ ] Fail |

**Decision rule:** All checks must pass before sealing. If any check fails, do not seal or submit. Escalate to the Project Manager and Finance lead for remediation.

**Authorized decision-maker:** Project Manager (per _CONTEXT.md Key Roles). [Source: ASSUMPTION — standard bid submission practice; _CONTEXT.md Key Roles]

No faxed or electronically submitted proposals are accepted. [Source: RFP §1.3.1]

**Step 5.2 — Obtain Delivery Confirmation**
Obtain proof of delivery (courier receipt, hand-delivery acknowledgment, or equivalent). Retain this record.

**Step 5.3 — Monitor Post-Submission Obligations (Enrichment: A-003)**
After submission, establish a tracking mechanism to monitor the following compliance windows:

| Obligation | Period | Start Trigger | End / Action |
|-----------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| Proposal irrevocability | 30 days | RFP closing date (April 2, 2026) | Approximately May 2, 2026 — proposals may be released if no award. [Source: RFP §2.16] |
| Award notification watch | Ongoing during irrevocability period | Submission | Upon receipt of award notification, immediately trigger 21-day contract execution window. |
| Contract execution window | 21 days | Contract presented to proponent (by hand delivery, registered mail, or courier) | Sign and return contract + provide required security and insurance, or bid security is subject to forfeiture. [Source: Add. 1 §4.11] |
| DEL-021-02 activation | Upon award | Award notification received | Escalate to DEL-021-02 (Performance Bonds) and DEL-021-04 (Contract Execution). Transfer surety information per handoff checklist (see Guidance — DEL-021-01 to DEL-021-02 Handoff). |

**Tracking mechanism:** The Project Manager shall set calendar reminders or project management system entries for:
1. The 30-day irrevocability expiry date (approximately May 2, 2026).
2. Daily monitoring for award notification during the irrevocability period.
3. Upon award notification: immediate calendar entry for the 21-day contract execution deadline.

**Responsible party:** Project Manager (per _CONTEXT.md Key Roles).

ASSUMPTION: The specific tracking tool (calendar, project management software, etc.) is at the Project Manager's discretion. The key requirement is that the monitoring is proactive, not reactive.
