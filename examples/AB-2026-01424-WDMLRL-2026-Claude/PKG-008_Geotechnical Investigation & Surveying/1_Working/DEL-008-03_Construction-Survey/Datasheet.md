## Attributes

### Survey Purpose and Role

The Construction Survey translates final Issued for Construction (IFC) design drawings into physical field control, positioning, grades, and alignment at the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition site. It is the primary spatial link between the design deliverables (PKG-001 through PKG-007) and the physical construction execution (PKG-010 through PKG-018).

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Description); WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §3 (SOW-0003); RFP §3.3.2

### Survey Scope Elements

| Element | Description | Source |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Layout and alignment survey | Establishing horizontal positions of building footprint, structural elements, pad locations, access routes, and utility corridors on the ground | _CONTEXT.md (Scope); WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §3 (SOW-0003) |
| Grade establishment | Setting and verifying vertical control (elevations) to support slab-on-grade, site grading, drainage, and cistern installation | _CONTEXT.md (Scope); RFP §3.3.2 |
| Construction staking | Physical placement of stakes/markers in the field for use by construction trades and subcontractors | _CONTEXT.md (Scope) |
| Field verification and documentation | Confirming that constructed elements match design positions and elevations; producing field records | _CONTEXT.md (Scope) |
| Progress survey updates | Periodic surveys during construction to verify ongoing positional compliance | _CONTEXT.md (Scope); MEMORY.md |

## Construction

### Relationship to Building Scope Elements

The construction survey directly supports the following site and building elements from the design requirements (RFP §3.4, Appendix B):

- Building concrete structure (35 ft ceiling) — requires precise column and foundation layout
- Two drive-through repair bays and one enclosed wash bay — requires bay layout and alignment staking
- Service pit / trench — requires accurate grade and positional control for below-grade element
- 50,000 L cistern — requires layout and grade control for cistern installation area
- Gravel driving surface (motor scraper-class loading) — requires grade establishment for pavement design
- Site grading with positive drainage and no impact to neighbouring properties — requires elevation control throughout the expansion area
- Cement pads as shown on conceptual drawings — requires positional staking per Appendix B layout
- Mud sump (exterior, wash bay) — requires positional and grade control

**Source:** RFP §3.4; Appendix B (Shop conceptual drawing); _CONTEXT.md (Scope)
