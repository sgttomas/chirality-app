# Procedure — DEL-008-03 Construction Survey

**Document Type:** Procedure (operational)
**Deliverable:** DEL-008-03 Construction Survey
**Package:** PKG-008 Geotechnical Investigation & Surveying
**Project:** 2026 Design Build — West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition
**Revision:** Pass 3 — 2026-02-26

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and deliver the Construction Survey for the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition (DEL-008-03). The procedure covers the activities from initial readiness confirmation through final field documentation handoff, supporting construction of the addition at SW 14–44–21–W4, near Ferintosh, Alberta.

The construction survey translates design intent (IFC drawings) to field positions, grades, and alignment so that construction trades and subcontractors can correctly execute all elements of the addition within the contractual deadline of December 31, 2026.

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (Description); WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §3 (SOW-0003); RFP §3.3.2

## Steps

### Phase A: Construction Survey Mobilization and Planning

**Step A1 — Retrieve Preliminary Survey Control Data**

Obtain from DEL-008-02 all control point coordinates (horizontal and vertical), benchmark descriptions, monument descriptions, and the control network diagram. Confirm the geodetic datum, map projection, and vertical datum used (see Datasheet Key Site Information and Specification REQ-008-03-01). Verify that at least two control points are accessible and undisturbed at the start of construction survey work.

- Record: Control point IDs, coordinates, monument descriptions, and datum confirmation. Initiate the chain-of-custody record (REQ-008-03-08b) documenting: date received, source surveyor, data format, and datum confirmation. [Lensing item E-004]
- Source: DEL-008-02 Preliminary Site Survey (_DEPENDENCIES.md upstream)

**Step A2 — Verify Control Integrity**

Before use, confirm the integrity of preliminary survey control points by occupying at least two points and performing a closure or redundant check. If any control point is disturbed or suspect:
- Document the condition
- Re-establish or replace the point using the remaining undisturbed network
- Notify the construction manager of any significant changes to the control network

- Record: Control verification check results (including measured closure values and pass/fail determination against the tolerance threshold)
- Note: Specific closure tolerances are TBD — to be defined by the licensed Surveyor per Alberta professional practice and documented in the Construction Survey Plan (REQ-008-03-06a). The closure tolerance threshold shall be stated in the Construction Survey Plan as a quantitative pass/fail criterion for this step. [Lensing item A-005]

**Step A3 — Prepare Construction Survey Plan**

Prepare a written Construction Survey Plan describing:
- Control network configuration (permanent and working control)
- Survey methodology to be used (total station, GNSS, or combination), including decision criteria for methodology selection based on: required accuracy for each element type, site conditions (obstructions, satellite visibility for GNSS), and efficiency considerations. The selected methodology shall be justified against the accuracy tolerances defined in the plan. [Lensing item A-004]
- Accuracy targets for horizontal and vertical control (TBD — per Surveyor's professional judgment and design precision requirements; see REQ-008-03-06 and REQ-008-03-06a)
- Closure tolerance threshold for control verification (Step A2)
- Key staking milestones keyed to the construction schedule
- Progress survey frequency and reporting approach
- Phasing provisions addressing geotech-dependent foundation timing (see Guidance Principle 6)
- Re-staking provisions for elements initially staked from preliminary drawings

- Record: Construction Survey Plan document
- Source: _CONTEXT.md (Scope); standard construction survey practice (ASSUMPTION for plan format)

**Step A4 — Establish and Protect Permanent Control Monuments**

Establish a minimum of two permanent control monuments on stable ground outside the expected construction disturbance zone, referenced to the preliminary survey network. Document with photographs and written descriptions.

- Record: Monument descriptions, photographs, coordinates
- Note: Monument count and placement are TBD pending field assessment; minimum two is an ASSUMPTION consistent with standard practice

### Phase B: Pre-Construction Layout

**Step B1 — Obtain and Review IFC Drawings for Initial Layout Elements**

Obtain P.Eng.-stamped IFC drawings for the building footprint, foundation, and site elements. Confirm drawing revision reference. Review for any ambiguities in the layout geometry before proceeding to the field.

- Record: IFC drawing reference (sheet number, revision, stamp date) for each element
- Source: REQ-008-03-02; RFP §3.3.2

**Step B2 — Establish Site Grid / Building Control**

From the permanent control monuments, establish a site grid or building control line system aligned with the building axes as shown on IFC drawings. Record all offsets, tie distances, and key reference points.

- Record: Building control line documentation

**Step B3 — Stake Building Footprint and Foundation Extents**

Stake the building corners, column grid lines, and foundation extents for the addition. Include:
- Building perimeter corners
- Column grid intersections
- Foundation excavation limits (with allowance for working space as required by the structural/foundation design)

**HOLD POINT — Foundation Final Staking Gate:** Final foundation form staking (as opposed to rough excavation layout) shall not proceed until:
1. The Structural Engineer has confirmed that the foundation IFC drawings are final and P.Eng.-stamped
2. Written confirmation is obtained from the Design-Builder or Construction Manager that the foundation design is approved for construction
3. The confirmation is documented in the staking record for this element

If foundation design is not yet final, stake only rough excavation extents from the preliminary building layout and flag as "PRELIMINARY — confirm before forming." Preliminary stakes shall be clearly marked as non-final in the field and in the staking record. [Lensing item F-003; see also Guidance Trade-off 3, Principle 6, and REQ-008-03-02]

- Record: Staking record for each corner/column; drawing reference
- Source: RFP §3.1, §3.4; WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §3 (SOW-0022, SOW-0023)

**Step B4 — Stake Service Pit / Trench**

Lay out the service pit or trench location, width, and length per IFC structural drawings. Establish invert grade control stakes for the pit floor elevation.

- Record: Staking record; invert elevation reference
- Source: RFP §3.4; WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §3 (SOW-0028)

**Step B5 — Stake Cistern and Mud Sump Locations**

Stake the location and any invert or inflow/outflow grade requirements for:
- 50,000 L cistern (per plumbing IFC)
- Mud sump (exterior, wash bay; per civil/structural IFC)

- Record: Staking record; invert elevations referenced to site benchmark
- Source: RFP §3.4; WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §3 (SOW-0041, SOW-0027b)

**Step B6 — Establish Subgrade Grade Control for Driving Surfaces**

Set grade stakes or offset hubs for all driving surface areas subject to topsoil stripping and gravel surfacing. Grade control shall be consistent with the civil grading design (PKG-005 IFC).

- Record: Grade sheets with design grade vs. set grade
- Source: RFP §3.3.2 (topsoil stripping), §3.4 (gravel surface); WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §3 (SOW-0075, SOW-0077)

**Step B7 — Stake Utility Corridors**

Stake the locations of utility tie-in corridors for natural gas, electrical service, and communications lines per the applicable design drawings.

- Record: Staking record; drawing reference
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §3 (SOW-0080, SOW-0081, SOW-0082)

**Step B8 — Stake Cement Pads and Septic Tank**

Stake the positions of cement pads and the new septic holding tank location as shown on the conceptual and IFC drawings.

- Record: Staking record; drawing reference
- Source: Appendix B (Shop); WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §3 (SOW-0043, SOW-0078)

### Phase C: Construction Phase Verification and Monitoring

**Step C1 — Foundation Excavation Verification**

After foundation excavation and before forming, survey the excavated elevations and horizontal extents. Compare to IFC drawings.

- If within tolerance: document as "verified — proceed"
- If out of tolerance: document discrepancy; report to construction manager and structural engineer; do not proceed until resolved

- Record: Verification survey results; discrepancy report (if applicable)
- Source: REQ-008-03-06; standard construction survey practice

**Step C2 — Foundation Form Verification**

After foundation forms are set and before concrete pour:
- Survey form tops and key reference points
- Confirm forms are positioned within tolerance relative to design

- Record: Form verification record; drawing reference
- Source: REQ-008-03-06

**Step C3 — Slab-on-Grade Grade Control**

Provide grade control for slab-on-grade pour. Establish grade references at intervals sufficient for the concrete crew to achieve design slab elevations. Confirm that slab drainage slopes (if any) are reflected in grade control.

- Record: Slab grade control records
- Source: REQ-008-03-04; RFP §3.4

**Step C3a — Slab Drainage Slope Verification**

Before the slab pour, explicitly verify that the slab drainage slopes match the civil grading design. Confirm:
- Slope direction is consistent with the drainage design (positive drainage away from building per REQ-008-03-04)
- Slope gradient matches the civil design values at multiple check points across the slab area
- Any discrepancy is reported to the civil design engineer and construction manager per the discrepancy resolution process in REQ-008-03-04

This step ensures that slab drainage compliance is verified as a discrete, documented check rather than a parenthetical note within general grade control. [Lensing item X-003]

- Record: Slab drainage slope verification record (measured slope direction and gradient vs. design; pass/fail determination)
- Source: REQ-008-03-04; RFP §3.4; civil grading design (PKG-005 IFC)

**Step C4 — Steel Plate Embedment Layout**

Before slab pour, confirm the positions of steel plate embedments in the concrete floor (for heavy tracked/packer equipment). Staking must match IFC structural embedment plan.

- Record: Embedment staking record; drawing reference
- Source: RFP §3.4; WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §3 (SOW-0024)

**Step C5 — Subgrade Verification Before Gravel Placement**

After topsoil stripping and subgrade preparation on driving surfaces, and before gravel is placed, survey subgrade elevations. Compare to design grades.

- Record: Subgrade elevation survey; comparison to civil design grades
- Source: REQ-008-03-05; RFP §3.3.2

**Step C6 — Progress Surveys**

At intervals established in the Construction Survey Plan (TBD — frequency per construction schedule), conduct progress surveys of the overall site and key construction elements. Document the status of completed work positions relative to IFC drawings.

- Record: Progress survey records; date of survey; elements checked
- Source: REQ-008-03-07; _CONTEXT.md (Scope)

**Step C7 — Notify County of Inspection Hold Points**

For any survey-related inspection milestone where the County project representative is required to be present (per RFP §3.3.2: "County representative shall be present at all inspections"), notify the County project manager in advance and provide copies of survey records after the inspection.

**Candidate survey-related inspection hold points** (TBD — to be confirmed by County PM and Construction Manager):
1. Foundation excavation verification (Step C1) — before foundation forming proceeds
2. Foundation form verification (Step C2) — before concrete pour
3. Slab-on-grade grade control verification (Step C3) — before slab pour
4. Subgrade verification before gravel placement (Step C5) — before paving
5. Final documentation package review (Step D1) — before project close-out

**ASSUMPTION**: the above list identifies the survey milestones most likely to constitute County inspection hold points, but RFP §3.3.2 states the County representative shall be present at "all inspections" without specifically enumerating survey-related hold points. The County PM and Construction Manager should confirm which of these milestones require County presence. [Lensing item D-002]

- Record: Notification and delivery records for each hold point; County acknowledgment of inspection attendance or waiver
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §3 (SOW-0084, SOW-0085); Specification REQ-008-03-10

### Phase D: Documentation and Handoff

**Step D1 — Compile Field Documentation Package**

Compile all field notes, staking records, grade sheets, verification records, and progress survey records into an organized documentation package. At minimum the package shall include:
- Control network documentation (from Step A4)
- Staking records for all elements (Steps B3–B8)
- Verification records (Steps C1–C5)
- Progress survey records (Step C6)

- Record: Documentation package
- Source: REQ-008-03-08; _CONTEXT.md

**Step D2 — Prepare Construction Survey Summary Report (if required)**

If required by the construction manager or County, prepare a brief Construction Survey Summary Report describing the survey work performed, control network used, elements staked, and any significant discrepancies encountered.

- Record: Construction Survey Summary Report
- Note: Whether a formal report is required is TBD — not explicitly stated in the RFP; to be confirmed with construction manager (ASSUMPTION that documentation is required; report format is TBD)

**Step D3 — Control Data Handoff to As-Built Survey Team**

Prepare and transfer a control network handoff package to the as-built survey team (DEL-008-04), including:
- Control point descriptions and coordinates
- Geodetic datum, map projection, and vertical datum confirmation
- Any changes or additions to the network made during construction survey
- Notes on disturbed or retired control points

Complete the chain-of-custody record (REQ-008-03-08b) documenting: date transferred, recipient, data format, and datum confirmation. [Lensing item E-004]

- Record: Control handoff package; chain-of-custody transfer record
- Source: _REFERENCES.md (downstream: DEL-008-04 As-Built Survey); Specification REQ-008-03-08b
