# Procedure — DEL-008-04 As-Built Survey

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce the As-Built Survey deliverable (DEL-008-04) for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. It covers activities from pre-field preparation through final document delivery and Owner acceptance.

The procedure applies to the Surveyor responsible for DEL-008-04 and the project manager (Proponent side) who coordinates with the Owner.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Pre-Field Preparation

1.1 Obtain and review DEL-008-03 construction survey records: control point coordinates, descriptions, and datum reference. Confirm that control points are still accessible and undisturbed.

1.2 Review the IFC drawings for all project components (building addition, site grading, cistern, septic, driving surfaces, utility tie-ins). Identify all elements to be verified and documented.

1.3 Review contractor as-built markups and any construction records for underground or buried infrastructure. Note locations of subsurface elements that cannot be directly observed post-construction.

1.4 Prepare a field survey plan:
- Define the survey traverse or control network to be used.
- List all features to be collected, with attribute requirements.
- **Confirm datum before field work commences** (this is a prerequisite gate -- field work shall not proceed with TBD datum): horizontal (NAD83 or provincial grid -- to be confirmed from DEL-008-03 records or Owner direction) and vertical (CGVD2013 or local benchmark -- to be confirmed from DEL-008-03 records or Owner direction). Record the confirmed datum in the field survey plan. [Lensing: X-002]

1.5 Confirm site access logistics with the project manager: entry point, escort requirements, active operations areas to avoid, safety requirements at the WDMLRL active landfill site.

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md; Guidance Principles P-2, P-3; Specification REQ-004, REQ-005.

---

### Step 2 — Field Survey

2.1 Establish or verify survey control by occupying or tying to DEL-008-03 control points. Check closure to confirm control integrity.

2.2 Conduct topographic survey of the completed site:
- Building addition: exterior corners, finished floor elevation, door thresholds, overhead door sills.
- Gravel driving surfaces: edges/limits, cross-section spot elevations where relevant.
- Grading and drainage features: swales, berms, drainage outlets, surface grades at key locations.
- Existing maintenance shop building: document reference points for positional context.

2.3 Collect as-built positions for infrastructure elements:
- Cistern: above-grade access points, fill connections, approximate location. (Underground body location from construction records — ASSUMPTION.)
- Septic holding tank (2,000 US gallon per RFP ss3.4): access lid location, approximate tank footprint from construction records. Confirm whether existing tank was relocated or replaced. (ASSUMPTION: underground body location from construction records.) [Lensing: B-004]
- Utility tie-in points: natural gas, electrical, communications — above-grade entry points and meters/disconnects.
- Mud sump / wash bay drain (exterior): location and invert if accessible.

2.4 Collect any additional features identified during field review that were not anticipated but are material to the as-built record.

2.5 Record field notes including: date, weather conditions, instrument type and serial number, crew members, control points occupied, features collected, and any anomalies or deviations from design observed during survey. [Lensing: E-001 -- field note content list normalized with Records table]

Source: Specification REQ-002, REQ-003, REQ-004; RFP §3.4; Guidance P-3, P-4.

---

### Step 3 — Office Reduction and Drawing Preparation

3.1 Download and process field data. Check for blunders or outliers; resolve against field notes.

3.2 Perform adjustment and closure check on the survey control network. Confirm accuracy meets the agreed-upon standard (TBD per Guidance GAP-001).

3.3 Prepare as-built drawings:
- Plan view showing all surveyed features with coordinates, dimensions, and elevations as appropriate.
- Elevation annotations at key finished floor, grade, and infrastructure points.
- Reference to IFC drawing numbers where applicable.
- Title block with project name, legal description, date of survey, surveyor name, and datum reference.

3.4 Compare as-built survey data against IFC drawings:
- Identify any deviations (position, elevation, extent).
- Classify deviations using the following categories. **Classification criteria are TBD -- the Surveyor and PM must define numerical thresholds or qualitative criteria before performing the comparison to ensure repeatable and consistent classification across survey personnel.** See Guidance GAP-004.
  - **Within tolerance:** Deviation is within the agreed conformance tolerance (TBD -- see REQ-003, REQ-005). No further action required.
  - **Minor:** Deviation exceeds tolerance but does not affect structural integrity, functionality, or regulatory compliance. Document in the as-built record.
  - **Material:** Deviation exceeds tolerance and may affect structural integrity, functionality, regulatory compliance, or CCC issuance. Requires separate deviation table/markup and prompt reporting to PM.
- Prepare a deviation table or markup if any material deviations are found. The deviation table is a separate artifact (see Specification Documentation section). [Lensing: C-003, E-002]

3.5 Prepare the final as-built report including:
- Survey scope and methodology.
- Datum and coordinate system used.
- Control points used and their provenance (DEL-008-03 reference).
- Accuracy statement.
- Construction verification summary (conformance or deviation findings).
- List of artifacts delivered.

Source: Specification REQ-003, REQ-004, REQ-005, REQ-006; Guidance P-3.

---

### Step 4 — Internal QA Review

4.1 Review drawings and report for completeness: all required components listed in Step 2 are present.

4.2 Check coordinate and elevation data for consistency across drawings and report.

4.3 Confirm datum reference is clearly stated and consistent throughout.

4.4 Confirm all material deviations from IFC design are documented and reported.

4.5 Confirm deliverable format meets agreed Owner requirements (TBD per Guidance GAP-002).

Source: Specification Verification section; Guidance P-2, P-3.

---

### Step 5 — Submission to Owner

5.1 Compile the complete deliverable package (drawings, data files, report — format TBD).

5.2 Submit to the Owner / Camrose County project manager for review and acceptance.

5.3 Respond to any Owner review comments; revise and resubmit if required.

5.4 Obtain Owner written acceptance or acknowledgment of the as-built survey deliverable.

Source: Specification REQ-006; RFP §2.14 (Completion and Acceptance); RFP §3.1.

---

### Step 6 — Final Deadline Check

6.1 Confirm that all field work, documentation, and Owner acceptance are completed on or before December 31, 2026.

6.2 If timeline is at risk, notify the project manager immediately so schedule mitigation can be applied.

Source: Specification REQ-007; RFP §3.1; OBJ-002.
