# Specification — DEL-008-01 Geotechnical Investigation & Report

---

## Scope

### In Scope

This specification governs the production and content of the Geotechnical Investigation & Report (DEL-008-01) for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project.

The deliverable covers:
- Conducting a full geotechnical field investigation at the site of the proposed Maintenance Shop Addition (SW 14-44-21-W4, expansion area shown in RFP Appendix C).
- Producing a complete geotechnical report documenting soil conditions, bearing capacity, settlement analysis, and foundation recommendations sufficient to support the foundation design (PKG-002) and to enable post-geotech negotiation of final foundation pricing under RFP §4.8.2.
- Providing the completed report to the Owner (Camrose County).

Sources: RFP §3.3.2; RFP §4.8.2; _CONTEXT.md; Decomposition SOW-0001.

### Out of Scope

- Foundation design (PKG-002 — Structural Design).
- Foundation construction (PKG-010 — Foundation Construction).
- Civil/site grading design (PKG-005 — Civil Design).
- Preliminary survey, construction survey, and as-built survey (DEL-008-02, DEL-008-03, DEL-008-04 — separate deliverables within PKG-008).
- Brushing, clearing, and bulk cut and fill (County responsibility per RFP §3.3.1).
- Environmental assessment — TBD: Confirm with regulatory authority whether environmental assessment is triggered by subsurface investigation on an active landfill site. Not mentioned in RFP scope for this deliverable; however, provincial environmental regulations may apply given the landfill context. This determination must be resolved before investigation program finalization. (Source: RFP scope silence; _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item C-002.)

## Requirements

### REQ-001 — Complete Geotechnical Report Required

A full, complete geotechnical report shall be produced and provided to the Owner.

> "Conduct Geotechnical Investigation and Provide the report to the Owner. A full complete geotechnical report will be required by the successful proponent to use during the design process."
> Source: RFP §3.3.2.

### REQ-002 — Report Used During Design Process

The geotechnical report shall be available and used during the design process, in particular for foundation design (PKG-002) and foundation cost reconciliation.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; RFP §4.8.2.

### REQ-003 — Foundation Pricing Basis

The geotechnical report shall be of sufficient scope and completeness to enable final foundation design and construction pricing to be negotiated between the Owner and the Design-Builder.

> "Final pricing for the design/construction of the foundation will be negotiated with the successful Proponent when the complete geotechnical report is available."
> Source: RFP §4.8.2.

### REQ-004 — Normal Ground Conditions Baseline

The initial proposal cost estimate for foundation design and construction is based on normal ground conditions without any deleterious subgrade material. The geotechnical report shall characterize subsurface conditions relative to this baseline, specifically identifying any deleterious subgrade materials if present.

Source: RFP §4.8.2.

### REQ-005 — Site-Specific Investigation

The geotechnical investigation shall be conducted at the specific expansion area identified in RFP Appendix C, within the SW 14-44-21-W4 site (Camrose County, Alberta), and shall reflect actual subsurface conditions at that location.

Source: RFP §3.1; RFP Appendix C; _CONTEXT.md.

### REQ-006 — Bearing Capacity Characterization (ASSUMPTION)

The report shall include bearing capacity analysis sufficient to support structural foundation design for the proposed approximately 13,000 sq ft concrete structure with a 35-foot ceiling height (RFP §3.1, §3.4). **ASSUMPTION**: bearing capacity is a standard and expected output of a geotechnical investigation for this deliverable type; explicitly listed in _CONTEXT.md description.

Source: _CONTEXT.md; RFP §3.1, §3.4.

### REQ-007 — Settlement Analysis (ASSUMPTION)

The report shall include settlement analysis relevant to the proposed structure and its loading conditions. **ASSUMPTION**: settlement analysis is a standard output explicitly listed in _CONTEXT.md description.

Source: _CONTEXT.md.

### REQ-008 — Foundation Recommendations

The report shall include foundation recommendations based on subsurface findings.

Source: _CONTEXT.md ("foundation recommendations"); RFP §4.8.2 (pricing negotiated when "complete geotechnical report is available").

### REQ-009 — Prepared by Geotechnical Engineer

The investigation and report shall be prepared by or under the responsible supervision of a Geotechnical Engineer.

Source: _CONTEXT.md (Responsible Role: Geotechnical Engineer); ASSUMPTION that a licensed engineer is required consistent with Alberta engineering practice requirements. TBD: Confirm whether the Alberta Engineering and Geoscience Professions Act (EGPA) or Engineering, Geological and Geophysical Professions Act requires a professional engineer seal on geotechnical reports specifically, and cite the regulatory basis. Consult APEGA or the applicable provincial legislation. (See _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md items A-002, F-001.)

### REQ-010 — Critical Path Timing

This deliverable is on the critical path. It must be completed before foundation design (PKG-002) can be completed and before foundation pricing can be finalized (RFP §4.8.2). Given the overall project completion deadline of December 31, 2026 (RFP §3.1), the investigation and report must be delivered as early as practicable in the project schedule.

Source: RFP §3.1; RFP §4.8.2; _CONTEXT.md (Critical Path Notes); _DEPENDENCIES.md.

### REQ-011 — Service Pit Geotechnical Characterization (ASSUMPTION)

The report shall include geotechnical characterization relevant to the below-grade service pit/trench (SOW-0028), including excavation recommendations, lateral earth pressure assessment, and groundwater control considerations for the service pit area. **ASSUMPTION**: the service pit is a below-grade structure (RFP §3.4; SOW-0028) and its geotechnical requirements are covered by the general scope of the investigation, even though the RFP does not explicitly call out service pit geotechnical analysis. Added to align Specification requirements with existing Guidance (Considerations — Service pit) and Procedure (Steps 5.8, 6.1) coverage.

Source: RFP §3.4; SOW-0028; Guidance — Considerations — Service pit; Procedure Steps 5.8, 6.1; _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item X-001.

### REQ-012 — Report Quality Review (ASSUMPTION)

The geotechnical report shall be subject to independent technical review (e.g., internal peer review or senior engineer review) prior to issuance to the Owner. The scope and form of this review are TBD. **ASSUMPTION**: independent technical review before issuance is standard geotechnical practice; Procedure Step 6.2 already anticipates senior review. Added to provide normative backing for the review step.

Source: Procedure Step 6.2 (ASSUMPTION — internal senior review); _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item A-003.
