# Procedure — DEL-008-01 Geotechnical Investigation & Report

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce the Geotechnical Investigation & Report (DEL-008-01) for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. The procedure covers the full production sequence: scoping, field investigation, laboratory testing, analysis, report preparation, and delivery to the Owner (Camrose County).

The procedure is anchored to the contractual requirements of RFP §3.3.2 and §4.8.2 and reflects the critical-path nature of this deliverable as a predecessor to foundation design (PKG-002) and foundation pricing negotiation.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Confirm Site Conditions and Coordinate Sequence

1.1 Confirm with the County (Owner) the current status of site brushing, clearing, and bulk cut and fill activities (County scope per RFP §3.3.1).

1.2 Determine whether County site preparation will occur before or after the geotechnical investigation. Document the agreed sequence. (See Guidance — Conflict Table CONF-008-01.)

1.3 Obtain site access authorization in writing from the County.

1.4 Obtain site safety induction requirements from the County; complete all required inductions before site personnel access.

Source: RFP §3.3.1; _DEPENDENCIES.md; ASSUMPTION (steps 1.4 — site-specific safety requirement).

### Step 2 — Develop Investigation Program

2.1 The Geotechnical Engineer reviews the proposed structure requirements:
   - Building footprint: approximately 13,000 sq ft (130' x 100') per RFP §3.1 and Appendix B (Shop drawings, not directly accessible in this context)
   - Structure type: concrete, 35-foot ceiling height (RFP §3.4)
   - Heavy loads: two 5-tonne overhead cranes, tracked/packer equipment, heavy mezzanine storage (RFP §3.4)
   - Below-grade elements: service pit/trench (RFP §3.4; SOW-0028)
   - Expansion area location: as shown in RFP Appendix C aerial photograph

2.2 The Geotechnical Engineer coordinates with the Structural Engineer (PKG-002) to obtain anticipated structural loads and foundation configuration preferences as input to investigation program design. **ASSUMPTION**: This coordination is standard engineering practice but is not explicitly described in the RFP. Specific load values are not available in accessible source documents; TBD from structural design. The Structural Engineer (PKG-002) should provide preliminary load data before this step proceeds. (See _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item B-001.)

2.3 Define the investigation program:
   - Number, location, and depth of boreholes or test pits (TBD by Geotechnical Engineer based on site conditions and structure footprint)
   - In-situ testing methods (TBD by Geotechnical Engineer — ASSUMPTION: Standard Penetration Test (SPT) or equivalent is typical for Alberta building foundations)
   - Laboratory testing requirements (TBD by Geotechnical Engineer)
   - Groundwater monitoring requirements and approach — TBD: The Geotechnical Engineer should determine whether groundwater monitoring is a single-reading observation during drilling or requires extended monitoring (standpipe/piezometer installation and repeat readings over time), and document the decision rationale in the investigation program. (See _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item B-002.)
   - Criteria for "deleterious subgrade material" and "normal ground conditions" to be established in consultation with the Owner before field work. (See Guidance — Considerations — Deleterious subgrade material; Conflict Table CONF-008-03.)

2.4 Submit investigation program to Owner for acceptance before mobilization (per client approval dependency in _DEPENDENCIES.md).

Source: RFP §3.1, §3.4; _DEPENDENCIES.md; ASSUMPTION on program details.

### Step 3 — Mobilize and Conduct Field Investigation

3.1 Mobilize field crew, equipment, and materials to site in accordance with approved investigation program and site safety requirements.

3.2 Execute borehole drilling, test pits, or other subsurface investigation methods per the investigation program. Document:
   - Borehole/test pit locations (with coordinates or site plan)
   - Soil and rock encountered at each depth interval
   - In-situ test results (e.g., SPT N-values)
   - Groundwater depths and conditions encountered
   - Any unusual conditions, fill, or potential deleterious materials

3.3 Collect soil samples for laboratory analysis per investigation program.

3.4 Install groundwater monitoring wells or standpipes if required by investigation program (TBD — see Step 2.3 for monitoring approach determination).

3.5 Maintain photographic documentation throughout field investigation, including: borehole and test pit locations, representative soil samples, site conditions, equipment used, and any unusual conditions encountered. Photographs shall be dated and location-referenced. (See _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item X-002; ASSUMPTION — standard geotechnical field practice.)

3.6 Document any deviations from the approved investigation program (e.g., additional boreholes, changed depths, modified test program) in the investigation program change log. (See Records table.)

3.7 Restore disturbed areas to the extent practicable upon completion of field activities.

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard geotechnical field investigation practice; site conditions TBD.

### Step 4 — Conduct Laboratory Testing

4.1 Submit collected soil samples to a geotechnical laboratory for testing per the investigation program.

4.2 **ASSUMPTION**: Typical laboratory tests for an Alberta building foundation investigation may include: grain size analysis, Atterberg limits, moisture content, unit weight, consolidation tests, and others as appropriate to the soils encountered. Specific test program is TBD by Geotechnical Engineer based on encountered conditions.

4.3 Review laboratory results for completeness and quality; request re-testing if results are anomalous or inconclusive. TBD: Apply the laboratory testing QA/QC criteria defined in the investigation program (see Specification — Laboratory Testing QA/QC Criteria table). Acceptance criteria for lab testing completeness and anomalous result thresholds should be established by the Geotechnical Engineer. (See _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item D-002.)

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard practice; specific test program TBD.

### Step 5 — Analyze Data and Develop Findings

5.1 Compile all field and laboratory data.

5.2 Develop soil profiles and subsurface model for the site.

5.3 Characterize groundwater conditions.

5.4 Conduct bearing capacity analysis for the proposed foundation type and loading conditions.

5.5 Conduct settlement analysis (total and differential settlement) for the proposed structure.

5.6 Assess whether subsurface conditions represent "normal ground conditions without any deleterious subgrade material" (baseline of RFP §4.8.2) or whether departure from this baseline exists. Apply the criteria established in Step 2.3 / Guidance — Considerations (Deleterious subgrade material). Document findings explicitly.

5.7 Develop foundation recommendations:
   - Recommended foundation type(s) (TBD — e.g., spread footings, pile foundations, etc.)
   - Allowable bearing pressures
   - Frost depth considerations (**ASSUMPTION**: frost depth is relevant in Alberta climate; TBD from investigation)
   - Any special design or construction requirements

5.8 Develop any recommendations relevant to the below-grade service pit (excavation, lateral earth pressure, groundwater control — ASSUMPTION; see Specification REQ-011).

5.9 Identify any recommendations relevant to civil site grading or subgrade preparation that would be useful to the Civil Design (PKG-005) — ASSUMPTION.

Source: _CONTEXT.md; RFP §4.8.2; ASSUMPTION on analytical steps.

### Step 6 — Prepare and Review Geotechnical Report

6.1 Draft the geotechnical report including:
   - Executive summary / project description
   - Site description and location (confirm consistency with Appendix C expansion area)
   - Scope of investigation (program, dates, personnel)
   - Site geology and regional context (ASSUMPTION: standard report section)
   - Field investigation results (borehole logs, test results)
   - Laboratory test results
   - Subsurface model and stratigraphy
   - Groundwater conditions
   - Bearing capacity analysis and results
   - Settlement analysis and results
   - Assessment of deleterious materials relative to RFP §4.8.2 baseline (using criteria from Step 2.3)
   - Foundation recommendations
   - Service pit recommendations (ASSUMPTION; per Specification REQ-011)
   - Limitations and assumptions of the investigation
   - Appendices: borehole logs, laboratory reports, location plans, field photographs

6.2 Report is reviewed internally by a senior geotechnical engineer (or independent technical reviewer) before issuance. **ASSUMPTION**: internal senior review is standard practice. The review should verify technical adequacy, cross-check findings against field and lab data, and confirm that all Specification requirements (REQ-001 through REQ-012) are addressed. Evidence of review completion shall be retained (see Specification REQ-012 and Records table).

6.3 **Owner draft review cycle (TBD):** Confirm with the Owner whether a draft review/comment period is required before final issuance. If required, submit draft report to Owner, incorporate Owner comments, and issue final report. If not required, proceed directly to final issuance. (See Guidance — Trade-offs; _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item D-001.)

6.4 Report is signed and sealed by the responsible Geotechnical Engineer per Alberta professional practice requirements (ASSUMPTION — specific regulatory basis TBD; see Specification REQ-009 and Standards table).

Source: _CONTEXT.md; RFP §3.3.2; RFP §4.8.2; ASSUMPTION on report structure.

### Step 7 — Deliver Report to Owner

7.1 Transmit the complete geotechnical report to the Owner (Camrose County) per RFP §3.3.2. TBD: Confirm required report format (hard copy, electronic, or both), medium, and number of copies with Owner before transmission (see Guidance — Considerations — Report transmission format).

7.2 Deliver a copy to the Design-Builder's project file for use in foundation design (PKG-002) and related design packages.

7.3 Notify the Structural Engineer (PKG-002 lead) that the geotechnical report is available; confirm foundation design can proceed.

7.4 Retain a transmission record confirming Owner receipt.

7.5 Initiate contact with Owner to schedule post-geotech foundation pricing negotiation (RFP §4.8.2), providing the report as the basis for discussion.
