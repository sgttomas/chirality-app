# Specification — DEL-008-02 Preliminary Site Survey

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-008-02 covers the complete preliminary site survey of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill (WDMLRL) expansion area at SW 14–44–21–W4, near Ferintosh, Alberta. The survey is to be conducted by the successful Proponent (Design-Builder) under SOW-0002 as part of the CCDC 14–2013 Design-Build contract.

The preliminary survey encompasses:
- Topographic survey and mapping of the project area
- Boundary identification/confirmation relevant to the expansion footprint
- Documentation of existing conditions (structures, services, features)
- Establishment of a control point network for use by all subsequent surveys

### What This Deliverable Excludes

| Exclusion | Rationale | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Construction survey (DEL-008-03) | Separate deliverable; occurs during construction phase | Decomposition, PKG-008 |
| As-built survey (DEL-008-04) | Separate deliverable; occurs at project completion | Decomposition, PKG-008 |
| Geotechnical investigation (DEL-008-01) | Separate deliverable; concurrent but distinct scope | Decomposition, PKG-008 |
| Foundation design (PKG-002) | Design package; uses survey data as input | Decomposition, scope boundaries |
| Civil / grading design (PKG-005) | Design package; uses survey data as input | Decomposition, scope boundaries |
| Brushing, clearing, bulk cut and fill | Performed by Camrose County forces, not the Proponent | RFP §3.3.1 |

## Requirements

### R-001 — Survey Scope
**Statement:** The Proponent shall conduct a preliminary survey of the project site at SW 14–44–21–W4.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0002); Decomposition DEL-008-02 entry.
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-002 — Topographic Coverage
**Statement:** The survey shall capture existing ground elevations across the project area sufficient to support site grading design.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — inferred from downstream use by grading design (RFP §3.3.2, §3.4 grading design requirements) and standard preliminary survey practice. **Location TBD** for specific coverage limits; to be defined in survey plan.
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION: required for design to proceed).

### R-003 — Boundary Establishment
**Statement:** The survey shall establish or confirm the property boundaries of the expansion area sufficient to support design and permitting.
**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (scope — "Boundary establishment").
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-004 — Existing Conditions Documentation
**Statement:** The survey shall document existing structures, services, access roads, and significant site features within the survey area.
**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (scope — "Existing conditions documentation").
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-005 — Control Point Network
**Statement:** The survey shall establish permanent or semi-permanent control points that can be used as reference for construction survey (DEL-008-03) and as-built survey (DEL-008-04).
**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (scope — "Control point establishment for future work"); _DEPENDENCIES.md (DEL-008-03 uses control points from preliminary survey).
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-006 — Coordinate System and Datum
**Statement:** All survey data shall be referenced to a consistent horizontal and vertical datum and coordinate system. The specific datum and coordinate system shall be confirmed with the Owner prior to fieldwork. Where the Owner does not specify a preference, the expected default shall be NAD83 (CSRS) horizontal and CGVD2013 vertical (ASSUMPTION — standard Alberta survey practice); the selected datum shall be documented in the survey plan before fieldwork commences.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — not specified in RFP; required for integration with design disciplines. **Location TBD** — coordinate system to be confirmed at survey plan stage. (Ref: Lensing B-003 — strengthened from deferral-only language)
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-007 — Survey Accuracy
**Statement:** Survey accuracy shall meet a defined accuracy class appropriate to support engineering design and construction staking at this site. The target accuracy class (e.g., Alberta Order of survey, or project-specific positional and elevation tolerances) shall be confirmed with the Owner and documented in the survey plan before fieldwork. The survey plan shall include specific numeric accuracy targets for horizontal position, vertical elevation, and control network closure.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — accuracy class not specified in RFP. Standard Alberta land survey practice applies. Specific accuracy requirements TBD in survey plan, subject to Owner approval. (Ref: Lensing A-001 — strengthened from "shall be appropriate" to require defined accuracy class)
**Priority:** Mandatory (accuracy class TBD).

### R-008 — Survey Products / Deliverable Artifacts
**Statement:** The survey shall produce, at a minimum:
  - A topographic drawing/map of the survey area
  - A written survey report documenting methodology, control network, and findings
  - Digital survey data files in a format confirmed with the design team prior to data delivery (e.g., DWG, DXF, CSV point files, or as agreed; format shall be documented in the survey plan)
**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (anticipated artifacts — topographic mapping completed, survey report and drawings finalized); ASSUMPTION for digital files. (Ref: Lensing F-001 — strengthened from "format TBD" to require pre-delivery confirmation mechanism)
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-009 — Owner Approval of Survey Plan
**Statement:** The survey plan (methodology, datum, coverage, accuracy targets) shall be submitted to Camrose County for review and approval prior to commencing fieldwork.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — inferred from Owner approval requirements throughout the project (RFP §3.3.2, §4.7.4); _DEPENDENCIES.md (client approval for survey methodology). See Guidance for rationale (Ref: Lensing F-002).
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION).

### R-010 — Completion Timing
**Statement:** The preliminary survey shall be completed in time to support the design schedule such that all design and construction work is completed on or before December 31, 2026.
**Source:** RFP §3.1.
**Priority:** Mandatory.

### R-011 — Surveyor Qualifications
**Statement:** The surveyor performing the work shall hold qualifications appropriate to the scope. Where the scope includes legal boundary survey work, the surveyor shall be a licensed Alberta Land Surveyor (ALS) or work under the direct supervision of a licensed ALS, as required by the Alberta Land Surveyors Act (RSA 2000, c. A-31.5). Qualifications shall be documented and submitted with the survey plan.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — Alberta Land Surveyors Act (RSA 2000, c. A-31.5) governs surveying practice in Alberta; not cited in RFP but applicable under Alberta law. (Ref: Lensing X-002 — new requirement addressing surveyor qualifications gap)
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION — applicable Alberta legislation).

### R-012 — QA/QC Procedures
**Statement:** The surveyor shall implement and document QA/QC procedures for survey data, including at minimum: independent checks on control network observations, redundancy analysis, and outlier detection for topographic data. The QA/QC approach shall be described in the survey plan and results documented in the survey report.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — inferred from standard survey quality practice; verification for R-007 references closure checks and redundancy analysis but no explicit QA/QC requirement existed. (Ref: Lensing F-004 — new requirement for quality accounting)
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION).

### R-013 — Survey Monument Protection
**Statement:** The surveyor shall identify, protect, and document any existing survey monuments encountered during fieldwork, in accordance with the Alberta Surveys Act (RSA 2000, c. S-26). Any disturbance to existing monuments shall be reported to the Owner and the appropriate provincial authority.
**Source:** Alberta Surveys Act (RSA 2000, c. S-26) — governs monument protection. **Location TBD** — specific clauses not verified against source text. (Ref: Lensing C-002 — new requirement addressing monument protection gap)
**Priority:** Mandatory (ASSUMPTION — applicable Alberta legislation).

Rejection criteria and dispute resolution process TBD — to be agreed with Owner (Ref: Lensing D-003). |
