# Procedure — DEL-008-02 Preliminary Site Survey

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce the DEL-008-02 Preliminary Site Survey for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill (WDMLRL) Maintenance Shop Addition project. The procedure covers planning, field execution, data processing, and deliverable production.

---

## Prerequisites

### Contractual Prerequisites
- CCDC 14–2013 Design-Build contract awarded to the Proponent (Source: RFP §2.7)
- Surveyor engaged or confirmed as part of the Proponent's project team
- Surveyor qualifications verified per R-011 (ALS license confirmed if boundary work is in scope)

### Site Prerequisites
- Site access arranged with Camrose County / WDMLRL facility management (Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md — site access for field surveying)
- Mandatory site meeting attended (RFP §3.2 — mandatory site meeting at SW-14-44-21-W4, scheduled for March 3, 2026, 10:00 a.m. MST, or rescheduled equivalent)
- County earthworks sequencing confirmed with project manager (brushing/clearing and bulk cut and fill per RFP §3.3.1)

### Technical Prerequisites
- **Existing geodetic control availability confirmed:** Before mobilization, confirm the availability and accessibility of existing geodetic benchmarks or control network points in the vicinity of SW 14–44–21–W4 that can be used to tie the project control network to the approved datum. If no existing geodetic control is available nearby, the control network approach must be adjusted (e.g., use of GNSS/PPP or other absolute positioning methods). (Ref: Lensing F-003 — prerequisite for geodetic benchmark availability)
- Confirmation of horizontal datum, vertical datum, and coordinate system with design team and Owner (Source: Specification R-006)
- Geotechnical Investigator (DEL-008-01) schedule known for coordination

### Health and Safety Prerequisites
- Site-specific health and safety requirements for field work at the active landfill confirmed with WDMLRL facility management and Proponent HSE function, including (Ref: Lensing A-004):
  - Site induction protocol (if required by facility)
  - Required personal protective equipment (PPE) for landfill environment
  - Emergency procedures and contact information
  - Hazard identification for active landfill operations (vehicle traffic, waste handling, gas migration, unstable ground — as applicable)

Source: ASSUMPTION — site is an active regional landfill; specific safety requirements not stated in RFP but implied by facility type and RFP §2.15 Prime Contractor designation.

### Reference Documents Required
- RFP §3.3.2 (survey scope)
- Appendix C — Site Maps (aerial, expansion area)
- Alberta Land Surveyors Act (RSA 2000, c. A-31.5) (applicable if boundary survey included)
- Alberta Surveys Act (RSA 2000, c. S-26) (applicable re: monument protection)
- Survey plan (produced in Step 1 below)

## Steps

### Step 1 — Develop and Submit Survey Plan

**Action:**
1. Prepare a survey plan that defines:
   - Survey scope (coverage extent, work types: topographic, boundary, control network)
   - Proposed datum and coordinate system (horizontal and vertical), including expected default if Owner has not yet confirmed (see R-006)
   - Target accuracy class with specific numeric targets for positional, elevation, and control network closure tolerances (see R-007)
   - Survey methodology and instrumentation
   - QA/QC approach (independent checks, redundancy analysis, outlier detection — see R-012)
   - Surveyor qualifications documentation (see R-011)
   - Digital data file format to be confirmed with design team (see R-008)
   - Field schedule
   - Health and safety plan for the active landfill site (see Prerequisites — Health and Safety)
2. Coordinate the survey plan with the geotechnical investigator (DEL-008-01) to align site access, control point sharing, and field scheduling.
3. Submit the survey plan to Camrose County (Owner) for review and approval before commencing fieldwork (see R-009).
4. Obtain written approval from the Owner.

**Output:** Approved survey plan; Owner approval record.

---

### Step 2 — Mobilize and Establish Control Network

**Action:**
1. Confirm existing geodetic control points or benchmarks in the vicinity of SW 14–44–21–W4 are accessible and usable to tie the survey to the approved datum (see Prerequisites — Technical).
2. Establish the project control network:
   - Set primary control points outside or at the perimeter of the construction area (semi-permanent or permanent monuments) (ASSUMPTION: location outside construction footprint preferred)
   - Set working control points within the survey area as required for data collection
3. Observe and calculate control network coordinates; perform closure check against the accuracy thresholds defined in the survey plan (see R-007).
4. Document all control point descriptions and coordinates.

**Output:** Established, documented project control network.

**Terminology note:** This procedure uses "control points" consistently to refer to both primary (perimeter/permanent) and working (within survey area) reference points. (Ref: Lensing X-004 — terminology normalized from previous use of "control stations")

---

### Step 3 — Conduct Field Survey

**Action:**
1. Collect topographic data across the project area:
   - Ground elevations at sufficient density to support contour mapping and grading design
   - Breaklines along grade changes, drainage features, roads, and structure edges
   - Location and dimensions of existing structures, access roads, services (above-ground), fences, and other features within the survey area
2. Perform boundary survey work as defined in the approved survey plan (coordinate with an Alberta Land Surveyor if legal boundary survey is required).
3. Identify, protect, and document any existing survey monuments encountered during fieldwork per R-013 and the Alberta Surveys Act (RSA 2000, c. S-26).
4. Document any existing below-ground services visible or known from site records (ASSUMPTION: flagging/potholing for buried services is outside scope unless directed by Owner; field observations noted only).
5. Observe and record field data; maintain field notes.
6. Check data coverage and quality in the field before demobilizing.

**Output:** Raw field data (total station observations, GPS/GNSS data, field notes).

---

### Step 4 — Data Reduction and Processing

**Action:**
1. Download and back up all raw field data. Implement data protection protocols: daily backup to separate storage, confirmation of data integrity before demobilizing (see Guidance — Data Backup and Security).
2. Process field observations:
   - Adjust control network; verify closure meets accuracy thresholds defined in survey plan (R-007)
   - Reduce topographic data to a 3D point cloud / surface
   - Verify data quality per QA/QC procedures (R-012): independent checks, redundancy analysis, outlier detection
3. Generate topographic surface model and contour data.
4. Compile boundary data and existing conditions information.
5. Prepare digital data files in the format confirmed with the design team (see R-008) for delivery.

**Output:** Processed survey data; digital files ready for design use.

---

### Step 5 — Produce Survey Drawing(s)

**Action:**
1. Produce topographic drawing(s) showing:
   - Site boundary and survey area limits
   - Contours at the interval confirmed with the Owner / civil engineer (see Datasheet — Mapping Scale / Contour Interval)
   - Existing structures, roads, services, fences, and features
   - Control point locations and identifiers
   - Legal boundary lines (if boundary survey performed)
   - Existing survey monuments encountered (if any, per R-013)
   - North arrow, scale, datum/coordinate system note, legend
   - Title block with project name, drawing title, date, revision, Surveyor identification
2. Apply appropriate professional certification / stamp per Alberta surveying practice (ALS stamp on boundary plan if legal boundary survey performed — see R-011, R-003 verification).

**Output:** Topographic drawing set (PDF and native CAD format).

---

### Step 6 — Produce Survey Report

**Action:**
1. Write the survey report to document:
   - Project description and site location
   - Survey scope and objectives
   - Methodology (equipment, datum, coordinate system, accuracy approach)
   - Surveyor qualifications (per R-011)
   - Control network description (point IDs, coordinates, monument types)
   - Accuracy assessment (closure results against defined thresholds, redundancy analysis — per R-007, R-012)
   - QA/QC results (per R-012)
   - Existing survey monuments encountered and their disposition (per R-013)
   - Summary of existing conditions observed
   - List of deliverables produced
   - Any observations, limitations, or recommended follow-up
2. Review and finalize report.

**Output:** Survey report (PDF).

---

### Step 7 — Deliver Artifacts and Obtain Acceptance

**Action:**
1. Assemble complete deliverable package:
   - Survey drawing(s) (PDF and CAD)
   - Survey report (PDF)
   - Digital data files (format as agreed per R-008)
   - Control point summary sheet
2. Submit to Camrose County project manager and design team.
3. Allow review period. **Expected review duration:** TBD — confirm with Owner and document in the survey plan or project schedule. Given the December 31, 2026 hard deadline (RFP §3.1), the review period and response turnaround should be agreed in advance to avoid schedule risk. (Ref: Lensing D-002 — review period specification)
4. Respond to review comments within the agreed turnaround period.
5. Obtain written acceptance from Owner / confirm design team has received and can use the data (see Specification — Deliverable Acceptance Criteria).

**Output:** Accepted survey deliverable; written acceptance record.

## Verification

After completing each step, verify:

| Check | Verification Action |
|---|---|
| Survey plan approved before fieldwork | Written approval on file before Step 2 commences |
| Surveyor qualifications documented | Credentials on file; ALS license confirmed if boundary work in scope (R-011) |
| Health and safety plan in place | Site-specific HSE requirements confirmed before mobilization |
| Existing geodetic control confirmed | Geodetic benchmark availability verified before mobilization (F-003) |
| Control network established and checked | Closure check within the accuracy thresholds defined in survey plan; documented (R-007) |
| Field data coverage complete | Coverage diagram or field check confirms no gaps in required area |
| Existing monuments documented | Any existing survey monuments encountered identified and protected (R-013) |
| Data processed and checked | QA/QC checks completed per R-012; no unexplained outliers |
| Data backed up securely | Raw field data backed up to separate storage; integrity confirmed |
| Drawing complete and correctly annotated | Drawing peer review; all required elements present; ALS stamp if applicable |
| Report complete and accurate | Report review against field data; data consistent; QA/QC results included |
| Deliverables accepted | Written acceptance from Owner on file per Deliverable Acceptance Criteria |
| Control points usable for future surveys | Control point descriptions clear and monuments intact |

This has been harmonized to a single policy pending Owner confirmation. If the Owner does not specify retention requirements, a minimum of project duration + 2 years is recommended as a fallback (ASSUMPTION — standard practice). (Ref: Lensing X-005 — retention period harmonization)
