## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps required to produce and complete DEL-018-06 Utility Tie-Ins — the coordination, permitting, installation, testing, and commissioning of natural gas, electrical service, and communication line connections to the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition.

The procedure is organized in two parts:
- **Part A**: Steps to produce the deliverable (planning, design coordination, permitting, and pre-construction)
- **Part B**: Steps to execute and close the deliverable (construction, testing, commissioning, and records)

Sources: RFP SS3.3.2; Decomposition SOW-0080, SOW-0081, SOW-0082; _DEPENDENCIES.md.

## Steps

### Part A — Planning, Design Coordination, and Permitting

**Step A1: Initiate Utility Provider Contacts (All Three Systems)**

1. Identify the applicable utility provider for each system:
   - Natural gas: TBD (confirm regional gas utility serving SW 14-44-21-W4)
   - Electrical service: TBD (confirm electrical distribution utility serving the area)
   - Communications: TBD (confirm telecom/communication providers available at site)
2. Submit service applications or initial inquiry letters to each provider.
3. Obtain provider requirements for service connection: service size, point of connection, metering specifications, easement requirements, lead times.
4. Record the estimated lead time for each provider to establish service (see Datasheet — Utility Provider Lead Times table). [Enrichment: B-004]
5. Record the distance from site boundary to the nearest infrastructure for each utility system (see Datasheet — Utility Infrastructure Proximity table). [Enrichment: B-001]
6. Document all provider communications.

Source: RFP SS3.3.2; ASSUMPTION — step detail reflects standard Alberta utility connection initiation practice.

**Step A2: Coordinate Utility Routing with Civil Design (PKG-005)**

1. Share utility provider connection point requirements with the civil design team (PKG-005).
2. Establish utility corridor routing on site, considering: separation distances between utilities (gas/electrical per Specification REQ-02.8), proximity to other site infrastructure, minimum depth requirements (per Specification REQ-01.6, REQ-02.7, REQ-03.5), and avoidance of conflicts with drainage (DEL-018-05), pads (DEL-018-04), and driving surface (DEL-018-03).
3. Confirm utility entry points into the building, coordinated with structural design (PKG-002) and mechanical/electrical design (PKG-003, PKG-004).
4. Obtain IFC drawings for utility installation, signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer licensed in Alberta.

Source: RFP SS3.3.2; ASSUMPTION — standard civil/utility coordination sequence.

**Step A3: Confirm Electrical Service Sizing**

1. Obtain total electrical load calculation from the electrical design (DEL-004).
2. Submit load information to the electrical utility for service sizing approval.
3. Confirm: three-phase voltage level, service capacity (amperage), transformer requirements (utility-supplied vs. customer-supplied — resolve per Specification REQ-02.9 and document determination [Enrichment: F-001]), and point of connection.
4. Obtain utility service agreement.

Source: RFP SS3.4; App B (Electrical); ASSUMPTION — sizing confirmation is required prior to permit application.

**Step A4: Confirm Natural Gas Service Sizing**

1. Obtain gas load calculations from the mechanical design (DEL-003), including heating system demand.
2. Submit to gas utility for service sizing and connection approval.
3. Confirm: service pressure, pipe size, meter location, and any easement requirements.
4. Assess whether a gas main extension is required. If so, obtain cost and timeline estimates from the provider and evaluate per the Guidance Gas Service Extension Cost/Schedule Risk Assessment. [Enrichment: F-004]
5. Obtain gas utility service agreement.

Source: RFP SS3.3.2; ASSUMPTION — sizing confirmation required prior to permit.

**Step A5: Confirm Communications Requirements**

1. Confirm with County the full scope of communications services required (minimum: conduit for antenna wiring per App B Electrical; potential additional: telephone, internet/data — TBD).
2. Contact communication provider(s) and obtain connection requirements.
3. Document and confirm conduit routing and building entry point for communication services.

Source: App B (Electrical); ASSUMPTION.

**Step A6: Obtain Safety Code Permits**

1. Verify that gas and electrical subcontractors hold valid licenses in the applicable Alberta Safety Code disciplines (per PREREQ-10 and Specification REQ-04.9). Document verification. [Enrichment: X-003]
2. Engage licensed gas contractor (or permit holder for gas discipline) to apply for and obtain the Gas Safety Code permit.
3. Engage licensed electrical contractor (or permit holder for electrical discipline) to apply for and obtain the Electrical Safety Code permit.
4. Obtain any additional permits required for excavation or road crossing (TBD — dependent on routing).
5. Confirm all permits are in hand before commencing installation.

Source: RFP SS3.3.2; Decomposition SOW-0007; ASSUMPTION — separate permit disciplines for gas and electrical.

### Part B — Construction, Testing, Commissioning, and Records

**Step B1: Notification and Pre-Construction Setup**

1. Notify the County project representative of the construction start date and inspection schedule for each utility system.
2. Verify that Alberta One-Call / Click Before You Dig locate has been completed (per PREREQ-11 and Specification REQ-04.8). Confirm all existing buried utilities in the work area are identified and marked. Retain locate confirmation documentation. [Enrichment: F-003]
3. Confirm site safety requirements with the Prime Contractor (per RFP SS2.15 — Prime Contractor status and Health & Safety obligations).
4. Set out trench lines per the IFC drawings.

Source: RFP SS2.15; ASSUMPTION — One-Call locate is standard Alberta practice before any excavation.

**HOLD POINT HP-01: Verify PREREQ-08 before proceeding to Step B2.** [Enrichment: D-002]

Confirm and document that site grading is substantially complete in the utility corridor areas before commencing trenching. Obtain written confirmation from the site superintendent or civil contractor. Record in site coordination records.

**Step B2: Trenching**

1. Excavate utility trenches per the IFC drawings, in accordance with the approved routing.
2. Maintain required separation distances between gas piping and electrical conduit per applicable codes (Specification REQ-02.8; TBD — specific separation requirements per CEC and CSA B149.1; values location TBD).
3. Maintain minimum burial depths per applicable codes and provider requirements (Specification REQ-01.6 for gas, REQ-02.7 for electrical, REQ-03.5 for communications; specific values TBD).
4. Stockpile excavated material for backfill; remove unsuitable material.

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard civil construction practice; specific dimensions TBD from IFC drawings and permit conditions.

**Step B3: Conduit, Casing, and Pipe Installation**

1. Install electrical service conduit per IFC drawings and electrical permit requirements.
2. Install gas service piping per IFC drawings and gas permit requirements; piping material per Specification REQ-01.4 (material to be specified and documented); cathodic protection per Specification REQ-01.5 and CSA B149.1 / gas utility standards (determination and method TBD — must be resolved before gas installation commences). [Enrichment: C-002, C-003]
3. Install communication conduit per IFC drawings and provider requirements.
4. Install building penetrations/sleeves for each utility at the building entry point, coordinated with the structural/foundation contractor.
5. Where conduits/piping share a trench, confirm and maintain required separation throughout (per Specification REQ-02.8).

Source: RFP SS3.3.2; ASSUMPTION — specific material specifications TBD from design and permit requirements.

**Step B4: Trench Backfill and Surface Restoration**

1. Upon inspection completion (where required before backfill — see Step B5), backfill trenches in lifts per IFC specifications and geotechnical requirements.
2. Compact backfill to meet the specified density requirements. TBD: Specific compaction acceptance criteria (required density percentage, maximum lift thickness, testing method — e.g., nuclear densometer or equivalent) to be sourced from the civil design (PKG-005) or geotechnical report. Until specified, the following minimum criteria apply as ASSUMPTION based on standard civil construction practice: [Enrichment: A-005]
   - Backfill in maximum 300 mm lifts (ASSUMPTION — standard practice)
   - Compact to minimum 95% Standard Proctor density under paved/gravel surfaces, 90% in other areas (ASSUMPTION — standard practice; TBD from civil design)
   - Testing method and frequency TBD from civil design specifications
3. Restore surface consistent with the site grading plan (PKG-005 civil design).
4. Verify no settlement or surface damage to adjacent site features.

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard civil construction practice; specific compaction requirements TBD from civil design.

**Step B5: Request Inspections — Coordinate with County**

1. Prior to backfilling trenches (where required for underground inspection), notify Safety Code officers and the County project representative.
2. Do not proceed with backfill until all required pre-backfill inspections are complete and the County representative has attended.
3. For each utility system, submit inspection requests to the applicable Safety Code authority.
4. For communications infrastructure, confirm inspection requirements per Specification REQ-04.10. TBD: Whether communications conduit requires Safety Code inspection in Alberta. [Enrichment: X-005]
5. Attend all inspections with the County project representative.
6. Obtain signed inspection reports. Verify that each report meets the minimum content requirements per Specification REQ-04.4 (inspector identification, date, items inspected, pass/fail status, deficiency list, inspector signature). [Enrichment: E-003]

Source: RFP SS3.3.2; Decomposition SOW-0084; RFP SS3.3.2.

**Step B6: Utility Service Connections**

1. Make connections at the utility provider's service point (transformer, gas meter riser, communication demarcation) per provider requirements. Note: connection to the provider's infrastructure may need to be performed by the utility provider's own crews — confirm with each provider.
2. Make connections at the building service entry points.
3. Install meters as required by each utility provider.
4. Obtain provider approval/sign-off on connections.

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard utility connection practice; provider-performed connection portions are provider-dependent.

**Step B7: System Testing and Commissioning**

1. **Electrical service**: After service energization by the utility, test service voltage, phase sequence, and capacity. Confirm three-phase supply is correct. Pass criteria: voltage within utility provider tolerance band (TBD); correct three-phase sequence confirmed; capacity matches service agreement. Coordinate with the electrical contractor (PKG-015) for building-side energization and load testing. [Enrichment: F-002]
2. **Natural gas**: After gas utility activates the meter, perform pressure test and leak test on service piping per Safety Code requirements. Pass criteria: no pressure drop beyond allowable tolerance per Safety Code gas inspection standards (TBD: specific tolerance); no detectable leaks. Coordinate with mechanical contractor (PKG-013) for connection to building gas distribution and heating system commissioning. [Enrichment: F-002]
3. **Communications**: Test conduit and cabling installation; confirm antenna wiring continuity; confirm any additional communication services are active and functional. Pass criteria: antenna wiring continuity confirmed; conduit free of obstructions; additional services (if any) functional. [Enrichment: F-002]
4. Document all test results per the Specification Commissioning Evidence Requirements table.

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard commissioning sequence for each utility type; Safety Code pressure testing for gas is a regulatory requirement.

**Step B8: Request Final Inspections**

1. Schedule and request final Safety Code inspections for gas and electrical service.
2. Ensure County project representative is present at all final inspections (RFP SS3.3.2; SOW-0084).
3. Obtain signed final inspection reports. Verify minimum content per Specification REQ-04.4. [Enrichment: E-003]
4. Resolve any deficiencies identified at inspection per the following protocol: [Enrichment: X-004]
   - **Documentation**: Record each deficiency with: deficiency ID, description, applicable code/requirement reference, date identified, and responsible party for resolution.
   - **Timeline**: All deficiencies shall be resolved within TBD calendar days of identification (ASSUMPTION — timeline to be established based on project schedule and Safety Code re-inspection requirements; standard practice is 14-30 days depending on severity).
   - **Re-inspection**: Following deficiency resolution, request re-inspection from the applicable Safety Code authority. The County project representative shall attend the re-inspection.
   - **Acceptance criteria**: A deficiency is resolved when the re-inspection results in a passing inspection report with no remaining deficiencies for that item.
   - **Escalation**: If a deficiency cannot be resolved within the established timeline, escalate to the General Contractor's project manager and notify the County project representative.

Source: RFP SS3.3.2; Decomposition SOW-0084, SOW-0085.

**Step B9: As-Built Documentation**

1. Prepare as-built drawings recording: actual trench routes and depths, pipe/conduit material and size, connection point locations, meter locations, and building penetration details.
2. Have as-built drawings reviewed and stamped where required.
3. Verify as-built drawings for completeness and accuracy per the Specification Verification table (As-Built Drawings row). [Enrichment: E-002]
4. Submit as-built drawings to the County.

Source: ASSUMPTION — as-built documentation is standard practice for underground infrastructure and is typically required for project close-out.

**Step B10: Guarantee Period and CCC Verification** [Enrichment: D-003, E-004]

1. Compile the utility-specific evidence required for Construction Completion Certificate (CCC) issuance. The following evidence shall be assembled and cross-referenced to demonstrate that all utility tie-in work is complete, inspected, and accepted:

   | Evidence Item | Specification Requirement | Procedure Step |
   |---------------|--------------------------|----------------|
   | Safety Code permits (gas, electrical) — closed out | REQ-01.3, REQ-02.4 | Step A6 |
   | All inspection reports (gas, electrical, communications where applicable) — final, passing | REQ-04.3, REQ-04.4 | Steps B5, B8 |
   | Commissioning test records — all systems pass | REQ-01 through REQ-03 series | Step B7 |
   | Utility provider sign-offs — all systems | REQ-01.1, REQ-02.1, REQ-03.1 | Step B6 |
   | As-built drawings — complete and reviewed | REQ-04.1 (IFC); As-Built verification row | Step B9 |
   | Deficiency log — all deficiencies resolved | REQ-04.2, REQ-04.3 | Step B8 |
   | Subcontractor license documentation | REQ-04.9 | Step A6 |
   | One-Call locate confirmation | REQ-04.8 | Step B1 |

2. Verify that the 2-year guarantee period (RFP SS2.10) is documented in the CCC package. The guarantee period commences from the CCC date and covers all utility tie-in work. Confirm that guarantee obligations are clearly assigned (General Contractor to County).
3. Submit compiled evidence package to the County for CCC review.

Source: RFP SS2.10, SS2.14.3; Datasheet Conditions (Guarantee Period); ASSUMPTION — CCC evidence compilation is standard project close-out practice.
