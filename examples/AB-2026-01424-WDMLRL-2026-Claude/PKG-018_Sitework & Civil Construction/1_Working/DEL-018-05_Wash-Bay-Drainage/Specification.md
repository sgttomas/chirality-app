# DEL-018-05 | Wash Bay Drainage & Mud Sump — Specification

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-018-05 covers the design-build construction of the wash bay drainage infrastructure for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. Specifically:

- Installation of floor drain(s) within the 24'-wide enclosed wash bay
- Installation of drainage piping connecting the wash bay floor drain(s) to the exterior mud sump
- Excavation and construction of the exterior mud sump pit, located on the east side of the building adjacent to the wash bay, designed for cleanout by excavator

This deliverable is assigned to the General Contractor under PKG-018 (Sitework & Civil Construction) and is governed by SOW-0027b.

Source: Decomposition SS7 PKG-018; SOW-0027b; _CONTEXT.md.

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Wash bay structural enclosure and steel plate floor (DEL-011-05 / SOW-0027a)
- Interior floor drains in repair bays (DEL-014-04 / SOW-0045)
- Plumbing design and IFC drawings (DEL-006 series / PKG-006)
- Civil design and IFC drainage plan drawings (DEL-005 series / PKG-005)
- Supply of aggregate or gravel (County responsibility per RFP SS3.3.1)
- Bulk cut and fill (County responsibility per RFP SS3.3.1)
- Permit fee payment (County responsibility per RFP SS3.3.1)

## Requirements

### REQ-001 — Mud Sump Provided on Building Exterior

**Statement:** A mud sump shall be provided on the exterior of the building, receiving wash bay drainage and accessible for cleanout by excavator.

**Source:** RFP SS3.4: "Mud sump from wash bay on exterior of building for clean out with excavator." App B (Shop): Mud sump shown labelled on east side of building.

**Type:** Explicit requirement.

---

### REQ-002 — Wash Bay Floor Drain(s) Installed

**Statement:** Floor drain(s) shall be installed within the wash bay to collect wash water and convey it to the exterior mud sump.

**Source:** SOW-0027b: "Construct wash bay drainage infrastructure including floor drains, connection to mud sump." App B (Plumbing): floor drain shown in wash bay.

**Type:** Explicit requirement.

**Note:** The scope boundary for the wash bay floor drain is addressed in Guidance Conflict Table CF-001 (SOW-0027b vs. SOW-0045/SOW-0046). Construction of the wash bay floor drain and its connection to the exterior mud sump is within DEL-018-05 scope per the explicit language of SOW-0027b. The resolution of CF-001 should be documented prior to construction commencement and recorded as a prerequisite in Procedure Step 1.3. [Enrichment: D-001]

---

### REQ-003 — Drainage Connection Between Floor Drain and Mud Sump

**Statement:** A drainage pipe or conduit shall connect the wash bay floor drain(s) to the exterior mud sump. The connection shall be watertight and shall maintain positive flow to the sump.

**Source:** SOW-0027b: "floor drains, connection to mud sump, and exterior mud sump for excavator cleanout."

**Type:** Explicit requirement.

---

### REQ-004 — Mud Sump Accessible for Excavator Cleanout

**Statement:** The mud sump shall be designed and positioned to allow cleanout by excavator. This requires sufficient access clearance around the sump and an open-top or removable-cover configuration.

**Source:** RFP SS3.4: "for clean out with excavator." Decomposition Vocabulary Map: "Exterior sump for wash bay effluent, cleanable by excavator."

**Type:** Explicit requirement.

**Note:** Specific sump dimensions, cover details, and excavator access clearance envelope dimensions are TBD pending civil/plumbing design (DEL-005, DEL-006). The Datasheet records the excavator access clearance envelope as a TBD parameter. See also Guidance — Excavator Access Clearance for design direction.

### REQ-009 — Environmental Discharge Compliance

**Statement:** The drainage system and mud sump shall manage wash bay wash water in a manner that prevents uncontrolled environmental discharge and meets any applicable environmental regulatory requirements.

**Source:** OBJ-004 (Decomposition SS5): "Install and commission all mechanical, plumbing, and water storage systems to operational readiness. This includes the wash bay drainage and mud sump." _DEPENDENCIES.md: "ENV-PERMIT-001 Environmental discharge permit — Pending."

**Type:** ASSUMPTION — derived from OBJ-004 and environmental permit dependency; specific regulatory requirements TBD pending regulatory review and permit authority confirmation.

**Note:** The specific Alberta environmental regulations applicable to wash bay wash water discharge at a landfill site must be identified through regulatory scoping with Alberta Environment and Protected Areas (AEPA) or the applicable regulatory authority. REQ-009 currently references OBJ-004 but does not identify the governing regulatory framework. The regulatory scoping outcome will determine whether a discharge permit is required and what compliance evidence is needed. See Guidance SS Principles item 4 and Conflict Table CF-002 for further context. [Enrichment: A-001]

### REQ-012 — Sump Structural Integrity Under Site Conditions

**Statement:** The mud sump pit shall be designed and constructed to maintain structural integrity under the anticipated site loading conditions, including frost heave forces, lateral soil pressure, and any surcharge loads from adjacent grade or equipment traffic.

**Source:** ASSUMPTION — derived from the combination of (a) Alberta freeze-thaw conditions at the site (Camrose County), (b) the requirement for excavator access adjacent to the sump (REQ-004, which implies equipment surcharge), and (c) the geotechnical report dependency (SOW-0001). The RFP does not explicitly state a structural integrity requirement for the sump, but structural adequacy is an implicit prerequisite for all below-grade concrete construction in Alberta.

**Type:** ASSUMPTION — no explicit requirement in RFP; to be confirmed by civil/structural engineer during IFC design.

**Note:** The geotechnical report (SOW-0001) should inform the structural design by providing soil bearing capacity, frost depth, groundwater level, and lateral earth pressure coefficients. Guidance SS Considerations (Frost Protection) discusses the frost-related design context. [Enrichment: C-002]
