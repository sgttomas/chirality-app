# DEL-018-05 | Wash Bay Drainage & Mud Sump — Procedure

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to construct the wash bay drainage infrastructure and exterior mud sump for DEL-018-05. It covers the sequence of activities from pre-construction coordination through to system verification and records submission, as required to satisfy SOW-0027b under the CCDC 14-2013 Design-Build contract.

Source: SOW-0027b; RFP SS3.4; _CONTEXT.md.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Pre-Construction Coordination

**Responsible:** General Contractor

1.1 Obtain and review civil IFC drawings (DEL-005-02, DEL-005-03) and plumbing IFC drawings (DEL-006-03) for the wash bay drainage system. Confirm all drawings are P.Eng.-stamped.

1.2 Confirm the mud sump location on the exterior east face of the building with the civil engineer. Verify excavator access clearance is maintained per design intent (see Guidance — Excavator Access Clearance).

1.3 Confirm the scope boundary with the Plumbing Contractor (PKG-014) regarding the wash bay floor drain versus repair bay sump drains (refer to Conflict CF-001 in Guidance.md). The scope boundary agreement shall be documented in a formal written confirmation letter, signed by both the General Contractor and the Plumbing Contractor, and countersigned by the Owner or Owner's representative. The letter shall clearly state which party is responsible for the wash bay floor drain(s), the connection piping to the exterior mud sump, and any shared interface points. This document shall be retained as a project record (see Records table). ASSUMPTION: the specific documentation format should be confirmed by the Project Manager; the minimum requirement is a signed written agreement, not an informal email. [Enrichment: D-002]

1.4 If ENV-PERMIT-001 has not been resolved, confirm regulatory status with Alberta Environment and Protected Areas (AEPA) or the applicable regulatory authority before proceeding with drainage connection installation. See Guidance Principles item 4 and CF-002.

1.5 Notify County project representative of planned construction schedule and inspection points.

Source: RFP SS3.3.2; _DEPENDENCIES.md; Guidance.md CF-001, CF-002.

---

### Step 2 — Mud Sump Excavation

**Responsible:** General Contractor

2.1 Mark out the mud sump extents per IFC drawings. Confirm no underground utilities conflict with the excavation zone (coordinate with DEL-018-06 Utility Tie-Ins as needed).

2.2 Excavate the sump pit to the dimensions and depth specified in IFC drawings. Ensure excavation depth provides clearance below the drainage pipe invert and accounts for any granular bedding layer.

2.3 **HOLD POINT — Geotechnical Confirmation:** Before proceeding with sump construction (Step 3), the General Contractor shall confirm that actual soil conditions encountered during excavation are consistent with the design assumptions in the geotechnical report (SOW-0001). Specifically, verify: (a) soil bearing capacity is adequate for the sump structure, (b) groundwater level is at or below the level assumed in design, and (c) no deleterious or unexpected materials are present. If actual conditions differ materially from design assumptions, **stop work** and notify the civil engineer and Owner in writing before proceeding. The engineer shall provide written confirmation that the design remains valid for the encountered conditions, or issue revised design instructions. This hold point requires documented sign-off before Step 3 can commence. [Enrichment: F-003]

ASSUMPTION: Excavate to or below the design frost depth per geotechnical report to prevent heave.

2.4 Prepare the pit base per IFC specification — ASSUMPTION: granular bedding layer or lean concrete base as specified by civil/structural engineer.

Source: RFP SS3.4; SOW-0027b; Datasheet — Physical Parameters (dimensions TBD).

### Step 3 — Mud Sump Construction

**Responsible:** General Contractor

3.1 Construct the sump pit walls and base per IFC drawings and specified material (TBD — ASSUMPTION: cast-in-place concrete or precast concrete structure; confirm from IFC drawings).

3.2 Install any required inlet penetration(s) for the drainage pipe connection from the wash bay floor drain.

3.3 Install sump access configuration per IFC drawings (TBD — open-top sump or removable-cover sump; see Guidance Trade-offs and Terminology Conventions for canonical terms).

3.4 Ensure excavator access clearance around the sump is maintained as constructed — do not allow adjacent fill or structures to encroach on the required access envelope.

Source: RFP SS3.4; SOW-0027b; Guidance — Trade-offs.

---

### Step 4 — Drainage Pipe Installation

**Responsible:** General Contractor (with Plumbing Contractor coordination as applicable per CF-001)

4.1 Install the drainage pipe from the wash bay floor drain penetration through the building wall (or foundation) to the exterior mud sump inlet. Pipe material, diameter, and slope per IFC drawings (TBD).

4.2 Ensure the pipe is laid at the design invert slope to maintain positive flow to the sump under gravity. Confirm invert elevations match IFC drawing specifications.

4.3 Bury the pipe at or below frost depth, or insulate per civil design specification, to prevent freeze-up in Alberta winter conditions. ASSUMPTION: frost depth per geotechnical report; specific burial requirement TBD from civil design.

4.4 Make all joints watertight per pipe material specification.

4.5 Backfill over the drainage pipe in lifts per IFC specification, ensuring no damage to the pipe during compaction. **Fallback standard:** If the IFC drawings do not specify backfill material or compaction requirements for the drainage pipe trench, the General Contractor shall use granular bedding material (e.g., clean sand or pea gravel) to 300 mm above the pipe crown, followed by native backfill in maximum 300 mm lifts compacted to 95% Standard Proctor density (ASTM D698), or as directed by the civil engineer. ASSUMPTION: this fallback is consistent with standard Alberta municipal infrastructure practice; the civil engineer should confirm or supersede this standard in the IFC specification. [Enrichment: X-002]

Source: SOW-0027b: "connection to mud sump"; RFP SS3.3.2 / SOW-0018 (IFC governs); Guidance — Frost Protection.

### Step 5 — Floor Drain Installation (Wash Bay)

**Responsible:** General Contractor / Plumbing Contractor (confirm per CF-001 scope boundary)

5.1 Install wash bay floor drain(s) at the location(s) shown on plumbing IFC drawings (App B Plumbing shows one floor drain in the wash bay; confirm final count per IFC drawings — see Datasheet Floor Drain Count note).

5.2 Set floor drain at correct elevation to match the wash bay concrete floor finish grade and to promote drainage toward the drain.

5.3 Connect floor drain to the drainage pipe installed in Step 4.

5.4 Coordinate with the Plumbing Contractor to confirm this installation does not conflict with other plumbing scope (SOW-0046 floor drains elsewhere in the shop).

Source: App B (Plumbing); SOW-0027b; Guidance CF-001.

### Step 6 — Site Grading Around Sump

**Responsible:** General Contractor

6.1 Complete any local grading in the vicinity of the mud sump to match the civil IFC grading plan (DEL-005-02).

6.2 Confirm positive drainage away from the building foundation and toward the sump catchment area as designed.

6.3 Confirm the completed grading does not direct runoff onto neighbouring properties (REQ-006).

Source: RFP SS3.4 / SOW-0020, SOW-0021; Specification REQ-005, REQ-006.

### Step 7 — Inspection and Notification

**Responsible:** General Contractor

7.1 Submit all required inspection requests to the applicable Safety Code officer(s) and building authority. Notify County project representative to ensure attendance at all inspections.

7.2 Upon inspection completion, obtain copies of all inspection reports and provide copies to the County.

7.3 Resolve any deficiencies identified at inspection before requesting final acceptance.

Source: RFP SS3.3.2 / SOW-0084, SOW-0085; Specification REQ-011.

### Step 8 — Functional Verification

**Responsible:** General Contractor

8.1 Conduct a water flow test: introduce water at the wash bay floor drain and confirm it flows freely to the exterior mud sump with no leakage at joints.

8.2 Confirm excavator access to the mud sump is unobstructed — walk the access clearance zone and verify it is free of obstruction.

8.3 Confirm constructed invert elevations and sump dimensions are consistent with IFC drawings. Record any deviations.

Source: Specification — Verification table (REQ-002, REQ-003, REQ-004).

### Cold-Weather Construction Considerations

**Applicability:** This section applies if any of Steps 2 through 6 are scheduled to occur during periods when ambient temperatures are at or below 0 degrees C, which is likely given the Alberta site location and the December 31, 2026 completion deadline. [Enrichment: C-003]

**Responsible:** General Contractor

CW-1. **Concrete placement in freezing conditions:** If mud sump concrete work (Step 3) is scheduled during sub-zero temperatures, the General Contractor shall implement cold-weather concreting measures in accordance with CSA A23.1 (Concrete Materials and Methods of Concrete Construction) or as specified by the structural engineer. Measures may include heated enclosures, insulated forms, hot water batching, and extended curing protection. ASSUMPTION: CSA A23.1 is the applicable standard for cold-weather concrete in Alberta; confirm with the civil/structural engineer.

CW-2. **Open excavation frost protection:** If the sump excavation (Step 2) must remain open overnight or for extended periods during freezing conditions, the General Contractor shall protect the excavation base from frost penetration using insulating blankets or other approved methods. Frost-damaged subgrade shall not be used as a foundation for the sump structure without engineer approval.

CW-3. **Pipe installation in freezing conditions:** If drainage pipe installation (Step 4) occurs during freezing conditions, ensure that trench bottoms are not frozen before pipe placement and that backfill material is not frozen. Frozen backfill shall not be used.

CW-4. **Schedule risk:** The General Contractor should assess whether winter construction for this deliverable introduces unacceptable quality or schedule risk, and if so, propose schedule adjustments to the Owner during pre-construction coordination (Step 1).

Source: ASSUMPTION — derived from Alberta site location and December 2026 deadline; CSA A23.1 reference is ASSUMPTION (standard practice for Alberta cold-weather concrete).
