# DEL-018-04 | Specification — Cement & Gravel Pads

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-018-04 encompasses the construction of all exterior cement (concrete) pads and the gravel pad at the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition site, as depicted on the conceptual drawings (Appendix B) and as further defined by the Issued for Construction (IFC) civil drawings.

Specifically in scope:
- Construction of cement pad(s) as shown on Appendix B conceptual drawing (SOW-0078), including subgrade preparation, formwork, concrete placement, finishing, and curing.
- Construction of gravel pad as shown on Appendix B conceptual drawing (SOW-0079), including subgrade preparation and placement/compaction of County/Landfill-supplied gravel.
- Coordination of crane pad specification with DEL-016-01 (Overhead Cranes) prior to construction.
- Slope and drainage integration per site grading design.

### What This Deliverable Excludes

| Item | Basis |
|------|-------|
| Supply of aggregate/gravel | County/Landfill responsibility (RFP §3.3.1, SOW-0203, App B note) |
| Brushing, clearing, and bulk cut/fill | County responsibility (RFP §3.3.1) |
| Site grading and topsoil stripping | Separate deliverables DEL-018-01, DEL-018-02 (prerequisites) |
| Gravel driving surface construction | DEL-018-03 |
| Wash bay drainage and mud sump | DEL-018-05 |
| Interior steel floor plates (inside New North Shop building) | PKG-011 Building Structure & Envelope |
| Foundation design | PKG-002 Structural Design; variable-price item |
| Concrete mix design engineering | PKG-005 Civil Design (P.Eng. to specify; contractor to execute) |

## Requirements

### REQ-018-04-01 — Cement Pads: Construct per Conceptual Drawings
**Statement:** The Contractor shall construct cement pads at all locations shown on Appendix B (conceptual drawing) and as confirmed in the IFC civil drawings.
**Source:** SOW-0078; App B
**Type:** Mandatory

### REQ-018-04-02 — Gravel Pad: Construct per Conceptual Drawings
**Statement:** The Contractor shall construct the gravel pad at the location shown on Appendix B (north of the New North Shop) as confirmed in the IFC civil drawings.
**Source:** SOW-0079; App B
**Type:** Mandatory

### REQ-018-04-03 — Material Supply Responsibility
**Statement:** The Contractor shall supply cement (concrete) and perform all associated preparation work for the cement pads. The County/Landfill shall supply gravel for the gravel pad. The Contractor shall prepare the subgrade for the gravel pad.
**Source:** App B note ("Contractor to supply cement and prep – Landfill to supply gravel"); RFP §3.3.1 (SOW-0203)
**Type:** Mandatory

### REQ-018-04-04 — Concrete Specification
**Statement:** Concrete mix design (compressive strength, slump, admixtures, air entrainment) shall be specified in the IFC civil drawings, signed and stamped by a professional engineer licensed in Alberta. The Contractor shall comply with the specified mix design.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (IFC drawings must be P.Eng.-stamped); RFP §3.4 (Alberta Safety Codes compliance)
**Type:** Mandatory
**Note:** Specific MPa class and mix parameters are TBD pending IFC civil design (ASSUMPTION: minimum 25 MPa as typical for exterior slabs-on-grade in Alberta industrial applications; this assumption must be confirmed by the P.Eng. of record). **Enrichment note (A-002):** The 25 MPa assumption may be insufficient for crane pads, which must support dynamic crane loads (DEL-016-01); a higher strength class (e.g., 30-35 MPa) may be required depending on crane supplier load data and P.Eng. structural analysis. The P.Eng. of record shall confirm the required strength class for each pad type; crane supplier load data (from DEL-016-01) is a prerequisite input for this determination.

### REQ-018-04-05 — Positive Drainage Slope
**Statement:** Cement pads shall be finished with a positive drainage slope per the site grading plan. The grading design shall not alter drainage conditions of neighbouring properties.
**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Grading Design shall account for positive site drainage and future storm events. The Grading design shall not alter the drainage conditions of neighboring properties.")
**Type:** Mandatory
**Note:** Specific slope gradient TBD in IFC civil drawings. **Enrichment note (C-002):** The IFC civil drawings shall specify a minimum drainage slope percentage for cement pad surfaces. The verification method for this requirement (see Verification table) shall reference that minimum slope value once established, rather than relying solely on visual inspection after a rain event.

### REQ-018-04-06 — Alberta Safety Codes Compliance
**Statement:** All construction work, including pad design and construction methods, shall comply with applicable Alberta Safety Codes.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 ("The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes")
**Type:** Mandatory
**Enrichment note (A-004):** The applicable Alberta Safety Code sections for pad construction (e.g., Building Code, Fire Code as applicable) shall be identified in the IFC drawing package or in a separate compliance matrix prepared by the P.Eng. of record. Acceptance criteria shall include identification of which code sections trigger inspections and the designated inspection authority. TBD pending IFC package.

### REQ-018-04-07 — IFC Drawing Conformance
**Statement:** Construction shall conform to the Issued for Construction drawings. All IFC drawings governing pad construction shall be signed and stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2
**Type:** Mandatory

### REQ-018-04-08 — Crane Pad Load Coordination
**Statement:** The design and construction of the cement pad(s) serving as crane bases shall accommodate the structural loads specified by the crane supplier for the two 5-tonne overhead cranes (DEL-016-01). Crane pad design shall be coordinated between the civil/structural engineer and the crane supplier prior to IFC. Coordination shall produce a documented output (P.Eng. coordination sign-off memo or equivalent) confirming that the pad design accommodates the crane supplier's specified loads, anchor bolt pattern, and base plate dimensions, before pad construction proceeds.
**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md (CRANE-COORDS-001); App B (2 x 5-tonne cranes shown); RFP §3.4 ("2 – 5-tonne overhead cranes on a trolley")
**Type:** Mandatory
**Note:** ASSUMPTION: crane base pads are the cement pads within or immediately adjacent to the building footprint. Exact configuration (embedded anchors, thickened slab, isolated pads) TBD in IFC structural/civil drawings. **Enrichment note (D-001):** The documented coordination sign-off is added to ensure traceability from crane supplier requirements to pad design; see also Documentation section (Required Artifacts).

### REQ-018-04-09 — Sequencing: Predecessor Completion
**Statement:** Pad construction shall not commence until site grading (DEL-018-02) is sufficiently complete and the subgrade for pad areas has been approved as ready for placement.
**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md (DEL-018-02 as upstream dependency)
**Type:** Mandatory

### REQ-018-04-10 — Inspection and Owner Notification
**Statement:** The Contractor shall submit all inspection requests for pad construction. The County project representative must be present at all inspections. The Contractor shall provide copies of all completed inspection reports to the County.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2
**Type:** Mandatory

### REQ-018-04-11 — Warranty
**Statement:** All materials furnished and work performed for cement and gravel pads are guaranteed for the construction period plus a 2-year warranty period following the date noted on the Construction Completion Certificate (CCC). Defects discovered during the guarantee period shall be rectified within 10 days of the Owner's written instruction.
**Source:** RFP §2.10.2, §2.10.6
**Type:** Mandatory

### REQ-018-04-12 — Gravel Pad Compaction (ASSUMPTION)
**Statement:** ASSUMPTION — Granular material for the gravel pad shall be compacted to a minimum density per the civil engineer's specification. Compaction testing shall be performed and records provided to the Owner.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION (standard civil practice for compacted granular pads; not explicitly stated in RFP for this pad type; to be confirmed in IFC civil drawings)
**Type:** ASSUMPTION — subject to IFC civil drawing requirements
**Enrichment note (A-003):** Gravel compaction is standard civil practice for load-bearing granular pads. This requirement should be elevated from ASSUMPTION to mandatory once the IFC civil drawings confirm compaction requirements, or explicitly excluded if the civil engineer determines compaction is not required. The civil engineer (PKG-005) shall confirm compaction requirement in IFC drawings.

### REQ-018-04-13 — Cold Weather Concreting Provisions (ASSUMPTION)
**Statement:** ASSUMPTION — When concrete placement occurs at ambient temperatures below +5 degrees C (or as otherwise defined in the IFC civil specification), the Contractor shall implement cold weather concreting provisions in accordance with the governing concrete standard (ASSUMPTION: CSA A23.1 or equivalent as identified by the P.Eng. of record). Provisions shall include, as applicable: heated enclosures, hot water batching, accelerating admixtures, extended cure monitoring, and insulated blankets.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION (standard Alberta construction practice for projects with December 31, 2026 completion deadline; concrete placement may occur in autumn/early winter; RFP §3.1 completion deadline; Guidance.md C2)
**Type:** ASSUMPTION — conditional requirement triggered by ambient temperature at time of placement
**Note:** This requirement addresses the gap identified in Guidance.md C2 and Procedure.md Step 3.4, where cold weather concreting is referenced as ASSUMPTION only with no mandatory threshold. The P.Eng. of record shall specify the trigger temperature and required provisions in the IFC civil specification. (Enrichment: A-001)

### REQ-018-04-14 — Dimensional Tolerance for Pad Construction
**Statement:** Cement and gravel pads shall be constructed within the dimensional tolerances specified in the IFC civil drawings. If the IFC civil drawings are silent on dimensional tolerance, the following defaults shall apply unless otherwise directed by the P.Eng. of record: plan dimensions +/-25mm; finished grade elevation +/-15mm. (ASSUMPTION)
**Source:** ASSUMPTION (standard civil construction practice; Procedure.md Verification references +/-25mm as ASSUMPTION; IFC drawings to govern)
**Type:** ASSUMPTION — subject to IFC civil drawing requirements
**Note:** This requirement addresses the gap identified in Procedure.md Verification where tolerances are referenced but not established in Specification.md. (Enrichment: A-006)

> **Enrichment consideration (X-002):** A pre-construction submittal requirement for Contractor concrete placement capability (approved concrete supplier, placement equipment, qualified crew) may be warranted given the safety-critical nature of the crane pads. This may already be covered by CCDC 14-2013 general conditions for design-build contracts. ASSUMPTION: not added as a standalone requirement at this time; to be reviewed against contract general conditions.
