# DEL-018-04 | Procedure — Cement & Gravel Pads

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and complete DEL-018-04 (Cement & Gravel Pads) — from design coordination through construction, inspection, and closeout. It covers both the cement pad(s) (SOW-0078) and the gravel pad (SOW-0079).

The Procedure is oriented to production of the deliverable (construction execution). It is intended for use by the General Contractor and site management team.

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Design Coordination and Pre-Construction

**Step 1.1 — Confirm Pad Layout with Civil Engineer (PKG-005)**
- Review Appendix B conceptual drawing with the civil engineer of record.
- Confirm all pad locations, approximate dimensions, and functional requirements (crane pads, equipment areas, storage zones).
- Initiate IFC civil drawing production for pad areas.
- Source: App B; RFP §3.3.2

**Step 1.2 — Obtain Crane Supplier Data for Crane Pad Design**
- Coordinate with DEL-016-01 procurement team to obtain crane supplier technical data sheet.
- Extract: anchor bolt pattern, base plate dimensions, dynamic and static loads (kN).
- Provide data to civil/structural engineer (P.Eng.) for crane pad design integration.
- Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (CRANE-COORDS-001); App B (2 x 5-tonne cranes)
- Timing: Must precede IFC drawing finalization for crane pad areas.

**Step 1.3 — Confirm Gravel Supply with County/Landfill**
- Contact County/Landfill representative to confirm gravel supply commitment, anticipated delivery timing, and gravel specification (gradation — ASSUMPTION).
- Align gravel delivery schedule with gravel pad construction window.
- Source: App B note; RFP §3.3.1 (SOW-0203)

**Step 1.4 — Obtain IFC Drawings and Permits**
- Receive P.Eng.-stamped IFC civil drawings for pad areas.
- Verify drawings address: pad dimensions, concrete mix design (MPa, slump, admixtures), reinforcement, drainage slopes, gravel pad specification, compaction requirements.
- Obtain applicable Safety Code permits as required.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2

**Step 1.5 — Resolve Material Supply Ambiguity (if not yet resolved)**
- Confirm with County/Landfill whether gravel aggregate for concrete mix (coarse/fine aggregate) is also County-supplied, or whether only gravel pad fill is County-supplied. (See CONF-018-04-02 in Guidance.md.)
- Document resolution and update procurement plan accordingly.
- Source: Guidance.md — Conflict Table CONF-018-04-02

---

### Phase 2 — Subgrade Preparation

**Step 2.1 — Verify Subgrade Readiness**
- Confirm DEL-018-02 (Site Grading) is complete in pad areas.
- Visually inspect and survey subgrade elevations against IFC design grades.
- **Enrichment (X-003):** Verify subgrade against explicit acceptance criteria as specified by the civil engineer in the IFC drawings. Acceptance criteria should include, as applicable: compaction density (% Standard Proctor or Modified Proctor), elevation tolerance, moisture content range, and visual confirmation of no organic material or soft spots. Do not proceed to pad construction until subgrade is formally approved against these criteria. TBD pending IFC civil drawing specification of subgrade acceptance criteria.
- Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (PRE-02); IFC civil drawings (TBD)

**Step 2.2 — Subgrade Compaction Testing (ASSUMPTION)**
- ASSUMPTION: Perform compaction testing of subgrade in pad areas per civil engineer's specification.
- Document test results and provide to County representative.
- Source: ASSUMPTION (standard civil practice; to be confirmed by IFC civil drawings)

**Step 2.3 — Place Granular Base Course (if specified)**
- ASSUMPTION: If IFC drawings specify a granular base course (crush, sub-base) beneath cement pads, place and compact to the specified density and depth.
- Source: ASSUMPTION; to be confirmed by IFC civil drawings and geotech recommendation

---

### Phase 3 — Cement Pad Construction (SOW-0078)

**Step 3.1 — Set Formwork**
- Install formwork per IFC drawing dimensions and finished grade/slope requirements.
- Check formwork for alignment, level, and drainage slope.
- Ensure crane pad areas are accurately dimensioned per crane supplier anchor bolt pattern.
- Source: IFC civil drawings; PRE-03, PRE-04

**Step 3.2 — Place Reinforcement (if specified)**
- ASSUMPTION: Install rebar or welded wire mesh as specified in IFC drawings.
- Verify bar size, spacing, cover, and placement per drawings.
- Install crane anchor bolts (if cast-in-place type) at exact locations per crane supplier template.
- Source: ASSUMPTION; IFC civil/structural drawings; crane supplier data

**Step 3.3 — Pre-Pour Inspection Hold Point**
- Notify County project representative of scheduled concrete pour.
- Conduct pre-pour inspection with County representative (formwork, reinforcement, anchor bolts).
- Obtain hold point release before placing concrete.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2 (County representative must be present at inspections)

**Step 3.4 — Place Concrete**
- Order concrete per approved mix design; verify batch ticket for each truck.
- Place concrete in lifts if required; consolidate with internal vibrator.
- Monitor ambient temperature; apply cold weather concreting provisions if temperature is below +5°C (ASSUMPTION: per CSA A23.1 or equivalent; to be specified in IFC drawings).
- Source: App B (contractor supplies cement); IFC mix design; ASSUMPTION (cold weather)

**Step 3.5 — Finish Concrete Surface**
- Screed to IFC design grades and drainage slopes.
- Apply specified surface finish (TBD in IFC drawings; ASSUMPTION: broom finish for exterior slabs).
- Install control joints as specified (location and spacing TBD in IFC drawings — ASSUMPTION: per engineer specification).
- Source: ASSUMPTION; IFC civil drawings

**Step 3.6 — Cure Concrete**
- Apply curing compound or wet cure method as specified in IFC drawings.
- ASSUMPTION: Maintain cure for minimum 7 days (or as specified) before formwork removal; achieve 28-day design strength before applying design loads (including crane installation loads).
- Monitor and document cure temperature if cold weather provisions are active.
- Source: ASSUMPTION (standard practice); IFC civil specification

**Step 3.7 — Remove Formwork and Backfill**
- Remove formwork after minimum cure period.
- Backfill edges as required per IFC drawings.
- Source: IFC civil drawings; standard practice

---

### Phase 4 — Gravel Pad Construction (SOW-0079)

**Step 4.1 — Confirm Gravel Delivery**
- Coordinate with County/Landfill for gravel delivery to site.
- Confirm gravel gradation meets civil engineer's specification (ASSUMPTION).
- Source: App B; RFP §3.3.1

**Step 4.1a — Verify Gravel Quality Upon Delivery (Enrichment: A-005)**
- Upon receipt of each gravel delivery from County/Landfill, verify that the delivered material meets the civil engineer's specification (gradation, cleanliness, maximum particle size — ASSUMPTION: specification to be provided in IFC civil drawings).
- Perform visual inspection at a minimum; if IFC specification requires gradation testing, obtain sample and submit to testing laboratory.
- Record delivery ticket information (date, quantity, source, truck ID) and acceptance decision.
- If delivered gravel does not meet specification, reject load and notify County/Landfill representative.
- Source: ASSUMPTION (standard practice for quality acceptance of owner-supplied materials); Guidance.md P3 (material supply split); App B

**Step 4.2 — Place and Spread Gravel**
- Spread County/Landfill-supplied gravel over prepared subgrade in the gravel pad zone (north of New North Shop per Appendix B).
- Place in lifts as specified (ASSUMPTION: lifts not exceeding 200mm loose depth, per typical compaction practice).
- Source: App B; ASSUMPTION

**Step 4.3 — Compact Gravel**
- Compact each lift to specified density using appropriate equipment (plate compactor, roller — ASSUMPTION).
- Perform compaction testing as specified (ASSUMPTION).
- Achieve finished grade per IFC civil drawing design grades and slopes.
- Source: ASSUMPTION; IFC civil drawings

**Step 4.4 — Verify Finished Grade and Drainage**
- Survey finished gravel pad grades against IFC design.
- Confirm positive drainage slope is achieved.
- Source: IFC civil drawings; RFP §3.4

---

### Phase 5 — Inspection, Verification, and Closeout

**Step 5.1 — Final Inspection — Cement Pads**
- Conduct final inspection of all cement pads with County representative.
- Verify: dimensions vs. IFC drawings; drainage slope; surface finish quality; no cracks or honeycombing; anchor bolt location and projection (crane pads).
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; REQ-018-04-10

**Step 5.2 — Final Inspection — Gravel Pad**
- Conduct final inspection of gravel pad with County representative.
- Verify: area coverage, surface grade and drainage, compaction (test records available).
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; REQ-018-04-10

**Step 5.3 — Provide Inspection Reports to County**
- Compile all inspection reports (pre-pour, Safety Code, final) and provide copies to County project representative.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2

**Step 5.4 — Complete As-Built Survey**
- Conduct survey of all pad areas after construction.
- Record actual dimensions, finished grades, and any deviations from IFC drawings.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2 (as-built survey required for project); standard practice

**Step 5.5 — Concrete Cylinder Break Results**
- ASSUMPTION: Receive and review 7-day and 28-day compressive strength cylinder break results from testing laboratory.
- Confirm design strength has been achieved before applying design loads.
- Provide results to County representative.
- Source: ASSUMPTION (standard practice for concrete QC)

**Step 5.6 — Confirm Downstream Readiness**
- Confirm with DEL-016-01 (Overhead Cranes) team that crane pads are complete, anchor bolts are set, and 28-day strength is confirmed.
- Issue written confirmation that crane pad areas are ready for crane installation.
- Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md

## Verification

| Verification Check | Method | Pass Condition | Timing |
|--------------------|--------|----------------|--------|
| Subgrade compaction (ASSUMPTION) | Compaction test reports (nuclear densometer or equivalent) | Meets civil engineer's specified density | Before placing base course or concrete |
| Subgrade acceptance (Enrichment: X-003) | Survey, compaction test, moisture test, visual inspection | Meets IFC-specified criteria for compaction density, elevation tolerance, moisture content; no organic material or soft spots | Before pad construction commences |
| Concrete mix compliance | Batch tickets; cylinder breaks at 7 and 28 days | Batch ticket matches approved mix; 28-day strength meets or exceeds specified MPa | During pour; 7 and 28 days post-pour |
| Formwork dimensions | Field measurement vs. IFC drawings | Within tolerance specified in IFC drawings, or default per REQ-018-04-14: plan dimensions +/-25mm, elevation +/-15mm (ASSUMPTION) | Before concrete pour |
| Drainage slope | Survey of finished grades | Positive slope per IFC design; no low points that pond water (ASSUMPTION) | Post-construction |
| Gravel pad compaction (ASSUMPTION) | Compaction test results | Meets civil engineer's specified density | During gravel placement |
| Gravel pad finished grade | Survey | Matches IFC design grades ± tolerance (TBD) | Post-compaction |
| Crane anchor bolt location (ASSUMPTION) | Template measurement before pour; survey after pour | Matches crane supplier template within tolerance specified by crane supplier (TBD) | Before pour; after pour |
| Safety Code compliance | Safety Code inspection sign-off | Inspector sign-off obtained | During / post-construction |
| Owner inspection attendance | Inspection request log; County rep sign-off on inspection reports | County representative present and reports delivered | Each inspection |
