# Procedure — DEL-018-03 Gravel Driving Surface

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce (construct) the gravel driving surface deliverable for the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition project. It covers the sequence of activities from pre-construction readiness through placement, compaction, inspection, and acceptance.

The procedure applies to the General Contractor and any subcontractors performing gravel surface work under DEL-018-03.

Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0077); _CONTEXT.md; Specification.md (REQ-018-03-01 through REQ-018-03-11).

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Design Confirmation and Planning

**Step 1.1 — Obtain and Review IFC Civil Drawings (HOLD POINT)**

Obtain the approved IFC civil drawings from PKG-005:
- DEL-005-04: Driving Surface & Pad Layout Plan — confirm the location, boundaries, and dimensions of all gravel driving surface areas.
- DEL-005-07: Civil Specification — extract the required aggregate type, gradation, layer thickness(es), and compaction standard.

Confirm that the drawings are signed/stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta (REQ-018-03-08).

**Document the date of IFC receipt and the name of the reviewer.** This constitutes the formal hold point release — no field work on the driving surface is authorized until this step is documented. (Lensing item D-001)

Source: RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0018.

**Step 1.2 — Establish Aggregate Coordination with Owner**

Contact the County/Landfill to confirm:
- Aggregate type and gradation to be supplied (must match civil spec).
- Delivery schedule and staging area on site.
- Quantity available and any delivery constraints.

**Staging Area Designation:** Designate a specific on-site staging area for Owner-supplied aggregate delivery. The staging area should be: (a) accessible to delivery trucks without interfering with active construction zones, (b) located to avoid drainage obstruction or interference with surface water flow paths, (c) of sufficient size for the planned delivery volumes. Document who designates the staging area (typically site superintendent in coordination with Owner) and confirm the location with the Owner before first delivery. (Lensing item X-001)

Document the coordination agreement. Note: the supply of aggregate is an Owner obligation; any delay in aggregate delivery must be communicated to the project schedule as an Owner-caused constraint.

Source: RFP §3.3.1; SOW-0203; App B note.

**Step 1.3 — Confirm Upstream Deliverable Completion**

Obtain written or documented confirmation that:
- DEL-018-01 (Topsoil Stripping) is complete on all driving surface areas.
- DEL-018-02 (Site Grading & Landscaping) is complete and subgrade is at design elevation.

Do not proceed to gravel placement without these confirmations.

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md; SOW-0075, SOW-0076.

**Step 1.4 — Confirm Inspection Notice Period**

Confirm and document the minimum notice period required by the County for scheduling inspections. This was identified as TBD in PRE-05 and must be resolved before construction begins. Record the agreed notice period and the name of the County contact who confirmed it. (Lensing item D-002)

Source: RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0084.

### Phase 2 — Subgrade Inspection

**Step 2.1 — Schedule Subgrade Inspection**

Notify the County project representative of the intended subgrade inspection date with the minimum advance notice confirmed in Step 1.4 (minimum notice period TBD until confirmed at project kickoff).

Source: RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0084.

**Step 2.2 — Perform Subgrade Inspection**

With the County representative present, inspect the subgrade across all driving surface areas for:
- Topsoil fully removed (no organic material present).
- Subgrade at correct design elevation per IFC drawings.
- Adequate cross-slope for drainage per DEL-005-02/DEL-005-03.
- No soft, wet, or unstable areas that would require remediation before placement.
- **Ground condition suitable for gravel placement** — subgrade must not be frozen, saturated, or in spring-thaw condition. If ground conditions are unsuitable, do not proceed; reschedule when conditions are suitable. (Lensing item F-003)

If deficiencies are found:
- Document deficiencies in the Deficiency Tracking Log (see Step 4.5).
- Coordinate with DEL-018-02 responsible party for remediation.
- Re-inspect before proceeding.

Source: REQ-018-03-04; REQ-018-03-05; standard geotechnical practice (ASSUMPTION).

**Step 2.3 — Record Subgrade Approval**

Document that the subgrade was inspected and approved by the County representative. Retain the pre-placement inspection form in the project records.

Source: SOW-0084, SOW-0085.

---

### Phase 3 — Gravel Placement

**Step 3.0 — Verify Weather and Ground Conditions**

Before commencing gravel placement each day (or resuming after a weather interruption), verify that:
- The subgrade is not frozen, saturated, or in a condition that would impair compaction quality.
- Active precipitation is not occurring at a rate that would prevent adequate compaction.
- The aggregate moisture content is within the range required by the civil specification (DEL-005-07), if specified.

If conditions are unsuitable, suspend placement operations until conditions improve. Document any weather shutdowns and the basis for the decision. (Lensing item F-003)

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard earthworks practice for central Alberta conditions.

**Step 3.1 — Verify Aggregate on Site and Perform Incoming Acceptance**

Confirm that Owner-supplied gravel aggregate is staged on site in sufficient quantity for the planned placement area. Perform incoming aggregate acceptance per REQ-018-03-07A:
- Visually inspect the delivered aggregate for type, size, and general conformance with the civil specification (DEL-005-07).
- Compare delivery documentation against the specified gradation requirements.
- If the aggregate does not conform, reject the delivery, document the non-conformance, and notify the Owner.
- Retain delivery tickets and incoming acceptance records for quantity and quality documentation. (Lensing item C-001)

Source: SOW-0203; REQ-018-03-07; REQ-018-03-07A.

**Step 3.2 — Spread Gravel to Design Thickness**

Using appropriate spreading equipment (ASSUMPTION: motor grader or dozer with blade), spread the aggregate over the approved subgrade area to the design thickness specified in DEL-005-07.

- Work in lifts if the civil specification requires compacted lift thickness limits.
- Maintain aggregate staging and spreading sequence to avoid tracking onto areas not yet graded.
- Maintain temporary drainage during placement to prevent water ponding on subgrade.

Source: REQ-018-03-01; REQ-018-03-06; civil spec (DEL-005-07); ASSUMPTION: standard gravel road construction practice.

**Step 3.3 — Compact Gravel**

Using approved compaction equipment (ASSUMPTION: vibratory roller or pad-foot compactor as specified by civil engineer), compact each lift of aggregate to the specified compaction standard in DEL-005-07.

- Perform compaction in consistent passes per civil specification.
- Compact from edges toward crown (or per civil engineer's direction).
- Maintain moisture content of aggregate within range required for compaction (if applicable per aggregate type and civil spec). If moisture content is outside the acceptable range, adjust by wetting or aerating before compaction. (Lensing item F-003)

Source: REQ-018-03-06; DEL-005-07 (pending).

**Step 3.4 — Compaction Testing**

Perform compaction testing per the method and frequency specified in DEL-005-07 (method TBD — likely nuclear densometer or sand cone, per civil specification).

- If tests fail: add additional compaction passes and re-test.
- Do not proceed to next lift until current lift passes compaction testing.
- Retain all test results.

Source: REQ-018-03-06; Specification.md Verification table.

**Step 3.5 — Final Grading and Surface Dressing**

After compaction of all lifts, final-grade the surface with a motor grader to achieve:
- Correct cross-slope for drainage per IFC drawings.
- Smooth surface without ridges, hollows, or windrows.
- Matching connections to adjacent pads, buildings, and access routes.

Source: REQ-018-03-05; REQ-018-03-08; ASSUMPTION: standard practice.

**Step 3.6 — Proof-Rolling (if required by civil specification)**

If the civil engineer requires proof-rolling per REQ-018-03-11:
- Perform proof-rolling with the specified loaded vehicle over the completed gravel surface.
- Observe and record any deflection, rutting, or pumping.
- If deflection/rutting exceeds the civil engineer's specified acceptance limit, identify and remediate affected areas (additional compaction, material replacement, or as directed by civil engineer).
- Re-proof-roll remediated areas.

Source: REQ-018-03-11; ASSUMPTION — subject to civil engineer determination. (Lensing item D-003)

### Phase 4 — Inspection and Acceptance

**Step 4.1 — Schedule Post-Placement Inspection**

Notify the County project representative of the post-placement/compaction acceptance inspection date with the agreed advance notice period (per Step 1.4).

Source: RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0084.

**Step 4.2 — Perform Post-Placement Inspection**

With the County representative present, inspect the completed gravel driving surface for:
- Surface material is gravel throughout (REQ-018-03-01).
- Surface dimensions and extents per IFC drawings (REQ-018-03-08).
- Adequate cross-slope and drainage (REQ-018-03-05).
- No soft spots, rutting, or areas of inadequate compaction (REQ-018-03-02, REQ-018-03-06).
- Connections to adjacent pads and access routes are correct.

Source: Specification.md Verification table; SOW-0084.

**Step 4.3 — Provide Inspection Report to County**

Prepare and provide a copy of the post-placement inspection report to the County project representative.

Source: SOW-0085; REQ-018-03-09.

**Step 4.4 — As-Built Verification**

Coordinate with the survey team (DEL-008-04 — As-Built Survey) to capture as-built dimensions and elevations of the gravel driving surface as required. Retain as-built records.

**Note:** The as-built survey deliverable ID (DEL-008-04) should be confirmed — see Guidance Conflict Table for an identified ambiguity between this reference and DEL-005-04 (Driving Surface & Pad Layout Plan). The project manager should confirm which deliverable ID governs the as-built survey for this work. (Lensing item X-004)

Source: SOW-0004; REQ-018-03-08.

**Step 4.5 — Initiate and Maintain Deficiency Tracking Log**

If any deficiencies are identified during inspection (Step 4.2) or at any other point during the construction or acceptance process:
- Create (or update) the Deficiency Tracking Log for DEL-018-03.
- For each deficiency, record: (a) deficiency ID, (b) date identified, (c) description, (d) location on site, (e) responsible party for correction, (f) target correction date, (g) correction action taken, (h) date closed, (i) sign-off.
- Track all deficiencies through to resolution and closure before the deliverable is marked complete.
- The Deficiency Tracking Log is a required record (see Records table) and must be included in the record package (Step 5.1). (Lensing item E-003)

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard construction practice for deficiency management. Referenced in Records table but previously not described in procedure steps.

---

### Phase 5 — Records and Closeout

**Step 5.1 — Compile Record Package**

Assemble the following records for the deliverable record package:
- IFC Drawing Review Sign-Off (documenting hold point release — Step 1.1).
- Pre-placement subgrade inspection form (signed, with County representative acknowledgement).
- Owner aggregate delivery tickets (quantity records).
- Incoming aggregate acceptance forms (per REQ-018-03-07A). (Lensing item C-001)
- Compaction test reports (all lifts).
- Post-placement inspection report (signed, with County representative acknowledgement).
- As-built survey or dimensional record.
- Photo documentation: pre-placement subgrade photos, during-placement photos, post-compaction surface photos. Photos should document the construction sequence and provide non-destructive evidence of conditions at each stage. (Lensing item X-002)
- Proof-roll test record (if proof-rolling was performed per REQ-018-03-11). (Lensing item D-003)
- Deficiency Tracking Log (all deficiencies documented and closed, or with open items and status noted). (Lensing item E-003)
- Any weather shutdown records and basis documentation. (Lensing item F-003)

Source: Specification.md Documentation table; SOW-0084, SOW-0085.

**Step 5.2 — Flag for Guarantee Register**

Notify the project management team (DEL-019 / PKG-019) that DEL-018-03 is complete and should be entered into the 2-year guarantee register upon CCC.

Source: RFP §2.10; REQ-018-03-10.
