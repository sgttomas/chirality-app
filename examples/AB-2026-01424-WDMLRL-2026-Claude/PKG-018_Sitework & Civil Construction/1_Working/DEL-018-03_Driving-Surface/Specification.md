# Specification — DEL-018-03 Gravel Driving Surface

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-018-03 covers the construction of the gravel driving surface at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill (WDMLRL) Maintenance Shop Addition site (SW 14–44–21–W4, near Ferintosh, Alberta). Specifically, this deliverable includes:

- Receipt and placement of Owner-supplied gravel aggregate across designated driving surface areas.
- Spreading and compaction of gravel to achieve a surface designed to support the weight and turning radius of heavy construction equipment (motor scraper class).
- All labour, equipment, and supervision required for surface preparation and gravel placement.
- Coordination with the civil design (PKG-005) for layout, depth, and compaction specifications.
- Coordination with and handoff to site operations (access for PKG-016 crane delivery, PKG-017 building construction, and ongoing landfill operations).

Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0077); WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md, PKG-018 table; _CONTEXT.md.

## Requirements

### REQ-018-03-01: Surface Material
**Requirement:** The final driving surface shall be gravel.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 — "The final driving surface shall be gravel and designed to accommodate the weight and turning radius of large construction equipment, such as a motor scraper."

**Priority:** Mandatory.

---

### REQ-018-03-02: Design Load — Weight
**Requirement:** The gravel driving surface shall be designed and constructed to accommodate the weight of large construction equipment of motor scraper class.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0077 — "designed for weight and turning radius of heavy construction equipment (motor scraper class)."

**Priority:** Mandatory.

**Note:** A motor scraper (e.g., Caterpillar 623 class) is a self-propelled earthmoving machine. Operating weight is typically in the range of 30,000–50,000 kg depending on configuration. ASSUMPTION: exact design axle load or equivalent single axle load (ESAL) to be specified by the civil engineer in DEL-005-07; this requirement establishes the performance intent.

---

### REQ-018-03-03: Design Geometry — Turning Radius
**Requirement:** The gravel driving surface shall be designed and constructed to accommodate the turning radius of large construction equipment of motor scraper class.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0077.

**Priority:** Mandatory.

**Note:** ASSUMPTION: specific turning radius dimensions to be confirmed by civil engineer in DEL-005-04 (Driving Surface & Pad Layout Plan). Motor scraper turning radius is typically 7–12 m (outside wheel). The layout plan must provide sufficient width and curve geometry.

---

### REQ-018-03-04: Subgrade Preparation
**Requirement:** All topsoil shall be stripped from driving surface areas prior to gravel placement.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0075 — "Strip topsoil from all driving surfaces (if not already performed by Owner)."

**Priority:** Mandatory.

**Note:** Topsoil stripping is the subject of DEL-018-01. This requirement is a prerequisite condition for DEL-018-03 construction activities; confirmation of DEL-018-01 completion is required before gravel placement begins.

---

### REQ-018-03-05: Grading and Drainage
**Requirement:** The driving surface area shall be graded to provide positive drainage, consistent with the site grading plan. The grading shall not alter drainage conditions of neighbouring properties.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 — "Grading Design shall account for positive site drainage and future storm events. The Grading design shall not alter the drainage conditions of neighboring properties."

**Priority:** Mandatory.

**Acceptance Criterion:** No ponding greater than 25 mm depth remaining 24 hours after a rain event, or positive cross-slope of minimum 2% confirmed by survey, whichever is specified in the civil design (DEL-005-02). TBD — the civil engineer must confirm the quantitative drainage acceptance standard in DEL-005-07; the values stated here are ASSUMPTION based on standard practice for heavy-duty gravel surfaces and should be replaced with the civil engineer's values when available. (Lensing item X-003)

**Note:** Site grading is the subject of DEL-018-02. Drainage compatibility of the gravel surface with the overall grading plan must be confirmed during civil design (PKG-005).

---

### REQ-018-03-06: Compaction
**Requirement:** Gravel shall be compacted to a standard sufficient to support the design load class specified in REQ-018-03-02. The compaction standard, including the target compaction percentage (e.g., percent Modified Proctor or Standard Proctor), shall be specified by the civil engineer in DEL-005-07. Until the civil specification is issued, the specific compaction standard remains TBD. The civil engineer shall provide the compaction standard no later than the IFC drawing issuance milestone for PKG-005. (Lensing item A-001)

**Source:** ASSUMPTION — inferred from RFP §3.3.2 performance intent (motor scraper class load). Specific compaction specification (e.g., proctor standard, percent compaction) to be set by civil engineer in DEL-005-07.

**Priority:** Mandatory.

**Verification:** Compaction testing per method specified in civil specification (DEL-005-07). The test method (e.g., nuclear densometer, sand cone, or other method) shall be named in DEL-005-07; until that specification is issued, the test method remains TBD. (Lensing item A-003)

---

### REQ-018-03-07: Aggregate Supply Coordination
**Requirement:** The Contractor shall coordinate with the County/Landfill for timely delivery of Owner-supplied gravel aggregate to the site.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.1 (SOW-0203) — "Supply of aggregate/gravel" is County/Landfill responsibility; App B — "Landfill to supply gravel."

**Priority:** Mandatory (coordination obligation on Contractor; supply obligation on Owner).

---

### REQ-018-03-07A: Incoming Aggregate Acceptance
**Requirement:** The Contractor shall verify that Owner-supplied aggregate delivered to site conforms to the aggregate specification in the civil specification (DEL-005-07) before accepting delivery for placement. Verification shall include, at minimum, visual inspection for aggregate type/size and comparison of delivery documentation against the specified gradation. If the aggregate does not conform, the Contractor shall reject the delivery and notify the Owner. (Lensing item C-001)

**Source:** ASSUMPTION — inferred from standard construction practice for Owner-supplied materials. REQ-018-03-07 addresses coordination for delivery but does not require verification that the delivered aggregate meets specification. Procedure Step 3.1 mentions "verify aggregate type/gradation matches civil spec" but this was not backed by a Specification requirement until this addition.

**Priority:** Mandatory.

**Note:** This requirement closes a gap between the coordination obligation (REQ-018-03-07) and the construction quality obligation. The incoming acceptance check makes the duty to ensure material quality enforceable.

---

### REQ-018-03-08: Conformance to Civil IFC Drawings
**Requirement:** The gravel driving surface shall be placed in conformance with the Issued for Construction (IFC) civil drawings produced by PKG-005, signed and stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 — "All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta"; SOW-0018.

**Priority:** Mandatory.

**Hold Point:** No gravel placement shall proceed until the IFC civil drawings (DEL-005-04 and DEL-005-07) have been received and formally reviewed by the Contractor's site superintendent or designate. Receipt and review shall be documented (see Procedure PRE-01 and Verification V-01). This constitutes a formal hold point — field work on the driving surface is not authorized without documented IFC receipt and review. (Lensing item D-001)

**Note:** Civil IFC drawings (DEL-005-04, DEL-005-07) are a prerequisite input; this deliverable cannot be executed in final form until those drawings are available.

---

### REQ-018-03-09: Inspection and County Presence
**Requirement:** The Contractor shall submit all inspection requests. The County project representative must be present at all inspections. Copies of all completed inspection reports shall be provided to the County.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0084, SOW-0085.

**Priority:** Mandatory.

---

### REQ-018-03-10: Guarantee Period Obligations
**Requirement:** The completed driving surface is subject to the 2-year guarantee period post-Construction Completion Certificate (CCC). Any defects or deficiencies must be corrected within 10 days of Owner's written instruction.

**Source:** RFP §2.10 (Guarantee Period); RFP §2.10.6.

**Priority:** Mandatory.

**Guarantee Period Monitoring:** TBD — the Owner or project manager should establish a protocol for periodic condition assessment of the driving surface during the 2-year guarantee period. At minimum, the protocol should specify: (a) the frequency of condition assessments (ASSUMPTION: semi-annual or after significant weather events), (b) the responsible party for initiating assessments, and (c) the criteria for determining whether observed conditions constitute defects requiring correction under the guarantee. This protocol is not specified in the RFP and should be established by the Owner/project manager. (Lensing item E-002)

---

### REQ-018-03-11: Design Load Verification — Proof-Rolling
**Requirement:** ASSUMPTION — consider requiring a proof-rolling or loaded-vehicle test as a direct performance verification for the design load capacity of the completed gravel surface (REQ-018-03-02). Proof-rolling with a loaded vehicle (e.g., loaded tandem axle truck or equivalent) provides a direct field performance check that compaction testing alone may not capture. The civil engineer should confirm whether proof-rolling is required and specify the test vehicle, number of passes, and acceptance criteria (e.g., maximum allowable deflection/rutting) in DEL-005-07. (Lensing item D-003)

**Source:** ASSUMPTION — standard practice for heavy-duty gravel surfaces; not explicitly required by RFP. Civil engineer to determine applicability.

**Priority:** TBD — subject to civil engineer determination.
