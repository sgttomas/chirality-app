# DEL-018-01 — Topsoil Stripping | Specification

---

## Scope

### Included in This Deliverable

This specification governs the topsoil stripping construction activity at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project site (SW 14–44–21–W4, near Ferintosh, Alberta).

**In scope:**
- Mechanical stripping and removal of topsoil from all driving surfaces within the project lot, as defined by the Issued for Construction (IFC) drawings.
- Temporary stockpiling of stripped topsoil on site for future reuse in landscaping and site restoration.
- Site preparation to expose subgrade for subsequent grading, surface construction, and pad construction activities.
- Coordination with upstream preconditions (site access, survey, utility locate) to ensure safe and compliant execution.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0075); Decomposition §PKG-018, §DEL-018-01.

## Requirements

### REQ-018-01-01 — Topsoil Stripping Coverage
**Requirement:** The General Contractor shall strip topsoil from all driving surfaces on the project lot.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2, SOW-0075.
**Note:** "All driving surfaces" is interpreted as the full extent of planned vehicle and equipment traffic areas. Exact limits are defined by the IFC site drawings (DEL-005-02). Where IFC drawings have not yet been issued, limits are TBD.

### REQ-018-01-02 — Scope Applicability Regardless of Owner Activity
**Requirement:** Topsoil stripping shall be performed by the General Contractor regardless of whether the County/Owner has previously performed any topsoil removal.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 ("if not already performed by Owner"); Decomposition D-010 (assumed required).
**Note:** Decision D-010 resolved this as unconditionally required from the Proponent's scope.

### REQ-018-01-03 — Utility Locate Prior to Excavation
**Requirement:** All underground utilities within the stripping area must be located, identified, and marked before any mechanical excavation commences.
**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md (UTILITIES-001 upstream dependency); ASSUMPTION: required by Alberta OH&S and standard civil earthworks practice.
**Note:** Utility locate is a safety-critical precondition.

### REQ-018-01-04 — Site Survey Precondition
**Requirement:** A site survey and topsoil depth assessment (SURVEY-001) must be completed before stripping commences, to define excavation depth, area limits, and estimated volume.
**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md (SURVEY-001 upstream dependency).

### REQ-018-01-05 — Topsoil Salvage for Reuse
**Requirement:** Stripped topsoil shall be salvaged and temporarily stockpiled on site for future reuse in landscaping and site restoration activities.
**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (context summary: "Topsoil salvage is important for later landscaping and site restoration work").
**Note:** Stockpile location and management plan are TBD pending site logistics design.

### REQ-018-01-06 — Inspection Coordination
**Requirement:** The General Contractor shall submit all applicable inspection requests. The County project representative must be present for all inspections, and the Contractor shall provide copies of all complete inspection reports to the County.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2.

### REQ-018-01-07 — IFC Drawing Compliance
**Requirement:** Topsoil stripping limits, depths, and extents shall conform to the IFC drawings issued by the Civil Engineer (DEL-005-02 — Site Grading Plan).
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (construct as per IFC drawings); ASSUMPTION: stripping limits are civil design output.

### REQ-018-01-08 — Alberta Safety Code Compliance
**Requirement:** All work shall adhere to applicable Alberta Safety Codes and OH&S requirements. **[A-003]** Specifically, the following Alberta regulatory domains are relevant to ground disturbance and excavation activities — the applicable sections must be identified and confirmed before execution:
- Alberta Occupational Health and Safety Act, Regulation, and Code (particularly provisions governing ground disturbance, excavation safety, and utility protection) — specific sections TBD.
- Alberta Environmental Protection and Enhancement Act (EPEA), if applicable to soil disturbance at a landfill site — applicability TBD.
- Municipal bylaws or County-specific requirements for ground disturbance — applicability TBD.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (building design must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes); ASSUMPTION: this requirement extends to all construction activities. **[A-001]** The generic reference to "all Alberta Safety Codes" requires narrowing to specific applicable sections for enforceability; the above list is a candidate starting point — confirmation TBD.

### REQ-018-01-09 — Erosion and Sediment Control During Stripping
**Requirement:** The General Contractor shall implement interim erosion and sediment control measures during and after topsoil stripping operations to prevent sediment migration from stripped areas. Trigger conditions: (a) whenever stripped subgrade is exposed and grading (DEL-018-02) has not yet commenced, and (b) during active stripping when precipitation is forecast or surface runoff is observed.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION: required by standard Alberta civil construction practice and applicable environmental regulations; specific regulatory requirements TBD pending confirmation of EPEA applicability to the site. **[A-002]** This requirement formalizes the erosion control gap identified between Guidance Considerations and Specification requirements.
**Note:** Specific control measures (e.g., silt fence, erosion blanket, temporary seeding) are TBD per site conditions and contractor means and methods.

### REQ-018-01-10 — Ground Disturbance Permit
**Requirement:** The General Contractor shall confirm whether a formal ground disturbance permit is required under Alberta regulations (Alberta OH&S Act and/or local municipal authority) for topsoil stripping at this site and scope. If required, the permit must be obtained before any mechanical excavation commences.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION: Alberta OH&S Act may require ground disturbance notification or permit for excavation activities; applicability to this specific site and scope is TBD. **[C-001]** Procedure Step 2 references ground disturbance authorization as "if required" but Specification did not previously establish whether it is required; this requirement formalizes the obligation to confirm.
**Note:** The determination of whether a permit is required should be completed during the pre-construction planning phase and documented in the OH&S plan.
