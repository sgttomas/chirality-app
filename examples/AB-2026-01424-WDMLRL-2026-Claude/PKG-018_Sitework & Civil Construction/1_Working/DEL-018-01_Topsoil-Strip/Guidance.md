# DEL-018-01 — Topsoil Stripping | Guidance

---

## Purpose

DEL-018-01 (Topsoil Stripping) exists to prepare the project site for all subsequent civil construction activities within PKG-018. Topsoil — the organic upper layer of soil — is unsuitable as a load-bearing substrate for driving surfaces, pads, and building infrastructure. Its removal exposes competent subgrade material on which grading, gravel driving surfaces, and cement/gravel pads can be properly constructed.

Additionally, topsoil is a valuable resource for site restoration. Salvaging and stockpiling stripped topsoil on site enables its reuse in landscaping and grading activities (DEL-018-02), supporting the site's eventual restoration to a functional and compliant condition.

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (context summary); RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0075); Decomposition §PKG-018.

DEL-018-01 is the first and foundational activity in PKG-018. All other sitework deliverables (DEL-018-02 through DEL-018-06) depend on it being complete. It is a critical path item for the entire project.

**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md; Decomposition §PKG-018 (downstream dependency list).

## Principles

### 1. Safety First — Utility Locate is Non-Negotiable
Underground utility strikes are a serious safety hazard and can cause project delays, regulatory violations, and physical harm. The utility locate (UTILITIES-001) must be completed and all utilities marked before any mechanical excavation occurs. This is not a scheduling convenience — it is a safety-critical precondition.

**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md (UTILITIES-001); ASSUMPTION: Alberta OH&S requirements mandate ground disturbance protection procedures.

### 2. Topsoil is a Project Asset — Salvage, Do Not Waste
Stripped topsoil must be stockpiled, not disposed of. It will be needed for final landscaping and site grading (DEL-018-02) and represents a material cost savings versus importing topsoil later. A stockpile area must be designated in the site logistics plan before stripping commences, located to avoid interference with other construction activities.

**[F-003] Economic basis:** The cost savings claim rests on the difference between salvaging on-site topsoil (handling and stockpiling cost) versus importing replacement topsoil (purchase, trucking, and placement cost). For Alberta rural sites, imported topsoil typically costs TBD $/m^3 delivered — an order-of-magnitude estimate should be developed once stripped volume is known (see Datasheet, Volume of Topsoil). This quantification is TBD but is relevant to the trade-off analysis and to justifying stockpile protection investment.

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md (context summary: "Topsoil salvage is important for later landscaping and site restoration work").

### 3. IFC Drawings Define the Limits — Do Not Over-Strip or Under-Strip
Stripping limits are defined by the IFC site grading plan (DEL-005-02, Civil Engineer). Over-stripping wastes topsoil and creates unnecessary rework; under-stripping leaves organic material beneath driving surfaces that will cause settlement and failure. Work within the limits shown on IFC drawings.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; ASSUMPTION: consistent with standard civil earthworks practice.

### 4. Sequence Matters — Start Only When Preconditions Are Met
The three upstream preconditions (SITE-ACCESS-001, SURVEY-001, UTILITIES-001) are not mere formalities. Starting before they are satisfied creates risk (utility strikes without locate; wrong depth without survey; equipment hazards without proper site access). Confirm all three are resolved before mobilizing earthworks equipment.

**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md.

### 5. Assume Required, Even If Owner Has Started
Per Decision D-010, the Proponent must include topsoil stripping in scope regardless of whether the County/Owner has previously performed any removal. At mobilization, assess the actual site condition, document it photographically, and proceed with stripping of any remaining topsoil on driving surfaces.

**[F-001] Rationale for D-010 (unconditional scope):** The RFP §3.3.2 uses conditional language ("if not already performed by Owner"), which could be read as making topsoil stripping optional if the Owner has already done the work. Decision D-010 resolved this as unconditionally required for the Proponent's scope based on the following reasoning: (a) the Proponent cannot verify the depth, quality, or completeness of any prior Owner stripping without a site survey; (b) leaving the scope conditional introduces schedule risk if the Owner's prior work is found insufficient at mobilization; (c) pricing the scope as unconditional provides budget certainty and eliminates a potential change-order dispute. This rationale is an ASSUMPTION inferred from the decomposition decision context — the human should confirm or revise the reasoning behind D-010.

**Source:** Decomposition D-010; RFP §3.3.2 ("if not already performed by Owner" — treated as unconditional per D-010).

## Considerations

### Scope Boundary — Bulk Cut/Fill vs. Topsoil Stripping
Topsoil stripping (SOW-0075) and bulk cut/fill (SOW-0201) are distinct activities with different responsibility assignments. Topsoil stripping is the Proponent's responsibility; bulk cut and fill is the County's responsibility. The distinction matters for:
- **Depth:** Topsoil stripping removes only the organic upper layer (depth TBD per SURVEY-001); bulk earthworks adjust subgrade elevations. Do not conflate the two.
- **Billing:** Activities outside the Proponent's scope should not be invoiced under DEL-018-01.
- **Timing:** The County may perform bulk cut/fill at a different time; coordination with County's schedule is needed.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.1 (SOW-0201 excluded); Decomposition §4 Scope Boundaries.

### Stockpile Location — Site Logistics Impact
The topsoil stockpile will occupy site space during construction. A poorly located stockpile can impede access for subsequent construction activities, equipment movement, and material delivery. Plan the stockpile location during pre-construction site layout, and confirm it does not interfere with:
- Crane or overhead equipment swing radii
- Building footprint and laydown areas
- Future grading areas and pad locations

**Source:** ASSUMPTION: derived from site logistics principles; specific stockpile location TBD in site logistics plan.

### Clearing Must Precede Stripping
The County is responsible for brushing and clearing the project area (SOW-0200). The Proponent should not commence topsoil stripping until the County's clearing work is complete, as vegetation and debris interfere with mechanical stripping. Confirm clearing completion status at site mobilization.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.1, Decomposition SOW-0200.

### Site Map Dependency — Clearing Limits
Appendix C – Site Maps (R-07) depicts the clearing limits performed by the County. These maps also define the project expansion area and site topography relevant to stripping extent. The maps should be reviewed by the site superintendent before commencing work.

### Environmental and Erosion Controls
Topsoil stripping exposes bare subgrade to weather and runoff. During the construction period, interim erosion and sediment control measures may be required to prevent sediment migration from the project site — particularly relevant given the landfill site context and Alberta regulatory environment. See Specification REQ-018-01-09 for the formal requirement.

**Source:** ASSUMPTION: standard Alberta civil construction practice; specific requirements TBD pending environmental review.

### Dust Control During Stripping Operations
**[E-001]** Mechanical topsoil stripping generates airborne dust, particularly during dry conditions. At a rural Alberta landfill site, dust control may be a regulatory requirement under Alberta Environment and Protected Areas regulations and/or municipal bylaws, and is an operational concern for worker safety and neighboring properties. Mitigation measures may include:
- Water application to working surfaces during dry/windy conditions.
- Limiting stripping area exposed at any one time.
- Monitoring wind conditions and suspending operations during high-wind events.

Specific dust control requirements and regulatory obligations for this site are TBD pending confirmation of applicable regulations (see Specification REQ-018-01-08 and Standards table).

**Source:** ASSUMPTION: standard Alberta earthworks practice for dust management. Specific regulatory requirements TBD — **location TBD** in Alberta Environment and Protected Areas regulations.

### Contingency for Unexpected Subsurface Conditions
**[E-002]** The project site is a landfill (West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill). During topsoil stripping, there is a possibility of encountering unexpected subsurface conditions, including but not limited to:
- Buried waste material, debris, or previously deposited fill.
- Contaminated soil or groundwater.
- Archaeological or historical finds (Alberta Historical Resources Act may apply).
- Unexpected utilities not identified during the locate process.

No document currently addresses what to do if unexpected conditions are encountered during stripping. A contingency protocol should be established during pre-construction planning, including: stop-work triggers, notification chain (site superintendent, County representative, environmental officer as applicable), documentation requirements, and resumption criteria.

## Terminology Notes

### Stockpile Terminology — [X-003]
The following terms are used across the four documents to refer to aspects of topsoil stockpile management: "temporary stockpile," "topsoil stockpile," "stockpile area," and "stockpile plan." For consistency, the canonical terms are:
- **Topsoil stockpile**: the physical pile of stripped topsoil material.
- **Stockpile area**: the designated on-site location where the topsoil stockpile is placed.
- **Stockpile plan**: the document describing stockpile area selection, management approach, and protection measures.

All documents should use these terms consistently. "Temporary" is implied — all stockpiles in this deliverable context are temporary pending reuse in DEL-018-02.

### "All Driving Surfaces" Scope Definition — [B-003]
"All driving surfaces" as used in this deliverable (originating from RFP §3.3.2) is interpreted by Specification REQ-018-01-01 as "the full extent of planned vehicle and equipment traffic areas" as defined by IFC drawings (DEL-005-02). Whether this scope extends to pedestrian paths, equipment staging areas, or building perimeter areas is TBD pending IFC drawing review. Until resolved, Specification (as normative authority) governs the interpretation. If ambiguity persists after IFC drawing issuance, this should be escalated for human ruling — see Conflict Table CFT-002.
