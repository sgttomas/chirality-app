# DEL-018-01 — Topsoil Stripping | Procedure

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and complete the Topsoil Stripping deliverable (DEL-018-01) at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project site. It covers pre-execution planning, field execution, verification, and records requirements.

The procedure applies to the General Contractor and any subcontractors engaged for earthworks. It is intended to be read in conjunction with the Specification (Specification.md) and the IFC Civil drawings (DEL-005-02 — Site Grading Plan, when issued).

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; _CONTEXT.md; _DEPENDENCIES.md; Decomposition §PKG-018.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Pre-Construction Site Assessment and Documentation

**Action:**
1. Conduct a pre-construction walkover of the entire project site with the site superintendent and, where appropriate, a County representative.
2. Document existing site conditions with dated photographs, noting:
   - Existing topsoil depth (visual estimate, pending formal survey confirmation).
   - Any areas where the County may have already performed topsoil removal.
   - Existing drainage patterns, surface features, and any visible utilities.
3. Confirm clearing completion against Appendix C – Site Maps (R-07) clearing limits.
4. Note any discrepancies between actual site conditions and information in the IFC drawings or site maps.
5. Record findings in the site daily log and pre-construction condition report.

**Per D-010:** Even if the County appears to have performed partial topsoil removal, the Proponent is required to verify and complete stripping to the specified depth and extent. Document the as-found condition.

**Source:** Decomposition D-010; RFP §3.3.2; _DEPENDENCIES.md.

**Output:** Pre-construction condition report; dated site photographs.

---

### Step 2 — Pre-Excavation Safety Verification

**Action:**
1. Confirm utility locate certificate is in hand for the stripping area (UTILITIES-001).
2. Brief all equipment operators and site workers on marked utility locations, exclusion zones, and emergency procedures.
3. Confirm site OH&S plan is active and ground disturbance authorization has been obtained if required (see Specification REQ-018-01-10 — the determination of whether a permit is required must be completed before this step; **[C-001]** do not proceed to excavation with the permit question unresolved).
4. Confirm any Alberta One Call / Click Before You Dig notifications have been made and response received.
5. Ensure all equipment is in safe working condition with applicable pre-use inspections completed.

**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md (UTILITIES-001); ASSUMPTION: Alberta OH&S requirements for ground disturbance.

**Output:** Safety briefing record; utility locate certificate on file; pre-use inspection records.

---

### Step 3 — Establish Stripping Limits and Stockpile Area

**Action:**
1. Review IFC site grading plan (DEL-005-02) to confirm:
   - The extents of all driving surface areas to be stripped.
   - Target subgrade elevations after stripping.
   - Topsoil stripping depth (to be confirmed against SURVEY-001 report).
2. Stake or mark stripping limits on the ground as required for equipment operators.
3. Identify and mark the designated temporary topsoil stockpile area on site, ensuring it:
   - Does not obstruct planned driving surfaces, building footprint, pad areas, or equipment access routes.
   - Is accessible to hauling equipment during stripping.
   - Is positioned to allow future loading for reuse in landscaping (DEL-018-02).
4. Confirm stockpile area with site superintendent and record in site logistics plan.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; _CONTEXT.md; ASSUMPTION: IFC drawings define stripping limits; stockpile siting per site logistics principles.

**If IFC drawings not yet issued:** Do not commence full stripping. Record TBD status; proceed only where limits are unambiguous and safe to proceed without full drawing set.

**Output:** Staked stripping limits; stockpile area designated and recorded; site logistics note.

---

### Step 4 — Mechanical Topsoil Stripping

**Action:**
1. Deploy earthworks equipment (type TBD — ASSUMPTION: dozer, scraper, or excavator appropriate to topsoil depth and area; selection per contractor means and methods).
2. **[F-002]** Before commencing stripping each day, confirm that weather and ground conditions are suitable: ground must be thawed and workable, and site conditions must allow mechanical operations without excessive rutting or topsoil quality degradation. If frozen ground, heavy rain, or excessively wet conditions are present, suspend operations and record the delay in the daily site log.
3. Strip topsoil from all driving surface areas as defined by IFC drawings and SURVEY-001 depth assessment:
   - Remove topsoil to the depth specified in IFC drawings or survey report.
   - Maintain uniform stripping to avoid leaving pockets of organic material that would cause differential settlement under driving surfaces.
4. Load and haul stripped topsoil to the designated on-site stockpile area.
5. Do not mix subgrade material into the topsoil stockpile — topsoil quality must be maintained for future landscaping reuse.
6. Monitor stripping progress against IFC drawing limits; do not over-strip beyond design extents.
7. Maintain daily site log entries recording:
   - Areas stripped each day.
   - Estimated topsoil volumes moved.
   - Equipment used.
   - Any unexpected conditions encountered (buried obstructions, utility near-misses, unusual soil conditions).

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0075); _CONTEXT.md; ASSUMPTION: execution method per standard earthworks practice.

**Output:** Stripped subgrade surface; topsoil stockpile built; daily site logs.

---

### Step 5 — Interim Inspection and Progress Monitoring

**Action:**
1. Conduct periodic site inspections during stripping to confirm:
   - Work is proceeding within IFC drawing limits.
   - Stripping depth is consistent with design requirements.
   - Topsoil stockpile is being managed correctly (no contamination with subgrade).
   - Erosion and sediment controls (if required) are in place and functioning.
2. Address any discrepancies identified during inspection before proceeding.
3. Submit any applicable interim inspection requests to the County as required by RFP §3.3.2.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; ASSUMPTION: erosion control per Alberta civil practice.

**Output:** Inspection notes in site log; County inspection requests submitted as applicable.

---

### Step 6 — Site Cleanup and Completion Preparation

**Action:**
1. Upon completion of stripping across all driving surface areas:
   - Remove any equipment and temporary works from the stripped area.
   - **[C-003]** Dress and stabilize the topsoil stockpile to prevent erosion and wind dispersion. The stabilization method shall be selected based on the anticipated duration of stockpile storage and site conditions. Decision criteria for method selection:
     - **Short-term storage (< 30 days before reuse):** Compaction of stockpile surface and perimeter silt fence may be sufficient.
     - **Medium-term storage (1-6 months):** Temporary cover (geotextile/poly sheeting) or surface seeding with fast-growing annual grass.
     - **Long-term storage (> 6 months):** Seeding with appropriate grass mix for erosion control; consider windbreak placement if stockpile is exposed to prevailing winds.
     - Specific method TBD — to be confirmed based on project schedule and anticipated reuse timing for DEL-018-02. Wind dispersion and erosion of unprotected stockpiles can degrade topsoil quality and reduce salvage value.
   - Remove debris, rocks, and organic matter from the stripped subgrade surface.
2. Implement any required interim erosion and sediment controls on the exposed subgrade if stripping is complete but grading (DEL-018-02) has not yet commenced.
3. Notify the site superintendent that stripping is complete and the area is ready for as-built survey and final verification.

**Source:** ASSUMPTION: good construction practice; specific erosion control requirements TBD.

**Output:** Site cleaned; stockpile protected; subgrade ready for survey.

---

### Step 7 — Final Inspection and County Coordination

**Action:**
1. Submit formal inspection request to County project representative for final stripping inspection.
2. Conduct joint inspection with County representative:
   - Walk all stripped areas.
   - Confirm stripping extent matches IFC drawing limits.
   - Confirm stockpile is in place and in suitable condition for future reuse.
3. Obtain County sign-off on inspection (or note any deficiencies requiring remediation).
4. Provide County representative with a copy of the completed inspection report.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (County representative present for inspections; copies of inspection reports to County).

**Output:** Joint inspection completed; signed inspection report; County copy provided.

---

### Step 8 — Close-Out and Lessons Learned

**[E-003]**

**Action:**
1. After County sign-off (Step 7), compile a brief lessons-learned summary capturing:
   - Actual topsoil volume stripped vs. planned/estimated volume.
   - Actual stripping duration vs. planned duration.
   - Equipment performance observations (suitability of selected equipment for site conditions).
   - Any unexpected conditions encountered (subsurface anomalies, weather delays, utility near-misses).
   - Stockpile management observations (location suitability, protection effectiveness).
   - Any regulatory or coordination issues encountered with County or Alberta authorities.
2. Distribute the lessons-learned summary to the site superintendent and project manager for use in planning subsequent PKG-018 deliverables (DEL-018-02 through DEL-018-06).
3. File the lessons-learned summary as a deliverable close-out record.

**Source:** ASSUMPTION: good project management practice; feedback mechanism benefits the six downstream deliverables in PKG-018 that depend on this foundational activity.

**Output:** Lessons-learned summary document; distributed to project team.
