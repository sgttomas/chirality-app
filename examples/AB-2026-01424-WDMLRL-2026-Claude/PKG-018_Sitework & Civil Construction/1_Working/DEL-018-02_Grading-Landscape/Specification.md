# Specification — DEL-018-02: Site Grading & Landscaping

---

## Scope

### In Scope
This specification governs the construction execution of site grading and landscaping for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project, deliverable DEL-018-02. The work includes:

- Grading the proposed lot expansion area to designed elevations per the IFC civil grading plan (DEL-005-02).
- Establishing positive drainage across the site to prevent water pooling and manage future storm events.
- Ensuring graded drainage does not alter drainage conditions on neighboring properties.
- Compaction of all graded surfaces to acceptable standards.
- Execution of landscaping work per the approved landscape plans (DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03).

Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0076); RFP §3.4 (SOW-0020, SOW-0021); `_CONTEXT.md`; Decomposition PKG-018.

### Out of Scope
The following are explicitly excluded from this deliverable:

- Brushing and clearing of the project area (County forces — RFP §3.3.1).
- Bulk cut and fill earthworks (County forces — RFP §3.3.1).
- Supply of aggregate (County — RFP §3.3.1; Appendix B note).
- Civil grading design (PKG-005 — Decomposition).
- Landscape architectural design (PKG-007 — Decomposition).
- Topsoil stripping (DEL-018-01 — prerequisite deliverable).
- Gravel driving surface construction (DEL-018-03).
- Cement and gravel pad construction (DEL-018-04).
- Utility tie-ins (DEL-018-06).

## Requirements

### REQ-01: Grade and Landscape the Proposed Lot
The General Contractor shall grade and landscape the proposed lot in accordance with the IFC civil and landscape drawings.

- **Source**: RFP §3.3.2; Decomposition SOW-0076.
- **Verification**: Visual inspection and survey confirmation against IFC plan elevations.

### REQ-02: Positive Site Drainage
The grading execution shall achieve positive site drainage as designed in the civil grading plan. Standing water shall not be permitted on graded surfaces upon completion.

- **Source**: RFP §3.4; Decomposition SOW-0020.
- **Verification**: Visual inspection during and after precipitation event, or drainage confirmation per civil engineer's acceptance criteria (TBD pending civil design).

### REQ-03: No Alteration of Neighboring Property Drainage
Grading work shall not alter the drainage conditions of neighboring properties. Any modification to existing drainage patterns must remain within the site boundary.

- **Source**: RFP §3.4; Decomposition SOW-0021.
- **Verification**: Civil engineer review and confirmation of pre- vs. post-grading drainage patterns. ASSUMPTION: Pre-grading and post-grading drainage assessment by civil engineer is the recommended verification method. Specific method TBD — to be defined in civil specification DEL-005-07. [Lensing: A-003]

### REQ-04: Conformance with IFC Civil Grading Plan
All grading shall conform to elevations, grades, and drainage features shown on the IFC civil grading plan (DEL-005-02), issued by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta. Fine grading tolerances (elevation tolerance from design grade) shall be as specified in the civil specification (DEL-005-07). [Lensing: A-002]

- **Source**: RFP §3.3.2.
- **Fine Grading Tolerances**: TBD — to be defined in civil specification DEL-005-07. ASSUMPTION: tolerances are necessary for acceptance of fine grading work; civil specification must define them as a prerequisite. [Lensing: A-002]
- **Verification**: Survey confirmation of as-built elevations against design elevations (as-built survey — DEL-008-04); field-level grade checks during execution by GC.

### REQ-05: Conformance with IFC Landscape Plans
All landscaping work shall conform to the IFC landscape plans (DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03), issued by the Landscape Architect.

- **Source**: RFP §3.3.2; Decomposition SOW-0017 (design) and SOW-0076 (construction execution).
- **Verification**: Landscape conformance site review. **Authority for definitive conformance ruling is TBD** — confirm with County whether Landscape Architect, Owner representative, or both must sign off on landscape conformance. [Lensing: D-001]
- **Completion Criteria**: TBD — landscape completion criteria (e.g., planting survival period, establishment criteria, seasonal deferral provisions) to be defined in landscape specification DEL-007-04. Given Alberta climate and December 2026 deadline, establishment criteria and seasonal deferral provisions are needed. ASSUMPTION: landscape specification should define completion criteria. [Lensing: D-002]

### REQ-06: Compaction of Graded Surfaces
Graded surfaces shall be compacted to the acceptance criteria defined in the civil specification (DEL-005-07). Compaction testing shall not commence until acceptance criteria (e.g., minimum Proctor density percentage, test method, test frequency) have been issued by the civil engineer.

- **Source**: ASSUMPTION — inferred from deliverable type, context, and downstream dependencies (graded surfaces serve as base for DEL-018-03 driving surfaces and DEL-018-04 pads). Specific compaction standard TBD pending civil specification (DEL-005-07).
- **Prerequisite**: Civil specification DEL-005-07 must issue compaction acceptance criteria before testing commences. [Lensing: A-001, C-002]
- **Verification**: Compaction testing by qualified testing laboratory per DEL-005-07 acceptance criteria (TBD — method and acceptance criteria to be specified in civil design). Quantified pass/fail threshold required before verification can be performed. [Lensing: C-002]

### REQ-07: Prerequisite Completion Prior to Commencement
Grading work shall not commence until DEL-018-01 (Topsoil Stripping) is confirmed complete, the civil grading plan is issued for construction, and site survey benchmarks are established.

- **Source**: `_DEPENDENCIES.md` (declared upstream dependencies).
- **Verification**: Superintendent confirmation of prerequisite completion before mobilization.

### REQ-09: Completion Deadline
All grading and landscaping work shall be complete in sufficient time to allow downstream deliverables (DEL-018-03, 04, 05, 06) to be completed on or before December 31, 2026.

- **Source**: RFP §3.1.
