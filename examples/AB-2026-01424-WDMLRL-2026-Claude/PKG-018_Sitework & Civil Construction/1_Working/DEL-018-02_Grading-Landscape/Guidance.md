# Guidance — DEL-018-02: Site Grading & Landscaping

---

## Purpose

DEL-018-02 exists to establish the finished site grades and landscape conditions necessary for all subsequent site construction and operational use of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition.

The deliverable serves two primary functions:

1. **Functional foundation**: Grading creates the elevated and drained surface upon which the gravel driving surface (DEL-018-03), cement and gravel pads (DEL-018-04), and associated site infrastructure (DEL-018-05, 06) will be constructed. Without correct grades, downstream surface construction cannot meet design intent.

2. **Drainage management**: The site is an active rural landfill in Central Alberta. Positive drainage is critical to prevent water pooling on driving surfaces and pads used by heavy equipment (motor scrapers, cranes, service vehicles), and to protect neighboring properties from altered drainage patterns.

Source: `_CONTEXT.md`; RFP §3.3.2, §3.4; Decomposition SOW-0020, SOW-0021, `_DEPENDENCIES.md`.

## Principles

### P-01: Follow the Design — Do Not Grade by Judgment
Grading elevations and drainage slopes must come from the IFC civil grading plan (DEL-005-02), not from field judgment. The civil grading plan is a P.Eng.-stamped document (RFP §3.3.2). Any field deviation must be authorized by the civil engineer of record before implementation.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; Decomposition SOW-0015.

### P-02: Positive Drainage is a Hard Requirement
The RFP explicitly requires that grading account for positive site drainage and future storm events (RFP §3.4). This is not a best-practice recommendation — it is a contractual design requirement flowing through to construction execution. The grading contractor must confirm drainage intent is achieved before accepting the graded surface.

Source: RFP §3.4; Decomposition SOW-0020.

### P-03: Protect Neighboring Properties
Grading must not redirect runoff onto neighboring properties (RFP §3.4; SOW-0021). At an active landfill site, this has both contractual and environmental significance. The civil engineer's drainage plan is the primary tool for ensuring compliance; the construction team's responsibility is to implement it faithfully.

Source: RFP §3.4; Decomposition SOW-0021.

### P-04: Sequence Discipline is Critical Path Management
DEL-018-02 is on the project critical path (second order). It cannot start until DEL-018-01 (Topsoil Stripping) is complete. It must finish before DEL-018-03 (Gravel Driving Surface), DEL-018-04 (Pads), DEL-018-05 (Wash Bay Drainage), and DEL-018-06 (Utility Tie-Ins) can commence. Delays in grading directly cascade to surface construction and the project's December 31, 2026 deadline.

Source: `_DEPENDENCIES.md`; RFP §3.1.

### P-05: County Representative Involvement at Inspections
The County project representative must be present at all inspections (RFP §3.3.2, SOW-0084). The General Contractor is responsible for scheduling inspections with sufficient lead time to ensure County availability. This is a contractual obligation, not a courtesy.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; Decomposition SOW-0084, SOW-0085.

## Considerations

### C-01: Civil and Landscape Design Must Be Available Before Construction Begins
Grading execution is entirely dependent on the IFC civil grading plan (DEL-005-02) and the landscape plans (DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03). These are produced by PKG-005 (Civil Design) and PKG-007 (Landscape Architectural Design) respectively. Construction should not commence without confirmed IFC-stamped drawings in hand. Track design package readiness as a prerequisite milestone.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; Decomposition PKG-005, PKG-007.

### C-02: County Supplies Aggregate — Coordinate Early
The County supplies the aggregate for site work (RFP §3.3.1; Appendix B note: "Landfill to supply gravel"). This means the General Contractor does not procure aggregate but must coordinate aggregate delivery timing with the County project manager. A mismatch between grading completion and aggregate availability will delay surface construction.

Source: RFP §3.3.1; Appendix B (Shop conceptual drawing note).

### C-03: Compaction Standards Drive Downstream Quality
Graded surfaces serve as the subgrade for the gravel driving surface and pads. Insufficient compaction will result in settling and surface failures under heavy equipment loading (motor scraper class vehicles — RFP §3.3.2). Compaction acceptance criteria should be confirmed with the civil engineer before testing begins. Specific Proctor density requirements are TBD pending civil specification (DEL-005-07).

Source: RFP §3.3.2; `MEMORY.md`; ASSUMPTION.

### C-04: The Expansion Area Context
The site maps (Appendix C) show the construction expansion area immediately adjacent to the existing Old North Shop building. Grading work occurs in an active facility context. The General Contractor should coordinate with the County facility manager regarding traffic management and operational continuity during grading activities. Specific coordination requirements are TBD.

Source: Appendix C — Site Maps.

### C-05: The Landscape Scope Is Design-Dependent
The extent and species of landscaping are defined by the landscape architect's design (PKG-007). Until DEL-007-02 (Landscape Site Plans) and DEL-007-03 (Planting & Restoration Plans) are issued, the landscape execution scope cannot be fully quantified. The construction team should anticipate that planting/restoration will occur at the perimeter of the graded and disturbed area, but specific areas and materials are TBD.

Source: Decomposition PKG-007, DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03.

### C-06: Alberta Climate and Frost Conditions
ASSUMPTION: Alberta's Central region experiences significant frost penetration. Grading work performed late in the season (fall 2026) may require attention to frost depth, soil trafficability, and seeding/planting windows. The civil and landscape specifications (DEL-005-07, DEL-007-04) should address seasonal limitations. This consideration should be raised with the civil engineer and landscape architect during design review if the construction schedule extends into late fall.

Source: ASSUMPTION based on project location (RFP §1.0, Appendix C).

### C-07: Interim Erosion and Sediment Control [Lensing: X-001]
After grading is complete and before surface construction (DEL-018-03 gravel driving surface, DEL-018-04 pads) begins, the graded surface will be exposed. At an active landfill site in Alberta, this interim period creates risk of erosion and sediment-laden runoff. Interim erosion and sediment control measures should be planned for this exposure window.

This is distinct from the late-season landscaping erosion concern addressed in Trade-off T-03, which addresses plant establishment. The concern here is the period between grading completion and the commencement of surface construction, which may be days to weeks depending on scheduling and weather.

ASSUMPTION: Interim erosion control is an expected construction management obligation at a landfill site. Specific measures (e.g., silt fencing, temporary seeding, surface roughening) are TBD — confirm requirements with the civil engineer and County facility manager. Environmental regulatory requirements may also apply (see Specification §Standards, EPEA note).

Source: ASSUMPTION; Datasheet §Conditions (active landfill context); Guidance §Trade-offs T-03 (partial mention); Specification §Standards (EPEA row).

### C-08: Guarantee Period Obligations [Lensing: X-005]
The Datasheet identifies a 2-year guarantee period post-Construction Completion Certificate (CCC) (RFP §2.10). During this period, the General Contractor may have obligations related to deficiency inspection, maintenance, or correction for both grading and landscaping work.

For grading: Potential issues include differential settlement of compacted areas, drainage performance changes due to settlement, and surface deterioration under operational traffic.

For landscaping: Plant survival and establishment are particularly relevant. If landscaping is installed late in the season (see T-03), a guarantee period creates ongoing monitoring obligations for plant survival through at least one growing season.

The specific inspection, notification, and correction process during the guarantee period is TBD. ASSUMPTION: CCDC 14-2013 contains provisions governing guarantee period obligations — these should be referenced and any project-specific variations confirmed with the County.

Source: Datasheet §Conditions (Guarantee Period: 2 years per RFP §2.10); ASSUMPTION re CCDC 14-2013 provisions.

### C-09: Scope Boundary Terminology [Lensing: F-002]
Three different terms are used across the production documents for what appears to be the same physical work area:
- "the proposed lot" (RFP §3.3.2 / SOW-0076; used in Specification §Scope)
- "proposed lot expansion area" (Datasheet §Attributes; Procedure references)
- "expansion area" (Procedure shorthand)

These terms likely refer to the same area but they are not explicitly equated in any source. To avoid scope interpretation disputes, the **canonical term** should be confirmed by the project team. ASSUMPTION: "proposed lot" per SOW-0076 is the contractual scope term; "expansion area" and "proposed lot expansion area" are shorthand references to the same physical area. The Appendix C site maps should be the definitive spatial reference.

Source: Specification §Scope; Datasheet §Attributes; Procedure §Steps; RFP §3.3.2; Appendix C.

## Trade-offs

### T-01: Grade Timing vs. Downstream Readiness
Performing grading early enables downstream work but requires the civil design to be complete first. Rushing to field-grade without IFC drawings risks rework. The preferred trade-off is to wait for IFC drawings rather than grade by assumption.

### T-02: Final Grade vs. Utility Installation Coordination
Utility trenching for DEL-018-06 (Utility Tie-Ins) may disturb graded surfaces. ASSUMPTION: Grading should be sequenced to allow for utility installation, with final grading and compaction performed after utility trenches are backfilled. Coordination with DEL-018-06 is required at the construction scheduling stage. Confirm sequence with the civil engineer.

Source: ASSUMPTION; `_DEPENDENCIES.md` (DEL-018-06 listed as downstream but utility trenching may affect grade).

### T-03: Landscaping Timing vs. Growing Season
If grading is completed in late 2026, seeding or planting may not establish before winter. ASSUMPTION: The landscape specification may allow for delayed planting with erosion control measures in the interim. The General Contractor should clarify acceptable interim measures with the landscape architect.

Source: ASSUMPTION based on project completion deadline (RFP §3.1) and Alberta climate.
