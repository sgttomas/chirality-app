# Procedure — DEL-018-02: Site Grading & Landscaping

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps for executing and closing out the site grading and landscaping work at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition expansion area. It covers: confirming prerequisites, mobilizing and executing grading, achieving drainage compliance, compaction testing, executing landscaping, coordinating inspections, and producing required records.

Source: RFP §3.3.2, §3.4; `_CONTEXT.md`; `_DEPENDENCIES.md`; Decomposition SOW-0076, SOW-0020, SOW-0021.

## Steps

### Phase A — Mobilization and Pre-Grading Setup

**A-1: Pre-Construction Meeting**
Conduct a pre-construction meeting with the County project manager, civil engineer, and relevant subcontractors to confirm:
- Grading design intent, elevations, and drainage patterns.
- Compaction standards and testing requirements (DEL-005-07 acceptance criteria must be issued — see REQ-06).
- Inspection schedule, County representative availability, and hold/witness point classification (see REQ-08). [Lensing: X-004]
- Aggregate supply timing and coordination with County.
- Construction phasing relative to DEL-018-06 utility trench installation.
- Equipment capability requirements and constraints for the active landfill site (e.g., equipment weight limits given subgrade conditions, equipment access restrictions, traffic management). [Lensing: F-003]
- Interim erosion and sediment control plan for the period between grading completion and surface construction commencement. [Lensing: X-001, X-002]
- Stormwater management measures during active grading operations (temporary drainage control to prevent sediment-laden runoff at the active landfill site). [Lensing: X-002]
- Applicability of environmental regulatory requirements (EPEA, landfill operating approval conditions) to grading operations. [Lensing: C-003]

Source: RFP §3.1; ASSUMPTION — standard pre-construction practice.

**A-2: Establish Site Layout and Control**
Working from the established survey benchmarks (P-3), set out grade stakes and drainage control points as directed by the IFC civil grading plan (DEL-005-02).

Source: RFP §3.3.2; ASSUMPTION — standard civil construction practice.

**A-3: Confirm Topsoil Strip Completion**
Verify the grading area is clear of topsoil (DEL-018-01 complete). Document the pre-grading surface condition.

Source: `_DEPENDENCIES.md`.

### Phase B — Grading Execution

**B-1: Rough Grading**
Grade the proposed lot to approximate design elevations using appropriate equipment. Work shall conform to the IFC civil grading plan. The County is responsible for bulk cut and fill earthworks; the General Contractor shall confirm the extent of County-performed bulk earthwork before commencing fine grading.

**Handoff from County bulk earthwork:** Before commencing fine grading, obtain documented sign-off from the County confirming that bulk earthwork is complete to the specified tolerances. The sign-off method shall be confirmed at the pre-construction meeting (A-1). ASSUMPTION: A written confirmation (e.g., survey check confirming bulk earthwork meets rough grade tolerances, or written sign-off from County PM) is required to establish a clear scope boundary and prevent disputes over responsibility for under- or over-excavation. [Lensing: A-004]

Source: RFP §3.3.1 (County bulk cut/fill scope boundary); RFP §3.3.2 (Proponent grading execution); Decomposition SOW-0076.

**B-2: Fine Grading**
Fine-grade surfaces to achieve design elevations and drainage slopes as shown on the IFC civil grading plan. Tolerances are TBD — confirm with civil engineer (to be specified in DEL-005-07).

Source: RFP §3.3.2; ASSUMPTION — tolerances TBD.

**B-3: Drainage Verification — Interim**
Before compaction, verify drainage slopes visually and with a level/grade rod against the IFC plan. Confirm positive drainage is achieved and no low points or ponding areas exist. Correct any deficiencies before proceeding.

Source: RFP §3.4; Decomposition SOW-0020.

**B-3a: Temporary Stormwater and Sediment Control** [Lensing: X-002]
During active grading operations, implement temporary stormwater management and sediment control measures to prevent sediment-laden runoff from exposed graded surfaces. This is particularly important given the active landfill site context, where uncontrolled runoff may have environmental compliance implications (see Specification §Standards, EPEA note). Specific measures are TBD — confirm requirements at the pre-construction meeting (A-1). ASSUMPTION: Temporary measures (e.g., silt fencing at low points, temporary drainage swales, stabilized construction entrances) are standard construction management obligations.

Source: ASSUMPTION; Datasheet §Conditions (active landfill context); Specification §Standards (EPEA row).

**B-4: Compaction**
Compact graded surfaces in lifts per the civil specification (DEL-005-07) or civil engineer's direction. Coordinate compaction testing schedule with the testing laboratory.

Source: ASSUMPTION — compaction required per `MEMORY.md` and downstream use as subgrade for DEL-018-03, 04.

**B-5: Compaction Testing**
Engage a qualified testing laboratory to perform compaction testing. Testing frequency and acceptance criteria are TBD pending civil specification (DEL-005-07). Retain all test reports.

Source: ASSUMPTION; `MEMORY.md`.

**B-6: Grading Deficiency Correction**
If compaction tests fail or grade survey reveals non-conformance, re-grade and re-compact as required. Repeat testing until acceptance criteria are met.

Source: RFP §2.14; ASSUMPTION.

### Phase C — Coordination with Utility Installation

**C-1: Utility Trench Coordination**
Coordinate with DEL-018-06 (Utility Tie-Ins) contractor to confirm sequencing. If utility trenching occurs after initial grading, graded surfaces in trench areas shall be restored to design grade and recompacted after backfill.

Source: ASSUMPTION — inferred from sequencing logic; `_DEPENDENCIES.md`.

**C-2: Final Grading After Utilities**
Perform final fine grading and compaction in utility trench areas after backfill is complete and any settlement has been managed.

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard construction practice.

### Phase D — Landscaping Execution

**D-1: Confirm Landscape Plan Availability**
Confirm that IFC Landscape Plans (DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03) and Landscape Specification (DEL-007-04) are issued and in hand before commencing landscaping work.

Source: Decomposition DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03, DEL-007-04.

**D-2: Prepare Landscape Areas**
Prepare designated landscape areas per the IFC landscape plans. This may include topsoil placement, seedbed preparation, and other grading adjustments specific to landscape zones. Scope TBD pending landscape design.

Source: Decomposition DEL-007-02, DEL-007-03; ASSUMPTION — scope TBD.

**D-3: Execute Planting and Restoration**
Install planting, seeding, or restoration materials per the IFC landscape plans and landscape specification. Species, quantities, and methods are TBD pending landscape design.

Source: Decomposition DEL-007-03; ASSUMPTION — scope TBD.

**D-4: Seasonal Considerations**
If landscape execution occurs in late fall (approaching December 31, 2026 deadline), confirm acceptable interim erosion control and establishment measures with the landscape architect. Planting/seeding windows and acceptable substitutions are TBD.

Source: ASSUMPTION — Alberta climate and project deadline (RFP §3.1).

### Phase E — Inspection and Acceptance

**E-1: Request Inspection**
Submit inspection requests to the County project representative in advance. Confirm County representative availability for all inspections. All inspections require County representative presence.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; Decomposition SOW-0084.

**E-2: Grading Inspection**
Conduct grading inspection with County representative. Verify elevations, drainage, and surface condition against IFC plan. Document findings.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; ASSUMPTION — standard inspection practice.

**E-3: Landscape Inspection**
Conduct landscape inspection with County representative (and landscape architect if required). Verify installation against IFC landscape plans. Document findings.

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard inspection practice.

**E-4: Deficiency Correction and Re-Inspection**
Correct any deficiencies identified during inspection. Re-inspect as required. Provide County representative with inspection reports for all inspections.

Source: RFP §2.14; SOW-0085.

**E-5: As-Built Survey**
Coordinate with surveyor (DEL-008-04) to confirm final graded elevations match design. As-built survey is a separate deliverable but is the primary verification tool for grading conformance.

Source: Decomposition DEL-008-04.
