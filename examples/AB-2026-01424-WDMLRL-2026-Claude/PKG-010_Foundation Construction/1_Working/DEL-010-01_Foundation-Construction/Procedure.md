## Purpose

This procedure describes the operational steps required to execute and complete the Foundation Construction deliverable (DEL-010-01) for the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill.

This procedure covers:
- Pre-construction gates and precondition confirmation
- Site preparation activities within the building footprint
- Excavation, subgrade preparation, and foundation system construction
- Inspection coordination
- Documentation and close-out of the foundation work

This procedure is read in conjunction with the Foundation Design Package (DEL-002-11), Foundation Plan (DEL-002-02), and geotechnical report (PKG-008). Steps marked **TBD** require confirmation from those documents, which are not yet available at this stage.

Source: RFP §3.3, §3.4, §4.8.2; _CONTEXT.md; Decomposition §7 PKG-010; Specification.md

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Pre-Construction Confirmation

**Step 1: Confirm Geotechnical Report Receipt and Review**

1.1. Confirm that the complete geotechnical report from PKG-008 has been received and reviewed by the structural engineer of record.

1.2. Confirm that the geotechnical report has been incorporated into the Foundation Design Package (DEL-002-11).

1.3. If geotechnical findings reveal conditions materially different from the normal ground condition assumed in the Proposal, notify the Owner (Camrose County) in writing and initiate variable-price negotiation per RFP §4.8.2.

**Gate:** Do not proceed to Step 2 until PRE-01 and PRE-02 are confirmed.

Source: RFP §4.8.2; REQ-010-01-001

---

**Step 2: Confirm Contract Price for Foundation Scope**

2.1. Review the geotechnical report findings with the Owner's project manager.

2.2. Negotiate and execute the variable-price foundation cost agreement as a contract supplement or schedule under CCDC 14–2013.

2.3. Confirm the agreed scope includes all foundation work covered by SOW-0023.

**Gate:** Executed cost agreement required before construction mobilization.

Source: RFP §4.8.2; REQ-010-01-008

---

**Step 3: Review IFC Foundation Drawings and Embedded Items**

3.1. Review the issued-for-construction Foundation Plan (DEL-002-02) and Foundation Design Package (DEL-002-11) in detail.

3.2. Identify all embedded items required at the foundation stage:
- Anchor bolt patterns and templates
- Conduit sleeves or blockouts for electrical routing (ASSUMPTION — confirm with PKG-015 drawings)
- Plumbing sleeves (ASSUMPTION — confirm with PKG-006/PKG-014 drawings)
- Service pit structural elements (canonical term; alias: "service trench" per App B — confirm scope boundary per Conflict Table CF-010-01 in Guidance.md)
- Any other embedments called out on structural drawings

3.3. Confirm all embedded items are procured and on-site before concrete placement.

**Note:** Missing embedded items discovered after concrete is placed are extremely difficult and costly to remediate. This step is critical.

Source: REQ-010-01-002; Guidance.md C-2; ASSUMPTION

---

**Step 4: Confirm Permit Status**

4.1. Confirm with PKG-009 that the building permit and applicable Safety Code permits are in place and valid.

4.2. Confirm the County has paid the permit fees per RFP §3.3.1.

4.3. Post permit documents on site as required by Alberta Safety Codes.

**Gate:** Do not commence construction without valid permits.

Source: RFP §3.3.1, §3.3.2; REQ-010-01-003

---

### Phase 2 — Site Preparation

**Step 5: Document Site Handoff Condition**

5.1. Before commencing any Proponent site work, conduct a site walk and document the existing condition:
- Photographs from all four sides of the building footprint
- Note any conditions that differ from assumptions (bulk cut/fill grade, clearing status, aggregate placement)
- Record in writing and communicate to County project manager

5.2. Confirm with County project manager that bulk cut and fill and clearing are complete per RFP §3.3.1.

Source: Guidance.md C-3; ASSUMPTION (standard construction documentation practice)

---

**Step 6: Topsoil Stripping (if not already performed by County)**

6.1. Confirm with County project manager whether topsoil stripping of the building footprint has already been performed.

6.2. If not yet performed: strip topsoil to depths specified in the geotechnical report and/or as directed by the Foundation Design Package.

6.3. Stockpile or dispose of stripped topsoil per the following direction (TBD — to be confirmed before stripping commences):
   - Confirm with the County project manager (Owner) whether topsoil is to be stockpiled on-site for reuse or disposed off-site.
   - If stockpiled on-site: confirm stockpile location with the civil design (PKG-005) to ensure it does not interfere with construction access, drainage, or future site work.
   - If disposed off-site: confirm disposal location and any County requirements for topsoil handling.
   - The authority for topsoil disposal/stockpile direction is the Owner (Camrose County) with input from PKG-005 civil design. (Source: Lensing item A-004)

6.4. Document: pre-stripping photos, post-stripping photos, estimated volumes stripped.

Source: RFP §3.3.2 ("Strip topsoil from all driving surfaces (if not already performed by Owner)"); REQ-010-01-007

---

**Step 7: Establish Survey Control**

7.1. Confirm survey control points from the construction survey (PKG-008 scope) are established and accessible.

7.2. Set out building corners and reference lines per the Foundation Plan (DEL-002-02).

7.3. Verify layout against the conceptual drawing dimensions (New North Shop: 130' wide × 100' deep per App B) and IFC drawings.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; R-04 (App B); ASSUMPTION

---

### Phase 3 — Excavation

**Step 8: Excavation to Design Depths**

8.1. Excavate to the depths and dimensions specified in the Foundation Design Package (DEL-002-11) and Foundation Plan (DEL-002-02).

8.2. Excavate at lines and grades shown on the IFC drawings.

8.3. Do not over-excavate beyond the dimensions shown without authorization from the structural engineer of record.

**Note:** Excavation depths are TBD pending foundation design; this step will be updated when DEL-002-11 is issued.

Source: REQ-010-01-002; ASSUMPTION

---

**Step 9: Subgrade Inspection and Preparation**

9.1. Upon reaching design excavation depth, notify the County project manager and arrange subgrade inspection.

9.2. Have the geotechnical engineer of record (or their representative) inspect the subgrade to confirm it matches the geotechnical report assumptions.

9.3. If unexpected conditions are encountered (soft spots, wet zones, deleterious material), stop work and notify the Owner in writing immediately.

9.4. Prepare the subgrade per the geotechnical engineer's recommendations:
- Compaction to specified density (TBD — per geotechnical report)
- Sub-base placement if required (TBD)
- Proof-roll if directed (TBD)

9.5. Obtain written confirmation from geotechnical engineer that subgrade is acceptable before proceeding to formwork.

Source: REQ-010-01-002; REQ-010-01-003; Guidance.md C-1; ASSUMPTION

---

**Step 10: Subgrade Compaction Testing**

10.1. Conduct compaction testing per the geotechnical report requirements.

10.2. Document test results; provide copies to County project manager.

10.3. Do not proceed to formwork until compaction test results meet specified requirements (per REQ-010-01-013 — minimum density TBD per geotechnical report).

Source: REQ-010-01-006; REQ-010-01-010; REQ-010-01-013; ASSUMPTION

---

### Phase 4 — Foundation Construction

**Step 11: Formwork and Embedded Items Installation**

11.1. Install formwork per the Foundation Plan and Foundation Design Package.

11.2. Install all embedded items identified in Step 3 (anchor bolts, sleeves, blockouts).

11.3. Have the General Contractor's quality representative inspect formwork and embedded item placement before calling for reinforcement inspection.

Source: REQ-010-01-002; Step 3

---

**Step 12: Reinforcement Installation**

12.1. Install reinforcement (rebar, mesh, or other) per the Foundation Design Package.

12.2. Confirm cover requirements per structural drawings and applicable code.

**Note:** Reinforcement type, size, and spacing are TBD pending DEL-002-11.

Source: REQ-010-01-002; REQ-010-01-003; ASSUMPTION

---

**Step 13: Pre-Pour Inspection**

13.1. Notify the County project manager and arrange the pre-pour inspection.

13.2. Submit inspection request per RFP §3.3.2 requirements.

13.3. Ensure County project representative is present for the inspection.

13.4. Ensure Safety Code Inspector (if required) is present and has cleared the pour.

13.5. Document: pre-pour inspection report, inspector signatures, County representative confirmation.

**Gate:** Do not proceed to concrete placement until inspection is cleared and documented.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; REQ-010-01-006

---

**Step 14: Concrete Placement**

14.1. Place concrete per the mix design and placement plan specified in the Foundation Design Package.

14.2. Record the following for each pour:
- Date and time of pour
- Concrete supplier and truck number(s)
- Mix design designation and batch ticket reference
- Volume placed
- Weather conditions (temperature, precipitation)
- Slump and air content test results
- Cylinder sample details (number, label, storage location)

14.3. Vibrate concrete per placement plan requirements to eliminate voids.

14.4. Do not place concrete in conditions that violate the mix design or code requirements (e.g., frozen subgrade, extreme cold without cold-weather protection measures).

**Note:** Alberta winter construction cold-weather concrete requirements apply (ASSUMPTION — standard Canadian practice in Alberta climate).

Source: REQ-010-01-002; REQ-010-01-003; REQ-010-01-010; REQ-010-01-014; ASSUMPTION

---

**Step 15: Curing**

15.1. Cure concrete per the curing plan in the Foundation Design Package, or per applicable code requirements if no specific plan is provided.

15.2. Protect concrete from freezing during the required curing period per Alberta climate conditions (ASSUMPTION).

15.3. Document curing method, duration, and any temperature monitoring records.

Source: REQ-010-01-002; REQ-010-01-003; ASSUMPTION

---

**Step 16: Concrete Testing — Cylinder Break Results**

16.1. Cure concrete test cylinders per CSA A23.2 or equivalent (ASSUMPTION).

16.2. Submit cylinders to a certified testing laboratory.

16.3. Record and distribute break results to the structural engineer of record and County project manager.

16.4. If break results are below specified strength, notify the structural engineer of record immediately and do not proceed with loading the affected foundation element until the engineer provides written acceptance or remediation direction.

Source: REQ-010-01-003; REQ-010-01-004; ASSUMPTION

---

**Step 17: Backfill and Compaction**

17.1. After foundation elements have achieved adequate strength (per structural engineer direction), proceed with backfill.

17.2. Place and compact backfill in lifts per the geotechnical engineer's recommendations.

17.3. Conduct compaction testing on backfill; document and distribute results.

17.4. Grade surface as required to match the civil grading plan for positive drainage.

Source: REQ-010-01-005; REQ-010-01-002; ASSUMPTION

---

### Phase 5 — Inspection, Documentation, and Close-out

**Step 18: Foundation Completion Inspection**

18.1. Notify the County project manager that foundation construction is complete.

18.2. Arrange and attend the foundation completion inspection with the County project representative and any required Safety Code inspectors.

18.3. Submit inspection request per RFP §3.3.2.

18.4. Provide copies of all inspection reports to the County project manager.

Source: RFP §3.3.2; REQ-010-01-006

---

**Step 19: As-Built Survey**

19.1. Conduct an as-built survey of the completed foundation to confirm dimensions and elevations match the IFC drawings.

19.2. Document any deviations from the design and submit to the structural engineer of record for acceptance.

19.3. Provide as-built survey data to the project record (ASSUMPTION — related to PKG-008 construction/as-built survey scope).

Source: REQ-010-01-010; ASSUMPTION

---

**Step 20: Handoff to PKG-011**

20.1. Confirm with the project manager and PKG-011 General Contractor that the foundation is ready to receive superstructure work. The foundation shall be deemed "ready" for handoff when ALL of the following acceptance criteria are met (Source: Lensing item D-003):

| Handoff Acceptance Criterion | Evidence Required |
|---|---|
| Concrete has achieved specified compressive strength (f'c — TBD per Specification REQ-010-01-004) | Cylinder break reports showing f'c achieved |
| As-built dimensions are within design tolerances (TBD per Foundation Design Package) | As-built survey confirming compliance |
| All required inspections are cleared (including County representative sign-off) | Signed inspection reports |
| All deviations have been resolved or accepted by the structural engineer of record | Written engineer direction per Guidance.md T-4 |
| All foundation construction documentation is compiled and transmitted | Document register (see 20.3 below) |

20.2. Provide PKG-011 team with:
- As-built drawings/survey
- Concrete test results (cylinder break reports)
- Compaction test reports
- Inspection reports and clearances
- List of any outstanding items or deviations (with engineer disposition for each)

20.3. Compile and issue a formal document register listing all artifacts produced for DEL-010-01, confirming completeness. TBD: the format of the document register (standalone index or checklist within handoff package) should be established before close-out. (Source: Lensing item X-003)

**Gate:** Do not authorize PKG-011 mobilization on the foundation until all acceptance criteria in 20.1 are confirmed.

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (Downstream: PKG-011); Specification.md REQ-010-01-010; Lensing items D-003, X-003; ASSUMPTION
