# DEL-017-03 | Washroom Renovation & Expansion — Procedure

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps required to produce the Washroom Renovation & Expansion (DEL-017-03) — from preconditions through construction execution to verification and records. It applies to the renovation of the existing washroom below the Old North Shop mezzanine, including the urinal expansion (SOW-0072, SOW-0074).

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Design and Documentation

**Step 1.1 — Existing Condition Survey**
- Document existing washroom footprint, fixture inventory, plumbing chase locations, drain positions, ventilation (or lack thereof), ceiling height, wall construction, and finishes.
- Take photographs of existing conditions.
- Record findings for use in design.
- Source: _STATUS.md milestone "Existing Condition Survey"; standard renovation practice.

**Step 1.2 — Code and Accessibility Review**
- Confirm applicable Alberta Building Code edition and Safety Code requirements for washroom renovation, urinal addition, and accessibility upgrades.
- Determine if expansion of footprint is required to meet code (fixture count, accessibility turning radius, etc.).
- Document findings for design team.
- Source: REQ-003, REQ-008; CODE-REVIEW-001, ACCESS-REVIEW-001.

**Step 1.3 — Design Development**
- Working with the Architect (PKG-001) and Plumbing Engineer (PKG-006), develop renovation layout incorporating:
  - Renovated washroom fixtures (toilet, sink, and other existing fixtures)
  - Urinal addition (SOW-0074) with plumbing rough-in
  - Accessibility-compliant layout (ASSUMPTION)
  - Exhaust ventilation (ASSUMPTION)
  - Updated finishes
- Produce IFC drawings: DEL-001-10 (Renovation Plans) and PKG-006 (Plumbing Design).
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; REQ-004.

**Step 1.4 — Permit Submission and Approval**
- Submit building permit application (DEL-009-02) incorporating washroom renovation scope.
- Submit Safety Code permit applications (DEL-009-03): plumbing permit, mechanical permit (ventilation), electrical permit, and building permit as applicable. [C-002]
- Obtain permit approvals before commencing construction.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0005, SOW-0006, SOW-0007.

---

### Phase 2 — Site Preparation and Demolition

**Step 2.0 — Hazardous Materials Assessment (TBD)**
- TBD: Determine whether a hazardous materials survey (asbestos, lead paint, other regulated substances) is required before demolition of existing washroom fixtures and finishes in the Old North Shop. ASSUMPTION: The Old North Shop is an existing building; demolition of existing finishes in older buildings typically requires a hazardous materials assessment under the Alberta Occupational Health and Safety Act and OHS Code. The GC OH&S program should confirm this requirement before any demolition work commences. If hazardous materials are identified, an abatement plan must be executed before demolition proceeds. [F-002]

**Step 2.1 — Site Safety Setup**
- Establish hoarding/barricades around washroom renovation area to protect adjacent spaces in the Old North Shop from dust, debris, and unauthorized access during construction.
- ASSUMPTION: Old North Shop may remain in partial use during construction; isolation of work zone is prudent. Confirm with County PM.

**Step 2.2 — Utility Isolation**
- Coordinate with plumbing contractor (PKG-014) to isolate water supply to the existing washroom prior to demolition.
- Confirm drain lines isolated or capped as required.
- ASSUMPTION: Isolation points to be confirmed from IFC plumbing drawings.

**Step 2.3 — Demolition of Existing Fixtures and Finishes**
- Remove existing washroom fixtures (toilet, sink, and other fixtures as required by design).
- Remove existing wall and floor finishes in areas affected by renovation.
- Remove or modify partitions as required to accommodate new layout and urinal addition.
- Dispose of demolition waste in accordance with applicable regulations.
- Source: DEL-001-09 (Demolition Plans); SOW-0072 (implied scope).

---

### Phase 3 — Rough-In and Structural/Framing

**Step 3.1 — Plumbing Rough-In**
- Install new plumbing rough-in for all fixtures per IFC plumbing drawings (PKG-006):
  - Water supply lines (cold water; hot water TBD — confirm whether hot water supply is required for washroom fixtures per Alberta Building Code and plumbing design, PKG-006. This affects plumbing rough-in scope and hot water distribution from the building's water heating system.) [A-004]
  - Drain and vent rough-in for renovated fixtures
  - Dedicated supply and waste rough-in for new urinal (SOW-0074)
  - Confirm floor drain is operational and connected (App B Plumbing shows drain at washroom)
- Coordinate with PKG-014 (Plumbing Contractor) for system connections to cistern distribution and septic.
- Source: SOW-0072, SOW-0074; App B (Plumbing); REQ-006.

**Step 3.2 — Mechanical Rough-In (Ventilation)**
- Install exhaust fan rough-in and ductwork per mechanical design (PKG-003/PKG-013).
- ASSUMPTION: Exhaust ventilation required per Alberta Building Code for washroom spaces.
- Coordinate with Mechanical Contractor (PKG-013).

**Step 3.3 — Electrical Rough-In**
- Install electrical rough-in for lighting, exhaust fan circuit, and receptacles as required by design.
- Coordinate with Electrical Contractor (PKG-015).
- Source: ASSUMPTION — standard renovation scope; specific requirements in electrical design (PKG-004).

**Step 3.4 — Framing / Partition Work**
- Install new or modified partitions as required by design to accommodate urinal addition and revised layout.
- Install wall backing/blocking for wall-mounted fixtures as required.
- ASSUMPTION: Extent of framing work TBD based on design layout.

---

### Phase 4 — Finishes and Fixture Installation

**Step 4.1 — Wall and Floor Finishes**
- Install wall and floor finishes per Finish Schedule (DEL-001-08).
- ASSUMPTION: Durable, cleanable finishes appropriate for industrial washroom (ceramic tile or equivalent).
- Allow finishes to cure as required before fixture installation.

**Step 4.2 — Fixture Installation**
- Install all washroom fixtures per IFC drawings and manufacturer instructions:
  - Toilet(s) — type and count per design
  - Sink(s) with faucet(s)
  - Urinal with flush valve (SOW-0074)
  - Accessories (soap dispenser, paper towel/hand dryer, mirror, toilet paper holder, grab bars if accessibility-required)
- Coordinate fixture procurement with project schedule to avoid delays.
- Source: SOW-0072, SOW-0074; REQ-002, REQ-006.

**Step 4.3 — Mechanical and Electrical Trim-Out**
- Install exhaust fan and connect to ductwork.
- Install lighting fixtures and cover plates.
- Install receptacles as required.
- Coordinate with PKG-013 and PKG-015.

---

### Phase 5 — Inspections

**Step 5.1 — Rough-In Inspections**
- Submit inspection requests for plumbing rough-in, mechanical rough-in, and electrical rough-in to the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) prior to closing walls. [C-001]
- Notify County project representative to be present at all inspections (RFP §3.3.2, SOW-0084).
- Receive and file inspection reports; provide copies to County (SOW-0085).
- Do not close walls until rough-in inspections are passed.

**Step 5.2 — Final Inspections**
- Submit inspection requests for plumbing final, mechanical final, electrical final, and building final as applicable. [C-001]
- Ensure County project representative is present.
- File all completed inspection reports and provide copies to County.
- Source: REQ-003, REQ-005; RFP §3.3.2.

---

### Phase 6 — Commissioning and Handover

**Step 6.1 — Functional Testing**
- Test all plumbing fixtures for operational function: flush cycles, water supply, drain flow, no leaks.
- Test exhaust ventilation for operation.
- Test lighting and electrical circuits.
- Confirm floor drain is clear and draining.
- Source: REQ-006; contributes to DEL-020-01 (Building Systems Commissioning).

**Step 6.2 — Punch-List and Deficiency Correction**
- Conduct internal punch-list inspection of completed washroom.
- Correct all deficiencies.
- Request County walk-through and final acceptance.
- Source: RFP §2.14; _STATUS.md milestone "Inspection & Punch-List".

**Step 6.3 — Warranty Initiation**
- Prepare warranty documentation for all materials and workmanship associated with this deliverable.
- Record warranty start date (aligned with CCC issuance date, DEL-020-02).
- Confirm warranty obligations are documented and communicated to County, including the 2-year warranty period and 10-day defect rectification requirement (RFP §2.10.2, §2.10.6).
- Include warranty documentation in project closeout file.
- Source: REQ-010; RFP §2.10. [X-003]

**Step 6.4 — Final Self-Inspection and CCC Support**
- Confirm washroom is in clean and tidy condition.
- Confirm completion as part of overall project CCC documentation (DEL-020-02).
- Source: RFP §2.14, SOW-0092, SOW-0093.
