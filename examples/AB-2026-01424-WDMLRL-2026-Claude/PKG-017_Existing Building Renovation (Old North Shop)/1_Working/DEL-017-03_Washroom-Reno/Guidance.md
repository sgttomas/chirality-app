# DEL-017-03 | Washroom Renovation & Expansion — Guidance

---

## Purpose

DEL-017-03 exists to bring the existing washroom in the Old North Shop up to a functional, code-compliant standard suitable for the expanded workforce that will use the facility upon completion of the overall Maintenance Shop Addition project. The RFP makes clear that the proposed facility requires a washroom with expanded capacity including a urinal (RFP §3.4; SOW-0074). The existing washroom below the Old North Shop mezzanine is the subject of this renovation (SOW-0072, App B).

This deliverable directly supports OBJ-001: delivering a code-compliant, fully functional maintenance shop that satisfies Camrose County's operational program and passes all applicable inspections.

The washroom renovation is a workforce amenity essential to the ongoing landfill operations. It is flagged as on the critical path (_DEPENDENCIES.md), as updated washroom facilities are required for facility operations.

---

## Principles

**1. Design-Build Authority**
The RFP explicitly states this is a design/build project where the County provides only a concept (RFP §3.4: "The County only has a concept in mind; therefore, it will be a design/build project"). The Design-Builder is responsible for all design decisions needed to produce a code-compliant washroom from the conceptual intent expressed in the RFP and App B drawings. This means the scope of the renovation, the specific layout after expansion, and the fixture schedule are all design-phase decisions.

**2. Code Compliance is Non-Negotiable**
All work must comply with Alberta Safety Codes and applicable building codes (RFP §3.3.2). The Design-Builder assumes full responsibility for identifying applicable code provisions during design. This includes plumbing codes, ventilation requirements, and accessibility provisions. The building permit and all required Safety Code inspections must be obtained and passed (DEL-009-02, DEL-009-03).

**3. Scope Boundary with DEL-017-04**
The RFP §3.4 states: "Expand washroom facilities to include urinal and locker change room, including laundry services." However, the Decomposition correctly separates this into two deliverables: DEL-017-03 (washroom renovation + urinal, SOW-0072 + SOW-0074) and DEL-017-04 (locker/change room with laundry services, SOW-0073). This boundary should be maintained in construction planning and scheduling. Physical adjacency between the two spaces may create coordination requirements in design and construction sequencing. [X-002]

The scope-split between DEL-017-03 and DEL-017-04 must be explicitly reflected in subcontract scope documents. Coordination obligations between the two deliverables during construction include: (a) shared wall or partition interface — design must define which deliverable is responsible for the common wall; (b) potential shared plumbing chase — routing and access to be coordinated in plumbing design (PKG-006); (c) construction sequencing — if the two spaces share structural or mechanical interfaces, the construction sequence must avoid rework. ASSUMPTION: The Project Manager should define a coordination protocol between DEL-017-03 and DEL-017-04 before subcontract scope documents are finalized. See Conflict Table C-01. [E-002]

**4. Existing Condition Documentation First**
The renovation scope is currently defined only at a conceptual level. A key early-phase activity is documenting existing conditions — existing fixture inventory, plumbing chase locations, drain locations, floor area, and ceiling height. The App B (Plumbing) drawing shows a floor drain and water tap at the washroom, confirming existing plumbing infrastructure is present. The existing condition survey (referenced in _STATUS.md milestones) should precede final design.

**5. Coordinate with Plumbing Trades Early**
The washroom plumbing is part of the overall plumbing system that connects to the 50,000L cistern distribution system (PKG-006/PKG-014). The Design-Builder must coordinate the washroom plumbing rough-in sequence with the overall plumbing schedule to avoid conflicts or rework.

## Considerations

### Phasing and Sequencing
- The Old North Shop is an existing operating building. Renovation sequencing should account for the need to maintain safe access and operations in adjacent areas during construction.
- ASSUMPTION: The Old North Shop renovation (PKG-017) will likely occur after or in parallel with the new building construction but before final commissioning. Exact sequencing TBD in the project schedule.
- The mezzanine renovation (DEL-017-02) and washroom renovation (DEL-017-03) are adjacent scopes and should be coordinated to minimize disruption.

### Urinal Addition
- SOW-0074 specifies adding a urinal. The urinal will require its own plumbing rough-in (supply and waste), wall blocking if wall-mounted, and potentially a separate ventilation connection. These are design-phase decisions.
- ASSUMPTION: The urinal addition may require expanding the washroom footprint into adjacent space. If this requires structural modifications to the existing building, coordination with structural design (PKG-002) will be required.

### Ventilation
- Washrooms in Alberta typically require mechanical exhaust ventilation per the Alberta Building Code. The existing washroom's ventilation status is unknown. ASSUMPTION: an exhaust fan will be required as part of the renovation. Coordination with PKG-003 (Mechanical Design) and PKG-013 (Mechanical Installation) is required.

### Accessibility
- ASSUMPTION: Renovations to existing washrooms in Alberta must meet applicable Alberta Building Code barrier-free accessibility provisions. The scope of accessibility upgrades will depend on the extent of renovation (trigger thresholds in the code) and will be determined in design. This may affect the required floor area and fixture arrangement. Note: "ADA" (Americans with Disabilities Act) does not apply in Alberta; the governing accessibility provisions are those of the Alberta Building Code. All documents use "Alberta Building Code accessibility provisions" as the canonical terminology. [B-003]

### Lifecycle Maintenance and Fixture Quality
- ASSUMPTION: Given the industrial maintenance facility context (landfill operations), fixture and finish selections should prioritize durability, ease of cleaning, and resistance to harsh use. Fixtures should be commercial-grade (e.g., vitreous china for water closets and urinals, vandal-resistant accessories). Finishes should be selected for moisture resistance, ease of cleaning, and resistance to impact damage. The design team should address lifecycle maintenance considerations in the Finish Schedule rationale (DEL-001-08). [A-005, C-003]
- No specific fixture quality or durability criteria are stated in the RFP; this is a design-build decision. However, the industrial context makes fixture quality a material factor for long-term value and should be explicitly addressed in design.

### Fire Separation
- TBD: Determine whether the washroom renovation triggers fire separation requirements under the Alberta Building Code (e.g., fire-rated wall or assembly between the washroom and adjacent spaces, or between the renovated area and the mezzanine above). The Old North Shop has a mezzanine above the washroom area. ASSUMPTION: The code review (CODE-REVIEW-001) should explicitly assess fire separation requirements as part of the building renovation scope. No fire separation requirement is currently stated in any production document. [F-001]

### Water Supply
- App B (Plumbing) shows a water tap near the washroom connected to the building's plumbing system, which in turn draws from the 50,000L cistern. The washroom fixtures will draw from this supply. Adequate water pressure and capacity from the cistern pump (minimum 100 LPM pump per RFP §3.4) should be confirmed in plumbing design to support washroom fixtures.

### Dual SOW Reference
- The dual SOW references (SOW-0072 for renovation, SOW-0074 for urinal expansion) appear to reflect two distinct but related scope items that together define this deliverable. They do not appear to indicate two separate phases; rather, they represent the renovation component and the expansion component of a single construction activity. ASSUMPTION: they will be executed as a single, coordinated scope of work under one construction phase.
