## Purpose

DEL-017-02 exists to deliver a renovated, code-compliant, and fully functional 2nd-level mezzanine (coffee room (break room)) within the existing Old North Shop at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill.

**Vocabulary note (enrichment ref: B-004):** Appendix B labels the space "2nd Level Mezzanine Coffee Room." All documents in this deliverable use "coffee room (break room)" as the standardized term. Previous drafts used variant orderings ("break room / coffee room", "staff break room/coffee room"); these have been normalized for consistency.

The Old North Shop is described as a 105 ft × 40 ft existing maintenance building. The Appendix B conceptual drawings show a "2nd Level Mezzanine Coffee Room" at the shared wall between the Old North Shop and the new building addition. The renovation of this space is one of four renovation scope items within PKG-017 (alongside the kitchenette, washroom, and new locker room), all of which together adapt the existing building to support an expanded, modernized maintenance facility operation.

The broader project objective (OBJ-001) is to deliver a code-compliant, fully functional maintenance shop addition and associated renovations satisfying the County's operational program, passing all applicable inspections.

*Source: SOW-0071; App B (Shop) floor plan; WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §5 OBJ-001, §7 PKG-017.*

## Principles

### 1. Structural Assessment First
The existing mezzanine is of unknown structural condition. No renovation design work should be finalized until a qualified structural engineer has assessed the existing structure and confirmed its adequacy (or specified remediation). This sequencing protects both safety and budget integrity.

*Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (STRUCT-ASSESS-001); general practice for renovation of existing structures.*

### 2. Code Compliance Is Non-Negotiable
The Alberta Safety Codes and Alberta Building Code apply in full. Stairs, railings, floor loads, means of egress, and electrical installations must all meet current code requirements regardless of the condition of the existing installation. The project must pass all applicable inspections.

*Source: RFP §3.3.2; OBJ-001 (WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §5).*

### 3. Design-Build Latitude Within the RFP Envelope
The RFP is a design-build contract. SOW-0071 defines the outcome (renovate the 2nd-level coffee room) but does not prescribe the design solution in detail. The General Contractor and their design team have latitude in the renovation approach, provided the outcome is code-compliant, functional, and within the approved design.

*Source: RFP §1.0 ("The County only has a concept in mind"); RFP §2.7 (CCDC 14-2013 contract form).*

### 4. Integration with Adjacent Renovation Scopes
The mezzanine sits directly above the washroom (DEL-017-03). Penetrations, utility routing, and structural connections between these two scopes must be coordinated. Sequencing decisions (e.g., which scope proceeds first, or whether they proceed in parallel) have direct implications for cost and schedule.

*Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (Interdependency Matrix); App B (Shop) floor plan.*

### 5. Minimal Disruption to Operations
The landfill is an active operational facility. Renovation works within the existing building should be planned and sequenced to minimize disruption to facility operations. Coordination with the Owner's representative is required.

*Source: RFP §3.2 (site meeting context indicating active facility); general construction management principle — ASSUMPTION that facility remains operational.*

## Considerations

### Scope Definition Risk
SOW-0071 states only "Renovate Old North Shop mezzanine (2nd-level coffee room)" and references Appendix B as the sole source. The Appendix B drawing shows the mezzanine label but does not itemize the specific renovation components. The detailed scope (flooring, stair upgrades, electrical, finishes, plumbing if any) must be developed through the design process. This is normal for a design-build project but means the cost and schedule for this deliverable carry more uncertainty than explicitly specified items.

*Source: SOW-0071 description (App B reference only); Appendix B (Shop) floor plan.*

### Structural Condition Unknown
The existing mezzanine's structural condition is unknown. The structural assessment (STRUCT-ASSESS-001) is a prerequisite. Outcomes could range from minor repairs to significant structural remediation, which would materially affect cost and schedule.

**Contingency pathway (enrichment ref: E-001):** In the extreme case where the structural assessment determines the mezzanine is not economically repairable (i.e., remediation cost exceeds the value of renovation), the following contingency pathway should be considered:
1. The structural engineer's report should explicitly state the "not economically repairable" finding with supporting analysis.
2. The General Contractor notifies the County project representative immediately.
3. A scope change process is initiated per the contract (CCDC 14-2013 change order provisions).
4. Alternative approaches are evaluated: (a) full demolition and reconstruction of the mezzanine, (b) reduction of the mezzanine scope (e.g., relocate the break room function to the main level), or (c) deferral of the mezzanine renovation pending further Owner direction.
5. The decision rests with the County; the General Contractor provides cost and schedule impact analysis for each alternative.

This contingency pathway is not currently addressed in Procedure Step 2's decision gate or in any Specification requirement. It should be formalized if the risk is considered material.

*Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (STRUCT-ASSESS-001); ASSUMPTION — contingency planning based on standard construction risk management. Enrichment ref: E-001.*

### Interface with Washroom Below
The washroom renovation (DEL-017-03) is directly below the mezzanine. Demolition and construction in either scope may affect the other. Careful sequencing and protection of completed work is required.

*Source: App B (Shop) floor plan; _DEPENDENCIES.md.*

### Electrical Scope Gap
The RFP does not include an explicit electrical scope item for the Old North Shop mezzanine renovation (the RFP's renovation electrical references focus on the New North Shop addition). However, a functional break room requires adequate lighting and outlets. The renovation design should address electrical adequacy as part of the design package, and an electrical sub-permit will likely be required.

*Source: ASSUMPTION — no explicit SOW item for mezzanine electrical in renovation section; general code requirement for habitable/useable space.*

### Fire and Life Safety
Renovation of an occupied 2nd-level space triggers fire and life safety code obligations under the Alberta Building Code. The design must address the following areas (specific code section references TBD pending edition confirmation per Specification Standards table):

- **Means of egress:** Minimum stair width, maximum travel distance, headroom clearance, exit signage, and emergency lighting for the mezzanine level. The existing stair is the primary (and likely sole) means of egress; adequacy must be confirmed.
- **Fire separation:** Required fire-resistance rating between the mezzanine floor/ceiling assembly and the space below (washroom, DEL-017-03). The required rating depends on building classification and occupancy load.
- **Detection and alarm:** Smoke detection and potentially CO detection requirements for a 2nd-level occupied break room. Alarm connectivity to the building's fire alarm system (if one exists or is required) must be assessed.
- **Occupancy classification:** The intended use (coffee room (break room)) determines occupancy load, which in turn drives egress width and fixture requirements.

These obligations are codified in the Alberta Building Code and are not discretionary. The design team must address each area and demonstrate compliance in the IFC drawings.

*Source: ASSUMPTION — Alberta Building Code requirements for 2nd-level occupied spaces; specific code sections and editions TBD pending design and permit application. Enrichment ref: C-001.*

### HVAC and Ventilation
A 2nd-level break room requires adequate ventilation per the Alberta Building Code. No document in this deliverable currently addresses HVAC, ventilation, or mechanical systems for the mezzanine. The design must assess whether existing ventilation is adequate for the intended occupancy and use (coffee-making produces heat and moisture), and whether mechanical upgrades are required. If the existing Old North Shop has a central HVAC system, the mezzanine tie-in should be evaluated; if not, standalone ventilation may be needed. Coordination with PKG-013 (Mechanical & HVAC Installation) should be considered if mechanical work extends beyond the renovation scope.

*Source: ASSUMPTION — Alberta Building Code ventilation requirements for occupied spaces; no HVAC scope identified in SOW-0071 or RFP for this specific space. Enrichment ref: D-002.*

### Building Envelope Integrity Prerequisite
The Procedure lists "building envelope integrity" as a prerequisite for interior renovation construction (sourced to _DEPENDENCIES.md cross-package note). The rationale is: interior renovation work (flooring, finishes, electrical) can be damaged or rendered defective if the building envelope (roof, exterior walls) has unresolved water infiltration or structural deficiencies. If the envelope has deficiencies, the fallback options include: (a) deferring interior renovation until envelope repairs are complete, (b) providing temporary protection to the work area during renovation, or (c) limiting renovation to non-finish work until the envelope is confirmed sound. The appropriate approach depends on the nature and severity of any envelope deficiency.

*Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (cross-package note); general construction sequencing principle — ASSUMPTION. Enrichment ref: F-003.*

## Lessons Learned and Quality Feedback

**Note (enrichment ref: D-003):** PKG-017 contains four similar renovation scopes (DEL-017-01 Kitchenette, DEL-017-02 Mezzanine, DEL-017-03 Washroom, DEL-017-04 Locker Room). These scopes share common characteristics: renovation of existing spaces within the Old North Shop, similar trade involvement, same code compliance framework, and shared building systems.

Lessons learned from the first renovation scope to reach completion should be formally captured and applied to the remaining scopes. Recommended mechanism:
- At the completion of each renovation scope's construction phase (before CCC), the General Contractor conducts a brief lessons-learned review covering: (a) scope definition adequacy, (b) structural assessment findings applicability to other scopes, (c) permitting process efficiency, (d) coordination effectiveness with adjacent scopes, and (e) any unexpected conditions encountered.
- Findings are documented and distributed to the project team before the next renovation scope's construction phase begins.
- This feedback loop is especially important for DEL-017-02 and DEL-017-03, which are structurally related (mezzanine above washroom).

*Source: ASSUMPTION — quality management best practice for multi-scope renovation projects; no explicit lessons-learned requirement in RFP. Enrichment ref: D-003.*
