## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and deliver the Old North Shop Mezzanine Renovation (DEL-017-02), from pre-construction planning through construction, inspection, and closeout. It is intended for use by the General Contractor and the project management team.

The procedure also supports the County's project representative in understanding the sequence of activities, decision gates, and verification points.

## Steps

### Phase 1: Pre-Construction and Design

**Step 1 — Site Investigation and Existing Documentation Collection**

1.1 Obtain (or confirm existence of) original mezzanine construction drawings (architectural and structural).
1.2 Commission a condition survey of the existing mezzanine structure, flooring, stairs, railings, and electrical systems.
1.3 Document existing conditions with date-stamped photographs and written notes. This pre-demolition photographic record is required per Specification Documentation (Photographic Documentation artifact; enrichment ref: X-003) and serves as baseline evidence for the audit trail.
1.4 Identify any immediately hazardous conditions and notify the Owner's representative.

*Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (EXISTING-DOCS-001, STRUCT-ASSESS-001); general renovation practice.*
*Note: If original drawings are not available, the structural engineer will need to perform a field investigation to establish existing structural dimensions and member sizes. Approach TBD.*

---

**Step 2 — Structural Engineering Assessment**

2.1 Engage a professional structural engineer licensed in Alberta (APEGA-registered P.Eng. with demonstrated structural engineering experience, per Specification REQ-017-02-002) to assess the existing mezzanine structure.
2.2 The assessment shall evaluate:
  - Structural adequacy of the existing mezzanine floor framing, columns, and connections.
  - Condition of stairs and stair structure.
  - Load capacity for intended use (coffee room (break room) occupancy).
  - Compliance with current Alberta Building Code requirements.
2.3 Receive and review the structural engineering assessment report.
2.4 Identify required structural repairs or upgrades.
2.5 Present assessment findings to the County project representative.

*Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (STRUCT-ASSESS-001); RFP §3.3.2 (code compliance); REQ-017-02-002.*

*Decision gate: If the structural assessment identifies significant remediation requirements, cost and schedule implications must be reviewed with the Owner before proceeding to design.*

---

**Step 3 — Renovation Design**

3.1 Develop renovation design based on structural assessment findings, existing documentation, and intended use: coffee room (break room).
3.2 Design package shall include, at minimum:
  - Architectural drawings: floor plan, reflected ceiling plan, interior elevations, door/window schedule (if applicable), finish schedule.
  - Structural drawings: any structural repairs, reinforcement, or upgrades required.
  - Electrical drawings: lighting layout, outlet locations, panel connections (if panel modification required).
  - Stair and railing details: new or upgraded stair and guardrail design to meet current code.
3.3 Ensure all drawings comply with applicable Alberta Safety Codes and Alberta Building Code.
3.4 Have all Issued for Construction drawings stamped and signed by a professional engineer licensed in Alberta.
3.5 Submit design package for County project team review and approval.
3.6 Incorporate any County review comments and obtain final design approval.

*Source: RFP §3.3.2; REQ-017-02-004; REQ-017-02-005; _DEPENDENCIES.md (DESIGN-001).*

---

**Step 4 — Permit Acquisition**

4.1 Prepare and submit building permit application to the relevant authority having jurisdiction (AHJ) based on approved design drawings.
4.2 Apply for all applicable Safety Code permits. Anticipated disciplines: **Building** (structural), **Electrical**, and potentially **Plumbing** and **Gas** if the renovation design includes plumbing fixtures or gas appliances (per Specification Standards table, enrichment ref: C-003). Specific permit types to be confirmed by the AHJ based on the approved design.
4.3 Track permit status and respond to any deficiency notices from the AHJ.
4.4 Obtain issued permits before commencing construction.
4.5 Post permits on site as required by the AHJ.

*Note: Permit fees are paid by the County (RFP §3.3.1). The General Contractor is responsible for obtaining the permits.*
*Source: RFP §3.3.1; RFP §3.3.2; REQ-017-02-006.*

---

### Phase 2: Construction

**Step 5 — Pre-Construction Coordination**

5.1 Hold a pre-construction coordination meeting with the County project representative, subcontractors, and the DEL-017-03 (Washroom Renovation) team to align on sequencing, work zones, access, and safety measures.
5.2 Confirm that the building envelope is sound and the work area is ready for renovation.
5.3 Establish temporary protection measures for the washroom below (DEL-017-03 work area) if mezzanine work proceeds first.
5.4 Prepare and submit a construction schedule showing sequencing of mezzanine renovation relative to other PKG-017 renovation scopes.
5.5 Designate construction access routes that minimize disruption to facility operations.

*Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (Interdependency Matrix); RFP §3.3.2 (coordinate inspections and weekly meetings).*

---

**Step 6 — Demolition and Preparation**

6.1 **Hazardous materials assessment (enrichment ref: A-005):** Before demolition begins, conduct a hazardous materials assessment of existing finishes and building materials in the mezzanine area. Assessment shall include, at minimum:
  - Asbestos-containing materials (ACM) testing for flooring, ceiling tiles, insulation, and pipe wrapping (ASSUMPTION -- the building is of unknown vintage; ACM testing is standard practice for renovation of existing structures in Alberta).
  - Lead-based paint testing on walls, ceilings, and stair/railing surfaces.
  - Any other regulated materials as identified by the assessment.
  If hazardous materials are found, abatement shall be completed by a qualified contractor in accordance with Alberta Occupational Health and Safety (OHS) regulations before general demolition proceeds. Waste disposal shall comply with Alberta Environment and Protected Areas requirements for regulated waste.
6.2 Remove existing finishes (flooring, wall finishes, ceiling if applicable) as required by the approved design.
6.3 Protect existing structural members from damage during demolition.
6.4 Remove and dispose of demolition debris in accordance with applicable waste management requirements. Segregate hazardous waste (if any) from general construction waste.
6.5 Perform any structural repairs or reinforcements identified in the structural assessment and shown on approved drawings.
6.6 Rough-in electrical (conduit, boxes, wiring) per approved electrical drawings.

*Source: Approved Renovation Design Package (DESIGN-001); REQ-017-02-008; REQ-017-02-010. Hazardous materials: ASSUMPTION -- standard renovation practice for existing buildings; Alberta OHS Act and Regulations; location TBD. Enrichment ref: A-005.*

---

**Step 7 — Stair and Railing Work**

7.1 Assess existing stair and railing condition against approved design requirements.
7.2 Repair or replace stairs as specified in the approved design.
7.3 Install new or upgraded guardrails and handrails to meet Alberta Building Code requirements for height, loading, and geometry.
7.4 Obtain required structural or Safety Code inspections for stair/railing work.

*Source: REQ-017-02-009; Approved Renovation Design Package.*

---

**Step 8 — Flooring Installation**

8.1 Install flooring per the approved finish schedule and manufacturer's instructions.
8.2 Ensure substrate is properly prepared and any structural repairs are complete and cured before flooring installation.
8.3 Flooring type and specification: per approved finish schedule (TBD at design stage).

*Source: REQ-017-02-008; Approved Renovation Design Package.*

---

**Step 9 — Electrical Completion**

9.1 Complete electrical installation (fixtures, devices, panel connections) per approved electrical drawings.
9.2 Arrange and facilitate Electrical Safety Code inspection.
9.3 Provide copies of electrical inspection reports to the County project representative.

*Source: REQ-017-02-010; RFP §3.3.2.*

---

**Step 10 — Interior Finishes**

10.1 Install wall finishes (paint, paneling, or other approved finish) per the approved finish schedule.
10.2 Install ceiling finishes as specified.
10.3 Install door hardware, trim, and any millwork specified in the design.
10.4 Install any fixtures or fittings specified for the coffee room function (ASSUMPTION — fixtures scope TBD at design stage; may include a counter, sink, or appliance connections if the design includes these).

*Source: REQ-017-02-011; Approved Renovation Design Package.*

---

### Phase 3: Inspection and Closeout

**Step 11 — Pre-Completion Inspection (Contractor Self-Review)**

11.1 Perform a thorough contractor self-inspection of all completed work.
11.2 Verify that all items on the approved design are complete.
11.3 Verify that the space is clean, tidy, and ready for Owner review.
11.4 Generate an internal punch list and resolve all items before requesting Owner inspection.

*Source: RFP §2.14.1.*

---

**Step 11A — Functional Verification (enrichment ref: C-004)**

11A.1 Before requesting the Owner's final inspection, verify that the renovated mezzanine is fit for its intended purpose as a coffee room (break room). Functional verification shall confirm:
  - Electrical systems operational: lighting, outlets, and any appliance connections functioning correctly.
  - Ventilation adequate: air circulation and temperature acceptable for occupancy (see Guidance, HVAC and Ventilation consideration).
  - Fixtures and fittings operational: counter, sink (if installed), and any built-in fixtures functioning.
  - Stair and railing access safe and code-compliant.
  - Finishes complete and free of defects.
  - Space is clean, furnished (if applicable per scope), and ready for occupancy.
11A.2 Document functional verification results. Resolve any functional deficiencies before proceeding to Step 12.

*Source: ASSUMPTION — standard commissioning/functional verification practice for renovated occupied spaces. Enrichment ref: C-004.*

---

**Step 12 — Final Inspection and CCC**

12.1 Submit written request to the Owner for final inspection.
12.2 Facilitate Owner (County project representative) final inspection attendance.
12.3 Provide copies of all inspection reports (Alberta Safety Code, building, electrical) to the County project representative.
12.4 Resolve any deficiencies identified during the final inspection.
12.5 Obtain Construction Completion Certificate (CCC) from the Owner upon satisfactory completion.

*Source: RFP §2.14.2; RFP §2.14.3; REQ-017-02-007.*

---

**Step 13 — Closeout Documentation**

13.1 Compile and submit as-built drawings reflecting actual constructed conditions.
13.2 Submit all warranty documentation (2-year guarantee) in writing.
13.3 Submit Statutory Declaration confirming no outstanding claims from suppliers or subcontractors.
13.4 Submit WCB Letter of Clearance.
13.5 Submit all permit close-out documentation as required by the AHJ.

*Source: RFP §2.13.2 (CCC documentation requirements); RFP §2.10 (guarantee); REQ-017-02-013.*

## Verification

### Inspection Notification Protocol (enrichment ref: X-001)

All inspection requests shall be submitted by the General Contractor per REQ-017-02-007. The following notification requirements apply consistently across all inspection points:
- **AHJ inspections:** Submit inspection request to the authority having jurisdiction with minimum notice per AHJ requirements (typically 48 hours; confirm with local AHJ). The County project representative must be notified of all scheduled AHJ inspections.
- **County inspections:** Notify the County project representative with minimum 48 hours advance notice (TBD -- to be confirmed at pre-construction meeting, Step 5).
- **Weekly coordination meetings:** Per RFP, weekly project meetings shall be held during construction. Inspection scheduling and results shall be standing agenda items.

*Source: RFP §3.3.2 (inspection coordination, weekly meetings); ASSUMPTION for specific notice periods. Enrichment ref: X-001.*

### Hold Points (enrichment ref: X-002)

The following are **mandatory hold points** -- construction work must stop and may not proceed past these points until the required inspection is completed and passed. Hold points are distinguished from routine verifications (which confirm quality but do not require work stoppage).

| Hold Point | Location in Steps | Required Inspection | Work That Cannot Proceed Until Passed |
|------------|-------------------|--------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| HP-1: Structural repairs complete | After Step 6.5 | Structural inspection by AHJ (if required) and/or design engineer sign-off | Flooring installation (Step 8), finishes (Step 10) |
| HP-2: Rough-in electrical complete | After Step 6.6 | Electrical rough-in inspection by Electrical Safety Code officer | Wall/ceiling closure, finishes (Step 10) |
| HP-3: Stair/railing installation complete | After Step 7.3 | Stair/railing code compliance inspection; structural sign-off | Finish work on stairs (Step 10), final inspection (Step 12) |

*Source: ASSUMPTION -- standard construction hold-point practice for renovation work with structural involvement; Alberta Safety Codes inspection requirements. Enrichment ref: X-002.*
