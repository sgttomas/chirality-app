## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and deliver the New Locker/Change Room (DEL-017-04) — from design through construction completion and handover. It is oriented toward the General Contractor (design-build proponent) executing the work under CCDC 14–2013.

The locker/change room must be designed and constructed to include laundry services and to comply with all applicable Alberta Safety Codes, in accordance with SOW-0073 and RFP §3.4.

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Design

**Step 1.1 — Confirm Space and Program**
- Meet with County to confirm workforce headcount and any specific locker room program requirements (number of lockers, shower facilities if desired, laundry machine count, bench requirements).
- Confirm available footprint within Old North Shop in coordination with DEL-017-02 (mezzanine) and DEL-017-03 (washroom) design teams.
- Determine physical boundary between locker room (DEL-017-04) and washroom renovation (DEL-017-03). Resolve CON-017-04-01 (see Guidance.md Conflict Table) with County ruling.
- **[D-002] DEL-017-03 Coordination Mechanism:** Schedule a joint coordination meeting with the DEL-017-03 (Washroom) design/construction team to review shared MEP coordination drawings, confirm the physical scope boundary, and agree on utility tie-in points. Document the coordination agreement in meeting minutes and obtain sign-off from both teams. This coordination handshake should occur before proceeding to Step 1.4 (Final Design).
- **Output:** Confirmed program brief and space allocation; DEL-017-03 coordination agreement documented.

**Step 1.2 — Preliminary Design**
- Develop preliminary locker room layout including: partition locations, locker layout, bench seating, laundry alcove, plumbing fixture locations, door(s), and electrical distribution.
- Coordinate layout with MEP designers for plumbing and electrical preliminary design.
- Submit Preliminary Design to County for review and approval per RFP §3.3.2.
- **Output:** Approved preliminary design.

**Step 1.3 — Select Locker System and Laundry Equipment**
- Evaluate and select locker system product (material, size, configuration) appropriate for maintenance shop environment. Document selection with product data sheet.
- Evaluate and select laundry equipment (washer/dryer); determine electrical load (circuit amperage/voltage) and plumbing connections required. Document selection.
- Confirm dryer exhaust duct routing (exterior penetration location).
- **Output:** Locker system and laundry equipment specifications confirmed.

**Step 1.4 — Final Design and IFC Drawings**
- Complete final design across all disciplines (architectural, structural if applicable, plumbing, electrical, mechanical/ventilation).
- Ensure all IFC drawings are signed and stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta (RFP §3.3.2).
- Coordinate locker room IFC drawings with DEL-001-10 (Old North Shop Renovation Plans), DEL-006 (Plumbing), and DEL-004 (Electrical).
- **Output:** IFC drawing set (P.Eng.-stamped) for locker room scope.

**Step 1.5 — Obtain Permits**
- Apply for and obtain building permit and all applicable safety code permits (plumbing, electrical, mechanical) from the relevant Alberta authorities.
- Note: Development permit fees are paid by the County (RFP §3.3.1); proponent is responsible for the application process.
- **Output:** Issued permits on file.

---

### Phase 2 — Construction

**Step 2.1 — Site Preparation and Temporary Protection**
- Establish work zone within Old North Shop. Install temporary hoarding/protection to maintain access to adjacent areas (washroom, mezzanine stair, connection to New North Shop) during construction, as applicable.
- Confirm utility isolation requirements with MEP subtrades before commencing demolition or rough-in.

**Step 2.2 — Demolition (if required)**
- Remove any existing finishes, partitions, or MEP elements within the locker room construction zone as shown on IFC drawings.
- **Note [A-004]:** This deliverable is new construction within existing space rather than renovation of previously built-out space. Demolition scope is expected to be limited unless existing elements (partitions, MEP runs, finishes) are present in the construction zone. If IFC drawings show no demolition, this step may be noted as "Not Applicable" in the execution record. If demolition is required (e.g., removal of existing finishes or partitions to clear the construction zone), scope shall be as shown on IFC drawings; coordinate with DEL-001-09 (Demolition Plans).

**Step 2.3 — Rough Framing and Partitions**
- Construct new partition walls and any structural elements as shown on IFC drawings.
- Frame openings for doors and any mechanical penetrations (exhaust fan, dryer duct).

**Step 2.4 — Plumbing Rough-In**
- Install drain, vent, and supply rough-in for: sink(s), floor drain(s) (if required), and laundry connections (washer supply/drain).
- Coordinate with DEL-017-03 (Washroom) plumbing work for shared stack/supply runs.
- Connect to project-wide plumbing system (cistern supply; septic holding tank drain).
- Schedule rough-in inspection with safety codes authority; ensure County representative is present (RFP §3.3.2) [E-003].

**Step 2.5 — Electrical Rough-In**
- Install conduit, wire, boxes, and panel connections for: lighting circuit(s), general receptacles (15A/120V), exhaust fan circuit, and dedicated laundry circuits (amperage/voltage per confirmed equipment specifications).
- Schedule rough-in inspection with safety codes authority; ensure County representative is present [E-003].

**Step 2.6 — Mechanical Rough-In (Ventilation)**
- Install exhaust fan ductwork and exterior penetration/louver for change room exhaust ventilation.
- Install dryer exhaust duct and exterior penetration/louver, including appropriate lint trap/termination.
- Schedule mechanical inspection if required by safety codes authority [E-003].

**Step 2.7 — Insulation (if required)**
- Install insulation in walls and ceiling as required by code and energy efficiency requirements.
- Scope TBD at design phase.

**Step 2.8 — Drywall / Wall Board and Ceilings**
- Install wall board and ceiling systems as specified in IFC drawings.
- Apply moisture-resistant board in wet areas (laundry alcove, near sink) per code.

**Step 2.9 — Flooring**
- Install flooring as specified — TBD. For a maintenance shop change room, durable, slip-resistant, and moisture-resistant flooring is expected (ASSUMPTION).

**Step 2.10 — Finish Electrical and Plumbing**
- Install fixtures: lighting, receptacles, switches, exhaust fan, sink faucet(s), and any other specified fixtures.
- Connect laundry equipment supply and drain.

**Step 2.11 — Locker Installation**
- Install locker system as per manufacturer's instructions and IFC drawings.
- Install bench seating.

**Step 2.12 — Laundry Equipment Installation**
- Install washer and dryer; connect to plumbing and electrical rough-in.
- Test operation per manufacturer's startup procedure.

**Step 2.13 — Door(s) and Hardware**
- Install door(s), frame(s), and hardware as specified.

**Step 2.14 — Painting and Final Finishes**
- Apply paint and any remaining finish materials as specified.

**Step 2.15 — Ventilation System Commissioning [C-003]**
- Perform functional testing of the change room exhaust ventilation system: verify exhaust fan operation, measure airflow at diffuser/grille, and confirm airflow rate meets or exceeds the code-required ventilation rate for the confirmed occupancy classification.
- Perform functional testing of the dryer exhaust duct: verify airflow, confirm lint trap is accessible and functional, confirm exterior termination is clear.
- Document commissioning results with measured airflow values.
- **Source:** Specification REQ-017-04-07 Verification ("Functional test of exhaust fan; airflow verification per code").

---

### Phase 3 — Verification and Closeout

**Step 3.1 — Pre-Inspection Self-Review**
- General Contractor conducts complete internal review of locker room: verify all items on the IFC drawings are complete, all MEP systems are connected and functional, all finishes are complete, and the space is clean and tidy (RFP §2.14.1).

**Step 3.2 — Safety Code Inspections**
- Submit all inspection requests for plumbing, electrical, mechanical, and building final inspection.
- County representative must be present for all inspections (RFP §3.3.2).
- Obtain and file all inspection reports; provide copies to County.

**Step 3.3 — Deficiency Correction**
- Address any deficiencies identified during inspections or County review.
- Re-inspect as required.

**Step 3.4 — Punch-List and Substantial Completion**
- Participate in punch-list walkthrough with County representative.
- Correct all punch-list items.

**Step 3.5 — Contribution to CCC**
- Ensure locker room completion is documented as part of the overall project Construction Completion Certificate (CCC) process (RFP §2.14.3).
