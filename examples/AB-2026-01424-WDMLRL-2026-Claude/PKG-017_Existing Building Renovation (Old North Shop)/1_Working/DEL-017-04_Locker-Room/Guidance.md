## Purpose

DEL-017-04 delivers a new locker and change room with laundry services within the Old North Shop of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill facility. The purpose of this deliverable is to provide facility workers with dedicated personal storage, change, and laundry facilities as part of the broader renovation of the existing Old North Shop building.

The RFP §3.4 states the design requirement: "Expand washroom facilities to include urinal and locker change room. including laundry services." The decomposition assigns this as SOW-0073: "Construct new locker/change room with laundry services." The facility is essential to the County's operational program for workforce amenities at the maintenance shop.

**Source:** RFP §3.4; SOW-0073 (WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md).

---

## Principles

### 1. Workforce Utility First
The locker/change room exists to serve the practical daily needs of the maintenance shop workforce — secure personal storage, a clean space to change before and after work, and on-site laundry. Design decisions should prioritize functionality, durability, and ease of cleaning over aesthetics.

**Source:** ASSUMPTION — consistent with intent of OBJ-001 and SOW-0073; the RFP describes the facility in operational terms (laundry services, change room) rather than aesthetic terms.

### 2. Code Compliance is Non-Negotiable
The space must pass all applicable Alberta Safety Code inspections for its occupancy classification. The General Contractor (design-build proponent) is responsible for identifying the correct occupancy classification and applying all associated code requirements for plumbing, electrical, ventilation, and egress.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; RFP §3.4.

### 3. Integration with Broader Renovation
This deliverable is physically and operationally adjacent to DEL-017-03 (Washroom Renovation & Expansion, which adds a urinal) and DEL-017-02 (Mezzanine Renovation). Design and construction coordination across all three PKG-017 deliverables will reduce rework, optimize utility routing, and may allow phased occupancy. The Appendix B drawings show the washroom at the south end of the Old North Shop, and the locker room is to be constructed in proximity.

**Source:** App B (Shop) drawing note; _DEPENDENCIES.md; WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md (PKG-017 package description).

### 4. Design-Build Latitude Within RFP Constraints
The County has stated only a concept; the proponent has design latitude. However, the RFP is explicit that laundry services are included and the locker room is part of the washroom facility expansion. The proponent should treat these as non-negotiable scope items and exercise design-build judgment on layout, locker system selection, finishes, and fixture counts.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 — "The County only has a concept in mind; therefore, it will be a design/build project."

## Considerations

### Space Planning
- The Old North Shop is 105' × 40' (4,200 sq ft). The locker room must fit within the available footprint after accounting for the existing functions shown in the Appendix B drawing (mezzanine coffee room area, wash station, existing washroom). Exact available area for the locker room is TBD pending design.
- The conceptual drawing (App B — Shop) shows the washroom at the south end of the Old North Shop, adjacent to the connection with the New North Shop. The locker room logically occupies a nearby portion of this zone.
- ASSUMPTION: The locker room footprint will be sized to accommodate the expected number of maintenance shop workers. Workforce headcount is not stated in the RFP or decomposition; the design team must obtain this from the County.

**Source:** App B (Shop) drawing; ASSUMPTION for workforce count.

### Plumbing
- The locker/change room requires plumbing connections for at minimum: a sink (hand-washing), floor drain, and laundry rough-in (hot/cold supply, drain). Shower facilities are not mentioned in the RFP and should be treated as TBD pending County confirmation.
- The project-wide plumbing system (50,000 L cistern, 2,000 US Gallon septic holding tank) provides the supply and waste infrastructure. The design team must size the locker room branch connections accordingly.
- Proximity to the existing washroom (DEL-017-03) is advantageous for sharing stack and supply runs.

**Source:** RFP §3.4; App B (Plumbing); ASSUMPTION for shower facilities.

### Electrical
- Minimum electrical scope: lighting (sufficient lux for a change room), general receptacles (15A/120V), and an exhaust fan circuit. The App B (Electrical) drawing shows exhaust fan symbols (E) and 15A circuits in the washroom vicinity of the Old North Shop.
- Laundry equipment will require dedicated circuits — typically 20A/240V for a washer and 30A/240V for a dryer (ASSUMPTION: standard North American residential-grade appliances; commercial appliances may require different loads). The electrical design must confirm appliance specifications before finalizing the panel.
- ASSUMPTION: Locker room lighting should match or exceed the minimum illumination levels required by Alberta OHS and the NBC for change room occupancies.

**Source:** App B (Electrical); ASSUMPTION for circuit specifications and illumination.

### Locker System
- Locker type, quantity, and configuration (single-tier, double-tier, etc.) are TBD. Selection should consider: number of workers, locker size requirements (work gear and personal effects), material durability in a maintenance shop environment (high humidity from laundry, potential contamination from industrial work), and ease of cleaning.
- ASSUMPTION: Metal lockers (powder-coated or stainless steel) are most appropriate for this environment. Plastic laminate lockers are also a common alternative. Selection TBD by design team.

**Source:** ASSUMPTION — no locker system specification in RFP or drawings.

### Laundry Services
- The RFP explicitly requires "laundry services" within the locker/change room (RFP §3.4; SOW-0073). This implies at minimum washer and dryer installation with appropriate plumbing and electrical rough-in.
- The number of machines and whether equipment is commercial or residential grade is TBD. For a maintenance shop workforce, a commercial-grade washer/dryer is advisable for durability and cycle capacity (ASSUMPTION).
- Adequate ventilation must account for dryer exhaust duct routing. This may require an exterior penetration through the Old North Shop wall.
- **TBD [E-001]:** The design team should clarify early whether a dryer exhaust duct penetration through the existing Old North Shop wall requires a building permit amendment or a separate mechanical permit. An exterior penetration may also trigger additional structural review depending on the wall assembly. This regulatory pathway question should be resolved before finalizing the dryer exhaust routing option (see Trade-offs table).

**Source:** SOW-0073; RFP §3.4; ASSUMPTION for equipment grade and dryer duct.

### Sequencing
- The locker/change room construction is new construction within an existing occupied (or partially active) building. Phasing and temporary protection to maintain access to adjacent areas during construction should be planned.
- Early completion of the locker/change room is noted as advantageous in the _DEPENDENCIES.md, as it would support workforce comfort during the broader construction period. This should be discussed with the project schedule team.
- The locker room is on a conditional critical path — it can proceed in parallel with other PKG-017 and PKG-018 work items but is dependent on space planning confirmation, utility availability verification, and design approval.
- **Permit Timeline [X-001]:** Procedure Step 1.5 requires permits before construction commences. The design team and Project Manager should account for permit application lead times when scheduling. Key considerations: (a) the design must be substantially complete before permit application; (b) County pays development permit fees (RFP §3.3.1) so fee payment timing must be coordinated; (c) if separate permits are needed for building, plumbing, electrical, and mechanical, each may have different lead times and sequencing implications. ASSUMPTION: permit lead time is not stated in available sources; the project schedule team should confirm typical lead times with the authority having jurisdiction.

**Source:** _DEPENDENCIES.md; Procedure.md Step 1.5; ASSUMPTION for phasing and permit lead times.

## Trade-offs

| Trade-off | Option A | Option B | Recommended Approach | Source |
|-----------|----------|----------|----------------------|--------|
| Locker room size | Larger space (more lockers, bench area, laundry room) | Smaller space (minimum viable) | Size to confirmed workforce headcount; obtain from County | ASSUMPTION |
| Laundry equipment grade | Residential (lower cost, standard availability) | Commercial (higher durability, faster cycles) | Commercial preferred for maintenance shop environment | ASSUMPTION |
| Locker system material | Painted steel (lower cost) | Stainless or phenolic (higher durability, moisture resistance) | Higher durability preferred given humidity from laundry | ASSUMPTION |
| Shared vs. separate laundry room | Laundry integrated in change room | Separate laundry alcove with door | Separate alcove reduces noise and humidity migration to locker area | ASSUMPTION |
| Dryer exhaust routing | Through existing wall | Through new penetration in roof | Routing through existing exterior wall is simpler; roof routing may be needed depending on layout | ASSUMPTION |

> **Cost Considerations [F-004]:** Neither the RFP nor the decomposition specifies a budget or cost constraint for this deliverable. As a design-build contract, cost is part of the proponent's stipulated price proposal. However, cost is a practical design driver that influences decisions such as locker material selection (painted steel vs. stainless/phenolic), laundry equipment grade (residential vs. commercial), and finish quality. The trade-off recommendations above implicitly favor durability over cost, which is consistent with the industrial maintenance shop environment, but the proponent should weigh these recommendations against overall project cost constraints. Explicit cost guidance is deferred to contract negotiation. ASSUMPTION: cost considerations are not addressed in available sources for this deliverable.

## Vocabulary Notes

### Laundry Terminology [E-002]
The following terms appear across the four documents referring to the laundry area and function:
- **"Laundry services"** — used in the RFP (§3.4), Specification (REQ-017-04-02, Scope), and decomposition (SOW-0073). This is the **authoritative term** for the scope of work as stated in the source documents.
- **"Laundry alcove"** — used in Guidance (Considerations, Trade-offs) and Procedure (Steps 1.2, 2.8) to describe the physical sub-space within the locker/change room.
- **"Laundry room"** — used in Guidance (Trade-offs Option B: "Separate laundry room") to describe a potential distinct room.

**Convention:** Use **"laundry services"** when referring to the contractual/scope requirement. Use **"laundry alcove"** when referring to the physical sub-space in its integrated configuration. Use **"separate laundry room"** only when discussing the trade-off option of a fully separate enclosed space.

### Inspection Authority Terminology [E-003]
The following terms appear across documents referring to the safety codes inspection authority:
- "Alberta safety codes authority" (Procedure Steps 2.4, 2.5)
- "safety code inspection(s)" (Specification Verification)
- "authority having jurisdiction" (Procedure Step 2.6)
- "Alberta Safety Codes" (Specification Standards)

**Convention:** Use **"safety codes authority"** (lowercase, no geographic prefix in running text) when referring to the inspection/enforcement body. Use **"Alberta Safety Codes"** (capitalized) only when referring to the statutory framework (Alberta Safety Codes Act) or the Standards table. Use **"safety codes inspection"** (lowercase) when referring to the inspection event.
