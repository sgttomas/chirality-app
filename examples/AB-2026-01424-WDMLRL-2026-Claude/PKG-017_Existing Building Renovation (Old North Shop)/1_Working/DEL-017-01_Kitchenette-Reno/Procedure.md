## Purpose

This procedure describes the operational steps required to produce and complete the Kitchenette Renovation deliverable (DEL-017-01) — covering the sequence from pre-construction planning through construction execution, inspection, and closeout. The procedure is oriented toward the General Contractor responsible for executing the work within the Old North Shop building.

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Pre-Construction Planning

**Step 1: Existing Conditions Documentation**
- Conduct a thorough survey of the existing kitchenette area.
- Record and photograph existing fixtures, cabinetry, finishes, plumbing rough-in (supply, drain, vent), and electrical rough-in (panel circuits, outlets, lighting).
- Confirm the boundaries of the 250 sq ft kitchenette area.
- **Hazardous materials assessment (Lensing: A-002):** For an existing-building renovation, conduct a hazardous materials assessment prior to demolition. Specifically:
  - If the Old North Shop building was constructed or renovated prior to the relevant regulatory thresholds (e.g., pre-1985 for asbestos-containing materials, pre-1978 for lead-based paint), a hazardous materials survey is **mandatory** under Alberta Occupational Health and Safety regulations before demolition begins.
  - If the building age/renovation history is unknown, a hazardous materials survey shall be conducted as a precautionary measure.
  - The assessment shall be performed by a qualified hazardous materials consultant.
  - If hazardous materials are identified, a remediation/abatement plan must be developed and executed before demolition proceeds.
  - **Proposed authority:** Alberta Occupational Health and Safety Act / Alberta Safety Codes.
- Identify any fire-rated assemblies (walls, floors, ceilings) within or adjacent to the kitchenette area that may be affected by the renovation (Lensing: X-001).
- Deliverable: Existing conditions documentation package submitted to design team (satisfies EXISTING-001).
- Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md; RFP §3.3.2

**Step 2: Utility Capacity Verification**
- Confirm available plumbing service capacity (domestic water supply, drain, vent) for the kitchenette within the existing Old North Shop plumbing system.
- Confirm available electrical panel capacity for kitchenette circuits within the existing Old North Shop electrical system.
- Report findings to the design team.
- Note: If existing services are undersized, communicate to the design team immediately to allow design revision before IFC drawing completion.
- Deliverable: Utility capacity verification report (satisfies UTILITY-001).
- Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md

**Step 3: Design Coordination and IFC Drawing Review**
- Coordinate with the Architect (PKG-001), Plumbing Engineer (PKG-006), and Electrical Engineer (PKG-004) during design development.
- Review preliminary design for constructability; provide Contractor input on preferred construction sequence and any access constraints within the Old North Shop.
- Review and confirm IFC drawings upon issuance.
- Confirm the appliance schedule and cabinetry specifications are finalized and approved.
- Confirm whether the appliance schedule includes gas appliances (which would trigger gas fitting permit and inspection requirements — Lensing: C-001).
- Confirm whether cooking appliances are included (which would trigger kitchen ventilation/exhaust requirements — Lensing: F-001).
- Confirm whether barrier-free / accessibility requirements apply (Lensing: D-001).
- **Identify procurement lead times** for cabinetry, appliances, and any long-lead items; establish procurement timeline that accounts for manufacturing, shipping, and delivery lead times relative to the December 31, 2026 deadline (Lensing: D-002).
- Deliverable: IFC drawing set received and reviewed (satisfies DESIGN-001).
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; _DEPENDENCIES.md

**Step 4: Permitting**
- Obtain building permit for the renovation work (DEL-009-02). Note: the "building permit" is a permit issued under the Alberta Safety Codes Act for the overall building renovation work (Lensing: F-002).
- Obtain Safety Code permits for plumbing, electrical, and gas fitting (if applicable) work as required (DEL-009-03). Note: "Safety Code permits" are trade-specific permits also issued under the Alberta Safety Codes Act, distinct from the building permit (Lensing: F-002).
- Post permits at the work site as required by Alberta regulations.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2

**Step 5: Workforce and Continuity Planning**
- Discuss phasing and temporary worker accommodation with Owner prior to commencement of demolition.
- ASSUMPTION: Owner may require a plan to maintain reasonable break facilities during construction. Confirm requirements with Owner at pre-construction meeting.
- Schedule work to minimize conflict with active Old North Shop operations to the extent practicable.
- Source: Guidance.md (Considerations — Workforce Continuity); ASSUMPTION

---

### Phase 2 — Demolition

**Step 6: Protect Adjacent Areas**
- Erect temporary hoarding, dust barriers, or protective sheeting as required to isolate the kitchenette work area from occupied or operational portions of the Old North Shop.
- Confirm that utilities serving adjacent active areas are not disrupted by demolition activities.
- Source: ASSUMPTION: standard commercial demolition practice.

**Step 7: Utility Isolation**
- Isolate plumbing supply, electrical circuits, and gas supply (if applicable) serving the kitchenette area, in coordination with the building's overall utility systems.
- Confirm isolation with the responsible trade subcontractors before beginning demolition.
- Notify Owner representative prior to any utility isolation affecting building operations.
- Source: ASSUMPTION: standard commercial demolition practice; RFP §3.3.2 (County representative involvement).

**Step 8: Demolition of Existing Kitchenette**
- Remove existing cabinetry, appliances, fixtures, and finishes (flooring, wall finish, ceiling as required by IFC drawings).
- Remove existing plumbing rough-in (supply piping, drains, vents) as required to accommodate new layout.
- Remove existing electrical rough-in (wiring, conduit, boxes) as required to accommodate new layout.
- **Waste disposal (Lensing: A-004):** Dispose of all demolished materials in accordance with applicable waste management regulations. Given that this project is located at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill, the following considerations apply:
  - If hazardous materials were identified in Step 1, disposal must follow the hazardous materials abatement plan and applicable Alberta Environmental Protection and Enhancement Act requirements.
  - Non-hazardous construction waste shall be disposed of in accordance with the landfill operator's protocols and any applicable municipal waste management bylaws.
  - Segregate recyclable materials (metals, etc.) from general waste where practicable.
  - **Proposed authority:** Alberta Environmental Protection and Enhancement Act; landfill operator protocols; Owner direction.
- Retain and protect any elements identified in the IFC drawings as to-remain.
- If fire-rated assemblies are encountered during demolition, protect and document their condition; coordinate with the design team regarding firestopping requirements (Lensing: X-001).
- Source: _CONTEXT.md; ASSUMPTION: scope of demolition governed by IFC drawings.

---

### Phase 3 — Rough-In Construction

**Step 9: Plumbing Rough-In**
- Install new plumbing rough-in (supply lines, drains, vents) per IFC plumbing drawings.
- Connect to existing building plumbing system as shown on IFC drawings.
- Coordinate with plumbing work for DEL-017-03 (Washroom Renovation) and DEL-017-04 (Locker Room) to avoid conflicts in shared building plumbing systems.
- Conduct rough-in inspection: notify Safety Codes Officer and Owner representative; arrange for County representative to be present.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; _DEPENDENCIES.md

**Step 10: Electrical Rough-In**
- Install new electrical rough-in (wiring, conduit, boxes, circuits) per IFC electrical drawings.
- Connect to existing building electrical panel as shown on IFC drawings.
- Conduct rough-in inspection: notify Safety Codes Officer and Owner representative; arrange for County representative to be present.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; _DEPENDENCIES.md

**Step 10A: Gas Fitting Rough-In (Conditional — Lensing: C-001)**
- If the appliance schedule includes gas appliances, install gas fitting rough-in per IFC drawings.
- Conduct gas fitting rough-in inspection: notify Safety Codes Officer and Owner representative; arrange for County representative to be present.
- Source: Alberta Gas Code — location TBD; conditional on appliance schedule.

**Step 10B: Mechanical / HVAC Rough-In (Conditional — Lensing: F-001, F-003)**
- If the design requires kitchen exhaust/ventilation (range hood, ductwork, makeup air), install mechanical rough-in per IFC mechanical drawings.
- Coordinate with PKG-013 (Mechanical & HVAC Installation) if the work connects to or modifies the building's overall HVAC system.
- Source: Alberta Building Code — location TBD; conditional on design requirements.

---

### Phase 4 — Finishes and Installation

**Step 11: Substrate Preparation**
- Prepare wall, floor, and ceiling substrates as required for the specified finish systems.
- Address any substrate deficiencies identified during demolition (e.g., damaged framing, moisture issues).
- If fire-rated assemblies were penetrated during rough-in work, install firestopping per IFC drawings and Alberta Building Code requirements before applying finishes (Lensing: X-001, REQ-013).
- Source: ASSUMPTION: standard commercial renovation practice.

**Step 12: Interior Finishes**
- Apply flooring, wall finishes, and ceiling finishes per the approved Finish Schedule (DEL-001-08).
- Ensure finishes are appropriate for a food preparation and break area (cleanable, durable).
- Source: Specification.md REQ-008; ASSUMPTION: Finish Schedule to be issued by Architect.

**Step 13: Cabinetry Installation**
- Install new cabinetry per the approved design drawings and cabinetry specifications.
- Ensure cabinetry is level, plumb, securely anchored, and consistent with IFC drawings.
- Source: _CONTEXT.md; Specification.md REQ-007.

**Step 14: Fixture and Appliance Installation**
- Install plumbing fixtures (sink, faucet, and associated trim) per IFC plumbing drawings and equipment schedule.
- Install appliances per the approved appliance schedule (appliance list TBD — confirm with Owner during design phase).
- Connect appliances to plumbing, electrical, and gas (if applicable) services.
- Verify functional operation of all installed appliances.
- Source: _CONTEXT.md; Specification.md REQ-007.

---

### Phase 5 — Inspection and Closeout

**Step 15: Trade Inspections**
- Schedule and submit all required inspections (plumbing final, electrical final, gas fitting final if applicable, mechanical ventilation if applicable, building inspection as applicable).
- Ensure the County representative is present at all inspections (RFP §3.3.2).
- Obtain and submit copies of all completed inspection reports to the County (RFP §3.3.2).
- Source: RFP §3.3.2; Specification.md REQ-009.

**Step 16: Punch-List and Deficiency Rectification**
- Conduct a thorough self-inspection of all completed work.
- Generate a punch-list of any outstanding or deficient items.
- Rectify all deficiencies before requesting Owner acceptance.
- Source: RFP §2.14.

**Step 17: Owner Acceptance**
- Request Owner walk-through and acceptance of the completed kitchenette renovation.
- Provide Owner with as-built drawings for plumbing, electrical, and architectural elements (layout, cabinetry, finishes) (Lensing: X-002 — as-built scope expanded to include architectural).
- Confirm that all required documentation has been submitted.
- Source: RFP §2.14.

**Step 18: Warranty Initiation and Project CCC Contribution**
- Confirm that the kitchenette renovation is complete and forms part of the overall project completion documentation for the Construction Completion Certificate (CCC).
- **Warranty deficiency tracking initiation (Lensing: E-002):** At the point of Owner acceptance, initiate warranty deficiency tracking by cross-referencing DEL-021-05 (Guarantee Period Tracking). Specification REQ-011 establishes a two-year post-CCC guarantee period; the operational handoff from construction completion to warranty period must be documented. Notify the Project Manager and DEL-021-05 responsible party that the kitchenette renovation warranty period has commenced.
- Coordinate with DEL-020-02 (Final Inspection and CCC) and DEL-020-03 (Closeout Documentation).
- Source: RFP §2.14; Decomposition PKG-020; Specification.md REQ-011.
