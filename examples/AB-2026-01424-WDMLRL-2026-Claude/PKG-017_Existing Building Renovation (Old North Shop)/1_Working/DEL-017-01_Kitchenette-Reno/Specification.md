## Scope

### What This Specification Covers

This specification governs the complete renovation of the existing 250 sq ft kitchenette area located within the Old North Shop building at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill site (SW 14–44–21–W4, near Ferintosh, Alberta).

The work covered includes:
- Demolition and removal of existing kitchenette fixtures, cabinetry, finishes, and associated plumbing and electrical rough-in (as required to accommodate the new layout)
- Plumbing rough-in updates or extensions to serve the new kitchenette
- Electrical rough-in updates or extensions to serve the new kitchenette
- Supply and installation of new cabinetry
- Supply and installation of appliances
- Application of interior finishes (flooring, wall finish, ceiling finish) per the approved finish schedule
- HVAC/mechanical ventilation modifications if required by the renovation (e.g., kitchen exhaust, makeup air, ductwork modifications) — scope TBD pending design; see REQ-012 (Lensing: F-003)

### What This Specification Excludes

- Design of the kitchenette renovation (governed by PKG-001 Architectural Design and PKG-006 Plumbing Design)
- Renovation of the Old North Shop mezzanine (DEL-017-02)
- Renovation of the Old North Shop washroom (DEL-017-03)
- Construction of the new locker/change room (DEL-017-04)
- New North Shop addition construction (PKG-011 through PKG-016)
- Site civil and utility tie-in work (PKG-018)

- **Source:** RFP §3.3.2
- **Verification:** IFC drawing set must be present and approved prior to commencement of any construction activity.

### REQ-003: Code Compliance
- **Statement:** All work shall adhere to all applicable Alberta Building Codes, regulations, and Alberta Safety Codes in force at the time of construction.
- **Source:** RFP §3.3.2, §3.4
- **Verification:** Building permit obtained (DEL-009-02); Safety Code permits obtained for all applicable trade disciplines — plumbing, electrical, and gas fitting if gas appliances are included in the appliance schedule (DEL-009-03); inspections submitted and reports provided to Owner. (Lensing: C-001 — gas fitting permit added as conditional requirement)
- **Note on permit terminology:** "Building permit" refers to the municipal building permit issued under the Alberta Safety Codes Act. "Safety Code permits" refer to trade-specific permits (plumbing, electrical, gas) also issued under the Alberta Safety Codes Act. Both are governed by the same legislation but are distinct permit instruments. See Guidance.md for clarification. (Lensing: F-002)

### REQ-012: Mechanical Ventilation (Conditional)
- **Statement:** If the kitchenette appliance schedule includes cooking appliances (range, cooktop, or similar), a kitchen exhaust/ventilation system (range hood or equivalent) shall be provided in accordance with the Alberta Building Code. If the renovation modifies the existing HVAC/ductwork layout, makeup air and ductwork modifications shall be designed and installed to maintain code compliance.
- **ASSUMPTION:** This requirement is conditional on the appliance schedule (TBD). Alberta Building Code typically requires mechanical exhaust ventilation for cooking areas. If no cooking appliances are included, this requirement may not apply. Confirm with design team (PKG-001, mechanical consultant). (Lensing: F-001, F-003)
- **Source:** Alberta Building Code — location TBD; _CONTEXT.md (scope narrative does not exclude HVAC).
- **Verification:** Mechanical inspection (if applicable); as-built confirmation.

### REQ-013: Fire Separation Integrity (Conditional)
- **Statement:** If the kitchenette renovation modifies or penetrates fire-rated assemblies (walls, floors, ceilings) within the Old North Shop, fire separation integrity shall be maintained. All penetrations of fire-rated assemblies shall be firestopped in accordance with the Alberta Building Code.
- **ASSUMPTION:** This requirement is conditional on the renovation scope relative to existing fire-rated assemblies, which will be determinable from the IFC drawings and existing conditions survey. An existing-building interior renovation that modifies walls and ceilings may penetrate fire-rated assemblies, triggering code requirements for firestopping. (Lensing: X-001)
- **Source:** Alberta Building Code — location TBD.
- **Verification:** Visual inspection of firestopping; fire separation documentation reviewed against IFC drawings.
