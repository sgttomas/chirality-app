## Purpose

This deliverable exists to improve workforce comfort and operational efficiency at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill maintenance facility by renovating the existing kitchenette in the Old North Shop. The kitchenette serves maintenance workers and operations staff who require a functional break and food preparation area. The RFP explicitly identifies the kitchenette renovation as part of the in-scope building renovation work (RFP §3.4 / SOW-0070), situating it alongside the mezzanine, washroom, and locker room renovations as investments in the overall usability and staff welfare of the existing building.

The renovation supports OBJ-001: delivering a code-compliant, fully functional facility that satisfies the County's operational program (Source: WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §5).

**Functional performance evaluation note (Lensing: D-003):** The documents define code compliance and physical completion as the acceptance basis. However, the stated purpose of the kitchenette — "workforce comfort and operational efficiency" — implies functional performance criteria beyond code compliance alone. The Owner should consider whether additional success criteria apply, such as: user satisfaction metrics, capacity for a defined number of workers, specific appliance functionality requirements, or post-occupancy evaluation. No functional performance criteria are stated in available sources; this is flagged as TBD for Owner ruling.

## Principles

1. **Source-driven design.** The 250 sq ft area limit is an explicit project requirement (RFP §3.4). Any design that enlarges or reduces the kitchenette footprint relative to the existing space requires Owner discussion and potential scope change.

2. **Code compliance is non-negotiable.** All renovation work — demolition, rough-in, finishes, and equipment installation — must comply with Alberta Building Code and Alberta Safety Codes. Permits must be obtained before work begins in the applicable trade disciplines. Note: The governing Alberta Building Code edition is not specified in the RFP (see Specification Standards table, Lensing: A-001); this should be confirmed early to establish the compliance basis.

3. **IFC drawings precede construction.** No physical construction activity should commence without approved, P.Eng.-stamped IFC drawings for the scope of work. This is particularly important for plumbing and electrical modifications that require Safety Code permits.

4. **Existing conditions inform design.** The existing kitchenette layout, fixture conditions, and rough-in locations are not fully documented in available sources. An existing conditions survey (EXISTING-001) must be completed before the design team finalizes the renovation layout, to avoid design-construction conflicts and unnecessary change orders.

5. **Coordinate with related PKG-017 deliverables.** The kitchenette renovation (DEL-017-01), mezzanine renovation (DEL-017-02), washroom renovation (DEL-017-03), and locker room construction (DEL-017-04) all occur within or adjacent to the Old North Shop building. Sequencing, temporary facility access, and shared utility systems should be coordinated across these deliverables to avoid conflicts and minimize disruption to facility operations.

6. **Utility service capacity before rough-in.** The existing plumbing and electrical service to the Old North Shop must be verified for capacity before new rough-in is designed or installed (UTILITY-001). If existing services are undersized, upgrade scope must be identified early to avoid schedule impacts.

7. **Permit terminology clarity (Lensing: F-002).** The Alberta Safety Codes Act governs both the "building permit" and trade-specific "Safety Code permits" (plumbing, electrical, gas). In this project's context: the "building permit" (DEL-009-02) is a permit issued under the Alberta Safety Codes Act for the overall building renovation work. "Safety Code permits" (DEL-009-03) refer to separate trade-specific permits for plumbing, electrical, and gas fitting disciplines. Both are issued under the same legislative framework but are distinct permit instruments with separate application and inspection processes. This distinction should be maintained consistently across all project documents to avoid confusion during the permitting step.

## Considerations

### Scheduling
- The kitchenette renovation is not identified as a hard critical path item for other project deliverables (Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md). However, it must be complete before the overall project CCC (December 31, 2026).
- ASSUMPTION: The renovation can proceed in parallel with other PKG-017 deliverables (mezzanine, washroom, locker room), subject to construction access constraints within the Old North Shop.
- Design work (PKG-001, PKG-006, PKG-004) must be sequenced before construction commencement. If design is delayed, this deliverable has limited float before the December 31, 2026 deadline.
- **Procurement lead times (Lensing: D-002):** Cabinetry and appliance specifications are TBD pending the design phase. Given that the December 31, 2026 deadline is fixed, procurement lead times for these items could independently compress the construction schedule. Long-lead items (custom cabinetry, specialized appliances) should be identified early in the design phase and ordered promptly upon specification approval. The Contractor and design team should establish a procurement timeline that accounts for manufacturing, shipping, and delivery lead times as part of the pre-construction planning process.

### Space and Layout
- The Old North Shop is depicted in Appendix B (Shop) as a 105' x 40' building with a mezzanine 2nd level coffee room, a wash station, and a washroom visible in the conceptual drawing. The kitchenette is a separately identified 250 sq ft area.
- ASSUMPTION: The conceptual drawing (Appendix B) shows the existing building layout. The kitchenette renovation area is distinct from the mezzanine coffee room and the wash station visible on the drawing, though their exact relative positions should be confirmed by the existing conditions survey and IFC drawings. See CF-001 in Conflict Table.
- The 250 sq ft area is a relatively compact space. Efficient layout of cabinetry, appliances, and circulation will be important to maximize functionality.

### Plumbing
- ASSUMPTION: Kitchenette plumbing typically includes a sink with hot and cold supply, drain, and vent connections. The extent of modifications to the existing plumbing rough-in is TBD pending existing conditions survey and design.
- Connection to the existing plumbing system within the Old North Shop must be coordinated with the washroom renovation (DEL-017-03) and the new locker room (DEL-017-04), which also involve plumbing work.
- The project includes a new 2,000 US gallon septic holding tank and 50,000L cistern for the overall facility (SOW-0041, SOW-0043). ASSUMPTION: The kitchenette drain will tie into the building drainage system connecting to the new septic system; confirm during design.

### Electrical
- ASSUMPTION: Kitchenette electrical scope includes circuits for lighting, receptacles, and appliances (refrigerator, microwave, coffee maker, or similar). Specific circuit requirements TBD pending appliance schedule.
- Coordinate with PKG-015 (Electrical Installation) for any service panel changes required to support the renovation.

### HVAC / Mechanical Ventilation (Lensing: F-001, F-003)
- ASSUMPTION: If the kitchenette appliance schedule includes cooking appliances (range, cooktop), a kitchen exhaust/ventilation system (range hood or equivalent) will likely be required under the Alberta Building Code. Makeup air provisions may also be required.
- The Specification and Procedure address plumbing and electrical as renovation trade disciplines, but HVAC/mechanical may also be affected by the kitchenette renovation if the existing ventilation layout is modified (exhaust hood, makeup air, ductwork modifications).
- The design team (PKG-001, mechanical consultant) should determine whether HVAC/mechanical modifications are required and, if so, incorporate them into the IFC drawing set and permit applications.

### Finishes
- The RFP does not specify a finish standard for the kitchenette renovation. ASSUMPTION: Finishes will be practical and durable for an industrial/landfill maintenance facility context (e.g., commercial-grade vinyl or epoxy flooring, painted or tile walls, suspended or painted ceiling). The Architectural Finish Schedule (DEL-001-08) will govern.
- Finishes must be cleanable and appropriate for a food preparation and break area.

### Accessibility / Barrier-Free Design (Lensing: D-001)
- No mention of barrier-free / accessibility requirements appears in any source document for this deliverable. However, the Alberta Building Code may require accessibility provisions depending on the occupancy classification of the Old North Shop and the number of employees served. If accessibility requirements apply, design elements such as counter height, clearances, and accessible sink provisions would need to be incorporated.
- ASSUMPTION: Accessibility applicability is TBD. The Owner / Architect should determine whether barrier-free requirements apply during the design phase, before IFC drawings are finalized.

### Fire Separation (Lensing: X-001)
- An interior renovation that modifies walls and ceilings in an existing building may penetrate fire-rated assemblies, triggering Alberta Building Code requirements for firestopping. The existing conditions survey and IFC drawings should identify any fire-rated assemblies affected by the renovation, and firestopping requirements should be addressed in the construction scope if applicable.

### Workforce Continuity
- ASSUMPTION: The existing kitchenette may be in active use during the construction period. The Contractor should discuss a phasing or temporary accommodation plan with the Owner prior to commencing demolition, to maintain reasonable worker break facilities during construction.
