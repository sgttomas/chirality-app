# Procedure — DEL-013-06: Ceiling Fans

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce, install, commission, and close out the ceiling fan installation in the main shop area (New North Shop) as required by SOW-0040. It covers the Mechanical Contractor's scope from design coordination through final documentation handover.

Source: _CONTEXT.md Scope; Specification.md; SOW-0040.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Engage Mechanical Contractor and Confirm Scope

1.1 Confirm Mechanical Contractor's scope includes supply, installation, and commissioning of 6 ceiling fans in the main shop area per SOW-0040.

1.2 Resolve scope interface with Electrical Contractor (DEL-015): confirm that Electrical Contractor provides dedicated branch circuit(s) to fan junction box locations; Mechanical Contractor installs fan assemblies and connects at junction box. (See Guidance Conflict Table CF-013-06-02 — pending Owner confirmation.)

1.3 Confirm any Mechanical Engineer of Record involvement in fan selection and layout design.

Source: _CONTEXT.md; Guidance CF-013-06-02.

---

### Step 2 — Fan Selection and Equipment Submittal

2.1 Select ceiling fans appropriate for a 35 ft industrial ceiling in a maintenance shop environment. Consider HVLS (High Volume Low Speed) or equivalent industrial-grade fans. (See Guidance P2, Considerations.) Ensure selected fans bear CSA, UL, or equivalent product safety certification per Specification REQ-013-06-08 (X-004).

2.2 Prepare and submit equipment submittals to Owner/Engineer for review, including:
   - Manufacturer product data sheets
   - Fan dimensions, blade diameter, motor data, weight
   - Structural attachment method and hardware specifications
   - Electrical data (voltage, full-load amps, circuit requirements)
   - Mounting/downrod configuration for 35 ft ceiling
   - Control options (speed control, direction reversal)
   - Noise rating data
   - NEMA or IP rating confirming suitability for shop environment (E-003)
   - CSA/UL or equivalent product safety certification documentation (X-004)
   - Manufacturer-specified vibration limits for balancing acceptance (F-001)
   - Manufacturer-specified fastener torque values for mounting hardware (A-005)

2.3 Obtain Owner/Engineer written approval of submittals before ordering equipment. **Do not proceed with equipment procurement until written submittal approval is confirmed (D-002).**

Source: Specification REQ-013-06-08; Specification Documentation section.

---

### Step 3 — Fan Layout Design and Drawing

3.1 Determine individual fan positions in the main shop area considering:
   - Even coverage of the ~13,000 sq ft shop footprint (RFP §3.1, R-01)
   - Minimum clearance from 5-tonne overhead crane operating envelopes (IFC crane drawings, DEL-016)
   - Minimum clearance from high-bay lighting fixtures (5 rows x 4 lights, App B-Elec, R-05)
   - Alignment with structural ceiling attachment points (IFC structural drawings, DEL-002) — note: ceiling structure type (concrete vs. steel purlin/beam) is TBD per Conflict Table CF-013-06-03; mounting hardware depends on structure type
   - Makeup air supply diffuser and exhaust extract locations (DEL-013-02, DEL-013-03, DEL-013-04)

3.2 Obtain Structural Engineer of Record written approval or stamped drawing confirming that ceiling attachment points can support fan dead load plus dynamic loading for each proposed mounting location.

**--- HOLD POINT (F-003) ---**
**Do not proceed to Step 5 (Installation) without documented structural engineer approval from Step 3.2. Structural approval documentation must be on file before any fan mounting hardware is installed.**

3.3 Show fan positions, mounting details, and downrod lengths on IFC mechanical or electrical drawings. Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed in Alberta.

3.4 Confirm final fan location coordinates with Electrical Contractor so branch circuit rough-in (DEL-015) targets the correct ceiling positions.

Source: Specification REQ-013-06-02, REQ-013-06-05, REQ-013-06-06, REQ-013-06-07; RFP §3.3.2 (R-01).

---

### Step 4 — Electrical Rough-In Coordination

4.1 Coordinate with Electrical Contractor (DEL-015) to ensure branch circuit rough-in (conduit, wire, junction boxes) is completed to each of the 6 fan locations prior to ceiling structure closure (if applicable) and prior to crane installation.

4.2 Confirm junction box type, mounting, and rating are compatible with fan assembly mounting hardware (fan-rated junction box required).

4.3 Confirm control wiring routing for wall switches or control panel.

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md; Guidance P5; App B-Elec (R-05).

---

### Step 5 — Fan Installation

5.1 Confirm all prerequisites are met: structural approval obtained (Step 3.2 — HOLD POINT confirmed), electrical rough-in complete (Step 4), Safety Code permits in hand (electrical and mechanical as applicable per C-002), equipment submittals approved (Step 2.3 — procurement authorization confirmed per D-002).

5.2 Install mounting brackets or HVLS mounting frames at each approved structural attachment point per manufacturer instructions and approved structural drawings.

5.3 Install downrods of length appropriate to achieve target operating height. Confirm blade tip clearance above floor level meets code minimum (value TBD per Specification REQ-013-06-07) and above crane operating envelope (value TBD per Specification REQ-013-06-06).

5.4 Install fan motor and blade assemblies per manufacturer instructions.

5.5 Connect fan to branch circuit at junction box. Electrical connection to be performed or supervised by qualified electrician in accordance with AEC/CEC.

5.6 Install speed controls and direction switches at designated wall locations.

5.7 Perform visual inspection of all mounting hardware, fastener torque (verify against manufacturer-specified torque values per A-005), blade attachment, and electrical connections before energisation.

Source: Specification REQ-013-06-04, REQ-013-06-05, REQ-013-06-06, REQ-013-06-07; manufacturer instructions (TBD).

---

### Step 6 — Safety Code Inspection

6.1 Request electrical Safety Code inspection(s) as required for fan wiring and connections.

6.2 If mechanical Safety Code inspection is required (TBD per C-002 and X-005), request and complete mechanical inspection before proceeding to commissioning. PROPOSAL: Confirm with Safety Codes Officer whether mechanical permit/inspection is required for ceiling-mounted fan installation in Alberta.

6.3 Ensure County project representative is present for inspections per RFP §3.3.2 (R-01).

6.4 Provide Owner with copies of all completed inspection reports per RFP §3.3.2 (R-01).

6.5 Resolve any deficiencies identified during inspection before proceeding to commissioning.

Source: RFP §3.3.2 (R-01); Specification REQ-013-06-04.

---

### Step 7 — Commissioning and Balancing

7.1 Energise fans one at a time. Confirm correct rotation direction for each operating mode (summer — downward airflow; winter — upward/destratification mode). Note: Whether direction reversal is mandatory or recommended is TBD per Specification REQ-013-06-11 (C-001). If direction reversal is confirmed as mandatory, both modes must be demonstrated. If recommended only, test both modes if equipment supports it but do not treat absence of reversal as a failure.

7.2 Test all speed settings for each fan. Confirm speed control operates correctly.

7.3 Balance each fan per manufacturer balancing instructions. Measure vibration at all speeds. Vibration shall not exceed manufacturer-specified limits (vibration acceptance threshold TBD per Specification REQ-013-06-12 / F-001). Adjust blade pitch or add balance weights as required.

7.4 Run all 6 fans simultaneously. Observe for any interference with co-located HVAC systems (heating, makeup air, air exchange, exhaust systems). **Assess against defined adverse interaction indicators (X-003):** TBD — indicators should include, at minimum: exhaust capture efficiency reduction at welding and equipment exhaust hoods, thermostat cycling instability, and pressure imbalance at exhaust hoods. Record observations.

7.5 Measure noise levels using defined measurement protocol (TBD per Specification REQ-013-06-10 / A-002, X-002). Confirm noise does not exceed specified dBA threshold at defined measurement point.

7.6 Run fans with overhead cranes operating (if safe and practical, and if cranes are installed per C-003 prerequisite) to confirm no interaction or clearance issues. If cranes are not yet installed at time of fan commissioning, document that this test is deferred and schedule for completion when cranes are operational.

7.7 Document all commissioning results (Steps 7.1-7.6) in the Commissioning Report. Include photographic documentation per Specification Documentation (X-006).

Source: _CONTEXT.md Scope ("Balancing and speed verification; Performance testing and commissioning"); Specification REQ-013-06-03, REQ-013-06-09, REQ-013-06-10, REQ-013-06-11, REQ-013-06-12.

---

### Step 8 — Documentation and Handover

8.1 Prepare as-built drawings showing final fan locations, mounting details, downrod lengths, and electrical connection points. As-built drawings supersede IFC Layout Drawings and must reflect all field changes (E-002).

8.2 Compile O&M manuals from fan manufacturer, including:
   - Operating instructions (speed settings, direction reversal)
   - Recommended maintenance schedule (blade cleaning, fastener check, motor service intervals)
   - Maintenance access method and recommended access equipment for 35 ft ceiling height (E-004)
   - Spare parts list
   - Warranty documentation

8.3 Assemble complete close-out package:
   - Commissioning Report
   - As-built drawings
   - O&M manuals (including maintenance access method per E-004)
   - Structural approval documentation
   - Safety Code inspection reports (electrical and mechanical as applicable per C-002)
   - Equipment submittals (approved set)
   - Installation photographs (X-006)
   - Fan warranty certificates (per Specification REQ-013-06-13 / F-002)

8.4 Submit close-out package to Owner.

8.5 Confirm with Owner/Project Manager that all milestones for this deliverable are satisfied and obtain sign-off.

Source: Specification Documentation section; _STATUS.md milestones; RFP §2.14 (R-01) Completion and Acceptance.
