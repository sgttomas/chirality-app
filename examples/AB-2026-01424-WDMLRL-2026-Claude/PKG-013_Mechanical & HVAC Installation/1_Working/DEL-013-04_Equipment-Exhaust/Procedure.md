# Procedure — DEL-013-04: Heavy Equipment Exhaust System

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to be followed by the Mechanical Contractor to install, test, and commission the heavy equipment exhaust system for the New North Shop repair bays. The procedure covers pre-installation readiness, field installation sequence, coordination activities, commissioning tests, and required records.

Source: `_CONTEXT.md`; SOW-0038; R-01 §3.4; OBJ-004.

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Pre-Installation and Layout

**Step 1 — Confirm Scope and Drawings Review**
1. Review approved IFC exhaust system drawings (DEL-003-04) and equipment schedule (DEL-003-05) with the site foreman and project manager.
2. Confirm that the drawings reflect the current building conditions (ceiling height, structural framing, crane runway locations, door locations).
3. Identify any drawing conflicts with field conditions; issue RFI if conflicts exist. Do not proceed until conflicts are resolved.
4. Confirm electrical circuit locations for exhaust fans with the electrical contractor.
5. Confirm controls functional specification is available (Specification REQ-012); if not available, issue RFI.
6. Confirm ductwork material specification (Specification REQ-015) and duct leakage testing requirements (Specification REQ-018).

Source: R-01 §3.3.2; standard mechanical installation practice (ASSUMPTION). Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-003, X-002, E-001.

---

**Step 2 — Material and Equipment Procurement**
1. Procure exhaust hose drops (or retractable hose reel assemblies), exhaust fans, ductwork materials (per specified material — Specification REQ-015), duct supports, access panels, filtration components (if required per CQ-002 resolution), exhaust outlet/stack components, and controls hardware per approved submittals.
2. Submit product data/submittals to the Mechanical Engineer for approval prior to ordering non-standard or long-lead items.
3. If any approved product becomes unavailable, submit a formal substitution request per Specification REQ-016 before procuring alternate products. Do not install substituted products without engineering approval.
4. Stage materials on site in a protected area.

Source: `_CONTEXT.md` Scope; standard procurement practice (ASSUMPTION). Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-003.

---

**Step 3 — Layout and Marking**
1. Using the approved drawings, mark out on the shop floor and ceiling structure the locations of:
   - Exhaust hose drop positions at each repair bay equipment position.
   - Ductwork centrelines and support hanger locations.
   - Fan mounting location.
   - Exhaust outlet/stack penetration through the building envelope.
   - Access panel locations along ductwork runs.
2. Confirm overhead clearances are maintained for crane travel and meet code requirements.
3. Obtain field authorization or confirmation from the site superintendent before making any permanent structural attachments or penetrations.

Source: Appendix B (Shop) floor plan; DEL-003-04 (IFC drawings — prerequisite).

---

### Phase 2 — Installation

**Step 4 — Install Ductwork Supports and Hangers**
1. Install ductwork support hangers from structural framing at spacing intervals per the approved drawings and applicable code requirements.
2. Verify that hanger anchors are attached to structural members (not to non-structural elements such as roof deck only), consistent with the structural engineer's guidance.
3. Confirm crane runway clearances are maintained.
4. **Photo documentation:** Photograph duct support attachment to structure at representative locations before concealment. Retain photos in project record (see Records).

Source: ASSUMPTION — standard ductwork installation practice; structural framing from PKG-002. Photo requirement per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-004.

---

**Step 5 — Install Exhaust Ductwork**
1. Install rigid ductwork from collection point(s) to exhaust fan and from fan to outlet, per approved drawings. Ductwork material shall be as specified (Specification REQ-015).
2. Install ductwork with positive slope toward drain points if condensate management is required by the mechanical specification (see Guidance CQ-004 for decision status). If condensate management is not required, install level or per drawing.
3. Install access panels at required intervals for cleaning and inspection; minimum panel spacing per applicable standard (per IFC drawings).
4. Seal all duct joints with approved sealant per the mechanical specification.
5. Install ductwork penetrations through the building envelope and through any fire-rated assemblies with fire-rated sleeves, dampers, or sealed assemblies as required by the National Fire Code of Canada and National Building Code of Canada (Specification REQ-017). Fire rating of penetration assembly shall match the fire resistance rating of the assembly being penetrated.
6. **Photo documentation:** Photograph penetration sealing at fire-rated assemblies before concealment by finishes. Retain photos in project record (see Records).

Source: `_CONTEXT.md` Scope; REQ-008 (cleanability); REQ-017 (fire-rated penetrations); standard practice (ASSUMPTION). Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md C-002, C-003, X-004.

---

**Step 6 — Install Exhaust Hose Drops / Hose Reel Assemblies**
1. Install exhaust hose drops (or retractable hose reel assemblies, per design specification) at each equipment position per the approved drawing locations.
2. Connect hose assembly inlets to the exhaust duct collection manifold per manufacturer instructions.
3. Ensure hose reach length is sufficient to connect to the furthest expected equipment tailpipe position in each bay — confirm with Mechanical Engineer if uncertain.
4. Install any required guide tracks, pulleys, or mounting brackets per manufacturer specifications.
5. **Photo documentation:** Photograph each completed exhaust hose drop installation. Retain photos in project record (see Records).

Source: R-01 §3.4 — "exhaust hoses and fans"; manufacturer installation instructions (TBD — specific manufacturer not confirmed in source material). Photo requirement per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-004.

---

**Step 7 — Install Exhaust Fan(s)**
1. Install exhaust fan(s) at the location(s) shown on the approved drawings, per the manufacturer's installation instructions.
2. Mount fans on vibration-isolation pads or supports as specified.
3. Make mechanical connections (duct flanges, flex connectors) between fan inlet/outlet and ductwork.
4. Coordinate with the electrical contractor for fan power connections, starter/VFD installation (if applicable), and controls wiring.
5. Do not energize fans until electrical connections are confirmed complete by the electrical contractor.

Source: R-01 §3.4 — "fans"; `_DEPENDENCIES.md` — electrical coordination.

---

**Step 8 — Install Filtration System (if required)**
1. If filtration is required per the mechanical design and environmental compliance determination (TBD — see Guidance CQ-002), install the filtration unit at the location specified in the IFC drawings.
2. Install per manufacturer instructions; confirm filter media type matches the specification.
3. Ensure filter housing includes access for media replacement.

Source: `_CONTEXT.md` Scope — "Filtration and treatment systems installation"; REQ-010; conditional on CQ-002 resolution.

---

**Step 9 — Install Exhaust Outlet and Stack**
1. Install the exhaust outlet and stack at the building penetration location per approved drawings.
2. Flash and seal the penetration against weather infiltration per applicable building code requirements.
3. Install bird/rain screens or cowl cap as specified.
4. Confirm outlet height and setback distance meets environmental permit conditions and code requirements (REQ-005).

Source: `_CONTEXT.md` Scope — "Exhaust outlet and stack installation"; REQ-005.

---

**Step 10 — Install Controls and Monitoring**
1. Install controls and monitoring components as specified in the approved mechanical drawings and controls functional specification (Specification REQ-012).
2. Coordinate with the electrical contractor for power and signal wiring.
3. Label all controls components per the mechanical drawings.
4. Confirm that interlock logic, alarm functions, and status indication are wired per the functional specification before commissioning.

Source: `_CONTEXT.md` Scope — "Controls and monitoring"; Specification REQ-012. Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-003.

### Phase 2A — Regulatory Inspection Hold Points

**Step 10A — Regulatory Inspection Hold Points**

The following mandatory inspection hold points require notification of the Alberta Safety Codes Officer and/or the authority having jurisdiction. Work beyond each hold point shall not proceed until the inspection is completed or waived in writing.

| Hold Point | Stage | Notification To | Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| HP-01 | Before concealment of duct penetrations through fire-rated assemblies | Safety Codes Officer | After Step 5.5, before finishes are applied |
| HP-02 | After penetration sealing, before ductwork is enclosed by ceiling or wall finishes | Safety Codes Officer / Inspector | After all penetrations sealed (Step 5), before concealment |
| HP-03 | Pre-commissioning inspection | Safety Codes Officer; Project Manager | After Step 11, before Step 12 (system start-up) |

**Note:** Additional hold points may be required by the Safety Codes Officer or the authority having jurisdiction. The Project Manager shall confirm required hold points during the permitting process. ASSUMPTION: the hold points above represent minimum recommended hold points; actual regulatory requirements TBD pending permit conditions.

Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-004; Specification REQ-004 verification references "Inspection by Alberta Safety Codes Officer" but prior Procedure did not sequence when this occurs.

---

**Step 11 — Pre-Commissioning Inspection and Leak Test**
1. Perform a visual inspection of all installed ductwork, connections, and equipment.
2. Confirm all access panels are installed and operable.
3. Perform duct leakage test per Specification REQ-018:
   - If leakage testing is required: perform test per the specified method, at the specified test pressure, and compare to the maximum allowable leakage rate. Document results.
   - If leakage testing has been waived: confirm the documented waiver is in the project record.
4. Confirm all penetration seals are in place (including fire-rated penetrations per REQ-017).
5. Confirm crane clearances are maintained throughout installed ductwork run.
6. Document all deficiencies found. Classify deficiencies by severity (see Deficiency Management Note below). Issue corrective actions for all deficiencies; rectify before proceeding to commissioning.

Source: Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md E-001, E-003.

### Deficiency Management Note

All deficiencies identified during pre-commissioning inspection (Step 11), commissioning (Steps 12-15), or final walkthrough (Step 16) shall be recorded in the **Deficiency List**. The Deficiency List is the single master record of all identified deficiencies. If a deficiency requires design clarification, an RFI shall be issued as a separate instrument referencing the Deficiency List entry. If a deficiency constitutes a non-conformance (deviation from approved design or specification), a non-conformance report shall be issued referencing the Deficiency List entry.

**Deficiency severity classification:**

| Severity | Definition | Response |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | Safety hazard or code violation; prevents commissioning | Stop work in affected area; issue NCR; rectify before proceeding |
| Major | Deviation from specification that affects system performance | Issue NCR; rectify before commissioning sign-off |
| Minor | Cosmetic or documentation issue; does not affect performance | Record in Deficiency List; rectify before final completion |

Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md E-003; aligns Procedure Step 11.6 language ("RFI or non-conformance report") with Records table ("Deficiency List and Close-Out") by establishing the Deficiency List as the master record with RFIs and NCRs as supplementary instruments.

### Phase 3 — Commissioning and Testing

**Step 12 — System Start-Up**
1. Prior to system start-up, confirm with the electrical contractor that power circuits are energized and correct voltage is available at the fan disconnect.
2. Confirm Hold Point HP-03 (pre-commissioning inspection) has been completed or waived.
3. Start the fan and confirm correct rotation direction per manufacturer instructions.
4. Measure noise level (dBA) at the nearest operator position and vibration at the fan; compare against acceptance criteria per Specification REQ-014. If criteria are not yet defined, record measurements for later comparison and flag as TBD.
5. Confirm controls respond correctly per the controls functional specification (REQ-012): fan on/off, interlock logic, status indication, alarm functions.

Source: OBJ-004 — "commission all mechanical... systems to operational readiness." Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-003, F-003.

---

**Step 13 — Airflow Balancing and Performance Test**
1. With the system running, measure airflow (CFM) at each exhaust hose drop and at the fan discharge, using calibrated test instruments. Document results.
2. Compare measured values against the design values in DEL-003-05 (Equipment Schedule) and DEL-003-04 (Exhaust Plans).
3. Adjust balancing dampers (if installed) to achieve design airflow distribution.
4. Confirm that fan motor amperage is within nameplate rating.
5. Document all test readings in the commissioning report per the format specified in Specification REQ-011.

Source: `_CONTEXT.md` Scope — "Performance testing and commissioning"; Specification REQ-011. Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md E-002.

---

**Step 14 — Exhaust Capture Effectiveness Test**
1. With the system running and an exhaust hose connected to a simulated or actual tailpipe source (if available at time of commissioning), perform a smoke pencil or tracer gas test at the hose connection point to confirm that exhaust is captured and not escaping into the shop space.
2. Measure capture velocity (FPM) at each hose connection point using calibrated instrument; compare against minimum capture velocity per Specification REQ-013.
3. Document results; confirm acceptable capture performance at design airflow.

Source: Specification REQ-003; REQ-011; REQ-013. Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-001.

---

**Step 15 — Environmental Compliance Verification**
1. Confirm that the exhaust outlet discharge does not direct exhaust toward building air intakes, occupied areas, or property boundaries in a manner that violates regulatory requirements.
2. Perform re-entrainment field test per the method defined in Specification REQ-005 acceptance criterion (method TBD — to be defined before commissioning).
3. If an environmental permit or approval was required, confirm the approved condition(s) are satisfied and place a copy of the permit in the project records.

Source: Specification REQ-005; `_DEPENDENCIES.md` External Dependencies. Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-001.

---

**Step 16 — Final Walkthrough and Owner Demonstration**
1. Walk the County project representative through the installed system; demonstrate fan operation, exhaust hose drop operation, and controls.
2. Provide the County representative with O&M manuals for all installed equipment.
3. Confirm all commissioning test results have been documented and reviewed per the commissioning report format (Specification REQ-011).
4. Obtain sign-off from the County representative or project manager.

Source: R-01 §2.14; OBJ-004.

---

**Step 16A — As-Built Documentation**
1. Prepare as-built mark-up drawings documenting:
   - Duct routing deviations from IFC drawings (if any).
   - Actual exhaust hose drop locations (with dimensions from reference points).
   - Actual exhaust outlet and stack position.
   - Any field changes approved by RFI during installation.
2. Submit as-built mark-ups to the Project Manager within the timeframe specified in the contract (ASSUMPTION: within 30 days of substantial completion — to be confirmed per CCDC 14-2013 requirements).
3. Format: marked-up IFC drawings (paper or digital per Project Manager direction).

Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-002; Specification Documentation "Installation Record" row.

---

**Step 17 — Operator Training and Maintenance Handover**
1. Provide formal operator training to County maintenance staff covering:
   - Daily system operation: exhaust hose drop connection procedure, system start/stop, hose stowage.
   - Alarm response: actions to take on fan failure alarm, filter differential pressure alarm (if applicable), or abnormal noise/vibration.
   - Basic troubleshooting for common issues (hose retraction failure, reduced suction).
2. Provide a written maintenance schedule to the County including:
   - Recommended filter replacement intervals (if filtration is installed).
   - Exhaust hose inspection and replacement criteria.
   - Fan bearing lubrication schedule.
   - Duct cleaning schedule.
   - Access panel inspection frequency.
3. Confirm that O&M manuals include maintenance procedures for all installed components.
4. Obtain sign-off from the County representative confirming training was completed.

Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-004; Step 16 included owner demonstration but did not address formal operator training or maintenance schedule handover. This step closes the operational completeness gap for a continuously operating facility.
