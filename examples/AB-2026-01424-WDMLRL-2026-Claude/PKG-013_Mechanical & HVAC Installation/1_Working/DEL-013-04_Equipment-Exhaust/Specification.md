# Specification — DEL-013-04: Heavy Equipment Exhaust System

---

## Scope

### In Scope

This specification covers the supply and installation of the heavy equipment exhaust system for the New North Shop of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition, as required by SOW-0038 and RFP §3.4.

The scope includes:

1. Exhaust hose drops (flexible hoses) installed at heavy equipment positions within the repair bays.
2. Exhaust fans to mechanically convey captured exhaust to the exterior.
3. Ductwork from collection points to exhaust fans and to the exhaust outlet/stack; material specification per SMACNA Industrial Duct Construction Standards or as specified by Mechanical Engineer (TBD — see REQ-015).
4. Exhaust collection hoods or connection points at equipment tailpipe capture locations.
5. Filtration and/or treatment systems as required by equipment type and applicable environmental and safety standards (subject to CQ-002 resolution — see Guidance Conflict Table).
6. Exhaust outlet(s) and stack penetration(s) through the building envelope.
7. Controls and monitoring for the exhaust system (functional specification per REQ-012).
8. Performance testing and commissioning of the complete system.
9. Coordination with mechanical design (PKG-003, DEL-003-04 Exhaust Plans) and with related systems (DEL-013-02, DEL-013-03, DEL-013-01, DEL-013-05).

Source: `_CONTEXT.md` Scope; SOW-0038; R-01 §3.4.

### Out of Scope

- Welding station ventilation/exhaust system (covered under DEL-013-05).
- Ceiling fans (covered under DEL-013-06).
- Electrical power circuits for fans (covered under DEL-015-04, PKG-015).
- Mechanical design drawings and calculations (covered under PKG-003, DEL-003-04).
- Equipment procurement of heavy machinery (out of project scope per decomposition).

## Requirements

### REQ-001 — Exhaust Capture at Source
**Requirement:** Exhaust hose drops and fan equipment shall be installed at heavy equipment positions within the repair bays to capture exhaust directly at or near the tailpipe source.

**Basis:** R-01 §3.4 — "Exhaust hoses and fans for heavy equipment"; SOW-0038 — "Install exhaust hoses and fans for heavy equipment in repair bays."

**Priority:** Mandatory.

---

### REQ-002 — Repair Bay Coverage
**Requirement:** The system shall serve the two drive-through repair bays shown in Appendix B (Shop) floor plan. The number of exhaust hose drops per bay shall be determined by the mechanical design to accommodate the range of equipment operated at the facility.

**Basis:** R-01 §3.1 (two drive-through repair bays); Appendix B (Shop) floor plan.

**Note:** Exact hose drop quantity and positioning TBD — to be specified in DEL-003-04 Exhaust Plans.

### REQ-003 — Safe Removal of Hazardous Exhaust
**Requirement:** The system shall safely capture and remove fumes and particulates generated by heavy equipment operation, preventing accumulation of hazardous concentrations within the occupied shop space. The system design shall ensure that worker exposure to exhaust contaminants (including CO, NO2, and diesel particulate matter) remains below applicable occupational exposure limits (OELs) as defined by Alberta OHS Code Part 25 (ASSUMPTION: Part 25 Table 4 TLVs are the applicable limits — specific values TBD pending standard access; location TBD).

**Basis:** `_CONTEXT.md` Key Requirements — "Safe capture of equipment exhaust"; "Proper fume and particulate handling." Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md C-001.

**Priority:** Mandatory.

### REQ-005 — Environmental and Safety Compliance
**Requirement:** The exhaust outlet location, stack height, and discharge characteristics shall comply with applicable environmental protection regulations and safety requirements. The outlet shall be positioned to prevent re-entrainment of exhaust into building air intakes or occupied areas. An environmental permit or regulatory approval artifact must be obtained (or a documented determination that no permit is required) before outlet construction proceeds.

**Acceptance criterion for re-entrainment:** TBD — a field test method (e.g., smoke release at outlet during building air intake operation) and acceptance threshold must be defined by the Mechanical Engineer or Environmental Reviewer. The test shall confirm that exhaust discharge does not re-enter building air intakes at concentrations exceeding applicable OELs.

**Basis:** `_CONTEXT.md` Key Requirements — "Environmental and safety compliance"; "Proper outlet location and height"; `_REFERENCES.md` — "Environmental protection agency regulations." Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-003, D-001.

**Note:** Specific regulatory references (Alberta Environment; EPA equivalent) TBD pending environmental compliance review.

### REQ-006 — Air Balance Coordination
**Requirement:** The exhaust volume removed by this system shall be communicated to the mechanical engineer responsible for the forced-air makeup system (DEL-013-03) to ensure adequate makeup air is provided to replace exhausted air and maintain building pressure balance.

**Basis:** `_DEPENDENCIES.md` Critical Integration Points — "Exhaust volume affects makeup air requirements."

**Priority:** Mandatory for system coordination.

---

### REQ-007 — Heat Recovery Interface (ASSUMPTION)
**Requirement (ASSUMPTION):** The design shall evaluate whether waste heat from equipment exhaust can be recovered and integrated with the high-recovery heating system (DEL-013-01). If recovery is viable, the exhaust system design shall accommodate the interface.

**Basis:** `_DEPENDENCIES.md` Internal Package Dependencies — "Heat recovery from exhaust"; labeled ASSUMPTION as this is stated as a potential rather than a confirmed requirement.

---

### REQ-008 — Cleanability and Maintainability
**Requirement:** The exhaust system, including ductwork and filtration components, shall be designed for routine cleaning and maintenance access. Access panels or openings shall be provided at intervals consistent with applicable duct maintenance standards.

**Basis:** `_DEPENDENCIES.md` Constraint Notes — "System must be cleanable and maintainable."

---

### REQ-009 — Fan Sizing Based on Equipment Exhaust Volume
**Requirement:** Exhaust fan(s) shall be sized by the mechanical engineer to accommodate the exhaust volume of the heaviest equipment served, with appropriate safety factor. Fan selection shall be documented in the mechanical equipment schedule (DEL-003-05).

**Basis:** `_DEPENDENCIES.md` Constraint Notes — "Exhaust volume determines fan sizing."

---

### REQ-010 — Filtration Requirements
**Requirement:** Filtration type and rating shall be determined based on the classification of exhaust contaminants generated by the specific equipment types operated at the facility. Filtration specifications shall be confirmed during mechanical design. Note: the inclusion of filtration in scope is subject to open question CQ-002 (see Guidance Conflict Table) — `_CONTEXT.md` Scope lists "Filtration and treatment systems installation" as in-scope, while R-01 §3.4 specifies only "exhaust hoses and fans" without mentioning filtration.

**Basis:** `_DEPENDENCIES.md` Constraint Notes — "Filtration requirements based on equipment type"; `_CONTEXT.md` Scope — "Filtration and treatment systems installation." Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-002.

### REQ-011 — Commissioning and Performance Testing
**Requirement:** Upon installation completion, the system shall be tested and commissioned to verify that:
- Exhaust capture velocity at hose connection points meets design specification (see REQ-013).
- Fan performance (airflow, static pressure) meets design specification.
- No exhaust recirculation into the building occurs (re-entrainment test per REQ-005).
- Controls and monitoring function as designed (per REQ-012).
- Noise and vibration are within acceptable limits (per REQ-014).

**Commissioning report format:** The commissioning report shall include, at minimum: system identification, date, personnel, test instruments used, measured airflow at each exhaust hose drop, fan static pressure, capture velocity measurements, re-entrainment test results, controls function confirmation, noise/vibration observations, pass/fail determination per each requirement, and sign-off. Report format/template TBD — to be defined by Mechanical Engineer or Commissioning Agent before testing.

**Basis:** `_CONTEXT.md` Scope — "Performance testing and commissioning"; OBJ-004 — "Install and commission all mechanical... systems to operational readiness." Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md E-002.

### REQ-013 — Minimum Capture Velocity
**Requirement:** Exhaust hose drops shall achieve a minimum capture velocity (face velocity in feet per minute, FPM) at the hose connection point sufficient to capture exhaust under operating conditions. The minimum capture velocity shall be specified by the Mechanical Engineer per ACGIH Industrial Ventilation Manual guidelines for the applicable source type (ASSUMPTION: vehicle exhaust in enclosed space — location TBD in ACGIH manual).

**Basis:** _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-001 — REQ-011 verification requires confirming capture velocity but no numeric threshold existed. This requirement establishes the measurable threshold.

**Note:** Specific capture velocity value TBD — to be determined by Mechanical Engineer.

---

### REQ-014 — Noise and Vibration Limits
**Requirement:** Exhaust fan operation shall not produce noise levels exceeding the applicable limit at the nearest operator position within the shop. Maximum noise level (dBA) and acceptable vibration amplitude TBD — to be specified by the Mechanical Engineer per equipment manufacturer data and applicable workplace noise regulations (ASSUMPTION: Alberta OHS Code Part 16 Noise Exposure governs workplace noise limits — location TBD).

**Basis:** _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-003 — Procedure Step 12.3 references "unusual vibration, noise" but no quantitative threshold existed. This requirement establishes the measurable acceptance criterion.

**Note:** Specific dBA and vibration values TBD — to be determined by Mechanical Engineer.

### REQ-015 — Ductwork Material Specification
**Requirement:** Ductwork material shall be specified by the Mechanical Engineer. Material shall be suitable for the exhaust contaminants present and comply with applicable fire and building codes. ASSUMPTION: galvanized steel per SMACNA Industrial Duct Construction Standards is typical for industrial exhaust applications — to be confirmed by Mechanical Engineer.

**Basis:** _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-002 — no prior document specified duct material; this is a verified procedural imperative that cannot be executed without material specification.

**Note:** Material specification TBD — Mechanical Engineer to specify.

---

### REQ-016 — Product Substitution Control
**Requirement:** If approved products (per approved submittals) become unavailable after submittal approval, a formal substitution request with engineering review shall be submitted and approved by the Mechanical Engineer before installation of alternate products. The substitution request shall demonstrate equivalent performance, code compliance, and compatibility with the approved system design.

**Basis:** _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md D-003 — Specification Documentation requires product submittals for approval but did not address the substitution scenario.

---

### REQ-017 — Fire-Rated Penetrations
**Requirement:** All ductwork penetrations through fire-rated assemblies (fire separations, fire walls) shall be installed with fire-rated sleeves, dampers, or sealed assemblies as required by the National Fire Code of Canada (NFC) and the National Building Code of Canada (NBC), Alberta adopted editions. The fire rating of the penetration assembly shall match the fire resistance rating of the assembly being penetrated.

**Basis:** _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md C-002 — Procedure Step 5.5 references fire-rated penetrations but no corresponding specification requirement existed and the Verification table did not include a fire code check. This requirement closes that gap.

---

### REQ-018 — Duct Leakage Testing
**Requirement:** The Mechanical Engineer shall determine during design whether duct leakage testing is required based on the exhaust classification and applicable standards (e.g., SMACNA leakage class). If required, the specification shall define: test method, test pressure, and maximum allowable leakage rate. If the Mechanical Engineer determines that leakage testing is not required, the determination shall be documented.

**Basis:** _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md E-001 — Procedure Step 11.3 contains conditional TBD language for duct leakage testing. This requirement ensures a definitive determination is made.
