# Specification — DEL-013-03: Forced-Air Makeup System

---

## Scope

### Included

This specification governs the procurement, installation, and commissioning of the forced-air makeup air system (MUA) for the New North Shop Addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop, Camrose County, Alberta.

**Note on normative authority:** The MUA system is sourced from RFP Appendix B (App B), which explicitly lists "Forced Air Makeup" as a building feature. RFP §3.4 does not name makeup air explicitly (it lists heating, air exchanger, exhaust, and ventilation at welding), creating an ambiguity regarding whether this deliverable is a §3.4 design requirement or an App B addition only. This ambiguity is recorded in Guidance.md Conflict Table (CFT-013-03-01) and is pending human ruling. For purposes of this Specification, the MUA system is treated as IN scope per App B as a co-governing source alongside §3.4. (Enrichment: A-001)

Work included under this deliverable:
- Supply and delivery of makeup air unit(s) to site
- Installation of the MUA unit at the designated location (Utility Room area — TBD per design)
- Fresh air intake ductwork from exterior to MUA unit, including exterior wall penetration
- Supply ductwork from MUA unit throughout the facility to all distribution points
- Dampers and control valves at intake, supply junctions, and terminal points
- Fire and smoke dampers at ductwork penetrations through fire-rated assemblies (Enrichment: C-002)
- Intake filtration — filter installation and initial filter set per manufacturer specification (Enrichment: C-003)
- Controls integration with the primary heating system (DEL-013-01) and air exchanger (DEL-013-02)
- Electrical disconnect switch and lockout/tagout provisions (Enrichment: X-002)
- Performance testing and commissioning of the complete MUA system
- Provision of as-built documentation and O&M manuals

Sources: _CONTEXT.md Scope; SOW-0037 (Decomposition); Enrichments A-001, C-002, C-003, X-002

### Excluded

- Primary heating system equipment (DEL-013-01)
- High-volume air exchanger equipment (DEL-013-02)
- Heavy equipment exhaust fans and hoses (DEL-013-04)
- Welding station exhaust system (DEL-013-05)
- Ceiling fans (DEL-013-06)
- Utility Room construction (DEL-012-02 — must be complete or concurrent per dependency)
- Electrical power supply circuits to MUA unit (ASSUMPTION: covered under DEL-015 Electrical Installation)
- Natural gas service to building (SOW-0080 — utility tie-in)
- Mechanical design drawings and specifications (DEL-003 series — design deliverables, not installation)

## Requirements

### REQ-013-03-01: System Function
The MUA system shall supply conditioned (heated) fresh outside air to the facility interior, providing supplemental air to support the operation of the primary air exchanger (DEL-013-02) and to replace air exhausted by the heavy equipment exhaust system (DEL-013-04) and welding station exhaust system (DEL-013-05).

Source: _CONTEXT.md Description; _DEPENDENCIES.md Internal Package Dependencies

### REQ-013-03-02: Supply Volume Balance
The MUA system supply volume shall be sized to balance the total exhaust volumes generated by DEL-013-04 and DEL-013-05, and to coordinate with the primary air exchanger (DEL-013-02). The mechanical engineer (DEL-003-06 Calculation Package) shall establish the required supply airflow rate.

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md Constraint Notes ("Makeup volume must match exhaust volumes")

Note: Specific airflow values (CFM/L/s) are TBD — subject to mechanical design calculations.

### REQ-013-03-03: Building Pressurization Control
The MUA system controls shall prevent excessive building pressurization. Control dampers shall modulate supply airflow to maintain appropriate indoor/outdoor pressure differential. The maximum allowable pressure differential shall be **TBD** — to be established by the mechanical engineer (DEL-003-06) with reference to the building envelope design and overhead door operating pressures. (Enrichment: A-003, A-005)

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md Constraint Notes ("Controls must prevent excessive pressurization")

### REQ-013-03-04: Air Quality and Intake Separation
The MUA system shall supply fresh outside air of adequate quality for occupant health, safety, and comfort in an industrial maintenance shop environment. Intake location shall be selected to avoid drawing in vehicle exhaust, equipment fumes, or other contaminants. Minimum intake-to-exhaust separation distance shall comply with **TBD** — ASHRAE 62.1 Table 5-1 (or equivalent Alberta Building Code provision) or as established by the mechanical engineer's intake separation analysis. Visual confirmation alone is insufficient for verification of this safety-critical requirement. (Enrichment: X-003)

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md Constraint Notes ("Fresh air quality critical for safety and comfort"); ASHRAE 62.1 (ASSUMPTION: likely applicable — location TBD)

### REQ-013-03-04A: Minimum Outdoor Air Ventilation Rate
The MUA system shall provide outdoor air ventilation at or above the minimum rate required by ASHRAE 62.1 (or the Alberta Building Code mechanical provisions, whichever is more stringent) for the facility's occupancy classification. The applicable minimum ventilation rate is **TBD** — to be established in DEL-003-06 with reference to ASHRAE 62.1 or Alberta Building Code. (Enrichment: C-001)

Source: ASHRAE 62.1 (ASSUMPTION: likely applicable — location TBD); Enrichment C-001

### REQ-013-03-05: Heating of Makeup Air
Makeup air shall be tempered/heated before distribution into the occupied facility. Heating shall be coordinated with the high-recovery heating system (DEL-013-01). Heating capacity shall be sufficient to handle the full makeup air supply volume under Alberta cold-climate design conditions. The design outdoor air temperature and the minimum supply air temperature entering the occupied space shall be **TBD** — to be established in DEL-003-06 and confirmed against ASHRAE climate data for the Camrose County region. (Enrichment: F-005)

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md Internal Package Dependencies ("DEL-013-01 (Heating System): Heating of makeup air"); Constraint Notes ("Heating capacity must handle makeup air volume")

### REQ-013-03-06: Distribution to All Areas
Supply ductwork shall be routed to serve all facility areas, including the main shop area, repair bays, welding area, and other occupied spaces as identified in the mechanical design drawings. **Note:** Whether the wash bay is included in "all facility areas" for MUA distribution purposes shall be confirmed by the mechanical design drawings (DEL-003-02). The wash bay has unique ventilation requirements (moisture, drainage) that may affect MUA distribution design. Currently treated as ASSUMPTION per Datasheet. (Enrichment: X-001)

Source: _CONTEXT.md Key Requirements ("Proper air distribution to all areas"); _DEPENDENCIES.md Constraint Notes ("Supply ductwork must reach all facility areas")

### REQ-013-03-07: Fresh Air Intake Location
The fresh air intake shall be located to coordinate with the air exchanger intake (DEL-013-02) to avoid short-circuiting of exhaust and intake air streams. Final intake location shall be confirmed in mechanical design drawings.

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md Critical Integration Points ("Fresh air intake coordinate with exchanger intake")

### REQ-013-03-08: Supply Ductwork Conflict Avoidance
Supply ductwork routing shall avoid conflicts with structural elements, crane rails, overhead doors, and other mechanical, electrical, and plumbing systems.

Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md Critical Integration Points ("Supply ductwork routing avoids conflicts")

### REQ-013-03-09: Controls Integration
MUA unit dampers and control valves shall be integrated with the building's mechanical control system to coordinate with the heating system (DEL-013-01) and air exchanger (DEL-013-02).

Source: _CONTEXT.md Scope ("Integration with heating and air exchange systems"); _DEPENDENCIES.md Critical Integration Points

### REQ-013-03-10: Code-Compliant Installation
The MUA system installation shall comply with all applicable Alberta Safety Codes, the Alberta Building Code (mechanical provisions), and local authority having jurisdiction (AHJ) requirements. Specific applicable code sections include **TBD** — e.g., Alberta Building Code Part 6 HVAC provisions, ASHRAE 62.1 minimum ventilation rates, and any AHJ-specific requirements for Camrose County jurisdiction. All work shall be performed by qualified journeyman trades. Issued-for-Construction drawings shall be signed/stamped by a Professional Engineer licensed in Alberta. (Enrichment: A-002)

Source: RFP §3.3.2 ("The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes"); _CONTEXT.md Key Requirements ("Code-compliant installation"); RFP §3.3.2 (P.Eng. stamp requirement)

### REQ-013-03-11: Operational Readiness
The MUA system shall be commissioned and demonstrated to be fully operational prior to project substantial completion (December 31, 2026).

Source: OBJ-004 ("Install and commission all mechanical...systems to operational readiness"); RFP §3.1 (completion deadline)

### REQ-013-03-12: Equipment Procurement per Design
MUA equipment shall be procured to the mechanical engineer's equipment schedule (DEL-003-05) and specification (DEL-003-07). Substitutions shall be submitted for engineer review and approval.

Source: ASSUMPTION — consistent with design-build delivery model and OBJ-004

### REQ-013-03-13: Fire and Smoke Dampers
Fire and smoke dampers shall be installed at all supply ductwork penetrations through fire-rated assemblies, in compliance with Alberta Building Code fire separation requirements. Damper locations, ratings, and types shall be per the mechanical design drawings and the fire separation drawings. (Enrichment: C-002)

Source: ASSUMPTION: standard code obligation for HVAC ductwork penetrating rated assemblies; Alberta Building Code fire separation requirements (location TBD); Enrichment C-002

### REQ-013-03-14: Duct Leakage Testing
Supply ductwork shall be tested for leakage per applicable standard (e.g., SMACNA duct leakage class as specified in DEL-003-07). Duct leakage class and maximum allowable leakage rate are **TBD** — to be established by the mechanical engineer. (Enrichment: F-003)

Source: ASSUMPTION: standard industry practice for supply air systems; SMACNA duct construction standards (location TBD); Enrichment F-003

### REQ-013-03-15: Condensate Management
Where the MUA unit heating coil or operating conditions may produce condensation (particularly during shoulder-season operation with moist intake air), the unit shall be equipped with a condensate drain pan and drain line routed to an appropriate drain. Condensate management requirements shall be confirmed per MUA manufacturer's installation manual and DEL-003-07. (Enrichment: F-004)

Source: ASSUMPTION: standard element for MUA units with heating coils in cold climates; MUA manufacturer manual (location TBD); Enrichment F-004

### REQ-013-03-16: Ductwork Insulation
Supply ductwork shall be insulated per the mechanical specification (DEL-003-07) and applicable energy code. Insulation type, thickness, and vapor barrier requirements are **TBD** — to be specified in DEL-003-07. (Enrichment: X-004)

Source: ASSUMPTION: ductwork in unheated spaces and exterior-facing sections requires insulation; DEL-003-07 (location TBD); Enrichment X-004

### REQ-013-03-17: Electrical Disconnect and Lockout/Tagout
An electrical disconnect switch shall be installed at or near the MUA unit per Alberta Electrical Code. Lockout/tagout provisions shall comply with Alberta OHS requirements. (Enrichment: X-002)

Source: ASSUMPTION: Alberta Electrical Code / Alberta OHS; Enrichment X-002

### REQ-013-03-18: Owner/Facilities Staff Training
The Mechanical Contractor shall provide operational and maintenance training to County facilities staff covering normal system operation, seasonal adjustments, filter maintenance, troubleshooting, and emergency shutdown. (Enrichment: D-001)

Source: OBJ-004; Procedure.md Phase 6, Step 6.3 (training step exists but had no corresponding Specification requirement); Enrichment D-001

### REQ-013-03-19: Integrated System Performance Test
An integrated system performance test shall be conducted with the MUA system operating in conjunction with DEL-013-01 (heating), DEL-013-02 (air exchanger), DEL-013-04 (equipment exhaust), and DEL-013-05 (welding exhaust) simultaneously, to verify that the MUA system maintains acceptable building pressurization, air distribution, and supply air temperature under combined operating conditions. (Enrichment: D-002)

Source: ASSUMPTION: MUA performance can only be conclusively verified under integrated operating conditions; Enrichment D-002
