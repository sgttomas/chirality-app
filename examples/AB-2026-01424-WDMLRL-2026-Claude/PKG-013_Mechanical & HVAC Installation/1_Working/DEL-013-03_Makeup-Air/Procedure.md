# Procedure — DEL-013-03: Forced-Air Makeup System

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to procure, install, and commission the forced-air makeup air unit (MUA) system for the New North Shop Addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop. It is intended as a production guide for the Mechanical Contractor, coordinated with the General Contractor and other PKG-013 trades.

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Submittal and Procurement

**Step 1.1 — Prepare Equipment Submittals**
Prepare and submit product data sheets, shop drawings, and any substitution requests for the MUA unit, supply ductwork, dampers, and control components to the mechanical engineer of record for review and approval.

Acceptance criterion: Written approval from mechanical engineer (or approved-as-noted) received before ordering.

Reference: REQ-013-03-12; PRE-11

**Step 1.2 — Procure MUA Unit and Materials**
Upon submittal approval, place purchase orders for:
- MUA unit(s) per DEL-003-05
- Supply ductwork (sheet metal, fittings, hangers per DEL-003-03)
- Dampers and control valves per design
- Fire and smoke dampers per fire separation drawings (REQ-013-03-13) (Enrichment: C-002)
- Fresh air intake components (weather hood, screen, duct section)
- Intake filters — initial filter set per MUA manufacturer specification or DEL-003-07 (Enrichment: C-003)
- Ductwork insulation materials per DEL-003-07 specification (Enrichment: X-004)
- Electrical disconnect switch and lockout/tagout hardware (Enrichment: X-002)
- Control wiring and accessories

Note: Confirm lead times early — MUA units with integrated heating can have 8–16 week lead times. ASSUMPTION based on typical industrial HVAC procurement; confirm with supplier.

Reference: PRE-01 through PRE-05

**Step 1.3 — Coordinate Delivery Logistics**
Confirm delivery timing with General Contractor. MUA units are typically large and heavy — confirm crane/rigging requirements and site access route to the Utility Room area.

Reference: _DEPENDENCIES.md External Dependencies (PRE-07)

---

### Phase 2 — Rough-In and Preparatory Work

**Step 2.1 — Mark and Confirm Ductwork Route**
With IFC drawings in hand, walk the site with the General Contractor and mark the supply ductwork route throughout the facility. Confirm:
- No conflicts with structural elements, crane rails, overhead doors, or other trades' rough-ins
- Hanger attachment points are structurally acceptable
- Ductwork fits within available ceiling/structure space given the 35' ceiling height
- Fire-rated assemblies identified — mark locations where fire/smoke dampers are required (Enrichment: C-002)

Reference: REQ-013-03-08; Guidance C4; _DEPENDENCIES.md Critical Integration Points

**Step 2.2 — Prepare Fresh Air Intake Penetration**
Coordinate with General Contractor and building envelope trade to:
- Core drill or frame the exterior wall opening for the fresh air intake duct
- Install sleeve, flashing, and weatherproof seal at wall penetration
- Confirm intake location separation from exhaust outlets (DEL-013-02, DEL-013-04, DEL-013-05)
- **Measure** intake-to-exhaust separation distance and verify compliance with ASHRAE 62.1 or mechanical engineer's specified minimum (Enrichment: X-003)

Reference: REQ-013-03-07; REQ-013-03-04; Guidance C1

**Step 2.3 — Install Ductwork Hangers and Supports**
Install all ductwork hanger rods, trapeze assemblies, and structural attachments per IFC drawings and approved structural details. Do not install hangers in crane travel zones without structural engineer confirmation.

Reference: Guidance C4; PRE-09

---

### Phase 3 — Equipment Setting and Ductwork Installation

**Step 3.1 — Set MUA Unit**
Set MUA unit in approved location (Utility Room area — TBD per design). Confirm:
- Unit is level and properly supported
- Vibration isolation provisions installed per manufacturer's requirements — confirmed as a prerequisite (PRE-12), not an optional assumption (Enrichment: A-004)
- Adequate service clearances maintained on all sides per manufacturer's installation manual
- Condensate drain pan connected to drain line routed to appropriate floor drain (if applicable per manufacturer's requirements — REQ-013-03-15) (Enrichment: F-004)

Reference: PRE-07; PRE-12; REQ-013-03-01; REQ-013-03-15; MUA manufacturer installation manual

**Step 3.2 — Connect Fresh Air Intake Ductwork**
Install fresh air intake ductwork from exterior wall penetration (Step 2.2) to MUA unit intake connection. Include:
- Weather hood / bird screen at exterior
- Duct insulation as required by design and code (ASSUMPTION: required to prevent condensation in cold climate)
- Flexible connection at MUA unit to isolate vibration

Reference: REQ-013-03-04; Guidance P2; MUA manufacturer installation manual

**Step 3.2A — Install Intake Filters (Enrichment: C-003)**
Install intake filters in MUA unit filter rack per manufacturer's specification. Confirm:
- Correct filter type, size, and MERV rating per DEL-003-07 or manufacturer's installation manual
- Filters seated properly in frame with no bypass gaps
- Filter access doors/panels functional for future replacement

Record initial filter specification for inclusion in O&M manual and filter maintenance schedule.

Reference: REQ-013-03-18 (training includes filter maintenance); MUA manufacturer manual; Enrichment C-003

**Step 3.3 — Install Supply Ductwork Main Runs**
Install main supply ductwork from MUA unit discharge through facility per IFC drawings (DEL-003-03). Sequence:
1. Main trunk from MUA unit
2. Primary branch runs to each major zone (main shop, repair bays, welding area)
3. Confirm all hangers are tight and supports adequate for duct weight plus insulation

Reference: REQ-013-03-06; REQ-013-03-08; IFC drawings

**Step 3.4 — Install Supply Ductwork Branch Runs and Terminals**
Install secondary branch ductwork and supply diffusers/registers at all distribution points per design. Include:
- Volume control dampers at each branch takeoff as required by design
- Supply diffusers/registers oriented per design (consider 35' ceiling height and throw requirements)

Reference: REQ-013-03-06; IFC drawings

**Step 3.5 — Install Main Supply and Intake Dampers**
Install motorized control dampers at:
- Fresh air intake (outdoor air damper)
- Main supply duct (system isolation / modulating damper)
- Any zone isolation points per design

Wire damper actuators per controls design.

Reference: REQ-013-03-03; REQ-013-03-09; _CONTEXT.md Scope

**Step 3.5A — Install Fire and Smoke Dampers (Enrichment: C-002)**
Install fire and smoke dampers at all supply ductwork penetrations through fire-rated assemblies, as marked in Step 2.1. Confirm:
- Damper ratings match fire separation ratings per design drawings
- Dampers are UL listed and labeled
- Dampers are accessible for future inspection and testing per code
- Fusible links or actuators installed per manufacturer's instructions

Reference: REQ-013-03-13; Alberta Building Code fire separation requirements (location TBD); Enrichment C-002

**Step 3.6 — Insulate Ductwork**
Insulate supply ductwork as required by mechanical specification and energy code (ASSUMPTION: ductwork in unheated spaces and exterior-facing sections requires insulation). Confirm insulation specification with DEL-003-07. Verify:
- Insulation type and thickness per DEL-003-07 specification (TBD)
- Vapor barrier installed where required (TBD per DEL-003-07)
- No gaps or exposed ductwork at joints and fittings

Reference: PRE-03 (DEL-003-07 Mechanical Specification); REQ-013-03-16 (Enrichment X-004)

---

### Phase 4 — Services Connection

**Step 4.1 — Connect Gas Supply (if gas-fired MUA)**
Coordinate with plumbing/gas contractor for natural gas connection to MUA unit. This connection must be made by a licensed gas fitter per Alberta Safety Codes. Confirm gas pressure and supply capacity match MUA unit requirements.

Reference: REQ-013-03-10; SOW-0080; Guidance C3 (ASSUMPTION: gas-fired)

**Step 4.2 — Connect Electrical Power and Disconnect Switch**
Coordinate with Electrical Contractor (DEL-015) to connect power circuit(s) to MUA unit and controls. Confirm:
- Circuit voltage, amperage, and phase match MUA unit nameplate requirements
- **Electrical disconnect switch installed** at or near the MUA unit per Alberta Electrical Code (Enrichment: X-002)
- **Lockout/tagout provisions** installed and functional per Alberta OHS requirements (Enrichment: X-002)
- Equipment ground properly terminated

Reference: REQ-013-03-10; REQ-013-03-17; DEL-015 (PKG-015 Electrical Installation); Enrichment X-002

**Step 4.3 — Connect Controls and BAS Integration**
Connect MUA unit controls to building mechanical control system per DEL-003-09 (ASSUMPTION: controls design deliverable) or as directed by mechanical engineer. Confirm:
- MUA controls can receive enable/disable signals from air exchanger (DEL-013-02) controls
- Damper actuators wired and responding to control signals
- Any interlock with heating system (DEL-013-01) wired and verified

Reference: REQ-013-03-09; _DEPENDENCIES.md Critical Integration Points; Guidance P3

---

### Phase 5 — Inspection, Testing, and Commissioning

**Step 5.1 — Pre-Commissioning Inspection**
Before powering up the MUA system, conduct a pre-commissioning inspection:
- Verify all ductwork connections are tight (no visible gaps)
- Verify all dampers (supply, intake, fire/smoke) are free to operate manually
- Verify all access panels and clean-out covers are installed
- Verify fresh air intake screen is clean and unobstructed
- Verify intake filters installed and seated properly (Step 3.2A)
- Verify all mechanical connections (gas, electrical) have been completed and inspected
- Verify electrical disconnect switch operational (Step 4.2)

Reference: REQ-013-03-10; RFP §2.14.1

**Step 5.2 — AHJ Inspection**
Request and obtain all required AHJ mechanical inspections. Ensure County project representative is present for inspections and receives copies of all inspection reports.

**Hold/Witness Points (Enrichment: F-002):**
- **HOLD POINT — Rough-in Inspection:** Before any ductwork is concealed (enclosed in walls, above ceilings, or otherwise inaccessible), request AHJ rough-in inspection. Do not proceed with concealment until rough-in inspection is passed.
- **HOLD POINT — Fire Damper Inspection:** Before concealing fire/smoke damper installations, request AHJ inspection of damper installations if required by local jurisdiction.
- **HOLD POINT — Final Inspection:** Before commissioning and Owner acceptance testing, request AHJ final mechanical inspection. Do not proceed with commissioning report submission until final inspection is passed.

Reference: REQ-013-03-10; RFP §3.3.2 ("The County project representative must be present for all inspections"); Enrichment F-002

**Step 5.3 — MUA Unit Start-Up**
Start up MUA unit per manufacturer's start-up procedure. Record:
- Unit start-up date
- Measured supply air temperature
- Motor amps at operating load
- Any fault codes or operational issues

Reference: REQ-013-03-01; MUA manufacturer installation manual

**Step 5.4 — Controls Functional Test**
Test all control sequences:
- MUA enable/disable via system controls
- Damper modulation in response to demand signal
- Interlock with air exchanger (DEL-013-02) — confirm no simultaneous conflicting operation
- Interlock with heating system (DEL-013-01) — confirm heating activation on makeup air demand

Reference: REQ-013-03-03; REQ-013-03-09; Specification.md Verification table

**Step 5.5 — Test and Balance (TAB)**
Engage a qualified test-and-balance contractor to:
- Measure airflow at all supply terminals
- Adjust volume control dampers to achieve design airflow distribution
- Measure and record total MUA system supply volume
- Confirm total supply volume balances total exhaust volume (DEL-013-04 + DEL-013-05)
- Document results in a TAB report

Reference: REQ-013-03-02; REQ-013-03-06; Guidance C6

**Step 5.5A — Duct Leakage Test (Enrichment: F-003)**
Prior to or concurrent with TAB, conduct duct leakage testing per SMACNA duct leakage class or as specified in DEL-003-07. Record:
- Test method and pressure
- Measured leakage rate
- Pass/fail against allowable leakage rate (TBD per DEL-003-07)
- Repair any leakage exceeding allowable limits and retest

Reference: REQ-013-03-14; SMACNA duct construction standards (location TBD); Enrichment F-003

**Step 5.6 — Pressurization Check**
With the MUA system operating at design conditions (along with DEL-013-02, DEL-013-04, DEL-013-05 operating simultaneously), measure building pressure differential. Confirm:
- Pressure differential within **TBD** Pa range at overhead door locations (Enrichment: A-003, A-005)
- Overhead doors can be opened without difficulty
- Control system responds to correct any pressure imbalance

Reference: REQ-013-03-03; _DEPENDENCIES.md Constraint Notes

**Step 5.7 — Integrated System Performance Test (Enrichment: D-002)**
Conduct an integrated system performance test with the MUA operating simultaneously with DEL-013-01 (heating), DEL-013-02 (air exchanger), DEL-013-04 (equipment exhaust), and DEL-013-05 (welding exhaust). Verify:
- Building pressurization remains within acceptable range under combined operation
- Air distribution to all served areas is maintained
- Supply air temperature meets design specification at distribution points
- No adverse interactions between systems (e.g., controls conflicts, pressure oscillation)
- Document results in commissioning report

Reference: REQ-013-03-19; Enrichment D-002

---

### Phase 6 — Closeout

**Step 6.1 — Prepare As-Built Documentation**
Mark up and submit as-built drawings showing:
- Actual MUA unit location and orientation
- Actual ductwork routing with dimensions
- Damper locations and identification (including fire/smoke dampers)
- Fresh air intake location with dimensions from reference points
- Electrical disconnect switch location

Reference: Documentation requirements in Specification.md

**Step 6.2 — Compile O&M Manuals**
Compile and submit Operation and Maintenance manuals including:
- MUA unit manufacturer's O&M manual
- Damper and controls manufacturer documentation
- Fire/smoke damper manufacturer documentation and inspection schedule
- Filter specification and replacement schedule
- TAB report
- Duct leakage test report
- Start-up report
- Commissioning report (including integrated system test results)

Reference: Specification.md Documentation section

**Step 6.3 — Owner Training**
Provide operational training to County facilities staff covering:
- Normal system operation and seasonal adjustments
- Filter maintenance schedule — filter type, replacement frequency, and replacement procedure per manufacturer's recommendation (confirmed from Step 3.2A initial filter installation)
- Troubleshooting common fault indications
- Emergency shutdown procedure
- Electrical disconnect / lockout/tagout procedure

Reference: REQ-013-03-18; OBJ-004; Enrichment D-001
