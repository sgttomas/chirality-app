# Procedure — DEL-013-01: High-Recovery Heating System

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce, install, and commission the High-Recovery Heating System (DEL-013-01) for the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project. Steps cover the full lifecycle of this deliverable from design receipt through commissioning sign-off.

**Applicable to:** Mechanical Contractor (primary), with coordination with General Contractor, Mechanical Engineer (P.Eng. of record — see Guidance Terminology Note), Electrical Contractor (PKG-015), and Controls Contractor as noted.

---

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Design Receipt and Pre-Procurement Review

**Step 1.1 — Receive and Review Design Documents**
Upon receipt of IFC mechanical drawings and specification (DEL-003-02, DEL-003-03, DEL-003-07), Mechanical Contractor reviews the heating system requirements, including:
- Equipment type, model, and performance specification
- Heating capacity (BTU/h or kW) from DEL-003-06
- Ductwork layout and connection points
- Controls integration requirements
- Utility connection types and locations

*Outcome:* Mechanical Contractor confirms understanding of scope and raises any RFIs before procurement.

---

**Step 1.2 — Prepare Equipment Submittal**
Mechanical Contractor prepares and submits an equipment submittal package to the Mechanical Engineer (P.Eng. of record) for review and approval, including:
- Manufacturer product data sheets
- Equipment performance data confirming high-recovery compliance (REQ-001), including recovery efficiency data
- Dimensional drawings confirming fit within utility room (REQ-005)
- Equipment weight for structural adequacy assessment *(Lensing ref: B-002)*
- Equipment noise rating *(Lensing ref: B-002)*
- Controls interface data sheet

*Outcome:* Equipment submittal approved in writing by Mechanical Engineer before ordering (REQ-015).

**Source:** ASSUMPTION: standard submittal process for design-build HVAC installation; consistent with R-01 §2.14 acceptance obligations.

---

**HOLD POINT 1 — Design-to-Procurement Gate** *(NEW — Lensing ref: A-004)*

Before proceeding to Phase 2 (Procurement), the following gate conditions must be satisfied:
- [ ] Equipment submittal approved in writing by Mechanical Engineer (REQ-015)
- [ ] All RFIs from Step 1.1 resolved
- [ ] Ductwork routing coordination confirmed (Step 1.3 complete)

**Sign-off required:** Mechanical Engineer written approval of submittal constitutes the gate clearance. Procurement shall not commence without this approval.

**Source:** Guidance Principle 3 (Design Before Procurement); Specification REQ-015. Lensing ref: A-004.

---

**Step 1.3 — Coordinate Ductwork Routing**
Prior to procurement and fabrication, confirm ductwork routing against:
- Structural drawings (avoid conflicts per REQ-006)
- Architectural floor plan (Appendix B — utility room, mezzanine, shop floor plan)
- Electrical routing (coordinate with PKG-015)
- Plumbing routing (coordinate with PKG-014)

Document any routing deviations from design drawings and obtain Mechanical Engineer approval.

*Outcome:* Ductwork routing confirmed, no structural conflicts, coordination sign-off on file.

---

### Phase 2 — Procurement and Delivery

**Step 2.1 — Procure Approved Equipment**
Following submittal approval (Hold Point 1 clearance), Mechanical Contractor procures the heating system equipment and all ancillary components (controls, sensors, ductwork fittings as applicable) from approved supplier.

*Outcome:* Purchase order placed for approved equipment.

---

**Step 2.2 — Receive and Inspect Equipment on Delivery**
Upon delivery to site, Mechanical Contractor inspects:
- Equipment model and specification match the approved submittal
- No shipping damage to equipment or controls components
- All ancillary components and installation hardware included

Damaged or non-conforming equipment must not be installed. Notify Project Manager and Mechanical Engineer immediately if issues are found.

*Outcome:* Equipment receipt inspection record on file.

---

### Phase 3 — Installation

**Step 3.1 — Confirm Utility Room Readiness**
Before commencing installation, confirm all items on the Pre-Installation Readiness Checklist are complete:
- DEL-012-02 (Utility Room) is complete and accepted by General Contractor
- Electrical supply circuit is energized and labelled (DEL-015-04)
- Gas supply (if applicable) is isolated and ready for connection; gas permit obtained (REQ-012)
- Area is clean, dry, and accessible
- Pre-Installation Readiness Checklist signed *(Lensing ref: F-003)*

*Outcome:* Utility room readiness sign-off; Pre-Installation Readiness Checklist on file.

---

**Step 3.2 — Position and Secure Equipment**
Install heating equipment in the utility room at the location shown on the IFC drawings:
- Position equipment per manufacturer installation specifications (REQ-011) and IFC drawings
- Verify all code-required clearances are maintained (REQ-005)
- Secure equipment to structure per manufacturer requirements and structural drawings
- Confirm vibration isolation if required by manufacturer

*Outcome:* Equipment in position, anchored, clearances verified.

---

**Step 3.3 — Ductwork Connections**
Connect ductwork to the heating unit and to the building distribution system per IFC ductwork plans (DEL-003-03):
- Install all supply and return ductwork connections
- Install all heat recovery connections to/from DEL-013-02 (Air Exchanger) per design
- Install connections to DEL-013-03 (Makeup Air) supply ducts per design
- Seal all joints per mechanical specification requirements
- Install access panels and clean-out provisions as required by code

*Outcome:* Ductwork fully connected and sealed.

---

**Step 3.4 — Utility Connections**
Complete all utility connections:
- **Gas connection** (if gas-fired): Certified gas fitter (REQ-012) performs gas line connection per applicable gas code; gas permit must be obtained and posted before work commences
- **Electrical connection**: Coordinate with Electrical Contractor (PKG-015) for final connection of power supply to equipment electrical panel
- **Controls wiring**: Install and connect controls wiring per controls drawings; coordinate with Controls Contractor and BMS integrator (see Guidance Terminology Note)

*Outcome:* All utility connections complete; gas permits obtained and gas installation performed by certified gas fitter (if applicable).

---

**Step 3.5 — Controls Installation and Integration**
Install and configure the heating system controls:
- Mount thermostats, sensors, and controllers per controls drawings
- Wire controls to heating unit and to BMS interface points
- Configure setpoints per Mechanical Engineer's commissioning requirements
- Verify BMS point list and communication protocol (REQ-008) against the defined protocol and minimum point list

*Outcome:* Controls physically installed and wired; ready for software commissioning.

---

**HOLD POINT 2 — Substantial Completion Gate** *(NEW — Lensing ref: D-002)*

Before proceeding to Phase 4 (Commissioning), confirm substantial completion per Specification REQ-013:
- [ ] All equipment installed, secured, and connected per IFC drawings
- [ ] All utility connections (electrical, gas, controls) complete
- [ ] All ductwork connections made and sealed
- [ ] Pre-commissioning inspection (Step 4.1) passed
- [ ] Safety Code inspection (Step 4.2) passed

**Sign-off required:** Mechanical Contractor substantial completion sign-off; Mechanical Engineer confirmation.

---

### Phase 4 — Verification and Commissioning

**Step 4.1 — Pre-Commissioning Inspection**
Before energizing, perform a complete pre-commissioning inspection:
- [ ] All ductwork connections made and sealed
- [ ] Equipment anchored per drawings
- [ ] All clearances meet code requirements
- [ ] Electrical connections complete and verified correct polarity/phase
- [ ] Gas connections tested for leaks (pressure test per B149 or applicable code — ASSUMPTION)
- [ ] Controls wiring complete and checked
- [ ] Safety Code mechanical permit posted
- [ ] Gas permit posted (if gas-fired, per REQ-012)
- [ ] No debris or tools left inside equipment or ductwork
- [ ] Manufacturer installation manual on site and installation verified against manufacturer requirements (REQ-011)

*Outcome:* Pre-commissioning checklist complete and signed by Mechanical Contractor supervisor.

---

**Step 4.2 — Safety Code Inspection**
Schedule and complete inspection by the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) under Alberta Safety Codes prior to first energization.

*Outcome:* Safety Code inspection sign-off on file.

**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2; REQ-003.

---

**Step 4.3 — Initial Start-Up**
Energize the heating system and perform initial start-up per manufacturer commissioning procedure:
- Verify ignition (if gas-fired) or heating element energization
- Confirm heat output
- Confirm fan/blower operation
- Confirm no abnormal noise, vibration, or odour
- Confirm heat recovery function active and functional

Document all start-up readings.

*Outcome:* System starts and operates without fault; start-up log on file.

---

**Step 4.4 — Integrated HVAC System Test**
Commission the heating system in conjunction with all interconnected PKG-013 subsystems:
- Operate DEL-013-01 (Heating) + DEL-013-02 (Air Exchanger) simultaneously; confirm heat recovery loop function
- Operate DEL-013-01 + DEL-013-03 (Makeup Air); confirm system handles makeup air thermal load
- Operate DEL-013-01 + DEL-013-04/05 (Exhaust); confirm no back-pressure or air-balance issues
- Operate DEL-013-01 + DEL-013-06 (Ceiling Fans); confirm air distribution
- Verify BMS controls all systems as designed
- **Air balance verification:** Confirm overall building air pressure is maintained within acceptable limits during simultaneous operation of all subsystems. TBD — air balance acceptance criteria to be defined by Mechanical Engineer. *(Lensing ref: C-003)*

*Outcome:* Integrated HVAC operation confirmed; air balance verified; commissioning data recorded.

**Source:** REQ-002; OBJ-004; `_DEPENDENCIES.md` Internal Package Dependencies.

---

**Step 4.5 — Performance Verification**
Verify system performance against design requirements:
- Confirm the system achieves the design heating output (per DEL-003-06 capacity) — TBD values
- Confirm temperature uniformity in shop space (ASSUMPTION: criteria to be defined in mechanical specification DEL-003-07)
- Confirm controls respond correctly to thermostat/BMS commands
- Record the following specific performance data points for the commissioning report *(Lensing ref: E-003)*:
  - Supply air temperature (degrees C or F)
  - Return air temperature (degrees C or F)
  - Recovery efficiency (percentage or temperature differential across recovery heat exchanger)
  - Airflow rate (CFM or L/s) at supply and return
  - Gas consumption rate (if gas-fired) or electrical consumption (kW)
  - Outdoor ambient temperature at time of test
  - BMS setpoint response time

*Outcome:* Performance criteria met; commissioning report data complete with specific data points recorded.

---

### Phase 5 — Documentation and Handover

**Step 5.1 — Prepare Commissioning Report**
Compile and submit the commissioning report to the Mechanical Engineer (P.Eng. of record) and Project Manager for review and acceptance, including:
- Pre-commissioning inspection checklist (signed)
- Safety Code inspection sign-off
- Start-up log with readings
- Integrated system test results (including air balance verification data)
- Performance verification data (with all specific data points from Step 4.5)
- Outstanding deficiencies list (if any)
- Explicit pass/fail determination against criteria defined in the commissioning plan (REQ-009)

*Outcome:* Commissioning report submitted; Mechanical Engineer reviews and accepts; Project Manager acknowledges. *(Note: Mechanical Contractor prepares and signs the report; Mechanical Engineer accepts it — see Guidance CONF-004.)*

---

**Step 5.2 — As-Built Mark-Ups**
Provide as-installed mark-ups on the IFC mechanical drawings, noting any deviations from the issued drawings. Submit to Mechanical Engineer for verification and incorporation into as-built drawing set.

As-built drawings shall accurately depict all deviations from IFC drawings, including equipment positioning, ductwork routing changes, utility connection locations, and any field modifications. Significant deviations shall be specifically noted and approved by Mechanical Engineer (REQ-016).

*Outcome:* As-built mark-ups submitted and accepted by Mechanical Engineer.

---

**Step 5.3 — O&M Manuals and Warranty Documentation**
Compile and submit Operation and Maintenance (O&M) manuals for all installed equipment, including:
- Equipment manuals from manufacturer
- Controls documentation and setpoint record
- Warranty registration and terms — including manufacturer warranty certificates, registration confirmation, warranty coverage period and scope, exclusions and conditions, and warranty claim contact information (REQ-014) *(Lensing ref: X-004)*
- Recommended maintenance schedule

Submit to Project Manager for handover to Owner (via PKG-020 — Commissioning & Closeout).

*Outcome:* O&M manuals and warranty documentation submitted to PKG-020.

---

**Step 5.4 — Deficiency Rectification**
If any deficiencies are identified during commissioning or the Owner's final inspection:
- Rectify within the timeframe specified in the contract (CCDC 14-2013 §2.11 Deficiency Holdback; R-01 §2.10 Guarantee Period — 10-day rectification obligation; see Guidance Consideration H for implications)
- Re-test affected functions after rectification
- Obtain sign-off that deficiencies are resolved

*Outcome:* All deficiencies closed; final acceptance confirmed.

**Source:** R-01 §2.10 Guarantee Period; R-01 §2.11 Deficiency Holdback; CCDC 14-2013.
