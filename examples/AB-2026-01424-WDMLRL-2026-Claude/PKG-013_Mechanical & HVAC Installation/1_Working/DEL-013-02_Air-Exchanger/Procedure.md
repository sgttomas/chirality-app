# Procedure — DEL-013-02: High-Volume Air Exchanger

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and install the High-Volume Air Exchanger for the New North Shop addition at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill. It covers the sequence from design confirmation through equipment procurement, installation, integration, commissioning, and closeout documentation.

The procedure is written from the perspective of the Mechanical Contractor (responsible party), operating under the direction of the Design-Builder and in coordination with the Mechanical Engineer (PKG-003 design authority).

---

The following conditions and inputs must be satisfied before the Mechanical Contractor begins installation work:

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Design Coordination and Equipment Procurement

**Step 1.1 — Confirm Equipment Selection**
Obtain the approved Mechanical Equipment Schedule (DEL-003-05) and Mechanical Specification (DEL-003-07). Confirm the specified air exchanger model, manufacturer, performance ratings, and physical dimensions. Verify the unit is rated for cold-climate operation including defrost provisions suitable for Alberta conditions. (Source: Specification REQ-011; Guidance — Cold-Climate HRV/ERV Performance)

**Step 1.2 — Prepare and Submit Shop Drawings**
Prepare shop drawings and equipment submittals including:
- Manufacturer's data sheet and model confirmation.
- Performance data (rated CFM, heat recovery efficiency at design conditions).
- Dimensional drawings and clearance requirements.
- Electrical data (voltage, phase, amperage, circuit requirements).
- Controls and sensor data sheet.
Submit to Mechanical Engineer of Record for review and approval before ordering equipment. (Source: Specification §Documentation)

**Step 1.3 — Procure Equipment**
Upon receipt of approved shop drawing, place equipment order with lead time appropriate to the project schedule (completion deadline: December 31, 2026 — Source: R-01 §3.1). Confirm delivery schedule with Mechanical Contractor's project schedule. (Source: _CONTEXT.md Scope: "Equipment procurement and delivery")

**Step 1.4 — Confirm Intake and Exhaust Locations on Site**
Coordinate with the General Contractor and Mechanical Engineer to confirm the exact locations of the fresh air intake and exhaust outlet wall penetrations on the building exterior. Verify:
- Adequate separation between intake and exhaust (per mechanical code — TBD).
- Intake is not located near vehicle entry doors, overhead door openings, or diesel exhaust routes.
- Exhaust is directed away from intake and public areas.
- Penetration locations are structurally feasible.
(Source: Specification REQ-005, REQ-006; _DEPENDENCIES.md Constraint Notes)

---

### Phase 2 — Rough-In and Ductwork Installation

**Step 2.1 — Obtain Mechanical Permit**
Ensure the Mechanical Safety Codes permit is in place before proceeding with installation. The County project representative must be notified and present at required inspections. (Source: R-01 §3.3.2)

**Step 2.2 — Install Wall Penetrations**
Coordinate with the General Contractor to core or frame the building exterior wall penetrations for the fresh air intake duct and exhaust outlet duct. Install sleeve/thimble, flashing, and weatherproofing at each penetration per manufacturer requirements and applicable mechanical code. **Where ductwork penetrates fire-rated wall assemblies, install fire stopping / firestopping per applicable fire code (ABC / NBC). Confirm fire rating of penetrated walls and applicable fire stopping requirements before proceeding.** (Lensing item C-003; Specification REQ-014) (Source: Specification REQ-005, REQ-006; _CONTEXT.md Scope; ASSUMPTION — fire stopping requirement per standard code practice)

**Step 2.3 — Install Fresh Air Intake Ductwork**
Install the fresh air intake ductwork from the exterior wall penetration to the equipment connection point in the Utility Room. Ductwork shall be installed per:
- Approved IFC drawings (DEL-003-02, DEL-003-03).
- Mechanical Specification (DEL-003-07).
- Manufacturer installation instructions.
Include insulation on cold-side ductwork to prevent condensation. **Ductwork insulation specification (type, R-value, vapor barrier requirements) shall be confirmed in DEL-003-07 prior to installation. Do not proceed with cold-side ductwork insulation until specification is confirmed.** (Lensing item A-004) (ASSUMPTION — ductwork insulation requirement; specific insulation type, R-value, and vapor barrier TBD per DEL-003-07.) (Source: _CONTEXT.md Scope: "Fresh air intake ductwork installation")

**Step 2.4 — Install Exhaust Outlet Ductwork**
Install the exhaust outlet ductwork from the equipment connection point in the Utility Room to the exterior wall penetration. Include bird screen/louver at the exterior termination. (Source: _CONTEXT.md Scope: "Exhaust outlet ductwork installation"; ASSUMPTION re: bird screen per standard practice)

**Step 2.5 — Install Supply and Return Distribution Ductwork**
Install supply air distribution ductwork from the air exchanger to facility areas, and return/exhaust collection ductwork from facility areas back to the unit, per approved IFC drawings. Coordinate routing with DEL-013-01 (Heating System), DEL-013-03 (Makeup Air), and DEL-013-04/05 (Exhaust Systems) to avoid conflicts. (Source: _CONTEXT.md Scope; _DEPENDENCIES.md Internal Package Dependencies)

### Phase 3 — Equipment Installation

**Step 3.1 — Receive and Inspect Equipment**
Upon delivery of the air exchanger unit:
- Inspect for shipping damage.
- Verify the unit model and serial number against the approved submittal.
- Store in a protected, dry location until installation.
- **Complete and sign a receiving inspection record/form documenting that equipment was inspected on delivery and found acceptable (or documenting any damage/discrepancies). Retain this record in the project file.** (Lensing item E-001)
(Source: Standard practice; Specification REQ-013 — warranty depends on proper handling)

**Step 3.2 — Install Air Exchanger Unit**
Install the air exchanger in the Utility Room per manufacturer installation instructions and approved IFC drawings. Ensure:
- Structural support is adequate for unit weight (confirm with structural design if required — ASSUMPTION for heavy unit).
- Required service clearances are maintained on all sides per manufacturer requirements.
- Unit is level and properly supported to prevent vibration transmission.
- Vibration isolation (if specified by Mechanical Engineer) is installed.
(Source: _CONTEXT.md Scope: "Equipment installation in utility space")

**Step 3.3 — Connect Ductwork to Unit**
Make final duct connections between the installed unit and the rough-in ductwork for:
- Fresh air intake (from exterior).
- Exhaust outlet (to exterior).
- Supply air distribution (to facility).
- Return/stale air collection (from facility).
Seal all duct connections per specification requirements. (Source: _CONTEXT.md Scope)

**Step 3.4 — Integrate with Heating System**
Complete the integration connection between the air exchanger and the High-Recovery Heating System (DEL-013-01) per approved IFC drawings and Mechanical Specification. This may include ductwork interconnection, hydronic coil connections, or control wiring coordination depending on system design. Details TBD pending mechanical design. (Source: _CONTEXT.md Scope: "Integration with heating system"; _DEPENDENCIES.md)

**Step 3.5 — Install Controls and Sensors**
Install all controls components (controller, thermostats, pressure sensors, CO/CO2 sensors, damper actuators — specific components TBD per DEL-003-07) and make all control wiring connections per:
- Approved controls drawings or sequence of operations.
- Manufacturer installation instructions.
- Electrical code requirements.
(Source: _CONTEXT.md Scope: "Controls and sensor setup")

**Step 3.6 — Connect Power Supply**
Coordinate with Electrical Contractor to confirm the power circuit and disconnect are in place. Mechanical Contractor to make final connection from disconnect to unit per manufacturer requirements and electrical permit. (ASSUMPTION — scope boundary per Guidance.md Conflict Table C-002; confirm with project team before proceeding.)

### Phase 4 — Commissioning and Testing

**Step 4.1 — Pre-Startup Checks**
Before energizing the unit, verify:
- All duct connections are complete and sealed.
- All filters are installed (if applicable at startup; follow manufacturer pre-startup checklist).
- Electrical connections are correct and confirmed by Electrical Contractor.
- Controls wiring is complete and confirmed.
- No obstructions in intake or exhaust ductwork.
- Access panels are in place.
(Source: Specification REQ-012)

**Step 4.2 — Initial Startup**
Energize the unit per manufacturer startup procedure. Observe:
- No unusual vibration or noise.
- Rotation direction correct (if applicable — belt-drive or direct-drive verification).
- No duct leakage at connections.
- Controls responding as expected.
(Source: Specification REQ-012)

**Step 4.3 — Airflow Measurement and Balancing**
Measure and record supply and exhaust airflow rates at design points. Adjust dampers and balancing devices to achieve design airflow per IFC drawings. Verify:
- Total supply airflow meets design requirement (REQ-001).
- Total exhaust airflow is balanced (REQ-004).
- Fresh air distribution reaches all facility areas (REQ-003).
Engage a certified air balancing agency if required by Mechanical Specification (DEL-003-07). (ASSUMPTION — requirement TBD.)

**Step 4.4 — Heat Recovery Performance Verification**
Verify heat recovery core performance by measuring supply and exhaust air temperatures at design conditions. Compare measured efficiency against manufacturer published ratings. Document results. (Source: Specification REQ-002)

**Step 4.5 — Controls and Integration Functional Test**
Test the full sequence of operations for the air exchanger, including:
- Normal operating mode.
- Heating system coordinated operation (with DEL-013-01).
- Defrost cycle (if applicable — cold-weather test or simulated).
- Any demand-controlled ventilation features (TBD per design).
- Alarm/fault indication.
(Source: Specification REQ-007, REQ-008)

**Step 4.6 — Safety Codes Inspection**
Arrange for and facilitate inspection by the Alberta Safety Codes Officer. Ensure the County project representative is notified and present. Resolve any deficiencies identified at inspection. (Source: R-01 §3.3.2; Specification REQ-009)

### Phase 5 — Closeout

**Step 5.1 — Commissioning Report**
Compile the commissioning report including:
- Unit identification (manufacturer, model, serial number).
- Measured airflow rates (supply and exhaust, by zone and total).
- Heat recovery efficiency data.
- Controls sequence of operations (as-built).
- Pre-startup checklist.
- Inspection sign-off records.
- Any deficiencies identified and corrections made.
Submit to Owner and Design-Builder. (Source: Specification REQ-012; R-01 §2.14)

**Step 5.2 — O&M Manual Submission**
Compile and submit the Operation and Maintenance (O&M) manual including:
- Manufacturer's O&M manual for the installed unit.
- Filter maintenance schedule and replacement specifications.
- Controls operating instructions.
- Emergency shutdown procedures.
- Manufacturer warranty documentation.
(Source: Specification §Documentation)

**Step 5.3 — As-Built Mark-Ups**
Mark up the IFC drawings to reflect any field deviations in ductwork routing, penetration locations, or equipment placement. Submit to Design-Builder for as-built drawing compilation. **Clarify with Design-Builder: (a) who reviews and accepts as-built mark-ups, (b) what format is acceptable (red-line on IFC drawings, digital overlay, etc.), and (c) what tolerance for deviations from IFC is acceptable without requiring design engineer review.** (Lensing item X-005) (Source: Standard construction closeout practice — ASSUMPTION; as-built submission requirements TBD per Design-Builder)

**Step 5.4 — Training (if required)**
Provide operational walkthrough and training to the Owner's designated facility operator covering:
- Normal operation and monitoring.
- Filter replacement procedure.
- Defrost operation (if applicable).
- Fault/alarm recognition and response.
**Confirm whether Owner training is contractually required under CCDC 14-2013 or the RFP, and if so, define minimum training content and duration. If required, document training delivery with a signed training record.** (Lensing item D-003)
(ASSUMPTION — Owner training typically expected for HVAC systems; confirm with Design-Builder whether formal training is required under this contract.)

**Step 5.5 — Warranty Registration**
Register the installed equipment warranty with the equipment manufacturer. Ensure: (Lensing item X-003)
- Installation has been performed per manufacturer requirements to preserve warranty coverage.
- Warranty registration documentation is completed and submitted to manufacturer.
- Copy of warranty registration confirmation is provided to the Owner.
- Warranty period aligns with the 2-year guarantee period per Specification REQ-013 (R-01 §2.10).
(Source: Specification REQ-013 establishes a 2-year guarantee period; manufacturer warranty activation requirements TBD pending equipment selection.)
