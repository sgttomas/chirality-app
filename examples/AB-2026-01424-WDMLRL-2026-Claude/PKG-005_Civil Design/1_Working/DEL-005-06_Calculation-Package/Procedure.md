# Procedure — DEL-005-06: Civil Calculation Package

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce the Civil Calculation Package (DEL-005-06) for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. It defines the sequence from data collection through to final sealed submission, including key verification checks and records to be produced.

### Personnel / Qualifications

- Calculations must be prepared by or under the direct supervision of a Professional Engineer (P.Eng.) licensed to practise in the Province of Alberta.
- Source: R-01 §3.3.2
- Checker qualifications [enriched per C-004]: The checker (Step 7) shall be independent of the preparer and shall be either a P.Eng. or working under the direct supervision of a P.Eng. "Independent" means the checker was not the primary preparer of any section being checked. The checker should have civil engineering competence relevant to the calculation types being reviewed (drainage, grading, pavement design). Where the checker is a P.Eng. from a different engineering discipline, a civil-competent reviewer should also verify discipline-specific methodology selections. ASSUMPTION — checker independence and competence criteria inferred from standard Alberta professional engineering practice; specific firm policy may apply.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Project Setup and Scope Confirmation

1.1 Review RFP §3.3.2 and §3.4 (R-01) to confirm the full list of civil scope items within Proponent responsibility.

1.2 Confirm County-performed scope exclusions: bulk cut and fill, aggregate supply, brushing and clearing (R-01 §3.3.1; Decomposition §4).

1.3 Attend mandatory site meeting (March 3, 2026, per RFP §3.2) to observe existing building and available expansion space. Record site observations relevant to drainage, grades, and soils.

1.4 Confirm design storm return period(s) with County or applicable Camrose County drainage standards (see Guidance Conflict Table CONF-001).

1.5 Confirm topsoil stripping responsibility scope (see Guidance Conflict Table CONF-002).

1.6 Open a calculation binder/file system with cover sheet fields: project name, deliverable ID (DEL-005-06), preparer, checker, P.Eng. supervisor, revision log.

1.7 [enriched per A-002, C-001] Research and document specific applicable code clauses: identify Alberta Safety Code sections, confirm NBC edition, locate applicable Alberta Drainage Design Guideline sections, and identify Camrose County bylaw numbers. Populate the Specification Standards table with clause-level references.

**Record:** Site visit notes; scope confirmation memo; design storm return period confirmation document [enriched per F-004].

---

### Step 2 — Establish Drainage Basin and Site Context

> **HOLD POINT HP-01** [enriched per C-003]: DEL-008-02 (Preliminary Site Survey) must be available before proceeding. If not available, do not begin Step 2 drainage basin delineation. Document the hold and notify the project manager.

2.1 Using Appendix C site maps (R-07) and the preliminary survey (DEL-008-02), delineate the pre-development drainage basin(s) affecting the expansion area.

2.2 Identify all property boundary locations where runoff currently exits the site.

2.3 Document pre-development runoff characteristics: contributing area, slope, land cover, and estimated peak flow rates at the boundary (to establish the "no alteration of neighbour drainage" baseline per SOW-0021). Select the hydrologic analysis method per Guidance Principle 5 — Pre-Development Baseline Method guidance. [enriched per E-002]

**Record:** Pre-development drainage basin delineation map; baseline flow rate calculations (including method selection rationale and documentation per Guidance Principle 5).

---

### Step 3 — Grading and Drainage Calculations

> **HOLD POINT HP-04** [enriched per X-002]: Before proceeding to Step 3.3 (drainage feature sizing), confirm that the stormwater management approach has been determined and documented per Guidance Trade-offs table. Document the approach decision basis.

3.1 Using the preliminary survey (DEL-008-02) and proposed building footprint (Appendix B), establish the proposed grading concept consistent with DEL-005-02 (Site Grading Plan).

3.2 Calculate post-development runoff for selected design storm(s) (confirmed in Step 1.4).

3.3 Size drainage features and components (swales, catch basins, culverts, outfalls, or detention/retention areas as applicable) to convey the design storm while achieving positive drainage across the site.

3.4 Verify that post-development drainage at the property boundary does not exceed pre-development conditions in rate, volume, or direction (SOW-0021, REQ-002).

3.5 Calculate finish grading volumes for Proponent scope only (bulk cut/fill is County scope). Calculate topsoil stripping volumes for driving surfaces. [terminology normalized per F-003]

3.6 Document all assumptions (soil type, Manning's n values, runoff coefficients, time of concentration methods, etc.) in a design assumptions summary.

3.7 [enriched per B-003] Coordinate with the Plumbing Engineer (PKG-006) to obtain the septic system drainage envelope from DEL-006-05. Incorporate the septic drainage field location and setback requirements as constraints in the civil drainage calculations per Specification REQ-011.

**Record:** Hydrologic and hydraulic calculations; grading volume calculations; design assumptions summary; stormwater management approach decision record [enriched per X-002]; septic interface coordination record [enriched per B-003].

---

### Step 4 — Driving Surface Design Calculations

> **HOLD POINT HP-02** [enriched per C-003]: DEL-008-01 (Geotechnical Investigation Report) must be available before proceeding with final driving surface design. If preliminary design is required before DEL-008-01, use interim conservative values per Guidance Principle 3 — Interim Design Parameter Values, and label all affected calculations as PRELIMINARY.

4.1 Using subgrade parameters from the geotechnical report (DEL-008-01), determine the required gravel driving surface thickness to support motor-scraper-class equipment (minimum design load per REQ-004). [terminology normalized per X-003]

4.2 Select an appropriate design method (e.g., CBR-based aggregate thickness design or equivalent). Document method selection rationale.

4.3 Calculate required gravel depth and material specifications consistent with the Civil Specification (DEL-005-07) and the Driving Surface Layout (DEL-005-04).

4.4 Verify consistency with the gravel material supply (County-supplied aggregate per Decomposition SOW-0203 — note: this is County scope; design specifies material properties, County supplies).

**Record:** Driving surface design calculations; subgrade and material parameter table.

---

### Step 4A — Utility Sizing Calculations [enriched per A-004]

4A.1 Identify all civil utility sizing calculations within the scope of this deliverable (gas, electrical, communications tie-in coordination per SOW-0080–SOW-0082 via PKG-018, and any civil site utilities such as storm sewer or catch basins).

4A.2 Perform utility sizing calculations for civil-scope utilities. Coordinate with other discipline packages (PKG-006 Plumbing, PKG-018 Sitework) for interface points.

4A.3 Document utility sizing assumptions and design criteria, including any coordination items with other disciplines.

**Record:** Utility sizing calculations; coordination notes with applicable discipline packages.

---

### Step 5 — Slope Stability Analysis (Conditional)

5.1 Review the geotechnical report (DEL-008-01) to determine whether slope stability analysis is required based on the trigger criteria defined in Specification REQ-010 (cut/fill height thresholds, slope angle thresholds, retaining structures, adverse soil/groundwater conditions). [enriched per F-001]

5.2 If required: perform slope stability analysis for all critical slopes using geotechnical parameters from DEL-008-01. Document method and factor of safety targets per applicable standards.

5.3 If not required: document the basis for the determination citing the REQ-010 trigger criteria evaluated and the reasons each does not apply (e.g., all slopes less than threshold height/angle; geotechnical engineer's recommendation) and record as a design assumption. [enriched per F-001]

**Record:** Slope stability calculation (if required) or documented determination that it is not required, citing trigger criteria evaluated.

---

### Step 6 — Design Compliance Verification Summary

6.1 Prepare a design compliance checklist verifying that each RFP requirement (SOW-0015, SOW-0020, SOW-0021) and each applicable code requirement is addressed in the calculation package. Reference specific code clauses identified in Step 1.7. [enriched per A-002]

6.2 Cross-reference calculations to drawing deliverables (DEL-005-02 Site Grading Plan, DEL-005-03 Drainage Plan) to confirm consistency of values, grades, and dimensions.

6.3 Ensure all design assumptions are labeled as such and are traceable to source data.

6.4 [enriched per C-002] Verify that environmental regulatory requirements related to the landfill site context (REQ-012) have been addressed, including any conditions from the landfill's EPEA operating approval.

**Record:** Design compliance checklist; cross-reference matrix.

---

### Step 7 — Internal Quality Review

7.1 Checker (independent of preparer, with civil engineering competence — see Prerequisites for full qualification criteria [enriched per C-004]) reviews all calculations for arithmetic correctness, method appropriateness, unit consistency, and completeness.

7.2 Resolve all checker comments. Document checker sign-off on calculation cover sheet.

7.3 Confirm that all calculation sections are internally consistent and cross-document consistent with Specification (DEL-005-07) and drawing set (DEL-005-02 through DEL-005-05).

7.4 [enriched per D-003] Verify calculation package completeness against REQ-014: all Datasheet Construction table topics addressed, all TBDs resolved or formally deferred, all Procedure records produced.

**Record:** Checker comments and resolution log; signed calculation cover sheet; completeness verification record [enriched per D-003].

---

### Step 8 — Preliminary Submission to County for Approval

> **HOLD POINT HP-03** [enriched per C-003]: County preliminary approval must be obtained before proceeding to Step 9 (IFC finalization).

8.1 Compile a preliminary version of the calculation package supporting the preliminary civil design (DEL-005-01).

8.2 Submit to County for preliminary design approval per RFP §3.3.2. The County project representative must be present for all inspection/review meetings (RFP §3.3.2).

8.3 Incorporate any County review comments into the calculation package before proceeding to IFC finalization.

8.4 [enriched per D-002] If the County rejects the preliminary design or requires fundamental changes that invalidate prior calculations:
- Document the rejection/change request with specific County comments.
- Assess which calculation sections are affected and require recalculation (partial rework) versus which are invalidated and require restart.
- Return to the earliest affected step (Step 2, 3, 4, or 5 as applicable) and re-execute from that point.
- Re-run Step 6 compliance verification and Step 7 quality review for all revised sections before resubmitting.
- Document the rework cycle in the revision log.

**Record:** Preliminary submission package; County review comments; response to comments; rejection/rework documentation (if applicable) [enriched per D-002].

---

### Step 9 — Finalize and Seal Calculation Package for IFC

9.1 Incorporate all revisions from County preliminary approval process.

9.2 Complete final design compliance verification summary (Step 6).

9.3 Responsible P.Eng. reviews the complete calculation package and applies seal/stamp to the cover sheet and relevant calculation sections as required by Alberta engineering practice standards.

9.4 Coordinate with the drawing production team to confirm that IFC drawings (DEL-005-02 through DEL-005-05) are consistent with the finalized calculations.

9.5 [enriched per X-001] Transmit final civil grades, drainage outfall locations, and erosion control requirements to the Landscape Architect (PKG-007) per the Guidance coordination notes. Document the information exchange.

**Record:** Final sealed calculation package; sealed IFC drawings (produced by drawing team, confirmed consistent with this package); PKG-007 coordination transmittal [enriched per X-001].
