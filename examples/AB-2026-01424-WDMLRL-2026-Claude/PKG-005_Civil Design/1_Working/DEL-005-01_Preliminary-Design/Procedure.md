# Procedure — DEL-005-01: Preliminary Civil Design

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to **produce** the Preliminary Civil Design package (DEL-005-01) for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill (WDMLRL) Maintenance Shop Addition. It covers the activities from initial information gathering through County approval submission.

The procedure applies to the Civil Engineer responsible for PKG-005 Civil Design under the CCDC 14–2013 Design-Build contract.

---

Before beginning production of the preliminary civil design, the following inputs must be available or their absence explicitly acknowledged in the design:

| Prerequisite | Source / Owner | Status Note |
|---|---|---|

## Steps

### Step 1 — Site Familiarization and Information Gathering

1.1 Review all RFP documents, addenda, and conceptual drawings (R-01, R-02, R-04, R-05, R-06, R-07).

1.2 Attend the mandatory site meeting (March 3, 2026, 10:00 a.m. MST at SW-14-44-21-W4) to observe existing conditions, expansion area, surrounding grades, drainage patterns, and existing driving surfaces.

1.3 At the site meeting, confirm with the County:
- Design vehicle for driving surface (confirm motor scraper class dimensions and turning radius)
- Extent of County-completed brushing, clearing, and bulk cut and fill
- Extent of topsoil stripping already performed by County
- Any known drainage constraints or existing drainage infrastructure
- County preferences for pad layout relative to App B conceptual drawings
- County acceptance criteria or evaluation priorities for the preliminary civil design submission (see Guidance — Purpose section)

1.4 Coordinate with the geotechnical and survey subcontractor (PKG-008) to confirm timeline for:
- Preliminary survey (SOW-0002)
- Geotechnical investigation and report (SOW-0001)

1.5 Obtain available as-built or record information for existing site (existing Old North Shop, existing septic, existing utilities) from County if available.

---

### Step 2 — Establish Design Basis

2.1 Document the design vehicle for the driving surface (motor scraper class) including:
- Assumed gross vehicle weight (GVW) — TBD from site meeting / County confirmation
- Assumed turning radius — TBD from site meeting / County confirmation

2.2 Document geotechnical design assumptions if the geotech report is not available:
- Assumed subgrade bearing capacity
- Assumed frost penetration depth (Alberta climate; approximately 1.5–2.0 m — ASSUMPTION, conservative Alberta value; see Guidance — Frost penetration depth assumption basis for rationale; confirm with geotech report from PKG-008)
- Assumed subgrade soil type (based on visual site observation or preliminary boring logs if available)

2.3 Document applicable codes and standards:
- Alberta Building Code (current edition — edition TBD)
- Camrose County development standards (TBD — confirm with County at permit application stage, PKG-009)
- Alberta Safety Codes Act requirements relevant to site works

2.4 Confirm scope boundary with County: which topsoil stripping and bulk cut/fill has been or will be completed by County forces (SOW-0200, SOW-0201).

2.5 **(F-003) Verify scope boundary alignment:** Before starting design, verify that the scope boundary between County-supplied and Proponent-supplied items (as recorded in Datasheet Conditions table) matches the agreed County responsibilities confirmed at the site meeting (Step 1.3). If any discrepancy exists, record it and resolve with County before proceeding. This verification ensures the design does not inadvertently include or exclude County-scope items.

---

### Step 3 — Develop Preliminary Grading Strategy

3.1 Using site survey data (or aerial/topographic data if survey not yet complete), establish existing grade conditions across the expansion area and surrounding site.

3.2 Develop a preliminary grading concept that:
- Achieves positive drainage (minimum slope away from building and pad areas — ASSUMPTION: minimum 2% grade on gravel surfaces and 1% on hardstand where achievable; confirm against Alberta practice; see Specification R-014 for normative treatment)
- Directs stormwater away from neighboring properties (R-01 §3.4; SOW-0021)
- Accounts for future storm events (design storm return period — TBD; confirm with County or apply local Alberta practice)
- Integrates with the building addition footprint (130 ft x 100 ft), existing Old North Shop (105 ft x 40 ft), Cement Pad West, Cement Pad East, Gravel Pad North, and driving surfaces
- Provides clearance and drainage for the exterior wash bay mud sump (east side)

3.3 Identify any areas requiring cut or fill beyond the County-completed bulk earthworks. Confirm scope with County.

3.4 Develop approximate finished grade elevations (spot elevations or preliminary contours) for:
- Building pad area
- Driving surfaces and turning areas
- Cement Pad West, Cement Pad East, and Gravel Pad North areas
- Mud sump surrounds

---

### Step 4 — Develop Preliminary Driving Surface Design

4.1 Establish the layout of gravel driving surfaces including:
- Primary access route(s) to the new addition
- Turning areas for motor scraper class equipment
- Connections between existing and new driving surfaces

4.2 Develop a preliminary cross-section for the gravel driving surface:
- Topsoil stripping depth (if not County-completed)
- Subbase/base course thickness (based on design vehicle loading and assumed subgrade)
- Gravel surface course thickness
- Cross-slope for drainage

4.3 Note: Gravel aggregate is to be supplied by the County/Landfill (App B notes; SOW-0203). The design should specify the gravel gradation and compaction requirements to be met by the contractor constructing the surface.

---

### Step 5 — Develop Preliminary Pad Layout and Design

5.1 Locate cement pads consistent with App B conceptual drawing:
- Cement Pad West (dimensions TBD at final design)
- Cement Pad East near wash bay / mud sump (dimensions TBD at final design)

5.2 Locate Gravel Pad North of new addition consistent with App B conceptual drawing (dimensions TBD at final design).

5.3 Establish preliminary grades and drainage for pad surrounds (pads to drain away from building and toward site drainage system).

5.4 Note: Contractor is responsible for cement supply and prep; Landfill/County supplies gravel (App B notes; SOW-0203).

---

### Step 6 — Develop Wash Bay Mud Sump Location and Access

6.1 Confirm exterior mud sump location on east side of building (per App B), coordinating with architectural/structural team for building wall interface.

6.2 Confirm sufficient clearance for an excavator to access and clean the sump (approach path, swing radius, overhead clearance — specific dimensions TBD from equipment data).

6.3 Establish surface drainage to direct wash bay effluent drainage toward sump.

6.4 Coordinate sump structural design with structural engineer (PKG-002) as the sump structure may require structural design input. See Guidance — Considerations — Mud sump structural coordination with PKG-002 for design implications.

---

### Step 7 — Prepare Preliminary Design Package

7.1 Compile the following artifacts for the preliminary design submission:

| Artifact | Content |
|---|---|
| Preliminary site layout drawing | Building footprints (new and existing), Cement Pad West, Cement Pad East, Gravel Pad North, driving surfaces, mud sump location, drainage direction arrows or preliminary contours, topsoil stripping limits |
| Conceptual grading strategy | Narrative and/or sketch showing drainage flow paths, approximate finished grades, design slope criteria |
| Drainage approach overview | Written description of stormwater management strategy; confirm positive drainage and no adverse impact on neighboring drainage |
| Driving surface design narrative | Design vehicle, design basis, assumed cross-section, topsoil stripping approach |
| Design summary report | Design basis assumptions, constraints, items to be confirmed at final design (particularly geotech-dependent items) |
| Presentation materials | Formatted for County project team review and approval |

7.2 Ensure all assumptions are clearly labeled as ASSUMPTION and all items contingent on geotech or survey data are labeled TBD.

7.3 Cross-check preliminary civil layout against Appendix B conceptual drawings for spatial consistency.

7.4 Coordinate with other disciplines (architectural, structural, plumbing) to confirm:
- Building setback from site boundary
- Mud sump connection to wash bay drainage (plumbing interface)
- Foundation perimeter drainage approach (structural interface)

---

### Step 8 — Internal QA Review

8.1 Civil Engineer reviews the preliminary design package for:
- Compliance with RFP drainage requirements (R-002, R-003)
- Driving surface design completeness (R-004, R-005)
- Consistency with App B conceptual layout
- Clear identification of all assumptions and TBD items
- Scope boundary clarity (County vs. Proponent responsibilities)
- Topsoil stripping completeness — confirm that the stripping strategy addresses all driving surface areas (R-006) (C-003)
- Mud sump access clearance — confirm that excavator access clearance is dimensioned or addressed in the preliminary layout (R-009) (C-003)

8.2 Resolve any identified issues or document them as items for County discussion.

---

### Step 9 — Submit to County for Approval

9.1 Submit preliminary civil design package to the County project manager (confirmed at project award).

9.2 Track submission date and expected turnaround. County approval is required before final civil design commences (SOW-0010e).

9.3 If County requests revisions, address comments and resubmit. Document all revision cycles.

9.4 Obtain written confirmation of County approval before proceeding to final civil design (DEL-005-02 through DEL-005-07).

---

## Verification

| Check | How to Verify |
|---|---|
| Positive drainage demonstrated | Grading plan shows drainage arrows or contours directing flow away from building; no closed low points without drainage outlet |
| Neighboring drainage unaffected | Design narrative confirms drainage is contained within site; supporting calculations or narrative if drainage direction changes significantly |
| Driving surface suitable for design vehicle | Design narrative states design vehicle, GVW, and turning radius; cross-section thickness justified by design basis |
| Pads located per App B | Preliminary layout overlay-checked against App B conceptual drawing |
| Mud sump accessible by excavator | Clearance dimensions noted on layout or in narrative |
| All assumptions labeled | Document review — ASSUMPTION label present on all inferred values |
| All TBDs identified | Document review — TBD present where data is outstanding |
| County approval received | Written approval document on file before final design commences |
