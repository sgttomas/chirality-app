# Specification — DEL-005-01: Preliminary Civil Design

---

## Scope

### What this deliverable covers

DEL-005-01 is the Preliminary Civil Design package for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill (WDMLRL) Maintenance Shop Addition. It covers:

- Preliminary site grading approach and drainage strategy for the proposed lot (SW 14–44–21–W4, near Ferintosh, Alberta)
- Preliminary driving surface layout and design approach (gravel surface for motor scraper class equipment)
- Preliminary cement pad and gravel pad layout, consistent with Appendix B conceptual drawings
- Topsoil stripping strategy for all driving surfaces
- Preliminary stormwater and drainage approach demonstrating positive site drainage without altering neighboring property drainage conditions
- Design approach for the exterior wash bay mud sump
- Design narrative and presentation materials sufficient for County project team approval

This deliverable covers **PKG-005 Civil Design** preliminary-phase scope only. Final civil design is addressed by DEL-005-02 through DEL-005-07.

**Mandatory input event:** The mandatory site meeting (March 3, 2026, 10:00 a.m. MST at SW-14-44-21-W4 per RFP §3.2) is a required input to this deliverable. Non-attendance disqualifies the proposal. Design vehicle confirmation, scope boundary verification, and County preferences are expected to be confirmed at or following this meeting. (Source: R-01 §3.2; Procedure Prerequisites)

### What this deliverable excludes

- Final signed/stamped IFC civil drawings (produced at final design stage)
- Building structural design (PKG-002), except for coordination on mud sump structural requirements
- Landscape planting design (PKG-007) — the preliminary civil design establishes the graded topography; landscape planting and restoration are excluded and addressed in PKG-007
- Utility routing/tie-in design (PKG-018 construction scope and discipline packages)
- Brushing, clearing, bulk cut and fill (County responsibility per RFP §3.3.1)
- Supply of aggregate/gravel (County/Landfill responsibility per RFP §3.3.1 and App B notes)
- Geotechnical investigation and report (PKG-008); however, preliminary geotech information, if available, should be referenced

## Requirements

### R-001 County Approval
The Preliminary Civil Design shall be delivered to the County project team for review and approval before proceeding to final civil design.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2; SOW-0010e
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Note (D-002):** County acceptance criteria for judging the preliminary civil design submission are TBD. Expected evaluation dimensions may include: completeness of drainage approach, consistency with App B conceptual drawings, and clarity of design basis and assumptions. ASSUMPTION: these evaluation dimensions are inferred from RFP emphasis areas; confirm County acceptance criteria at site meeting or project award.

### R-002 Positive Site Drainage
The grading design shall account for positive site drainage and shall accommodate future storm events. Ponding or drainage toward the building or adjacent areas shall be avoided.
**Source:** R-01 §3.4; SOW-0020
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-003 Neighboring Property Drainage Protection
The grading design shall not alter the drainage conditions of neighboring properties.
**Source:** R-01 §3.4; SOW-0021
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-004 Driving Surface — Material
The final driving surface shall be gravel. The preliminary design shall establish the layout and cross-section approach for gravel driving surfaces.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2; SOW-0077
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-005 Driving Surface — Design Loading
The driving surface design shall accommodate the weight and turning radius of large construction equipment of motor scraper class.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2; SOW-0077
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-006 Topsoil Stripping
The preliminary design shall include a topsoil stripping strategy for all driving surfaces (if not already completed by the County).
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2; SOW-0075
**Priority:** Mandatory
**ASSUMPTION:** Per Decomposition D-010 (SOW-0075 note), topsoil stripping is assumed to be required from the Proponent scope unless confirmed otherwise by County at project award.

### R-007 Cement Pads
The preliminary design shall locate and provide preliminary design for cement pads consistent with the Appendix B conceptual drawings. This includes Cement Pad West and Cement Pad East (near wash bay).
**Source:** R-04 App B (Shop drawing); SOW-0078
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Note:** Contractor to supply cement and prep; Landfill/County to supply gravel (per App B notes). See CNF-005-01-01 in Guidance Conflict Table regarding cement pad configuration ambiguity.

### R-008 Gravel Pad
The preliminary design shall locate and provide preliminary design for the Gravel Pad North consistent with the Appendix B conceptual drawings.
**Source:** R-04 App B (Shop drawing); SOW-0079
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-009 Wash Bay Mud Sump
The preliminary design shall include the exterior wash bay mud sump location on the east side of the building, sized and positioned for excavator cleanout access.
**Source:** R-04 App B (Shop drawing); R-01 §3.4
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-010 Site Grading and Landscaping
The preliminary design shall establish a grading and landscaping approach for the proposed lot that integrates with the building addition, existing Old North Shop, pads, and driving surfaces.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2; SOW-0076
**Priority:** Mandatory

### R-011 Code Compliance
The civil design shall adhere to all applicable Alberta building codes, regulations, and Alberta Safety Codes.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2, §3.4; SOW-0008, SOW-0009
**Priority:** Mandatory
**Note (A-001, A-002):** Specific applicable codes remain TBD. The Alberta Building Code edition year applicable to this project has not been identified in accessible source documents. Specific Camrose County municipal development standards applicable to site grading and drainage have not been identified. Both require confirmation — see Standards table below.

### R-012 P.Eng. Stamp (Final Design — referenced for context)
While the preliminary design does not require a stamp for County approval, the Civil Engineer responsible must be licensed to practice in Alberta, as all IFC drawings at final design must be signed/stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2; SOW-0018
**Priority:** Informational at preliminary stage; mandatory at final design stage.

### R-013 Coordination with Geotechnical Investigation
The preliminary civil design shall be developed with reference to available geotechnical and survey information. If the geotechnical report (PKG-008 / SOW-0001) is not available at the time of preliminary design, the design shall state the assumptions made and identify items to be confirmed at final design.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2; SOW-0001, SOW-0002
**Priority:** Mandatory (best-effort at preliminary stage)

### R-014 Minimum Slope Criteria (ASSUMPTION — to be confirmed)
The preliminary grading design shall achieve minimum slopes of 2% grade on gravel surfaces and 1% on hardstand areas where achievable.
**Source:** Procedure Step 3.2 (ASSUMPTION — derived from Alberta engineering practice; not stated in RFP)
**Priority:** TBD — ASSUMPTION. The Civil Engineer should confirm these values against Alberta practice and applicable municipal standards. If confirmed, this requirement should be elevated to Mandatory. If not applicable, remove at final design stage.
**Note (F-001):** This requirement was identified during semantic lensing as a weak statement in the Procedure. It is recorded here as a formal TBD requirement to ensure normative treatment rather than being embedded only in procedural text.
