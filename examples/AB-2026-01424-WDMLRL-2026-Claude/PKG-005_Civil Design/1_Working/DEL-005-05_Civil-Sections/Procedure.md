# Procedure — DEL-005-05: Civil Sections & Details

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps required to produce the Civil Sections & Details drawing set (DEL-005-05) for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. It covers the drafting, engineering review, coordination, and IFC issuance activities that result in a complete, P.Eng.-stamped drawing set ready for construction use.

**Source for deliverable context:** RFP §3.3.2; _CONTEXT.md; Decomposition — DEL-005-05.

## Steps

### Step 1: Confirm Scope of Details Required

1.1 Review the Site Grading Plan (DEL-005-02), Drainage Plan (DEL-005-03), and Driving Surface & Pad Layout Plan (DEL-005-04) to identify all unique civil elements that require a section or detail drawing.

1.2 Prepare a drawing register listing anticipated sheets, detail numbers, and subjects. Confirm the register with the civil engineering team lead before drafting commences.

1.3 Confirm with the structural engineer (PKG-002) whether any civil-structural interface conditions (e.g., at pad edges adjacent to foundation walls, service pit perimeter, or retaining wall footings) require joint coordination details.

1.4 Confirm with PKG-006 (plumbing) the wash bay mud sump connection invert and any civil-plumbing interface boundary.

1.5 Confirm with PKG-004 (electrical) and utility providers the routing and crossing locations of electrical, gas, and communications services beneath civil surfaces.

**Output:** Confirmed drawing register.

---

### Step 2: Obtain Geotechnical Inputs

2.1 Receive the geotechnical investigation report from PKG-008.

2.1a **(F-003) Survey Datum Confirmation:** Before drafting any sections that reference elevations (drainage inverts, finished grades, cut/fill depths), confirm the survey datum and benchmark reference point with PKG-008 surveyor. Document the datum reference (e.g., geodetic datum, local benchmark ID and elevation) on the general notes sheet. Sections that reference elevations without a confirmed datum are incomplete. **Proposed authority:** PKG-008 surveyor. *[Source: Procedure.md, Step 2 and Prerequisites table — lensing item F-003]*

2.2 Extract the following design parameters:
- Subgrade soil classification and CBR (or equivalent bearing value)
- Recommended subbase/base course thickness for heavy construction equipment loading (motor scraper equivalent axle load)
- Frost depth for central Alberta at this site
- Subgrade preparation and compaction requirements
- Groundwater depth (for mud sump invert design)

2.3 If the geotechnical report is not yet available: document TBD placeholders for the above parameters in draft section drawings and flag for update before IFC stamp.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (geotech report required for design).

---

### Step 3: Draft Typical Sections

3.1 Draft the typical gravel driving surface section showing:
- Wearing course (gravel) — depth TBD pending geotech
- Subbase — depth TBD pending geotech
- Subgrade preparation — type TBD pending geotech
- Cross-fall slope (% — TBD consistent with drainage plan)
- Material note referencing DEL-005-07 specification
- Note: "County to supply aggregate — see RFP §3.3.1"

3.2 Draft gravel pad and cement pad typical sections, showing:
- Pad material and depth (cement pad — thickness TBD; gravel pad — depth TBD)
- Subgrade preparation
- Edge restraint/condition at pad perimeter (TBD)

3.3 Draft typical grading/fill section showing:
- Topsoil stripping depth (TBD — confirm with grading plan)
- Fill compaction lifts and compaction reference
- Finished grade cross-fall
- Bench or transition geometry (TBD — confirm with grading design)

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; §3.4; ASSUMPTION: standard civil drawing practice.

---

### Step 4: Draft Drainage Structure Details

4.1 For each drainage structure type identified in the Drainage Plan (DEL-005-03) — swales, culverts, catchbasins, or equivalent — draft construction details showing:
- Section through structure showing material, dimensions, inverts, and connections
- Inlet/outlet protection details (TBD)
- Connection to downstream drainage system

4.2 Coordinate drainage structure inverts with the grading plan finished grades to confirm positive drainage is maintained through all structures.

4.3 If invert elevations cannot be confirmed without survey data, hold as TBD and flag for update before IFC.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 (positive site drainage requirement).

---

### Step 5: Draft Wash Bay Mud Sump Detail

5.1 Draft plan and section views of the exterior mud sump showing:
- Overall dimensions (TBD — coordinate with PKG-006 for capacity requirements)
- Construction material (concrete, liner, or equivalent — TBD)
- Connection pipe from wash bay to sump — size, invert, slope (TBD — coordinate with PKG-006)
- Sump outlet / cleanout provision for excavator access
- Sump bottom elevation relative to finished grade (TBD — confirm no groundwater conflict)
- Notation of County-supplied vs. contractor-supplied components (TBD)

5.2 Confirm with PKG-006 plumbing drawings the wash bay floor drain connection point and invert before finalizing the connection detail.

5.3 Confirm with the Owner / PKG-018 contractor the intended method of mud sump cleanout (excavator cleanout as indicated in RFP §3.4) to ensure dimensions are appropriate.

**Source:** RFP §3.4; App B (Shop) — conceptual drawing.

---

### Step 6: Draft Sump Drain Details (Repair Bays)

6.1 Draft section/detail at each sump drain location in the repair bays showing:
- Drain frame and grate (type TBD — coordinate with PKG-006)
- Sump cast into slab or below slab — geometry TBD
- Connection pipe routing from drain to building perimeter (TBD — coordinate with PKG-006)
- Civil-plumbing boundary notation

6.2 Coordinate with PKG-002 (structural) to confirm drain placement does not conflict with structural slab design, rebar, or steel plate embedments.

**Source:** RFP §3.4.

---

### Step 7: Draft Utility Crossing Details

7.1 For each confirmed utility crossing beneath civil surfaces (electrical, gas, communications):
- Draft section showing utility type, pipe/conduit size (TBD), sleeve/casing, bedding, cover depth, and backfill specification
- Reference DEL-005-07 for backfill material requirements
- Show finished surface restoration

7.2 Confirm cover depth requirements for each utility type against applicable standards (gas — NGTL or AER requirements; electrical — CEC requirements; ASSUMPTION: specific cover depths TBD pending utility design and permit conditions).

7.3 If utility routing is not yet confirmed, draft a generic utility crossing detail with TBD note indicating it will be updated once routing is finalized.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (utility coordination); ASSUMPTION: standard detail required for any road crossing of utilities.

---

### Step 8: Draft Driveway Apron and Pad Interface Details

8.1 Draft driveway apron/transition details at entry/exit points from public or internal road to gravel driving surface, if applicable. ASSUMPTION: required if gravel surface connects to a paved or hardened road.

8.2 Draft pad interface/edge details at all transitions between surface types:
- Gravel driving surface to gravel pad
- Gravel pad to cement pad
- Cement pad to building slab/foundation
- Edge restraint/curb at pad perimeters (TBD)

8.3 Confirm edge condition design with PKG-002 (structural) at building foundation/pad interface.

**Source:** App B (Shop) — conceptual drawing (pad locations); ASSUMPTION: standard civil practice.

---

### Step 9: Incorporate General Notes Sheet

9.1 Prepare a general notes and legend sheet (or notes block) that includes:
- Specification cross-reference (DEL-005-07)
- Standard abbreviations and drawing symbols
- Geotechnical report reference (PKG-008 report number TBD)
- Survey datum reference (TBD pending PKG-008 survey)
- Compaction standard reference (e.g., Proctor, ASTM D698 or equivalent — TBD)
- County aggregate supply notation (per RFP §3.3.1)
- Note: "All IFC drawings to be signed and stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta per RFP §3.3.2"
- Note: "Contact the County project representative prior to all inspections — RFP §3.3.2"

---

### Step 10: Internal Engineering Review

10.1 Civil engineering team lead reviews all drafted sections and details for:
- Engineering accuracy and consistency with geotechnical recommendations
- Consistency with grading plan (DEL-005-02), drainage plan (DEL-005-03), and layout plan (DEL-005-04)
- Compliance with applicable Alberta Safety Codes and permit conditions
- Completeness of notes, dimensions, and material callouts

10.2 Resolve all internal review comments. Document resolution of TBD items.

10.3 Confirm that all mandatory prerequisites (Step 1, Step 2) have been satisfied before proceeding to Step 11.

---

### Step 11: Interdisciplinary Coordination Review

11.1 Circulate relevant detail sheets to:
- PKG-002 (structural) — for slab/foundation interface, sump drain conflicts
- PKG-006 (plumbing) — for mud sump, sump drain, and wash bay drain interface
- PKG-004 (electrical) and utility providers — for utility crossing cover and location confirmation
- PKG-018 (construction) — for constructability review, if contractor is engaged at time of design

11.2 Record and resolve all coordination comments. Update drawings accordingly.

11.3 **(X-003) Coordination Record Requirements:** Formal documentation of interdisciplinary coordination outcomes shall include, at minimum:
- Date of coordination review
- Disciplines/packages involved
- Comments received (with originator identified)
- Resolution status and action taken for each comment
- Drawing revision reference where change was incorporated
- Sign-off or acknowledgment by the commenting discipline

**Note:** A coordination log template or minimum content standard should be established at project initiation. Current instruction ("record and resolve") does not specify what constitutes an adequate coordination record. *[Source: Procedure.md, Step 11 — lensing item X-003]*

---

### Step 12: IFC Stamp and Issue

12.1 Confirm all TBD items have been resolved or formally documented as deferred items with Owner acceptance.

12.2 Apply P.Eng. stamp and signature of the civil engineer of record, licensed in Alberta, on each sheet in the drawing set.

12.3 Issue drawing set as part of the coordinated IFC package to:
- Owner (Camrose County) / Owner's project representative
- Construction contractor (PKG-018)
- Permit authority (if required)
- Project records

12.4 Retain stamped originals in project records.

12.5 **(A-004) Inspection Hold-Points:** Prior to cover-up of civil elements, confirm that all required inspection hold-points or witness-points have been identified for regulatory/County review. At minimum, the following hold-points should be scheduled (TBD — confirm with County project representative and permit authority):
- Subgrade inspection before gravel placement
- Compaction testing witness-point before surface course placement
- Drainage structure inspection before backfill
- Utility crossing inspection before backfill

**Note:** The RFP references County inspection involvement. Specific hold-point requirements are TBD pending permit conditions and County project representative confirmation. **Proposed authority:** Civil engineer / County project representative. *[Source: Procedure.md, Step 12 — lensing item A-004]*

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2.
