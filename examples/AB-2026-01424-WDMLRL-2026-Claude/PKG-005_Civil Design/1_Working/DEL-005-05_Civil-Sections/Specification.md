# Specification — DEL-005-05: Civil Sections & Details

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-005-05 (Civil Sections & Details) is the drawing set that provides cross-sectional views and construction-level detail drawings for all civil infrastructure elements associated with the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition at SW 14–44–21–W4, near Ferintosh, Alberta.

The drawing set documents the constructed form of civil elements designed under PKG-005, providing contractors with the information required to install gravel driving surfaces, grading/fill transitions, drainage features, pad interfaces, utility crossings, and other site civil elements. It is issued as part of the coordinated Issued for Construction (IFC) drawing set.

This deliverable covers:
- Typical and site-specific pavement/gravel driving surface sections
- Grading and fill section profiles at key transitions
- Drainage structure sections and connection details (swales, culverts, catchbasins, or similar — type TBD based on grading and drainage design)
- Curb, gutter, and edge condition details (if applicable to design solution)
- Driveway apron and transition details
- Retaining wall sections (if the grading design requires retained earth — TBD)
- Utility crossing and protection details
- Wash bay mud sump section and connection detail
- Standard construction notes and material specifications in detail format

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Site Grading Plan (covered by DEL-005-02)
- Drainage Plan (covered by DEL-005-03)
- Driving Surface & Pad Layout Plan (covered by DEL-005-04)
- Civil Calculation Package (covered by DEL-005-06)
- Civil Specification (covered by DEL-005-07)
- Structural details for the building foundation, service pit, or crane supports (PKG-002)
- Plumbing/mechanical details for cistern, septic, or interior floor drains (PKG-006)
- Landscape planting details (PKG-007)
- Bulk cut/fill work (Owner/County scope — OUT)

## Requirements

### REQ-005-05-001: IFC Drawing Stamp
All drawings in this set issued for construction shall be signed and stamped by a professional engineer (P.Eng.) licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 | **Mandatory**

---

### REQ-005-05-002: Driving Surface Section — Design Load
The typical gravel driving surface section shall be designed to accommodate the weight and turning radius of large construction equipment, including a motor scraper.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 | **Mandatory**

---

### REQ-005-05-003: Driving Surface Material
The final driving surface material shall be gravel. The section detail shall specify gravel material type, gradation, depth, and compaction requirements.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 | **Mandatory**
**Note:** Specific gravel gradation and depth values are TBD, pending geotechnical report (PKG-008) and Civil Specification (DEL-005-07).
**TBD (C-001):** Specify the compaction test standard to be used (e.g., ASTM D698 Standard Proctor, ASTM D1557 Modified Proctor, or equivalent Alberta standard). Procedure Step 9.1 references "Proctor, ASTM D698 or equivalent" in a general notes context; this should be elevated to a normative requirement. **Proposed authority:** Civil engineer / geotech report. *[Source: Specification.md, REQ-005-05-003; Procedure.md, Step 9.1 — lensing item C-001]*

---

### REQ-005-05-004: Grading — Positive Site Drainage
Grading sections shall reflect a design that achieves positive site drainage and accounts for future storm events. Cross-sections shall show finished grades directing surface runoff away from building foundations and pad surfaces toward intended drainage features.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 | **Mandatory**
**TBD (C-002):** Specify minimum cross-fall slope percentage for gravel surfaces to constitute "positive drainage," or reference the Drainage Plan (DEL-005-03) as the governing source for slope values. Current wording does not provide a quantitative standard for what constitutes "positive drainage" in gravel surface conditions. **Proposed authority:** Civil engineer / drainage design (DEL-005-03). *[Source: Specification.md, REQ-005-05-004 — lensing item C-002]*

---

### REQ-005-05-005: Grading — No Alteration of Neighbouring Drainage
The grading design depicted in sections shall not alter the drainage conditions of neighbouring properties. Where sections show grade transitions at property or site boundaries, the design shall demonstrate containment of altered drainage within the project site.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 | **Mandatory**

---

### REQ-005-05-006: Wash Bay Mud Sump Detail
A detail drawing of the exterior mud sump shall be included, showing construction section, connection to the wash bay drain, dimensions, and materials sufficient for construction. The mud sump is located on the exterior of the building on the east/wash bay side.

**Source:** RFP §3.4; App B (Shop) — conceptual drawing | **Mandatory**
**Note:** Mud sump dimensions, invert elevations, and connection invert are TBD pending drainage design (DEL-005-03) and plumbing design (PKG-006 coordination).

### REQ-005-05-007: Sump Drain Details in Repair Bays
Section/detail drawings shall show the sump drain configuration within repair bays, including drain type, connection routing, and interface with the building slab. Coordination with PKG-006 (plumbing) is required for drain sizing and connection details.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 | **Mandatory**
**Note:** Specific invert levels and drain sizing are TBD pending coordination with PKG-006.

### REQ-005-05-008: Topsoil Stripping Detail
Where applicable, section drawings shall document the topsoil stripping depth and subgrade preparation requirements for driving surfaces and pad areas (if not already performed by Owner).

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 | **Mandatory**
**Note:** Areas already stripped by County forces at time of design shall be confirmed and noted accordingly on drawings. ASSUMPTION: Proponent is responsible for stripping of any remaining driving surface areas not addressed by County.

### REQ-005-05-009: Utility Crossing Details
Details shall be provided for civil utility crossings (gas, electrical, communications) where they pass beneath or through gravel driving surfaces, pads, or graded areas. Details shall show cover depths, sleeving/protection requirements, and backfill specifications.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (utility tie-in coordination); ASSUMPTION: utility crossing details are a standard component of civil section drawing sets for projects of this type. | **Anticipated**
**Note:** Specific utility locations and routing are TBD pending coordination with PKG-004 (electrical) and utility providers.

Detail drawings shall show the interface conditions between gravel driving surfaces, gravel pad, and cement pad areas (as shown on the conceptual floor plan). Details shall include edge conditions, transitions, and any required edging or restraint.

**Source:** App B (Shop) — conceptual drawing showing Gravel Pad, Cement Pad; ASSUMPTION: pad interface details are required for construction clarity. | **Anticipated**
