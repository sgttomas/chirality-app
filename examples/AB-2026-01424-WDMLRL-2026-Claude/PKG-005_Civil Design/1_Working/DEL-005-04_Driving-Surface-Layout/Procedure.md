# Procedure — DEL-005-04: Driving Surface & Pad Layout Plan

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce the Driving Surface & Pad Layout Plan drawing set for the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition (DEL-005-04). It covers the workflow from design inputs through to IFC drawing issue, including coordination checkpoints with upstream and parallel deliverables. The target output is a complete, P.Eng.-stamped drawing set ready for use by the construction contractor (PKG-018 — Sitework & Civil Construction).

## Steps

### Step 1 — Confirm Design Inputs and Scope Boundaries

1.1 Obtain and review the geotechnical report from PKG-008. Extract design subgrade parameters (CBR or equivalent) for use in pavement section design.

1.2 Obtain topographic survey data from PKG-008. Establish a working coordinate datum and base drawing.

1.3 Confirm building footprint, drive-through bay locations (24' wide bays per App B), wash bay location and mud sump location (east side of building per App B) with PKG-001 (Architectural) and PKG-002 (Structural).

1.4 Confirm final pad extents (cement pad and gravel pad) with the Owner/Architect. Note: Appendix B is conceptual; final dimensions require Owner confirmation. Record any confirmed dimensions or any remaining assumptions (**ASSUMPTION** label required if unconfirmed).

1.5 Obtain or confirm the specific motor scraper class vehicle envelope (turning radius, vehicle dimensions, axle loads, tire contact pressure) from the Owner/Operator (Camrose County / WDMLRL). If not available, record a conservative class assumption (**ASSUMPTION**) and confirm with the Owner whether a conservative class assumption is acceptable as a permanent design basis or whether specific model confirmation is required before IFC issue. **TBD (F-003):** The specific motor scraper model(s) operated at the landfill have not been confirmed. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item F-003.)

1.6 Confirm aggregate supply boundary: Landfill/County supplies gravel aggregate; Contractor supplies cement and prep. Ensure this is reflected in drawing callouts and coordinated with DEL-005-07 (Civil Specification).

1.7 **(F-004) Document the aggregate supply boundary** in a form traceable on the drawings and in coordination with DEL-005-07. The documentation should clearly indicate on each relevant drawing sheet which materials are Owner/Landfill-supplied and which are Contractor-supplied, to avoid cost disputes during construction. Verify that the supply boundary notation is consistent across the drawing set and with the civil specification. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item F-004; Guidance Principle 5.)

---

### Step 2 — Establish Pavement Design Section

2.1 Select an appropriate pavement design method for gravel industrial yard surfaces based on Alberta practice and the geotechnical report subgrade parameters.

2.2 Design the structural pavement section for the gravel driving surface to carry the confirmed motor scraper class design loading. Document layer materials and thicknesses.

2.3 Confirm adequacy of the pavement section for the cement pad structural requirements (cement pad prep and construction details to be included in the drawing set).

2.4 Record pavement design assumptions and design vehicle parameters. These form the basis for the civil calculation package (DEL-005-06).

---

### Step 3 — Develop Driving Surface Layout Plan

3.1 On the topographic survey base, lay out all driving surfaces, circulation routes, and apron areas. Ensure all routes serving the drive-through bays, wash bay, building entries, and pad areas are geometrically confirmed.

3.2 Check turning radius adequacy for the design vehicle (motor scraper class) at all corners, entries, and apron areas. Overlay turning templates on the plan. Adjust layout geometry as needed.

3.3 Lay out pad areas (cement pad and gravel pad) in confirmed locations consistent with Appendix B intent and Owner-confirmed dimensions.

3.4 **(A-004) Coordinate the wash bay mud sump area layout.** Confirm the mud sump footprint and excavator cleanout access zone (SOW-0027b) on the east side of the building. Verify that the driving surface layout around the east side of the building accommodates: (a) the sump's physical footprint, (b) clearance for excavator access for cleanout, and (c) unimpeded equipment circulation. Coordinate with building structure/plumbing design (PKG-001, PKG-002) and the plumbing drawing (App B - Plumbing). (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item A-004; Guidance Considerations, Coordination with the Wash Bay Mud Sump.)

3.5 Show topsoil stripping limits encompassing all driving surface areas and pad areas. Coordinate stripping limits with DEL-005-02 Site Grading Plan.

3.6 Add dimensional annotations, curve data, and horizontal geometry callouts to fully define the layout for construction.

---

### Step 4 — Establish Surface Grades and Cross-Slopes

4.1 In coordination with DEL-005-02 (Site Grading Plan), establish finished surface grades and cross-slopes for all driving surfaces and pads. Target positive drainage away from building entries and toward site drainage features.

4.2 Confirm that driving surface grades are consistent with the site grading design and that surface runoff from hardstand areas is accommodated by the drainage design (DEL-005-03).

4.3 Confirm that the design does not alter drainage conditions on neighboring properties (RFP §3.4, SOW-0021). Coordinate with DEL-005-03 for confirmation.

4.4 Add surface grade and cross-slope callouts to the layout plan drawings.

---

### Step 5 — Develop Construction Details

5.1 Prepare pavement design section drawing(s) showing layer thicknesses, materials, and compaction requirements for:
- Gravel driving surface
- Cement pad (sub-base and pad construction)
- Gravel pad (sub-base and surface)

5.2 Prepare edge condition and transition details at:
- Building interfaces (drive-through bay entries, building perimeter)
- Transition from driving surface to pad areas
- Site perimeter and property limits

5.3 Prepare traffic marking and signage callouts as required (TBD during design — confirm requirements with Owner and coordinate with applicable standards).

---

### Step 6 — Coordinate with Parallel Civil Deliverables

6.1 Cross-check driving surface layout and grades against DEL-005-02 (Site Grading Plan). Resolve any grade conflicts.

6.2 Cross-check surface drainage paths against DEL-005-03 (Drainage Plan). Confirm that drainage infrastructure locations (ditches, inlets, outlets) are consistent with driving surface layout.

6.3 Cross-check pad details and sections against DEL-005-05 (Civil Sections & Details). Avoid duplication; confirm which details reside in which deliverable.

6.4 Confirm that pavement design calculations are captured in DEL-005-06 (Civil Calculation Package).

6.5 Confirm that material specifications (gravel gradation, compaction standards, cement specifications) are addressed in DEL-005-07 (Civil Specification) rather than duplicated on drawings.

---

### Step 7 — Internal Review and QA

7.1 Complete an internal Civil Engineer review of all drawings for:
- Dimensional consistency and completeness
- Grade and drainage direction correctness
- Turning radius geometric adequacy
- Compliance with REQ-01 through REQ-10 (see Specification)
- Consistent terminology and callouts with other PKG-005 deliverables

7.2 Resolve any conflicts identified. If unresolvable from available information, record as TBD or flag for Owner ruling.

7.3 Confirm all non-trivial values and design parameters are source-cited or labeled ASSUMPTION.

---

### Step 8 — Preliminary Issue for County Review (Preliminary Design Stage)

> **Note:** This step applies to the preliminary design phase (DEL-005-01). The driving surface layout is one component of the preliminary civil design package. County approval of the preliminary design is a gate before IFC issue.

8.1 Include driving surface layout concept in the Preliminary Civil Design submission (DEL-005-01) for County approval.

8.2 Record County approval or comments. Incorporate approved or directed changes into the final design.

---

### Step 9 — P.Eng. Review, Stamp, and IFC Issue

9.1 The Civil Engineer of Record reviews all final drawings for technical adequacy, code compliance, and consistency.

9.2 The Civil Engineer of Record signs and stamps all IFC drawing sheets in accordance with RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0018). The stamping engineer must be licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.

9.3 Issue the complete IFC drawing set (DEL-005-04) to the construction contractor and County as required by the project delivery schedule.
