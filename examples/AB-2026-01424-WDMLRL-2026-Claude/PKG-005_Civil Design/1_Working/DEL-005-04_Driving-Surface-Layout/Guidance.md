# Guidance — DEL-005-04: Driving Surface & Pad Layout Plan

---

## Purpose

The Driving Surface & Pad Layout Plan exists to provide the construction team with a complete, buildable, P.Eng.-stamped design for all ground-level circulation, parking, and pad areas at the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition site. This deliverable translates the RFP's intent — a gravel driving surface capable of handling motor scraper class equipment, plus cement and gravel pad areas shown on the conceptual drawings — into precise dimensions, pavement sections, and construction details suitable for execution by PKG-018 (Sitework & Civil Construction).

Without this deliverable, the construction contractor cannot confirm turning geometry, pad extents, pavement structural design, or surface grades for the hardstand areas. It also establishes topsoil stripping limits, which must be defined before earthworks begin.

The deliverable directly supports:
- **OBJ-001**: Delivering a fully functional, code-compliant facility satisfying the County's operational program (including site access for large equipment). (Source: WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §5)
- **OBJ-003**: Producing complete, P.Eng.-stamped IFC design documentation before construction begins. (Source: WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §5)

## Principles

### 1 — Design for the Operational Vehicle, Not a Generic Standard
The RFP explicitly identifies the motor scraper as the design vehicle for driving surfaces (RFP §3.3.2, SOW-0077). Motor scrapers are large self-propelled earth-moving machines with significant turning radii, axle loads, and tire contact pressures. The Civil Engineer should verify the specific motor scraper model(s) operated at the landfill (if available) and use the worst-case geometric and loading envelope for design. If the specific model cannot be confirmed, a conservative assumption for the class should be used and labeled **ASSUMPTION**.

### 2 — Pavement Section Must Be Grounded in the Geotechnical Report
The pavement section design for the gravel driving surface is only as reliable as the subgrade characterization it is based on. The geotech report (PKG-008) is a prerequisite to finalizing the structural section. The Civil Engineer should coordinate with the geotechnical engineer to obtain appropriate design subgrade CBR or equivalent parameters, then size aggregate base thickness accordingly. Proceeding with a generic section without geotech input is not recommended.

**Pavement Design Method Selection (F-001):** The RFP does not prescribe a specific pavement design method (e.g., AASHTO 1993, empirical layer design for gravel roads, mechanistic-empirical). The Civil Engineer of Record should select a method appropriate for an industrial gravel yard subject to heavy repeated loading from motor scraper class equipment. Selection criteria should include: (a) compatibility with the geotechnical report's subgrade characterization parameters; (b) suitability for unpaved/gravel surface applications with heavy vehicle loading; (c) consistency with Alberta transportation and municipal engineering practice. The selected method and its rationale should be documented in DEL-005-06 (Civil Calculation Package). (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item F-001; Specification ASSUMPTION note after REQ-10.)

**Subgrade Preparation (E-002):** Between topsoil stripping (REQ-03) and aggregate base placement, the subgrade must be prepared. The RFP addresses topsoil stripping (SOW-0075) and the geotech basis for pavement design (SOW-0001) but does not specify intermediate subgrade preparation steps such as proof-rolling, moisture conditioning, or subgrade compaction. The Civil Engineer should address subgrade preparation requirements in either this deliverable's pavement design sections or in DEL-005-07 (Civil Specification), ensuring no gap exists between "strip topsoil" and "place aggregate." (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item E-002; Specification REQ-03, REQ-10; Procedure Steps 2-3.)

### 3 — Drainage Is a Cross-Deliverable Constraint
Driving surface grades are constrained by the site grading plan (DEL-005-02) and must feed stormwater management as designed in the drainage plan (DEL-005-03). The layout and slope design for this deliverable should be developed in close coordination with the grading and drainage deliverables, not in isolation. The RFP's requirement that grading not alter neighboring property drainage (SOW-0021) applies to the driving surface grades as much as to the broader site.

### 4 — Pad Extents Are Conceptual Until Confirmed
The cement pad and gravel pad shown on Appendix B (Shop) are conceptual. The drawing shows approximate relative positions and rough proportions but is not dimensionally precise. The Civil Engineer should:
- Confirm final pad extents with the Architect and Owner,
- Coordinate with PKG-018 on construction sequencing (pads may be constructed before or after the building, with implications for equipment access),
- Confirm that pad locations do not conflict with underground utilities, mud sump, or drainage infrastructure.

**Decision Gate for Conceptual-to-Confirmed Transition (D-003):** Pad extents remain "conceptual" until a documented Owner sign-off on final dimensions is obtained. The transition from conceptual to confirmed requires: (a) the Civil Engineer presents proposed final pad dimensions with supporting rationale (e.g., equipment clearance, operational layout review); (b) the Owner/Architect reviews and approves in writing (letter, email, or signed meeting minutes); (c) the approved dimensions are recorded in the design file and referenced on the drawings. Until this gate is passed, all pad dimensions on drawings should carry a "PRELIMINARY -- SUBJECT TO OWNER CONFIRMATION" notation. This gate should be passed before the Preliminary Design submission (Step 8) if possible, or documented as outstanding if not. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item D-003; Guidance Trade-offs table, "Gravel pad extents"; Specification REQ-06, REQ-07.)

### 5 — Aggregate Supply Boundary Affects Drawing Callouts
The RFP and Appendix B establish a material supply split: the Contractor supplies cement and preparation; the Landfill/County supplies gravel aggregate (SOW-0203). This division of supply must be clearly reflected in drawing callouts and the civil specification (DEL-005-07) so the construction contractor understands their supply obligations. Ambiguity here could result in cost disputes.

## Considerations

### Turning Radius for Motor Scraper Class Equipment
Motor scrapers (e.g., CAT 623 or similar) have overall lengths in the range of 15–16 m (approximately 50–52 ft) and minimum turning radii that may require wide circulation aprons. The driving surface layout should verify that access routes to the building's drive-through bays (24' wide per App B) and to the wash bay have sufficient apron width and turning clearance for these vehicles. **ASSUMPTION:** The Civil Engineer will verify actual turning envelope requirements against the specific equipment used at the landfill.

### Coordination with the Wash Bay Mud Sump
The Wash Bay Mud Sump is located on the exterior east side of the building (App B, App C). The driving surface layout around the east side of the building must accommodate the sump's footprint, provide clearance for excavator cleanout access (SOW-0027b), and not impede equipment circulation. This is a coordination point between the civil design and the building structure/plumbing.

### Topsoil Stripping Sequencing
Topsoil stripping (SOW-0075) is listed as a Proponent responsibility conditional on the Owner not having already performed it. The stripping limits shown on this deliverable should be clearly coordinated with DEL-005-02 and the overall site earthworks sequence. Stripping should occur before gravel placement and ideally before any site grading is finalized to avoid double-handling.

### Surface Grades on Hardstand Areas
Gravel driving surfaces and pads should be graded to drain away from building entries and toward site drainage features, with typical cross-slopes (ASSUMPTION: 2–4% cross-slope is common practice for gravel surfaces in Alberta, but the Civil Engineer should confirm based on site conditions and drainage design). Slopes that are too flat risk ponding; slopes that are excessive risk aggregate migration under heavy equipment loading.

### Frost Depth and Seasonal Design Considerations (E-001)
The project site is located in central Alberta (near Ferintosh, approximately 52 degrees N latitude), where frost depths are significant and affect pavement and pad design. The Civil Engineer should address the following seasonal and climate-related considerations:

- **Frost heave potential:** Subgrade soils susceptible to frost heave may require additional aggregate depth, non-frost-susceptible fill, or other mitigation. The geotechnical report (PKG-008) is expected to characterize frost susceptibility of the subgrade soils. **ASSUMPTION:** Frost depth considerations will be addressed in the geotech report; specific frost depth values are TBD pending receipt of that report.
- **Freeze-thaw impacts on aggregate base:** Repeated freeze-thaw cycles can degrade aggregate base performance. Aggregate gradation and quality specifications (in DEL-005-07) should account for freeze-thaw durability.
- **Construction season limitations:** Central Alberta's construction season for earthworks and gravel placement is generally limited by frozen ground and spring thaw conditions. The Civil Engineer should consider whether the pavement section design assumes warm-weather placement or accommodates cold-weather conditions.

(Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item E-001; general central Alberta climate conditions; Specification REQ-10, Standards.)

### IFC Drawing Coordination
As a member of a coordinated civil drawing set (PKG-005), this deliverable's drawing set must be cross-referenced to DEL-005-02 (Site Grading), DEL-005-03 (Drainage), and DEL-005-05 (Civil Sections). Drawing numbering, north arrow, scale, and drawing border standards should be consistent across all PKG-005 deliverables.

**Drawing Standards Reference (E-003):** The specific drawing standards to be used (border format, scale conventions, north arrow placement, sheet numbering system) should be defined by reference to the PKG-005 drawing standard or project CAD standards document. ## Trade-offs

| Trade-off | Option A | Option B | Recommended Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pavement section design before vs. after geotech | Design with assumed subgrade (faster) | Wait for geotech report (slower but accurate) | Wait for geotech report (REQ-10); risk of over/under design if assumed |
| Gravel pad extents — follow App B exactly vs. refine with Owner | Use App B dimensions as-is | Confirm with Owner/Architect | Confirm with Owner; App B is conceptual (ASSUMPTION) |
| Turning radius — use worst-case generic motor scraper vs. site-specific model | Generic conservative design | Site-specific verified model | Use site-specific if available; generic if not, labeled ASSUMPTION |
| Surface drainage — shed to perimeter vs. central collection | Shed to perimeter (simpler for hardstand) | Central collection with inlets | Coordinate with DEL-005-03; perimeter approach typical for gravel yards |
