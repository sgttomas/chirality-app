# Specification — DEL-005-04: Driving Surface & Pad Layout Plan

---

## Scope

### Included

This deliverable covers the complete civil design of all driving surfaces, parking and circulation areas, and pad areas at the WDMLRL Maintenance Shop Addition site (SW 14–44–21–W4, Camrose County, Alberta), specifically:

- Layout and dimensional design of all gravel driving surfaces
- Structural pavement section design for motor scraper class heavy equipment loading
- Layout and construction details for the cement pad
- Layout and construction details for the gravel pad
- Topsoil stripping limits for all driving surface areas
- Surface grades and cross-slopes to achieve positive site drainage
- Edge conditions, transitions, and interface treatments
- Horizontal geometry including turning radii for motor scraper class vehicles
- Traffic markings and signage callouts (TBD -- to be developed during design; Owner to confirm requirements; resolution trigger and deadline to be established by Project Manager. Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item C-002)

## Requirements

### REQ-01 — Driving Surface Material
**Statement:** The final driving surface shall be gravel.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0077)
**Verification:** Drawing callouts confirm surface material as gravel.

### REQ-02 — Driving Surface Design Loading
**Statement:** The driving surface shall be designed to accommodate the weight and turning radius of heavy construction equipment of motor scraper class.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0077)
**Verification:** Pavement design section demonstrates adequate structural capacity against a quantified acceptance criterion (TBD -- to be defined after geotech report receipt; e.g., minimum aggregate base thickness, design CBR, or load equivalency); turning radius diagrams confirm geometric adequacy against a defined minimum turning radius or design vehicle template (TBD -- to be confirmed after motor scraper model confirmation; see Procedure Step 1.5).

> **TBD (F-002):** The acceptance criterion for "adequate structural capacity" is not yet quantified. The Civil Engineer of Record shall define quantified acceptance criteria (e.g., minimum aggregate base thickness, design CBR, or load equivalency) after receipt of the geotechnical report (PKG-008). (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item F-002.)

> **TBD (D-002):** The measurable acceptance criterion for turning radius adequacy is not yet defined. The Civil Engineer shall specify the minimum turning radius or design vehicle template that constitutes geometric adequacy after the specific motor scraper model is confirmed. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item D-002.)

### REQ-03 — Topsoil Stripping
**Statement:** Topsoil shall be stripped from all driving surface areas prior to driving surface construction.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0075); WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md D-010 note
**Verification:** Topsoil stripping limits shown on drawings encompass all driving surface areas.

> **NOTE (C-001):** SOW-0075 states topsoil stripping is a Proponent responsibility "if not already performed by Owner," introducing conditionality not reflected in the unconditional statement above. See Guidance Conflict Table C-002 for proposed resolution: design drawings should show full stripping limits regardless of execution responsibility; contract to clarify who performs the work. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item C-001.)

### REQ-04 — Positive Site Drainage
**Statement:** The driving surface and pad layout design shall achieve positive drainage across all paved and gravel surfaces, consistent with the site grading design. Ponding on driving surfaces shall be avoided.
**Source:** RFP §3.4 (SOW-0020)
**Verification:** Surface slopes and cross-fall callouts on drawings checked against an acceptable cross-slope range (TBD -- see note below); coordinated with DEL-005-02 Site Grading Plan.

> **TBD (A-005):** The acceptable cross-slope range for gravel driving surfaces is not yet defined. Common practice for gravel surfaces in Alberta is 2-4% (**ASSUMPTION** -- see Guidance Considerations, Surface Grades on Hardstand Areas). The Civil Engineer of Record shall confirm the minimum and maximum percent cross-slope acceptable for this site based on drainage design and equipment loading conditions. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item A-005.)

### REQ-05 — No Alteration of Neighboring Property Drainage
**Statement:** The design of driving surfaces and pad areas shall not alter drainage conditions of neighboring properties.
**Source:** RFP §3.4 (SOW-0021)
**Verification:** Civil Engineer review confirming drainage remains within site boundaries; coordinated with DEL-005-03 Drainage Plan.

### REQ-06 — Cement Pad Construction
**Statement:** A cement pad shall be constructed as shown on the conceptual drawings (Appendix B). The Contractor shall supply cement and preparation; the Landfill supplies aggregate/gravel.
**Source:** App B (Shop) (SOW-0078); RFP §3.3.1 (SOW-0203)
**Verification:** Cement pad shown on layout plan with dimensions and material callouts consistent with Appendix B intent.

> **NOTE (A-002):** The term "cement pad" as used in the RFP and conceptual drawings does not specify whether this refers to a Portland cement concrete slab or a cement-stabilized gravel pad. The material composition affects construction means, specification content, and cost allocation. Civil Engineer / Owner clarification is required. See Guidance Conflict Table C-003. **ASSUMPTION:** "Cement pad" refers to a Portland cement concrete slab based on common industry usage. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item A-002.)

### REQ-07 — Gravel Pad Construction
**Statement:** A gravel pad shall be constructed as shown on the conceptual drawings (Appendix B). Aggregate supply is the Landfill's responsibility.
**Source:** App B (Shop) (SOW-0079)
**Verification:** Gravel pad shown on layout plan with dimensions and material callouts consistent with Appendix B.

### REQ-08 — IFC Stamping
**Statement:** All Issued for Construction drawings produced under this deliverable shall be signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0018)
**Verification:** P.Eng. stamp and signature present on all IFC drawing sheets.

### REQ-09 — Preliminary Design Approval
**Statement:** The Preliminary Civil Design (DEL-005-01) shall be approved by the County before the final civil design drawings (including this deliverable) are issued for construction.
**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0010e)
**Verification:** Record of County approval of preliminary design on file prior to IFC issue. The form of approval evidence (written letter, signed meeting minutes, or email confirmation) shall be defined by the Project Manager.

> **TBD (D-001):** The acceptance criteria for what constitutes "County approval of preliminary design" are not yet defined. Is written sign-off required, or is verbal acceptance sufficient? The artifact that evidences approval must be specified. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item D-001.)

### REQ-10 — Geotechnical Basis
**Statement:** The pavement structural section design shall be based on or consistent with the project geotechnical investigation report (PKG-008).
**Source:** SOW-0001; WDMLRL_Decomposition_Claude.md §6 PKG-008
**Verification:** Design notes on drawings reference geotechnical report; pavement section design consistent with subgrade conditions identified in geotech report.

> **ASSUMPTION:** A specific pavement structural design method (e.g., empirical layer design for gravel roads) is not prescribed in the RFP. The Civil Engineer shall select an appropriate method consistent with Alberta practice and the geotechnical report findings. This assumption should be confirmed with the Owner or Civil Engineer of Record.

### Identified Gaps Requiring Resolution

> **TBD (X-004 -- Compaction Requirements):** Compaction requirements for gravel driving surface and pad sub-base are mentioned in Procedure Step 5.1 but have no corresponding requirement in this Specification and no verification criterion. Compaction standards shall either be added as a new requirement here or confirmed as covered in DEL-005-07 (Civil Specification). (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item X-004; Procedure.md Step 5.1.)

> **TBD (X-003 -- Edge Conditions Scope):** The scope of "edge conditions and transitions" requires definition. The Civil Engineer of Record shall specify which interfaces require detail drawings and what constitutes a complete set. Candidate interfaces include: (a) building perimeter (drive-through bay entries, building foundation edge), (b) pad-to-driving-surface transitions (gravel pad to driving surface, cement pad to driving surface), and (c) site perimeter / property line conditions. (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item X-003; Procedure.md Step 5.2.)

> **TBD (E-002 -- Subgrade Preparation):** The gap between topsoil stripping (REQ-03) and aggregate placement (REQ-02 pavement section) is not addressed. Subgrade preparation requirements (proof-rolling, moisture conditioning, subgrade compaction) shall be specified here or explicitly deferred to DEL-005-07 (Civil Specification). (Source: _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md item E-002; Specification REQ-03, REQ-10.)
