# Procedure — DEL-005-07: Civil Specification

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce DEL-005-07 (Civil Specification) within PKG-005 Civil Design for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. The Civil Specification is a written normative document that governs all civil materials, workmanship, and quality standards. It is authored by the Civil Engineer and must be issued as part of the Issued for Construction (IFC) document set, signed and stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Confirm Scope and Discipline Interfaces
1. Review _CONTEXT.md and this Specification.md (Requirements section) to confirm the full scope of DEL-005-07.
2. Confirm with PKG-002 (Structural) the scope boundary for concrete pad structural design and mud sump structure (if concrete).
3. Confirm with PKG-006 (Plumbing) the scope boundary for exterior mud sump vessel and drainage connection.
4. Confirm with the County whether bulk topsoil stripping has been completed (conditional per RFP §3.3.2; see CF-CIVIL-01 in Guidance.md). TBD: define the specific verification method for obtaining this confirmation (e.g., written confirmation from County PM, site visit, or both). See item B-003 in _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md.
5. Confirm with the County project manager the aggregate quality expectations and testing responsibility (see CF-CIVIL-02 in Guidance.md).
6. Confirm with the County the status of all County-performed pre-works (brushing/clearing SOW-0200, bulk cut and fill SOW-0201) and obtain written confirmation of completion status before civil design assumptions are finalized.

### Step 1A — Pre-Construction Condition Survey
1. Before civil works commence, conduct a pre-construction condition survey to formally document existing site grades, drainage conditions, and surface conditions in the project area.
2. Survey shall record: existing ground elevations on a grid sufficient for comparison with design grades, existing drainage flow paths and outfall points, condition of neighbouring property drainage features at the site boundary, and photographic documentation.
3. This baseline survey provides the reference condition against which REQ-CIVIL-002 (No Alteration of Neighbouring Property Drainage) will be verified during and after construction.
4. Survey results shall be filed as a permanent project record (see Records table).

Source: Specification.md REQ-CIVIL-002; see item F-003 in _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md. ASSUMPTION: pre-construction condition survey is standard engineering practice for civil works affecting drainage.

### Step 2 — Obtain and Review Geotechnical Report
1. Receive completed Geotechnical Investigation Report (DEL-008-01) from the geotechnical engineer.
2. Extract: subgrade bearing capacity, CBR values, groundwater depth, frost penetration depth, recommended subgrade treatment, and granular material specifications.
3. Use geotechnical values to finalize subgrade preparation requirements, granular base depth, compaction standards, and frost protection specifications for driving surfaces.
4. Document geotechnical basis in the Civil Calculation Package (DEL-005-06).

### Step 3 — Establish Design Criteria and Standards
1. Identify applicable Alberta Building Code edition, Safety Codes, and any applicable municipal or County standards.
2. Establish drainage design storm return period and rainfall intensity values per applicable Alberta standards or local design guidance.
3. Establish driving surface load design criteria (motor scraper axle loads and turning geometry).
4. Record all design criteria in the Civil Calculation Package.

### Step 4 — Prepare Civil Drawing Set (DEL-005-02 through DEL-005-05)
1. Prepare Site Grading Plan (DEL-005-02): show finished grades, drainage flow directions, drainage features, spot elevations, and confirm positive drainage throughout site. Verify no adverse impact to neighbouring property drainage.
2. Prepare Drainage Plan (DEL-005-03): show drainage features, swales, culverts (if any), and connection to or diversion away from existing site drainage.
3. Prepare Driving Surface and Pad Layout Plan (DEL-005-04): show gravel driving surface extents, turning areas, concrete pad locations (per Appendix B), and gravel pad locations. Confirm motor scraper turning radius is accommodated.
4. Prepare Civil Sections and Details (DEL-005-05): show pavement cross-section (stripping depth, subgrade preparation, granular base depth and specification, surface gravel depth and specification), concrete pad section details, mud sump excavation detail, and erosion control details.
5. Coordinate all drawings with geotechnical recommendations and structural/plumbing inputs.

### Step 5 — Prepare Civil Calculation Package (DEL-005-06)
1. Document drainage design calculations: catchment areas, runoff coefficients, design storm, drainage capacity.
2. Document driving surface design: subgrade preparation, granular base design using geotechnical CBR.
3. Document that grading does not alter neighbouring property drainage.
4. File all design criteria, standards references, and geotechnical basis.

### Step 6 — Draft Civil Specification Sections (DEL-005-07)
Author the specification using the following structure (ASSUMPTION: CSI MasterFormat format; Civil Engineer to confirm):

**Division 31 — Earthwork**
- Section 31 10 00 — Site Clearing (topsoil stripping, clearing limits — if not County-performed)
- Section 31 20 00 — Earth Moving (subgrade preparation, cut/fill requirements, compaction standards)
- Section 31 23 00 — Fill (granular material specifications, gradation, compaction, testing requirements)

**Division 32 — Exterior Improvements**
- Section 32 11 23 — Aggregate Base Courses (granular base specification for driving surface)
- Section 32 15 40 — Crushed Stone Surfacing (surface gravel specification, motor scraper-capable design)
- Section 32 13 13 — Concrete Paving (concrete pad specification: mix design, reinforcement, surface finish, curing)
- Section 32 91 00 — Site Grading and Drainage (drainage swales, finished grading standards)
- Section 32 84 00 — Planting Irrigation (TBD — confirm if irrigation is within scope or PKG-007)

For each section, include:
- Part 1: General (scope, standards references, submittals, quality requirements)
- Part 2: Products (materials specifications, gradation, mix design as applicable)
- Part 3: Execution (installation methods, compaction requirements, tolerances, testing, inspection hold points)

Flag all values that depend on geotechnical report as `[GEO TBD]` until confirmed.

### Step 7 — Cross-Reference with Other Disciplines
1. Verify that civil grading plan is coordinated with architectural site plan and structural foundation plan.
2. Verify that mud sump location on civil drawings matches plumbing and structural drawings.
3. Verify that concrete pad locations are consistent with Appendix B conceptual drawings and any architectural site plan.
4. Resolve scope gaps or overlaps identified in Steps 1 discipline interface confirmations.
5. Update the Conflict Table in Guidance.md if new unresolvable conflicts are identified.

### Step 8 — Internal Quality Review
1. Civil Engineer reviews all specification sections for completeness, consistency with drawings, and compliance with applicable codes.
2. Verify all REQ-CIVIL-001 through REQ-CIVIL-017 requirements from Specification.md are addressed in the specification sections.
3. Verify all `[GEO TBD]` placeholders have been resolved with geotechnical values.
4. Verify no values are referenced as TBD in issued specifications (all TBDs must be resolved before IFC issue).

### Step 9 — County Approval — Preliminary Phase
1. Submit DEL-005-01 (Preliminary Civil Design) to County project team.
2. Obtain written approval from County before advancing to final IFC documents.
3. Record approval in project communication file.

### Step 10 — IFC Issue — P.Eng. Stamp
1. Finalize all civil drawings and specification for IFC issue.
2. Civil Engineer (P.Eng. licensed in Alberta) reviews, signs, and stamps all IFC civil drawings and specification.
3. Issue IFC civil package to County and coordinate distribution to all construction trade packages.
