# Specification — DEL-005-07: Civil Specification

---

## Scope

### Included

This specification governs the civil design deliverables for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project (AB-2026-01424). It covers:

1. Site grading design with drainage features and components for the Addition expansion area.
2. Stormwater management — ensuring positive site drainage and accommodating future storm events.
3. Driving surface design — gravel surface designed for motor scraper-class heavy construction equipment (load capacity and turning radius).
4. Pad layout — concrete and gravel pads as shown on Appendix B conceptual drawings.
5. Topsoil stripping design for all driving surfaces.
6. Exterior mud sump design for wash bay drainage (interface with plumbing and structural).
7. Coordination with geotechnical, structural, and plumbing disciplines.
8. Civil calculation package supporting all grading and drainage design decisions.
9. Specification sections governing all civil materials, workmanship, and quality standards for the above elements.

Scope of Work references: SOW-0015, SOW-0020, SOW-0021, SOW-0075, SOW-0076, SOW-0077, SOW-0078, SOW-0079.
Source: RFP §3.3.2, §3.4; Decomposition §7, PKG-005.

## Requirements

### REQ-CIVIL-001 — Positive Site Drainage
The site grading design shall provide positive drainage across all driving surfaces and building approaches such that no standing water accumulates under normal precipitation conditions.
Source: RFP §3.4 (SOW-0020). Verification: Review of grading plan slope calculations.

### REQ-CIVIL-002 — No Alteration of Neighbouring Property Drainage
The grading design shall not alter the drainage conditions of neighbouring properties. Grading must be contained within the project site boundaries.
Source: RFP §3.4 (SOW-0021). Verification: Review of grading plan and drainage calculations relative to site boundary.

### REQ-CIVIL-003 — Future Storm Event Accommodation
The drainage design shall account for future storm events. The design storm return period and intensity shall be determined by the Civil Engineer in accordance with applicable Alberta standards and local design guidance. The Civil Engineer shall identify the specific Alberta regulatory threshold or municipal standard that establishes the minimum design storm return period for this site classification (active landfill). TBD: confirm whether Alberta Environment and Protected Areas (AEPA) imposes specific stormwater management requirements for landfill sites that would establish a binding regulatory floor for the design storm return period.
Source: RFP §3.4 (SOW-0020). Specific return period: TBD (not stated in RFP; Civil Engineer to confirm per applicable standard and regulatory requirements — see items A-001, C-001 in _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md).

### REQ-CIVIL-004 — Gravel Driving Surface — Load Capacity
The gravel driving surface shall be designed to accommodate the weight of motor scraper-class heavy construction equipment. The specification shall include aggregate quality and gradation requirements that County-supplied aggregate must meet. TBD: resolve whether Proponent or County is responsible for testing County-supplied aggregate to confirm specification compliance (see CF-CIVIL-02 in Guidance.md and item A-002 in _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md).
Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0077). Specific axle load or CBR design input: TBD pending geotechnical report (DEL-008-01).

### REQ-CIVIL-005 — Gravel Driving Surface — Turning Radius
The gravel driving surface layout shall accommodate the turning radius of motor scraper-class heavy construction equipment.
Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0077). Motor scraper turning radius design value: TBD (Civil Engineer to confirm equipment class and turning geometry).

### REQ-CIVIL-006 — Topsoil Stripping
The specification shall include requirements for stripping topsoil from all driving surfaces prior to subgrade preparation and gravel surface construction. This work is performed by the Proponent if not already completed by the Owner.
Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0075). ASSUMPTION: this scope has not been pre-completed by County at time of specification; Civil Engineer to confirm with Owner at project award.

### REQ-CIVIL-007 — Grading and Landscaping
The specification shall include requirements for grading and landscaping the proposed lot in conformance with the Issued for Construction drawings.
Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0076).

### REQ-CIVIL-008 — Concrete and Gravel Pads
The specification shall include requirements for constructing concrete pads and gravel pads as shown on the Appendix B conceptual drawings. The specification shall include concrete mix design requirements (TBD: minimum compressive strength, air entrainment for freeze-thaw resistance in Central Alberta climate) or explicitly defer structural concrete design requirements to PKG-002 (Structural) with a documented cross-reference. Pad thickness, reinforcement, and surface finish appropriate for heavy equipment use shall be specified or cross-referenced to PKG-002.
Source: Appendix B (Shop) conceptual drawings (SOW-0078, SOW-0079). Pad dimensions and exact locations: TBD — to be determined from final IFC drawings. See item F-001 in _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md. Note: this requirement uses "concrete pads" as the technically correct term; see item X-002 in _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md regarding terminology harmonization between "cement" and "concrete."

### REQ-CIVIL-009 — Exterior Mud Sump
The specification shall address the exterior mud sump located outside the wash bay, designed for excavator cleanout of wash bay effluent.
Source: RFP §3.4 (SOW-0027b). Sump dimensions and structural specification: interface with PKG-002 (structural) and PKG-006 (plumbing drainage). Specifics TBD pending coordination.

### REQ-CIVIL-010 — P.Eng. Stamp
All Issued for Construction (IFC) civil drawings shall be signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.
Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0018).

### REQ-CIVIL-011 — Code Compliance
The civil design shall adhere to all applicable Alberta building codes, regulations, and Alberta Safety Codes. The Civil Engineer shall identify the specific code sections (clause-level) applicable to the civil scope and record these in the code compliance register (DEL-009-04). TBD: specific Alberta Building Code and Alberta Safety Code clauses applicable to civil grading, drainage, and site works to be determined by the Civil Engineer for traceable compliance verification.
Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0008, SOW-0009). See item A-003 in _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md.

### REQ-CIVIL-012 — Geotechnical Integration
The civil design shall be informed by the geotechnical investigation and report (DEL-008-01). Subgrade design for the gravel driving surface shall incorporate geotechnical recommendations for subgrade preparation, bearing capacity, and frost protection depth.
Source: RFP §4.8.2, Decomposition §4 (SOW-0304). ASSUMPTION: geotechnical report will be available prior to final civil design completion.

### REQ-CIVIL-013 — County Approval — Preliminary Design
Preliminary civil design (DEL-005-01) shall be submitted to and approved by the County project team before progressing to final design.
Source: RFP §3.3.2 (SOW-0010e).

### REQ-CIVIL-014 — Erosion and Sediment Control During Construction
The specification shall include requirements for an erosion and sediment control (ESC) plan to be implemented during civil construction. The ESC plan shall address sediment discharge prevention, temporary erosion control measures, and dust control appropriate for an active landfill site. TBD: confirm whether this is addressed in another deliverable or whether DEL-005-07 is the governing specification for ESC.
Source: Guidance.md — Site Context (Active Landfill); RFP §2.15 (Prime Contractor OH&S obligations). ASSUMPTION: ESC plan is required for civil construction at an active landfill site. See items A-004, C-002 in _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md.

### REQ-CIVIL-015 — Environmental Regulatory Compliance
The civil design and specification shall comply with applicable Alberta environmental regulatory requirements for construction at an active landfill site. TBD: confirm whether Alberta Environment and Protected Areas (AEPA) approval conditions or the landfill's operating approval impose specific requirements affecting civil works (e.g., runoff quality standards, sediment discharge limits, construction activity restrictions).
Source: ASSUMPTION: environmental regulatory compliance likely required for civil construction at an active landfill; specific requirements not identified in accessible sources. See item C-002 in _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md.

### REQ-CIVIL-016 — Construction Submittals
The contractor shall submit the following construction submittals for Civil Engineer review and approval before commencing civil work: shop drawings, material certificates, aggregate gradation test reports, concrete mix designs (for pads), and any other submittals required by the specification sections. Submittal review and approval process shall be in accordance with the general conditions of CCDC 14-2013.
Source: ASSUMPTION: standard construction practice under CCDC 14-2013; the Documentation table lists "General conditions and submittals requirements" as an anticipated artifact but no corresponding normative requirement existed. See item F-002 in _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md.

### REQ-CIVIL-017 — County-to-Proponent Handoff Criteria
The specification shall define measurable handoff criteria that the County's bulk cut and fill (SOW-0201) must achieve before Proponent civil works can begin. TBD: define finished subgrade elevation tolerance and compaction standard for the County-prepared subgrade. These criteria shall be documented in the civil drawings and coordinated with the County project manager.
Source: RFP §3.3.1; Guidance.md — Coordination with County-Performed Bulk Works. See item D-001 in _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md.
