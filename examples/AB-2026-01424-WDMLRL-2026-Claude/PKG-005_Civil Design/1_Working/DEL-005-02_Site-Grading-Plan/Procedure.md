# Procedure — DEL-005-02: Site Grading Plan

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce the Site Grading Plan drawing set (DEL-005-02) for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project. It covers the workflow from data collection through IFC drawing issue, including internal review and County approval checkpoints.

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Data Collection and Site Understanding

**Step 1.1 — Attend Mandatory Site Meeting**
Attend the mandatory site meeting (March 3, 2026, 10:00 a.m. MST, SW-14-44-21-W4). Observe:
- Existing building location and expansion area extents
- Current surface conditions (gravel, bare earth, topsoil strip status)
- Visible drainage patterns and any existing drainage features
- County-completed work (brushing/clearing, bulk cut/fill)

Note: Attendance is mandatory; non-attending proponents are disqualified (R-01 §3.2).

**Step 1.2 — Obtain Survey Data**
Receive preliminary site survey deliverable (DEL-008-02) from the Surveyor. Confirm:
- Existing topographic contours and spot elevations
- Site boundary and legal description confirmation (SW 14–44–21–W4)
- Existing structure locations and elevations
- Utility locations (if surveyed)

**Step 1.3 — Obtain and Review Geotechnical Report**
Receive the **final issued** geotechnical investigation report (DEL-008-01) (Lensing: F-002). Confirm report is final (not draft or preliminary) before using its recommendations for IFC design inputs. Review for:
- Subgrade soil classification and bearing capacity
- Recommended fill compaction criteria (to inform REQ-011)
- Any recommendations affecting grading design (e.g., stripping depths, unsuitable materials)
- Groundwater conditions
- Driving surface load capacity recommendations (to inform REQ-005)

**Step 1.4 — Obtain Control Elevations from Other Disciplines**
Coordinate with Structural Engineer and Architect to obtain:
- Proposed finished floor elevation(s) of the new building
- Building pad design elevations (top of concrete or top of granular base)

These elevations govern the grading datum. (ASSUMPTION — interface protocol TBD at project kickoff)

### Phase 2 — Preliminary Grading Design (feeds DEL-005-01)

**Step 2.1 — Establish Grading Objectives**
Based on gathered data, establish:
- Design storm return period (to be confirmed with County — see Guidance, AMB-001)
- Minimum surface grades for positive drainage (ASSUMPTION: minimum 1–2% on gravel surfaces per standard practice — confirm per applicable standards)
- Property boundary drainage constraints

**Step 2.2 — Develop Preliminary Grading Concept**
Develop a preliminary grading concept showing:
- Proposed finished grade contours
- General drainage flow direction
- Approximate cut/fill areas
- Driving surface extents and grades
- Drainage feature types and locations (in coordination with DEL-005-03 Drainage Plan)

**Step 2.3 — Incorporate into Preliminary Civil Design Package (DEL-005-01)**
Include the grading concept in DEL-005-01 (Preliminary Civil Design) for County project team approval.

**Step 2.4 — Obtain County Approval of Preliminary Design**
Submit DEL-005-01 and receive written County project team approval before proceeding to IFC grading design. **The acceptable format for County approval (Lensing: X-002)** shall be confirmed with the County project representative -- e.g., formal written letter, email confirmation from an authorized County representative, or documented meeting minutes with approval recorded. Whatever format is used, it must constitute a verifiable record of approval that can be filed as a project record (see Records table).
**Gate: Do not proceed to Phase 3 without County approval of preliminary design (R-01 §3.3.2).**

---

### Phase 3 — Final IFC Grading Design

**Step 3.1 — Develop Final Grading Plan Drawing Set**
Produce the IFC drawing set incorporating:
- Final proposed contours and spot elevations
- Existing contours (from survey — DEL-008-02)
- Cut and fill area delineation
- Building pad elevation callouts
- Driving surface grades and geometry
- Topsoil stripping extent (or notation that County has completed stripping)
- Drainage features and components integrated with grading (coordinated with DEL-005-03)
- Erosion and sediment control measures
- Grading cross-sections at key locations

**Step 3.2 — Verify Positive Drainage (REQ-001)**
Perform a drainage flow analysis confirming:
- All surface grades direct water away from structures
- No ponding areas created in graded surfaces
- Drainage features have capacity for the design storm (TBD — AMB-001)

**Step 3.3 — Verify Neighbour Drainage Impact (REQ-002)**
Confirm grading design does not increase runoff rate or volume to neighbouring properties:
- Review drainage patterns at all property boundaries
- Add notation to drawings confirming no adverse impact (or document mitigation measures taken)

**Step 3.4 — Develop Grading Cross-Sections**
Produce transverse and longitudinal cross-sections at:
- Building footprint perimeter
- Key driving surface transitions
- Drainage swales or berms (if used)
- Property boundaries (where drainage analysis requires)

**Step 3.5 — Confirm Driving Surface Design (REQ-005)**
Include on drawings:
- Gravel driving surface designation
- Note confirming design accounts for heavy equipment weight and turning radius (motor scraper referenced — R-01 §3.3.2)
- Subgrade preparation notes based on geotechnical report recommendations

**Step 3.6 — Prepare General Notes, Legend, and Abbreviations Sheet**
Prepare a drawing notes sheet including:
- Reference to geotechnical report
- Reference to applicable Alberta codes and standards
- Compaction specifications (per geotechnical recommendations)
- Material specifications for gravel surface
- Erosion control requirements

**Step 3.7 — Internal Quality Review**
Perform internal quality review of completed drawing set:
- Verify all drawing cross-references are correct
- Verify spot elevations are internally consistent across sheets
- Verify drainage features match DEL-005-03 (Drainage Plan) in type, location, and invert elevations
- Verify building pad elevations match structural/architectural input
- Confirm positive drainage at all locations
- **Drawing set completeness check (Lensing: D-002):** Verify that all required artifacts listed in Specification Documentation table are present in the final drawing set. Use a completeness checklist (format TBD -- see Records table) to confirm each artifact is included before proceeding to P.Eng. stamp.
- Verify drawing revision tracking is maintained on both title blocks and revision log (Lensing: X-003, aligned with Specification Documentation table)

---

### Phase 4 — IFC Issue

**Step 4.1 — P.Eng. Stamp and Sign**
Civil Engineer of Record reviews completed drawing set and applies Alberta P.Eng. stamp and signature to all IFC sheets (REQ-004 — R-01 §3.3.2).

**Step 4.2 — Issue IFC Drawing Set**
Issue the stamped IFC drawing set to:
- County project representative
- Sitework contractor (PKG-018) for construction
- Project file

**Step 4.3 — Submit Inspection Requests as Required**
Coordinate with County project representative for any required inspections. County project representative must be present for inspections and provided with copies of inspection reports (R-01 §3.3.2).

**Inspection milestones for site grading (Lensing: X-001):** TBD -- the specific County inspections required for site grading and the milestones at which they are required shall be confirmed with the County project representative during preliminary design. Likely inspection hold points include (ASSUMPTION):
- Subgrade inspection before gravel placement (confirm compaction and subgrade condition)
- Drainage feature inspection before backfill (confirm inverts, grades, and pipe/swale installation)
- Final grading inspection before substantial completion (confirm positive drainage, surface grades, and erosion control measures)

The Civil Engineer / PM shall request confirmation from the County representative regarding which inspections are mandatory, optional, or not required, and update this list accordingly.
