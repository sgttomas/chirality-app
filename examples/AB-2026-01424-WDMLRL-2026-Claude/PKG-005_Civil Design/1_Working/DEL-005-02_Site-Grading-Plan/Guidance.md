# Guidance — DEL-005-02: Site Grading Plan

---

## Purpose

The Site Grading Plan (DEL-005-02) exists to define the engineered finished grades for the maintenance shop addition expansion area at the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill. It is the authoritative civil design document that directs earthwork construction, ensures positive drainage, and protects adjacent properties from altered runoff conditions.

Without this deliverable, sitework construction (PKG-018) cannot be executed to a defined engineering standard. The grading plan also provides control elevations that flow to landscape/restoration design (PKG-007) and to building construction coordination (PKG-011 sitework interface).

**Sources:** R-01 §3.3.2, §3.4; Decomposition §7 PKG-005, PKG-018; _CONTEXT.md

---

## Principles

### 1. Positive Drainage is a Hard Requirement
The RFP explicitly states that grading design shall account for positive site drainage and future storm events (R-01 §3.4). This is not a best-practice aspiration — it is a specified design requirement (SOW-0020). All grading decisions must be tested against this criterion. Ponding or flat grades that risk water accumulation adjacent to building foundations or driving surfaces are unacceptable.

### 2. Neighbour Drainage is a Hard Constraint
The grading design shall not alter drainage conditions of neighbouring properties (R-01 §3.4 / SOW-0021). This means the grading solution must be self-contained within the site boundaries with respect to drainage pattern changes. Any concentration or redirection of runoff toward property boundaries must be analyzed and mitigated.

### 3. Design-Build Context Requires Early Coordination
This is a design-build project (CCDC 14–2013). The Civil Engineer must coordinate grading control elevations with the Structural Engineer (building pad/foundation — DEL-002-02) and the Architectural team (floor level — DEL-001-02) early in the design process. Grading cannot be finalized in isolation from the building design.

**Source:** R-01 §2.7, §3.1; Decomposition PKG-005 boundary

### 4. County Forces Perform Bulk Work — Design Must Reflect This Interface
Brushing/clearing and bulk cut/fill are performed by County forces, not by the proponent (R-01 §3.3.1). The grading plan must clearly delineate: (a) what has been accomplished by County forces versus (b) what remains for proponent-directed sitework. Ambiguity at this boundary creates risk for scope disputes.

**County Bulk Work Handoff Condition (Lensing: B-003):** The acceptance condition for County-completed bulk earthwork -- i.e., what state the site must be in before the proponent's grading design can be implemented -- is TBD. For example: must bulk cut/fill be completed to within a specified tolerance of proposed subgrade elevations? Must the site be free of unsuitable material? Must the County provide as-built survey data of completed earthwork? These questions should be resolved during preliminary design coordination with the County project representative. The grading plan should include notes defining the assumed site condition at handoff, with a mechanism for field verification before proponent grading work commences. **Source:** R-01 §3.3.1 (County scope); Decomposition PKG-005 boundary note.

### 5. Geotechnical Report is a Required Input — Do Not Finalize Before It Is Available
The RFP requires a full geotechnical investigation (DEL-008-01, PKG-008) and states it will be used during design (R-01 §3.3.2). Grading design, particularly subgrade preparation depths, compaction specifications, and any fill placement requirements, should not be finalized until the geotechnical report is available and reviewed.

### 6. Driving Surface Must Handle Heavy Equipment
The final driving surface shall be gravel and must be designed to handle the weight and turning radius of equipment such as a motor scraper (R-01 §3.3.2). This is an operational requirement reflecting the landfill's working environment with heavy equipment. The grading design should anticipate surface geometry suited to heavy equipment maneuver.

## Considerations

### Stormwater Design Storm
The RFP specifies that grading shall account for "future storm events" (R-01 §3.4) but does not specify a design return period (e.g., 1:10, 1:25, 1:100). **The design engineer will need to determine an appropriate design storm based on applicable Alberta standards, municipal requirements, and professional judgment.** This is a material design decision that should be confirmed with the County during preliminary design.

### Topsoil Stripping Coordination
The RFP notes topsoil stripping is to be done "if not already performed by Owner" (R-01 §3.3.2). The grading plan must either: (a) show topsoil stripping extents as a proponent work item, or (b) note that stripping has been completed by County with reference to actual conditions. This coordination should be resolved at the preliminary design and site visit stage.

### Drainage Feature Type Selection
The RFP requires drainage features and components to be included in the grading plan (R-01 §3.3.2) but does not specify their type (e.g., swales, catch basins, culverts, berms). Feature selection is at the Civil Engineer's professional discretion based on site topography (from preliminary survey — DEL-008-02), hydrology analysis, and constructability in the landfill environment. Note that detailed drainage design is covered in DEL-005-03 (Drainage Plan) — the grading plan should show drainage features in coordination with that deliverable.

### Interface with DEL-005-03 (Drainage Plan)
The boundary between the Site Grading Plan (DEL-005-02) and the Drainage Plan (DEL-005-03) must be clearly managed. Both deliverables share SOW-0015, SOW-0020, and SOW-0021 per the decomposition, and both relate to drainage. The recommended approach is to show drainage feature extents and inverts on the grading plan as grading-integrated elements, with hydraulic sizing and drainage network details addressed in DEL-005-03. This boundary should be confirmed with the project team.

### Schedule Risk from Upstream Dependencies (Lensing: F-003)
REQ-010 sets a hard completion deadline of December 31, 2026 (R-01 §3.1). The Site Grading Plan cannot be finalized until several upstream deliverables are received:
- **DEL-008-01 (Geotechnical Report):** Required for subgrade design, compaction specifications, and bearing capacity criteria.
- **DEL-008-02 (Preliminary Survey):** Required for existing topography, control points, and elevation datum.
- **DEL-005-01 (Preliminary Civil Design):** Must receive County approval before IFC proceeds (R-01 §3.3.2).
- **DEL-002-02 / DEL-001-02 (Foundation/Floor Plans):** Required for building pad control elevations (ASSUMPTION).

If any of these upstream deliverables are delayed, the grading IFC issue date is pushed forward, compressing the available construction window before the December 31, 2026 deadline. The Civil Engineer and PM should: (a) establish a critical-path schedule showing the dependency chain, (b) identify the latest acceptable date for receipt of each upstream input that still permits IFC issue with adequate construction lead time, and (c) communicate these dates to the responsible parties. This risk is not currently addressed in the Specification or Procedure but is material to feasibility of the completion deadline.

**Source:** R-01 §3.1 (deadline); Specification REQ-010; Procedure Prerequisites table.

### Alberta Professional Engineer Stamping
All IFC drawings must be stamped by a P.Eng. licensed in Alberta (R-01 §3.3.2). ASSUMPTION: This means the civil engineer of record must hold an active APEGA license at the time of IFC issue. Ensure this is verified before the drawing set is issued.

### Post-Construction Warranty Obligations (Lensing: E-001)
The Datasheet records a 2-year warranty period following the Construction Completion Certificate (R-01 §2.10.2). During this period, the proponent guarantees all materials and work, including correction of defects (R-01 §2.10.3). For grading design, this has implications:
- **Durability:** The grading design should account for settlement, erosion, and drainage performance over the 2-year warranty period. Gravel surfaces in a landfill environment with heavy equipment traffic may experience accelerated wear. The design should specify surface material thickness and compaction criteria that provide reasonable durability through the warranty period.
- **Maintenance Access:** If warranty-period repairs to grading or drainage features are needed, the design should not create conditions that make maintenance access impractical (e.g., drainage features should be accessible for inspection and repair without major excavation).
- **Performance Monitoring:** ASSUMPTION -- The grading design should include provisions for post-construction observation of drainage performance (e.g., during spring thaw or after significant storm events) to identify warranty-period deficiencies early.

**Source:** R-01 §2.10.2, §2.10.3; Datasheet Attributes (Warranty Period).

### Wash Bay Drainage Interface
The wash bay drainage infrastructure — including floor drains, connection to mud sump, and exterior mud sump (SOW-0027b) — is assigned to PKG-018 (Sitework and Civil Construction), not to the civil design packages. ASSUMPTION: The grading plan should show the exterior mud sump location as a grading feature to ensure adequate grade and access for excavator cleanout, but detailed design of the mud sump itself is a separate scope item.

## Trade-offs

| Decision | Option A | Option B | Consideration |
|---|---|---|---|
| Drainage feature type | Open swales (lower cost, simpler) | Closed catch basin/pipe system (higher cost, more robust) | Landfill environment may favor simpler robust solutions; geotechnical and freeze-thaw conditions relevant; TBD pending geotech report and site survey |
| Grading plan / drainage plan boundary | Combined into one sheet set | Kept strictly separate | Coordination risk increases if too separated; recommend close coordination during production |
| Design storm selection | Lower return period (1:10) | Higher return period (1:25 or more) | RFP says "future storm events" without specifying; conservative approach protects against liability; to be confirmed with County |
| Topsoil stripping — include or exclude from plan | Show stripping extents on grading plan | Treat as pre-condition completed by County | Depends on what County has actually done; field verification required at mandatory site meeting (March 3, 2026) |

**ASSUMPTION:** Standard Alberta civil engineering practice for graded industrial sites at landfill facilities would typically include:
- Minimum 1–2% cross-fall on paved/gravel surfaces for drainage
- Perimeter swales or berms to contain runoff
- Sediment control fencing or silt traps during construction

These are ASSUMPTION values. Specific design values must be derived from geotechnical report, survey data, and the Civil Engineer's professional judgment.
