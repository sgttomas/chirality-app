# Specification — DEL-005-02: Site Grading Plan

---

## Scope

### In Scope

This deliverable covers the **Issued for Construction (IFC) Site Grading Plan drawing set** for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project. It addresses the detailed grading design for the expansion area at SW 14–44–21–W4, Camrose County, Alberta.

Specifically, this deliverable covers:
- Existing and proposed grading of the maintenance shop addition expansion area (source: R-01 §3.3.2, §3.4; R-07)
- Drainage features and components integrated into the grading design (source: R-01 §3.3.2 — "site grading plan that will include drainage features and components")
- Positive drainage design to handle future storm events (source: R-01 §3.4 / SOW-0020)
- Design confirming no alteration of drainage conditions on neighbouring properties (source: R-01 §3.4 / SOW-0021)
- Topsoil stripping extents for driving surfaces (if not already performed by County forces) (source: R-01 §3.3.2)
- Gravel driving surface grading, designed to accommodate weight and turning radius of large construction equipment such as a motor scraper (source: R-01 §3.3.2)
- Integration with building pad elevations established via structural/architectural design (ASSUMPTION: interface coordination required; specific interface TBD)
- P.Eng.-stamped IFC drawings (source: R-01 §3.3.2)

## Requirements

### REQ-001 — Positive Site Drainage
**Statement:** The grading design shall account for positive site drainage and future storm events. A minimum surface slope shall be established to ensure positive drainage across all graded surfaces (minimum slope value TBD -- to be determined per applicable Alberta standards and confirmed with County during preliminary design; see Guidance AMB-001). The applicable design storm return period (e.g., 1:10, 1:25, 1:100) shall be identified per Alberta stormwater management standards and confirmed with the County (Lensing: A-001, C-001).
**Source:** R-01 §3.4 (SOW-0020)
**Verification:** Review of proposed contours and drainage feature details to confirm positive grades directed away from structures and to drainage features. Confirm minimum slope criteria are met on all graded surfaces (minimum slope value TBD). Confirm design storm return period selection is documented and applied.

### REQ-002 — No Alteration of Neighbouring Drainage
**Statement:** The grading design shall not alter the drainage conditions of neighbouring properties.
**Source:** R-01 §3.4 (SOW-0021)
**Verification:** Quantitative comparison of pre- and post-grading drainage patterns at property boundaries, including drainage area delineation and flow-rate or flow-direction analysis (method TBD -- e.g., rational method, drainage area mapping, or reference to applicable standard). Confirmation shall be documented in the design narrative or drawing notes (Lensing: D-001).

### REQ-003 — Drainage Features and Components Included
**Statement:** The site grading plan shall include drainage features and components.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2
**Verification:** Drawing set includes drainage feature plan, details, and/or notes referencing drainage components.

### REQ-004 — P.Eng. Stamp
**Statement:** All IFC drawings shall be signed and stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2
**Verification:** Review of drawing title block for valid P.Eng. stamp; confirmation of Alberta licensure.

### REQ-005 — Gravel Driving Surface Grading
**Statement:** The graded driving surface shall be designed to accommodate the weight and turning radius of large construction equipment, including a motor scraper. The final driving surface material shall be gravel. Quantitative acceptance criteria for driving surface load capacity shall be established (e.g., minimum bearing pressure or subgrade CBR value) based on the geotechnical report (DEL-008-01) recommendations (Lensing: A-005).
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2
**Verification:** Drawing notes and cross-sections confirm surface material specification and design criteria for equipment loading. Quantitative load capacity acceptance criteria TBD -- to be established upon receipt of geotechnical report (DEL-008-01) and confirmed by Civil Engineer.

### REQ-006 — Topsoil Stripping Extents Shown
**Statement:** Topsoil stripping from driving surfaces shall be reflected in the grading plan (if not already performed by County forces).
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2
**Verification:** Drawing notes or stripped-area delineation shown on grading plan.

### REQ-007 — County Approval of Preliminary Design
**Statement:** Preliminary civil design (DEL-005-01) must receive County project team approval before final grading design proceeds to IFC.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2
**Verification:** Record of County approval of DEL-005-01 prior to IFC drawing issue.

### REQ-008 — Coordination with Building Pad and Foundation Design
**Statement:** ASSUMPTION — Finished floor elevation(s) and building pad design from structural/architectural packages shall be used as control elevations in the site grading plan. Specific interface values TBD pending DEL-002-02 (Foundation Plan) and DEL-001-02 (Floor Plans).
**Source:** ASSUMPTION based on standard civil-structural interface practice; supported by Decomposition PKG-005 boundary note ("Building structural design" as interface)
**Verification:** Grading plan annotates control elevation reference to IFC structural/architectural drawings.

### REQ-009 — Geotechnical Report as Input
**Statement:** ASSUMPTION — The geotechnical investigation report (DEL-008-01) shall be used to inform grading design, particularly regarding subgrade conditions and compaction requirements.
**Source:** R-01 §3.3.2 ("A full complete geotechnical report will be required by the successful proponent to use during the design process"); Decomposition PKG-008 → PKG-005 dependency
**Verification:** Grading drawings or specifications reference the geotechnical report by document number.

### REQ-010 — Completion by December 31, 2026
**Statement:** All work, including site grading construction, must be complete on or before December 31, 2026.
**Source:** R-01 §3.1
**Verification:** Project schedule confirms grading IFC issued with sufficient lead time for construction completion by deadline.

### REQ-011 — Compaction Specification
**Statement:** ASSUMPTION -- Earthwork compaction requirements shall be specified in accordance with the geotechnical report (DEL-008-01) recommendations. Compaction specifications shall include minimum compaction density (e.g., % Standard Proctor), lift thickness, and moisture content range as applicable (Lensing: F-001).
**Source:** ASSUMPTION based on standard civil earthwork practice; supported by Procedure Step 3.6 reference to "compaction specifications (per geotechnical recommendations)" and R-01 §3.3.2 requirement for geotechnical report use during design.
**Verification:** Drawing general notes or specification sections cite compaction criteria from DEL-008-01. Verification method: field density testing during construction (TBD -- specific test standard to be confirmed, e.g., ASTM D698 / D1557).

### REQ-012 — Erosion and Sediment Control Adequacy
**Statement:** ASSUMPTION -- Erosion and sediment control measures shall be designed and specified for both the construction phase (temporary) and permanent condition. Acceptance criteria for measure type, placement, and maintenance requirements shall be established (Lensing: C-003).
**Source:** ASSUMPTION based on standard civil construction practice; erosion control details are listed as an anticipated artifact in _CONTEXT.md and appear in Datasheet Construction table.
**Verification:** Drawing details and/or specifications include erosion control measure types, locations, installation criteria, and maintenance requirements during construction. Review for adequacy of coverage relative to site drainage patterns and disturbed area extents.
