# Specification — DEL-003-03 Ductwork & Distribution Plans

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-003-03 (Ductwork & Distribution Plans) is the drawing set that documents the routing, sizing, and layout of the ductwork and air distribution system for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. It is part of PKG-003 (Mechanical Design) and directly addresses SOW-0013 (complete final mechanical design — HVAC, ventilation, exhaust systems).

This drawing set covers:

- Supply air distribution ductwork layout throughout the new North Shop addition (~13,000 sq ft, 35 ft ceiling)
- Ductwork connections to the forced-air makeup air (MUA) unit
- Ductwork connections to the high-volume air exchanger
- Ductwork connections to the high-recovery heating system
- Air distribution to all served spaces: main shop (repair bays), wash bay, service pit/trench ventilation, office, parts room, utility room, and mezzanine (extent TBD)
- Duct routing plans with dimensions, elevations, and clearances
- Duct sizing schedule or notes
- Coordination interfaces with structural (penetrations, clearances), architectural (ceiling/space), and exhaust systems (DEL-003-04)

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Mechanical equipment selection and placement (governed by DEL-003-02: HVAC Layout Plans)
- Exhaust hose systems and fans for heavy equipment repair bays (governed by DEL-003-04: Exhaust System Plans)
- Welding station exhaust ductwork (governed by DEL-003-04: Exhaust System Plans)
- Mechanical sizing calculations (governed by DEL-003-06: Mechanical Calculation Package)
- Mechanical equipment schedule (governed by DEL-003-05)
- Mechanical specification text (governed by DEL-003-07: Mechanical Specification)
- Plumbing systems (PKG-006)
- Electrical power to mechanical equipment (PKG-004/PKG-015)

> **Boundary Note:** The division between DEL-003-03 (Ductwork & Distribution Plans) and DEL-003-04 (Exhaust System Plans) should be confirmed with the Mechanical Engineer. ASSUMPTION: DEL-003-03 covers supply, return, and general ventilation distribution ductwork; DEL-003-04 covers dedicated local exhaust systems (equipment bays, welding station, service pit dedicated exhaust). Where exhaust systems share ductwork with general ventilation, the Mechanical Engineer shall determine which drawing set governs and note the coordination interface. (Lensing: F-003 — normalized boundary language to use consistent categorical terms across Datasheet, Specification, and Guidance; see also CON-003-03-01.)

## Requirements

### REQ-003-03-01: Coverage of All Served Spaces
The ductwork and distribution plans shall show air distribution routing to all mechanically conditioned or ventilated spaces in the addition, including but not limited to: main shop area (both repair bays), wash bay, service pit, office, parts room, utility room, and mezzanine (where served).
- Source: RFP §3.1, §3.4; Appendix B (Shop) conceptual drawing
- Verification: Drawing completeness review — all named spaces shown

### REQ-003-03-02: Forced-Air Makeup Air System Distribution
The drawing set shall include ductwork routing for the forced-air makeup air (MUA) unit, including supply air distribution to the main shop.
- Source: RFP Appendix B (Shop) — "Forced Air Makeup" noted on conceptual drawing
- Verification: MUA ductwork shown on drawings with connection to unit location from DEL-003-02

### REQ-003-03-03: High-Volume Air Exchanger Ductwork
The drawing set shall include ductwork connections for the high-volume air exchanger, showing both supply and exhaust connections where they form part of the distribution network.
- Source: RFP §3.4 — "High volume air exchanger"
- Verification: Air exchanger duct connections shown and coordinated with equipment location in DEL-003-02

### REQ-003-03-04: High-Recovery Heating System Distribution
The drawing set shall include ductwork distribution for the high-recovery heating system.
- Source: RFP §3.4 — "High recovery heating system"
- Verification: Heating system duct distribution shown and connected to equipment location

### REQ-003-03-05: Duct Sizing
Duct sizes shall be indicated on the drawings or in an associated duct sizing schedule. Sizing shall be based on calculated airflow requirements documented in DEL-003-06 (Mechanical Calculation Package).
- Source: ASSUMPTION — industry standard IFC drawing requirement; Alberta Building Code (edition TBD, location TBD)
- Verification: All duct runs dimensioned; sizing schedule or keynotes present; cross-check with DEL-003-06 to confirm all runs have CFM annotation and duct velocities are within applicable SMACNA/ASHRAE limits (specific acceptance thresholds TBD — to be established by Mechanical Engineer once applicable standards editions are confirmed) (Lensing: C-001)

### REQ-003-03-06: Code Compliance
Ductwork design shall comply with all applicable Alberta Building Code provisions and Alberta Safety Codes governing HVAC and ventilation systems. Specific code editions (Alberta Building Code, Alberta Safety Codes, ASHRAE, SMACNA) shall be identified by the Mechanical Engineer at design commencement; prescriptive direction cannot be fully established until governing code editions are confirmed (Lensing: A-001).
- Source: RFP §3.3.2 ("building design must adhere to all applicable Alberta building codes and regulations"); RFP §3.3.2 ("proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes")
- Verification: Mechanical Engineer written certification of compliance identifying the specific code sections checked and the form of certification (e.g., code compliance letter or stamped compliance statement); Safety Code permit inspection by Safety Code Officer (Lensing: A-003 — verification method strengthened to specify what constitutes compliance confirmation)

### REQ-003-03-07: P.Eng. Stamp
All IFC drawings, including this drawing set, shall be signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer (P.Eng.) licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2 — "All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta"
- Verification: Stamp and signature present on all IFC sheets

### REQ-003-03-08: Preliminary Design Approval
A preliminary mechanical design (DEL-003-01) must receive County approval before final ductwork plans are issued for construction.
- Source: RFP §3.3.2 — "Deliver Preliminary Design for the County project team to approve"
- Verification: County written approval documented prior to IFC issuance; approval form TBD — to be confirmed with County (e.g., signed approval letter, signed drawing set, or documented meeting minutes with explicit approval statement; specify retention location in project document control system) (Lensing: X-001)

### REQ-003-03-09: Coordination with Structural Drawings
Ductwork routing shall be coordinated with the structural drawings (DEL-002 set) to confirm clearances at structural members, required penetrations, and hanger attachment points. Under the CCDC 14-2013 design-build contract, the Design-Builder bears design liability; accountability for coordination failures should be documented per the project's coordination protocol (Lensing: D-001).
- Source: ASSUMPTION — standard IFC coordination requirement for concrete structure with 35 ft ceiling
- Verification: No unresolved conflicts with structural drawings at IFC; penetration schedule or coordination notes included; evidence of "no unresolved conflicts" shall be documented as: (a) written sign-off from Structural Engineer, or (b) clash detection report with zero critical clashes, retained in project document control system (Lensing: F-002 — evidence standard for coordination sign-off defined)

### REQ-003-03-10: Wash Bay Ventilation Coordination
The ductwork plans shall address general ventilation distribution to the enclosed wash bay (single bay, motor scraper-sized), coordinating with the wash bay enclosure structure. ASSUMPTION: the scope boundary between supply/general ventilation distribution (this deliverable) and dedicated local exhaust (DEL-003-04) applies to the wash bay per CON-003-03-01; the precise boundary is TBD pending Mechanical Engineer confirmation (Lensing: B-004 — normalized to align assumption-qualified language with Datasheet system coverage table).
- Source: RFP §3.1, §3.4 — "Single enclosed bay dedicated for washing large equipment"; wash bay ventilation implied by enclosed industrial use
- Verification: Wash bay ventilation supply/general distribution paths shown on drawings

### REQ-003-03-11: Service Pit Ventilation Coordination
The ductwork plans shall address ventilation to the service pit/trench. The service pit is a below-grade space with potential for hazardous atmosphere accumulation (exhaust fumes from vehicles positioned above); the specific code section governing below-grade ventilation in occupied maintenance pits shall be identified by the Mechanical Engineer and compliance demonstrated (Lensing: F-001 — safety-critical gap identified for below-grade ventilation compliance).
- Source: RFP §3.4 — "Ventilated and lighted service pit area for mechanics to perform servicing under equipment"
- Verification: Service pit ventilation shown on drawings; Mechanical Engineer to confirm compliance with applicable Alberta Building Code provisions for below-grade ventilation in areas with potential hazardous atmosphere; verification method for code compliance TBD pending identification of governing code section (Lensing: F-001)

### REQ-003-03-12: 35 ft Ceiling Accommodation
Ductwork routing and distribution shall be designed to accommodate the 35 ft ceiling height of the main shop, including appropriate drop distances and diffuser/discharge arrangements for effective air distribution.
- Source: RFP §3.4 — "Concrete structure 35' ceiling"; Appendix B (Shop)
- Verification: Duct elevation details and section views provided; diffuser/discharge points shown at appropriate heights

### REQ-003-03-13: Quantitative Performance Acceptance Criteria
The ductwork system design shall meet quantitative performance thresholds for duct system operation, including but not limited to: maximum allowable pressure drop per duct run, maximum duct velocity limits per application type (main supply, branch, exhaust), and confirmation of design airflow delivery at terminal devices. Specific thresholds TBD — to be established by the Mechanical Engineer based on applicable ASHRAE/SMACNA standards and the calculation results in DEL-003-06 (Lensing: A-005 — addresses gap where all verification methods were drawing-review-based with no quantitative operational performance thresholds).
- Source: ASSUMPTION — industry standard for IFC-level mechanical design; applicable ASHRAE/SMACNA performance criteria (editions TBD, location TBD)
- Verification: TBD — quantitative acceptance criteria to be defined by Mechanical Engineer; at minimum, duct sizing schedule shall include velocity and pressure drop data for cross-check against applicable limits

### REQ-003-03-14: Crane Clearance Verification
Ductwork routing shall not conflict with the crane travel envelope for the two 5-tonne overhead cranes on trolley rails. Duct routing shall maintain adequate clearance from crane rail beams, trolley travel paths, and hook reach zones.
- Source: RFP §3.4 — two 5-tonne overhead cranes on trolley; Appendix B (Shop) — crane locations; ASSUMPTION: standard coordination requirement for overhead crane facilities (Lensing: X-003 — provides normative anchor for Procedure's crane clearance verification check)
- Verification: Ductwork routing verified by overlay/clash check against crane travel envelope; no conflicts at IFC issuance

### REQ-003-03-15: Ceiling Fan Spatial Coordination
Ductwork routing in the main shop area shall be coordinated with the six ceiling fan locations (Source: Appendix B Electrical) to ensure duct routing does not conflict with fan mounting positions or sweep radius. Fan location coordination to be confirmed with Electrical Engineer (DEL-004-04, DEL-015-04).
- Source: Appendix B (Electrical) — six ceiling fans in main shop; CON-003-03-02 in Guidance.md (Lensing: X-002 — provides normative anchor for the ceiling fan coordination conflict identified in Guidance Conflict Table and addressed in Procedure Step 1.2)
- Verification: Drawing coordination review confirming no duct/fan conflicts; coordination note on drawings referencing electrical fan layout
