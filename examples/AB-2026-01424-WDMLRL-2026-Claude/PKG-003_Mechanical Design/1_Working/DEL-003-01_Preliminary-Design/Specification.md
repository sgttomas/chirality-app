# Specification — DEL-003-01 Preliminary Mechanical Design

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-003-01 covers the preliminary mechanical design for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition (the "Addition"), executed as a Design Presentation for review and approval by Camrose County.

The scope of mechanical design (PKG-003) includes:
- HVAC system design: heating, makeup air, air exchange
- Exhaust systems: heavy equipment exhaust in repair bays, welding station ventilation/exhaust
- Ceiling fans in main shop area
- Wash bay ventilation and heating (extent TBD -- see REQ-003-01-12)

The preliminary design deliverable (DEL-003-01, covering SOW-0010c) is the first-stage design submission that must receive County approval before proceeding to final design and IFC drawings.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; Decomposition PKG-003 definition; Scope Ledger SOW-0010c.

### What This Deliverable Excludes

| Excluded Item | Rationale | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Plumbing design (cistern, pumps, septic, drains) | Covered by PKG-006 / DEL-006-01 | Decomposition §6 |
| Electrical design (three-phase, lighting, receptacles, fan power supply) | Covered by PKG-004 / DEL-004-01 | Decomposition §6 |
| Structural design (concrete structure, mezzanine, service pit structural) | Covered by PKG-002 / DEL-002-01 | Decomposition §6 |
| Welding equipment supply or specification | County responsibility; County supplies welding equipment specifications | RFP §3.4; SOW-0204 |
| Final IFC mechanical drawings | Subsequent deliverables DEL-003-02 through DEL-003-07 | Decomposition PKG-003 |
| Mechanical installation | PKG-013 (construction phase) | Decomposition §6 |

### REQ-003-01-08 — Service Pit Ventilation

**Statement:** The design shall include ventilation for the service pit/trench area.

**Basis:** RFP §3.4 ("Ventilated and lighted service pit area"); SOW-0028.

**Notes:** The ventilation requirement for the service pit is stated in RFP §3.4. Whether a dedicated mechanical system or integration with general shop exhaust is appropriate is TBD by Mechanical Engineer. Structural design of the pit is PKG-002 scope. **Note [B-004]:** Scope ownership between PKG-003 and PKG-011 is flagged in Guidance Conflict Table C-003-01-02.

> **Enrichment [A-002]:** The obligation level is strengthened from "shall address" to "shall include" to be consistent with the RFP language ("Ventilated and lighted service pit area" -- RFP §3.4) and with the mandatory tone of other REQs in this specification. The Mechanical Engineer shall determine the appropriate ventilation approach (dedicated or integrated) and include it in the Design Presentation.

**Verification:** Design Presentation includes service pit ventilation strategy with system type identified (dedicated or integrated) and preliminary airflow basis. (Source: RFP §3.4; SOW-0028. Enriched per A-002, F-002.)

### REQ-003-01-10 — P.Eng. Stamp (Final Design — Constraint on Preliminary)

**Statement:** Final mechanical IFC drawings must be signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer licensed to practice in Alberta.

**Basis:** RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0018.

**Notes:** This requirement applies to final design deliverables (DEL-003-02 through DEL-003-07), not to the preliminary design presentation itself. Stated here as a design constraint that the preliminary design must be developed with final P.Eng. stamping intent.

**Verification (final stage):** Final IFC drawings bear P.Eng. stamp (Alberta licence).

**Verification (preliminary stage) [F-001]:** The preliminary design shall be reviewed by the intended P.Eng. of Record (or a licensed P.Eng. on the design team) to confirm the design approach is compatible with eventual P.Eng. stamping. Acceptance criterion: documented confirmation (email, memo, or review sign-off) from a P.Eng. that the preliminary design direction is consistent with professional engineering standards and can proceed to final design. PROPOSAL per F-001: Mechanical Engineer / P.Eng. of Record to confirm. (Source: RFP §3.3.2; SOW-0018. Enriched per F-001.)

### REQ-003-01-12 — Wash Bay Mechanical Design (Preliminary Stage)

> **New requirement added per X-003 enrichment.**

**Statement:** The preliminary design shall address ventilation and heating requirements for the wash bay, or shall explicitly document why the wash bay is excluded from preliminary mechanical scope with a commitment to address it in final design.

**Basis:** RFP §3.4 ("Single enclosed bay dedicated for washing large equipment such as a motor scraper"); RFP §3.1 (wash bay in building program); App B (Shop) (24 ft wide wash bay shown).

**Notes:** The wash bay is a significant space with unique mechanical requirements (humidity, potential freezing, equipment exhaust during wash operations). The RFP does not explicitly differentiate wash bay mechanical requirements from the main shop, but the space is distinct and enclosed. Heating status (heated / unheated / freeze-protection-only) is TBD (see Datasheet B-001). ASSUMPTION: The wash bay requires at minimum ventilation consideration at the preliminary stage given its enclosed nature and equipment wash function.

**Verification:** Design Presentation includes wash bay ventilation/heating strategy or documented exclusion rationale. Acceptance criterion: presentation addresses wash bay as a distinct mechanical zone or documents the basis for deferral to final design. PROPOSAL per X-003: Mechanical Engineer to determine. (Source: RFP §3.4; §3.1; App B (Shop). Enriched per X-003.)

### REQ-003-01-13 — Crane Clearance Coordination

> **New requirement added per C-003 enrichment.**

**Statement:** The preliminary mechanical design shall verify that all proposed mechanical equipment locations and ductwork routing do not conflict with the crane runway envelope for the two 5-tonne overhead cranes.

**Basis:** RFP §3.4; SOW-0067 (two 5-tonne overhead cranes on trolley); App B (Shop) (crane locations shown).

**Notes:** Crane hook height and runway elevation are TBD at structural preliminary design stage. Mechanical routing must remain below the crane travel envelope. This requirement creates a verification checkpoint for the coordination concern identified in Procedure Step 3.2. ASSUMPTION: Crane clearance is a standard coordination requirement for industrial shop buildings with overhead cranes.

**Verification:** Design Presentation includes a statement confirming mechanical equipment and ductwork routing has been checked against the crane runway envelope (or documents the assumed clearance dimensions used). Acceptance criterion: preliminary layout drawing shows a clearance zone notation or coordination note referencing PKG-002 / crane supplier. PROPOSAL per C-003: Mechanical Engineer. (Source: RFP §3.4; SOW-0067; App B (Shop). Enriched per C-003.)
