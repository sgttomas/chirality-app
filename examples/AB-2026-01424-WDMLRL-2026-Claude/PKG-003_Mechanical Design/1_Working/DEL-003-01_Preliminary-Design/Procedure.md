# Procedure — DEL-003-01 Preliminary Mechanical Design

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce DEL-003-01: the Preliminary Mechanical Design for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. It covers the activities required to develop the Design Presentation, obtain County approval, and establish the mechanical design basis for subsequent final design deliverables (DEL-003-02 through DEL-003-07).

The procedure also describes the use of this deliverable once produced -- specifically how it functions as an approval gate under the design-build contract.

## Steps

### Step 1 -- Assemble Source Documentation

1.1 Obtain and review the full RFP (R-01), with specific attention to:
  - §3.1 Project Background (building program, useable area of approximately 13,000 square feet)
  - §3.3.2 Scope of Work (Proponent services)
  - §3.4 Design Requirements (mechanical systems list)
  - Appendix B (Shop) -- conceptual floor plan and notes

1.2 Extract all mechanical systems explicitly required:
  - High-recovery heating system (SOW-0035)
  - High-volume air exchanger (SOW-0036)
  - Forced-air makeup air unit (SOW-0037)
  - Heavy equipment exhaust hoses and fans -- repair bays (SOW-0038)
  - Welding station ventilation/exhaust (SOW-0039)
  - 6 ceiling fans in main shop (SOW-0040)
  - Service pit ventilation (RFP §3.4)
  - Wash bay ventilation/heating (RFP §3.4; see REQ-003-01-12)

1.3 Confirm the building program dimensions from Appendix B (Shop):
  - Overall Addition: 130 ft x 100 ft (13,000 sq ft gross footprint; see Datasheet Note B-003 re: gross vs. usable area distinction)
  - Clear ceiling height: 35 ft
  - Two drive-through repair bays (each nominally 24 ft wide)
  - Single wash bay (24 ft wide)
  - Parts room (~400 sq ft)
  - Utility room (location per App B)
  - Office space
  - Mezzanine (over parts room, utility room, wash bay)
  - Service pit/trench

**Verification:** Source documents confirmed accessible and reviewed; building program confirmed.

---

### Step 2 -- Establish Mechanical Design Basis

2.1 Determine applicable Alberta codes and standards governing the mechanical design (Alberta Building Code -- specific edition TBD per A-001/A-003, Alberta Safety Codes, applicable ASHRAE and CSA standards -- specific numbers TBD per C-002). Document the code basis statement for inclusion in the Design Presentation. Reference the method or standard used for each calculation (e.g., ASHRAE Fundamentals for heating load, ASHRAE 62.1 for ventilation rates) -- see A-004 note at Step 2.3.

2.2 Confirm the fuel source: natural gas is assumed (SOW-0080 -- natural gas tie-in required). **TBD [C-001]:** Confirm natural gas availability at site and tie-in timeline relative to mechanical system commissioning. If gas is unavailable at site prior to occupation, identify interim heating strategy. The natural gas tie-in is PKG-018 scope; coordinate with civil/utility discipline for confirmation.

2.3 Perform a preliminary heat loss/gain calculation for the Addition to size the heating system. **Enrichment [A-004]:** Specify the calculation method or standard to be used (e.g., ASHRAE Handbook -- Fundamentals load calculation method, CAN/CSA-F280 for residential heating/cooling loads if applicable, or proprietary software with documented assumptions). The selected method should be noted in the design basis documentation. PROPOSAL per A-004: Mechanical Engineer to identify calculation method. ASSUMPTION: This is standard practice for a preliminary design even before final geotechnical and architectural drawings are available; assumptions about building envelope performance shall be documented and labeled as ASSUMPTION.

2.4 Develop an integrated air-handling strategy: quantify exhaust air volumes (equipment exhaust, welding exhaust, general exhaust), size the MUA and air exchanger to balance the exhaust loads, and determine pressure strategy (positive, neutral, or zoned). See Guidance -- Building Pressure Strategy consideration (enriched per X-001) for rationale on pressure strategy selection.

2.5 Determine service pit ventilation approach (see Guidance -- Conflict C-003-01-02) and document as a design decision requiring confirmation. Per E-002 enrichment, proceed on interim assumption that PKG-003 owns service pit ventilation design until PM obtains ruling.

2.6 Identify mechanical equipment room / utility room spatial requirements and coordinate with architectural and plumbing disciplines.

2.7 Determine wash bay ventilation and heating approach (see Specification REQ-003-01-12 and Guidance -- Wash Bay Heating and Ventilation). Confirm wash bay heating status (heated / unheated / freeze-protection-only) with the design-build team. (Added per X-003.)

**Verification:** Design basis documented; fuel source confirmed (or TBD noted per C-001); preliminary sizing parameters established; pressure strategy selected; calculation method identified.

---

### Step 3 -- Develop Preliminary Layouts

3.1 Prepare conceptual mechanical system layout drawing(s) showing:
  - Heating unit(s) location(s) and approximate coverage zones
  - MUA unit location and discharge direction
  - Air exchanger location
  - Exhaust fan locations (repair bay drops, welding station hood, service pit, wash bay -- per REQ-003-01-12)
  - Ceiling fan locations (6 fans in main shop -- confirm positions relative to crane runway per REQ-003-01-13)
  - Ductwork routing zones (schematic, not fully detailed)
  - Natural gas routing from tie-in to equipment (schematic)

3.2 Confirm crane runway clearance: the two 5-tonne overhead cranes (SOW-0067) operate on trolleys at high level; mechanical equipment and ductwork must not conflict with crane envelope. Coordination with structural (PKG-002) and crane supplier required. Per REQ-003-01-13, include a clearance zone notation or coordination note on the layout drawing.

ASSUMPTION: Crane hook height and runway elevation TBD at structural preliminary design stage; mechanical routing must remain below crane travel envelope.

3.3 Confirm overhead door clearance: large overhead doors in repair bays and wash bay are locations of significant air infiltration; MUA discharge and heating coverage should be assessed relative to door openings.

**Verification:** Layouts reviewed against building program and preliminary structural/architectural information; major conflicts identified and resolved or flagged; crane clearance check documented per REQ-003-01-13.

---

### Step 4 -- Prepare Preliminary Equipment List

4.1 Compile a preliminary mechanical equipment list including:
  - Heating unit(s): type, preliminary capacity (kW or BTU/h), fuel
  - MUA unit: type, preliminary airflow (CFM or L/s), heating capacity
  - Air exchanger: type, preliminary airflow (CFM or L/s)
  - Exhaust fans (repair bay): quantity, preliminary airflow per fan
  - Exhaust fan (welding station): type, preliminary airflow
  - Exhaust fan(s) (service pit): quantity, type, preliminary airflow
  - Ceiling fans: quantity (6), preliminary diameter and motor size
  - Wash bay ventilation/heating equipment (if applicable per REQ-003-01-12)
  - Any additional fans or HVAC units identified during design basis

4.2 For each equipment item, note interdisciplinary coordination requirements per the canonical interface list (Specification REQ-003-01-11):
  - Power requirement (voltage, amperage -- for PKG-004 coordination, interface (c))
  - Weight and structural support requirements (for PKG-002 coordination, interface (b))
  - Fuel connection requirements (for utility tie-in, interface (a))
  - Spatial coordination with plumbing in utility room (interface (d))
  - Clearance relative to crane runway (interface (e))

> **Enrichment [X-002]:** The coordination output list has been aligned with the canonical interface list in Specification REQ-003-01-11 to ensure consistency across documents.

**Verification:** Equipment list complete with sufficient detail for interdisciplinary coordination; all five interface categories addressed.

---

### Step 5 -- Prepare Design Philosophy Narrative

5.1 Write a narrative describing:
  - System selection rationale for each major mechanical system (with reference to Trade-offs table in Guidance)
  - How the air-handling strategy integrates heating, MUA, exhaust, and air exchange
  - Selected building pressure strategy and rationale (per Guidance -- Building Pressure Strategy, X-001)
  - Approach to code compliance (referencing specific code editions per A-001/A-003)
  - Capital cost vs. operating cost considerations for heating system selection (per Guidance Trade-offs, F-004)
  - Key assumptions made (labelled as ASSUMPTION)
  - Outstanding coordination items with other disciplines (referencing canonical interface list, X-002)
  - Items that require County direction or clarification (if any)

5.2 Include a statement of applicable codes and standards with specific editions where confirmed, and explicit TBD designations where editions are pending (per A-001, A-003).

**Verification:** Narrative is complete, source-anchored, and assumptions are clearly labelled.

---

### Step 6 -- Assemble Design Presentation Document

6.1 Compile the Design Presentation document including:
  - Cover/identification (project, deliverable, date, engineer name)
  - Design philosophy and approach narrative (Step 5)
  - Conceptual mechanical system layout drawing(s) (Step 3)
  - Preliminary equipment list (Step 4)
  - Code basis statement (Step 2.1)
  - Interdisciplinary interface summary (per canonical list, REQ-003-01-11)
  - List of assumptions and open items
  - Completeness checklist confirming each REQ verification criterion is addressed (per Specification D-002)

> **Step 6.1a -- Preliminary Code Compliance Check [D-001]:** Before County submission, conduct an internal discipline review of the Design Presentation against applicable code requirements. This check should verify that the proposed system selections, ventilation rates, and equipment capacities are consistent with applicable Alberta Building Code and Safety Codes provisions (or that specific code provisions are identified for detailed verification at the final design stage). Document the review findings and any items deferred to final design. PROPOSAL per D-001: Mechanical Engineer to perform.

6.2 Conduct internal quality review of the Design Presentation prior to County submission. The quality review should include the completeness checklist (D-002) and the preliminary code compliance check (D-001).

6.3 Submit Design Presentation to the County project manager for review and approval.

**Verification:** Document assembled; completeness checklist confirmed; preliminary code compliance check completed; internal review complete; submitted to County.

---

### Step 7 -- County Review and Approval

7.1 Facilitate County review of the Design Presentation. The County project manager will be confirmed at time of project award (RFP §3.1).

7.2 Attend any review meetings as required by the County.

7.3 Receive County written approval or revision comments. **Enrichment [E-001]:** Obtain County approval in a documented written format. Acceptable formats: TBD -- confirm with County project manager whether approval is via formal letter, signed form, or email confirmation. The approval record must be sufficient to demonstrate that REQ-003-01-01 is satisfied. The responsible party for archiving the approval record is the Project Manager. PROPOSAL per E-001: PM to clarify acceptable approval format with County.

7.4 If revision comments received: revise the Design Presentation per County direction, resubmit, and repeat Step 7 until written approval is received.

7.5 Upon County written approval: proceed to final mechanical design deliverables (DEL-003-02 through DEL-003-07).

**Verification:** County written approval received in documented format and archived in project records.

---

### Step 8 -- Transition to Final Design

8.1 Use the approved preliminary design as the basis for final mechanical design.

8.2 Issue approved preliminary design layouts and equipment list to other discipline leads as input for their final design work.

8.3 Confirm coordination issues raised at preliminary stage are resolved before final design commences.

**Verification:** Final design commencement meeting held; preliminary design distributed to discipline leads.
