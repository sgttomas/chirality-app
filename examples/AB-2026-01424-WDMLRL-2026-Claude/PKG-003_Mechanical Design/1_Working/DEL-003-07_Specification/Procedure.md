# Procedure — DEL-003-07 Mechanical Specification

---

## Purpose

This Procedure describes the steps to **produce** the Mechanical Specification (DEL-003-07) artifact — the normative written specification for all mechanical systems in the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill (WDMLRL) Maintenance Shop Addition.

This document guides the Mechanical Engineer and the design-build team through the workflow for developing, coordinating, reviewing, and finalizing the specification to a state suitable for inclusion in the Issued for Construction (IFC) package.

**Scope of this procedure:** Producing the DEL-003-07 specification document, not installing the mechanical systems. Installation is governed by PKG-013 (Mechanical & HVAC Installation).

## Steps

### Step 1 — Establish Mechanical Systems Inventory

**Action:** Compile the complete list of mechanical systems required for the project based on RFP §3.4, App B (Shop), and the Decomposition (SOW-0013, SOW-0035–SOW-0040, SOW-0066).

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Heating system (REQ-M-001)
- [ ] Forced-air makeup air system (REQ-M-002)
- [ ] High-volume air exchanger (REQ-M-003)
- [ ] Heavy equipment exhaust system — hoses and fans (REQ-M-004)
- [ ] Welding station local exhaust ventilation (REQ-M-005)
- [ ] Service pit/trench ventilation (REQ-M-006)
- [ ] Ceiling fans — quantity, size, placement (REQ-M-007)
- [ ] General Building Exhaust Fan(s) — coordinate with electrical package (per Decomposition SOW-0066; see Guidance CONF-M-02 for scope boundary)
- [ ] Natural gas distribution within building — piping to heating and makeup air units (REQ-M-010)
- [ ] Mechanical controls and interlocks (thermostats, CO sensors, VFDs as applicable)

**Output:** Confirmed systems inventory. Cross-reference with DEL-003-05 (Equipment Schedule) scope.

**Source:** RFP §3.4; App B (Shop); Decomposition SOW-0013.

---

### Step 2 — Perform Design Load Calculations (in DEL-003-06)

**Action:** Develop or direct the Mechanical Calculation Package (DEL-003-06) to determine design parameters that will drive specification requirements.

**Key calculations required:**
- [ ] Heating load calculation for the full building (main shop, wash bay, pit area, ancillary spaces) based on Alberta design outdoor temperature.
- [ ] Ventilation/exhaust volume for each system (makeup air, air exchanger, equipment exhaust, welding exhaust, pit ventilation).
- [ ] Air balance summary confirming makeup air volume meets or exceeds total exhaust volume.
- [ ] Ceiling height/stratification analysis to confirm ceiling fan sizing and placement.
- [ ] Natural gas load (BTU/hr) for all connected gas appliances.

**Output:** Design parameters (heating capacity in BTU/hr or kW; ventilation volumes in CFM or L/s; gas supply sizing) to be incorporated as minimum performance requirements in this Specification.

**Note:** Calculations are a prerequisite to completing Specification sections REQ-M-001 through REQ-M-006. Use TBD placeholders in this Specification until calculations are finalized, then update.

**Source:** Standard mechanical engineering practice; ASHRAE standards (ASSUMPTION: applicable); Alberta Safety Codes (location TBD).

---

### Step 3 — Select Equipment and Develop Equipment Schedule (DEL-003-05)

**Action:** Based on calculated loads and the design requirements, select mechanical equipment and document in DEL-003-05 (Mechanical Equipment Schedule).

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Heating unit(s): type, model/performance tier, fuel input, thermal output, efficiency rating (AFUE or equivalent).
- [ ] Makeup air unit: type, supply volume (CFM), heating capacity, heat recovery type (if applicable).
- [ ] Air exchanger: type, volume (CFM), heat/energy recovery effectiveness.
- [ ] Equipment exhaust fan(s): number, volume per fan (CFM), static pressure, motor size.
- [ ] Welding exhaust unit: capture device type, exhaust volume (CFM), motor size.
- [ ] Pit ventilation fan(s): volume (CFM), configuration (supply/exhaust/both).
- [ ] Ceiling fans: diameter, motor rating, mounting height, control type.
- [ ] Gas train and controls for fuel-burning equipment.
- [ ] Thermostats, sensors, interlocks as required.

**Output:** DEL-003-05 Equipment Schedule; equipment selections feed into Specification requirements.

**Source:** DEL-003-06 calculations; RFP §3.4.

---

### Step 4 — Confirm Spatial Coordination

**Action:** Coordinate mechanical system layout with architectural and structural drawings to confirm spatial feasibility before finalizing the Specification.

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Obtain final architectural floor plan (DEL-001-02) and confirm room locations match App B (Shop) intent.
- [ ] Obtain structural crane girder heights and crane clearance envelopes (from PKG-002).
- [ ] Confirm ductwork routing does not conflict with crane travel paths in main shop bays.
- [ ] Confirm equipment exhaust hose/reel locations in repair bays and wash bay are accessible and code-compliant.
- [ ] Confirm welding station local exhaust routing (App B (Shop) shows welding station at northeast corner; confirm with architectural plans).
- [ ] Confirm rooftop or wall penetration locations for makeup air intake, exhaust discharge, and equipment curbs with structural and architectural teams.
- [ ] Confirm utility room space allocation for mechanical equipment adjacent to cistern and plumbing equipment.
- [ ] Confirm mezzanine structural capacity is adequate for any rooftop mechanical equipment (mezzanine is load-bearing per App B (Shop) notes).

**Output:** Coordination transmittals/meeting notes confirming spatial layout. Issue RFIs to other designers as required.

**Source:** App B (Shop); RFP §3.3.2; Decomposition PKG-003 dependencies.

---

### Step 5 — Identify and Confirm Applicable Standards and Permits

**Action:** Confirm the applicable Alberta Safety Codes, building code provisions, and occupational health standards that govern mechanical system design and installation for this project.

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Identify applicable Alberta Safety Codes edition for mechanical systems.
- [ ] Confirm NBC / Alberta Building Code ventilation requirements for industrial/repair garage occupancy.
- [ ] Confirm applicable ASHRAE standard for ventilation rate design.
- [ ] Confirm applicable ACGIH or equivalent standard for welding fume local exhaust ventilation.
- [ ] Confirm CSA B149.1 edition for natural gas installation.
- [ ] Confirm whether service pit qualifies as a "repair garage" under the applicable code and what ventilation rate applies.
- [ ] Contact the authority having jurisdiction (AHJ) to confirm Safety Code Permit requirements and inspection process.

**Output:** Confirmed standards list for inclusion in Specification.md §Standards section. Update any ASSUMPTION labels to confirmed references.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.1, §3.3.2.

---

### Step 6 — Draft the Mechanical Specification Document

**Action:** Using the systems inventory (Step 1), design parameters (Step 2), equipment selections (Step 3), coordination confirmations (Step 4), and standards list (Step 5), draft the full written Mechanical Specification.

**Specification structure (minimum sections to include):**
- [ ] **Division 23 or equivalent** (CSC MasterFormat or project-specific format): HVAC systems.
  - General requirements (scope, submittals, coordination)
  - Products (equipment minimum performance requirements, acceptable types)
  - Execution (installation requirements, startup, testing and balancing)
- [ ] Heating system specification section (one section per system type, or combined)
- [ ] Makeup air unit specification section
- [ ] Air exchanger specification section
- [ ] Equipment exhaust system specification section
- [ ] Local exhaust ventilation (welding station) specification section
- [ ] Service pit ventilation specification section
- [ ] Ceiling fans specification section
- [ ] Controls and thermostats specification section
- [ ] Testing, Adjusting, and Balancing (TAB) requirements
- [ ] Startup and commissioning requirements

**Note on format:** The RFP does not prescribe a specification format. ASSUMPTION: CSC MasterFormat Division 23 (Heating, Ventilating, and Air Conditioning) or equivalent structured format is appropriate for an IFC specification in Alberta. **TBD: Confirm with the design-builder whether CSC MasterFormat Division 23 or an alternative specification format will be used.** The final format is subject to the design-builder's quality management plan and should be confirmed early in the specification development process to avoid restructuring work. *(Lensing ref: D-001)*

**Output:** Draft Mechanical Specification document.

**Source:** RFP §3.4; Steps 1–5 above.

---

### Step 7 — Internal Review and Discipline Coordination

**Action:** Circulate the draft specification for review by the design-build team.

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Mechanical Engineer self-review against RFP §3.4 requirements checklist.
- [ ] Structural Engineer reviews for equipment support, penetration, and load impacts.
- [ ] Electrical Engineer reviews for power supply, control wiring, and interface requirements.
- [ ] Plumbing Engineer reviews for condensate drainage, wash bay interface, and utility room coordination.
- [ ] Architect reviews for space allocation and louver/penetration locations.
- [ ] Resolve all coordination comments and update specification accordingly.
- [ ] Document coordination in transmittal log.
- [ ] Retain formal records of all MEP coordination meetings, including: attendees, decisions made, action items, and responsible parties. These records serve as verification evidence that inter-discipline coordination occurred and issues were resolved. *(Lensing ref: X-002)*

**Output:** Coordinated specification ready for P.Eng. review and stamping.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (fully coordinated IFC design requirement); OBJ-003.

---

### Step 8 — P.Eng. Review, Seal, and Issue for Construction

**Action:** Finalize the specification and associated mechanical drawings for IFC release.

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Mechanical Engineer (P.Eng., Alberta) reviews final specification against calculations and drawings for consistency.
- [ ] Confirm that all design requirements from RFP §3.4 are addressed in the specification or in the drawing set.
- [ ] Confirm that the specification and equipment schedule (DEL-003-05) are mutually consistent.
- [ ] Confirm that the specification cross-references the correct drawing sheet numbers from DEL-003-02, DEL-003-03, DEL-003-04.
- [ ] Obtain Alberta P.Eng. seal on IFC drawing set (note: specification may be sealed by reference or separately — confirm with AHJ).
- [ ] Issue IFC package per project document control procedures.
- [ ] Submit Safety Code Permit application(s) to AHJ.

**Output:** Sealed and issued Mechanical Specification (IFC).

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 (P.Eng. stamp requirement); OBJ-003.

---

### Step 9 — County Preliminary Design Approval (if applicable to mechanical)

**Action:** If the preliminary mechanical design (DEL-003-01) requires County approval before final specification is issued, obtain written approval from the County project representative.

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Confirm with County project manager whether preliminary mechanical design approval gate applies to the specification.
- [ ] Submit preliminary mechanical design package (DEL-003-01) to County for review if not already completed.
- [ ] Obtain written County approval before proceeding to IFC.

**Note:** RFP §3.3.2 states "Deliver Preliminary Design for the County project team to approve." ASSUMPTION: This applies to mechanical design as part of the coordinated preliminary design package.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2; OBJ-003.
