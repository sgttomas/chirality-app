# Specification — DEL-003-07 Mechanical Specification

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

This Mechanical Specification (DEL-003-07) provides the normative written specification governing:

1. **Materials and equipment** for all mechanical systems in the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill (WDMLRL) Maintenance Shop Addition (New North Shop).
2. **Installation requirements and standards** for all mechanical systems.
3. **Performance criteria** for heating, ventilation, makeup air, air exchange, exhaust, and associated systems.
4. **Coordination requirements** with structural, electrical, plumbing, and architectural disciplines.

This specification covers SOW-0013 ("Complete final mechanical design — HVAC, ventilation, exhaust systems") as it applies to the written specification artifact. It is one deliverable within PKG-003 Mechanical Design. Drawing deliverables (DEL-003-02, DEL-003-03, DEL-003-04) and the Equipment Schedule (DEL-003-05) and Calculation Package (DEL-003-06) are companion documents.

**Source:** RFP §1.0, §3.4; Decomposition SOW-0013, PKG-003.

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- **Plumbing systems**: Covered under PKG-006 (Plumbing Design). The cistern, pump systems, floor drains, mud sump connection, and septic system are excluded from this specification except where mechanical-plumbing coordination is noted.
- **Electrical systems**: Power supply to mechanical equipment is covered under PKG-004 (Electrical Design). This specification may reference electrical interface points but does not specify electrical systems.
- **Structural design**: Mechanical equipment support and penetrations are coordinated with PKG-002 (Structural Design) but the structural elements are not specified here.
- **Construction installation**: Detailed installation work is executed under PKG-013 (Mechanical & HVAC Installation). This specification provides the requirements that PKG-013 must satisfy.
- **Ceiling fan power circuits**: Electrical power supply and wiring for ceiling fans is addressed in PKG-004 (Electrical Design). This specification (REQ-M-007) governs ceiling fan airflow function, quantity, placement, and mechanical coordination. (ASSUMPTION: mechanical/electrical responsibility boundary for ceiling fans is split — power to electrical, placement and airflow function to mechanical — pending MEP coordination confirmation. See Guidance CONF-M-01.)

## Requirements

### REQ-M-001: Heating System

**Requirement:** The New North Shop shall be provided with a high-recovery heating system capable of maintaining comfortable working temperatures throughout the main shop, repair bays, wash bay, service pit area, utility room, parts room, and office areas during Alberta winter conditions.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("High recovery heating system"); SOW-0035.

**Notes:**
- System type (e.g., radiant tube, forced-air furnace, unit heaters, in-floor radiant) is TBD pending mechanical design and design-builder selection.
- Heating capacity shall be determined by the Mechanical Engineer in the Calculation Package (DEL-003-06).
- **Minimum heating capacity:** TBD BTU/hr (or kW) — to be determined by DEL-003-06 heating load calculation based on Alberta design outdoor temperature and building thermal envelope. *(Lensing ref: A-005)*
- ASSUMPTION: Natural gas is the primary fuel source per RFP §3.3.2 ("Coordinate natural gas tie-ins").
- The heating system shall be compatible with the 35 ft concrete structure ceiling height. (Source: RFP §3.4; App B (Shop).)

### REQ-M-002: Forced-Air Makeup Air System

**Requirement:** The building shall include a forced-air makeup air system to replace air exhausted by ventilation and exhaust systems, maintain neutral or positive building pressure in occupied areas, and support heating distribution.

**Source:** RFP §3.4; App B (Shop) ("Forced Air Makeup").

**Notes:**
- Makeup air unit capacity shall be sized to balance exhaust air volumes (equipment exhaust, welding exhaust, general ventilation).
- **Minimum makeup air volume:** TBD CFM (or L/s) — shall equal or exceed total exhaust volume; documented air balance calculation required in DEL-003-06. *(Lensing ref: A-005, F-004)*
- Makeup air shall be tempered (heated) before distribution into the shop during cold-weather operation. (ASSUMPTION: required for Alberta climate occupant comfort and building envelope protection.)
- Coordination required with the air exchanger (REQ-M-003) to avoid pressure imbalances.

### REQ-M-003: High-Volume Air Exchanger

**Requirement:** The building shall include a high-volume air exchanger to provide general air change and ventilation throughout the shop space.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("High volume air exchanger"); SOW-0036.

**Notes:**
- Volume and air-change rate shall be determined by the Mechanical Engineer and documented in DEL-003-06.
- **Minimum air change rate:** TBD ACH or CFM (or L/s) — to be determined by DEL-003-06 ventilation calculation per applicable code requirements. *(Lensing ref: A-005)*
- ASSUMPTION: Unit shall comply with applicable Alberta Safety Codes for industrial occupancy ventilation.
- Heat recovery from the air exchanger is encouraged for energy efficiency but the specific configuration is TBD by design.

### REQ-M-004: Heavy Equipment Exhaust System

**Requirement:** All vehicle repair bays shall be provided with exhaust hoses and fans capable of capturing and removing diesel and gasoline exhaust emissions from heavy equipment operating within the building.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Exhaust hoses and fans for heavy equipment"); SOW-0038.

**Notes:**
- The system shall accommodate large equipment such as motor scrapers and other landfill heavy equipment. (Source: RFP §3.4.)
- Number of drops/hose reels per bay: TBD pending layout coordination with DEL-003-02 (HVAC Plans).
- **Minimum exhaust capacity per bay:** TBD CFM (or L/s) — to be determined by DEL-003-06 per applicable ventilation standards. *(Lensing ref: A-005)*
- Ductwork routing shall be coordinated with the 35 ft ceiling structure and crane clearance envelopes. (Source: RFP §3.4; App B (Shop).)

### REQ-M-005: Welding Station Exhaust/Ventilation System

**Requirement:** A dedicated local exhaust ventilation system shall be provided at the welding station to capture and remove welding fumes at the source.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Ventilation at welding station"); App B (Shop) (welding station shown at northeast corner); SOW-0039.

**Notes:**
- The welding station is located per conceptual floor plan in App B (Shop); exact location subject to final architectural layout.
- System type (e.g., fume arm, backdraft hood, ductless filter unit) is TBD by design.
- ASSUMPTION: System shall comply with ACGIH Industrial Ventilation guidelines or equivalent standard for welding fume control (standard reference location TBD).
- **Minimum capture velocity at welding hood/arm:** TBD fpm (or m/s) — to be determined by Mechanical Engineer per ACGIH Industrial Ventilation manual or equivalent (specific edition and table reference TBD). *(Lensing ref: A-005, X-005)*
- Coordination required with makeup air supply to maintain effective capture velocity at the welding hood/arm.

### REQ-M-006: Service Pit/Trench Ventilation

**Requirement:** The service pit/trench shall be ventilated to prevent accumulation of combustion gases, fuel vapours, and CO from equipment being serviced overhead.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Ventilated and lighted service pit area for mechanics to perform servicing under equipment"); App B (Shop).

**Notes:**
- **Minimum pit ventilation rate:** TBD CFM per sq ft (or L/s per m^2) of pit floor area — to be determined per applicable code requirements (see Guidance C-4 for code classification question). *(Lensing ref: A-005)*
- ASSUMPTION: A forced mechanical ventilation system (supply and/or exhaust) is required for below-grade pit areas per applicable Safety Codes.
- Lighting in the pit is addressed by the Electrical package (PKG-004); the Mechanical package coordinates ventilation only.

### REQ-M-007: Ceiling Fans

**Requirement:** Ceiling fans shall be provided in the main shop area to promote air circulation and destratify heated air at the 35 ft ceiling height.

**Source:** Decomposition SOW-0040 ("Install 6 ceiling fans in main shop area"); App B-Elec (as referenced in Decomposition).

**Notes:**
- Quantity: 6 (per Decomposition SOW-0040). ASSUMPTION: Best-effort from Decomposition citing App B-Elec; final quantity subject to design review and MEP coordination. **NOTE:** Ceiling fan count authority is unresolved — Decomposition SOW-0040 cites App B-Elec (6 fans), but App B (Shop) floor plan does not explicitly confirm this count. See Guidance CONF-M-01 for conflict detail. County or design team confirmation required. *(Lensing ref: F-003)*
- Fan size and motor specifications: TBD pending design.
- Power supply and wiring for fans is covered by PKG-004 (Electrical Design).
- Fan placement shall be coordinated with crane clearance envelopes and structural elements. (Source: App B (Shop) — cranes shown at 5-tonne capacity.)

### REQ-M-008: Alberta Safety Code Compliance — Mechanical

**Requirement:** All mechanical systems shall comply with applicable Alberta Safety Codes and associated permits shall be obtained prior to installation and occupancy.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.1 ("Pay for development permit, building permit, and Safety Code Permits Fees"); §3.3.2 ("The proposed building must adhere to all Alberta Safety Codes").

**Notes:**
- The Proponent is responsible for obtaining all applicable mechanical Safety Code Permits.
- All inspections shall be coordinated with the County project representative, who must be present for all inspections. (Source: RFP §3.3.2.)
- Copies of all inspection reports shall be provided to the County. (Source: RFP §3.3.2.)

### REQ-M-009: P.Eng. Stamp — Mechanical Drawings

**Requirement:** All Issued for Construction (IFC) mechanical drawings shall be signed and stamped by a Professional Engineer (P.Eng.) licensed to practice in the Province of Alberta.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 ("All Issued For Construction Drawings must be signed/stamped by a professional engineer licensed to practice in the province of Alberta").

**Notes:**
- Applies to DEL-003-02, DEL-003-03, DEL-003-04 companion drawing deliverables.
- The Mechanical Specification (this document) shall be coordinated with and referenced by the stamped drawing set.

### REQ-M-010: Natural Gas Coordination

**Requirement:** Mechanical design shall coordinate the natural gas tie-in for heating equipment with the utility provider and the civil/site design.

**Source:** RFP §3.3.2 ("Coordinate natural gas, electrical tie-ins, and communication lines").

**Notes:**
- Gas service sizing shall be determined based on total connected load from heating and makeup air equipment.
- Coordination required with PKG-005 (Civil Design) for site routing.

### REQ-M-011: Coordination with Plumbing (Floor Drains in Wash Bay)

**Requirement:** Mechanical design shall coordinate with plumbing design (PKG-006) regarding the wash bay drainage, mud sump, and any floor drain sump pumping that may require mechanical integration.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Mud sump from wash bay on exterior of building for clean out with excavator"); SOW-0047 (absorbed into SOW-0027b per Decomposition).

**Notes:**
- The Mechanical Specification does not govern wash bay drainage design; this is a coordination note only.
- If sump pumping is required, coordination is with PKG-006 (Plumbing Design).

### REQ-M-012: Wash Bay Exhaust Ventilation

**Requirement:** The enclosed wash bay shall be provided with exhaust ventilation to control humidity, prevent condensation on building elements, and maintain acceptable indoor air quality during and after wash operations.

**Source:** RFP §3.4 ("Single enclosed bay dedicated for washing large equipment such as a motor scraper"); Guidance C-6 (humidity and freeze protection consideration). *(Lensing ref: A-002)*

**Notes:**
- **Minimum exhaust ventilation rate:** TBD CFM (or L/s) — to be determined by Mechanical Engineer based on wash bay volume and moisture generation rate.
- Exhaust system shall be capable of removing moisture-laden air to prevent freeze damage to building elements during non-operational periods.
- Coordination required with heating system to maintain wash bay temperature above freezing at all times.
- ASSUMPTION: Dedicated exhaust ventilation is required for the enclosed wash bay based on engineering best practice for humidity control in cold climates.

### REQ-M-013: Testing, Adjusting, and Balancing (TAB)

**Requirement:** All mechanical HVAC and ventilation systems shall undergo Testing, Adjusting, and Balancing (TAB) after installation and prior to final acceptance, performed by a qualified TAB contractor.

**Source:** Procedure Step 6 (TAB requirements as a specification section); Procedure Records (TAB report as a required record). *(Lensing ref: C-003)*

**Notes:**
- TAB shall confirm that all airflow volumes, temperatures, and pressures meet the design values specified in DEL-003-06 and this Specification.
- A written TAB report shall be provided to the Owner as a commissioning deliverable.
- ASSUMPTION: TAB by a qualified independent contractor is standard practice for industrial HVAC installations in Alberta.

### REQ-M-014: Startup and Commissioning

**Requirement:** All mechanical systems shall undergo a startup and commissioning process to verify proper installation, operation, and performance prior to occupancy.

**Source:** Procedure Step 6 (startup and commissioning requirements as a specification section). *(Lensing ref: C-004)*

**Notes:**
- Commissioning shall include: equipment startup verification, control sequence testing, safety interlock verification, and operational demonstration.
- Commissioning results shall be documented and provided to the Owner.
- Commissioning shall be coordinated with TAB (REQ-M-013) and Safety Code inspections (REQ-M-008).
- ASSUMPTION: Commissioning is required per standard engineering practice for industrial HVAC systems.

### REQ-M-015: Mechanical Equipment Submittals

**Requirement:** The design-builder shall submit mechanical equipment shop drawings, cut sheets, and performance data for review and approval prior to equipment procurement and installation.

**Source:** Standard design-build contractual practice; CCDC 14-2013 framework. *(Lensing ref: F-002, X-003)*

**Notes:**
- Submittals shall include, at minimum: equipment manufacturer data, performance curves or rated capacities, dimensional drawings, and electrical requirements.
- **Submittal evaluation criteria:** Equipment performance data shall meet or exceed the minimum thresholds specified in this Specification (REQ-M-001 through REQ-M-007 and REQ-M-012). Submittals that do not demonstrate compliance with specified minimums shall be rejected or returned for revision.
- ASSUMPTION: Submittal review is required per standard design-build practice; specific submittal process to be defined in the design-builder's quality management plan.
