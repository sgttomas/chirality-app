# Specification: DEL-004-09 Electrical Specification

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

This Electrical Specification governs the electrical design for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition (the "Addition"). It establishes written requirements for the complete final electrical design (SOW-0014) of the new approximately 13,000 sq ft maintenance shop building, including:

- Three-phase power supply and service tie-in to the building
- Power distribution (panels, feeders, branch circuits)
- Lighting systems (high bay, LED linear, ceiling fans)
- Exhaust ventilation circuits (exhaust fans)
- Receptacle layouts (15 A through 50 A, 120 V and 240 V)
- Dedicated equipment circuits (overhead cranes, overhead doors, compressor, pumps)
- Low-voltage systems (security camera wiring, 2-way radio antenna wire)
- Electrical service tie-in coordination (SOW-0081)
- Communications lines tie-in coordination (SOW-0082)

This specification is intended to be read alongside the PKG-004 drawing deliverables (DEL-004-02 through DEL-004-07) and the Electrical Calculation Package (DEL-004-08). Together these constitute the full electrical design package.

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Mechanical/HVAC equipment selection (PKG-003)
- Plumbing system design (PKG-006)
- Structural supports for cranes (PKG-002; crane power circuits are IN scope for PKG-004)
- Procurement of electrical equipment or materials (installation packages PKG-015 and PKG-016)
- Supply of welding equipment (County responsibility per RFP §3.4 and Decomp SOW-0204)
- Electrical design for existing Old North Shop renovation (scope TBD — **BLOCKING**: no explicit requirement in sources for Old North Shop electrical work; Owner direction required to confirm exclusion or inclusion; ASSUMPTION: excluded from this specification, limited to new Addition only, unless Owner directs otherwise) *(Lensing ref: D-002)*

### REQ-021: Grounding and Bonding System
**Statement:** The electrical design shall include a complete grounding and bonding system in accordance with CEC requirements for commercial/industrial installations. The grounding system design shall address service entrance grounding, equipment grounding conductors, bonding of metallic piping and structural steel, and grounding electrode system.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — CEC Part I requires grounding and bonding for all commercial/industrial electrical installations; no specific grounding requirements were stated in RFP or App B, but this is a statutory requirement for any installation of this type. *(Lensing ref: F-001)*
**Verification:** Grounding system shown on power distribution plans (DEL-004-03); grounding calculations in DEL-004-08; compliance confirmed via Safety Code inspection.

### REQ-022: Service Voltage and Capacity
**Statement:** The service entrance voltage level and ampacity shall be confirmed with the electrical utility and shall be sufficient to serve the total calculated demand of the facility.
**Source:** RFP §3.4; Decomp SOW-0051, Decomp SOW-0081. Service voltage level (e.g., 480Y/277V or 208Y/120V) is TBD pending utility confirmation. *(Lensing ref: A-001)*
**Verification:** Utility confirmation documentation; demand calculation (DEL-004-08) confirms service entrance capacity is adequate for total connected load. *(Lensing ref: F-002)*

### REQ-023: Wiring Methods and Conduit System
**Statement:** The wiring method and conduit system for the electrical installation shall be specified by the Electrical Engineer, appropriate to the industrial maintenance shop environment and compliant with CEC requirements for the applicable location classifications (dry, damp, wet).
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — CEC Part I requires appropriate wiring methods for the installation environment; no conduit type specified in RFP or App B. *(Lensing ref: A-002)*
**Note:** Conduit/wiring method selection is a design decision (see Guidance Trade-off 1). The Electrical Engineer shall document the rationale for the selected wiring method.
**Verification:** Wiring method noted on power distribution plans (DEL-004-03) and referenced in calculation package (DEL-004-08).

### REQ-024: Emergency and Egress Lighting
**Statement:** Emergency lighting and illuminated exit signage shall be provided in accordance with CEC, Alberta Building Code, and Alberta Fire Code requirements for a commercial/industrial building of this size and occupancy classification.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — CEC, Alberta Building Code, and Alberta Fire Code require emergency/egress lighting for commercial/industrial buildings; not explicitly addressed in RFP or App B. *(Lensing ref: X-003)*
**Note:** Specific emergency lighting fixture types, locations, and battery backup duration are TBD pending code review by the Electrical Engineer.
**Verification:** Emergency lighting shown on lighting plans (DEL-004-04); compliance confirmed via code compliance checklist (see REQ-019 verification) and Safety Code inspection.

### REQ-025: Lighting Control Strategy
**Statement:** A lighting control strategy (switching, dimming, and/or occupancy-based control) shall be developed for all illuminated spaces.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — lighting control is a standard design element for commercial/industrial installations; no control strategy specified in RFP or App B. Owner input required to confirm operational preferences. *(Lensing ref: X-002)*
**Note:** Control strategy is TBD pending Owner preference. See Guidance Trade-off 2 for design considerations.
**Verification:** Lighting control noted on lighting plans (DEL-004-04); switching/control circuits on panel schedules (DEL-004-06).

### REQ-026: Minimum Illumination Levels
**Statement:** The electrical design shall achieve minimum illumination levels (lux or foot-candles at work plane) appropriate to the occupancy and task requirements of each space, in accordance with applicable CEC and occupational health and safety (OH&S) code requirements.
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — CEC and Alberta OH&S regulations establish minimum illumination requirements for industrial workspaces; specific lux values are TBD pending calculation by the Electrical Engineer. *(Lensing ref: C-001)*
**Note:** Minimum illumination levels for each space (main shop, wash bay, ancillary spaces, service pit) shall be documented in the calculation package (DEL-004-08).
**Verification:** Lighting calculation (DEL-004-08) confirms illumination levels meet or exceed applicable minimums at work plane height. *(Lensing ref: C-003)*

### REQ-027: Voltage Drop Compliance
**Statement:** Branch circuit and feeder conductor sizing shall ensure voltage drop does not exceed the maximum permissible percentage per CEC recommendations (ASSUMPTION: 3% for branch circuits, 5% combined feeder and branch circuit, per CEC Appendix D, current edition).
**Source:** ASSUMPTION — CEC Appendix D provides voltage drop guidelines for electrical installations; Procedure Step 5.3 references voltage drop as a design consideration. *(Lensing ref: X-004)*
**Verification:** Voltage drop calculations included in DEL-004-08; compliance confirmed by design review.
