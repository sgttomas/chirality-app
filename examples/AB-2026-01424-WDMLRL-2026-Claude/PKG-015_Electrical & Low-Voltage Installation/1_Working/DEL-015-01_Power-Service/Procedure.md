# Procedure — DEL-015-01 Three-Phase Power Service

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and deliver the Three-Phase Power Service installation for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition. It covers the sequence of activities from design coordination through utility tie-in, installation, inspection, and commissioning of the main electrical service and main distribution panel (MDP).

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Design Coordination (Pre-Construction)

**Step 1.1 — Confirm utility three-phase availability**
Contact the applicable electrical utility serving SW 14–44–21–W4 to confirm:
- Three-phase service is available or can be extended to the site.
- Service voltage options available.
- Utility application process and lead time for service connection.

**[A-004] Record the utility's estimated lead time and incorporate it into the project schedule as a constraint with milestone dates. Document: (a) date of initial contact, (b) utility's estimated lead time, (c) scheduled energization date, (d) any conditions or prerequisites imposed by the utility.**

*Note: This should occur during design phase (PKG-004), not after construction mobilization. Rural utility lead times can be significant.*

**Step 1.2 — Submit utility service application**
Submit all required utility application forms and pay applicable fees to initiate the service connection process (SOW-0081). Document the utility service application date and expected energization date.

**[X-005] Record the following utility coordination milestones with dates for audit trail purposes: (a) application submission date, (b) utility acknowledgment/confirmation date, (c) scheduled energization date, (d) any interim milestone dates (e.g., utility site visit, infrastructure extension start/completion). These records support SOW-0081 completion verification.**

**Step 1.3 — Coordinate service entrance requirements with design engineer**
Confirm with PKG-004 engineer:
- Service voltage and configuration (208Y/120V, 480Y/277V, or other — TBD)
- Service ampacity based on full IFC load schedule
- Meter base and service entrance specifications per utility requirements
- Grounding electrode system design
- Location of service trench (as indicated on App B-Elec conceptual drawing)

**Step 1.4 — Obtain Electrical Safety Code permit**
Submit permit application to the Alberta Safety Codes authority (electrical discipline). Permit must be in hand before installation begins.

**[F-003] Step 1.5 — Prerequisite Validation Gate**
Before proceeding to Phase 2, formally confirm and document that all prerequisites (PRE-1 through PRE-7) are satisfied. The Proponent project manager shall sign off on the prerequisite checklist. If any prerequisite is not met, document the deviation and obtain written approval to proceed (or halt until resolved). Retain the signed checklist in the project file.

---

### Phase 2 — Rough-In / Service Entrance Installation

**Step 2.1 — Install service trench conduit and underground conductors**
Install underground conduit and service conductors from the utility point of connection to the service entrance location on the building, per IFC drawings and utility requirements.
*Note: Service trench is indicated on conceptual drawing (App B-Elec). Exact routing per IFC design.*

**Step 2.2 — Install service entrance components**
Install service entrance equipment including:
- Weatherhead or service entrance cap (if overhead) or underground termination (if underground)
- Meter base per utility specifications (note: the revenue meter itself is utility-owned and utility-installed in Alberta — see Datasheet Attributes [X-006])
- Service entrance conduit and conductors
- Main disconnecting means
*Per IFC electrical drawings and utility service requirements.*

**Step 2.3 — Install main distribution panel (MDP)**
Mount and wire the main distribution panel in the electrical room of the new shop addition per IFC drawings. Install all breakers, bus bars, and internal wiring per panel schedule on IFC drawings.

**Step 2.4 — Install grounding electrode system**
Install grounding electrodes (type per IFC design — TBD; may include ground rods, concrete-encased electrodes, or other per CSA C22.1), grounding electrode conductors, and all bonding connections per IFC drawings and code requirements.

**Step 2.5 — Pull and terminate service conductors to MDP**
Install and terminate service entrance conductors into the MDP. Verify conductor sizing per IFC load schedule and applicable code.

---

### Phase 3 — Inspection and Utility Energization

**Step 3.1 — Request Safety Code inspection (rough-in)**
Submit inspection request to Alberta Safety Codes authority for rough-in inspection of service entrance and grounding. Notify County project representative — County representative must be present.

**Step 3.2 — Correct deficiencies (if any)**
Address any deficiencies identified by the Safety Codes officer. Re-schedule inspection as required.

**Step 3.3 — Coordinate utility energization**
Notify the utility company that the service entrance work is complete and inspection has passed. Schedule utility energization of the service.
*Note: Utility energization timing must align with project schedule to allow downstream electrical commissioning before December 31, 2026.*

**Step 3.4 — Utility energization and MDP live testing**
With utility service energized, verify:
- Correct voltage at MDP terminals per IFC design
- **[X-003] Correct phase rotation: verify phase rotation direction (e.g., ABC rotation per IFC design) using a phase rotation meter or phase sequence indicator. Record the verified rotation direction. Incorrect phase rotation can damage three-phase motor loads (compressor, pressure washer, fire hose pump, cranes).**
- All breakers and disconnecting means function correctly
- No visible faults, arcing, or overheating

**Step 3.5 — Final Safety Code inspection**
Request final electrical Safety Code inspection. Notify County representative. Provide copy of all completed inspection reports to County project representative.

---

### Phase 4 — Commissioning and Handover

**Step 4.1 — Confirm MDP operational**
Once MDP is commissioned and inspected, confirm that the MDP is operational and ready to serve downstream loads. **[E-003] Note: Energization and verification of downstream circuits (DEL-015-02 through DEL-015-05) is the responsibility of the respective downstream deliverables. This deliverable's commissioning scope ends at the MDP output terminals.**

**Step 4.2 — Confirm MDP capacity and breaker assignments**
Verify that the MDP has sufficient capacity and appropriate breaker assignments for all loads per the IFC panel schedule, including:
- Lighting branch circuits
- General receptacle circuits
- High-demand circuits (40A compressor, 70A fire hose pump, 70A pressure washer)
- Welding circuits (50A 240V)
- Crane power circuits (ampacity TBD)
- Overhead door circuits

**Step 4.3 — Prepare commissioning record**
Document that the three-phase service is energized and the MDP is operational. Record:
- Date of utility energization
- MDP panel schedule confirmed against IFC
- Inspection report numbers and pass dates
- Utility service confirmation / account information
- **[X-003] Phase rotation verification result**
- **[A-003, X-004] Ground resistance test result and method used**

**Step 4.4 — Prepare as-built markups**
Record any deviations from IFC drawings on as-built markup drawings. Submit to PKG-004 engineer for as-built drawing production. **[X-001] As-built drawings are standard practice for electrical service installations. Confirm whether formal as-built drawings are contractually required under CCDC 14-2013 contract terms — project manager to resolve. Regardless of contractual obligation, as-built markups should be prepared and retained.**

**[D-002] Step 4.5 — Prepare Conformance Closure Checklist**
Prepare and submit the Conformance Closure Checklist attesting that all requirements (REQ-015-01-001 through REQ-015-01-014) have been satisfied. Submit to Proponent project manager and County project representative prior to CCC issuance. See Specification REQ-015-01-014.
