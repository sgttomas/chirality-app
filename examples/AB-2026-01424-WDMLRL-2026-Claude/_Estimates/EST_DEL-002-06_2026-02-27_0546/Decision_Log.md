# Decision Log -- EST_DEL-002-06_2026-02-27_0546

| DecisionID | Decision | Rationale | Alternatives Considered |
|---|---|---|---|
| DEC-001 | Used LOE-based parametric model (hours x rates) rather than percentage-of-construction fee model | Level_of_Effort.csv provides direct hour allocations per deliverable and role, which is more granular and traceable than applying a fee percentage to an unknown construction value. The Professional_Design_Fees.csv (DF-02: structural fee 1.2%-2.5% of construction value) requires a construction value estimate that is not in scope for this run. | Alternative: Apply DF-02 recommended 1.8% to an assumed construction value. Rejected because construction value is not established and would introduce a circular dependency. |
| DEC-002 | Priced all labor lines as PARAMETRIC (consistent with BASIS_OF_ESTIMATE) | All pricing sources are parametric in nature (estimated hours and estimated rates). No quotes, historical actuals, or allowance tables were provided. FALLBACK_POLICY=ALLOW_PARAMETRIC is satisfied without needing fallback. | No alternative methods available from provided price sources. |
| DEC-003 | Created scope-trace lines (DL-005 through DL-018) at $0 rather than allocating hours to individual structural design elements | The LOE model provides hours by role, not by design element (wall design, floor slab, cover system, etc.). Allocating the 84 Structural Engineer hours across 10 design elements would require invention of sub-allocations not supported by the pricing sources. Zero-amount scope-trace lines maintain traceability without fabricating data. | Alternative: Pro-rate 84 hours across design elements. Rejected because this would be fabricated precision not supported by source data. |
| DEC-004 | CBS categories set as LABOR-MGMT and LABOR-DESIGN | No CBS scheme was provided in the brief or decomposition. Applied a simple labor-type CBS consistent with the deliverable type (professional engineering drawing set with no material/equipment costs). | Alternative: Single CBS category (LABOR). Rejected in favor of finer granularity distinguishing management from design effort. |
| DEC-005 | Did not apply rounding | ROUNDING=NONE per brief. All amounts are exact products of integer hours and integer rates. | |
| DEC-006 | Scope traceability items derived from all four documents rather than Datasheet alone | The service pit drawing set deliverable has priceable scope elements traceable to Specification requirements (R-001 through R-012), Procedure steps (Steps 1-9), Guidance considerations (C-4 edge conditions), and Datasheet structural attributes. All four documents contribute meaningful scope items. | Alternative: Limit scope items to Datasheet only. Rejected because Specification, Guidance, and Procedure identify distinct engineering activities that are part of this deliverable's scope. |
| DEC-007 | Service pit drawing set estimated as a design labor deliverable only; no construction material/equipment costs included | DEL-002-06 is a Drawing Set deliverable under PKG-002 (Structural Design). The physical construction of the service pit is under DEL-011-06 (PKG-011). This estimate covers only the professional engineering effort to produce the structural drawings. | Alternative: Include construction cost estimate for the service pit. Not applicable -- construction is a separate deliverable with separate scope. |
