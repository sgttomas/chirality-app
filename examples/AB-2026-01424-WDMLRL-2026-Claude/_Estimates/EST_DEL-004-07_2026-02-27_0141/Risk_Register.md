## RSK-001 — Scope Expansion from Additional Low-Voltage Systems

**Risk:** If fire alarm, data/network, access control, or intercom systems are confirmed as in-scope at preliminary design (Specification REQ-007-08/09), the LOE hours (130 total) may be insufficient, and the estimate total would increase.

**Likelihood:** MEDIUM — fire alarm is likely code-required for this occupancy type (ASSUMPTION in Specification and Guidance). Data/network and access control are plausible for a facility with an office and secure parts room.

**Impact:** MEDIUM to HIGH — adding a fire alarm system design could add 20-40+ Electrical Engineer hours depending on complexity. Multiple additional systems could double the current 84-hour engineering allocation.

**Mitigation:** Resolve REQ-007-08/09 at preliminary design stage. Rerun estimate after scope confirmation.

## RSK-002 — Old North Shop Scope Inclusion

**Risk:** If Old North Shop renovation areas are confirmed as in-scope for low-voltage systems (Conflict Table CON-007-02), additional design hours are needed for survey, layout, and coordination with renovation design.

**Likelihood:** LOW to MEDIUM — Specification mentions it but Datasheet and Guidance record as TBD.

**Impact:** LOW to MEDIUM — incremental effort likely 10-20 Electrical Engineer hours for renovation area low-voltage plans.

**Mitigation:** County ruling at preliminary design to confirm scope boundary. Rerun estimate if in-scope.

## RSK-003 — Parametric Hour Model Underestimation

**Risk:** The LOE model assigns standard drawing-set hours that may not reflect the actual complexity of this deliverable, particularly given the number of TBD scope items requiring County consultation, code research (CEC edition identification, permit pathway), and multi-discipline coordination.

**Likelihood:** LOW — the confirmed scope (2 low-voltage systems) is relatively simple, and the 84-hour engineering allocation is reasonable for plan-level design. Risk increases if additional systems are added (see RSK-001).

**Impact:** LOW — if hours are modestly underestimated (e.g., by 10-20%), the cost impact is approximately $1,650-$3,300.

**Mitigation:** Validate LOE hours against actual project complexity once preliminary design is complete.
