The actual area will be determined by the Structural Engineer of Record in IFC drawings (DEL-002-05). A change in area would directly scale the framing, deck, and labour line items (DL-001, DL-002, DL-006, DL-007, DL-017, DL-018, DL-019), which together account for approximately $93,167 (66.8% of the total).

2. **Structural system is assumed steel-framed.** Guidance Trade-offs indicate steel framing is the likely system. If a concrete mezzanine structure were selected instead, unit rates would change materially.

3. **Mezzanine extent over wash bay is unresolved (CFT-011-07-01).** Full-span vs. partial-span affects total area and framing complexity.

4. **Scope boundary with PKG-012 is unresolved (CFT-011-07-02).** This estimate includes the structural deck only (bare/structural finish). Any wearing surface, non-slip treatment, or finish flooring is excluded and assumed to fall under PKG-012.

5. **Connection hardware is an allowance ($4,500).** Actual connection scope depends entirely on the IFC structural drawings and superstructure configuration. Confidence is LOW.

6. **No equipment or crane costs are included.** Overhead cranes (EQ-01) are separate scope. No lifting equipment rental for erection has been explicitly priced; it is assumed included in the parametric framing rate.

7. **Design live load TBD.** The Guidance references 4.8-7.2 kPa for heavy industrial storage. Higher load ratings would increase structural steel member sizes and potentially increase $/m2 rates.
