## Key Warnings and Coverage Gaps

1. **Trench distance is assumed (75m).** Actual distance from utility service points to building is TBD pending civil design (PKG-005) and utility provider confirmation. Cost is linearly sensitive to this distance.

2. **Utility service connection costs are parametric allowances.** No quotes from utility providers are available. The gas ($25,000) and electrical ($45,000) allowances are based on parametric estimates for rural Alberta new service connections and carry LOW confidence.

3. **Transformer ownership is unresolved.** If the electrical transformer is customer-owned rather than utility-owned, add $30,000 to $80,000 to the electrical service cost.

4. **Gas main extension risk.** If the site is far from the nearest gas main, a main extension could add $50,000 to $200,000+ (TBD from provider). The estimate assumes no main extension is required.

5. **Gas as heating fuel is an assumption.** If gas is not confirmed as the heating fuel, the gas tie-in scope may change materially.

6. **Communication scope is minimal.** Only antenna wiring conduit and basic telecom are priced. If the County requires additional services (internet, telephone), costs would increase.

7. **No contingency is included.** The estimate does not include a contingency allowance. Given the number of TBD items and LOW confidence on major cost drivers, a 15-25% contingency would be warranted.
