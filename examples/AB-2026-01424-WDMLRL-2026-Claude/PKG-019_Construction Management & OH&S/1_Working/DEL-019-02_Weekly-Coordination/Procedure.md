## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps required to establish, operate, and close out the weekly coordination meeting program and the billing coordination process for DEL-019-02. It covers both the production of the deliverable (setting up the process at project award) and its ongoing operation through to project completion.

The procedure fulfils SOW-0086 (Coordinate weekly meetings with County project manager) and SOW-0087 (Coordinate billing through County project manager), as required by RFP §3.1, p.11.

### Phase C — Billing Coordination Cycle (Ongoing — Per Invoice Cycle)

**Step C-1: Prepare Invoice**
- Project Manager (or designated accounting contact) prepares a "proper invoice" in accordance with the Alberta Prompt Payment and Construction Lien Act and any County-specific requirements confirmed in Step A-5.
- Invoice must reflect Work performed in the billing period.
- Submit at minimum once every thirty-one (31) days.
- Source: RFP §2.13.2, p.11.

**Step C-2: Submit Invoice Through County Project Manager**
- Submit the invoice to the County project manager through the agreed channel (confirmed in Step A-5).
- Retain a copy and record submission date in the Billing Correspondence Log.
- Source: RFP §3.1, p.11; SOW-0087.

**Step C-3: Track Payment**
- Monitor receipt of payment within twenty-eight (28) days of invoice submission.
- Record payment amounts, dates, and applied holdback (10%) in the Billing Correspondence Log.
- Source: RFP §2.13.2, p.11.

**Step C-4: Flag Discrepancies or Withheld Payments**
- If payment is withheld or reduced, document the reason per Owner notification.
- Raise on the next weekly meeting agenda for resolution.
- Source: RFP §2.13.1, p.11.

**Step C-5: Maintain Holdback Tracking**
- Track the cumulative lien holdback balance held by the Owner throughout the project.
- Holdback release is triggered only upon conditions in Step D-2.
- Source: RFP §2.13.2, §2.11, p.11, p.7.

### Phase D — Project Completion and Closeout

**Step D-1: Final Meeting(s) and Commissioning Transition**
- As the project transitions to commissioning and closeout, ensure the weekly meeting agenda incorporates commissioning status updates and final inspection coordination.
- Coordinate with DEL-020-01 (Commissioning) and DEL-020-02 (Final Inspection).
- Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md (External Package Dependencies — PKG-020).

**Step D-2: Assemble Holdback Release Package**
- Upon Owner issuance of signed Construction Completion Certificate (CCC) and correction of all deficiencies, assemble the holdback release documentation package:
  1. Signed CCC issued by Owner (RFP §2.13.2.1, p.11–12)
  2. Letter from Design-Builder confirming all payment claims have been submitted (RFP §2.13.2.2, p.12)
  3. WCB Letter of Clearance and Statutory Declaration of no outstanding claims from suppliers, subcontractors, labourers, or other affected parties (RFP §2.13.2.3, p.12)
- Submit to Owner through the County project manager.
- Source: RFP §2.13.2, p.11.

**Step D-3: Final Meeting and Record Closure**
- Hold final weekly coordination meeting to confirm project closeout status.
- Confirm all action items closed.
- Archive all meeting records and billing records in the **Project Document Archive** (canonical term). [Lensing: X-001]
