## Purpose

DEL-019-02 exists to satisfy the RFP requirement that the successful Design-Builder "coordinate inspections, weekly meetings, and billing through the County project manager" (RFP §3.1, p.11). This deliverable serves two related functions:

1. **Communication infrastructure:** The weekly meeting forum is the primary structured mechanism by which the Design-Builder and Camrose County maintain project alignment throughout construction. It creates a predictable, documented touchpoint that supports on-time delivery (OBJ-002) and safe site operations (OBJ-007).

2. **Financial administration channel:** Billing coordination through the County project manager provides a single point of contact for payment claims and invoice processing, consistent with the payment framework established in RFP §2.13.

The two functions are grouped in a single deliverable because both flow through the same County counterparty (the County project manager, identity confirmed at project award per RFP §3.1) and both require sustained operational execution across the full project duration.

## Principles

### P-1: The County project manager is the sole coordination counterparty
All weekly meetings and all billing must flow through the County project manager. Do not establish parallel channels to other County representatives for these functions without explicit direction. (Source: RFP §3.1, p.11)

### P-2: Consistency of cadence protects the project
Weekly meetings held consistently — even when there is little to report — maintain relationship continuity, preserve a documented record, and provide an early-warning mechanism for emerging issues. Skipping meetings creates gaps in the record and can mask deteriorating coordination. (Source: OBJ-007 — "weekly County meetings must be fulfilled"; _CONTEXT.md — Success Criteria)

### P-3: Documentation is evidence
Meeting minutes and billing records are not administrative overhead — they are the evidence base that supports payment claims, holdback release, dispute resolution, and OH&S compliance. Minutes should be complete, accurate, and archived promptly. (Source: _CONTEXT.md — Success Criteria; RFP §2.13.2)

### P-4: Billing compliance is time-sensitive
The 31-day maximum invoice interval and 28-day payment terms create a rhythm that must be managed actively. Missing an invoice cycle delays cash flow and may complicate holdback tracking. (Source: RFP §2.13.2, p.11)

### P-5: Upstream governance must precede operations
The authority and governance structures established under DEL-019-01 (Prime Contractor Designation) are prerequisites. Do not attempt to formally establish weekly meeting governance before DEL-019-01 is complete. (Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md)

The weekly meeting is not limited to DEL-019-02 topics. ### C-4: Inspection coordination appears in this forum
RFP §3.2 (Scope of Work) and SOW-0084–SOW-0085 require that the County representative be present at all inspections and receive copies of all completed inspection reports. While inspection coordination is formally assigned to DEL-009-04, the weekly meeting is the natural scheduling and status forum for this activity. (Source: RFP §3.3.2, p.12)

### C-5: The 10% lien holdback is a statutory requirement
The holdback is governed by the Alberta Prompt Payment and Construction Lien Act (referenced in RFP §2.13.2). This is not a negotiable project term. Billing processes and cash flow planning must account for the fact that 10% of all progress payments will be retained until final project completion and documentation. (Source: RFP §2.13.2, p.11)

### C-6: Payment withholding triggers must be actively managed
The Owner has the right to withhold payment for a range of causes including unsatisfactory progress, defective unremedied work, claims by third parties, failure to pay subcontractors, and fraudulent documentation (RFP §2.13.1, p.11). The weekly meeting is the appropriate forum to surface and address issues that could trigger withholding before they escalate. Proactive disclosure and corrective action documentation in meeting minutes reduces this risk.

## Trade-offs

### T-1: Meeting frequency vs. operational overhead
Weekly meetings are mandated (SOW-0086). There is no discretion to reduce frequency. However, meeting duration and agenda scope can be managed to minimize overhead. Standing agenda templates and efficient facilitation reduce the burden without compromising compliance.

### T-2: Billing cycle timing vs. cash flow vs. administrative load
The RFP requires invoices at minimum every 31 days but does not prevent more frequent invoicing (subject to what constitutes a "proper invoice"). More frequent invoicing may improve cash flow but increases administrative processing. Less frequent invoicing risks missing the 31-day maximum. ASSUMPTION: monthly invoicing aligned with the 31-day requirement is the practical baseline, adjusted based on project cash flow needs and County PM agreement.

### T-3: Meeting documentation completeness vs. meeting duration
Detailed minutes are evidence but also take time to produce and review. A standing template structure for minutes (recurring sections for safety, schedule, RFIs, financial, action items) balances completeness with efficiency. TBD — format to be agreed with County project manager.
