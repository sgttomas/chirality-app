# Procedure — DEL-019-03: Subcontractor Management

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and operate the Subcontractor Management deliverable for Project AB-2026-01424-WDMLRL-2026-Claude. It covers: establishing the management framework before construction commences; engaging and onboarding Subcontractors; monitoring and administering Subcontractors during construction; and closing out Subcontractor obligations at project completion.

The procedure is the Design-Builder's operational guide for fulfilling SOW-0088 (Manage all subcontractors) and satisfying the requirements in DEL-019-03 Specification, OBJ-002, and OBJ-007.

## Steps

### Step 1 — Establish Subcontractor Management Framework (Pre-Construction)

**Timing:** Before any Subcontractor is engaged or invited to bid. Target: within two weeks of contract award (see Guidance C-09). This is a critical-path early activity given the December 31, 2026 hard deadline.

**Actions:**

1.1 Draft the Subcontractor Qualification Criteria document (REQ-012). At minimum, criteria categories shall address:
- Safety record and OH&S compliance history
- WCB account standing in Alberta
- Insurance coverage capability (confirm ability to provide certificates)
- Relevant trade experience and references
- Performance history on previous Camrose County projects (see RFP §5.1.2, Item D)
- Financial capacity to perform the subcontract
- Specific threshold values and scoring methodology within each category: TBD (see GAP-001 in Guidance Conflict Table)

If a tiered qualification approach is adopted (see Guidance T-01), define the tier criteria: which trades are Tier 1 (full assessment) and which are Tier 2 (streamlined assessment).

1.2 Prepare the Subcontractor Register template capturing: Subcontractor name, trade, contact, qualification status (approved/conditional/not approved), qualification tier (if applicable), insurance certificate expiry, WCB account, contract value, payment status, safety metrics, performance ratings, schedule performance, open deficiencies, and non-compliance notices.

1.3 Establish the Performance Monitoring Framework (REQ-013), defining:
- Performance metrics: at minimum (a) financial performance, (b) safety performance, (c) schedule performance, (d) quality performance (coordinated with DEL-019-04)
- Monitoring frequency: at minimum monthly (aligned with payment cycle per RFP §2.13.2); more frequent for high-risk trades if warranted
- Performance thresholds and escalation triggers: TBD — define what score or condition triggers Step 6 escalation. At minimum, any safety incident or WCB non-compliance shall be an immediate escalation trigger.
- Performance rating scale: TBD (see Guidance C-11)

1.4 Prepare the Compliance Checklist (REQ-014) for OH&S and contractual compliance verification, with defined pass/fail criteria for each item. At minimum, compliance items shall include:
- Current OH&S compliance (alignment with Prime Contractor OH&S program)
- Valid and current insurance certificates
- WCB account in good standing
- Contractual obligations current
- Content and pass/fail thresholds: TBD (see GAP-003 in Guidance Conflict Table)

1.5 Prepare Subcontractor contract template (or review and adapt standard templates). Determine whether performance bonuses or penalties will be included: TBD (see GAP-005 in Guidance Conflict Table). Ensure back-to-back deficiency rectification obligations consistent with RFP §2.10.6 (10-day rectification period). Review CCDC 14-2013 provisions for subcontractor engagement, change orders, and non-compliance/termination once contract text is available post-award (see Guidance C-08).

1.6 Confirm County project manager identity for coordination routing once available at project award (RFP §3.1; SOW-0303).

1.7 If delegating day-to-day subcontractor oversight (see Guidance C-10, T-03), formally document the delegation and identify delegated personnel in the project organizational chart.

**Output:** Completed Subcontractor Qualification Criteria document (approved by Project Manager); Subcontractor Register (template); Performance Monitoring Framework (approved by Project Manager); Compliance Checklist (draft); Subcontractor contract template; delegation documentation (if applicable).

---

### Step 2 — Subcontractor Pre-Qualification and Approval (Pre-Construction / Ongoing)

**Timing:** For each Subcontractor before engagement. Repeat as new Subcontractors are added.

**Actions:**

2.1 Identify required Subcontractor trades and services for the project. Cross-reference with the project scope packages (PKG-010 through PKG-018 cover construction trades that may require Subcontractors — see Datasheet, Related Construction Packages). ASSUMPTION: the full list of required trades will be determined from the project schedule and scope breakdown.

2.2 Issue qualification enquiries or RFQ/RFP documents to candidate Subcontractors.

2.3 Assess each candidate against the Qualification Criteria established in Step 1.1, applying the appropriate tier (if tiered approach adopted).

2.4 Verify insurance capability: confirm each Subcontractor can provide certificates of insurance meeting project requirements (RFP §4.2) and minimum coverage requirements established in REQ-015.

2.5 Verify WCB account standing in Alberta.

2.6 Check performance records on previous Camrose County projects if applicable (RFP §5.1.2, Item D).

2.7 Record qualification outcome in the Subcontractor Register using the defined categories: **approved** (meets all criteria), **conditional** (meets criteria with identified conditions that must be satisfied before or during mobilisation), or **not approved** (does not meet minimum criteria). Document the justification for each determination.

2.8 If a Subcontractor does not meet all qualification criteria but engagement is considered necessary, obtain documented Project Manager approval with stated justification before proceeding. Record the approval and conditions in the Subcontractor Register.

**Output:** Completed qualification assessments for each Subcontractor; Subcontractor Register updated with qualification status and justification.

---

### Step 3 — Subcontractor Onboarding (Before Each Subcontractor Mobilises)

**Timing:** For each Subcontractor, before first day on site. Prerequisite: Design-Builder's own insurance verified (see Prerequisites — Design-Builder Insurance Verification).

**Actions:**

3.1 Confirm Subcontractor contract is fully executed before mobilisation.

3.2 Collect and verify Certificates of Insurance from the Subcontractor (confirming coverage types and limits per REQ-015). Verify the Design-Builder's own Certificates of Insurance confirm coverage for independent Subcontractors (min. $5,000,000 general liability per RFP §4.2.1.2). File all certificates in the Subcontractor Register.

3.3 Confirm current WCB account status. Record in Subcontractor Register.

3.4 Conduct or arrange OH&S site induction:
- Site rules and emergency procedures
- Prime Contractor OH&S requirements (from DEL-019-01)
- Site-specific hazard identification and controls for the Subcontractor's trade
- Reporting obligations (incidents, near misses, hazards)
- Stop-work authority procedures
- Coordination requirements for the specific trade

3.5 Issue site access credentials or documentation as applicable.

3.6 Record onboarding completion date in the Subcontractor Register.

**Output:** Executed Subcontractor contracts; filed Certificates of Insurance (both Subcontractor's own and Design-Builder's); WCB records; completed OH&S induction records; updated Subcontractor Register.

---

### Step 4 — Ongoing Coordination and Administration (During Construction)

**Timing:** Continuous throughout the construction phase.

**Actions:**

4.1 Coordinate Subcontractor activities with the overall project schedule. Confirm sequencing and scheduling requirements with each Subcontractor. Subcontractor-specific coordination meeting frequency: TBD (see GAP-004 in Guidance Conflict Table). Note: this is distinct from the weekly County coordination meetings covered by DEL-019-02.

4.2 Route all Owner-directed communications regarding Subcontractor activities through the County project manager (RFP §3.1).

4.3 Escalate Subcontractor issues requiring Owner awareness to the County project manager through the weekly coordination meeting forum (DEL-019-02) or earlier if urgent.

4.4 Process Subcontractor payment applications in compliance with the Alberta Prompt Payment and Construction Lien Act. Confirm payment milestones, the 31-day invoice cycle, 28-day payment timeline, and 10% lien holdback obligations (RFP §2.13.2).

4.5 Update the Subcontractor Register with current payment status, insurance certificate expiry tracking, and WCB status at each payment cycle.

4.6 Renew or update Certificates of Insurance before expiry. Do not permit Subcontractors with lapsed insurance to remain on site. Flag certificates expiring within 30 days.

4.7 Manage Subcontractor scope changes and change orders:
- Subcontractor-initiated scope change requests shall be reviewed by the Project Manager and assessed for impact on the prime contract scope, schedule, and cost.
- Changes affecting the prime contract shall be processed in accordance with the CCDC 14-2013 change order provisions (specific clause references TBD — see Guidance C-08).
- All scope changes shall be documented as amendments to the subcontract agreement.
- Record all scope changes in the Subcontractor Register.

Source: Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md C-003.

**Output:** Subcontractor Register (continuously updated); payment records; correspondence records; change order documentation.

---

### Step 5 — Performance Monitoring and Compliance Verification (During Construction)

**Timing:** Periodic throughout construction. At minimum monthly (aligned with payment cycle per Specification REQ-013). More frequent monitoring may be warranted for high-risk trades.

**Actions:**

5.1 Conduct periodic performance assessments for each active Subcontractor against the Performance Monitoring Framework established in Step 1.3. Apply the established performance rating scale (see Guidance C-11). Record results in the Subcontractor Register.

5.2 At minimum, assess:
- Financial performance: payment to sub-sub-contractors and suppliers on time; contract compliance; change order management
- Safety performance: safety incidents, near misses, compliance with Prime Contractor OH&S program
- Schedule performance: progress against agreed milestones
- Quality performance: coordinate with QC Manager (DEL-019-04) for quality feedback; incorporate QC non-conformance records into performance assessment (Source: _DEPENDENCIES.md)

5.3 Complete the Compliance Checklist (Step 1.4) for OH&S and contractual compliance at the frequency defined in the framework. Record pass/fail results for each compliance item.

5.4 If a Subcontractor is not meeting performance thresholds or compliance requirements (as defined in the Performance Monitoring Framework and Compliance Checklist), initiate the escalation process (Step 6).

**Output:** Completed performance assessment records; completed compliance checklists with pass/fail results; updated Subcontractor Register.

---

### Step 5A — Periodic Regulatory Compliance Audit (During Construction)

**Timing:** ASSUMPTION: At least quarterly, or more frequently if warranted by non-compliance incidents. This step supplements the ongoing performance monitoring (Step 5) with a focused audit of regulatory compliance matters.

**Actions:**

5A.1 Conduct a formal audit of each active Subcontractor's regulatory compliance status:
- OH&S compliance: verify alignment with Prime Contractor OH&S program (DEL-019-01); verify corrective actions from any prior OH&S incidents have been closed
- Insurance currency: verify all certificates of insurance are current and meet coverage requirements
- WCB account standing: confirm active status with no outstanding assessments
- Prompt Payment Act compliance: verify no overdue payments to sub-sub-contractors or suppliers

5A.2 Record audit findings in the Subcontractor Register.

5A.3 If non-compliance is identified, initiate corrective action per Step 6.

Source: Added per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-005. ASSUMPTION: A formal audit cycle distinct from routine performance monitoring is standard practice for Prime Contractor regulatory compliance management on construction projects. The RFP does not specify an audit frequency; quarterly is a reasonable default.

**Output:** Completed regulatory compliance audit records; updated Subcontractor Register; corrective action notices if required.

---

### Step 6 — Non-Compliance and Performance Escalation (As Required)

**Timing:** Triggered by performance or compliance failures identified in Step 5 or Step 5A.

**Actions:**

6.1 Document the non-compliance or performance deficiency in writing, referencing the specific requirement(s) not met and the applicable Compliance Checklist item or performance threshold.

6.2 Issue a written notice to the Subcontractor specifying the deficiency and required corrective action, with a response/cure period. Cure period: TBD (see GAP-003 in Guidance Conflict Table; review CCDC 14-2013 provisions post-award per Guidance C-08).

6.3 If the deficiency creates an immediate OH&S risk, direct the Subcontractor to stop the relevant activity and implement corrective action before resuming. As Prime Contractor, the Design-Builder has authority and obligation to enforce this. (Source: RFP §2.15; OBJ-007.)

6.4 Escalate to Project Manager if not resolved within the cure period.

6.5 Record all corrective action steps and outcomes in the Subcontractor Register.

6.6 If a Subcontractor issue requires Owner awareness (e.g., schedule impact, significant safety event), report through the County project manager at the next weekly coordination meeting (DEL-019-02) or earlier if urgent.

**Output:** Written non-compliance notices; corrective action records; escalation correspondence; updated Subcontractor Register.

---

### Step 7 — Subcontractor Close-Out (At or Near Project Completion)

**Timing:** During project closeout phase, in advance of final payment claim.

**Actions:**

7.1 Confirm all Subcontractor work is substantially complete and that all deficiencies identified by QC (DEL-019-04) have been addressed.

7.2 For each Subcontractor, obtain written confirmation that all sub-sub-contractors and material suppliers have been paid in full.

7.3 Obtain a current WCB Letter of Clearance from the WCB, confirming no outstanding claims related to the project from Subcontractors or labourers. File with closeout documentation.

Source: RFP §2.13.2.3.

7.4 Prepare a Statutory Declaration confirming no outstanding claims for payment from suppliers, Subcontractors, labourers, or any other parties affected by the Work.

Source: RFP §2.13.2.3.

7.5 Confirm that all Certificates of Insurance remain in force through the close-out period and through the Guarantee Period as required.

7.6 Communicate remaining Guarantee Period obligations to Subcontractors: any defects in their work reported during the two-year Guarantee Period post-CCC must be rectified within 10 days of Design-Builder's instruction (consistent with RFP §2.10.6 requirement on the Design-Builder). The guarantee period includes the construction period and two (2) years warranty following the CCC date (RFP §2.10.2).

7.7 Archive the complete Subcontractor Register and all supporting documentation.

7.8 Resolve any outstanding payment disputes or lien-related matters. Document resolution in the Subcontractor Dispute and Lien Records (see Specification Documentation item 13).

7.9 Conduct a lessons-learned review of subcontractor management effectiveness (see Guidance C-12). Document findings for future project application.

**Output:** WCB Letter of Clearance; Statutory Declaration; final Subcontractor payment confirmations; archived Subcontractor Register and records; dispute resolution records (if any); lessons-learned document.
