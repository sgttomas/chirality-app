# Specification — DEL-019-03: Subcontractor Management

---

## Scope

### What This Deliverable Covers

DEL-019-03 covers all activities and documentation required to manage Subcontractors engaged by the Design-Builder (Proponent / Prime Contractor) for the 2026 Design Build of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition (Project AB-2026-01424-WDMLRL-2026-Claude). This includes:

- Subcontractor qualification and approval prior to engagement
- Onboarding and orientation of Subcontractors to site requirements and OH&S obligations
- Direction, coordination, and administrative management of Subcontractors throughout the construction phase
- Performance monitoring and compliance verification for Subcontractors
- Subcontractor payment administration in compliance with contract terms
- Coordination with the County project manager as required
- Management of Subcontractor scope changes and change orders

Source: _CONTEXT.md (Scope Definition); Decomposition §7 (DEL-019-03 row); RFP §1.0.

### What This Deliverable Excludes

- Permitting and inspection activities (covered by PKG-009 — DEL-009-04) (Source: Decomposition §6, PKG-019 Exclusions)
- Commissioning and closeout activities (covered by PKG-020) (Source: Decomposition §6, PKG-019 Exclusions)
- Quality control management (covered by DEL-019-04) (Source: Decomposition §7; _DEPENDENCIES.md)
- Prime Contractor OH&S Program documentation (covered by DEL-019-01) (Source: Decomposition §7)
- Weekly meeting and billing coordination (covered by DEL-019-02) (Source: Decomposition §7)
- County-supplied labour, materials, or equipment (brushing/clearing, bulk cut and fill, aggregate supply — all County responsibility) (Source: Decomposition §4 Scope Boundaries, SOW-0200 through SOW-0203; RFP §3.3.1)
- The Design-Builder's own insurance procurement (covered by DEL-021-03) (Source: Decomposition §7), though verification that the Design-Builder's insurance covers independent Subcontractors is within scope (REQ-003)

## Requirements

### REQ-001: Subcontractor Definition Compliance
All parties engaged by the Design-Builder to perform any portion of the Work shall be treated as Subcontractors as defined in RFP §1.1: "any person, firm, or corporation hired by the successful Proponent."

Source: RFP §1.1.

### REQ-002: Prime Contractor Responsibility for Subcontractors
The Design-Builder, as Prime Contractor under Alberta OH&S legislation, shall retain full responsibility for all Subcontractors on site for the full project duration and whenever Proponent personnel, equipment, or Subcontractors are on site.

Source: RFP §2.15.

### REQ-003: Insurance Coverage for Subcontractors
All insurance policies maintained by the Design-Builder shall include coverage for independent Subcontractors. Minimum general liability coverage: $5,000,000 inclusive, including coverage of independent Subcontractors (RFP §4.2.1.2). Employer's liability insurance shall cover any Subcontractor engaged by the Proponent at not less than $5,000,000 per employee per accident (RFP §4.2.4).

Source: RFP §4.2.1.2, §4.2.4.

### REQ-004: Subcontractor Payment
The Design-Builder shall make timely payments to Subcontractors for materials, labour, and other obligations. Failure to do so is grounds for the Owner to withhold payment from the Design-Builder. Invoices shall be submitted at least every thirty-one (31) days, and payment shall be made within twenty-eight (28) days of receiving a proper invoice. A ten percent (10%) lien holdback applies in accordance with the Prompt Payment and Construction Lien Act.

Source: RFP §2.13.1.4, §2.13.2.

### REQ-005: WCB Clearance at Closeout
At project close, the Design-Builder shall provide a Letter of Clearance from the Workers' Compensation Board (WCB), confirming no outstanding WCB claims from Subcontractors or labourers related to the project.

Source: RFP §2.13.2.3.

### REQ-006: Statutory Declaration — No Outstanding Subcontractor Claims
At project close, the Design-Builder shall provide a Statutory Declaration confirming there are no outstanding claims for payment from suppliers, Subcontractors, labourers, or any other parties affected by the Work.

Source: RFP §2.13.2.3.

### REQ-007: Deficiency Rectification — Subcontractor Work
During the two-year Guarantee Period following CCC, deficiencies or defects in work performed by Subcontractors remain the Design-Builder's responsibility. Rectification shall be completed within ten (10) days of Owner's written instruction.

Source: RFP §2.10.6, §2.11.

### REQ-008: Safety Management of Subcontractors
The Design-Builder's Health and Safety Program shall explicitly address safety management of Subcontractors and third parties on the jobsite. This is a mandatory evaluation criterion (10% weighting, Criterion C). At minimum, the OH&S program content addressing Subcontractors shall include: site-specific hazard identification and controls for each Subcontractor trade; Subcontractor OH&S orientation requirements; incident and near-miss reporting obligations for Subcontractors; corrective action and stop-work authority procedures applicable to Subcontractors. ASSUMPTION: The specific minimum OH&S program content requirements for Subcontractor coverage are derived from the evaluation criterion description and standard Prime Contractor practice under Alberta OH&S; the RFP does not enumerate specific program sections.

Source: RFP §5.1.2, Item C; RFP §2.15; OBJ-007 (Decomposition §5). Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md F-001.

### REQ-009: Subcontractor Performance Records — County History
Subcontractors' past performance records on previous Camrose County projects are an evaluation consideration. The Design-Builder shall be aware that negative performance records of Subcontractors on County projects will affect proposal scoring.

Source: RFP §5.1.2, Item D.

### REQ-010: Manage All Subcontractors (SOW-0088)
The Design-Builder shall manage all Subcontractors as required by SOW-0088. This obligation applies throughout the full construction phase, from mobilisation through substantial performance.

Source: Decomposition §3 SSOW, SOW-0088; RFP §1.0.

### REQ-011: Coordination Through County Project Manager
All coordination with the Owner shall be routed through the County project manager, whose identity is confirmed at project award (TBD per SOW-0303).

Source: RFP §3.1; Decomposition §4 Scope Boundaries, SOW-0303.

### REQ-012: Subcontractor Qualification Criteria
Subcontractors shall be qualified and approved against objective criteria before engagement. The Design-Builder shall establish and obtain Project Manager approval of a Subcontractor Qualification Criteria document before issuing any subcontract solicitations. At minimum, qualification criteria categories shall address:
- Safety record and OH&S compliance history
- WCB account standing in Alberta
- Insurance coverage capability (confirm ability to provide certificates meeting project requirements per RFP §4.2)
- Relevant trade experience and references
- Performance history on previous Camrose County projects (RFP §5.1.2, Item D)
- Financial capacity to perform the subcontract

The specific threshold values and scoring methodology within each category are TBD — to be established by the Design-Builder and approved by the Project Manager. (See Guidance GAP-001.)

Qualification decisions shall be recorded in the Subcontractor Register as one of three categories: **approved** (meets all criteria), **conditional** (meets criteria with identified conditions that must be satisfied before or during mobilisation), or **not approved** (does not meet minimum criteria). Engagement of any Subcontractor not meeting all qualification criteria requires documented Project Manager approval with stated justification.

Source: _CONTEXT.md (Success Criteria — "All subcontractors qualified and approved per project requirements"); RFP §5.1.2 Item D. Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-001, C-001, D-001.

### REQ-013: Performance Monitoring
Subcontractor performance shall be monitored against established standards throughout the construction phase. The Design-Builder shall establish a Performance Monitoring Framework defining:
- Performance metrics covering at minimum: (a) financial performance (payment to sub-sub-contractors and suppliers, contract compliance, change order management); (b) safety performance (incidents, near misses, compliance with Prime Contractor OH&S program); (c) schedule performance (progress against agreed milestones); (d) quality performance (coordinated with DEL-019-04 QC Management)
- Monitoring frequency: TBD — at minimum, performance assessments shall be conducted no less frequently than once per payment cycle (i.e., at least monthly, consistent with the 31-day invoice cycle per RFP §2.13.2). ASSUMPTION: Monthly assessment aligns with the payment cycle and provides a reasonable minimum cadence; more frequent assessment may be warranted for high-risk trades.
- Performance thresholds and escalation triggers: TBD — the Design-Builder shall define what score or condition triggers the escalation process (Procedure Step 6). At minimum, any safety incident or WCB non-compliance shall be an immediate escalation trigger.

Source: _CONTEXT.md (Success Criteria); _DEPENDENCIES.md (DEL-019-04 integration). Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-002, A-007, F-002.

### REQ-014: Compliance Verification
The Design-Builder shall maintain a compliance verification process confirming Subcontractors meet OH&S and contractual requirements. A Compliance Checklist shall be established defining specific pass/fail criteria for each compliance item. At minimum, compliance items shall include:
- Current OH&S compliance (alignment with Prime Contractor OH&S program per DEL-019-01)
- Valid and current insurance certificates (not expired; meeting coverage minimums)
- WCB account in good standing
- Contractual obligations current (no unresolved non-compliance notices)

The specific compliance checklist content and pass/fail thresholds for each item are TBD — to be established by the Design-Builder. (See Guidance GAP-003.)

Source: _CONTEXT.md (Scope Definition); OBJ-007 (Decomposition §5); RFP §2.15. Enriched per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md A-003, A-004.

### REQ-015: Subcontractor Insurance Verification
ASSUMPTION: In addition to the Design-Builder's own insurance covering independent Subcontractors (REQ-003), the Design-Builder should verify that individual Subcontractors carry their own insurance coverage appropriate to the scope and risk of their work. Minimum coverage types and limits required from individual Subcontractors are TBD — to be determined by the Design-Builder in consultation with their insurance advisor. At minimum, evidence of Workers' Compensation coverage (RFP §4.2.2) and general liability coverage should be obtained and verified before mobilisation.

Source: RFP §4.2; Guidance C-03. Added per _SEMANTIC_LENSING.md X-002.

## Documentation

Anticipated artifacts for this deliverable (Source: _CONTEXT.md Scope Definition; Specification Requirements):

1. **Subcontractor Register** — Comprehensive register of all engaged Subcontractors with qualification status, approval records, insurance certificate expiry, WCB account status, contract value, payment status, safety metrics, performance ratings, and open deficiencies
2. **Subcontractor Qualification Criteria** — Approved document defining qualification categories, threshold values, and scoring methodology (REQ-012)
3. **Subcontractor Qualification Records** — Evidence of qualification assessment for each Subcontractor with approved/conditional/not-approved determination and justification
4. **Subcontractor Onboarding Records** — OH&S orientation and site induction documentation for each Subcontractor
5. **Performance Monitoring Framework** — Approved document defining performance metrics, monitoring frequency, thresholds, and escalation triggers (REQ-013)
6. **Performance Monitoring Records** — Periodic performance assessments against established standards
7. **Compliance Checklist** — Approved checklist with pass/fail criteria for OH&S and contractual compliance (REQ-014)
8. **Compliance Verification Records** — Completed compliance checklists at defined frequency
9. **Subcontractor Payment Records** — Payment tracking and accounts payable documentation
10. **WCB Clearance Letter** — Obtained at project close (RFP §2.13.2.3)
11. **Statutory Declaration** — No outstanding claims; obtained at project close (RFP §2.13.2.3)
12. **Certificates of Insurance** — Design-Builder's own (confirming subcontractor coverage per REQ-003) and individual Subcontractor certificates (per REQ-015)
13. **Subcontractor Dispute and Lien Records** — Documentation of any payment disputes, lien filings, or lien-related correspondence (if applicable)
