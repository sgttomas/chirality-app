# Specification — DEL-019-01: Prime Contractor Designation & OH&S Program

---

## Scope

### What this deliverable covers

DEL-019-01 governs the formal assumption of Prime Contractor status by the successful Proponent under Alberta occupational health and safety legislation, and the establishment and maintenance of a comprehensive Occupational Health and Safety (OH&S) program for the full duration of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project (AB-2026-01424-WDMLRL-2026-Claude).

Scope includes:
- Execution of the Prime Contractor Designation Form (issued by Camrose County).
- Development, implementation, and maintenance of a project OH&S program.
- Completion and submission of the Contractor Health and Safety Pre-Qualification Evaluation (Appendix A) at proposal stage.
- Maintenance of the designation and associated obligations for the full project duration, including all periods when the Proponent's personnel, equipment, or subcontractors are on site.
- Integration of subcontractor safety management within the OH&S program.

Sources: RFP §2.15; Appendix A; Decomposition SOW-0083, OBJ-007.

### What this deliverable excludes

- Weekly coordination meetings and billing coordination (covered by DEL-019-02).
- Subcontractor procurement and management activities beyond OH&S compliance oversight (covered by DEL-019-03).
- Construction quality control management (covered by DEL-019-04).
- Inspection request submission and inspection report provision (covered by DEL-009-04).
- Permitting activities (covered by PKG-009).
- Commissioning and closeout (covered by PKG-020).

Source: Decomposition §6 (package exclusions); `_DEPENDENCIES.md`.

## Requirements

### REQ-019-01-001 — Prime Contractor Designation Form
The Proponent shall complete the Prime Contractor Designation Form issued by Camrose County upon contract award, prior to commencing any site activities.

Source: RFP §2.15 ("The successful Proponent will be required to complete a Prime Contractor Designation form issued by the County.").

### REQ-019-01-002 — Assumption of Prime Contractor Obligations
The Proponent shall fulfill all obligations and responsibilities associated with the Prime Contractor designation as defined by Alberta Health and Safety agencies for the full project duration and at all times when the Proponent's personnel, equipment, or subcontractors are on site.

Source: RFP §2.15 ("shall fulfill all obligations and responsibilities associated with this designation").

### REQ-019-01-003 — Health and Safety Pre-Qualification
The Proponent shall submit a completed Contractor Health and Safety Pre-Qualification Evaluation form (Appendix A) with all supporting documentation as part of the proposal submission.

Supporting documentation required:
1. Certificate of Recognition (COR, SECOR, or TLC) — preferred.
2. Current WCB Premium Rate Statement.
3. WCB Clearance Letter issued within 30 days.
4. Hazard Assessments appropriate for the project.
5. Safe Work Procedures appropriate for the project.
6. Insurance Certificate(s) — see REQ-019-01-006.

Source: RFP §4.9; Appendix A.

### REQ-019-01-004 — Disclosure of OHS Incidents and Orders
The Proponent shall declare on the Health and Safety Pre-Qualification Evaluation form whether, within the last 3 years, the company has had:
(a) any fatalities;
(b) any OHS orders issued against the company;
(c) any Alberta Environmental Orders issued.

Where any answer is affirmative, the Proponent shall submit an action plan.

Source: Appendix A — Contractor Health and Safety Pre-Qualification Evaluation.

### REQ-019-01-005 — Documents Required Prior to Commencing Work
The Proponent shall provide the following documents before commencing any site work:
- Emergency Response Plan (project-specific).
- Health, Safety and Environmental Manual.
- Safety/Training Certificates for on-site employees (e.g., First Aid, WHMIS, trades), if requested.

Source: Appendix A — H&S Pre-Qual ("OTHER DOCUMENTS THAT MAY BE REQUIRED PRIOR TO COMMENCING WORK").

### REQ-019-01-006 — Insurance Requirements
The Proponent shall maintain and provide evidence of the following insurance coverages as a condition of contract:

| Coverage | Minimum Limit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Commercial General Liability | $5,000,000 inclusive | Camrose County to be named as additional insured; includes non-owned automobiles, independent subcontractors, contractual liability, broad form property damage, environmental liability, product and completed operations coverage |
| Automobile (owned and hired) | $5,000,000 inclusive | |
| Workers' Compensation | Per Alberta law | |
| Professional Liability (E&O) | $5,000,000 | |
| Employer's Liability | $5,000,000 per employee | |

All policies shall name Camrose County as additional insured (except E&O) and include a 30-day cancellation notice to the County.

Source: RFP §4.2.1–4.2.6; Appendix A — H&S Pre-Qual (item 6).

### REQ-019-01-007 — OH&S Program — Subcontractor Management
The OH&S program shall address the safety management of subcontractors and other third parties on the jobsite.

Source: RFP §5.1.2, Item C ("safety management of subcontractors and other 3rd parties on jobsites").

### REQ-019-01-008 — OH&S Performance Target
The Proponent shall maintain safe site operations with a target of zero lost-time incidents for the full project duration.

Source: Decomposition OBJ-007 ("zero lost-time incidents").

### REQ-019-01-009 — OH&S Program — Hazard Assessments and Safe Work Procedures
The Proponent shall maintain current, site- and task-appropriate hazard assessments and safe work procedures throughout the project duration.

Source: Appendix A — H&S Pre-Qual (items 4 and 5); ASSUMPTION (standard Alberta Prime Contractor obligation under Occupational Health and Safety Act, RSA 2000, c. O-2, location TBD — specific statute text not accessible in available sources).

### REQ-019-01-010 — Proposal Safety Screening
The Proponent shall demonstrate reasonable qualifications to assume Prime Contractor status for a project of this size and scope to pass initial proposal screening.

Source: RFP §5.1.1.

### REQ-019-01-011 — OH&S Program Document Minimum Content [F-001]
The OH&S Program Document shall, at minimum, address all of the following elements:
1. Site hazard identification and assessment specific to the project site.
2. Safe work procedures for all significant site activities.
3. Subcontractor safety onboarding, monitoring, and reporting requirements.
4. Incident reporting and investigation procedures.
5. Emergency response procedures.
6. OH&S roles, responsibilities, and escalation paths.
7. OH&S program review frequency.

**ASSUMPTION:** This requirement is derived from the minimum content list in Procedure Step 2.3 and from standard Alberta Prime Contractor obligations (statute text not directly accessible; location TBD). Elevated from operational guidance to normative requirement to close the gap between Specification (which lacked a structural requirement for the overall OH&S Program Document) and Procedure (which specified minimum content as operational steps).

Source: Procedure Step 2.3; Appendix A (pre-work document requirements); RFP §5.1.2 Item C; ASSUMPTION (Alberta OHS Act, RSA 2000, c. O-2 — location TBD).
