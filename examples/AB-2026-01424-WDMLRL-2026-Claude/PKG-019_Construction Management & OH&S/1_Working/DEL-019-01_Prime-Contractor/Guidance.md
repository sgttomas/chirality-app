# Guidance — DEL-019-01: Prime Contractor Designation & OH&S Program

---

## Purpose

The Prime Contractor designation and associated OH&S program exist to establish clear, singular accountability for site safety for the full duration of the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project. Alberta occupational health and safety legislation (ASSUMPTION: Alberta OHS Act, location TBD) requires that one party be formally designated as Prime Contractor whenever multiple employers share a worksite. By designating the successful Proponent as Prime Contractor, Camrose County creates an unambiguous chain of command for safety obligations, simplifies County oversight, and protects the County from diffuse liability across multiple trades.

DEL-019-01 is the foundational governance instrument for all subsequent PKG-019 deliverables. Without a confirmed and documented Prime Contractor designation, the downstream management deliverables (DEL-019-02 through DEL-019-04) lack an authoritative structure within which to operate.

Sources: RFP §2.15; Decomposition OBJ-007, PKG-019 description; `_CONTEXT.md` (Success Criteria).

## Principles

### P1 — Single point of accountability
The Prime Contractor designation creates a single entity responsible for site safety. All subcontractors and third parties on site work under the safety authority of the Prime Contractor. Fragmented or shared safety accountability is incompatible with the Prime Contractor model and with the RFP's requirement that the Proponent manage subcontractor safety.

Source: RFP §2.15; §5.1.2 Item C.

### P2 — Designation precedes site mobilization
The Prime Contractor Designation Form must be completed before any site activities commence. The OH&S program and all pre-work documents (Emergency Response Plan, HSE Manual) must likewise be in place before mobilization. Safety structures established after work begins create unacceptable exposure.

Source: RFP §2.15; Appendix A (pre-work documents requirement).

### P3 — Proactive, documented safety management
The OH&S program is not a filing exercise. It must be a living operational system: hazard assessments updated as site conditions change, safe work procedures actively used, and subcontractor safety actively monitored. The RFP's evaluation criteria favour contractors with "more detailed/diligent safety programs."

Source: RFP §5.1.2 Item C.

### P4 — Continuity for project duration
The Prime Contractor obligation persists for the full project duration and is not limited to periods of peak activity. Any gap in coverage — for example, when subcontractors are on site without the Proponent's direct personnel — remains the Proponent's responsibility.

Source: RFP §2.15 ("full duration of this project and whenever the Proponent's personnel, equipment or Subcontractors are on site").

### P5 — Subcontractor integration
The OH&S program must actively encompass subcontractors. A Prime Contractor that relies solely on subcontractors' own safety programs without integrating them into a unified site safety system does not fulfill the Prime Contractor role as evaluated by the County.

Source: RFP §5.1.2 Item C.

## Considerations

### Proposal-stage safety screening
The County conducts an initial screening of all proposals for reasonable qualifications to assume Prime Contractor status (RFP §5.1.1) before proceeding to the scored evaluation. Health and Safety Program is then separately scored at 10% weight (RFP §5.1.2 Item C). This means safety documentation directly affects both disqualification risk and competitive score.

### Alberta OH&S legislative framework
The Prime Contractor designation is a legal concept under Alberta OHS legislation (ASSUMPTION: Alberta Occupational Health and Safety Act, RSA 2000, c. O-2 — specific clause citations TBD as statute text not directly accessible in provided sources). The obligations attached to this designation are set in statute and regulation, not solely in the RFP. The Project Manager and Safety Officer should confirm the current legislative requirements with legal counsel or an Alberta-certified safety professional.

### Certificate of Recognition (COR/SECOR)
Holding a COR, SECOR, or TLC certificate (items listed as supporting documentation in Appendix A) signals a mature, externally audited safety management system. While listed as supporting documentation rather than a mandatory requirement, proponents with such certifications are likely to be viewed more favourably in the safety evaluation. ASSUMPTION: COR/SECOR is not a stated pass/fail criterion in the RFP, but its absence may affect competitive scoring.

### WCB Clearance Letter timing
The WCB Clearance Letter must be issued within 30 days of submission (Appendix A). This creates a scheduling constraint: the letter must be obtained close to the proposal submission date, not months in advance.

### Landfill site context
The project is located at an active regional landfill facility (West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill). ASSUMPTION: this introduces site-specific hazards — potentially including waste gas exposure, heavy equipment interaction with landfill operations, and access/egress controls — that should be reflected in project-specific hazard assessments and the Emergency Response Plan. The specific operational context of the landfill is not further detailed in available sources; the Safety Officer should conduct a site-specific hazard review at the earliest opportunity.

### Leading safety indicators and OH&S effectiveness metrics [D-003]
The current document set defines one lagging indicator for OH&S performance: the zero lost-time incident target (REQ-019-01-008, OBJ-007). **ASSUMPTION:** A proactive safety management program (per Principle P3) should also track leading indicators to drive early intervention and demonstrate program effectiveness. Recommended leading indicators for consideration:
- Toolbox talks / safety briefings conducted (frequency and attendance).
- Near-miss reporting rate.
- Safety observation frequency (positive observations and corrections).
- Subcontractor safety orientation completion rate.
- Hazard assessment update currency (percentage current vs. overdue).

These are not requirements (no source mandates specific leading indicators), but their inclusion in the OH&S Program Document would support the "more detailed/diligent safety programs" evaluation criterion and provide early warning before lagging indicators materialize.

Source: RFP §5.1.2 Item C (favours detailed safety programs); Decomposition OBJ-007; Guidance P3; ASSUMPTION (industry best practice for construction safety programs).

### Non-conformance and corrective action triggers [X-004]
**TBD:** The documents describe ongoing oversight and incident tracking but do not define what specific events constitute a non-conformance requiring formal corrective action under the Prime Contractor's OH&S program. ASSUMPTION: The OH&S Program Document (REQ-019-01-011) should define escalation triggers, which may include:
- Any OHS order issued by a government inspector.
- Any lost-time incident.
- Repeated near-miss incidents of the same type.
- Failed spot check or safety audit finding.
- Subcontractor non-compliance with site safety requirements.

The absence of defined triggers creates ambiguity about when the OH&S program's corrective action process activates. This should be resolved during OH&S Program Document development (Procedure Step 2.3).

Source: Specification REQ-019-01-002, REQ-019-01-008; Procedure Step 3.3; ASSUMPTION (standard corrective action trigger categories for Alberta construction safety programs).

### Subcontractor non-compliance enforcement [E-001]
Principle P5 (Subcontractor integration) establishes that subcontractors must be integrated into the unified site safety system. However, neither the Guidance nor the Procedure addresses the enforcement mechanism when a subcontractor fails to comply with OH&S program requirements. **TBD:** The OH&S Program Document should define the consequence framework for subcontractor non-compliance, which may include:
- Verbal warning and documented corrective action.
- Stop-work authority exercised by the Safety Officer.
- Removal of non-compliant workers from site.
- Remediation requirements before work resumes.
- Reporting to the County project representative.

ASSUMPTION: The Prime Contractor's stop-work authority is implied by the Prime Contractor designation under Alberta OHS legislation, but the specific enforcement mechanism should be documented in the OH&S program and communicated to subcontractors during safety onboarding (Procedure Step 3.2).

Source: Guidance P5; Procedure Step 3.2; RFP §5.1.2 Item C; ASSUMPTION (Prime Contractor enforcement authority under Alberta OHS Act — location TBD).

### Integration with DEL-009-04 (inspection coordination)
SOW-0084 and SOW-0085 (submit inspection requests; provide copies of inspection reports to County) are assigned to DEL-009-04, not to this deliverable. However, the Prime Contractor is the party responsible for ensuring inspections are coordinated. The Project Manager should ensure clear handoff and communication between the OH&S program (DEL-019-01) and the inspection coordination function (DEL-009-04) to avoid gaps.

Source: Decomposition §8 Scope Ledger (SOW-0084, SOW-0085 assigned to PKG-009/DEL-009-04).
