# Procedure — DEL-019-01: Prime Contractor Designation & OH&S Program

---

## Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to produce and activate the Prime Contractor Designation & OH&S Program deliverable for the West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill Maintenance Shop Addition project. It covers actions from proposal preparation through to active site safety management, addressing both the establishment of the designation and the operationalization of the OH&S program.

Sources: RFP §2.15; §4.9; Appendix A; Decomposition SOW-0083, OBJ-007.

## Steps

### Phase 1 — Proposal Preparation (Pre-Award)

**Step 1.1 — Assign Safety Officer and Project Site Manager**

Identify the individual who will serve as the project Safety Officer (or Contractor's Safety Representative) and the individual who will serve as the Project Site Manager. Record names, phone numbers, and email addresses. These individuals will be named on the Appendix A H&S Pre-Qualification Evaluation form.

Responsible Party: Project Manager
Source: Appendix A.
Note: `MEMORY.md` identifies "Who will serve as primary safety officer contact?" as an open question (as of 2026-02-25). This must be resolved prior to proposal submission.

---

**Step 1.2 — Obtain WCB Clearance Letter**

Request a WCB Clearance Letter from the Workers' Compensation Board of Alberta. The letter must be dated within 30 days of the proposal submission date (April 2, 2026). ASSUMPTION: the letter should be obtained no earlier than approximately March 3, 2026 (30 days prior to submission).

Responsible Party: Safety Officer / Administration
Source: Appendix A; RFP §1.3.1 (submission deadline: April 2, 2026, 2:00 p.m. MST).

---

**Step 1.3 — Assemble H&S Pre-Qualification Package (Appendix A)**

Complete the Contractor Health and Safety Pre-Qualification Evaluation form:
- Record Company Name, Date, Address, Scope of Work.
- Enter Project Site Manager and Safety Representative contact details.
- Answer all three declaration questions (fatalities, OHS orders, Environmental Orders in last 3 years). If any answer is Yes, prepare and attach an action plan.
- Collect and attach all supporting documentation:
  1. COR/SECOR/TLC certificate (if held).
  2. Current WCB Premium Rate Statement.
  3. WCB Clearance Letter (within 30 days).
  4. Project-appropriate Hazard Assessments.
  5. Project-appropriate Safe Work Procedures.
  6. Insurance Certificate(s) meeting §4.2 requirements with Camrose County named as additional insured.

Responsible Party: Safety Officer, Project Manager
Source: Appendix A; RFP §4.9.

---

**Step 1.4 — Prepare OH&S Program Summary for Proposal**

Prepare a summary or outline of the company OH&S program for inclusion in the proposal. The RFP evaluation (§5.1.2, Item C — 10% weight) favours more detailed and diligent safety programs that include subcontractor safety management. The submission should demonstrate:
- The structure of the OH&S program.
- How subcontractor and third-party safety will be managed on site.
- Safety record and track record.

Responsible Party: Safety Officer, Project Manager
Source: RFP §5.1.2, Item C.

---

**Step 1.5 — Include Appendix A in Proposal**

Attach the completed Appendix A (H&S Pre-Qualification Evaluation with all supporting documentation) to the proposal in the Proposal Response Forms section (§4.0 of the proposal).

Responsible Party: Project Manager
Source: RFP §4.9 ("Contractor Health and Safety Pre-Qualification/Evaluation — Include all Supporting Documentation").

---

### Phase 2 — Post-Award, Pre-Mobilization

**Step 2.1 — Obtain Prime Contractor Designation Form from County**

Upon contract award, contact Camrose County to obtain the Prime Contractor Designation Form. The County issues this form; the Proponent cannot substitute an alternative form.

Responsible Party: Project Manager
Source: RFP §2.15.
County Contact: Darren King, Manager West Dried Meat Lake Regional Landfill, dking@county.camrose.ab.ca, (780) 679-6519. Source: RFP §1.3.4.

---

**Step 2.2 — Execute Prime Contractor Designation Form**

Complete and sign the Prime Contractor Designation Form. Obtain County countersignature / acknowledgment. File the executed form as a project record.

Responsible Party: Project Manager (authorized company representative)
Source: RFP §2.15.

---

**Step 2.3 — Develop Full OH&S Program Document**

Develop or adapt the company OH&S program into a project-specific OH&S Program Document. At minimum, the document must address:
- Site hazard identification and assessment specific to the landfill site context.
- Safe work procedures for all significant site activities.
- Subcontractor safety onboarding, monitoring, and reporting requirements.
- Incident reporting and investigation procedures.
- Emergency response procedures (see Step 2.4).
- OH&S roles, responsibilities, and escalation paths. (MEMORY.md open question: "What is the escalation path for OH&S issues?" — must be resolved.) **[F-002]:** TBD — ASSUMPTION: A typical escalation path for Alberta construction projects would be: Worker -> Supervisor -> Safety Officer -> Project Manager -> County Representative (Darren King, per RFP §1.3.4). The OH&S Program Document must define the specific escalation path, including contact details, authority levels, and response time expectations at each tier. See also Guidance §Non-conformance and corrective action triggers [X-004] for trigger definitions. Human ruling required.
- OH&S review frequency. (MEMORY.md open question: "How frequently will OH&S program reviews occur?" — must be resolved and documented.)

Responsible Party: Safety Officer
Source: Appendix A (pre-work document requirements); RFP §5.1.2 Item C; Decomposition OBJ-007; MEMORY.md (open questions).

---

**Step 2.4 — Develop Emergency Response Plan**

Prepare a project-specific Emergency Response Plan (ERP) for the site. ASSUMPTION: the ERP should account for the active landfill context (proximity to landfill operations, potential site-specific hazards). The ERP must be in place before any work commences.

Responsible Party: Safety Officer
Source: Appendix A ("Emergency Response Plan for the project").

---

**Step 2.5 — Confirm Pre-Work Document Package**

Confirm the following are complete and available on site prior to mobilization:
- [ ] Executed Prime Contractor Designation Form (Step 2.2).
- [ ] Project OH&S Program Document (Step 2.3).
- [ ] Emergency Response Plan (Step 2.4).
- [ ] Health, Safety and Environmental Manual.
- [ ] Project-specific Hazard Assessments.
- [ ] Safe Work Procedures for initial site activities.
- [ ] Safety/Training Certificates for initial on-site employees (if requested by County).

Responsible Party: Safety Officer, Project Manager
Source: Appendix A; RFP §2.15.

---

**Step 2.6 — Confirm Insurance Certificates on File**

Confirm that current Insurance Certificates are held, Camrose County is named as additional insured on all required policies (except E&O), and certificates are available to provide to the County on request.

Responsible Party: Project Manager / Administration
Source: RFP §4.2.

---

### Phase 3 — Active Project (Ongoing)

**Step 3.1 — Maintain and Update Hazard Assessments and Safe Work Procedures**

As site conditions, work activities, and subcontractor scope change, update hazard assessments and safe work procedures to remain current and site-appropriate.

Responsible Party: Safety Officer
Source: Appendix A; RFP §5.1.2 Item C; Decomposition OBJ-007.
Frequency: TBD — specific review intervals to be defined in OH&S Program Document (MEMORY.md open question). **[F-003]:** ASSUMPTION: Industry practice for active construction sites suggests hazard assessment reviews at minimum upon (a) significant scope changes, (b) introduction of new subcontractors or trades, (c) seasonal or weather-related condition changes, and (d) following any incident or near-miss. A fixed minimum interval (e.g., monthly) should also be established. Human ruling recommended on appropriate interval for this project.

---

**Step 3.2 — Subcontractor Safety Onboarding**

Prior to each subcontractor commencing work on site, conduct a safety orientation or onboarding session in accordance with the OH&S program. Confirm subcontractor compliance with site safety requirements.

Responsible Party: Safety Officer
Source: RFP §5.1.2 Item C; RFP §2.15 (Prime Contractor designation encompasses periods when subcontractors are on site).

---

**Step 3.3 — Incident Tracking and Reporting**

Maintain records of all safety incidents, near-misses, and OHS orders issued during the project. Investigate incidents in accordance with the OH&S program. Track lost-time incidents against the zero LTI target (OBJ-007).

Responsible Party: Safety Officer
Source: Decomposition OBJ-007.

---

**Step 3.4 — Maintain WCB Clearance and Insurance Currency**

Monitor the expiry of WCB Clearance Letters and insurance policies. Renew and provide updated documentation to Camrose County as required throughout the project.

Responsible Party: Project Manager / Administration
Source: RFP §4.2; Appendix A.

---

**Step 3.5 — Periodic OH&S Program Audit and Management Review [A-004]**

Conduct periodic internal audits and management reviews of the OH&S program to ensure it remains current, effective, and responsive to changing site conditions. The review shall assess:
- Currency of hazard assessments and safe work procedures.
- Incident and near-miss trends.
- Subcontractor safety compliance status.
- Corrective action closure rates.
- Alignment with Alberta OHS legislative obligations (ASSUMPTION: specific obligations TBD pending statute text review — see Specification Standards table).

**Frequency: TBD** — to be defined in the OH&S Program Document. ASSUMPTION: Monthly or per-construction-phase reviews are typical for projects of this scale and duration; human ruling recommended.

**Output:** Written audit/review record documenting findings, corrective actions, and any OH&S program updates.

Responsible Party: Safety Officer (audit execution); Project Manager (management review sign-off)
Source: Guidance P3 ("a living operational system"); Specification REQ-019-01-002 (ongoing Prime Contractor obligations); ASSUMPTION (standard Alberta construction safety management practice).

---

**Step 3.6 — Support Inspection Coordination (Interface with DEL-009-04)**

Coordinate with the party responsible for DEL-009-04 to ensure that inspection requests are submitted in a timely manner and that the County project representative is available for all inspections. Note: inspection request submission is assigned to DEL-009-04/PKG-009, but the Prime Contractor has overall site responsibility and must enable this function.

Responsible Party: Project Manager
Source: Decomposition SOW-0084, SOW-0085 (assigned to DEL-009-04); `_DEPENDENCIES.md` (no explicit upstream dependencies on this deliverable from inspection coordination noted, but operational interface exists).
