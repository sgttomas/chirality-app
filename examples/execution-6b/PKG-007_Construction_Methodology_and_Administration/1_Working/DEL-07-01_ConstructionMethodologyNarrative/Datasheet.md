## Conditions

The following seven topic areas are required by RFP §7.2. Each constitutes a required subsection of the narrative.

| Topic | Required Content | Source | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Health and Safety Plan | Alberta OH&S Act compliance framework; Prime Contractor responsibilities and site authority; site access controls; training plans; muster points; emergency procedures; specific site challenges | RFP §7.2, H&S bullet | RFP provides explicit detail for this topic |
| Staging of Work | Proponent-defined construction staging and sequencing approach | RFP §7.2, Staging bullet | RFP lists as header only; proponent elaborates |
| Permits, Inspections and Approvals | Approach to obtaining and managing all required permits; AHJ inspection coordination; all re-work and subsequent inspection costs | RFP §7.2, Permits bullet | RFP provides explicit detail for this topic |
| Budget Control (including Change Management) | Proponent-defined budget tracking and change management approach | RFP §7.2, Budget Control bullet | RFP lists as header only; proponent elaborates |
| Schedule Control | 20-day post-award baseline schedule submission; Project Committee acceptance; monthly updates with % completion per task; Change Orders incorporated | RFP §7.2, Schedule Control bullet | RFP provides explicit detail for this topic |
| Communications | Proponent-defined project communications approach | RFP §7.2, Communications bullet | RFP lists as header only; proponent elaborates |
| Quality Management (QA/QC) and Documentation | Work to current industry standards and best practice; NMS format recommended; Alberta-registered professional seal on all construction documents | RFP §7.2, QA/QC bullet | RFP provides explicit detail for this topic |

> **Note:** RFP §7.2 provides detailed requirements for Health and Safety, Permits/Inspections/Approvals, Schedule Control, and QA/QC. Staging of Work, Budget Control, and Communications are listed as topic headers without elaborating detail — the proponent is expected to define a project-specific approach for these three topics.
