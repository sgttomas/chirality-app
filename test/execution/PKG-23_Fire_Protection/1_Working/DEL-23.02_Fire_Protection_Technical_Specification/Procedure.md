# Procedure: DEL-23.02 Fire Protection Technical Specification

## Purpose

This procedure defines the process for **producing** the **Fire Protection Technical Specification** within **PKG-23 Fire Protection** for the Canola Oil Transload Facility — Phase 1 Design & Build project.

**Scope of This Procedure:**

This procedure covers the production workflow for developing, checking, coordinating, and issuing the fire protection technical specification document per project quality and document control requirements.

**Deliverable Information:**
- **Deliverable ID:** DEL-23.02
- **Deliverable Type:** Specification
- **Responsible Party:** D&B Contractor
- **Discipline:** Specialty (Fire Protection)
- **Source:** Decomposition DEL-23.02 and `_CONTEXT.md`

## Steps

### Step 1: Planning and Setup

**1.1 Review Scope and Requirements**
- Review deliverable scope (`_CONTEXT.md`, Specification.md, Guidance.md)
- Review Employer's Requirements for fire protection material/equipment/workmanship requirements
- Review applicable codes and standards
- **Output:** Understanding of specification scope and requirements

**1.2 Obtain Specification Template**
- Obtain project specification template or CSI MasterFormat template
- Confirm specification format requirements (3-part format, section numbering, page layout)
- **TBD:** Project specification template
- **Output:** Specification template ready for content development

**1.3 Develop Specification Outline**
- Develop table of contents and section list
- Typical sections: Fire water piping, fire hydrants, foam systems, fire pump (if applicable), fire alarm, general requirements
- Assign section numbers per CSI MasterFormat or project system
- **Output:** Specification outline and section list

### Step 2: Content Development

**2.1 Develop Fire Water Piping Specification (Section 21 10 00 or equivalent)**
- **Part 1 General:** Scope, submittals, quality assurance, coordination
- **Part 2 Products:** Pipe materials (ductile iron, steel), fittings, valves, supports, pipe markers, freeze protection
- **Part 3 Execution:** Installation procedures, pipe joining, testing (hydrostatic test per NFPA 24), commissioning
- **Basis:** DEL-23.01 drawings, DEL-23.03 calculations, NFPA 24, AWWA C151/C509, ASTM A53
- **Output:** Fire water piping specification section (draft)

**2.2 Develop Fire Hydrant Specification (Section 21 11 00 or equivalent)**
- **Part 1 General:** Scope, submittals, quality assurance, coordination with fire department
- **Part 2 Products:** Dry-barrel hydrant per AWWA C502, accessories, valve boxes
- **Part 3 Execution:** Installation procedures, hydrant setting and grading, testing (flow test per NFPA 291)
- **Basis:** DEL-23.01 drawings, DEL-23.03 calculations, DEL-23.04 hydrant data sheet, AWWA C502, NFPA 291
- **Output:** Fire hydrant specification section (draft)

**2.3 Develop Fire Alarm Specification (Section 28 30 00 or equivalent)**
- **Part 1 General:** Scope, submittals, quality assurance, coordination with I&C (PKG-19)
- **Part 2 Products:** Fire alarm control panel (ULC-S527), detection devices (ULC-S530/S536/S531), notification devices (ULC-S525), wiring (ULC-S139)
- **Part 3 Execution:** Installation procedures, wiring and conduit, testing and commissioning per NFPA 72, training
- **Basis:** DEL-23.01 drawings, DEL-23.04 fire alarm panel data sheet, NFPA 72, ULC standards
- **Output:** Fire alarm specification section (draft)

**2.4 Develop Foam Systems Specification (Custom section or 21 30 00 series)**
- **Part 1 General:** Scope, submittals, quality assurance
- **Part 2 Products:** Foam concentrate (AR-AFFF or fluoroprotein), foam proportioning system, foam discharge devices, foam piping
- **Part 3 Execution:** Installation, foam system testing per NFPA 11, training
- **Basis:** DEL-23.01 drawings, DEL-23.03 foam calculations, NFPA 11/16
- **Output:** Foam systems specification section (draft)

**2.5 Develop Fire Pump Specification (Section 21 13 13 or equivalent) — if applicable**
- **Part 1 General:** Scope, submittals, quality assurance
- **Part 2 Products:** Fire pump per NFPA 20 (horizontal split-case centrifugal), driver (electric or diesel), controller, accessories
- **Part 3 Execution:** Installation, pump room requirements, testing and commissioning per NFPA 20
- **Basis:** DEL-23.01 drawings, DEL-23.03 pump sizing, DEL-23.04 fire pump data sheet, NFPA 20
- **Output:** Fire pump specification section (draft) — if fire pump in Contractor scope

**2.6 Develop Fire Protection Specialties Specification**
- Fire department connections, test headers, valve identification and tagging, pipe markers, freeze protection accessories
- **Output:** Fire protection specialties specification section (draft)

**2.7 Develop General Requirements Section**
- Scope of fire protection work, related sections, references, submittals (shop drawings, product data, test reports), quality assurance, delivery and storage, project conditions, coordination with other disciplines
- **Output:** General requirements section (draft)

**2.8 Compile Specification Document**
- Assemble all sections into single specification document
- Add cover page, table of contents, revision tracking
- Format per project standards (page layout, headers/footers, fonts)
- **Output:** Complete fire protection technical specification (draft)

### Step 3: Self-Check and Quality Review

**3.1 Self-Check**
- Author reviews specification for completeness, accuracy, clarity, internal consistency
- Verify all anticipated artifacts covered (fire water piping, hydrants, fire alarm per Decomposition)
- Verify code compliance (NFPA, ULC, AWWA, NBCC, BCFC)
- Verify specification writing conventions ("shall" for requirements, clear language)
- **Output:** Self-checked specification, self-check notes/corrections

**3.2 Cross-Document Consistency Check**
- Cross-check specification with DEL-23.01 (drawings) — ensure drawing callouts align with specification sections
- Cross-check specification with DEL-23.03 (calculations) — ensure performance requirements match calculated values
- Cross-check specification with DEL-23.04 (data sheets) — ensure equipment specifications align
- Resolve any inconsistencies
- **Output:** Consistency-checked specification, consistency check log

### Step 4: Interdisciplinary Check (IDC)

**4.1 Issue Specification for IDC**
- Distribute specification to affected disciplines (civil, electrical, I&C, process piping, construction/estimating)
- Request review for interface coordination, constructability, completeness for pricing
- **TBD:** IDC distribution list and timeline
- **Output:** IDC transmittal

**4.2 Review and Resolve IDC Comments**
- Collect IDC comments
- Review comments and coordinate with commenting disciplines
- Incorporate valid comments into specification
- Document comment resolutions
- **Output:** Revised specification with IDC comments resolved, IDC comment log

### Step 5: Independent Check

**5.1 Issue Specification for Independent Check**
- Issue specification to qualified independent checker (P.Eng., not the author)
- Provide checker with reference materials (codes, standards, drawings, calculations)
- **TBD:** Check timeline and checker assignment
- **Output:** Independent check transmittal

**5.2 Checker Review**
- Checker reviews specification for:
  - Technical correctness and code compliance
  - Completeness for design-build pricing and construction
  - Constructability and availability of specified materials/equipment
  - Clarity and enforceability of requirements
  - Consistency with drawings, calculations, and data sheets
- **Output:** Independent check comments

**5.3 Resolve Check Comments**
- Author reviews check comments and incorporates corrections
- Coordinate with checker to resolve disagreements
- Update specification
- **Output:** Revised specification with check comments resolved

**5.4 Checker Sign-off**
- Checker verifies comments resolved satisfactorily and signs off
- **Output:** Checker sign-off (**TBD:** sign-off format per project)

### Step 6: Approval and Issue

**6.1 Estimator / Procurement Review (if required)**
- Estimator and/or procurement team reviews specification for pricing completeness and competitive procurement provisions
- Resolve estimator comments
- **TBD:** Estimator review requirement and timing
- **Output:** Estimator-reviewed specification (if applicable)

**6.2 Constructability Review (if required)**
- Construction team reviews specification for constructability
- Resolve constructability comments
- **TBD:** Constructability review requirement and timing
- **Output:** Constructability-reviewed specification (if applicable)

**6.3 Discipline Lead Approval**
- Discipline lead reviews specification for overall adequacy and consistency with project requirements
- Discipline lead approves specification for issue
- **Output:** Discipline lead sign-off (**TBD:** approval format per project)

**6.4 Employer Review (if required)**
- Submit specification to Employer for review at specified milestone (e.g., IFC) if required by project
- Address Employer comments and revise specification
- **TBD:** Employer review requirement and timing
- **Output:** Employer-reviewed specification (if applicable)

**6.5 AHJ Review (if required)**
- Submit specification to AHJ (fire marshal, building official) if required for permit
- Address AHJ comments and revise specification
- **TBD:** AHJ review requirement and timing
- **Output:** AHJ-approved specification (if applicable)

**6.6 Issue Specification**
- Finalize specification with all sign-offs and approvals
- Assign final revision number (**TBD:** per project revision system)
- Generate PDF from editable file (Word or equivalent)
- Issue specification via project document management system or per document control procedures
- **TBD:** Distribution list and issue procedures
- **Output:** Issued specification, transmittal record

**6.7 Update Deliverable Status**
- Update `_STATUS.md` to reflect deliverable lifecycle state:
  - INITIALIZED → IN_PROGRESS (when work commences)
  - IN_PROGRESS → CHECKING (when issued for formal review)
  - CHECKING → ISSUED (when formally issued)
- Copy issued specification to `3_Issued/` folder per framework conventions
- **Output:** Updated `_STATUS.md`, issued specification archived in `3_Issued/`

### Step 7: Specification Revisions

**7.1 Revise Specification (if changes required)**
- If changes required during design, procurement, or construction, revise specification following steps 2–6 above
- Increment revision number per project system
- Document revision description in revision tracking table or cover page
- **TBD:** Revision approval authority and change control process
- **Output:** Revised specification
