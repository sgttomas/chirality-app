# Specification: DEL-23.04 Fire Protection Data Sheet Package

## Scope

This specification defines the requirements for **Fire Protection Data Sheet Package** within **PKG-23 Fire Protection** for the Canola Oil Transload Facility — Phase 1 Design & Build project.

**Project Context:**
- Location: Fraser Surrey Terminal, 11060 Elevator Road, Surrey, BC
- Employer: DP World Fraser Surrey Inc.
- Contract Type: Design & Build
- Source: Decomposition document Section 1.1

**Deliverable Scope:**

This deliverable defines and substantiates fire protection equipment in accordance with Employer's Requirements (ER), including:

**Anticipated deliverable artifacts (equipment data sheets):**
- Fire hydrant data sheets (Source: Decomposition DEL-23.04 anticipated artifacts)
- Fire alarm panel data sheet (Source: Decomposition DEL-23.04 anticipated artifacts)

**Additional data sheets (typical for comprehensive fire protection equipment package):**
- Fire pump data sheet (if applicable) — **ASSUMPTION**: If fire pump in Contractor scope
- Foam proportioning system data sheets — **ASSUMPTION**: Required for combustible liquid protection
- Foam concentrate storage tank data sheet — **ASSUMPTION**: Foam system component
- Foam discharge device data sheets — **ASSUMPTION**: Tank and marine foam systems
- Fire detection device data sheets — **ASSUMPTION**: Fire alarm system components
- Fire alarm notification device data sheets — **ASSUMPTION**: Fire alarm system components
- Fire water valve data sheets (key valves) — **ASSUMPTION**: Major system valves
- Equipment tagging and identification data — **ASSUMPTION**: Equipment identification system

**Purpose of Data Sheets:**

Equipment data sheets serve to:
1. Document equipment specifications and performance requirements
2. Provide basis for equipment procurement and selection
3. Communicate equipment requirements to vendors and suppliers
4. Support interdisciplinary coordination (electrical, I&C, structural)
5. Provide equipment information for installation, testing, and commissioning
6. Create permanent equipment record for operations and maintenance
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Standard purpose of equipment data sheets

**Scope Boundaries:**

| Included | Excluded / Interface |
|----------|---------------------|
| Equipment specifications and performance requirements | Detailed vendor submittals and shop drawings (vendor responsibility per DEL-23.02 specification) |
| Equipment sizing from design calculations | Procurement activities and vendor selection (procurement team activity) |
| Equipment tagging and identification | Installation procedures (covered in DEL-23.02 specification and vendor manuals) |
| Equipment interfaces (electrical, I&C, structural) | Operations and maintenance procedures (separate O&M manual deliverable) |

**Project Objectives Supported:**

This deliverable supports **OBJ-1: Safe & Reliable Operation** — data sheets ensure fire protection equipment meets specifications and performance requirements.
- Source: Decomposition Section 2 (Project Objectives) and Section 6 (Objective-to-Deliverable Mapping)

## Requirements

### Functional Requirements

**FR-1: Comprehensive Equipment Coverage**
- Data sheet package shall include data sheets for all major fire protection equipment requiring procurement or specification
- Equipment list shall be consistent with fire protection drawings (DEL-23.01) and equipment list
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Data sheet completeness requirement
- Verification: Cross-check data sheet list with equipment list from DEL-23.01

**FR-2: Equipment Specifications**
- Each data sheet shall specify equipment type, model (if specified), capacity/performance, materials, accessories, and applicable standards
- Specifications shall be consistent with technical specification (DEL-23.02) and design calculations (DEL-23.03)
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Data sheet content requirement
- Verification: Cross-check data sheets with DEL-23.02 and DEL-23.03

**FR-3: Performance Requirements**
- Each data sheet shall state equipment performance requirements (flow, pressure, capacity, power, etc.) based on design calculations
- Performance requirements shall be verifiable through testing
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Performance documentation requirement
- Verification: Performance requirements traceable to DEL-23.03 calculations

**FR-4: Operating Conditions**
- Each data sheet shall document operating conditions (temperature, pressure, environment, hazardous area classification)
- Operating conditions shall reflect actual facility conditions
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Operating condition documentation requirement
- Verification: Operating conditions consistent with site data and design basis

**FR-5: Interface Requirements**
- Data sheets shall document equipment interfaces with other disciplines (electrical power, control system I/O, structural loads, piping connections)
- Interface data shall be coordinated with affected disciplines
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Interdisciplinary coordination requirement
- Verification: Interface data coordinated with PKG-17 (Electrical) and PKG-19 (Control Systems)

**FR-6: Equipment Tagging**
- Each data sheet shall include equipment tag number consistent with project equipment tagging system
- Equipment tags shall match tags on drawings (DEL-23.01)
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Equipment identification requirement
- Verification: Tags consistent across data sheets and drawings

**FR-7: Codes and Standards Compliance**
- Equipment specifications shall reference applicable codes and standards (NFPA, ULC, AWWA, ASTM, CSA)
- Equipment shall meet listing/approval requirements (UL, ULC, FM) where applicable
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Code compliance and approval requirement
- Verification: Codes/standards and approvals cited on data sheets

### Performance Requirements

**PR-1: Data Sheet Accuracy**
- Data sheet information shall be accurate and based on design calculations, specifications, and manufacturer data
- No conflicting information between data sheets and other design documents
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Data sheet accuracy requirement
- Verification: Data sheet review and cross-document consistency check

**PR-2: Data Sheet Completeness**
- Data sheets sufficiently complete to enable equipment procurement, vendor selection, and installation
- No significant information gaps requiring assumptions by vendors or installers
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Data sheet adequacy requirement
- Verification: Procurement and construction team review of data sheets

**PR-3: Consistency with Design Documents**
- Data sheet specifications and performance requirements shall be consistent with drawings (DEL-23.01), specifications (DEL-23.02), and calculations (DEL-23.03)
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Document set consistency requirement
- Verification: Cross-document consistency check

**PR-4: Constructability and Availability**
- Specified equipment shall be commercially available and constructible
- Equipment lead times and availability considered in equipment selection
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Design-build constructability requirement
- Verification: Procurement team review of equipment availability

**PR-5: Lifecycle Cost Consideration**
- Equipment specifications shall consider lifecycle cost (capital cost + operating/maintenance cost) consistent with OBJ-9 (Lifecycle Cost Optimization)
- Source: Decomposition Section 2 (OBJ-9) and **ASSUMPTION**
- Verification: Lifecycle cost consideration documented in equipment selection rationale (if performed)

### Interface Requirements

**INT-1: Fire Protection Design Drawings (DEL-23.01)**
- Equipment tags on data sheets shall match equipment tags on drawings
- Equipment locations and quantities on data sheets shall match drawings
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Drawing-data sheet coordination
- Verification: Cross-check tags, locations, quantities

**INT-2: Fire Protection Technical Specification (DEL-23.02)**
- Equipment specifications on data sheets shall be consistent with technical specification requirements
- Data sheets supplement specification with detailed equipment information
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Specification-data sheet coordination
- Verification: Cross-check specifications

**INT-3: Fire Protection Design Calculations (DEL-23.03)**
- Equipment performance requirements on data sheets shall match calculated values from DEL-23.03
- Equipment sizing on data sheets shall be based on calculations
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Calculation-data sheet coordination
- Verification: Cross-check performance requirements and sizing

**INT-4: Fire Protection Installation & Test Records (DEL-23.05)**
- Equipment testing requirements on data sheets shall define basis for test records
- Data sheet performance requirements shall be verified during commissioning
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Data sheet-testing coordination
- Verification: Test procedures aligned with data sheet requirements

**INT-5: Electrical Power Distribution (PKG-17)**
- Data sheets shall provide electrical load data (voltage, phase, frequency, power consumption, motor data) for electrical design
- Electrical design team provides power supply data (available voltage, disconnect sizing) for data sheets
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Electrical interface coordination
- Verification: Electrical load data exchange and verification

**INT-6: Control Systems (PKG-19)**
- Data sheets shall provide control system interface data (I/O requirements, communication protocols, monitoring/alarm points) for control system design
- Control system design team provides interface specifications for data sheets
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Control system interface coordination
- Verification: I&O data exchange and verification

**INT-7: Structural (PKG-06)**
- Data sheets shall provide equipment loads (weight, seismic loads) for structural design of supports, foundations, and anchorage
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Structural interface coordination
- Verification: Equipment load data provided to structural team

**INT-8: Employer's Requirements**
- Data sheets shall incorporate equipment requirements from Employer's Requirements (ER)
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: ER compliance
- Verification: ER requirements incorporated into data sheets

## Verification

**Verification Methods for Equipment Data Sheets:**

**V-1: Data Sheet Self-Check**
- Engineer reviews data sheets for completeness, accuracy, and consistency with design documents
- Verification record: Self-check notation
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Engineering practice

**V-2: Interdisciplinary Check (IDC)**
- Affected disciplines review data sheets for interface coordination:
  - Electrical team reviews electrical load data
  - I&C team reviews control system interface data
  - Structural team reviews equipment load data
  - Procurement team reviews equipment specifications for availability
- IDC comments tracked and resolved
- Verification record: IDC sign-off and comment log
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Interdisciplinary coordination

**V-3: Cross-Document Consistency Check**
- Data sheets cross-checked with drawings (DEL-23.01), specifications (DEL-23.02), calculations (DEL-23.03) for consistency
- Verification record: Consistency check log
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Document coordination

**V-4: Independent Technical Review**
- Qualified reviewer checks data sheets for technical correctness and completeness
- Verification record: Independent review sign-off
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Quality control

**V-5: Codes and Standards Compliance Review**
- Data sheets reviewed for compliance with applicable equipment standards and listing/approval requirements
- Verification record: Standards compliance checklist
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Standards compliance verification

**V-6: Procurement/Constructability Review**
- Procurement and construction teams review data sheets for equipment availability, lead times, constructability
- Verification record: Procurement/constructability review comments
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Design-build review

**V-7: Equipment Selection Verification (during procurement)**
- Vendor-proposed equipment verified against data sheet requirements
- Verification record: Equipment submittal review and approval
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Procurement verification (occurs after data sheet issue)

**Acceptance Criteria:**

Data sheets are acceptable when:
- All equipment requiring data sheets have data sheets prepared
- Data sheets complete with all required information
- Cross-document consistency verified (with drawings, specifications, calculations)
- Interdisciplinary coordination completed (electrical, I&C, structural interfaces)
- Independent review completed with sign-off
- Standards compliance verified
- Procurement/constructability review completed
- Source: **ASSUMPTION**: Data sheet acceptance criteria — specific criteria **TBD** per project
