# Procedure: DEL-21.02 Building Technical Specification

## Purpose

This procedure defines the process for **producing** the **Building Technical Specification** within **PKG-21 Building Structures & Envelope**.

**Deliverable Purpose:** Defines performance, materials, and workmanship requirements for building per ER requirements.

**Source:** Decomposition document line 514

**Deliverable Type:** Specification
**Responsible Party:** D&B Contractor

**Procedure Scope:** This procedure describes the workflow for developing, coordinating, reviewing, and issuing building technical specifications from initial requirements review through issued-for-construction (IFC) status.

## Steps

### Step 1: Requirements Review and Section Planning

**Objective:** Establish scope and organize specification sections.

**Activities:**

1.1 **Review Design Drawings and Scope**
   - Review building design drawings (DEL-21.01) to identify systems and materials requiring specifications
   - List building components: structural framing, foundations, envelope systems, roofing, doors, windows, finishes

1.2 **Review Employer's Requirements**
   - **TBD**: Review ER Volume 2 Part 3 for material requirements, approved manufacturers/products, finish selections
   - Identify any prescriptive requirements (specific products mandated by Employer)

1.3 **Organize Specification Sections**
   - Organize per CSI MasterFormat divisions — **ASSUMPTION**: Divisions 03, 05, 07, 08, (09, 13 if applicable)
   - List sections to be developed (see Datasheet.md for anticipated content)
   - Assign section numbers per project numbering system — **TBD**

1.4 **Coordinate with Design Calculations**
   - Coordinate with structural engineer (DEL-21.03) for material properties, concrete mixes, steel grades
   - Ensure specifications match design assumptions

**Deliverables:** Specification section list and outline

### Step 2: Develop Specification Sections

**Objective:** Write technical specification content per CSI 3-part format.

**Activities:**

2.1 **Set Up Specification Templates**
   - Use project specification templates (if available) or CSI MasterFormat section templates — **TBD**
   - Include standard Part 1 articles (Summary, References, Submittals, Quality Assurance, etc.)

2.2 **Write Part 1: General**
   - **1.1 Summary:** Section scope, systems included/excluded
   - **1.2 References:** Applicable codes, standards, test methods
   - **1.3 Submittals:** Product data, shop drawings, samples, test reports
   - **1.4 Quality Assurance:** Manufacturer/installer qualifications, certifications
   - **1.5 Delivery/Storage/Handling:** Protection requirements
   - **1.6 Warranty:** Warranty periods and coverage

2.3 **Write Part 2: Products**
   - **2.1 Manufacturers:** List acceptable manufacturers (3-name or performance-based per Guidance.md)
   - **2.2 Materials:** Material types, grades, properties (reference ASTM, CSA standards)
   - **2.3 Fabrication:** Factory fabrication requirements
   - **2.4 Finishes:** Coatings, colors, surface preparation
   - **2.5 Source Quality Control:** Factory testing, quality control procedures

2.4 **Write Part 3: Execution**
   - **3.1 Examination:** Site condition verification before installation
   - **3.2 Preparation:** Surface preparation, substrate requirements
   - **3.3 Installation:** Installation methods (reference trade standards like SMACNA, CSSBI)
   - **3.4 Field Quality Control:** Inspection, testing, acceptance criteria
   - **3.5 Adjusting:** Adjustment and tuning (doors, operable components)
   - **3.6 Cleaning/Protection:** Final cleaning and protection

2.5 **Address Coastal/Marine Environment**
   - Specify corrosion-resistant materials and finishes per Guidance.md Principle P2
   - Galvanized or stainless fasteners, marine-grade coatings, UV-resistant sealants

2.6 **Address Code Compliance**
   - NBC 2020 material requirements (structural, fire, thermal, accessibility)
   - Test standards and acceptance criteria (ASTM, CSA, ULC)

**Deliverables:** Draft specification sections (Part 1, 2, 3 complete)

**Source:** CSI 3-part section format per Guidance.md Principle P3

### Step 3: Coordination Review

**Objective:** Ensure specifications coordinate with drawings and other disciplines.

**Activities:**

3.1 **Coordinate with Drawings (DEL-21.01)**
   - Verify material callouts on drawings match specification sections
   - Verify detail references align (drawings reference specification sections)
   - Resolve conflicts: **TBD** — Establish precedence rule (specifications govern materials, drawings govern quantities/locations)

3.2 **Coordinate with Calculations (DEL-21.03)**
   - Verify material properties in specifications match design assumptions (concrete strength, steel grades)
   - Verify structural member sizes match

3.3 **Coordinate with Other Disciplines**
   - Civil/site: Foundation waterproofing, drainage connections
   - MEP (PKG-22): Penetrations, flashing, roof equipment support
   - Coatings (PKG-26): Protective coating specifications for building steel

3.4 **Document Coordination**
   - Record coordination issues and resolutions
   - Update specifications to resolve conflicts

**Deliverables:** Coordinated specifications, coordination issue log

### Step 4: Quality Review

**Objective:** Review specifications for technical accuracy, completeness, and code compliance.

**Activities:**

4.1 **Self-Check**
   - Specification writer reviews for completeness (all required articles present)
   - Check internal consistency (cross-references, terminology)
   - Check for placeholders or incomplete sections — resolve **TBD** items

4.2 **Code Compliance Review**
   - Verify materials and methods comply with NBC 2020 requirements
   - Verify test standards are appropriate and current
   - Verify fire-rated assemblies reference ULC-tested systems (if required)

4.3 **Constructability Review**
   - Verify specified materials and products are available
   - Verify installation methods are feasible
   - **TBD**: Engage trade contractors for input (if design-build or design-assist)

4.4 **Independent Review**
   - Independent reviewer (qualified engineer or specification writer) reviews specifications
   - Check technical accuracy, code compliance, completeness
   - Check coordination with drawings and calculations
   - Document review comments

4.5 **Address Review Comments**
   - Specification writer addresses review comments
   - Update specifications to resolve comments

**Deliverables:** Reviewed specifications, review comments log

**Source:** Specification.md Verification methods

### Step 5: Approval and Issue

**Objective:** Obtain approvals and issue specifications for construction.

**Activities:**

5.1 **Final Review and Approval**
   - Lead designer/discipline manager reviews and approves
   - Professional seal (if required) — **TBD**: P.Eng. seal requirements for specifications in BC

5.2 **Prepare Issue Package**
   - Generate PDF from source documents
   - Prepare transmittal letter
   - Assign revision number and issue date
   - Update document index

5.3 **Issue Specifications**
   - Upload to project document management system (DMS)
   - Transmit per project distribution list (Employer, construction team)
   - Record issue in document control log
   - Update `_STATUS.md` to reflect issue status (IN_PROGRESS → CHECKING → ISSUED)
   - Place issued copies in `3_Issued/` folder per deliverable folder structure

**Deliverables:** Issued building technical specifications (PDF and source files)

**Source:** Specification.md Documentation section; README.md deliverable lifecycle

### Step 6: Post-Issue Management

**Objective:** Manage specification revisions during construction.

**Activities:**

6.1 **Respond to RFIs and Substitution Requests**
   - Review contractor requests for information (RFIs) related to specifications
   - Evaluate material/product substitution requests (if "or equal" provisions used)
   - Issue clarifications or specification addenda as needed

6.2 **Issue Specification Revisions**
   - If changes required: Update specification sections with changes
   - Mark revisions (revision clouds or change bars)
   - Update revision block
   - Repeat review and approval process (Steps 4-5) for revised sections
   - Issue revised specifications with incremented revision number

6.3 **Maintain Specification Records**
   - Maintain current specifications in project DMS
   - Archive superseded revisions per document retention policy — **TBD**

6.4 **Support Construction and Installation**
   - Review submittals for specification compliance
   - Review shop drawings (DEL-21.06) for specification compliance
   - Support construction inspection and testing (DEL-21.05)
   - **TBD**: Specification writer role during construction

**Deliverables:** RFI responses, substitution approvals, revised specifications (if changes made)

## Verification

**Verification Activities:**

**V1: Specification Review** — Step 4 (Quality Review)
- Technical review by independent reviewer
- Review against ER, codes, design basis
- Check coordination with drawings and calculations

**V2: Code Compliance Check** — Step 4 (Quality Review)
- Verify materials comply with NBC 2020 and applicable standards
- Verify test standards appropriate

**V3: Constructability Review** — Step 4 (Quality Review)
- Verify materials available and installation feasible
- Engage trade contractors for input

**Acceptance Criteria:**

Per Specification.md:
- Specification complete (all sections present, no placeholders)
- Coordinated with drawings (DEL-21.01) and calculations (DEL-21.03)
- Complies with NBC 2020 and applicable standards
- Materials and products available and constructable
