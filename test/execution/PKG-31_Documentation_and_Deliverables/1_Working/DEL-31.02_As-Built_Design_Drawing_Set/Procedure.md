# Procedure: DEL-31.02 As-Built Design Drawing Set

## Purpose

This procedure defines the process for producing and managing **As-Built Design Drawing Set** within **PKG-31 Documentation & Deliverables**.

**Objective:**
To produce a complete, accurate, and field-verified set of As-Built Drawings that document the Canola Oil Transload Facility Phase 1 as actually constructed, in accordance with Employer's Requirements (ER) and VFPA standards.

**Source:** Decomposition DEL-31.02 (line 687); _CONTEXT.md

**Deliverable type:** Drawing Set
**Responsible party:** D&B Contractor

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md

**Scope:**
This procedure addresses the production of As-Built Drawings from construction completion through final issuance and handover. It applies to all disciplines contributing to the as-built drawing set (Civil, Structural, Mechanical, Process, Electrical, I&C, Marine, Buildings, Fire Protection).

## Steps

### Step 1: Planning and Baseline Preparation

**Objective:** Establish the as-built production plan, baseline, and schedule.

**Activities:**

1.1. **Prepare As-Built Drawing Register:**
   - Using the Record Drawing Register (DEL-31.01) as the baseline, prepare an as-built drawing register
   - Identify all drawings to be updated to as-built status
   - Add fields for as-built status tracking (field markup received, field verified, CAD updated, checked, approved)
   - **Source:** Specification FR-01; **ASSUMPTION** per document management practice

1.2. **Obtain Record Drawings Baseline:**
   - Obtain complete Record Design Drawing Set (DEL-31.01) in electronic format (CAD files)
   - Verify Record Drawings are complete and issued for construction
   - Set up CAD file structure for as-built drawing production
   - **Source:** Specification FR-05, IR-01; **ASSUMPTION** per as-built production workflow

1.3. **Develop As-Built Production Schedule:**
   - Align as-built production with construction completion and contract closeout schedule
   - Consider phased production (by discipline, construction phase, or facility area) if beneficial
   - Identify resource requirements (CAD, survey, review) and allocate resources
   - Coordinate with contract closeout schedule to ensure as-builts are completed on time
   - **Source:** Specification PR-03; Guidance (Considerations: Schedule and Resourcing)

1.4. **Establish As-Built Procedures and Conventions:**
   - Confirm project as-built markup conventions (revision clouds, color coding, notation format)
   - Establish field verification sign-off requirements and formats
   - Clarify change documentation cross-reference requirements
   - Develop templates and checklists for as-built production and checking
   - **Source:** Specification PR-02; **ASSUMPTION** per as-built quality procedures

1.5. **Coordinate with Stakeholders:**
   - Coordinate with construction team to collect field markups and change documentation
   - Coordinate with commissioning team to obtain test records and acceptance documentation
   - Coordinate with operations team (if available) for review and input
   - **Source:** Specification IR-02, IR-03, IR-04; **ASSUMPTION** per project coordination

**Deliverables from Step 1:**
- As-Built Drawing Register
- As-Built Production Schedule
- As-Built Procedures and Templates
- CAD file structure for as-built production

**Verification:**
- As-Built Drawing Register reviewed and approved by As-Built Coordinator and discipline leads
- Schedule integrated with overall contract closeout schedule

### Step 2: Collect and Review Construction Markups and Changes

**Objective:** Collect all construction field markups, redline drawings, and change documentation to inform as-built production.

**Activities:**

2.1. **Collect Field Markups:**
   - Collect all field-marked-up drawings from construction personnel
   - Organize markups by discipline and drawing number
   - Review markups for completeness and clarity
   - **Source:** Specification IR-02; **ASSUMPTION** per construction documentation collection

2.2. **Compile Change Documentation:**
   - Compile RFI register, field order log, design change register, and construction change notice register
   - Identify all approved changes that affect physical installations
   - Cross-reference changes to affected drawings
   - **Source:** Specification FR-03; **ASSUMPTION** per change management practice

2.3. **Review and Clarify:**
   - Review field markups and change documentation with construction personnel to clarify unclear markups or missing information
   - Identify areas requiring field verification or additional measurement
   - Document any questions or gaps for resolution
   - **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per markup review and quality control

2.4. **Organize Documentation:**
   - Organize field markups, change documentation, and notes by drawing for efficient CAD updating
   - Create preliminary change cross-reference table linking changes to affected drawings
   - **Source:** Specification QR-04; **ASSUMPTION** per documentation organization

**Deliverables from Step 2:**
- Collected field markups and redline drawings (organized by discipline and drawing)
- Compiled change documentation package
- Preliminary change cross-reference table
- List of field verification requirements

**Verification:**
- Field markup completeness confirmed by construction management
- Change documentation completeness confirmed by project controls

### Step 3: Field Measurement and Verification

**Objective:** Conduct field surveys, measurements, and inspections to verify as-constructed conditions.

**Activities:**

3.1. **Plan Field Verification:**
   - Develop field verification plan identifying what will be measured, measurement methods, accuracy requirements
   - Prioritize critical dimensions and safety-critical systems
   - Schedule field verification activities considering access availability
   - **Source:** Specification FR-02, PR-01; Guidance (Considerations: Field Measurement and Verification)

3.2. **Survey and Measurement:**
   - Conduct field surveys to verify dimensions, locations, elevations, and configurations
   - Use survey equipment (total stations, GPS) for control point verification and critical measurements
   - Use measurement tools (tape measures, laser distance meters) for detailed measurements
   - Document measurements in field notes and survey data logs
   - **Source:** Specification FR-02, PR-01; **ASSUMPTION** per surveying practice

3.3. **Cross-Check with Commissioning Records:**
   - Cross-check field conditions against commissioning test records (PKG-30) to confirm final operating configurations
   - Verify valve positions, equipment settings, control logic as-commissioned
   - **Source:** Specification IR-03; cross-reference PKG-30

3.4. **Physical Inspection:**
   - Conduct visual inspections to verify installations, configurations, materials
   - Verify equipment tags and nameplates against Asset Hierarchy (DEL-31.06)
   - Photograph critical or complex installations for reference
   - **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per field inspection practice

3.5. **Document Field Verification:**
   - Record all field verification data in survey logs, measurement sheets, inspection checklists
   - Obtain field personnel sign-offs confirming field verification completion
   - Identify any discrepancies requiring resolution or clarification
   - **Source:** Specification FR-02, QR-02; **ASSUMPTION** per field verification documentation

**Deliverables from Step 3:**
- Field survey data and measurement records
- Commissioning records cross-check confirmation
- Field inspection reports and photographs
- Field verification sign-offs

**Verification:**
- Field verification plan completed and signed off
- All critical dimensions and safety-critical systems verified
- Field personnel sign-offs obtained

### Step 4: CAD Update and As-Built Drawing Production

**Objective:** Update CAD drawings with construction markups, field changes, and field verification data to produce as-built drawings.

**Activities:**

4.1. **CAD File Setup:**
   - Create as-built CAD files from Record Drawing baseline (DEL-31.01)
   - Update drawing title blocks to indicate "As-Built" status
   - **Source:** Specification FR-05, PR-02; **ASSUMPTION** per CAD workflow

4.2. **Incorporate Construction Changes:**
   - Update CAD drawings to reflect field markups, changes, and deviations
   - Apply as-built markup conventions (revision clouds, change notes) to indicate changes from record drawings
   - Add field verification data (dimensions, locations, elevations) to drawings
   - **Source:** Specification FR-03, FR-05; **ASSUMPTION** per CAD update procedures

4.3. **Change Documentation Cross-Check:**
   - Cross-check that all RFIs, field orders, design changes, and construction change notices are incorporated
   - Update change cross-reference table with final drawing references
   - Add traceability notes on drawings linking changes to source documentation (e.g., "See RFI-1234")
   - **Source:** Specification FR-03, QR-04; Verification method 2

4.4. **VFPA Standards Compliance Check:**
   - Review drawings for compliance with VFPA standards (symbology, formats, annotations, scales)
   - Verify as-built markup conventions are applied consistently and per VFPA requirements (if specified)
   - Verify title blocks, coordinate systems, and other VFPA-specific requirements
   - **Source:** Specification FR-04, PR-02; Verification method 4

4.5. **Asset Tag Verification:**
   - Verify equipment tags and identifiers on as-built drawings match Asset Hierarchy (DEL-31.06)
   - **Source:** Specification IR-05; cross-reference DEL-31.06

4.6. **Self-Check:**
   - Originator performs self-check for completeness, accuracy, and standards compliance
   - Correct errors and omissions identified during self-check
   - **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per engineering QC practice

**Deliverables from Step 4:**
- Draft as-built drawings (CAD files and PDFs)
- Change cross-reference table (updated with final drawing references)
- Self-check completion records

**Verification:**
- Originator self-check completed
- All construction changes incorporated and traceable

### Step 5: Technical Review and QA

**Objective:** Conduct technical review, checking, and QA of as-built drawings to ensure accuracy and compliance.

**Activities:**

5.1. **Technical Checking:**
   - Qualified checker reviews as-built drawings for technical accuracy, completeness, and compliance with specifications and standards
   - Checker verifies field verification data, change incorporation, and as-built markup accuracy
   - Checker records comments and issues in comment tracking system
   - **Source:** Specification QR-02; Verification method 5

5.2. **Interdisciplinary Coordination Check:**
   - Verify as-built drawings reflect interdisciplinary coordination and as-constructed interfaces
   - **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per multi-discipline coordination

5.3. **Field Verification Sign-Off:**
   - Confirm field verification sign-offs are obtained for all as-built drawings
   - **Source:** Specification FR-02, QR-02; **ASSUMPTION** per field verification requirements

5.4. **Regulatory Submittal Readiness Check:**
   - Verify as-built drawings are suitable for submission to VFPA and other regulatory authorities for project closeout
   - **Source:** Specification IR-06; cross-reference PKG-32

5.5. **Final Approval:**
   - Authorized approver (e.g., Chief Engineer, Project Manager) provides final approval sign-off
   - Approval recorded on drawing title blocks and in as-built drawing register
   - **Source:** Specification QR-03

5.6. **Comment Resolution:**
   - Originator addresses all checker and reviewer comments
   - Comments closed out when resolution confirmed
   - Drawings revised as needed
   - **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per QA/QC comment management practice

**Deliverables from Step 5:**
- Checked as-built drawings with checker sign-off
- Comment tracking log showing all comments closed
- Approver sign-off

**Verification:**
- Checker and approver sign-offs obtained and recorded
- All comments resolved and closed

### Step 6: Final Completeness Verification

**Objective:** Verify as-built drawing set is complete and ready for issuance.

**Activities:**

6.1. **Completeness Check:**
   - Verify all drawings listed in As-Built Drawing Register are completed and approved
   - Cross-check against Record Drawing Register to ensure no missing drawings
   - **Source:** Specification FR-01; Verification method 3

6.2. **Change Documentation Completeness:**
   - Verify change cross-reference table is complete and all changes are traceable
   - Compile change documentation package for handover
   - **Source:** Specification FR-03, QR-04

6.3. **Field Verification Documentation:**
   - Verify all field verification records, survey data, and field personnel sign-offs are complete
   - Compile field verification documentation package for handover
   - **Source:** Specification FR-02

6.4. **Final QA Review:**
   - Final quality assurance review to confirm all checks completed, comments resolved, and approvals obtained
   - Verify as-built drawing set completeness and consistency across disciplines
   - **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per quality management final checks

6.5. **As-Built Status Update:**
   - Update drawing status to "As-Built" or "Final" (or equivalent project-specific status)
   - Update as-built drawing register with issue date and status
   - **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per document status conventions

6.6. **Electronic and Hard Copy Preparation:**
   - Prepare electronic deliverables (CAD native files, PDFs) per Specification requirements
   - Prepare hard copy sets if required
   - **Source:** Specification (Documentation section)

**Deliverables from Step 6:**
- Complete As-Built Drawing Set (verified for completeness)
- Change documentation package
- Field verification documentation package
- Final As-Built Drawing Register

**Verification:**
- As-Built Drawing Register 100% complete
- All drawings approved and ready for issuance
- All supporting documentation compiled

   - Transmit to Employer per contract handover procedures
   - **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per contract handover requirements; **TBD** — specific handover format per Employer's Requirements

7.3. **Operations Team Handover:**
   - Provide as-built drawings to operations team for use in operations manuals (DEL-31.03), maintenance manuals (DEL-31.04), and day-to-day facility operations
   - Conduct handover meeting or orientation on as-built drawing content and use (if required)
   - **Source:** Specification IR-04, LR-01; **ASSUMPTION** per operations handover

7.4. **Drawing Transmittal:**
   - Prepare drawing transmittals documenting issued as-built drawings
   - Transmit as-built drawings to all recipients per distribution matrix
   - Upload to project document management system
   - **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per document transmittal procedures

7.5. **Filing and Archiving:**
   - Place issued as-built drawing copies in `3_Issued/` folder
   - Maintain as-built drawing register as master record of issued as-builts
   - Archive supporting documentation (field markups, survey data, change documentation)
   - **Source:** README.md (How to use the framework); **ASSUMPTION** per document archiving practice

**Deliverables from Step 7:**
- Issued As-Built Drawing Set (electronic and hard copy)
- As-Built Drawing Transmittals

## Verification

**Verification activities for As-Built Drawing Set deliverables:**

The following verification methods are applied at appropriate stages throughout the procedure:

1. **Field Verification and Measurement** (Step 3)
   - Physical field surveys and measurements to confirm as-constructed dimensions, locations, elevations
   - **Acceptance Criterion:** As-built drawings match field conditions within specified tolerances; field personnel sign-offs obtained

2. **Change Documentation Cross-Check** (Step 4.3)
   - Verify all RFIs, field orders, design changes, and construction change notices are incorporated and traceable
   - **Acceptance Criterion:** All approved changes reflected in as-built drawings; change cross-reference table complete

3. **Completeness Check** (Step 6.1)
   - Verification that all required as-built drawings are included
   - **Acceptance Criterion:** As-built drawing register 100% complete; no missing drawings

4. **Format and Standards Compliance** (Step 4.4)
   - Review for compliance with VFPA drawing standards and project as-built markup conventions
   - **Acceptance Criterion:** Full compliance with VFPA standards and project conventions confirmed

5. **Technical Review and QA** (Step 5)
   - Independent technical review by qualified checker
   - **Acceptance Criterion:** Checker and approver sign-offs obtained; no unresolved technical issues

6. **Stakeholder Review and Acceptance** (Step 7)
   - Employer review and acceptance
   - Regulatory authority review and acceptance (as required)
   - **Acceptance Criterion:** Employer handover completed; regulatory approvals obtained

**Overall Acceptance Criteria:**
- All construction changes and field conditions accurately incorporated
- Field verification sign-offs obtained for all as-built drawings
- Technical review and checker sign-offs obtained
- As-built drawings issued with "As-Built" or "Final" status
- Compliance with VFPA standards confirmed
- Employer acceptance obtained
- As-built drawing register complete and accurate
- Regulatory submittals completed and accepted

**Source:** Specification (Verification section); **ASSUMPTION** per as-built documentation quality requirements
