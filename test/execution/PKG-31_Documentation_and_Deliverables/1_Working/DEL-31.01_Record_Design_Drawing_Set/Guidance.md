# Guidance: DEL-31.01 Record Design Drawing Set

## Purpose

This guidance document supports the development of **Record Design Drawing Set** for **PKG-31 Documentation & Deliverables**.

**Deliverable Purpose:**
Defines and substantiates record design drawings in accordance with Employer's Requirements (ER) and VFPA standards, providing the authoritative design baseline for the Canola Oil Transload Facility Phase 1.

**Source:** Decomposition DEL-31.01 (line 686); _CONTEXT.md

**Role in Project Lifecycle:**

Record Design Drawings serve as the bridge between the design phase and construction/operations phases:
- They document the final approved design intent as developed by the engineering team
- They form the contractual basis for construction activities
- They provide the baseline reference for As-Built documentation (DEL-31.02)
- They support future operations, maintenance, modifications, and regulatory compliance
- They enable lifecycle cost optimization (OBJ-9) by providing accurate documentation for asset management

**Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per industry practice; OBJ-9 (Decomposition line 67, line 788)

This deliverable is classified as a **Drawing Set** under the **Project Delivery** discipline, to be produced by **D&B Contractor**.

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md

## Principles

**Engineering rationale (Project Delivery discipline):**

### Principle 1: Design Intent Documentation

Record Design Drawings capture the design as intended, not as-built. Key considerations:
- Drawings reflect the final approved design calculations, specifications, and design verification outcomes
- Design assumptions, criteria, and basis decisions are documented (in calculations and specifications), with drawings providing the graphical representation
- Any deviations during construction require formal change management and eventual incorporation into As-Built drawings (DEL-31.02)

**Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per engineering documentation practice; distinction from As-Built drawings (DEL-31.02, Decomposition line 687)

### Principle 2: Multi-Discipline Integration

The Record Design Drawing Set is inherently integrative:
- It consolidates outputs from all discipline-specific design deliverables (Civil, Structural, Mechanical, Process, Electrical, I&C, Marine, Buildings, Fire Protection)
- Cross-discipline coordination is essential to avoid conflicts and ensure constructability
- Drawing coordination is typically managed through periodic coordination meetings, clash detection (if 3D models used), and formal interdisciplinary review cycles

**Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per multi-discipline project practice; Decomposition DEC-01 (line 799) — discipline-organized packages

### Principle 3: Standardization and Consistency

VFPA drawing standards ensure consistency and usability:
- Standardized symbology, annotations, and formats enable efficient review and interpretation
- Consistent coordinate systems, scales, and units prevent errors
- Adherence to standards facilitates regulatory approval and future maintenance

**Source:** Specification FR-03, PR-02; Decomposition DEL-31.01 (line 686) — "per VFPA standards"

### Principle 4: Traceability and Configuration Control

Record Design Drawings are configuration-controlled documents:
- Each revision is tracked with change descriptions, dates, and approvers
- Superseded revisions are archived but remain accessible for audit and forensic purposes
- Drawing registers provide comprehensive indexing for retrieval

**Source:** Specification FR-05, QR-04; **ASSUMPTION** per document management best practice

### Principle 5: Fitness for Multiple Purposes

Record Design Drawings serve multiple downstream uses:
- **Construction reference:** Basis for construction activities and shop drawing development
- **Regulatory submittals:** Supporting documentation for permits and approvals (cross-reference PKG-32)
- **Operations & maintenance:** Reference for system understanding, troubleshooting, and maintenance planning (cross-reference DEL-31.03, DEL-31.04)
- **Future modifications:** Baseline for engineering changes, expansions (OBJ-8), and retrofits
- **Asset management:** Foundation for lifecycle cost tracking and optimization (OBJ-9)

**Source:** Specification IR-03, IR-04, LR-01, LR-02; OBJ-8 (Decomposition line 66), OBJ-9 (line 67)

## Considerations

**Factors to consider during development:**

### Scope and Completeness
- **Drawing List Development:** Early development of a comprehensive drawing register is critical. The register should be cross-checked against the deliverable scope (all disciplines, all facility systems and components) to ensure no gaps.
- **Interface Drawings:** Consider whether dedicated interface drawings are needed (e.g., civil/structural interfaces, mechanical/electrical penetrations, marine/civil transitions).
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per project management practice

### Coordination and Clash Resolution
- **3D Modeling:** If 3D models (e.g., BIM, plant design software) are used during design, leverage these for automated clash detection and coordination.
- **Coordination Milestones:** Establish formal coordination review milestones (e.g., 30%, 60%, 90% design stages) to identify and resolve conflicts early.
- **Interdisciplinary Meetings:** Regular coordination meetings between discipline leads reduce late-stage conflicts.
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per engineering project practice; **TBD** — specific coordination procedures per project Quality Plan

### VFPA Standards Compliance
- **Early Engagement:** Obtain VFPA drawing standards and templates early in the design process.
- **Template Development:** Develop project-specific drawing templates that comply with VFPA requirements (title blocks, symbology, layers, etc.).
- **Preliminary Review:** Consider submitting sample drawings to VFPA for preliminary review to confirm compliance before full drawing set development.
- **Source:** Specification FR-03; **ASSUMPTION** per regulatory coordination practice

### Regulatory and Permit Requirements
- **Permit Drawing Requirements:** Confirm which drawings are required for permit applications (building permits, marine works permits, environmental permits, etc.).
- **Submission Timing:** Coordinate drawing issuance with permit application schedules (cross-reference PKG-32).
- **Regulatory Review Cycles:** Allow sufficient time for regulatory authority review and comment incorporation.
- **Source:** Specification IR-03; **ASSUMPTION** per permitting practice

### Quality Assurance and Checking
- **Checking Resources:** Ensure qualified checkers are available and allocated to the drawing review effort.
- **Check Cycles:** Plan for multiple check cycles (self-check, interdisciplinary check, independent technical check, final QA check).
- **Comment Management:** Use a structured comment management system (e.g., spreadsheet tracker, dedicated review software) to ensure all review comments are addressed and closed.
- **Source:** Specification QR-02; **ASSUMPTION** per QA/QC practice

### Electronic Deliverables and Data Management
- **File Formats:** Clarify required electronic formats (CAD native files, PDFs, raster images, 3D models, etc.).
- **Metadata:** Ensure drawing files include appropriate metadata (drawing number, title, revision, date) for document management system integration.
- **Data Transfer:** Plan for data transfer to Employer's document management system (format compatibility, file size limits, transfer protocols).
- **Source:** Specification (Documentation section); **ASSUMPTION** per data management practice — **TBD** — specific requirements per Employer's Requirements

### Schedule and Resourcing
- **Drawing Production Schedule:** Align drawing production with overall project design schedule and permit/construction milestones.
- **Resource Loading:** Ensure sufficient CAD resources (designers, drafters) are allocated, particularly during peak production periods.
- **Critical Path Drawings:** Identify drawings that are on the critical path (e.g., foundation drawings for early construction, marine drawings for permit-critical items) and prioritize accordingly.
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per project scheduling and resource management practice

### Constructability
- **Contractor Input:** If design-build or early contractor involvement, solicit contractor input on drawing clarity, level of detail, and constructability.
- **Shop Drawing Coordination:** Consider what information must be on Record Drawings versus what will be detailed in shop drawings.
- **Field Use:** Ensure drawings are suitable for field use (legibility, appropriate scales, clear dimensions and notes).
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per constructability and design-build practice

### Operability and Maintainability
- **Operations Input:** Engage operations personnel (if available) to review drawings for operational clarity and maintainability considerations.
- **Maintenance Access:** Ensure drawings clearly show access points, clearances, and maintenance zones for equipment.
- **Labeling and Tagging:** Coordinate equipment and system labeling on drawings with asset management and maintenance systems (cross-reference DEL-31.06 Asset Hierarchy).
- **Source:** Specification LR-01; OBJ-9 (Lifecycle Cost Optimization, Decomposition line 67); **ASSUMPTION** per O&M integration practice

- **PKG-31 Documentation & Deliverables:** This deliverable is part of the project's overall documentation package. Coordination with other PKG-31 deliverables (As-Built drawings, manuals, vendor documentation, etc.) is essential to ensure consistency and completeness.

## Trade-offs

**Competing concerns to evaluate:**

### Level of Detail vs. Clarity
- **Trade-off:** More detail can improve construction accuracy but may reduce drawing clarity and increase production effort.
- **Consideration:** Balance design-level detail with reliance on shop drawings for fabrication-level detail. Record Drawings should provide sufficient information for construction planning and general arrangement without overwhelming with minutiae.
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per drawing practice; **TBD** — specific detail requirements per Employer's Requirements or project drawing standards

### Standardization vs. Optimization
- **Trade-off:** Strict adherence to VFPA standards ensures regulatory acceptance but may limit use of project-specific optimizations (e.g., custom symbology, enhanced visualization techniques).
- **Consideration:** Prioritize VFPA compliance for submittals and official record. Consider supplemental drawings or 3D visualizations for internal coordination if needed.
- **Source:** Specification FR-03; **ASSUMPTION** per regulatory compliance priority

### Schedule vs. Quality
- **Trade-off:** Accelerated drawing production can meet tight project schedules but may compromise drawing quality and increase error rates.
- **Consideration:** Ensure adequate time for checking and review cycles. Rushed drawings often result in construction delays due to RFIs and clarifications, negating any initial schedule gains.
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per project management experience; OBJ-1 (Safe & Reliable Operation) prioritizes quality and reliability

### Electronic vs. Hard Copy Deliverables
- **Trade-off:** Electronic deliverables are easier to manage, update, and distribute, but hard copies may be required for field use or regulatory submittals.
- **Consideration:** Confirm Employer's requirements for hard copy deliverables. Plan for hybrid approach if needed (electronic as primary, hard copy for specific uses).
- **Source:** Specification (Documentation section); **TBD** — specific requirements per Employer's Requirements

### Design Freeze vs. Continuous Improvement
- **Trade-off:** Freezing the design enables orderly drawing production and issuance, but may preclude late-stage design optimizations or lessons learned.
- **Consideration:** Establish formal design freeze milestones with change management procedures for post-freeze modifications. Ensure change processes are efficient enough to accommodate critical improvements without causing major disruption.
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION** per design management practice; **TBD** — project change management procedures

### Cost vs. Comprehensiveness
- **Trade-off:** Comprehensive drawing sets (more drawings, more detail) increase design cost but may reduce construction and operations risks.
- **Consideration:** Balance cost against OBJ-9 (Lifecycle Cost Optimization). Invest in drawing quality and completeness where it reduces downstream costs (fewer RFIs, better maintainability, easier future modifications).
- **Source:** OBJ-9 (Decomposition line 67, line 788); **ASSUMPTION** per value engineering principles
