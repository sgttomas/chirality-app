# Specification: DEL-31.04 Maintenance Manuals

## Scope

This specification defines the requirements for **Maintenance Manuals** within **PKG-31 Documentation & Deliverables**.

**Purpose:** Provides comprehensive guidance for maintaining facility equipment and systems per Employer's Requirements (ER).

**Source:** Decomposition DEL-31.04 (line 689)

**Anticipated deliverable artifacts:** Equipment maintenance manuals, spare parts lists

**Source:** _CONTEXT.md; Decomposition line 689

## Requirements

### Functional Requirements

**FR-01: Completeness** — Maintenance Manuals shall cover all facility equipment and systems requiring maintenance.
- **Verification:** See Procedure.md (Step 1.1, Step 6)

**FR-02: Accuracy and Currency** — Manuals shall accurately reflect as-built configurations and validated maintenance procedures.
- **Source:** Cross-reference DEL-31.02, PKG-30

**FR-03: Usability** — Manuals shall be written in clear language for maintenance personnel with appropriate visual aids.

**FR-04: Safety Integration** — Maintenance procedures shall include hazard identification, safety precautions, and lockout/tagout requirements.
- **Source:** OBJ-1 (Safe & Reliable Operation, Decomposition line 59)

**FR-05: Spare Parts Lists** — Comprehensive spare parts lists with part numbers, descriptions, recommended quantities, and sourcing information.

### Performance Requirements

**PR-01: Procedure Quality** — Maintenance procedures shall be complete, accurate, step-by-step instructions with safety cautions.

**PR-02: Document Format and Standards** — Manuals shall comply with project documentation standards; equipment tags aligned with DEL-31.02 and DEL-31.06.

**PR-03: PM Schedule Adequacy** — Preventive maintenance schedules shall be based on vendor recommendations, industry standards, and equipment criticality.

### Interface Requirements

**IR-01: As-Built Drawings Interface** — Reference DEL-31.02 for equipment configurations.

**IR-02: Operation Manuals Interface** — Coordinate with DEL-31.03 for operations vs. maintenance scope boundaries.

**IR-03: Vendor Documentation Interface** — Reference DEL-31.05 for equipment-specific maintenance.

**IR-04: Asset Hierarchy Interface** — Equipment tags aligned with DEL-31.06.

**IR-05: Warranty Interface** — Maintenance procedures shall comply with warranty requirements (DEL-31.08, DEL-31.09).

### Quality Requirements

**QR-01: QMS Compliance** — Comply with project Quality Management Plan.

**QR-02: Technical Review and Approval** — Technical, maintenance personnel, and HSE review required.

**QR-03: Version Control and Updates** — Change management process for lifecycle updates.

### Safety and Regulatory Requirements

**SR-01: Safe & Reliable Operation Support** — Support OBJ-1 by ensuring proper equipment maintenance.
- **Source:** OBJ-1 (Decomposition line 59, line 780)

**SR-02: Regulatory Compliance** — Support equipment inspection and testing per regulatory requirements.

### Lifecycle Requirements

**LR-01: Lifecycle Cost Optimization** — Support OBJ-9 through effective maintenance planning and execution.
- **Source:** OBJ-9 (Decomposition line 67, line 788)

**LR-02: Asset Integrity** — Support long-term asset integrity and equipment lifecycle management.

## Verification

1. **Completeness Review** — All equipment covered
2. **Technical Review** — Accuracy verified
3. **Maintenance Personnel Review** — Usability confirmed
4. **HSE Review** — Safety content approved
5. **PM Schedule Verification** — Schedules validated against vendor recommendations and industry standards

**Acceptance Criteria:** All equipment covered; technical accuracy verified; maintenance personnel confirm usability; Employer acceptance obtained (if required)

## Documentation

**Required outputs:**
1. Equipment Maintenance Manuals (electronic and hard copy)
2. Spare Parts Lists
3. PM Schedule Master List
4. Review and Approval Records

**Filing:** Working documents in `1_Working/`; issued manuals in `3_Issued/`
