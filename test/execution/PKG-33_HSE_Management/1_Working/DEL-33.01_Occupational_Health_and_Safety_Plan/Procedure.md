## Steps

### Section 2: Producing the OHS Plan Document

This section describes the process for developing the OHS Plan document.

#### Step 2.1: Scope Definition and Requirements Review

**Objective:** Define the scope and understand the requirements for the OHS Plan.

**Actions:**
1. Review Employer's Requirements Volume 2 Part 1 (General Requirements) for HSE plan requirements
   - Identify mandatory sections, format, approval process
   - Extract specific HSE performance criteria and acceptance standards
2. Review Project Objectives (OBJ-5, OBJ-6, OBJ-7) and understand alignment
3. Review decomposition for project scope and anticipated hazards across 36 packages
4. Review applicable regulations (WorkSafeBC OH&S Regulation, permits)
5. Review Contractor's corporate HSE management system for corporate requirements
6. Define OHS Plan scope boundaries:
   - **Inclusions:** All Contractor construction activities, subcontractor management, interfaces
   - **Exclusions:** Employer HSE activities, off-site fabrication (subcontractor managed)

**Outputs:**
- OHS Plan requirements register
- Scope statement
- Document outline (sections and subsections)

**Verification:** Requirements review meeting with Employer (if required by ER)

**Source:** Specification Section 2 (Requirements); Decomposition Section 2 (Objectives), Section 5 (Package Details)

#### Step 2.2: Hazard Identification and Risk Profiling

**Objective:** Identify project-specific hazards to inform OHS Plan content.

**Actions:**
1. Conduct kickoff hazard identification workshop with multi-discipline team:
   - Review each package (PKG-00 through PKG-35) for discipline-specific hazards
   - Identify project-specific hazards (marine, rail, heavy lifting, confined space, hazardous substances, electrical, terminal interface, excavation)
2. Assess risk profile for each hazard (likelihood × consequence)
3. Prioritize hazards for detailed treatment in OHS Plan (high-risk activities)
4. Document anticipated control strategies (hierarchy of controls)
5. Cross-reference to DEL-33.02 (Risk Assessments) for detailed risk assessment process

**Outputs:**
- Project hazard register (summary for OHS Plan Annex C)
- High-risk activity list (requiring SWMS, permits, engineered plans)

**Verification:** Hazard identification workshop report, reviewed by Project Manager and HSE Manager

**Source:** Specification FR-4 (Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment); Datasheet Project-Specific Hazards; Guidance Project-Specific Hazard Considerations

#### Step 2.3: OHS Plan Content Development

**Objective:** Develop substantive content for each section of the OHS Plan.

**Actions:**

1. **Section 1: Policy and Commitment**
   - Adapt Contractor's corporate HSE policy to project-specific context
   - Define zero harm objective and project HSE targets (e.g., zero fatalities, LTIF <0.5)
   - Align HSE objectives with project objectives (OBJ-5, OBJ-6, OBJ-7)

2. **Section 2: Organization and Responsibilities**
   - Define HSE organizational structure (matrix model: centralized specialists + embedded coordinators)
   - Develop roles and responsibilities matrix (RACI format) for HSE-critical roles
   - Define competency requirements for HSE-critical roles (cross-reference PKG-35)
   - Develop HSE organizational chart and 24/7 emergency contact list

3. **Section 3: Legal and Regulatory Compliance**
   - Develop regulatory compliance matrix (WorkSafeBC OH&S Regulation Parts vs. project controls)
   - Reference permit register (cross-reference PKG-32)
   - Define regulatory inspection and order response process

4. **Section 4: Risk Management**
   - Define hazard identification process (cross-reference DEL-33.02)
   - Define risk assessment methodology and criteria
   - Document control hierarchy application
   - Include project hazard register (summary)

5. **Section 5: Safe Work Procedures**
   - Define permit-to-work system (hot work, confined space, excavation, work at height, marine operations)
   - Define LOTO procedure
   - Define SWMS requirements (cross-reference DEL-33.03)
   - Develop or reference activity-specific procedures

6. **Section 6: Emergency Preparedness**
   - Cross-reference DEL-33.07 (Emergency Response Plan) for detailed procedures
   - Define emergency response organization and communication protocols
   - Define emergency equipment and facilities requirements
   - Define drill and exercise schedule

7. **Section 7: Training and Competency**
   - Define site induction program content
   - Develop task-specific training matrix (cross-reference PKG-35)
   - Define competency verification process
   - Define toolbox talk schedule and topics

8. **Section 8: Incident Management**
   - Define incident reporting criteria (injuries, near-misses, property damage, environmental)
   - Define investigation methodology (root cause analysis)
   - Define CAPA process and tracking system
   - Define lessons learned distribution process

9. **Section 9: Monitoring and Review**
   - Define inspection program (daily HSE walks, weekly formal inspections, monthly audits)
   - Define HSE performance metrics (LTIF, TRIR, near-miss frequency, leading indicators)
   - Define internal audit program (quarterly, scope, personnel)
   - Define management review process (monthly HSE meetings)

10. **Section 10: Environmental Integration**
    - Cross-reference environmental deliverables (DEL-33.04–33.08)
    - Define interface protocols (environmental incident reporting, spill response)
    - Define Environmental Coordinator role

**Outputs:**
- Draft OHS Plan (all sections)
- Supporting annexes (organizational chart, forms/templates, hazard register, emergency contacts, training matrix)

**Verification:** Section-by-section review by discipline leads and Employer (if required)

**Source:** Specification Section 2 (Requirements FR-1 through FR-10); Datasheet Plan Structure; Guidance Example 1 (Integrated HSE Plan Structure)

#### Step 2.4: Cross-Document Integration

**Objective:** Ensure the OHS Plan is consistent with and properly cross-referenced to supporting deliverables.

**Actions:**
1. Review DEL-33.02 through DEL-33.08 for consistency:
   - DEL-33.02 (Risk Assessments) — verify risk assessment process alignment
   - DEL-33.03 (Method Statements) — verify SWMS requirements alignment
   - DEL-33.04–33.06 (Environmental Records) — verify environmental interface definitions
   - DEL-33.07 (Emergency Response Plan) — verify emergency response integration
   - DEL-33.08 (Waterway Pollution Control) — verify waterway protection measures
2. Review PKG-32 (Regulatory & Permits) for permit-related HSE obligations
3. Review PKG-35 (Training & Handover) for training delivery and record-keeping alignment
4. Update cross-references in OHS Plan to ensure accuracy
5. Verify terminology consistency across all PKG-33 deliverables

**Outputs:**
- Cross-reference verification checklist
- Updated OHS Plan with accurate cross-references

**Verification:** Cross-document consistency review (compare terminology, requirements, processes)

**Source:** Specification Section 2 (Interface Requirements); Cross-document consistency requirement

#### Step 2.5: Review and Approval

**Objective:** Obtain internal and external review and approval for the OHS Plan.

**Actions:**
1. **Internal Review (Contractor):**
   - Technical review by discipline HSE coordinators
   - Constructability review by Construction Manager
   - Regulatory compliance review by regulatory specialist
   - Management review by Project Manager and Project Director
   - Independent check by Contractor's corporate HSE team (if applicable)
2. **External Review (as required by ER):**
   - Submit OHS Plan to Employer for review and comment
   - Address Employer comments and revise OHS Plan
   - Submit to regulatory authorities if required by permits (WorkSafeBC, other)
3. **Approval:**
   - Contractor's Project Director signs OHS Plan policy statement
   - Employer issues acceptance (if required by ER)
   - Record approval in document management system

**Outputs:**
- Reviewed and approved OHS Plan
- Review comment log and disposition
- Approval records

**Verification:** Signed approval page, Employer acceptance letter (if applicable)

**Source:** Specification Section 5 (Verification), Section 6 (Documentation); TBD: Employer's Requirements approval process

#### Step 2.6: Distribution and Communication

**Objective:** Distribute the OHS Plan to relevant stakeholders and communicate key requirements.

**Actions:**
1. Upload approved OHS Plan to project document management system
2. Distribute controlled copies to:
   - All Contractor management and supervision
   - All subcontractors (via subcontract document requirements)
   - Employer (as required by ER)
   - Regulatory authorities (if required by permits)
   - Site HSE notice board (executive summary poster)
3. Conduct OHS Plan rollout briefings:
   - Management briefing (Project Director, Project Manager, Construction Manager, discipline leads)
   - Supervision briefing (site supervisors, HSE coordinators)
   - Workforce communication (site induction, toolbox talks)
4. Incorporate OHS Plan key requirements into site induction program

**Outputs:**
- Distribution register (controlled copy recipients)
- Briefing records (attendance, presentation materials)

**Verification:** Distribution register complete, briefing records on file

**Source:** Specification Section 6 (Documentation); ASSUMPTION: D&B document distribution process

### Section 3: Managing OHS Plan Revisions

**Objective:** Maintain the OHS Plan as a living document throughout the project lifecycle.

**Actions:**
1. **Revision Triggers:**
   - Changes in scope, design, or construction methods affecting hazards or controls
   - Significant incidents requiring changes to HSE controls
   - Regulatory changes or new permit conditions
   - Employer requests or regulatory orders
   - Management review findings (continuous improvement)
   - Periodic review (minimum annually, or as required by ER)

2. **Revision Process:**
   - Identify need for revision (trigger event)
   - HSE Manager initiates revision (document change request)
   - Develop revised content (affected sections only or full reissue)
   - Internal review (as per Step 2.5, proportionate to significance of change)
   - External review if required (Employer, regulatory authorities)
   - Approval and reissue (increment revision number, update document register)
   - Communication (distribute revision, briefings as needed)

3. **Revision Records:**
   - Maintain revision log in OHS Plan (date, revision number, description of changes, approver)
   - Archive superseded versions per document retention requirements

**Outputs:**
- Revised OHS Plan (as needed)
- Revision log

**Verification:** Revision approval records, distribution register updated

**Source:** Specification QR-2 (Plan Maintenance); ASSUMPTION: D&B document revision control

### Section 4: Implementing the OHS Plan (Operation)

This section describes how to implement and operate the OHS Plan during project execution.

#### Step 4.1: Project Mobilization and HSE Setup

**Objective:** Establish HSE infrastructure and systems at project mobilization.

**Actions:**
1. **HSE Personnel Mobilization:**
   - Appoint Project HSE Manager and HSE Coordinators (competency verification per Prerequisites)
   - Mobilize first aid attendants (WorkSafeBC-certified, coverage per Part 3)
   - Establish 24/7 emergency contact protocols
2. **HSE Facilities and Equipment:**
   - Establish site HSE office (if separate from main site office)
   - Procure and install first aid facilities and equipment (per WorkSafeBC Part 3)
   - Procure emergency response equipment (fire extinguishers, spill kits, rescue equipment)
   - Establish HSE notice boards (site entrance, lunch rooms, high-traffic areas)
3. **HSE Systems Setup:**
   - Activate incident management database
   - Activate training management system and populate training matrix
   - Establish permit-to-work system (forms, register, approval workflows)
   - Establish inspection program (schedules, checklists, reporting)
4. **Terminal Interface:**
   - Establish communication protocols with DP World Terminal HSE and operations teams
   - Participate in Terminal HSE induction (Contractor HSE Manager and key personnel)
   - Set up joint HSE coordination meetings (daily and weekly, per Guidance Example 2)

**Outputs:**
- HSE organization established
- HSE facilities and equipment installed and inspected
- HSE systems activated
- Terminal interface protocols confirmed

**Verification:** HSE mobilization checklist, facility inspection records, Terminal coordination meeting schedule

**Source:** Guidance Organizational Considerations; Guidance Terminal Operations Interface; OBJ-5 Terminal Continuity

#### Step 4.2: Site Induction and Training

**Objective:** Ensure all personnel are inducted and trained per OHS Plan requirements.

**Actions:**
1. **Site Induction Program:**
   - Deliver site induction to all personnel before site access (Contractor employees, subcontractors, visitors)
   - Induction content (per OHS Plan Section 7):
     - Project overview and HSE policy
     - Site rules and access control
     - Key hazards and controls
     - Emergency response and muster points
     - Incident reporting
     - Environmental controls (CEMP, SPPP, spill response)
     - Stop-work authority and refusal of unsafe work
   - Record induction attendance (cross-reference PKG-35 Training Records)
2. **Task-Specific Training:**
   - Deliver task-specific training per training matrix (e.g., confined space, working at height, WHMIS)
   - Verify competency for HSE-critical roles (confined space entry supervisor, lift supervisor, etc.)
   - Record training and competency verification (cross-reference DEL-35.02)
3. **Toolbox Talks:**
   - Deliver weekly toolbox talks on relevant HSE topics (upcoming high-risk activities, lessons learned, seasonal hazards)
   - Record toolbox talk attendance

**Outputs:**
- Site induction records (all personnel inducted)
- Task-specific training records
- Competency verification records
- Toolbox talk records

**Verification:** Training records audit (100% induction compliance), competency matrix populated

**Source:** Specification FR-7 (Training and Competency); Procedure Prerequisites (Personnel Requirements); Cross-reference PKG-35

#### Step 4.3: Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment

**Objective:** Continuously identify hazards and assess risks throughout project execution.

**Actions:**
1. **Ongoing Hazard Identification:**
   - Conduct task-based risk assessments for new or changed activities (per Guidance Example 3 trigger criteria)
   - Conduct area-based hazard inspections (weekly formal inspections, daily HSE walks)
   - Review design changes for HSE implications (design review HSE participation)
   - Monitor for emerging hazards (weather, site conditions, adjacent activities)
2. **Risk Assessment Process:**
   - Apply risk assessment methodology per OHS Plan Section 4 and DEL-33.02
   - Evaluate risks using likelihood × consequence matrix
   - Apply control hierarchy (eliminate, substitute, engineer, admin, PPE)
   - Document risk assessments and control measures
   - Obtain approval for residual risks (HSE Manager or Project Manager, based on risk level)
3. **SIMOPS Management:**
   - Identify simultaneous operations (multiple packages in shared work area)
   - Conduct SIMOPS risk assessment
   - Implement additional controls (communication protocols, exclusion zones, designated coordinators)
   - Monitor SIMOPS activities continuously

**Outputs:**
- Task-based risk assessments (cross-reference DEL-33.02)
- Hazard inspection records
- SIMOPS risk assessments and control plans

**Verification:** Risk assessment register maintained, all high-risk activities have approved risk assessments before work commences

**Source:** Specification FR-4 (Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment); Cross-reference DEL-33.02; Guidance Example 3 (Risk Assessment Trigger Criteria)

#### Step 4.4: Safe Work Procedures and Permits

**Objective:** Implement safe work procedures and permit-to-work system for high-risk activities.

**Actions:**
1. **SWMS Development and Approval:**
   - Require SWMS for all high-risk activities per OHS Plan Section 5
   - Review and approve SWMS (HSE Coordinator or HSE Manager, based on risk)
   - Communicate SWMS to workers via toolbox talk
   - Cross-reference DEL-33.03 (Method Statements)
2. **Permit-to-Work System:**
   - Issue permits for specified hazardous tasks:
     - Hot work (welding, cutting, grinding near flammables)
     - Confined space entry (tanks, manholes, deep excavations)
     - Excavation (all excavations >1.2m or near utilities)
     - Work at height (scaffolding, rope access, mobile elevated work platforms)
     - Marine operations (barge work, over-water activities, vessel interface)
   - Verify permit prerequisites (risk assessment, SWMS, competent personnel, equipment inspection, atmospheric testing for confined space)
   - Approve permit (competent person or HSE Coordinator)
   - Monitor compliance with permit conditions
   - Close out permit upon completion
3. **LOTO Procedures:**
   - Implement LOTO for energy isolation (electrical, mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, chemical)
   - Train authorized personnel in LOTO procedures
   - Verify LOTO compliance (locks applied, energy zero-verified, try-out)

**Outputs:**
- Approved SWMS (cross-reference DEL-33.03)
- Issued permits (permit register maintained)
- LOTO records (locks applied, energy verification)

**Verification:** Permit register complete, no high-risk work without approved permit, LOTO audits

**Source:** Specification FR-5 (Safe Work Procedures); Cross-reference DEL-33.03; Guidance Considerations (SIMOPS, Terminal Interface)

#### Step 4.5: Emergency Preparedness

**Objective:** Maintain emergency readiness throughout project execution.

**Actions:**
1. **Emergency Equipment Maintenance:**
   - Inspect first aid equipment (monthly, per WorkSafeBC Part 3)
   - Inspect fire suppression equipment (extinguishers, fire hoses — monthly)
   - Inspect spill response equipment (spill kits, absorbents, containment — monthly)
   - Inspect marine rescue equipment (life rings, throw bags, rescue boat — weekly for marine operations)
   - Maintain inspection records
2. **Emergency Drills:**
   - Conduct emergency drills per OHS Plan Section 6 and DEL-33.07 schedule:
     - Evacuation drill (quarterly)
     - Fire drill (quarterly, coordinated with Terminal)
     - Spill response drill (semi-annually)
     - Confined space rescue drill (before first confined space entry, then quarterly)
     - Marine rescue drill (before first marine operations, then quarterly)
   - Debrief drills and implement improvements
   - Record drill reports
3. **Emergency Response Coordination:**
   - Maintain 24/7 emergency contact list (HSE Manager, Project Manager, First Aid Attendants, external emergency services)
   - Coordinate with Terminal emergency response organization (per Guidance Example 2)
   - Coordinate with external emergency services (fire department, ambulance, Coast Guard)

**Outputs:**
- Emergency equipment inspection records
- Emergency drill reports
- Updated emergency contact list

**Verification:** Equipment inspection records up to date, drill reports complete with corrective actions, emergency contact list current

**Source:** Specification FR-6 (Emergency Preparedness and Response); Cross-reference DEL-33.07; Guidance Terminal Operations Interface

#### Step 4.6: Incident Management

**Objective:** Report, investigate, and learn from incidents and near-misses.

**Actions:**
1. **Incident Reporting:**
   - Report all incidents per OHS Plan Section 8 criteria (injuries, near-misses, property damage, environmental)
   - Immediate notification (verbal) to HSE Manager for serious incidents (injury requiring medical treatment, regulatory reportable events)
   - Written incident report within 24 hours (incident management database)
   - Regulatory notification (WorkSafeBC within required timeframes for serious injuries/fatalities; environmental authorities for spills)
   - Employer notification per ER requirements
2. **Incident Investigation:**
   - Investigate all incidents using root cause analysis (5-Whys, Fishbone, FMEA)
   - Investigation team (HSE Manager, supervisor, worker representative, subject matter experts)
   - Identify root causes (immediate, contributing, underlying)
   - Develop corrective and preventive actions (CAPA)
3. **CAPA Implementation:**
   - Assign CAPA responsibilities and due dates
   - Track CAPA completion (incident management database)
   - Verify CAPA effectiveness (follow-up verification)
4. **Lessons Learned:**
   - Distribute lessons learned (toolbox talks, safety alerts, management meetings)
   - Share lessons learned with Terminal and other projects (as appropriate)

**Outputs:**
- Incident reports (all incidents and near-misses)
- Investigation reports (root cause analysis)
- CAPA register (actions tracked to completion)
- Lessons learned communications

**Verification:** All incidents investigated within 7 days, CAPA completion rate >90%, lessons learned distributed within 14 days

**Source:** Specification FR-8 (Incident Management); Guidance Principles (Zero Harm, Continuous Improvement)

#### Step 4.7: Monitoring, Inspection, and Audit

**Objective:** Continuously monitor HSE performance and verify compliance with the OHS Plan.

**Actions:**
1. **Daily HSE Activities:**
   - HSE Coordinators conduct daily HSE walks (observe work activities, verify permit compliance, inspect housekeeping)
   - Supervisors conduct pre-start briefings (review day's activities, hazards, controls)
   - Workers conduct pre-task hazard assessments (stop and think before task)
2. **Weekly Inspections:**
   - HSE Coordinators conduct formal weekly inspections (use checklists, document findings)
   - Inspection focus areas (housekeeping, PPE compliance, permit compliance, emergency equipment, environmental controls)
   - Issue corrective action notices for non-compliances
   - Track corrective action completion
3. **Monthly Audits:**
   - HSE Manager conducts monthly internal audits (sample packages/activities)
   - Audit against OHS Plan requirements, SWMS, permits, training records
   - Document audit findings and recommendations
   - Present audit results at monthly HSE management review
4. **HSE Performance Metrics:**
   - Track and report HSE KPIs (per OHS Plan Section 9):
     - LTIF (Lost Time Injury Frequency) = (Lost time injuries / Hours worked) × 200,000
     - TRIR (Total Recordable Incident Rate) = (Recordable injuries / Hours worked) × 200,000
     - Near-miss frequency = (Near-misses / Hours worked) × 200,000
     - Leading indicators (inspections completed, training completion %, permit compliance %, corrective action closure rate)
   - Report metrics in monthly project reports and HSE management review
5. **Management Review:**
   - Conduct monthly HSE management review meeting (Project Manager, Construction Manager, HSE Manager, discipline leads)
   - Review: incident trends, KPIs, audit findings, upcoming high-risk activities, resource needs
   - Document decisions and actions

**Outputs:**
- Daily HSE walk records
- Weekly inspection reports and corrective action notices
- Monthly audit reports
- HSE performance metrics (monthly KPI dashboard)
- Management review meeting minutes

**Verification:** Inspection and audit schedules met, corrective actions closed out within target timeframes, KPIs reported monthly

**Source:** Specification FR-9 (Monitoring, Inspection, and Audit); Guidance Considerations (Organizational Structure)

#### Step 4.8: Environmental Integration

**Objective:** Integrate HSE and environmental management throughout project execution.

**Actions:**
1. **Environmental Monitoring:**
   - Coordinate with Environmental Coordinator for CEMP compliance monitoring (DEL-33.04)
   - Coordinate stormwater monitoring and BMP inspections (DEL-33.05)
   - Coordinate waste management (DEL-33.06)
   - Coordinate waterway pollution control measures (DEL-33.08)
2. **Environmental Incident Response:**
   - Report environmental incidents (spills, unauthorized discharges, erosion) per incident management process
   - Implement spill response procedures (DEL-33.08, DEL-33.07)
   - Coordinate with regulatory authorities (environmental spill reporting)
3. **Integrated Inspections:**
   - Conduct combined HSE and environmental inspections (verify both safety and environmental controls)
   - Use integrated checklists (safety + environmental criteria)

**Outputs:**
- Integrated HSE/environmental inspection records
- Environmental incident reports
- Coordination meeting minutes (HSE Manager + Environmental Coordinator)

**Verification:** Environmental compliance confirmed in monthly HSE management review, environmental incidents managed per OHS Plan incident process

**Source:** Specification FR-10 (Environmental Management Interface); Cross-reference DEL-33.04, DEL-33.05, DEL-33.06, DEL-33.07, DEL-33.08; OBJ-7 Environmental Protection
