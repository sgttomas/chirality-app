## Purpose

This procedure defines the process for conducting weekly Terminal coordination meetings and producing **Coordination Meeting Installation & Test Records** within **PKG-34 Coordination & Interfaces**.

**Deliverable type:** Record
**Responsible party:** D&B Contractor (QA/QC)

## Steps

### Step 1: Meeting Preparation (Weekly, 2 days before meeting)

**1.1 Develop Meeting Agenda**
- Review previous meeting minutes and open action items
- Solicit agenda items from key stakeholders (Employer, Terminal Operations, key subcontractors)
- Prioritize agenda items per coordination criticality and time constraints
- Allocate time per agenda item (typical agenda structure per Guidance.md Example 1)
- **TBD** — Agenda approval workflow (if required)

**1.2 Distribute Agenda and Pre-Reads**
- Issue agenda to distribution list at least 2 days before meeting — **TBD**: Confirm distribution timeline
- Include supporting materials (drawings, schedules, reports) as pre-reads if applicable
- Request confirmation of attendance and any additional agenda items
- **Verification:** Confirm agenda distributed and received by key attendees

**1.3 Prepare Meeting Materials**
- Prepare presentation materials (construction progress photos, schedules, coordination drawings)
- Print sign-in sheet and previous meeting minutes
- Set up meeting venue and audiovisual equipment
- Test teleconference/videoconference connection
- **Verification:** Meeting venue ready; materials prepared

### Step 2: Conduct Meeting (Weekly, per schedule)

**2.1 Meeting Opening**
- **Meeting chair** calls meeting to order at scheduled start time
- Circulate sign-in sheet (physical or electronic attendance tracking)
- Review agenda and confirm time allocation
- **Safety moment** — brief safety topic or recent observation (5 min)
- **Verification:** Attendance captured; agenda reviewed

**2.2 Review Previous Minutes and Action Items**
- Briefly review previous meeting minutes (approve if not yet approved)
- Review action item register — responsible parties provide status updates
- Update action item status (open, in progress, closed)
- Identify overdue actions and escalate per project procedures (per Specification FR-5)
- **Verification:** Action item status updated; overdue actions escalated

**2.3 Agenda Item Discussion**
- Work through agenda items in sequence (per Guidance.md Example 1 structure or project-specific agenda)
- **Key discussion areas (per Specification FR-4):**
  - Construction progress and upcoming activities
  - Terminal operations interface and coordination windows
  - Marine access and vessel scheduling coordination
  - Utility outages and shutdowns
  - Traffic management and access control
  - Health, safety, and environmental matters
  - Permit and regulatory compliance updates
  - Interface with adjacent projects or tenants
- **Minutes preparer** captures key discussion points, decisions, and new action items
- **Meeting chair** ensures discussions remain focused and on schedule
- **Verification:** All agenda items addressed; key points and decisions captured

**2.4 Action Item Assignment**
- Identify new action items arising from discussions
- Assign responsible party and due date for each action item (per Specification FR-5)
- Confirm acceptance of action item assignment with responsible party
- Add action items to action item register
- **Verification:** All action items assigned and acknowledged

**2.5 Meeting Closing**
- Summarize key decisions and action items
- Confirm next meeting date, time, and location
- Request any final comments or questions
- Adjourn meeting
- **Verification:** Next meeting scheduled; attendees aware of action commitments

### Step 3: Minutes Preparation and Issuance (Within 2 business days)

**3.1 Draft Minutes Preparation**
- **Minutes preparer** compiles draft minutes using project minutes template — **TBD**: Obtain/develop template
- **Required content (per Specification FR-3):**
  - Meeting identification (number, date, time, location)
  - Attendee roster (from sign-in sheet with affiliations)
  - Agenda items
  - Discussion summary (key points and decisions)
  - Action items (description, responsible party, due date, status)
  - Open issues register (if maintained separately)
  - Upcoming activities and constraints
  - Next meeting details
- Attach supporting materials if applicable (photos, drawings, reports)
- **Verification:** All required content sections completed; action items match register

**3.2 Minutes Review and Correction**
- **Meeting chair** reviews draft minutes for accuracy and completeness
- Distribute draft minutes to attendees for review and comment (optional but recommended) — **TBD**: Confirm review process
- Incorporate corrections and clarifications
- Resolve any discrepancies with attendees
- **Timeline:** Draft minutes prepared within 2 business days of meeting per Specification PR-1 — **TBD**: Confirm timeline
- **Verification:** Minutes reviewed; corrections incorporated

**3.3 Minutes Finalization and Approval**
- **Meeting chair** approves final minutes
- Apply document control numbering and classification per project procedures — **TBD**: Document numbering convention
- Apply electronic signatures or approval stamps per project document control system — **TBD**
- **Verification:** Minutes approved; document control applied

**3.4 Minutes Distribution**
- Issue final minutes to distribution list per project communication plan — **TBD**: Confirm distribution list
- **Distribution recipients (typical):**
  - All meeting attendees
  - D&B Contractor senior management
  - Employer's project management and senior management
  - Terminal operations management
  - Key subcontractors
  - Regulatory authorities (if applicable)
  - Utility representatives (if applicable)
- Confirm distribution via document management system or email tracking
- **Timeline:** Final minutes issued within 5 business days of meeting per Specification PR-1 — **TBD**: Confirm timeline
- **Verification:** Minutes distributed to all required recipients; distribution confirmed

### Step 4: Archival and Action Tracking (Ongoing)

**4.1 Archive Minutes in Document Management System**
- Upload final minutes to project document management system under PKG-34
- Apply appropriate folder structure and metadata (meeting date, meeting number, keywords)
- Ensure minutes are searchable and retrievable
- **TBD** — Hard copy filing (if required per project procedures)
- **Verification:** Minutes archived and retrievable from document management system

**4.2 Update Action Item Register**
- Transfer new action items from minutes to master action item register (if maintained separately)
- Update action item register with status from Step 2.2
- Track action item progress between meetings
- Identify action items approaching due date and send reminders to responsible parties
- Escalate overdue action items per project escalation protocol — **TBD**
- **Verification:** Action item register current; overdue actions escalated

**4.3 Meeting Records Quality Check**
- Periodically review meeting minutes for quality and consistency (per Specification QR-1)
- Ensure all required sections are consistently populated
- Verify action items are being tracked and closed in a timely manner
- Identify opportunities for improving meeting effectiveness or minutes quality
- Document quality check findings and implement improvements
- **TBD** — Quality check frequency and responsible party
- **Verification:** Quality checks conducted; improvement actions identified and implemented

### Step 5: Handover and Closeout (End of construction phase)

**5.1 Compile Complete Meeting Records Package**
- Compile all weekly meeting minutes from project start to completion
- Compile master action item register showing all actions and closure status
- Compile attendance records showing stakeholder participation throughout project
- Verify completeness of records package
- **Verification:** All meeting minutes accounted for; no missing records

**5.2 Final Review and Acceptance**
- Conduct final review of meeting records package for completeness and compliance with Specification requirements
- Obtain acceptance from Employer (if required) — **TBD**: Acceptance protocol
- Address any outstanding items or clarifications
- **Verification:** Meeting records package reviewed and accepted

**5.3 Archive and Handover**
- Transfer complete meeting records package to `3_Issued/` folder
- Archive in project document management system with appropriate retention classification
- Provide copy to Employer for project close-out documentation
- **Retention period:** Per Datasheet Attributes — **TBD**
- **Verification:** Records archived per retention requirements; handover to Employer complete
