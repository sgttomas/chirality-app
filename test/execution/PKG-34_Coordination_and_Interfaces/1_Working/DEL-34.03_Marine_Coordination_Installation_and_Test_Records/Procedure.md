# Procedure: DEL-34.03 Marine Coordination Installation & Test Records

## Purpose

This procedure defines the process for conducting marine coordination activities and producing **Marine Coordination Installation & Test Records** within **PKG-34 Coordination & Interfaces**.

**Deliverable purpose:** Provides evidence of completion, inspection, and testing for marine coordination during construction (per Decomposition line 751, `_CONTEXT.md`).

**Objective alignment:** Supports OBJ-5 "Terminal Continuity" — minimizing disturbance to existing Terminal commercial vessel operations (Decomposition lines 63, 784).

**Deliverable type:** Record
**Responsible party:** D&B Contractor (QA/QC)

## Steps

### Step 1: Marine Coordination Planning (Before marine construction mobilization)

**1.1 Identify Marine Construction Activities**
- Review project schedule for marine construction activities requiring coordination:
  - Barge and work vessel mobilization/demobilization
  - Marine structures construction (berth, fender, mooring)
  - Underwater work or dredging
  - Loading arm installation or maintenance
  - Marine equipment delivery
- Identify navigation hazards and marine access restrictions
- **Verification:** Marine construction activities identified; hazards assessed

**1.2 Develop Marine Coordination Strategy**
- Identify marine stakeholders (Terminal Marine Operations, vessel operators, pilots, VTS, Canadian Coast Guard, Transport Canada)
- Establish marine coordination meeting schedule (frequency based on activity levels)
- Develop Notices to Mariners issuance plan
- Coordinate with general Terminal coordination (DEL-34.01)
- **TBD** — Prepare project marine coordination plan (if required)
- **Verification:** Marine coordination strategy developed; stakeholders identified

**1.3 Obtain Regulatory Approvals**
- Identify required Transport Canada permits (navigable waters protection, marine construction)
- Submit permit applications with adequate lead time — **TBD**
- Coordinate with Canadian Coast Guard for Notices to Mariners protocols
- Coordinate with VTS for vessel traffic management protocols
- **Verification:** Regulatory approvals obtained or in process

### Step 2: Issue Notices to Mariners (Before marine construction activity)

**2.1 Prepare Notice to Mariners**
- **Marine coordinator** prepares Notice to Mariners using standard format (per Guidance.md Example 1)
- **Required content (per Specification FR-2):**
  - Notice number, issue date, effective dates
  - Location (geographic coordinates WGS84, chart datum, chart references)
  - Description of activity or hazard
  - Restrictions or precautions for mariners
  - Contact information (marine coordinator, emergency contact)
- Verify geographic coordinates accuracy
- Attach supporting information (site plan, navigation chart excerpt) if applicable
- **Verification:** Notice complete and accurate

**2.2 Obtain Internal Approval**
- Submit draft Notice to D&B Contractor project manager for review
- Verify consistency with construction schedule
- Obtain approval to issue
- **Verification:** Notice approved for issuance

**2.3 Distribute Notice to Mariners**
- Distribute Notice to (per Specification FR-3):
  - Canadian Coast Guard (for broadcasting via Notices to Mariners system)
  - Vessel Traffic Services (VTS Vancouver or MCTS Comox)
  - Fraser River Pilots
  - Terminal Marine Operations
  - Transport Canada regional office
  - Adjacent berth operators or marine facilities
- **TBD** — Distribution method (email, fax, marine notice distribution system)
- Maintain distribution records (confirmation of receipt)
- **Timeline:** Issue with adequate advance notice per Specification PR-1 — **TBD** (e.g., 72 hours minimum for routine activities)
- **Verification:** Notice distributed; distribution confirmed

**2.4 Monitor and Update Notice**
- Monitor construction activity and maintain Notice accuracy
- Issue updated Notice if construction schedule or location changes
- Issue cancellation Notice promptly upon completion of activity
- **Verification:** Notices current and accurate; cancellation issued when activity complete

### Step 3: Conduct Marine Coordination Meetings (As needed during construction)

**3.1 Prepare for Meeting**
- **Marine coordinator** prepares agenda (per Guidance.md Example 2)
- Solicit agenda items from stakeholders (Terminal Marine Operations, vessel operators)
- Gather supporting materials (construction schedule, vessel schedule, current Notices to Mariners)
- Distribute agenda and supporting materials in advance (2 days)
- **Verification:** Agenda prepared and distributed

**3.2 Conduct Meeting**
- **Marine coordinator** chairs meeting
- Review marine construction progress and upcoming activities
- Coordinate vessel schedules and berth availability
- Review Notices to Mariners (current and upcoming)
- Discuss marine safety and environmental matters
- Capture action items (responsible party, due date)
- **Verification:** Meeting conducted; key decisions and action items documented

**3.3 Prepare and Distribute Minutes**
- Prepare meeting minutes (similar structure to DEL-34.01 coordination meeting minutes)
- Distribute minutes to attendees and stakeholders
- Track action items to closure
- **Timeline:** Issue minutes within 2-5 business days (similar to DEL-34.01)
- **Verification:** Minutes distributed; action items tracked

### Step 4: Coordinate Marine Construction Activities (Ongoing)

**4.1 Daily/Weekly Coordination**
- Coordinate daily marine construction activities with Terminal Marine Operations:
  - Barge movements and positioning
  - Berth access and restrictions
  - Loading arm status and outages
  - Vessel traffic (avoid conflicts with commercial vessels)
- Maintain regular communication via VHF radio, phone, email
- Escalate issues per project coordination protocols
- **Verification:** Daily coordination conducted; conflicts resolved

**4.2 VTS and Pilot Coordination**
- Notify VTS of construction barge positions and movements
- Coordinate with Fraser River Pilots for pilotage constraints
- Monitor vessel traffic via VHF radio (VTS broadcasts)
- Respond promptly to VTS or pilot requests for information
- **Verification:** VTS and pilot coordination maintained

**4.3 Marine Safety Monitoring**
- Inspect marine construction equipment for safety compliance (lighting, marking, seaworthiness)
- Monitor weather and tidal conditions (suspend operations if unsafe)
- Maintain marine safety equipment readiness (life jackets, first aid, spill response)
- Conduct marine safety briefings with construction crews
- **Verification:** Marine safety monitored; equipment compliant

**4.4 Marine Environmental Protection**
- Monitor water quality during in-water construction (turbidity, contaminants)
- Maintain spill response capability and equipment
- Prevent and recover construction debris
- Report and respond to marine environmental incidents
- **Verification:** Environmental protection measures implemented; monitoring conducted

### Step 5: Marine Incident Reporting and Investigation (As needed)

**5.1 Report Marine Incident**
- Immediately report marine incidents (collision, near-miss, equipment failure, environmental spill) to:
  - D&B Contractor project management
  - Terminal Marine Operations
  - VTS (if navigation safety affected)
  - Canadian Coast Guard (if environmental spill or significant incident)
  - Transport Canada (per regulatory requirements)
- Secure incident scene; prevent further damage or injury
- **Verification:** Incident reported to appropriate parties

**5.2 Investigate Incident**
- Conduct incident investigation (per Guidance.md Example 3):
  - Document incident details (date, time, location, description)
  - Identify contributing factors
  - Document immediate actions taken
  - Identify corrective actions to prevent recurrence
- Prepare incident report
- **Verification:** Investigation conducted; report prepared

**5.3 Implement Corrective Actions**
- Implement corrective actions per incident investigation
- Track corrective actions to closure
- Update procedures or coordination protocols if needed
- Communicate lessons learned to project team and stakeholders
- **Verification:** Corrective actions implemented; lessons learned documented

### Step 6: Compile and Archive Marine Coordination Records (Ongoing and at project closeout)

**6.1 Compile Marine Coordination Records**
- Compile all marine coordination records:
  - Notices to Mariners (issued, updated, cancelled)
  - Marine coordination meeting minutes
  - Vessel schedules and berth coordination records
  - Marine construction work permits
  - Marine equipment mobilization/demobilization records
  - Berth and loading arm outage schedules
  - VTS and pilot coordination records
  - Marine environmental monitoring records
  - Marine incident reports and investigations
  - Regulatory correspondence (Transport Canada, Canadian Coast Guard, VTS)
- Verify completeness
- **Verification:** Records compiled; completeness verified

**6.2 Archive Records**
- Archive marine coordination records in project document management system under PKG-34
- Apply retention classification per Specification QR-2 and Transport Canada requirements — **TBD** (typically 7 years for marine safety records)
- Provide copy to Employer for project closeout documentation
- **Verification:** Records archived per retention requirements
