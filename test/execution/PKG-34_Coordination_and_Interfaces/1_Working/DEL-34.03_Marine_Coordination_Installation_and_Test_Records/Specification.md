# Specification: DEL-34.03 Marine Coordination Installation & Test Records

## Scope

This specification defines the requirements for **Marine Coordination Installation & Test Records** within **PKG-34 Coordination & Interfaces**.

**Purpose:** Provides evidence of completion, inspection, and testing for marine coordination activities during construction (per Decomposition line 751, `_CONTEXT.md`).

**Objective alignment:** Supports OBJ-5 "Terminal Continuity" — minimizing disturbance to existing Terminal commercial activities, including marine vessel operations (Decomposition lines 63, 784).

**Anticipated deliverable artifacts:**
- Notices to Mariners (NOTMARs)
- Marine coordination records

**Package scope context:** Marine coordination subset of PKG-34 (Decomposition line 743).

**Inclusions:**
- Notices to Mariners issuance and management
- Marine coordination meetings and records
- Vessel traffic and berth operations coordination
- Marine safety and navigation compliance
- Regulatory coordination (Transport Canada, Canadian Coast Guard, VTS)

**Exclusions:**
- Marine structures design and construction (PKG-08)
- Marine equipment technical specifications (PKG-09, PKG-11)
- General Terminal coordination (DEL-34.01)

## Requirements

### Functional Requirements

**FR-1: Notices to Mariners**
- Notices to Mariners shall be issued for all construction activities that:
  - Create navigation hazards (barges, work vessels, floating equipment)
  - Restrict marine access or navigation (construction zones, exclusion areas)
  - Affect vessel operations (berth outages, loading arm outages, mooring equipment work)
  - Involve underwater work or dredging
- Notices shall comply with Canadian Coast Guard and Transport Canada protocols
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION**: Transport Canada marine safety requirements; **TBD**: Employer's Requirements — **location TBD**

**FR-2: Notice Content**
- Each Notice to Mariners shall include:
  - Notice number, issue date, effective dates
  - Location (geographic coordinates, chart datum, navigation chart references)
  - Description of activity or hazard
  - Restrictions or precautions for mariners
  - Contact information (D&B Contractor marine coordinator, emergency contact)
  - Cancellation notice when activity complete
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION**: Canadian Coast Guard Notices to Mariners format; Datasheet Construction section

**FR-3: Notice Distribution**
- Notices to Mariners shall be distributed to:
  - Canadian Coast Guard (for broadcasting)
  - Vessel Traffic Services (VTS Vancouver or MCTS Comox)
  - Fraser River Pilots
  - Terminal Marine Operations
  - Transport Canada regional office
  - Adjacent berth operators or marine facilities
- **TBD** — Distribution list per project marine coordination plan
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION**: Marine stakeholder engagement per ISO 14001

**FR-4: Marine Coordination Meetings**
- Regular marine coordination meetings shall be conducted as needed (frequency based on marine construction activity levels)
- Meetings shall include:
  - D&B Contractor (project management, marine coordinator)
  - Terminal Marine Operations
  - Vessel operators / charterers (as applicable)
  - Fraser River Pilots (as applicable)
  - VTS / Canadian Coast Guard (as applicable)
  - Transport Canada (as applicable)
- **TBD** — Meeting frequency and attendees per project marine coordination plan
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION**: Marine coordination best practice; relation to DEL-34.01

**FR-5: Marine Coordination Records**
- Marine coordination records shall document:
  - Marine coordination meeting minutes
  - Vessel schedules and berth availability coordination
  - Marine construction work permits
  - Marine equipment mobilization/demobilization
  - Berth and loading arm outage schedules
  - Marine traffic management
  - Marine environmental monitoring
  - Marine incidents and near-misses
  - Regulatory correspondence and approvals
- **Source:** Datasheet Construction section; **ASSUMPTION**: Marine safety documentation per ISO 45001

### Performance Requirements

**PR-1: Notice Timeliness**
- Notices to Mariners shall be issued with adequate advance notice:
  - **TBD** — Minimum advance notice period (e.g., 72 hours for routine activities, 7 days for significant restrictions)
  - Emergency notices may be issued with shorter notice
- Cancellation notices shall be issued promptly upon completion of activity
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION**: Marine safety best practice; **TBD**: Transport Canada requirements

**PR-2: Record Accuracy**
- Marine coordination records shall accurately reflect activities, decisions, and commitments
- Geographic coordinates shall be accurate to required precision
- Vessel schedules and berth availability shall be current and verified
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION**: Marine safety and navigation accuracy requirements

**PR-3: Completeness**
- All marine construction activities requiring coordination shall be documented
- No marine safety incidents shall go unreported
- All regulatory submittals and correspondence shall be tracked
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION**: Quality management and marine safety regulatory compliance

### Interface Requirements

**IR-1: General Terminal Coordination Integration**
- Marine coordination shall be integrated with general Terminal coordination (DEL-34.01):
  - Marine topics discussed in weekly Terminal coordination meetings
  - Marine-specific issues escalated to dedicated marine coordination meetings
  - Consistent communication protocols and stakeholder engagement
- **Source:** Relation to DEL-34.01; **ASSUMPTION**: Integrated coordination approach

**IR-2: Marine Construction Packages Coordination**
- Marine coordination records shall support marine construction activities:
  - PKG-08 (Marine Structures) — berth, fender, mooring construction
  - PKG-09 (Marine Outfitting) — fender systems, mooring equipment, navigation aids
  - PKG-11 (Marine Loading System) — loading arm installation and commissioning
- Construction schedules and marine coordination requirements aligned
- **Source:** PKG-34 and marine construction packages scope

**IR-3: Regulatory Compliance**
- Marine coordination shall comply with regulatory requirements:
  - Transport Canada (marine safety, navigation, vessel operations)
  - Canadian Coast Guard (Notices to Mariners, navigation aids, environmental response)
  - Vessel Traffic Services (vessel traffic management protocols)
  - Fraser River Pilots (pilotage coordination)
- **TBD** — Specific regulatory requirements and approval processes
- **Source:** **ASSUMPTION**: Canadian marine safety regulatory framework; Datasheet References
