# Specification: DEL-05.02 Concrete Structures Technical Specification

## Scope

This deliverable defines **performance, materials, and workmanship requirements** for concrete structures within PKG-05, per the Employer's Requirements. (Source: test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:233.)

**Package scope context:** foundations, containment walls, equipment pads, thrust blocks, ground liner system. (Source: test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:226.)

**Project context:** Canola Oil Transload Facility Phase 1, Fraser Surrey Terminal, Surrey, BC. Design & Build contract. Storage capacity 45,000 MT (3 × 15,000 MT tanks). (Source: test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:10; test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:44; test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:41-42.)

**Anticipated artifacts within this technical specification:**
- Concrete specification (materials, placement, curing, durability)
- Reinforcement specification (materials, fabrication, placement, inspection)
- Formwork specification (materials, tolerances, finishes, embedded items)

(Source: test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:233; Datasheet.md: Construction.)

**Exclusions:**
- Employer-responsible items except for interface connections. (Source: test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:49.)
- Building interior concrete work (covered under PKG-21/PKG-22 if applicable).
- Marine structures concrete (covered under PKG-08 if applicable).

## Requirements

### Functional Requirements

**FR-01: Specification Artifact Coverage**
The technical specification shall include three core sections:
1. Concrete specification (materials, placement, curing, durability, quality control)
2. Reinforcement specification (materials, fabrication, placement, inspection)
3. Formwork specification (materials, tolerances, finishes, embedded items)
Each section shall include requirements, acceptance criteria, and verification methods.
(Source: test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:233; Datasheet.md: Construction.)

**FR-02: Concrete Materials and Mix Design**
The concrete specification shall define:
- Concrete materials (cement type, aggregate grading/sources, water quality, admixtures)
- Mix design requirements (compressive strength by element type, durability requirements, workability/slump)
- Proportioning and batching requirements (tolerances, mixing procedures, batch documentation)
- Concrete strength by element type:
  - Tank foundations (TBD from design calculations)
  - Containment walls (TBD from design calculations)
  - Equipment pads (TBD from design calculations)
  - Thrust blocks (TBD from design calculations)
(Source: Datasheet.md: Construction; test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:226; test/Volume_2_Part_2_Employers_Requirements.pdf, location TBD.)

**FR-03: Durability and Exposure Requirements**
The concrete specification shall define durability requirements based on exposure classifications:
- Exposure class assignments by element type and location (freeze-thaw, de-icing salts, sulfates, chlorides, marine environment)
- Water-cement ratio limits by exposure class
- Air entrainment requirements for freeze-thaw protection
- Minimum cover requirements by exposure class and element type
- Special durability requirements for BC coastal location
- Containment-related durability requirements (OBJ-7 Environmental Protection)
**TBD:** Specific exposure class definitions and requirements from Employer's Requirements Volume 2 Part 2 concrete section.
(Source: test/Volume_2_Part_2_Employers_Requirements.pdf, location TBD; Datasheet.md: Conditions; test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:786; Guidance.md: Principles.)

**FR-04: Concrete Placement and Consolidation**
The concrete specification shall define:
- Delivery and handling requirements (delivery time limits, discharge requirements)
- Placement procedures (sequence, lift heights, cold joints, construction joints)
- Consolidation requirements (vibration procedures, access, over-vibration prevention)
- Placement of concrete for containment structures (special requirements for joints, waterstops, liner interfaces)
- Hot weather concreting requirements (temperature limits, cooling procedures, protection)
- Cold weather concreting requirements (temperature limits, heating procedures, protection)
(Source: Datasheet.md: Construction; test/Volume_2_Part_2_Employers_Requirements.pdf, location TBD.)

**FR-05: Finishing and Curing Requirements**
The concrete specification shall define:
- Finishing requirements by element type and surface (smooth finish, rough finish, formed surfaces)
- Curing requirements (methods, duration, protection) by exposure class
- Seasonal curing requirements for BC climate
- Special finishing/curing for equipment interfaces (equipment pads, anchor bolt areas)
- Special finishing/curing for containment surfaces (OBJ-7 Environmental Protection)
(Source: Datasheet.md: Construction; test/Volume_2_Part_2_Employers_Requirements.pdf, location TBD; test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:786.)

**FR-06: Concrete Quality Control and Testing**
The concrete specification shall define quality control requirements:
- Testing frequencies (slump, air content, temperature, cylinder sampling) per placement volume or element type
- Acceptance criteria (minimum strength, maximum w/c ratio, air content range, slump range)
- Sampling procedures and cylinder handling/curing
- Defect identification and repair procedures
- Rejection criteria and corrective actions
- Hold points for inspection before placement
**Note:** Requirements must enable generation of DEL-05.04 (pour records, cylinder test results).
(Source: Datasheet.md: Construction; test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:235; test/Volume_2_Part_2_Employers_Requirements.pdf, location TBD.)

**FR-07: Reinforcement Materials and Standards**
The reinforcement specification shall define:
- Reinforcing steel grades and types (likely CSA G30.18 or equivalent for BC location — **ASSUMPTION**)
- Material certifications and mill test reports
- Epoxy-coated or corrosion-resistant reinforcement requirements (if required for exposure/containment)
- Bar identification and marking requirements
- Storage and protection requirements (prevent corrosion, contamination)
(Source: Datasheet.md: Construction; Datasheet.md: Attributes; test/Volume_2_Part_2_Employers_Requirements.pdf, location TBD.)

**FR-08: Reinforcement Fabrication and Placement**
The reinforcement specification shall define:
- Fabrication requirements (bending tolerances, cutting procedures, threading if applicable)
- Placement tolerances (cover, spacing, location)
- Support requirements (chairs, bolsters, spacers, ties)
- Lap splice requirements (lengths, locations, stagger) by bar size and concrete strength
- Mechanical coupler requirements (types, locations, testing, acceptance)
- Welding requirements if applicable (procedures, qualifications, inspection)
- Special placement requirements for congested areas
(Source: Datasheet.md: Construction; test/Volume_2_Part_2_Employers_Requirements.pdf, location TBD; Guidance.md: Considerations.)

**FR-09: Reinforcement Inspection and Hold Points**
The reinforcement specification shall define:
- Placement inspection requirements (cover verification, spacing, support, lap splices)
- Hold points before concrete placement (rebar inspection complete and accepted)
- Inspection documentation requirements (enable generation of DEL-05.04 rebar inspection records)
- Acceptance criteria for placement tolerances
- Corrective action procedures for non-conformances
(Source: Datasheet.md: Construction; test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:235; test/Volume_2_Part_2_Employers_Requirements.pdf, location TBD.)

**FR-10: Formwork Materials and Design**
The formwork specification shall define:
- Formwork materials (wood, steel, plastic, composite) and design requirements
- Formwork strength and stiffness requirements (prevent deflection, bulging)
- Form coatings and release agents (compatibility with concrete, surface finish requirements)
- Form cleaning and reuse requirements
(Source: Datasheet.md: Construction; test/Volume_2_Part_2_Employers_Requirements.pdf, location TBD.)

**FR-11: Formwork Tolerances and Finishes**
The formwork specification shall define:
- Dimensional tolerances for formed surfaces (alignment, plumbness, levelness, dimensions)
- Surface finish requirements by element type:
  - Smooth finish (equipment pads, exposed surfaces)
  - Rough finish (buried surfaces, general construction)
  - Form-liner textures if applicable (architectural elements)
- Chamfers, rustication strips, and architectural features if applicable
- Finish acceptance criteria and remediation for defects
(Source: Datasheet.md: Construction; test/Volume_2_Part_2_Employers_Requirements.pdf, location TBD.)

**FR-12: Embedded Items and Formwork Removal**
The formwork specification shall define:
- Embedded items installation requirements (sleeves, blockouts, anchor bolts, plates, inserts)
- Coordinate embedded items with other disciplines (PKG-14 piping, PKG-17 electrical, etc.)
- Formwork removal (stripping times by element type, procedures, surface protection)
- Re-shoring requirements if applicable
- Inspection requirements for forms before concrete placement (hold point)
- Inspection requirements for embedded items before concrete placement (hold point)
(Source: Datasheet.md: Construction; test/Volume_2_Part_2_Employers_Requirements.pdf, location TBD; test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:49.)

### Performance Requirements

**PR-01: Durability Performance**
The specification shall specify durability performance requirements to support project lifecycle:
- Concrete shall meet durability requirements for BC coastal environment (freeze-thaw, marine exposure if applicable)
- Reinforcement shall be protected from corrosion through adequate cover and concrete quality
- Containment structures shall maintain integrity and prevent leakage throughout design life (OBJ-7 Environmental Protection)
- All requirements shall support OBJ-9 Lifecycle Cost Optimization through durable materials and construction quality
(Source: test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:786; test/Volume_2_Part_2_Employers_Requirements.pdf, location TBD; Datasheet.md: Conditions.)

**PR-02: Equipment Interface Performance**
The specification shall specify tolerances and finish requirements appropriate for equipment interfaces:
- Equipment pad surface tolerances (flatness, levelness, elevation control)
- Anchor bolt installation tolerances (location, projection, plumbness)
- Grout pocket requirements (size, surface preparation, bonding)
- Surface preparation for equipment mounting
- Vibration isolation requirements if applicable (**TBD**)
(Source: test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:226; Datasheet.md: Construction; test/Volume_2_Part_2_Employers_Requirements.pdf, location TBD.)

**PR-03: Containment Integrity**
The specification shall specify containment-related requirements to support OBJ-7 Environmental Protection:
- Joint details and construction (construction joints, control joints, expansion joints)
- Waterstop installation and testing requirements
- Penetration sealing requirements
- Liner interface requirements (surface preparation, termination details)
- Special quality control for containment structures (enhanced inspection, testing, acceptance)
(Source: test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:786; test/Canola_Oil_Transload_Facility_Decomposition_REVISED_v2.md:226; Guidance.md: Principles.)
