# Procedure: DEL-26.01 Coating Technical Specification

## Purpose

This procedure defines the process for **producing** the **Coating Technical Specification** deliverable within **PKG-26 Protective Coatings & Painting**.

## Steps

### Step 1: Requirements Gathering

**Objective:** Compile all applicable requirements for coating systems from source documents.

**Activities:**

1.1. **Review Employer's Requirements (ER):**
- Extract coating-specific requirements (performance, materials, testing)
- Identify mandatory vs. guidance requirements
- Note any product restrictions or approvals
- **TBD** — ER reference location

1.2. **Review Design Basis and Environmental Conditions:**
- Obtain corrosivity classification (ISO 12944 category)
- Obtain environmental data (temperature range, humidity, marine exposure)
- Obtain design life requirement
- Source: DEL-27.01 or equivalent design basis documents

1.3. **Review Applicable Standards:**
- Confirm applicable SSPC, NACE, ISO 12944, FDA/NSF standards
- Obtain current versions of referenced standards
- Note any project-specific deviations or clarifications

1.4. **Coordinate with Related Disciplines:**
- **PKG-13 (Storage Tanks):** Tank internal geometry, access, food-contact requirements
- **PKG-06 (Structural Steelwork):** Shop vs. field coating responsibilities, galvanizing interfaces
- **PKG-08/09/11 (Marine):** Marine exposure zone definitions, fender/loading arm coating needs
- **PKG-05 (Concrete):** Steel-to-concrete interface coating requirements

1.5. **Identify Coating System Options:**
- Research coating systems appropriate for each application (tank internal, tank external, structural steel, marine)
- Obtain manufacturer product data sheets for reference systems
- Verify food-grade certifications for tank internal coatings

**Deliverable from Step 1:** Requirements compilation (may be informal working document or formal requirements register per project practice)

**Source:** Specification.md (Requirements section); Guidance.md (Considerations)

### Step 2: Draft Preparation

**Objective:** Prepare initial draft specification documents using project template and compiled requirements.

**Activities:**

2.1. **Establish Document Structure:**
- Use project specification template — **TBD** — or create structure per industry practice
- Typical sections:
  - Scope and applicability
  - Referenced standards and documents
  - Material requirements
  - Surface preparation requirements
  - Application requirements
  - Inspection and testing requirements
  - Acceptance criteria
  - Submittals and documentation

2.2. **Draft Tank Internal/External Coating Specification:**
- **Scope:** Storage tanks per PKG-13
- **Internal coatings:**
  - Food-grade compliance requirements (FDA/NSF)
  - Material specifications (performance-based)
  - Surface preparation (likely SSPC-SP 10 or SP-5)
  - DFT requirements
  - Inspection and testing (adhesion, DFT, holiday detection)
- **External coatings:**
  - Atmospheric corrosion protection per ISO 12944 corrosivity category
  - Material specifications
  - Surface preparation
  - DFT requirements
  - Inspection and testing

2.3. **Draft Structural Steel Coating Specification:**
- **Scope:** Structural steel per PKG-06
- **Shop coatings:**
  - Define shop coating system and responsibilities
  - Surface preparation requirements
  - DFT requirements
  - Handling and touch-up requirements
- **Field coatings:**
  - Define field coating system (full application or touch-up only)
  - Surface preparation requirements
  - Environmental controls during application
  - DFT requirements
- **Inspection and testing**

2.4. **Draft Marine Coating Requirements (if separate or included):**
- Define immersion, splash, and atmospheric zone coating systems
- Coordinate with PKG-08/09/11 design deliverables
- **TBD** — May be integrated into structural steel spec or separate section

2.5. **Cite All Requirements:**
- Provide traceability to source documents (ER, standards, design basis)
- Mark items **TBD** where information is not yet available
- Label **ASSUMPTION** where engineering judgment is applied pending confirmation

**Deliverable from Step 2:** Draft specification documents ready for internal review

**Source:** Datasheet.md (Construction section for coating types); Specification.md (Requirements and Standards sections); Guidance.md (Examples)

### Step 3: Internal Technical Review

**Objective:** Ensure technical accuracy, completeness, and standards compliance.

**Activities:**

3.1. **Coatings Discipline Self-Check:**
- Verify all requirements are traceable to sources
- Verify standards are correctly cited
- Check internal consistency (no contradictions)
- Verify completeness (all coating applications addressed)

3.2. **Interdisciplinary Review:**
- Circulate draft to related disciplines:
  - PKG-05 (Concrete Structures)
  - PKG-06 (Structural Steelwork)
  - PKG-08 (Marine Structures)
  - PKG-09 (Marine Outfitting)
  - PKG-11 (Marine Loading System)
  - PKG-13 (Storage Tanks)
- Collect comments on interfaces, coordination items, and conflicts
- Hold coordination meetings if needed

3.3. **QA/QC Review:**
- Verify compliance with project quality procedures
- Verify standards compliance
- Verify requirements traceability
- Check document format and numbering

3.4. **Document Review Comments:**
- Compile all review comments
- Categorize by severity (mandatory vs. advisory)
- Assign responses

**Deliverable from Step 3:** Reviewed draft with comment register

**Source:** Specification.md (Verification section)

### Step 4: Comment Resolution and Revision

**Objective:** Address all review comments and revise specification accordingly.

**Activities:**

4.1. **Resolve Technical Comments:**
- Address each comment with accept/reject/modify response
- Revise specification text as needed
- Escalate unresolved conflicts to discipline lead or project management

4.2. **Resolve Interdisciplinary Comments:**
- Coordinate interface requirements with adjacent disciplines
- Update specification to reflect agreed coordination
- Document interface agreements in comment responses

4.3. **Update Traceability:**
- Ensure all revisions maintain traceability to source requirements
- Update **TBD** and **ASSUMPTION** labels as information becomes available

4.4. **Prepare Revised Draft:**
- Incorporate all accepted revisions
- Update revision number per project document control
- Prepare comment disposition report

**Deliverable from Step 4:** Revised specification with comment disposition

**Source:** Standard engineering review and comment resolution process

### Step 5: Employer Review (if required)

**Objective:** Obtain Employer review and approval per project requirements.

**Activities:**

5.1. **Submit to Employer:**
- Package specification documents per Employer submittal requirements — **TBD**
- Include supporting documentation (traceability matrix, referenced standards list)
- Track submission in document control system

5.2. **Address Employer Comments:**
- Review Employer comments and categorize
- Prepare responses and revisions
- Coordinate with Employer to resolve open items
- Update specification per approved resolutions

5.3. **Obtain Employer Approval:**
- Submit revised specification for approval
- Obtain approval documentation per project requirements

**Deliverable from Step 5:** Employer-approved specification (if approval required) or specification with Employer comments incorporated

**Source:** **ASSUMPTION** — Typical D&B project process; verify with project-specific submittal procedures — **TBD**

### Step 6: Finalization and Issue

**Objective:** Finalize specification for use in downstream deliverables and construction.

**Activities:**

6.1. **Final Quality Check:**
- Verify all comments resolved
- Verify document formatting and numbering
- Verify all cross-references are correct
- Verify signature/approval blocks complete

6.2. **Issue for Use:**
- Update document status to "Issued" or equivalent per project lifecycle
- Place issued copy in `3_Issued/` folder (per project convention)
- Update document register and distribution list
- Notify downstream deliverable owners (DEL-26.02, DEL-26.03, DEL-26.04)

6.3. **Update Deliverable Status:**
- Update `_STATUS.md` to reflect issuance
- Record issue date and revision

**Deliverable from Step 6:** Issued coating technical specification ready for implementation

**Source:** Project document control procedures; README.md (lifecycle states)
