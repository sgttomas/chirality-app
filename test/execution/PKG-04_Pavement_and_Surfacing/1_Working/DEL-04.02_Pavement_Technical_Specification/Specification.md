# Specification: DEL-04.02 Pavement Technical Specification

## Scope

### Included
This specification document defines the technical requirements for producing the Pavement Technical Specification (DEL-04.02), which establishes performance, materials, and workmanship requirements for all pavement and surfacing work shown in DEL-04.01 and analyzed in DEL-04.03.

The specification shall cover:
- Material requirements for asphalt, concrete, aggregates, admixtures, line marking materials
- Workmanship requirements for subgrade preparation, base/subbase placement, asphalt paving, concrete placement, line marking application
- Quality assurance/quality control requirements including testing frequencies, methods, and acceptance criteria
- Interface coordination requirements with adjacent packages (PKG-03, PKG-05, PKG-07, PKG-08)
- OBJ-8 future expandability provisions for Phase 2 expansion corridors
- Submittal, documentation, and recordkeeping requirements supporting DEL-04.04

### Excluded
- Pavement design calculations and structural analysis (covered by DEL-04.03)
- Graphical representation of pavement layout, sections, and details (covered by DEL-04.01)
- Field inspection and test records (covered by DEL-04.04)
- Underground utility construction specifications (covered by PKG-03 deliverables)

## Requirements

### R1: Specification Content Requirements

#### R1.1: Material Requirements Section
**Requirement**: The specification shall include material requirements for:

**Asphalt Materials**:
- Performance Grade (PG) asphalt cement binder grade suitable for Fraser Surrey, BC climate (reference Datasheet **ASSUMPTION**: PG 58-34)
- Aggregate gradation for wearing course, binder course, and base course (reference **TBD**: OPSS or MMCD gradation specification)
- Aggregate durability requirements (Los Angeles abrasion test, soundness test, fractured faces)
- Filler material (mineral filler, lime, etc. if used in mix design)
- Recycled asphalt pavement (RAP) content limits (if permitted by ER)
- Tack coat and prime coat materials

**Concrete Materials**:
- Portland cement type and strength class (reference Datasheet **ASSUMPTION**: 30-35 MPa 28-day strength)
- Aggregate gradation and quality requirements (coarse and fine aggregates)
- Admixtures (air-entraining agent for freeze-thaw durability, water reducers, etc.)
- Reinforcement (welded wire mesh or fiber reinforcement – reference **TBD** from DEL-04.03)
- Joint sealant and backer rod materials
- Curing compounds (membrane-forming curing compound or alternative methods)

**Base/Subbase Materials**:
- Aggregate gradation, plasticity index, and soundness requirements
- Moisture-density relationship (Standard Proctor) and optimum moisture content

**Line Marking Materials**:
- Paint or thermoplastic material specifications (reference Datasheet **ASSUMPTION**: thermoplastic for high-traffic, paint for low-traffic)
- Color specifications (reference **TBD**: specific color codes)
- Retroreflective glass bead specifications (reference Datasheet **ASSUMPTION**: Type I)
- Primer materials for surface preparation

**Source**: Datasheet "Key Performance Requirements" section; Employer's Requirements Volume 2 Part 2 (**location TBD**)
**Verification**: Guidance material clause review checklist; cross-check material properties against DEL-04.03 design inputs

#### R1.2: Execution/Workmanship Requirements Section
**Requirement**: The specification shall include workmanship requirements for:

**Subgrade Preparation**:
- Clearing, grubbing, and stripping topsoil (if required)
- Subgrade excavation or fill to establish design grades per DEL-04.01
- Moisture conditioning to achieve optimum moisture content (±2% typically – **ASSUMPTION**)
- Compaction requirements (reference Datasheet **ASSUMPTION**: 95% Standard Proctor for top 300 mm)
- Proof rolling procedure with loaded truck (tandem-axle, minimum gross weight **TBD**)
- Correction of soft spots, pumping areas, or rutting identified during proof rolling

**Base and Subbase Placement**:
- Layer placement thickness limits (maximum loose lift thickness prior to compaction)
- Moisture control during placement (prevent segregation, maintain gradation)
- Compaction requirements (reference Datasheet **ASSUMPTION**: 98% Standard Proctor)
- Surface preparation and tack coat (if required between lifts)
- Grade and cross-slope tolerances per DEL-04.01 drawings

**Asphalt Paving**:
- Mix temperature requirements at plant, during transport, and at placement
- Weather limitations (ambient temperature, surface temperature, precipitation, wind)
- Placement procedures (paver operation, screed adjustment, compaction pattern)
- Compaction equipment (roller types, number of passes, rolling pattern)
- Compaction density requirements (reference Datasheet **ASSUMPTION**: 95% TMD wearing/binder, 93% TMD base)
- Joint construction (longitudinal joints, transverse joints, construction joints)
- Surface smoothness tolerances (reference **TBD** from Datasheet)
- Edge treatment and tack coat application

**Concrete Placement**:
- Formwork and grade control (screed rails, string lines, laser screeds)
- Concrete mixing, transporting, and placement procedures
- Finishing procedures (screeding, floating, troweling, texturing)
- Joint construction (control joints, construction joints, expansion joints) per DEL-04.01 details
- Curing procedures and duration (reference Datasheet **ASSUMPTION**: 7-day moist curing or membrane curing compound)
- Protection of finished surface during curing period

**Line Marking Application**:
- Surface preparation (cleaning, drying, priming if required)
- Application equipment and procedures (spray equipment, extrusion equipment for thermoplastic)
- Material application rate and thickness (reference Datasheet **TBD**)
- Retroreflective bead application (broadcast rate, embedment)
- Weather limitations (ambient temperature >10°C, surface dry, no rain within 4 hours – **ASSUMPTION**)
- Traffic control and protection during curing/drying

**Source**: Datasheet "Construction" and "Key Performance Requirements" sections; OPSS or MMCD workmanship standards (**TBD** specific clause references)
**Verification**: Guidance workmanship clause review checklist; verify consistency with DEL-04.01 drawing notes and tolerances

#### R1.3: Quality Control Requirements Section
**Requirement**: The specification shall include quality control requirements for:

**Material Testing**:
- Aggregate gradation testing (frequency: per truckload or per **TBD** volume)
- Asphalt mix design verification (Marshall stability, flow, density, air voids – frequency: per **TBD** tonnage)
- Concrete strength testing (cylinder compression tests – frequency: per **TBD** volume, minimum 3 cylinders per test)
- Concrete air content testing (fresh concrete air meter – frequency: per truck load or per **TBD** volume)

**In-Place Testing**:
- Subgrade compaction testing (nuclear density gauge or sand cone – frequency: reference Datasheet **ASSUMPTION**: 1 per 500 m² per lift)
- Base/subbase compaction testing (frequency: 1 per 500 m² per lift – **ASSUMPTION**)
- Asphalt pavement compaction testing (nuclear density gauge or cores – frequency: 1 per 500 m² per lift – **ASSUMPTION**)
- Asphalt pavement thickness verification (cores – frequency: reference Datasheet **ASSUMPTION**: 1 per 2000 m²)
- Concrete pavement thickness verification (cores or GPR – frequency: 1 per 2000 m² – **ASSUMPTION**)
- Surface smoothness testing (straightedge or profilograph – frequency: 100% coverage or per **TBD**)

**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Material test results meeting specified requirements (gradation within specification limits, strength exceeding minimum, air content within range)
- Compaction density meeting specified percentage (95% subgrade, 98% base, 95% asphalt – per Datasheet **ASSUMPTION**)
- Thickness within specified tolerance (reference **TBD** tolerance from DEL-04.01)
- Surface smoothness within specified tolerance (reference **TBD** from Datasheet)

**Non-Conformance Procedures**:
- Identification and documentation of non-conforming work
- Proposed corrective action (rework, overlay, grinding, reconstruction, acceptance with reduced payment)
- Re-testing after corrective action
- Final disposition and acceptance decision

**Documentation Requirements** (supporting DEL-04.04):
- Daily quality control reports with test locations, results, and acceptance status
- Material certifications and test reports
- Mix design submittals and approval records
- Compaction test location plans (tied to survey coordinates or station/offset from DEL-04.01 plans)
- Core sample locations and test results
- Non-conformance reports and corrective action records

**Source**: Datasheet "Testing and Acceptance" section; Employer's Requirements QA/QC requirements (**location TBD**)
**Verification**: Guidance testing clause review checklist; verify testing frequencies and methods are feasible and sufficient for acceptance decision-making

#### R1.4: Interface Coordination Requirements Section
**Requirement**: The specification shall include interface coordination requirements for:

**PKG-03 (Underground Utilities) Interfaces**:
- Utility trench backfill compaction requirements (must match pavement subgrade compaction to prevent differential settlement)
- Pavement restoration over utility trenches (sequence, materials, thickness, testing)
- Catch basin and manhole rim elevation adjustments to match final pavement grade
- Coordination of pavement paving schedule with utility commissioning

**PKG-05 (Concrete Structures) Interfaces**:
- Structure-to-pavement transitions (grade matching, joint construction, drainage)
- Protection of structure foundations during pavement grading and compaction
- Coordination of pavement edge restraint (curbs, edge beams) with structure design

**PKG-07 (Rail Works) Interfaces**:
- Rail crossing pavement details (embedment, transitions, joint sealing)
- Track elevation coordination (pavement grade relative to top-of-rail)
- Compaction restrictions near track (prevent track disturbance)
- Drainage coordination at rail crossings

**PKG-08 (Marine Structures) Interfaces**:
- Pavement termination at wharf edge (drainage, edge protection, loading compatibility)
- Coordination of pavement loading with wharf structural capacity
- Construction sequence (pavement before or after wharf completion)

**Source**: Datasheet "Interface Constraints" section; DEL-04.01 interface annotations
**Verification**: Coordination meetings with PKG-03, PKG-05, PKG-07, PKG-08 package leads; Guidance interface review checklist

#### R1.5: OBJ-8 Future Expandability Requirements Section
**Requirement**: The specification shall include OBJ-8 future expandability requirements for:

**Design for Ultimate Loading**:
- Pavement in Phase 2 expansion corridors designed and constructed for ultimate Phase 2 loading (reference DEL-04.03 design case)
- Avoidance of construction methods or materials that would require reconstruction for Phase 2

**Construction Joints at Tie-In Locations**:
- Sawcut joints or formed joints at Phase 2 tie-in locations (prevent cold joints that would require removal)
- Joint protection during Phase 1 operations (membrane, sealing, protection boards)
- Joint documentation for future reference (as-built survey, photographs)

**Utility Penetration Documentation**:
- Record locations of all utility penetrations through pavement (coordinate with PKG-03)
- Provide utility penetration as-built drawings for Phase 2 design reference
- Document utility depths, types, and protective measures

**Enhanced As-Built Documentation**:
- Survey verification of pavement grades in expansion corridor areas (beyond standard acceptance survey)
- Material records for expansion corridor pavement (mix designs, material certifications, compaction test results)
- Compaction test location plans with higher density of testing in expansion corridors (e.g., double standard frequency – **ASSUMPTION**)

**Source**: Datasheet "OBJ-8 Future Expansion Requirements" section; Decomposition OBJ-8 description
**Verification**: Guidance OBJ-8 callout review checklist; verify OBJ-8 provisions are clearly stated and enforceable
