## Attributes

### Specification Scope
This technical specification defines performance, materials, and workmanship requirements for all pavement and surfacing elements shown in DEL-04.01 and validated by DEL-04.03, including:

- **Asphalt paving**: Mix design, aggregate gradation, binder grade, compaction requirements, thickness tolerances
- **Concrete surfacing**: Strength class, admixtures, reinforcement, finishing, curing, joint construction
- **Subgrade preparation**: Compaction requirements, proof rolling, moisture conditioning, bearing capacity verification
- **Base and subbase courses**: Aggregate gradation, plasticity limits, compaction density, layer thickness tolerances
- **Line marking**: Material selection (paint vs. thermoplastic), color specifications, reflectivity standards, application methods
- **Quality assurance/quality control**: Testing frequencies, sampling procedures, acceptance criteria, non-conformance procedures
- **Interface requirements**: Coordination with adjacent packages (PKG-03, PKG-05, PKG-07, PKG-08) for pavement transitions
- **Future expandability provisions**: Construction methods and materials supporting OBJ-8 Phase 2 expansion corridors

### Conditions

#### Environmental/Climatic Constraints
- **Fraser Surrey, BC climate**: Freeze-thaw cycles require frost-resistant materials (air-entrained concrete, flexible asphalt binder grades)
- **Precipitation**: Construction scheduling to avoid heavy rain during paving operations; surface drainage provisions per DEL-04.01 plans
- **Temperature limitations**: Asphalt paving ambient temperature >10°C, concrete placement ambient temperature 5-30°C (hot/cold weather provisions may be required – **ASSUMPTION**)

#### Construction Constraints
- **Phased construction**: Specification must allow for phased paving operations to maintain site access during construction
- **Traffic management**: Temporary paving, detours, or access routes may be required (coordinate with site establishment package PKG-00)
- **Interface coordination**: Sequence of pavement construction relative to underground utilities (PKG-03), structures (PKG-05), rail works (PKG-07) must be specified to prevent damage or rework
- **Curing time**: Concrete curing period (7 days) may constrain construction schedule; accelerated curing methods may be specified if schedule-critical (**TBD**)

#### OBJ-8 Future Expansion Requirements
- **Design for ultimate loading**: Pavement in Phase 2 expansion corridors shall be designed and constructed for ultimate Phase 2 loading, even if initial Phase 1 use is lighter (to avoid future reconstruction)
- **Construction joints**: Construction joints at Phase 2 tie-in locations shall be detailed to facilitate future pavement extension without removal (sawcut joints, membrane protection, etc.)
- **Service penetrations**: Specification shall require documentation of all utility penetrations through pavement (coordinate with PKG-03) for future reference during Phase 2 expansion
- **As-built requirements**: Enhanced as-built documentation for expansion corridor areas (survey, material records, compaction test locations) per DEL-04.04

## Construction

### Material Sourcing and Approval
- **Aggregate sources**: Contractor shall submit aggregate source information and test results for approval prior to construction (crushing plant location, gradation test results, durability test results)
- **Mix design approval**: Asphalt and concrete mix designs shall be submitted for approval minimum 30 days prior to paving (**ASSUMPTION** – typical lead time)
- **Material certifications**: All materials shall have manufacturer certifications or test reports demonstrating compliance with specification requirements

### Workmanship Standards
- **Subgrade preparation**: Proof rolling, compaction testing, moisture conditioning as specified; non-conforming areas corrected prior to base course placement
- **Layer placement**: Each pavement layer (subbase, base, binder, wearing course) placed in specified thickness and compacted to specified density prior to next layer
- **Joint construction**: Construction joints, expansion joints, control joints constructed as detailed in DEL-04.01 drawings with specified joint sealant and backer rod
- **Surface finishing**: Final surface grade and smoothness meeting specified tolerances; areas not meeting tolerance shall be corrected (overlay, grinding, or reconstruction)
- **Line marking application**: Surface preparation (cleaning, drying), material application per manufacturer recommendations, reflective bead application while material is wet

### Testing and Acceptance
- **Compaction testing**: Nuclear density gauge or sand cone testing at specified frequency (e.g., one test per 500 m² per lift – **ASSUMPTION**)
- **Thickness verification**: Pavement cores at specified locations to verify as-built layer thicknesses (e.g., one core per 2000 m² – **ASSUMPTION**)
- **Material sampling**: Asphalt and concrete samples for laboratory testing (density, strength, air content, gradation) per specified frequency
- **Acceptance criteria**: All test results meeting specification requirements prior to final acceptance; non-conforming areas identified and dispositioned (acceptance, rework, rejection)
- **Records**: All test results documented in DEL-04.04 with location references, drawing revision, and specification clause cross-references
