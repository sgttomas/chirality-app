# Procedure: DEL-35.01 Training Materials

## Purpose

This procedure defines the process for producing and managing **Training Materials** within **PKG-35 Training & Handover**.

**Deliverable purpose:** Provides project management/control documentation for training materials required by the Employer's Requirements.

*Source: _CONTEXT.md, Decomposition Section 5 (PKG-35, DEL-35.01)*

**Deliverable classification:**
- **Type**: Document
- **Responsible party**: D&B Contractor (with OEM/Supplier)

*Source: _CONTEXT.md*

**Scope:** This procedure addresses both:
1. **Production of training materials** (how to create the deliverable artifacts: training manuals and presentations)
2. **Use of training materials** (how training materials will be deployed for facility personnel training)

*Source: Agent instructions (dual-use recursive procedure)*

## Steps

### Phase 1: Planning and Scoping (Early Project Phase)

**Step 1.1: Review Employer's Requirements**

**Objective:** Identify training material requirements specified by the Employer.

**Actions:**
1. Review Employer's Requirements (Volume 2 Parts 1, 2, 3) for training-related requirements
2. Extract specific requirements for:
   - Training material content and coverage
   - Format and deliverable requirements (electronic/printed)
   - Language requirements
   - Submittal and approval requirements
   - Quantity of copies required
3. Document requirements in a training material requirements register
4. Identify any ambiguities or gaps requiring clarification

**Outputs:**
- Training material requirements register
- List of clarifications/RFIs to be issued to Employer (if needed)

**Verification:** Requirements register reviewed by project management and training lead.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical requirements review process*

**Step 1.2: Develop Training Material Plan**

**Objective:** Establish the approach, structure, and schedule for training material development.

**Actions:**
1. Define training material structure (list of manuals and presentations to be produced)
2. Assign subject matter experts to each training topic
3. Establish document numbering scheme (coordinate with project document control)
4. Define format standards (templates, style guide)
5. Identify OEM/supplier coordination requirements
6. Develop training material development schedule aligned with project milestones
7. Identify upstream deliverable dependencies and required maturity levels
8. Define review and approval workflow

**Outputs:**
- Training Material Development Plan (may be standalone document or section within project Training Plan)
- Training material schedule
- Template documents (manual template, presentation template)

**Verification:** Training Material Development Plan reviewed and approved by project management and Employer (if required).

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical planning process*

**Step 1.3: Establish Coordination with Upstream Deliverables**

**Objective:** Ensure timely availability of input documentation.

**Actions:**
1. Review training material dependencies (see Prerequisites section above)
2. Coordinate with package leads to confirm document availability schedules
3. Request draft versions of key documents to enable early training material development
4. Flag critical dependencies to project scheduling team
5. Establish interface points with OEM/suppliers for equipment-specific content

**Outputs:**
- Coordination matrix showing dependencies and required dates
- Agreements with package leads for document availability

**Verification:** Dependencies captured in project coordination records (see `_DEPENDENCIES.md` mode: NOT_TRACKED — dependencies coordinated externally).

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical coordination process; reference to _DEPENDENCIES.md*

### Phase 2: Content Development (Design and Construction Phase)

**Step 2.1: Collect Source Materials**

**Objective:** Gather all reference documents and input data required for training material content development.

**Actions:**
1. Obtain design documentation (P&IDs, drawings, specifications, calculations)
2. Obtain equipment OEM manuals and datasheets
3. Obtain draft operating procedures (from PKG-27)
4. Obtain draft maintenance procedures (from PKG-31)
5. Obtain safety and environmental procedures (from PKG-32, PKG-33)
6. Obtain control system documentation (from PKG-19)
7. Conduct site walkdowns to take photographs of equipment and installations
8. Interview subject matter experts to capture tacit knowledge
9. Organize source materials in shared workspace accessible to training material authors

**Outputs:**
- Organized repository of source materials
- Site photographs and diagrams
- Interview notes from subject matter experts

**Verification:** Training material lead confirms all required source materials are available (or TBD items are documented).

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical content collection process*

**Step 2.2: Develop Training Manual Content (Iterative)**

**Objective:** Author comprehensive training manuals covering all facility systems per Specification.md requirements FR-01 through FR-06.

**Actions (for each training manual):**
1. **Define scope**: Determine what will be covered in this manual (system boundaries, target audience)
2. **Create outline**: Develop detailed table of contents following template structure
3. **Author content sections**:
   - Introduction and overview
   - Safety and environmental considerations
   - System description (based on P&IDs, equipment lists, control descriptions)
   - Operating procedures (based on PKG-27 deliverables and SME input)
   - Maintenance procedures (based on PKG-31 deliverables and OEM manuals)
   - Troubleshooting guides (based on SME input and OEM manuals)
   - References and appendices (glossary, contact information, related documents)
4. **Incorporate diagrams and visuals**: Insert P&IDs, system diagrams, equipment photos, flowcharts
5. **Cite sources**: Reference source documents for all technical content
6. **Mark TBDs**: Clearly flag any content gaps where source information is not yet available
7. **Format and number**: Apply template formatting, section numbering, page numbering, headers/footers
8. **Generate draft**: Produce draft manual for review

**Outputs (per manual):**
- Draft training manual (PDF and editable source format)

**Verification:** Subject matter expert review of draft content for technical accuracy.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical manual development process; aligns with Datasheet.md content organization and Specification.md requirements*

**Step 2.3: Develop Training Presentations (Iterative)**

**Objective:** Create instructional slide decks for classroom or self-study training.

**Actions (for each presentation):**
1. **Define learning objectives**: What should trainees know/be able to do after completing this training module?
2. **Create storyboard**: Outline presentation flow and key topics
3. **Author slides**:
   - Title slide with training module name and date
   - Learning objectives slide
   - Content slides (one key concept per slide; use visuals)
   - System overview diagrams
   - Operational sequences (step-by-step with visuals)
   - Safety and hazard awareness content
   - Examples and case studies
   - Review questions or discussion prompts
   - Summary slide recapping key points
4. **Incorporate visuals**: Use photos, diagrams, screenshots, and videos (if available)
5. **Add speaker notes**: Include detailed notes to guide instructors
6. **Apply branding/formatting**: Use project presentation template and style guide
7. **Generate draft**: Produce draft presentation for review

**Outputs (per presentation):**
- Draft training presentation (PowerPoint and PDF)

**Verification:** Subject matter expert review of draft content for technical accuracy and instructional effectiveness.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical presentation development process; aligns with Guidance.md recommendations*

**Step 2.4: Coordinate OEM Training Materials**

**Objective:** Obtain and integrate OEM/supplier training materials into facility training program.

**Actions:**
1. Request training materials from OEM/suppliers (per equipment supply contracts)
2. Review OEM materials for quality, completeness, and alignment with facility training approach
3. Identify gaps where OEM materials need supplementing with facility-specific procedures
4. Integrate OEM content into appropriate training manuals and presentations (with attribution)
5. Request OEM review of integrated training materials (if contractually required)
6. Coordinate OEM hands-on training sessions (if included in equipment supply scope)

**Outputs:**
- OEM training materials (incorporated into facility training manuals and presentations)
- OEM training coordination schedule (if hands-on training provided)

**Verification:** OEM materials reviewed and approved for integration.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical OEM coordination process; aligns with _CONTEXT.md (Responsible: D&B Contractor with OEM/Supplier)*

**Step 2.5: Update Training Materials During Construction**

**Objective:** Maintain training material accuracy as design changes and as-built conditions emerge.

**Actions:**
1. Establish change notification process (how construction team notifies training material lead of design changes)
2. Review design change notices and field change requests for impact on training materials
3. Update affected training manuals and presentations to reflect changes
4. Track changes in revision history
5. Maintain current draft versions accessible to training delivery team

**Outputs:**
- Updated training materials reflecting as-built conditions
- Revision logs documenting changes

**Verification:** Training material lead confirms all known design changes have been incorporated.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical as-built update process; aligns with Specification.md requirement QR-04 (As-Built Alignment)*

### Phase 3: Review and Quality Assurance (Late Construction / Pre-Commissioning Phase)

**Step 3.1: Internal Technical Review**

**Objective:** Verify technical accuracy and completeness of training materials before submittal to Employer.

**Actions:**
1. Assemble review team (subject matter experts, operations/maintenance representatives, quality reviewer)
2. Conduct page-by-page technical review of each training manual:
   - Verify technical accuracy against design documentation
   - Verify procedures align with PKG-27 operating procedures
   - Check for completeness (all required content covered)
   - Check for internal consistency (no contradictions)
   - Verify all diagrams and images are legible and correctly labeled
3. Conduct slide-by-slide review of each training presentation:
   - Verify technical accuracy
   - Check instructional effectiveness
   - Verify alignment with corresponding training manual
4. Document review comments in review register
5. Assign comments to training material authors for resolution
6. Track comment resolution

**Outputs:**
- Technical review comments register
- Comment resolution documentation

**Verification:** All review comments resolved or carried forward as TBDs with Employer concurrence.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical technical review process; aligns with Specification.md verification method VM-01*

**Step 3.2: Quality Assurance Review**

**Objective:** Verify training materials comply with project quality requirements and document control standards.

**Actions:**
1. Review training materials against project document control checklist:
   - Document numbering correct and consistent
   - Revision control information present and correct
   - Headers/footers formatted correctly
   - Page numbering correct
   - Table of contents accurate
   - References cited correctly
2. Check formatting and style guide compliance
3. Check spelling, grammar, and readability
4. Verify all TBD items are clearly flagged
5. Verify all assumptions are clearly labeled
6. Verify sources are cited for technical content

**Outputs:**
- Quality assurance review checklist (completed)
- Corrective actions (if non-conformances identified)

**Verification:** Quality assurance sign-off confirming training materials comply with project quality requirements.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical QA review process; aligns with Specification.md verification method VM-03*

**Step 3.3: Prepare Submittal Package**

**Objective:** Compile training materials for submittal to Employer for review and approval.

**Actions:**
1. Compile complete set of training materials (all manuals and presentations)
2. Prepare transmittal letter summarizing contents and requesting approval
3. Include any supporting documentation (training plan, training schedule, competency assessment forms)
4. Convert materials to submittal format (typically PDF for review)
5. Upload to project document management system
6. Issue formal transmittal to Employer via project document control procedures

**Outputs:**
- Training material submittal package (electronic)
- Transmittal documentation

**Verification:** Document control confirms submittal completeness and correct routing.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical submittal process*

**Step 3.4: Employer Review and Comment Resolution**

**Objective:** Obtain Employer approval of training materials.

**Actions:**
1. Respond to Employer review comments:
   - Accept comments and revise training materials as appropriate
   - Provide technical justification for comments not accepted
   - Seek clarification for ambiguous comments
2. Track comment resolution in response register
3. Update training materials based on accepted comments
4. Re-issue training materials to Employer for final approval (if required per contract)
5. Obtain Employer approval sign-off

**Outputs:**
- Employer comment response register
- Revised training materials (approved version)
- Employer approval documentation

**Verification:** Employer approval obtained (or conditional approval with minor outstanding items).

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical Employer review process; aligns with Specification.md verification method VM-04*

### Phase 4: Commissioning Phase Updates (Commissioning Phase)

**Step 4.1: Capture Commissioning Learnings**

**Objective:** Incorporate operational learnings from commissioning phase into training materials.

**Actions:**
1. Attend commissioning activities to observe actual operations
2. Document lessons learned during commissioning:
   - Procedures that worked differently than expected
   - Troubleshooting insights
   - Operational tips and tricks
   - Equipment quirks or special considerations
3. Take photographs and videos of operations for training material enhancement
4. Interview commissioning team and operators for feedback on training material accuracy and completeness

**Outputs:**
- Commissioning lessons learned log
- Updated photos and videos
- Feedback from commissioning team

**Verification:** Commissioning manager confirms key learnings have been captured.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical commissioning feedback loop; aligns with Guidance.md Consideration 2 (training timing and facility readiness)*

**Step 4.2: Issue Final Training Materials**

**Objective:** Produce final, as-built training materials incorporating commissioning updates.

**Actions:**
1. Update training materials based on commissioning learnings
2. Resolve any outstanding TBD items (or confirm TBDs remain for post-handover resolution)
3. Conduct final technical review of updated content
4. Update revision control information (mark as "Final" or "As-Built" revision)
5. Obtain final approval from Employer (if contractually required)
6. Publish final training materials to project document management system
7. Prepare final copies (electronic and printed per contract requirements)

**Outputs:**
- Final training materials (approved, as-built version)
- Electronic copies (uploaded to document management system)
- Printed copies (per contract requirements)

**Verification:** Final training materials verified as complete and approved.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical final issue process; aligns with Specification.md requirement QR-04 (As-Built Alignment)*

### Phase 5: Training Delivery Support (Pre-Handover Phase)

**Note:** This phase describes how training materials are **used** (operational procedure). If training delivery is outside Contractor scope, this phase may be informational only.

**Step 5.1: Prepare for Training Delivery**

**Objective:** Set up training program logistics and prepare instructors.

**Actions (if training delivery in Contractor scope):**
1. Schedule training sessions (coordinate with Employer and operations team)
2. Arrange training facilities (classroom, projector, handouts)
3. Arrange site access for hands-on training
4. Print training materials (manuals and handout copies of presentations)
5. Train the trainers (brief instructors on training materials and delivery approach)
6. Prepare training attendance records and competency assessment forms (cross-reference DEL-35.02)

**Outputs:**
- Training schedule
- Training facilities arranged
- Printed training materials
- Instructor preparation completed
- Training records templates (cross-reference DEL-35.02)

**Verification:** Training logistics confirmed and ready for training commencement.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical training preparation; cross-reference to DEL-35.02 per Specification.md requirement VM-06*

**Step 5.2: Deliver Training**

**Objective:** Train facility personnel using developed training materials.

**Actions (if training delivery in Contractor scope):**
1. Conduct classroom training sessions using training presentations
2. Provide hands-on training at facility using training manuals as reference
3. Demonstrate procedures and allow trainees to practice
4. Conduct competency assessments (written tests and/or practical demonstrations)
5. Record training attendance and competency assessment results (per DEL-35.02)
6. Provide feedback to trainees and issue certificates of completion (if applicable)

**Outputs:**
- Training delivered to facility personnel
- Training attendance records (per DEL-35.02)
- Competency assessment results (per DEL-35.02)

**Verification:** All personnel required to be trained have completed training and demonstrated competency.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical training delivery process; cross-reference to DEL-35.02*

**Step 5.3: Collect Training Feedback**

**Objective:** Gather feedback to improve training materials and identify gaps.

**Actions:**
1. Distribute training evaluation forms to trainees
2. Conduct debrief with instructors
3. Review competency assessment results to identify common areas of difficulty
4. Document feedback and improvement opportunities
5. Update training materials based on feedback (minor corrections)

**Outputs:**
- Training feedback summary
- Updated training materials (post-training revision)

**Verification:** Feedback documented and actionable items addressed.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical training feedback loop; aligns with Guidance.md best practices*

### Phase 6: Handover and Closeout (Handover Phase)

6. Prepare training material index listing all documents with revision and date
7. Deliver training materials to Employer with handover certificate

**Outputs:**
- Training materials delivered to Employer (cross-reference DEL-35.03)
- Handover documentation package including training material index

**Verification:** Employer acknowledges receipt of training materials as part of handover acceptance.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical handover process; cross-reference to DEL-35.03 per package scope*

**Outputs:**
- Training material maintenance guidance provided to Employer

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical knowledge transfer at handover; reference to DEL-35.05 (Defects Liability)*

## Verification

- **Activity**: Technical subject matter expert review of all training content
- **Criteria**: Zero technical errors identified in final review
- **Responsibility**: Discipline SMEs and QA reviewer
- **Implementation**: See Step 3.1 (Internal Technical Review)

*Source: Specification.md (VM-01)*

- **Activity**: Checklist review verifying all required systems and topics are covered

*Source: Specification.md (VM-02)*

- **Activity**: Quality assurance review of document formatting, numbering, and revision control

- **Responsibility**: QA reviewer and document control

*Source: Specification.md (VM-03)*

- **Activity**: Employer review and approval of training materials

- **Criteria**: Training materials enable effective learning (trainee comprehension assessment)
