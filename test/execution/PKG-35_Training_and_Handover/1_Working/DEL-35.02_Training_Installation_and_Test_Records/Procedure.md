# Procedure: DEL-35.02 Training Installation & Test Records

## Purpose

This procedure defines the process for producing and managing **Training Installation & Test Records** within **PKG-35 Training & Handover**.

**Deliverable purpose:** Provides evidence of completion, inspection, and testing for training activities.

*Source: _CONTEXT.md, Decomposition Section 5 (PKG-35, DEL-35.02)*

**Deliverable classification:**
- **Type**: Record
- **Responsible party**: D&B Contractor (with OEM/Supplier)

*Source: _CONTEXT.md*

**Scope:** This procedure addresses:
1. **Production of training records** (how to create and manage attendance records and competency records during training delivery)
2. **Compilation of training records** (how to organize and package records for handover)
3. **Use of training records** (how records support handover acceptance and future workforce management)

*Source: Agent instructions (dual-use recursive procedure)*

## Steps

### Phase 1: Planning and Preparation (Pre-Training)

**Step 1.1: Develop Training Record Forms**

**Objective:** Create standardized forms for training attendance and competency assessment per Specification.md requirements.

**Actions:**
1. Review Specification.md requirements FR-01 (attendance records) and FR-02 (competency records)
2. Draft training attendance record form including all required fields per FR-01.1 through FR-01.11
3. Draft competency assessment record form including all required fields per FR-02.1 through FR-02.12
4. Include privacy notice on forms (personal information management per Canadian privacy legislation)
5. Pilot test forms with training coordinator and sample instructor
6. Revise forms based on feedback
7. Submit forms to Employer for review and approval (if required by contract)
8. Finalize and distribute forms to all instructors/trainers and assessors

**Outputs:**
- Approved training attendance record form (template)
- Approved competency assessment record form (template)
- Training program overview document template
- Trainer/assessor qualification record form

**Verification:** Forms reviewed and approved by project quality manager and Employer (if required).

*Source: Specification.md FR-01, FR-02, FR-03; Guidance.md Best Practice 1 (standardize forms early)*

**Step 1.2: Establish Training Record Management System**

**Objective:** Set up filing system and procedures for creating, managing, and archiving training records.

**Actions:**
1. Designate training coordinator role and assign personnel
2. Create folder structure for training records (by trainee, by module, by date)
3. Establish record numbering/identification system
4. Set up secure storage for paper records (if used) with access controls
5. Configure project document management system folders for electronic records
6. Define record completion workflow (who completes what, when, approval process)
7. Define quality check process (real-time checks after each session)
8. Brief all instructors/trainers on record completion procedures

**Outputs:**
- Training record management plan (may be section within overall training plan)
- Folder structure established (physical and/or electronic)
- Training coordinator assigned
- Record procedures briefed to training team

**Verification:** Training coordinator confirms readiness to manage records.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical record management setup process; Guidance.md Best Practice 2 (dedicated training coordinator)*

**Step 1.3: Develop Competency Matrix and Assessment Plan**

**Objective:** Define which competencies are required for which positions, and how they will be assessed.

**Actions:**
1. Review training materials (DEL-35.01) to identify all competencies addressed in training program
2. Create competency matrix mapping competencies to positions (operations, maintenance, management, etc.)
3. Define competency levels (e.g., Required/Basic/Awareness) per position
4. Define assessment method for each competency (written test, practical demonstration, simulator, etc.)
5. Define assessment criteria and pass thresholds
6. Develop or obtain assessment tools (tests, checklists, practical demonstration procedures)
7. Identify and qualify assessors for each competency
8. Document assessor qualifications (trainer/assessor qualification records)
9. Submit competency matrix and assessment plan to Employer for review (if required)

**Outputs:**
- Competency matrix (competencies × positions)
- Assessment plan (method and criteria for each competency)
- Assessment tools (tests, checklists, procedures)
- Assessor qualifications documented

**Verification:** Competency matrix and assessment plan reviewed and approved by project management and Employer (if required).

*Source: Specification.md FR-03 (training program overview includes competency matrix); Guidance.md Example 4 (competency matrix example)*

### Phase 2: Training Delivery and Record Creation (Pre-Commissioning and Commissioning)

**Step 2.1: Prepare for Training Session**

**Objective:** Ensure all prerequisites are in place before each training session.

**Actions (before each session):**
1. Confirm training schedule (date, time, location)
2. Confirm trainees are notified and available
3. Confirm instructor/trainer is assigned and prepared
4. Confirm training materials are available (manuals, presentations, equipment for hands-on)
5. Prepare training attendance record form (pre-populate session details: training topic, date, training material reference)
6. Confirm training material document number and revision being used (cross-reference DEL-35.01)
7. Arrange training facility (classroom, projector, site access if hands-on)

**Outputs:**
- Training session ready to commence
- Pre-populated attendance record form ready for completion

**Verification:** Training coordinator confirms session readiness.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical training session preparation; cross-reference to DEL-35.01 for training material version control*

**Step 2.2: Conduct Training Session and Complete Attendance Record**

**Objective:** Deliver training and document attendance in real-time.

**Actions (during and immediately after training session):**
1. Instructor conducts training using training materials (DEL-35.01)
2. At start of session, circulate attendance record form for trainees to sign in (or mark attendance on roster)
3. During session, note any partial attendance or early departures
4. At end of session, verify all trainees have signed attendance record
5. Instructor completes remaining fields on attendance record:
   - Training duration
   - Training method
   - Instructor name and qualifications
   - Notes on any absences or incomplete training
6. Instructor signs and dates attendance record
7. Instructor submits completed attendance record to training coordinator immediately (same day)
8. Training coordinator performs quality check:
   - All required fields completed
   - All signatures present
   - Training material cross-reference correct
   - Notes any issues for follow-up

**Outputs:**
- Completed training attendance record (per Specification.md FR-01)
- Record filed by training coordinator (paper file and/or scanned to electronic file)

**Verification:** Training coordinator quality check (real-time) per Guidance.md Best Practice 3.

*Source: Specification.md FR-01 (attendance record requirements); Guidance.md Best Practice 3 (real-time quality checks)*

**Step 2.3: Conduct Competency Assessment and Complete Competency Record**

**Objective:** Assess trainee competency and document assessment results.

**Actions (immediately following training or at scheduled assessment time):**
1. Assessor conducts competency assessment using defined assessment method (written test, practical demonstration, simulator, etc.)
2. Assessor completes competency assessment record form:
   - Trainee identification
   - Competency being assessed
   - Assessment method
   - Assessment date and location
   - Assessor name and qualifications
   - Assessment criteria
3. Assessor evaluates trainee performance against criteria
4. Assessor records assessment result (pass/fail, score if applicable)
5. Assessor documents evidence of assessment (attach test results, observation checklist, or describe performance)
6. Assessor provides feedback to trainee (especially if fail result)
7. Trainee signs assessment record (acknowledgment of result)
8. Assessor signs assessment record (verification)
9. If trainee fails assessment:
   - Schedule remedial training or practice
   - Schedule re-assessment
   - Document re-assessment plan on form
10. Assessor submits completed competency record to training coordinator immediately (same day)
11. Training coordinator performs quality check:
    - All required fields completed
    - Both signatures present (trainee and assessor)
    - Evidence of assessment documented or attached
    - Re-assessment plan documented if applicable

**Outputs:**
- Completed competency assessment record (per Specification.md FR-02)
- Re-assessment scheduled if needed
- Record filed by training coordinator

**Verification:** Training coordinator quality check (real-time).

*Source: Specification.md FR-02 (competency record requirements); Guidance.md considerations on assessment rigor*

**Step 2.4: Track Training Progress and Outstanding Items**

**Objective:** Maintain visibility of training program status and identify follow-up actions.

**Actions (ongoing throughout training program):**
1. Training coordinator maintains training progress tracker:
   - Which training sessions have been delivered
   - Which personnel have attended which sessions
   - Which competency assessments have been completed
   - Which assessments are outstanding
   - Which re-assessments are scheduled
2. Identify personnel with incomplete training or outstanding competency assessments
3. Coordinate make-up training sessions for personnel who missed sessions
4. Coordinate re-assessments for personnel who did not pass initial assessment
5. Report training progress to project management regularly (weekly or as requested)
6. Escalate any issues (personnel refusing training, persistent assessment failures, schedule delays)

**Outputs:**
- Training progress tracker (spreadsheet or dashboard)
- Regular progress reports
- Follow-up actions coordinated

**Verification:** 100% training and competency assessment completion achieved before handover.

*Source: Specification.md PR-01 (completeness requirement); **ASSUMPTION** on progress tracking*

**Step 2.5: Coordinate OEM-Provided Training and Records**

**Objective:** Ensure OEM training is delivered and documented per project requirements.

**Actions (if OEM/suppliers provide training):**
1. Coordinate with OEM to schedule training sessions
2. Provide OEM with training record form templates (attendance and competency)
3. Brief OEM instructors on record completion requirements
4. Contractor training coordinator attends OEM training sessions to:
   - Verify attendance record is completed correctly
   - Verify competency assessments are conducted and documented
   - Answer questions on project record management procedures
5. Collect completed OEM training records immediately following OEM training
6. Perform quality check on OEM training records (same standards as Contractor records)
7. If OEM provides training certificates instead of detailed records, supplement with attendance record documenting certificate receipt
8. Incorporate OEM training records into overall training record package

**Outputs:**
- OEM training records incorporated into project training records
- OEM training certificates (if provided) included in record package

**Verification:** OEM training records meet project requirements per Specification.md.

*Source: _CONTEXT.md (Responsible: D&B Contractor with OEM/Supplier); Guidance.md Consideration 5 (OEM training coordination)*

### Phase 3: Record Compilation and Quality Assurance (Late Commissioning / Pre-Handover)

**Step 3.1: Compile Training Record Package**

**Objective:** Organize all training records into structured package for handover.

**Actions:**
1. Collect all training attendance records (Contractor-led and OEM-led training)
2. Collect all competency assessment records (all personnel, all competencies)
3. Organize records in multiple ways for easy retrieval:
   - By trainee (individual training history file for each person)
   - By training module (all attendance records for each module)
   - By date (chronological record of training delivery)
4. Create training program overview document (per Specification.md FR-03):
   - Summary of training program structure
   - Training schedule (as-planned and as-delivered)
   - Training material index (cross-reference to DEL-35.01)
   - Competency matrix
   - Trainer/assessor qualifications summary
   - Training program statistics (personnel trained, hours delivered, pass rates)
5. Create training record index (master list of all records with document numbers, cross-references)
6. Scan all paper records to searchable PDF (if paper forms were used)
7. Organize electronic files in logical folder structure
8. Upload to project document management system

**Outputs:**
- Training record package (electronic, organized and indexed)
- Training program overview document
- Training record index

**Verification:** Training coordinator confirms all records collected and organized.

*Source: Specification.md FR-03 (training program overview), Output-01 through Output-04 (required outputs); Guidance.md Example 3 (indexing structure)*

**Step 3.2: Perform Completeness and Accuracy Review**

**Objective:** Verify training record package is complete and accurate before submittal.

**Actions:**
1. Assemble review team (training coordinator, quality reviewer, project management representative)
2. Review training record package against checklist:
   - All required personnel have training records (100% coverage)
   - All required training sessions are documented (attendance records for each session)
   - All required competency assessments are documented (competency records for each required competency)
   - All forms are fully completed (no blank fields without justification)
   - All signatures are present (trainee, instructor, assessor)
   - Training material cross-references are correct (document numbers and revisions match DEL-35.01)
   - Dates and times are consistent and logical
   - Any failed assessments have re-assessment records showing subsequent pass
3. Perform spot-check accuracy review (sample 10% of records minimum):
   - Verify data against source documents (training schedules, original forms if electronic copies made)
   - Verify signatures are authentic
   - Verify assessment results are correctly recorded
4. Document review findings in review checklist or register
5. Address any gaps or errors identified:
   - Obtain missing signatures (contact trainee/instructor/assessor)
   - Correct errors with authorized corrections (initial and date changes)
   - Document any exceptions (e.g., personnel who could not be trained due to absence)
6. Obtain sign-off from review team that training records are complete and accurate

**Outputs:**
- Training record completeness checklist (completed)
- Training record accuracy review register (spot-check results)
- Any corrective actions completed
- Sign-off from review team

**Verification:** Quality reviewer confirms training records meet Specification.md requirements VM-01, VM-02, VM-03, VM-04.

*Source: Specification.md verification methods VM-01 through VM-04; Guidance.md Best Practice 3 (real-time and final quality checks)*

**Step 3.3: Prepare Training Record Summary for Handover**

**Objective:** Create summary document for handover package highlighting training program outcomes.

**Actions:**
1. Prepare training record summary document including:
   - Training program overview (period, total hours, total personnel)
   - Training completion status (by position or department)
   - Competency assessment results (pass rates, final competency status)
   - Training records delivered (inventory of records)
   - Conclusion statement (workforce readiness)
2. Include summary in handover documentation package (cross-reference DEL-35.03)
3. Prepare transmittal documentation for training record package

**Outputs:**
- Training record summary (for handover package)
- Transmittal documentation

**Verification:** Training record summary accurately reflects training program outcomes.

*Source: Guidance.md Example 5 (training record summary for handover); cross-reference to DEL-35.03*

### Phase 4: Submittal and Handover (Handover Phase)

**Step 4.1: Submit Training Records to Employer**

**Objective:** Deliver training record package to Employer as part of handover documentation.

**Actions:**
1. Compile complete training record package:
   - All attendance records
   - All competency records
   - Training program overview document
   - Training record index
   - Training record summary
   - Trainer/assessor qualification records
2. Prepare electronic copies:
   - Upload to project document management system (if not already done)
   - Prepare USB drive or other electronic media (if required by contract)
   - Ensure files are organized in logical folder structure with clear naming
3. Prepare printed copies (per contract requirements — quantity TBD)
4. Include training records in handover documentation package (cross-reference DEL-35.03)
5. Prepare transmittal letter summarizing training record package contents
6. Submit training records to Employer via project document control procedures
7. Include confidentiality notice (training records contain personal information, manage per privacy legislation)

**Outputs:**
- Training record package submitted to Employer (electronic and printed copies per contract)
- Transmittal documentation
- Training records incorporated into handover package (cross-reference DEL-35.03)

**Verification:** Document control confirms submittal completeness and correct routing.

*Source: Specification.md Documentation section (DM-02 compilation and packaging); cross-reference to DEL-35.03 for handover integration*

**Step 4.2: Support Employer Review and Acceptance**

**Objective:** Respond to Employer questions and facilitate training record acceptance.

**Actions:**
1. Be available to answer Employer questions about training records
2. Provide clarification on any records that are unclear
3. Provide additional information if requested (e.g., copies of training material revisions used)
4. Address any Employer concerns about training program completeness or personnel competency
5. Obtain Employer acceptance of training records as part of handover acceptance criteria

**Outputs:**
- Employer acceptance of training records
- Any supplementary information provided to Employer

**Verification:** Employer confirms training records are acceptable and workforce readiness acceptance criteria are met.

*Source: Specification.md VM-05 (integration with handover); cross-reference to DEL-35.03*

**Step 4.3: Transfer Training Record Ownership to Employer**

**Objective:** Transition responsibility for training record management to Employer.

**Actions:**
1. Brief Employer on training record organization and indexing
2. Provide guidance on using training records:
   - Individual training history files for personnel management
   - Training material cross-references for future training updates
   - Competency records for ongoing competency management
3. Advise Employer on training record retention requirements:
   - Legal retention period (typically 7-10 years for training records or per regulatory requirements)
   - Confidentiality and privacy requirements for personal information
   - Backup and archival recommendations
4. Provide guidance on post-handover training record management:
   - Process for documenting training of new hires (use same forms for consistency)
   - Process for refresher training documentation
   - Process for updating records when facility modifications occur
5. Provide Contractor contact information for any questions during defects liability period
6. **TBD** — Confirm Contractor support for training record updates during defects liability period (cross-reference DEL-35.05)

**Outputs:**
- Training record management guidance provided to Employer
- Ownership and responsibility transferred

**Verification:** Employer confirms understanding of training record management approach.

*Source: **ASSUMPTION** — typical knowledge transfer at handover; cross-reference to DEL-35.05 (Defects Liability)*

## Verification

### Verification Activities

**Verification Method 1: Completeness Check (VM-01)**
- **Activity**: Review training record package against checklist of required records per Specification.md requirements
- **Criteria**: All required records present and complete (100% coverage of personnel, training sessions, competency assessments)
- **Responsibility**: Training coordinator and quality reviewer
- **Implementation**: See Step 3.2 (Completeness and Accuracy Review)

*Source: Specification.md (VM-01)*

**Verification Method 2: Accuracy Verification (VM-02)**
- **Activity**: Spot-check training record data (minimum 10% sample) against source documents and verify signatures
- **Criteria**: 100% accuracy in spot-check sample; all signatures authentic
- **Responsibility**: Quality reviewer
- **Implementation**: See Step 3.2 (Completeness and Accuracy Review)

*Source: Specification.md (VM-02)*

**Verification Method 3: Witness Signature Verification (VM-03)**
- **Activity**: Verify all required signatures are present (instructor, assessor, trainee)
- **Criteria**: All required signatures present and authenticated
- **Responsibility**: Training coordinator
- **Implementation**: Real-time checks after each training session/assessment (Step 2.2, 2.3) and final verification (Step 3.2)

*Source: Specification.md (VM-03)*

**Verification Method 4: Archival Format Compliance (VM-04)**
- **Activity**: Verify records are in approved archival format (searchable PDF), legible, and properly organized
- **Criteria**: 100% of records meet archival format standards
- **Responsibility**: Training coordinator and document control
- **Implementation**: See Step 3.1 (Compile Training Record Package)

*Source: Specification.md (VM-04)*

**Verification Method 5: Integration with Handover (VM-05)**
- **Activity**: Verify training records are included in handover documentation package (cross-reference DEL-35.03)
- **Criteria**: Employer accepts training records as part of handover; workforce readiness acceptance criteria met
- **Responsibility**: Project management
- **Implementation**: See Step 4.1, 4.2 (Submit and Support Employer Review)

*Source: Specification.md (VM-05); cross-reference to DEL-35.03*

**Verification Method 6: Regulatory Compliance Verification (VM-06)**
- **Activity**: Review training records against applicable regulatory requirements (occupational health and safety, environmental, etc.)
- **Criteria**: Training records demonstrate full regulatory compliance
- **Responsibility**: HSE manager and quality reviewer
- **Implementation**: See Step 3.2 (regulatory compliance spot-check); **TBD** — specific regulatory requirements

*Source: Specification.md (VM-06); regulatory requirements TBD*
