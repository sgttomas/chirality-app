## R-801: Geotechnical Conditions Worse Than Assumed

**Risk Driver:** Scope / Quantity / Productivity

**Cause:**
- Marine geotechnical investigation (DEL-08.04) not yet performed
- Soil parameters, pile bearing capacity, and scour depth unknown
- Pile type, size, quantity, and embedment depth are assumptions

**Consequence:**
- Pile quantity may increase (more piles required for capacity)
- Pile length may increase (deeper embedment required)
- Pile installation costs may increase (difficult driving, refusal, pre-drilling)
- Potential for pile design changes (H-pile vs pipe pile, larger sections)

**Affected Buckets:**
- MAT (Materials): A-812 Marine Piling Supply
- CD (Construction Directs): A-819 Marine Piling Installation
- E (Engineering): A-803, A-804 (additional geotechnical work, re-design)

**Potential Cost Impact:** +$200k to +$500k (15% to 40% increase in piling scope)

**Suggested Mitigation:**
1. Prioritize marine geotechnical investigation (DEL-08.04) immediately
2. Obtain multiple geotechnical consultant quotes to confirm investigation scope
3. Review existing geotechnical data for Fraser River site if available
4. Include pile load testing in investigation scope to verify capacity assumptions

**Contingency Handling:** Partially covered by 25-30% contingency on MAT/CD piling line items

## R-802: Marine Logistics Constraints

**Risk Driver:** Productivity / Schedule

**Cause:**
- Marine logistics plan not yet developed
- Barge/crane availability unknown
- Weather and tide window constraints not analyzed
- Contractor marine capabilities and mobilization costs unknown

**Consequence:**
- Marine logistics costs may increase (barge/crane rates, demurrage, standby time)
- Construction productivity may decrease (tide/weather delays, restricted work windows)
- Schedule may extend (seasonal weather restrictions, barge availability)
- Specialized marine contractor premium rates may apply

**Affected Buckets:**
- FRT (Freight): A-818 Marine Logistics
- CD (Construction Directs): A-819, A-820, A-821 (all marine installation line items)
- CI (Construction Indirects): Extended supervision and marine safety oversight

**Potential Cost Impact:** +$150k to +$400k (20% to 50% increase in marine logistics and productivity losses)

**Suggested Mitigation:**
1. Develop preliminary marine construction methodology and logistics plan
2. Pre-qualify marine contractors with barge/crane capabilities
3. Analyze tide and weather windows for Fraser River site
4. Obtain budgetary quotes from marine contractors including logistics costs
5. Consider seasonal restrictions and optimal construction windows in schedule

**Contingency Handling:** Partially covered by 25% contingency on FRT and 30% contingency on CD marine installation line items

## R-803: Interfacing Equipment Loads Exceed Structural Capacity

**Risk Driver:** Scope / Interface

**Cause:**
- Equipment loads from PKG-09 (fenders, bollards) and PKG-11 (marine loading arm) not yet defined
- Structural design assumptions (platform framing, equipment supports) may be inadequate
- Interface coordination between PKG-08, PKG-09, and PKG-11 not yet performed

**Consequence:**
- Platform structural steel tonnage may increase (heavier framing, larger sections)
- Foundation loads may increase (more/larger piles required)
- Potential for structural re-design if equipment loads significantly exceed assumptions
- Fender reaction loads may require additional structural bracing

**Affected Buckets:**
- MAT (Materials): A-812 (piling), A-814 (platform steel)
- CD (Construction Directs): A-819 (piling), A-821 (platform erection)
- E (Engineering): A-803 (structural re-design)

**Potential Cost Impact:** +$100k to +$300k (10% to 30% increase in platform and foundation scope)

**Suggested Mitigation:**
1. Initiate early interface coordination with PKG-09 and PKG-11
2. Obtain preliminary equipment loads and envelopes from equipment vendors
3. Develop preliminary platform structural design with conservative load assumptions
4. Include interface coordination in project management plan

**Contingency Handling:** Partially covered by 25% contingency on MAT and 30% contingency on CD

## R-805: Environmental Design Basis More Severe Than Assumed

**Risk Driver:** Scope / Design Basis

**Cause:**
- Marine environmental data (wind, current, wave, water levels, seismic) not yet obtained
- Current assessment (DEL-08.10) not yet performed
- Environmental loads used for structural design are assumptions
- Climate change impacts and extreme events not yet analyzed

**Consequence:**
- Structural design loads may increase (wind, current, wave, seismic)
- Pile capacity requirements may increase (lateral loads)
- Structural steel sections may increase (larger members, heavier framing)
- Foundation design may require re-analysis

**Affected Buckets:**
- MAT (Materials): A-812 (piling), A-813 (trestle steel), A-814 (platform steel)
- E (Engineering): A-803 (structural re-design), A-810 (current assessment)

**Potential Cost Impact:** +$100k to +$400k (10% to 40% increase in structural scope)

**Suggested Mitigation:**
1. Obtain marine environmental data from ER or conduct field measurements
2. Complete current assessment report (DEL-08.10) early
3. Review historical environmental data for Fraser River site
4. Engage marine engineering consultant with local experience
5. Consider climate change impacts and extreme events in design basis

**Contingency Handling:** Partially covered by 25% contingency on MAT and 20% contingency on E

## R-808: Schedule Compression Requiring Premium Rates

**Risk Driver:** Schedule / Productivity

**Cause:**
- Project schedule not yet defined
- Marine construction schedule constraints unknown (tide/weather windows, seasonal restrictions)
- Potential for accelerated schedule to meet project milestones

**Consequence:**
- Marine contractor may charge premium rates (overtime, shift work, accelerated schedule)
- Marine logistics costs may increase (expedited mobilization, standby barge/crane)
- Productivity may decrease (extended work hours, compressed schedule)
- Engineering costs may increase (accelerated design, expedited deliverables)

**Affected Buckets:**
- CD (Construction Directs): All installation line items
- FRT (Freight): Marine logistics
- E (Engineering): Engineering deliverables
- CI (Construction Indirects): Extended supervision

**Potential Cost Impact:** +$150k to +$500k (10% to 30% schedule premium)

**Suggested Mitigation:**
1. Define realistic project schedule with appropriate marine construction windows
2. Identify critical path and long-lead items early
3. Avoid schedule compression unless necessary
4. Obtain marine contractor quotes for both normal and accelerated schedules
5. Plan for seasonal restrictions and optimal work windows

**Contingency Handling:** Partially covered by overall 25% contingency
