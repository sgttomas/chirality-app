### 2. Define procedure structure and content outline

**Action:** Establish the procedure structure aligned with anticipated artifacts and confirm content requirements for each procedure.

**Procedure list (from deliverable scope):**

1. Traffic management plan
2. Site access procedure
3. Mobilization procedure

**Content outline per procedure (ASSUMPTION: standard procedure format):**

- **Traffic management plan:**
  - Purpose and scope
  - Roles and responsibilities
  - Traffic routing (construction vs. terminal operational)
  - Speed limits, signage, and pavement markings
  - Pedestrian safety and crossings
  - Flagging and traffic control protocols
  - Emergency vehicle access
  - Phasing and updates
  - Monitoring and enforcement

- **Site access procedure:**
  - Purpose and scope
  - Roles and responsibilities
  - Security gate operations and hours
  - Badging and visitor sign-in
  - Vehicle inspection and parking
  - After-hours and emergency access
  - Coordination with terminal security
  - Access violations and revocation

- **Mobilization procedure:**
  - Purpose and scope
  - Roles and responsibilities
  - Mobilization schedule and sequencing
  - Temporary facility setup
  - Utility connections and testing
  - Equipment delivery and positioning
  - Personnel onboarding and orientation
  - Readiness verification
  - Demobilization planning

(Cross-reference: Guidance.md Examples section for typical procedure content.)

### 3. Develop execution methods and controls

**Action:** Populate procedure methods and controls incorporating safety, quality, and Terminal Continuity objective (OBJ-5).

**Sub-steps:**

3.1. Define execution methods:
- Step-by-step procedures for each activity
- Decision points and alternate paths
- Prerequisites and sequencing constraints
- Tools, equipment, and resources required

3.2. Define safety controls:
- Hazard identification and mitigation
- Personal protective equipment (PPE) requirements
- Permit requirements (hot work, confined space, etc.)
- Emergency response procedures

3.3. Define quality controls:
- Inspection hold points and criteria
- Testing and verification requirements
- Documentation and record-keeping
- Non-conformance and corrective action procedures

3.4. Define operational coordination:
- Communication protocols with Employer and terminal operations
- Interface procedures (traffic coordination, security, utility tie-ins)
- Methods to minimize disruption to terminal operations (OBJ-5)

3.5. Coordinate procedures with Employer to confirm operational constraints and security protocols. (**TBD** — coordination process not yet defined)

3.6. Refine procedures based on Employer feedback and coordination with other deliverables (DEL-00.01, DEL-00.02, HSE/QA plans). (**TBD** — coordination process not yet defined)
