### Typical Specification Structure (per CSI MasterFormat or Similar)

**ASSUMPTION**: Standard technical specification format to be confirmed with project specification standards:

Each specification section typically includes:

1. **General**
   - Section scope and description
   - Related sections (cross-references to other specification sections and drawings)
   - References (applicable standards and codes)
   - Submittals (shop drawings, product data, samples, test reports)
   - Quality assurance (qualifications, certifications, testing)
   - Delivery, storage, and handling

2. **Products**
   - Materials specifications (types, grades, properties)
   - Equipment specifications (capacities, performance, efficiency)
   - Manufacturers and acceptable products
   - Fabrication requirements
   - Source quality control

3. **Execution**
   - Installation requirements and methods
   - Field quality control (inspections, testing)
   - Startup and commissioning requirements
   - Cleaning and protection
   - Warranty requirements
